## Hen and Chickens reef

Hen and Chickens reef is a reef dive site in Florida Keys, North America.

## Overview

Hen and Chickens is a cluster of patch reefs off Islamorada whose coral formations, seen from above, radiate outward like a mother hen surrounded by chicks — a named Sanctuary Preservation Area protecting large populations of ESA-listed star corals and brain corals. The site is one of the best beginner dives in the Florida Keys, with depths of 10–22 feet and coral mounds reaching up to 15 feet in height above the sand, creating channels that form a three-dimensional maze of coral architecture. A brick barge that broke free from its tow in July 1949 rests within the SPA with its bow rising to within 10 feet of the surface, extending along a 100-foot axis and surrounded by large tarpon and resident barracuda attracted to the structure.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Florida Keys, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 5.3 meters

## Marine Life

ESA-listed star corals and brain corals are the protected centerpiece of the SPA, with coral mounds rising up to 15 feet from the bottom creating a maze-like landscape. The 1949 brick barge wreck extends 100 feet within the SPA; large soft corals colonize the wreck, and resident barracuda and large tarpon consistently shelter around the structure. Islamorada Dive Center reports yellowtail snapper, French grunt, sea turtles, spotted eagle rays, tarpon, green moray eels, nurse sharks, goliath grouper, barracuda, queen angelfish, blue tang, parrotfish, and spiny lobster at this site. Eagle rays visit but are noted as exceptional rather than guaranteed sightings.

## Dive Profile

The patch reef sits in 10–22 feet of water in the middle of Hawk Channel about 3 miles offshore of Plantation Key. The brick barge extends along a 100-foot axis with its bow rising to within 10 feet of the surface; only the forward half of the vessel remains on the seafloor (research suggests the stern half was raised and towed away after the grounding). Most dives are slow circuits around the central "hen" patch and its surrounding smaller patches, working between the coral mounds and out to the barge ribs.

## Entry and Exit

Hen and Chickens is reached by boat from Islamorada and Plantation Key dive operators. Mooring buoys are installed within the SPA so vessels never anchor in coral; entry and exit are from the operator's tie-off to a buoy.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Designated a Sanctuary Preservation Area (SPA): no spearfishing, no lobstering, no collecting (including coral and shells), and no fish-feeding.
- The shallow depth makes this a strong choice for new divers, refresher dives, and long second-tank dives, and the site doubles as a top snorkeling stop.
- The brick barge wreck is the photogenic centerpiece — plan time around the bow where the tarpon and barracuda tend to hold.

## Safety Considerations

- Surface intervals exposed to strong Florida sun — bring water, sun protection, and rash guards.
- Watch for fire coral on the patch reef heads when maneuvering between coral structures.
- The site is in Hawk Channel with active recreational boat traffic; deploy a dive flag and surface within sight of the dive vessel.
- Touching ESA-listed corals is a federal offense within the SPA — maintain neutral buoyancy and good fin awareness.
