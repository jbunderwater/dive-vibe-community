# La Tantine

A small deep wreck approximately 500 metres from Bagaud island — the Barge aux Congres (conger eel barge) — resting at 48 metres on the sandy seabed, with virtually no known history.

## Overview

La Tantine, also known as the *Barge aux Congres* (conger eel barge), is a wreck site approximately 500 metres from the island of Bagaud, within the broader Port-Cros area. The wreck is approximately 20 metres long, 6 metres wide, and 3 metres high, lying flat on a sandy bottom at 48 metres.

**Almost nothing is known about this vessel's history**: when it sank, why it sank, and what it was used for are all undocumented. Some divers speculate it was a tugboat equipped with a crane at the stern, and that it sank in heavy weather — but this is unverified. Any description of a specific sinking date, cause, or original function would be fabricated. The wreck is named informally for the dense conger eel population it shelters.

The site is described by dive operators as a difficult dive, both because of its depth and because strong current frequently runs at the site. Its small size also makes it harder to locate on the seabed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Approximately 500 m from Bagaud island, Port-Cros maritime zone, Var, France
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive (small barge)
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 48 meters
- **Wreck dimensions**: approximately 20 m long × 6 m wide × 3 m high
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 13–25°C
- **Current**: Often strong; site is current-sensitive
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The confirmed marine life at La Tantine, per local dive operator records, includes conger eels (*congres*), rockfish (*mostelles*), red mullet (*rougets*), white sea bream (*sars*), and spiny lobsters (*langoustes*). The conger eel population is abundant enough that the wreck earned its nickname "Barge aux Congres."

No other marine life lists are attributed to site-specific sources. Any broader regional species list would not be specific to this wreck.

## Dive Profile

Plan a direct descent to the wreck at 48 metres. Bottom time is severely limited by no-decompression limits at this depth — expect 15–20 minutes maximum on air. Explore the wreck's exterior and accessible openings before beginning a staged ascent. Nitrox 32 or 36% significantly improves available time. Plan a safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. A descent line is essential. Do not free-ascend from this depth without a reference line. Coordinate ascent and safety stop timing with your dive buddy before entry. Deploy SMB before surfacing.

## Tips

- Nitrox qualification is strongly advisable — the difference in no-decompression time at 48 metres between air and EAN36 is substantial
- The wreck's small size means orientation is quickly established, but current can make the descent and ascent more demanding than the wreck itself
- Confirm current conditions with the operator before the dive

## Safety

Advanced dive with depth-related risks. Monitor gas carefully — begin ascent with substantial reserve. Current can be significant; ensure the descent line is secured. A torch is useful for viewing the interior spaces and the conger eels within.

## Photography

The conger eels at La Tantine are the signature subject — patient wide-angle work with a torch to illuminate the dark interior spaces shows the animals in their sheltering behaviour. The encrusted metalwork provides macro subjects. Powerful artificial lighting is essential at 48 metres.
