## Gabiniėre Est

Gabiniėre Est is the eastern drop-off dive site of the La Gabinière islet at Port-Cros National Park — one of three dives around the islet, sheltered from easterly winds, with the densest grouper encounters in the western Mediterranean.

## Overview

Gabiniėre Est (the eastern drop-off of La Gabinière, also locally called "MerouLand" after its grouper density) is one of the three main sections of diving around the La Gabinière islet in Port-Cros National Park. The eastern face descends from shallow rocky terrain to approximately 40 metres, and the islet's orientation provides relative shelter from easterly winds, making this the most reliably diveable aspect in variable weather.

The eastern drop-off descends from shallow rocky terrain to approximately 40 metres, with the wall decorated by red gorgonian fans and sea urchins. The La Gabinière islet holds the highest concentration of brown groupers (*Epinephelus marginatus*) in the western Mediterranean — a 2008 park scientific census counted approximately 268 individuals centred on the islet, and current sources cite more than 300. Groupers are encountered throughout the eastern drop-off, approaching divers at close range due to decades of fishing prohibition. From summer 2001, a large school of Mediterranean barracuda — approximately 200 individuals — has been regularly observed on the eastern and southern aspects at 10–20 metres. The local OSM description notes anglerfish (monkfish/*baudroies*) are found at depth.

The eastern aspect receives moderate current that drives mid-water life: shoals of barracuda, yellowtail amberjack, dentex, and seabream are frequently seen. Compared to the southern aspects, the eastern drop-off has more moderate conditions and is somewhat more accessible.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat only; mooring buoys around the islet
- **Depth range**: 10–40 m
- **Conditions**: Sheltered from easterly winds; moderate current possible
- **Visibility**: Typically 15–25 m in summer
- **Best season**: May–October

## Marine Life

- **Groupers**: Brown groupers present throughout; approach closely due to long-term park protection; the islet population exceeds 300 individuals per current park monitoring
- **Barracuda**: School of approximately 200 Mediterranean barracuda present since 2001, cruising at 10–20 m
- **Pelagics**: Yellowtail amberjack, dentex in mid-water
- **Reef fish**: Seabream, bogue, wrasse
- **Invertebrates**: Moray eels, conger eels, sea urchins
- **Flora**: Red gorgonian fans

## Site Information

- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Protected Status**: Port-Cros National Park
