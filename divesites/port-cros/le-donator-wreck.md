# Le Donator Wreck

One of the most celebrated shipwrecks on the French Mediterranean coast, a fully intact 78-metre cargo ship lying upright in the Hyères channel with an extraordinary density of gorgonians and fish life.

## Overview

The *Donator* — officially named *Prosper Schiaffino* — is one of the most famous wreck dives in the western Mediterranean. Built in Bergen, Norway in 1931 (78 metres long, 12 metres wide), the vessel was originally named *Donator* and served as a cargo ship. In 1939 she was purchased by the Compagnie Schiaffino, converted into a wine tanker (*pinardier*), and renamed *Prosper Schiaffino*. During the Second World War she transported troops and supplies between Algeria and liberated French ports. Sources disagree on the exact sinking date: the Neptune Project (a dedicated marine heritage resource) gives 10 November 1945, while other regional sources state October 1945. What is agreed is that she struck a drifting mine while returning from Algeria near the islets of Grand and Petit Sarranier south of Porquerolles, sinking within four minutes. She settled upright on a sandy bottom between Porquerolles and Port-Cros. The wreck lies within the Hyères Bay Natura 2000 zone and is managed cooperatively with Port-Cros National Park. She is fully intact and penetrable, with depths from 35 metres on the deck to 52 metres at the propeller.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Between Porquerolles and Port-Cros, Hyères Bay, Var, France
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 52 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 13–25°C
- **Current**: Strong current possible; check conditions
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The wreck is densely colonised by enormous gorgonian red sea fans (*Paramuricea clavata*) and yellow gorgonians covering the superstructures — some of the finest gorgonian growth recorded on any wreck in France. Schools of swallowtail sea perch swarm around the mast and bridge. Dentex, groupers, two-banded sea bream, and striped red mullet are abundant throughout. The interior passageways shelter large conger eels and moray eels. A memorial mooring structure near the wreck is worth noting — the park maintains the area as a protected historic site.

## Dive Profile

Descend directly to the deck at 35 metres and begin exploration of the wreck's major features: the superb steering wheel in the aft section, the wine vat cargo holds, the engine room access, the galley, and the crew quarters (complete with bathtub). The propeller and rudder are at 52 metres — plan your deepest point first. The mast rising from the deck at 35 metres provides an orientation reference. Nitrox is strongly recommended. Begin ascent with substantial reserve; the gorgonian-covered mast and upper superstructure provide excellent subjects for the shallower portion of the dive.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from dive operators based in La Londe, Le Lavandou, and Hyères. A descent line to the wreck is essential given the depth and possible current. Giant stride entry; descend directly to the wreck. Ascend via the line and complete safety stop at 5 metres before surfacing. Deploy an SMB from the safety stop depth.

## Tips

- The steering wheel is one of the most memorable features — large, intact, and deeply atmospheric at depth
- Penetration of the interior (galley, crew quarters) is straightforward for experienced wreck divers with good buoyancy
- Ask the dive guide about current conditions before descending — the channel position makes this site sensitive to tidal flow
- The gorgonians on the mast section at 35 metres are world-class — allocate ascent time here deliberately

## Safety

Advanced diving with depth-related risks. Minimum recommended experience: advanced open water with deep specialty certification. Nitrox 32–36% strongly recommended for safe bottom time management at 35–52 metres. Never penetrate the wreck without proper wreck training and a torch. Plan conservative no-decompression limits. Strong current can develop; abort the dive if conditions deteriorate.

## Photography

Le Donator is one of the most photographic wrecks in France. The gorgonian-covered mast section at 35 metres, shot wide-angle with a diver for scale, is an iconic image. The steering wheel at depth is a powerful close-focus wide-angle subject. Macro work on gorgonian polyps and the encrusted metal surfaces shows remarkable detail. At 35–52 metres, artificial lighting is essential — bring a powerful strobe and a torch.
