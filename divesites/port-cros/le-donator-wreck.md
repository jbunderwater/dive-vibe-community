# Le Donator Wreck

One of the most celebrated shipwrecks on the French Mediterranean coast — a fully intact 78-metre cargo ship lying upright on the sandy seabed with an extraordinary density of gorgonians and fish life.

## Overview

The *Donator* — officially named *Prosper Schiaffino* — is one of the most famous wreck dives in the western Mediterranean. Built in 1931 at the Holz Worksted AS shipyard in Bergen, Norway (78 metres long, 12 metres wide, 1,200 hp engine), the vessel passed through several owners. In 1933 it was sold to a French company and renamed *Petite-Terre* for banana transport; in 1939 the Compagnie Schiaffino of Algeria purchased it, transformed it into a wine tanker (*pinardier*), and renamed it *Prosper Schiaffino*. Tonnage was 1,634 GRT (some sources cite 1,698 tons).

On 10 November 1945, the *Prosper Schiaffino* was returning from Algeria with a cargo of wine. Seeking shelter from heavy Mistral conditions, she skirted the south coast of Porquerolles. At approximately 1:10–1:15 pm, she struck a surface mine south of the island. The bow was destroyed, the ship flooded rapidly, and she sank in four minutes. Of the 29 sailors aboard, 27 were rescued; 2 were lost.

Note on sinking date: one official tourism source (Provence Méditerranée) gives "October 1945," but the more detailed and cross-confirmed accounts — Neptune Project, Bormes Plongée, and Wikidata — consistently give 10 November 1945. The November date is used here.

The wreck lies upright on a sandy bottom at 35–52 metres depth. The mast, which once rose 25 metres from the deck, fell to the port side in January 2000 and now lies along the hull rather than standing vertical. The propeller and rudder are at the stern at approximately 51–52 metres; the deck begins at 35 metres. She is fully intact and remains one of the finest recreational wreck dives in France.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Between Porquerolles and Port-Cros, Hyères Bay, Var, France
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 52 meters
- **Deck depth**: 35 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 13–25°C
- **Current**: Strong current possible; check conditions before each dive
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The wreck is densely colonised by red gorgonian sea fans (*Paramuricea clavata*) and yellow gorgonians covering the superstructures — among the finest gorgonian growth recorded on any wreck in France. Schools of swallowtail sea perch (*castagnoles*) swarm around the bridge structures. Dentex, groupers, two-banded sea bream, and striped red mullet are reported throughout. Conger eels occupy interior passageways.

## Dive Profile

Descend directly to the deck at 35 metres. The mast now lies to port along the hull rather than rising vertically (fell January 2000) — use the superstructure for orientation instead. Explore the major features: the steering wheel at the stern castle, the wine vat cargo holds, the engine room access, the galley, and the crew quarters. The propeller and rudder are at the stern at 51–52 metres — plan your deepest point first. Nitrox is strongly recommended. Begin ascent with substantial reserve; the gorgonian-covered superstructure provides excellent subjects for the shallower portion of the dive.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from dive operators based in La Londe, Le Lavandou, and Hyères. A descent line to the wreck is essential given the depth and possible current. Giant stride entry; descend directly to the wreck. Ascend via the line and complete safety stop at 5 metres before surfacing. Deploy an SMB from the safety stop depth.

## Tips

- The steering wheel at the stern castle is one of the most memorable features — large, intact, and atmospheric at depth
- Penetration of the interior (galley, crew quarters) is possible for experienced wreck divers with appropriate training and a torch
- The mast no longer stands vertically — it lies to port along the hull since January 2000. Use the hull and superstructure for orientation
- Ask the dive guide about current conditions before descending — the channel position makes this site sensitive to tidal flow

## Safety

Advanced diving with depth-related risks. Minimum recommended experience: advanced open water with deep specialty. Nitrox 32–36% strongly recommended for safe bottom-time management at 35–52 metres. Never penetrate the wreck without proper wreck training and a torch. Plan conservative no-decompression limits. Strong current can develop; abort if conditions deteriorate.

## Photography

The gorgonian-covered superstructure, steering wheel, propeller, and the downed mast along the hull are all outstanding subjects. Macro work on gorgonian polyps and encrusted metal shows remarkable detail. At 35–52 metres, artificial lighting is essential.
