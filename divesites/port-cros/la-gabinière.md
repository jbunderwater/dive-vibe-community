## La Gabinière

La Gabinière is the wall and drop-off dive site at the small islet south of Port-Cros island — the most strictly protected marine zone in France and the highest-density grouper habitat in the western Mediterranean.

## Overview

La Gabinière is a small rocky islet located south of the island of Port-Cros, at the heart of Port-Cros National Park — Europe's oldest and most strictly protected marine and terrestrial national park, established in 1963. The islet is an integral reserve, the most protected category within the park, where human landing is prohibited.

The site is structured around three distinct dives: the eastern drop-off (Tombant Est), the western slope (Tombant Ouest), and the dark canyon (La Calanque Sombre). Depths extend from 0 to 40 metres, with the richest drop-off topography on the eastern and western faces. The structures alternate between large rocky screes and near-vertical walls draped with red gorgonian fans, sponges, and yellow anemones. Currents on the southern aspect are pronounced and deliver nutrients that sustain the exceptional biomass.

La Gabinière holds the highest concentration of brown groupers (*Epinephelus marginatus*) in the western Mediterranean. A 2008 scientific census by the national park counted 565 groupers total across the park, with approximately 47% centred on the Gabinière islet. Current sources cite the islet population as exceeding 300 individuals. The groupers are massive and habitually approach divers at close range, a behaviour conditioned by decades of fishing prohibition. From summer 2001, a large school of Mediterranean barracuda — around 200 individuals — has been regularly observed circling between 10 and 20 metres on the eastern and southern aspects. Moray eels, conger eels, and crayfish occupy the deep crevices.

Access is by boat only, with dive centres in Hyères, La Londe-les-Maures, and Le Lavandou offering guided trips (45–50 minutes navigation). Four mooring buoys are available on site. Strong currents on the western slope and southern aspects make the deeper sections technically demanding; the eastern drop-off is more sheltered. The site requires authorisation through the CaPeL online dive registry (maximum 40 simultaneous divers).

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat only; 4 mooring buoys (anchoring prohibited)
- **Depth range**: 0–40 m (eastern drop-off to 45 m possible on western/southern aspects)
- **Tombant Est** (eastern drop-off): 10–40 m, moderate current, most sheltered aspect
- **Tombant Ouest** (western drop-off): 10–35 m, stronger current
- **La Calanque Sombre** (dark canyon): shallow to ~20 m, used for ascent and safety stops
- **Visibility**: Typically 15–25 m in summer
- **Best season**: May–October

## Marine Life

- **Groupers**: Brown grouper population at the islet exceeds 300 individuals (park census 2008: ~268 at this islet; current reports above 300); most are large and approach closely
- **Barracuda**: A school of approximately 200 Mediterranean barracuda has been present on the eastern and southern aspects since 2001, cruising at 10–20 m
- **Pelagics**: Greater amberjack (yellowtail), dentex
- **Reef fish**: Seabream, bogue, wrasse, castagnoles
- **Invertebrates**: Moray eels, conger eels, crayfish (spiny lobster), octopus
- **Flora**: Red gorgonian fans, yellow cluster anemones, sea sponges

## Photography

La Gabinière is one of the premier underwater photography destinations on the French Riviera. The habituated groupers allow extended close-focus portrait opportunities. Wide-angle shots of the gorgonian-draped walls are highly rewarding.

## Safety

Strong currents at the western slope and southern aspects require Advanced certification and current awareness. Mooring to buoys is mandatory; anchoring is prohibited. Always follow the dive centre briefing for current conditions and buoy assignments.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Port-Cros National Park, Var, France
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters (40 m on eastern drop-off)
- **Protected Status**: Port-Cros National Park integral reserve
