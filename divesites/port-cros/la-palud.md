## La Palud

La Palud is the most celebrated shore dive and snorkelling site in Port-Cros National Park — a marked 1 km underwater trail through posidonia meadows, rocky screes, and sandy banks at depths up to 12 metres.

## Overview

La Palud is the signature dive and snorkel site of Port-Cros National Park — Europe's oldest marine protected area, established in 1963. The bay features a marked underwater trail designated by six yellow buoys, each indicating a distinct habitat zone: sandy seabed, posidonia seagrass bed, rocky rift, wave-beaten rocks. The trail takes approximately 40 minutes to complete and runs 1 km. Depth ranges from 1 m to 10 m near the rocky areas, with posidonia meadow beginning from approximately 2 metres.

Decades of strict protection have produced a pronounced reserve effect. At La Palud, brown meagre (*Sciaena umbra*) — almost impossible to encounter outside this national park — are regularly sighted. Schools of salema porgy (*Sarpa salpa*), gilt-head bream (*Sparus aurata*), sargo (*Diplodus sargus*), and common two-banded seabream (*Diplodus vulgaris*) are abundant. Groupers and moray eels inhabit deeper rocky screes. Seagrass sections host broadnosed pipefish (*Syngnathus typhle*), Mediterranean rainbow wrasse, East Atlantic peacock wrasse, and red-black triplefin. Red starfish (*Echinaster sepositus*) are common throughout. Sea anemones and young mullet are found close to shore.

Access is by a 30-minute walk from the Port-Cros village harbour along the Estissac trail. The bay is managed as an integral part of the national park — no anchoring, no collecting, no touching marine life.

## Site Information

- **Location**: La Palud bay, Île de Port-Cros, Port-Cros National Park, France
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Trail length**: approximately 1 km (6 marked buoy stations)
- **Protected Status**: Port-Cros National Park

## Marine Life (confirmed at this site)

- Brown meagre (*Sciaena umbra*) — emblematic, regularly sighted
- Salema porgy (*Sarpa salpa*), gilt-head bream (*Sparus aurata*), sargo (*Diplodus sargus*), two-banded seabream (*Diplodus vulgaris*)
- Mediterranean rainbow wrasse (*Coris julis*), East Atlantic peacock wrasse (*Symphodus tinca*)
- Broadnosed pipefish (*Syngnathus typhle*)
- Red-black triplefin (*Tripterygion tripteronotus*)
- Thicklip grey mullet (*Chelon labrosus*)
- Groupers and moray eels in deeper rocky screes
- Red starfish (*Echinaster sepositus*)
- Snakelocks anemone (*Anemonia sulcata*)
- Posidonia seagrass (*Posidonia oceanica*)
