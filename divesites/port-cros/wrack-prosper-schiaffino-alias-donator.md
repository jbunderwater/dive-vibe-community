# Wrack 'Prosper Schiaffino' (Alias Donator)

An alternative access point to the legendary Donator wreck — France's most celebrated intact shipwreck lying upright at 35–52 metres between Porquerolles and Port-Cros.

## Overview

This entry references an alternate buoy access point to the *Prosper Schiaffino* — the official name of the wreck universally known among divers as "Le Donator." The ship was built in 1931 at the Holz Worksted AS shipyard in Bergen, Norway (78 metres long, 12 metres wide, 1,634 GRT/approximately 1,698 tons). After being renamed *Petite-Terre* in 1933 for banana transport, the Compagnie Schiaffino of Algeria acquired it in 1939 and renamed it *Prosper Schiaffino* for wine-tanker service.

On 10 November 1945, returning from Algeria with a cargo of wine and seeking shelter from Mistral conditions south of Porquerolles, she struck a surface mine at approximately 1:10–1:15 pm. The bow was destroyed; the ship sank in four minutes. Of 29 sailors, 27 were rescued; 2 were lost.

The wreck lies upright and fully intact on a sandy bottom. The deck begins at 35 metres; the propeller and rudder at the stern reach 51–52 metres. The mast, which once rose 25 metres from the deck, fell to the port side in January 2000. The wreck lies within the Hyères Bay Natura 2000 zone.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Between Porquerolles and Port-Cros, Hyères Bay, Var, France (alternate buoy position)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 52 meters
- **Deck depth**: 35 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 13–25°C
- **Current**: Strong current possible; approach operators for conditions report
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The wreck is densely colonised by red gorgonian sea fans and yellow gorgonians on the superstructures. Schools of swallowtail sea perch swarm around the bridge and crane structures. Dentex, groupers, two-banded sea bream, and striped red mullet are reported throughout. Conger eels occupy interior passageways. The wine vat cargo holds are recognisable and shelter fish and crustaceans.

## Dive Profile

From this alternate buoy position, descend to the deck at 35 metres. Note that the mast fell to the port side in January 2000 and no longer provides a vertical orientation reference — use the hull superstructure instead. Major landmarks: the large steering wheel at the stern castle, the spare propeller aft, the crane, and the accessible holds. Plan the deepest objectives first (propeller at 51–52 metres). Nitrox is strongly recommended. Ascend via the descent line.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from La Londe, Le Lavandou, or Hyères-based operators. Descent line essential. Giant stride entry. Ascend on the line; deploy SMB from the 5-metre safety stop before surfacing.

## Tips

- Confirm which buoy position the boat is using before the dive so your wreck orientation is correct
- The steering wheel at the stern castle is one of the iconic images from this wreck
- The mast is no longer upright — it fell to port in January 2000
- Interior penetration (galley, crew quarters, engine room) requires wreck training and a torch

## Safety

Minimum advanced open water certification required; deep specialty experience strongly preferred. Nitrox 32–36% recommended. Strong current is possible; abort or relocate if the descent line shows significant drag. Wreck penetration requires wreck training and illumination.

## Photography

See Le Donator Wreck for full photography notes. The gorgonian-covered superstructure, steering wheel, propeller, and dense fish schools are outstanding subjects. Bring a powerful strobe and torch — at 35–52 metres, artificial lighting is essential.
