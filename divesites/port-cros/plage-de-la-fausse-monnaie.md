## Plage de la Fausse Monnaie

Plage de la Fausse Monnaie ("Fake Money Cove") is a quiet shore dive site on Port-Cros's west coast — less crowded than La Palud and offering a rocky drop-off over posidonia at 4–8 metres with confirmed brown meagre sightings.

## Overview

Plage de la Fausse Monnaie (Anse de la Fausse Monnaie) sits on the western coast of Port-Cros island, approximately 30 minutes' walk south of the village. It is significantly less crowded than the famous La Palud beach while offering comparable marine richness within the national park's protected zone.

The most productive diving is found close to the right-hand shore when facing the sea, beyond the buoy line. A rocky drop-off overlooks a sandy and posidonia bed at 4–8 metres. Brown meagre (*Sciaena umbra*) — an emblematic Mediterranean species very rarely encountered outside national park waters — are regularly spotted here. Sargo (*Diplodus sargus*) and wrasses (*Coris julis*, *Symphodus tinca*) are common on the rocky drop-off. Salema porgy (*Sarpa salpa*) move through the seagrass patches. Sea anemones (*Anemonia sulcata*), young mullet (*Chelon labrosus*), and red-black triplefin (*Tripterygion tripteronotus*) are found in the final metres before shore. The site is calm and rarely crowded.

There is no lifeguard on duty. The site requires a 30-minute walk from Port-Cros village; no boat access is needed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast of Île de Port-Cros, Port-Cros National Park, France
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters
- **Protected Status**: Port-Cros National Park

## Marine Life (confirmed at this site)

- Brown meagre (*Sciaena umbra*) — confirmed, regularly sighted
- Sargo (*Diplodus sargus*), salema porgy (*Sarpa salpa*)
- Mediterranean rainbow wrasse (*Coris julis*), East Atlantic peacock wrasse (*Symphodus tinca*)
- Red-black triplefin (*Tripterygion tripteronotus*)
- Thicklip grey mullet (*Chelon labrosus*)
- Snakelocks anemone (*Anemonia sulcata*)
- Posidonia seagrass (*Posidonia oceanica*)
