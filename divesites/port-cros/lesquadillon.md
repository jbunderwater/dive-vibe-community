# l'Esquadillon

A rocky reef in the outer waters near Port-Cros, offering intermediate Mediterranean diving with groupers, Posidonia meadows, and good visibility.

## Overview

L'Esquadillon is a submerged rocky structure in the outer waters northeast of Port-Cros, in the broader Hyères Bay marine area. The site sits in well-oxygenated open Mediterranean water that produces clear conditions and a productive community of species typical of the western Mediterranean reef ecosystem. The combination of rocky structure descending to 25 metres and adjacent Posidonia seagrass meadows creates the habitat diversity characteristic of the best sites in this region. Brown groupers are present in the rock structure, and the Posidonia beds harbour smaller, more cryptic species.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Outer Hyères Bay area, northeast of Port-Cros, Var, France
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–30 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 13–25°C
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Brown groupers move between the reef structure and the Posidonia meadow edges. Moray eels, octopus, and spiny lobsters inhabit the rock crevices. Sea bream (two-banded, Saddled, and Sargus varieties), salema, oblades, and sea bass are active in the water column above the reef. The Posidonia meadow shelters cuttlefish, seahorses, and pipefish. Scorpionfish blend into the rocky substrate. Nudibranchs (particularly aeolids and dorids) are found on encrusted rock surfaces. Red and yellow gorgonian fans appear on the deeper rock faces below 18 metres.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the upper reef structure and work progressively deeper to 25 metres. The rocky features at the deeper end carry the most developed gorgonian growth. A transit across the Posidonia meadow, either at the start or end of the dive, adds species diversity. The moderate maximum depth allows good bottom time within standard recreational limits. Complete the dive with a safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from operators in La Londe, Le Lavandou, or Hyères. Back roll or giant stride entry. The open-water location means surface conditions should be checked before diving. Deploy SMB before ascending; surface well clear of the descent line.

## Tips

- L'Esquadillon's outer location means excellent visibility when weather permits — check forecasts for easterly winds that can stir sediment
- The combination of reef and Posidonia in one dive makes this a good site for photographers wanting both habitat types
- Cuttlefish in the seagrass change colour rapidly when approached — maintain distance and patience for best behaviour
- Gorgonian fans on the deeper rock faces develop well in clear outer-bay water

## Safety

Standard Mediterranean intermediate dive conditions. Exposed outer bay location means surface conditions should be evaluated before entry. Dive with an operator familiar with local weather patterns. Deploy an SMB before surfacing. The moderate depth range keeps decompression risks low.

## Photography

The Posidonia meadow provides excellent macro subjects (seahorses, cuttlefish, pipefish), while the rocky reef offers grouper portraits and gorgonian wide-angle shots. The clear outer-bay water produces excellent natural-light photography in the 5–15 metre range. A strobe adds accuracy to colour in the deeper sections.
