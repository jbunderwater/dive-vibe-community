## Trinity Caves

Grand Cayman's dramatic West Bay canyon complex — a network of large coral canyons and swim-throughs (not true caves) famous for its appearance in the Tom Cruise film *The Firm* and its abundant Caribbean reef life.

## Overview

Trinity Caves is a popular West Bay dive site famous for a notable cameo in the 1993 film *The Firm*, starring Tom Cruise. Despite the name, Trinity Caves does not contain true caves — the site consists of large, open coral canyons and dramatic swim-throughs that are navigated with natural light throughout. The canyons are sizable enough that multiple divers can explore them comfortably in parallel, and the variety of canyon passages means repeated dives reveal different routes and perspectives. Trinity Caves is located on the West Side of Grand Cayman in no more than 60 feet (18 meters) of water, making it accessible to Intermediate divers and notably comfortable in the consistently warm, clear Cayman conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West Bay, Grand Cayman
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral canyons / swim-throughs (named "caves" colloquially)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters (80 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–40 meters (65–130 feet)
- **Current**: Light
- **Water Temperature**: 26–29°C (79–84°F)
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The coral canyon walls of Trinity Caves support diverse encrusting life — sponges, sea fans, and hard corals create a layered habitat on the vertical faces. Tarpon are frequently encountered inside the larger canyon passages, hovering in the shade. Large barracuda station themselves at canyon exits. French and gray angelfish are ubiquitous throughout. Nurse sharks occasionally rest in the wider canyon bases. The surrounding reef areas accessible outside the canyon system add typical Grand Cayman reef species including Caribbean reef sharks.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the reef plateau and descend into the canyon system, which naturally guides divers through swim-throughs and across different depth zones. The canyons vary from 10 meters to 24 meters in depth. Light penetration from above is consistent throughout — these are not overhead environments requiring cave training. Plan the dive to cover the major canyon routes and exit back to the reef plateau for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from West Bay operators — Trinity Caves is a frequently dived site in Grand Cayman's West Side rotation. Mooring buoys are typically present. Giant stride entry. West Side conditions are excellent year-round.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A guide who knows the canyon routes is highly recommended for the first dive — the maze-like nature can confuse navigation
- Watch for tarpon in the deeper canyon recesses — they are significantly calmer here than in open water
- The canyons are well-lit throughout — no torch needed for canyon navigation, though one helps with photography
- The film *The Firm* reference is a talking point for dive guides — it adds a pop culture dimension to the dive
- Combine with Eagle Ray Pass or Orange Canyon on the same day for a varied West Side experience

## Safety Considerations

Trinity Caves are technically coral canyon swim-throughs, not true caves — there are no overhead sections requiring cave diving certification. However, good buoyancy is needed to avoid contact with the canyon walls, which can be narrow in sections. Maintain continuous awareness of your exit direction to avoid disorientation in the maze. Standard recreational dive depth management at 24 meters.

## Photography

The coral canyon environment creates dramatic compositions with natural light streaming from above. Tarpon in the canyon shadows with a bright exit visible behind them are classic images. Wide-angle shots of divers in the canyon passages capture the scale of the formations. Sea fans on the canyon walls photograph beautifully with a single strobe at close range.
