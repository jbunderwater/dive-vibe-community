## Eagle Ray Pass

A spectacular coral chasm on Grand Cayman's North Wall where massive coral heads, gorgonian fans, and regular eagle ray aggregations create one of the island's most memorable wall dive experiences.

## Overview

Eagle Ray Pass is one of Grand Cayman's most celebrated North Wall dive sites, featuring a dramatic deep coral chasm that leads from the shallow reef plateau down to the wall edge. The passage is characterized by towering coral heads, multiple archways, and narrow crevices packed with magnificent sponge formations, gorgonian fans, and black coral. The site takes its name from the spotted eagle rays that are reliably encountered here, often gathering in small groups that cruise along the wall face in formation. This is among the most photogenic passes on the North Wall and is listed as a top site by local dive operators who bring experienced divers seeking both the dramatic landscape and reliable large animal encounters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Wall, West Bay area, Grand Cayman
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall / coral chasm
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 36 meters (118 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–40 meters (65–130 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate — can run along the wall
- **Water Temperature**: 26–29°C (79–84°F)
- **Best Time**: Year-round (December–April for calmest conditions)

## Marine Life

Eagle rays are the defining wildlife encounter at this site, with groups of two to six animals routinely seen gliding along the wall face between 15–30 meters. Caribbean reef sharks patrol the deeper sections of the wall beyond the pass. Large Nassau grouper occupy coral ledges within the chasm. Schools of horse-eye jacks move through the blue water above the wall edge. The crevice interiors of the pass are packed with black coral (now heavily protected), sponges in multiple colors, and gorgonian fans that host flamingo tongue cowries.

## Dive Profile

Enter the top of the chasm from the shallow reef plateau and work down through the archways and coral formations. Spend the mid-dive exploring the chasm faces between 15–25 meters before working out to the wall edge for the eagle ray encounter zone at 20–35 meters. Begin ascending along the wall face and finish on the upper reef for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive with mooring buoys at the site or operator-managed deployment. All North Wall operators service this site regularly. Giant stride entry. Current along the wall should be assessed at the start of the dive — plan your direction so the return is with or across the current, not against it.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Eagle rays appear unpredictably — spend extended time at the wall edge between 15–25 meters
- Approach eagle ray groups slowly and horizontally — hovering neutral in their path is more effective than pursuing
- The coral formations in the pass reward slow exploration — there is more to see than a quick swim-through
- Nitrox extends no-decompression time at the working depths on this site
- Combine with Trinity Caves or Doc Poulson for a complete North Wall day

## Safety Considerations

The 36-meter maximum depth requires careful no-decompression limit management. Current along the North Wall can be strong — dive against the current on the outward leg for an easier return. The dramatic environment can distract divers from depth monitoring. Use a dive computer and establish a firm turnaround depth before descending.

## Photography

Eagle Ray Pass is one of Grand Cayman's premier photography sites. Eagle ray groups in formation against the infinite blue wall backdrop are iconic Caribbean images. The coral chasm with its towering formations provides dramatic architectural wide-angle compositions. Black coral and gorgonian fans reward close-up macro work with a strobe. An underwater video light improves colors on the chasm walls during the descent.
