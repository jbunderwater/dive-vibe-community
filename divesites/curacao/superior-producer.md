## Superior Producer

The Superior Producer is Curaçao's most celebrated wreck dive — a 50-metre cargo freighter resting upright at 30 metres, blanketed in decades of coral and sponge growth.

## Overview

The MV Superior Producer was built in 1957 by Handel- & Scheepsbouw Maatschappij Kramer and Booy N.V. in the Netherlands, originally delivered as the MV Andromeda. The vessel was renamed MV Superior Producer in February 1962. She measured 50.22 metres (165 feet) in length, 7.79 metres in beam, and displaced approximately 400 tons.

On September 30, 1977, the Superior Producer entered Willemstad harbour to load cargo bound for Venezuela. The ship was reportedly loaded beyond capacity with goods including whiskey, perfume, clothing, and bags. Shortly after departing at around 13:00, the vessel began to list dangerously; improperly stowed cargo shifted and unclosed portholes allowed water ingress. By 16:30 — three hours after departure — she sank approximately 150 metres offshore outside the harbour entrance. The captain and crew abandoned ship safely and were rescued by the Harbour Police.

Today the wreck rests upright on a sandy bottom at approximately 30 metres, bow facing west. It is consistently listed among the Caribbean's top wreck dives.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Approximately 150 metres off the Willemstad waterfront, west of the Mega Pier
- **Entry Type**: Boat
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 metres
- **Current**: Usually minimal to moderate

## Marine Life

The wreck has accumulated over 45 years of coral and sponge growth, supporting an active reef community. Confirmed sightings at this site include large schools of tarpon that patrol the wreck, groupers, snappers, and barracuda around the superstructure. The coral-encrusted surfaces host parrotfish, angelfish, and various reef fish. Interior spaces shelter moray eels and crustaceans. Sea turtles are periodically observed at the site.

## Dive Profile

Descent is directly to the wreck at around 30 metres. The highest point — the bridge area — sits at approximately 18 metres. The wreck can be circumnavigated in a single dive. Open cargo holds and accessible doorways allow interior exploration for divers with appropriate wreck training and equipment. The surrounding sandy bottom offers additional time for observation.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by boat dive through local operators. Shore diving is theoretically possible but requires approximately 500 metres of surface swimming and is not recommended by operators. Dive operators time trips to avoid conflicts with cruise ship activity at the adjacent Mega Pier.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book with local operators who run regular trips to the wreck
- Bring a dive light for exploring interior spaces
- Check cruise ship schedules — the nearby pier generates boat traffic
- Wreck penetration requires proper certification, lights, and a safety line

## Safety Considerations

Wreck penetration carries inherent risk and requires appropriate training and equipment. The depth demands careful monitoring of no-decompression limits. Boat traffic near the Mega Pier is a hazard during surface intervals; use a surface marker buoy on ascent.
