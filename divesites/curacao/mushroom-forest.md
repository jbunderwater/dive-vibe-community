## Mushroom Forest

Mushroom Forest is Curaçao's most iconic reef dive — a boat-only site on the northwest coast where massive mountainous star corals, bio-eroded at their bases into narrow columns, create an underwater forest of mushroom-shaped pillars.

## Overview

Mushroom Forest lies off the northwest coast near Santa Cruz Bay and is accessible by boat only. The site's distinctive formations are mountainous star corals (*Orbicella faveolata*) that have grown upward over centuries while boring clams and sponges eroded the coral bases to narrow stems — leaving the healthy upper crowns supported on columns. The resulting mushroom shapes can reach several metres in height and create a maze-like seascape of crevices, tunnels, and overhangs.

The site consistently appears on Caribbean and global top-10 dive lists. Dive operator Go West Diving includes Mushroom Forest in their regular morning boat schedule multiple days per week.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest coast off Santa Cruz Bay; boat only
- **Entry Type**: Boat
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 metres (100 feet)
- **Best Diving Depth**: 10–18 metres (33–60 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–30 metres (80–100 feet)
- **Current**: Usually calm; occasionally moderate

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at this site include spotted moray eels, porcupine fish (burrfish), southern stingrays, sea turtles, nurse sharks, cleaner shrimp, nudibranchs, parrotfish, angelfish, and butterflyfish. Giant moray eels are noted by Go West Diving as resident species. Barracuda and lobster are also regularly observed. An inflated surface marker buoy is required due to boat traffic in the area.

## Dive Profile

The dive begins from a boat anchored over the coral formations. The mushroom structures are most concentrated at depths of 10–18 metres on a sandy plateau. The reef extends downward to 30 metres and beyond. The Sandy plateau between formations allows easy navigation between the coral pillars. The Blue Room — a partially submerged air-filled cave — is frequently combined as a second dive or snorkel from the same boat.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Entry via giant stride or back roll. The remote location and open-water position mean divers must be comfortable with boat diving procedures. A surface marker buoy is mandatory — this is an active boating area. Shore access is theoretically possible but extremely long and not recommended.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book with Go West Diving (Playa Kalki) or Ocean Encounters — both run regular scheduled trips
- Excellent buoyancy control is essential to avoid damaging the fragile coral stems
- Combine with the Blue Room snorkel for a full day on the northwest coast
- An underwater camera is almost obligatory — the formations are highly photogenic

## Safety Considerations

Boat diving protocols apply. Deploy a surface marker buoy before ascending. The site is within Curaçao National Marine Park — do not touch or collect anything.
