## Playa Jeremi

Playa Jeremi is a quiet northwest coast shore dive featuring fire cup corals along rocky cliffs, a wall that extends to 20 metres, and confirmed sightings of yellow-headed jawfish, squid, and rays — all accessible from uneven rock steps carved into the cliff.

## Overview

Playa Jeremi sits on Curaçao's northwest coast near Lagun village, enclosed by dramatic limestone cliffs. The beach is small, facilities are essentially nonexistent, and crowds are rare. Entry is via rough steps carved into the rock face.

The reef starts close to shore with a shallow area of fire cup corals along the cliff faces on both sides of the bay. The right side outside the cove is reported to offer the best marine life and coral formations. A reef wall extends deeper, with the best diving between 12 and 20 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest coast, near Lagun village
- **Entry Type**: Shore (uneven rock steps into water)
- **Site Type**: Coral reef with wall
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Usually minimal to none

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at this specific site: squid, yellow-headed jawfish, rays, cornetfish, fire cup corals along the cliff faces. Diver reports from ScubaBoard note the right-side wall outside the cove produces good fish life, and the wall provides good juvenile fish photography during safety stops.

## Dive Profile

Enter via the rock steps with care — the substrate is uneven and can be slippery. A shallow area with fire cup corals greets divers near the cliff faces on both sides of the bay. The reef wall is best explored by heading to the right (outside the cove) for the richest coral and fish life. The wall reaches 20+ metres. Return through the bay for the exit.

## Entry and Exit

Rock steps carved into the cliff provide entry access — reef shoes or sturdy booties are required. This is not a sandy beach entry. Wade carefully until you can float before putting on fins. Exit requires the same careful attention to the rock steps.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Explore the right side outside the cove for the best marine life
- Look for yellow-headed jawfish hovering above burrows in sandy patches
- Bring all supplies — there are no facilities at this beach
- Quiet weekday visits provide the most undisturbed experience

## Safety Considerations

The rocky entry is the main hazard — take time with the steps and have a buddy assist with equipment. The remote location means emergency services are not immediately available; dive conservatively. A surface marker buoy is recommended.
