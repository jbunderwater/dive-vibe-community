## Marie Pampoen

Marie Pampoen is a local shore dive on Curaçao's southeast coast known for its artificial Car Pile reef — cars and industrial equipment from the 1940s–1960s dumped 20–30 metres offshore — surrounded by typical Caribbean reef life and reliably visited by large tarpon schools.

## Overview

Marie Pampoen (Pierbaai) is a small local beach adjacent to the Sunscape Curaçao Resort area on the southeast coast. The dive entry is from a small harbour. Within 20–30 metres of the harbour wall lies the site's defining feature: an underwater field of dumped vehicles and scrap metal from the mid-20th century, which has developed into a functioning artificial reef.

The Dive Bus operates from this location and offers equipment rental, guided dives, and night dives. Non-resort guests can also access the same reef from Pierbaai (the local public beach), which connects to Oswaldo's Drop Off at the other end of the reef system.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southeast coast near Sunscape Curaçao Resort (Pierbaai/Willemstad area)
- **Entry Type**: Shore (from small harbour)
- **Site Type**: Reef (with artificial reef Car Pile element)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 metres (Car Pile area)
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Usually limited

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at this site: large schools of tarpon (a defining feature, particularly near the Car Pile), moray eels, lionfish, parrotfish, angelfish, scorpionfish, octopus, pufferfish, snappers, grunts, and various reef fish. The tarpon schools are a reliable encounter — one of the better tarpon sites on the island for shore diving.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the small harbour. Swim 20–30 metres and descend to the Car Pile — vehicles, cranes, and scrap metal from approximately the 1940s–1960s resting at 20–30 metres depth, now heavily encrusted with corals and sponges. Explore the artificial reef structure before returning along the natural coral reef towards shore. Night dives at this site are a specialty offering of The Dive Bus.

## Entry and Exit

Harbour entry with minimal coral rubble. Reef shoes recommended. The protected harbour area means calm entry year-round. The Dive Bus is on-site with full equipment and support.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Consider a guided night dive — The Dive Bus specialises in this and the Car Pile looks different after dark
- Tarpon schools are most reliably seen around the artificial reef structures
- The adjacent Oswaldo's Drop Off (accessible as a longer dive or separate entry from Pierbaai) offers a contrasting wall dive experience

## Safety Considerations

The Car Pile has entanglement hazards — navigate carefully around metal protrusions. A surface marker buoy is recommended for ascent. Be aware of boat traffic in the harbour area.
