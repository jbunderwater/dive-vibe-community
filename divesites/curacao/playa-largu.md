## Playa Largu

Playa Largu ("Long Beach" in Papiamentu) is a secluded shore dive within San Juan Plantation featuring a sponge-rich reef with a 45-degree drop-off beginning at 7 metres — one of Curaçao's best sites for sponge diversity including Elephant Ear, barrel, stovepipe, vase, rope, and encrusting varieties.

## Overview

Playa Largu is located inside San Juan Plantation on Curaçao's west coast. Access requires paying a fee at the plantation gate (approximately 10 NAf per car). There are no facilities at the beach and phone reception is poor. The beach is named for its length — it is one of the longer plantation beaches on the island.

The reef is notable for exceptionally high sponge diversity. Divecuracao.info specifically highlights it as a site for "sponge enthusiasts" and lists more sponge varieties here than at most Curaçao sites. Coral coverage on the reef face is described as above 50% density in the main reef zone, with good hard coral growth on the reef shoulder. Coral formations extend close to shore. The drop-off begins at 7 metres and the sandy bottom is reached at 35 metres.

Unlike some plantation beaches, the entry here is on coral rubble — booties are essential.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast, inside San Juan Plantation (entrance fee at gate; no facilities)
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Usually weak; occasional drift dive conditions

## Marine Life

The defining feature is sponge diversity: Elephant Ear sponges, Touch-me-not sponges, stovepipe sponges, barrel sponges, vase sponges, rope sponges, and encrusting sponges are all confirmed at this site. Soft coral formations cover the reef shoulder. Hard coral density is strong in the 10–20 metre zone.

No site-specific fish species documentation found beyond sponge and coral communities. Regional Caribbean reef fish and typical Curaçao species should be expected.

## Dive Profile

Coral rubble beach entry — booties required. Coral formations start close to shore. The reef drop-off begins at 7 metres at a 45-degree angle, with the best reef between 10–20 metres. Beyond 30 metres the reef transitions to sandy bottom. The site is quiet and suitable for extended single-tank exploration.

## Entry and Exit

Coral rubble entry — booties or reef shoes essential; sandals insufficient. Entry is described as fairly clean despite the rubble. After entering the water, the drop-off is close. A surface marker buoy is recommended if drift diving is considered due to potential boat traffic.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This is an exceptional macro/photography site for sponge diversity
- Combine with Playa Manzaliña (nearby within the same plantation) for a full day
- Bring all supplies — no food, water, or equipment available on-site
- The plantation road may be rough after rain — 4WD recommended in wet season

## Safety Considerations

Remote site with no immediate emergency access. Dive conservatively and inform someone of your plans. The plantation gate closes at specific times — confirm closing hours before visiting.
