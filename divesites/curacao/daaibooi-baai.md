## Daaibooi Baai

Daaibooi Bay (Sint Willibrordus) is a west coast shore dive at a calm, secluded bay where a surface swim across a sandy plateau leads to a healthy fringing reef — confirmed for pipefish, cuttlefish, turtles, Elephant Ear sponges, and brain corals — and a drop-off to 35 metres.

## Overview

Daaibooi is a bay near Sint Willibrordus on Curaçao's west coast, approximately 30 minutes' drive northwest of Willemstad. The site is enclosed by cliffs that border Porto Marie to the south and Coral Estates to the north.

The dive requires a surface swim of approximately 5–10 minutes across a sandy plateau before reaching the drop-off, which is positioned far from shore. This characteristic is noted by multiple dive guides as a trade-off: the swim is required but the coral biodiversity at the reef is considered good, including soft coral, Elephant Ear sponges, and brain corals.

A marine park entrance fee of approximately 10 NAf may be charged at the beach.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast, Sint Willibrordus, approximately 30 minutes from Willemstad
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25 metres
- **Current**: Usually minimal to none

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at this site: pipefish, cuttlefish, sea turtles, parrotfish, and stingrays — specifically called out by dive guides covering this site. The reef structure features soft coral, Elephant Ear sponges, and brain corals. Young fish are described as particularly abundant. Towards the end of the bay, the coral structures are described as especially rich in Caribbean reef fish.

## Dive Profile

Sandy beach entry in very shallow water. Surface swim of 5–10 minutes to the drop-off. The reef features a large sandy plateau leading to healthy coral biodiversity including soft coral, sponges, and brain corals, starting at around 8–10 metres. The drop-off continues to 35 metres. The bay setting creates a long, shallow approach but rewards divers with calm conditions throughout.

## Entry and Exit

Sandy beach entry — easy for all experience levels including photographers with camera equipment. Basic beach facilities (shade, parking) may be available. A marine park entrance fee may apply. A surface marker buoy is required due to recreational and commercial boat traffic.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The 5–10 minute surface swim is essential — inflate a surface marker buoy before descending
- Look specifically for pipefish and cuttlefish, which are notably present at this site
- Towards the southern end of the bay, coral structures are more diverse
- This site suits certification and training dives due to the calm, easy entry conditions

## Safety Considerations

The extended surface swim places divers at distance from shore. A surface marker buoy is mandatory. The site is within the Curaçao marine park system.
