## Boka Sami

Boka Sami is a central west coast shore dive at Playa Boca Sami — a wide, shallow bay requiring a long surface swim before a 45-degree sloping reef descends to 45 metres, with coral density reaching near 100% in some sections to the right of the entry point.

## Overview

Boka Sami sits at Playa Boca Sami on Curaçao's central west coast, adjacent to Snake Bay. The bay is large and shallow, requiring divers to swim some distance before reaching the drop-off. The reef does not begin directly at the shore; divecuracao.info notes this is a site where patience on the approach is rewarded by healthy coral on arrival.

Coral density is variable: directly in front of the bay it is lower, but after swimming right from the entry for approximately 15 minutes, coral coverage approaches 100% in places. The drop-off is approximately 45 degrees and descends to 45 metres and beyond. A large mooring buoy serves as a navigational reference point.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Playa Boca Sami, central west coast (adjacent to Snake Bay)
- **Entry Type**: Shore (beach entry with extended surface swim)
- **Site Type**: Coral reef (45-degree sloping drop-off)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Usually minimal

## Marine Life

No specific fish species are documented beyond "coral and sponges" in available sources — generic Caribbean reef fish are to be expected. Sea turtles and eagle rays are reported by multiple guides covering the Sint Michiel area broadly. The high coral density sections to the right of the main entry are likely the richest for marine life.

## Dive Profile

Beach entry from Playa Boca Sami. Surface swim across the large, shallow bay. Upon reaching the drop-off (look for the mooring buoy as a landmark), the reef slopes at 45 degrees to 45 metres. Head right from the main entry for the highest coral coverage sections. The reef meanders along the coastline rather than running straight.

## Entry and Exit

Beach entry with adequate parking nearby. A surface marker buoy is mandatory due to recreational and commercial boat traffic. The long surface swim is the defining logistical challenge of this site.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Swim right from the main entry for the best coral density
- Use the large mooring buoy as your navigational reference at the drop-off
- Combine with nearby Snake Bay for a second dive
- The site requires more surface swimming than most Curaçao shore dives — factor this into air planning

## Safety Considerations

The long distance from the entry to the reef means you are far from shore when diving. Carry a surface marker buoy for return. Check current conditions before committing to the swim — a surface marker buoy is required.
