## Catherine

The Catherine is a 37-metre (120-foot) shipwreck lying upside down and split in two at 6 metres depth on Black Sand Beach (Boca Santu Pretu) — a boat dive combined with the nearby Mushroom Forest as a standard two-tank northwest coast excursion.

## Overview

The Catherine wreck lies at Black Sand Beach, also known as Boca Santu Pretu (Playa Chikitu), inside the historic Santa Cruz Plantation on Curaçao's northwest coast. The beach name reflects the dark, volcanic-looking sand that distinguishes it from Curaçao's typical white beaches.

The wreck is approximately 37 metres (120 feet) in length, lies upside down in about 6 metres (20 feet) of water, and is split into two sections. No information has been found regarding the vessel's original purpose, how it sank, or when it went down. The hull is well-encrusted with brain corals, sponges, and other marine growth.

Go West Diving includes this site as "Black Sand Beach / Boca Santu Pretu" in their boat schedule, typically as a combination dive with a subsequent crossing of the sandy plateau to the northern end of Mushroom Forest.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Black Sand Beach (Boca Santu Pretu), Santa Cruz Plantation, northwest coast
- **Entry Type**: Boat
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 6 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable (shallow, can be affected by surge)
- **Current**: Usually minimal

## Marine Life

Confirmed at this site: colonies of garden eels surrounding the wreck; brain corals and sponges encrusting the hull. The wreck provides habitat for reef fish typical of northwest Curaçao.

## Dive Profile

Boat access. Descend to the wreck at 6 metres. The upside-down hull has small areas that can be explored. After exploring the wreck, divers typically cross the sandy plateau toward the northern end of Mushroom Forest — the site is commonly used as the first dive of a two-tank boat excursion with Mushroom Forest as the second dive. Garden eels are found on the sandy bottom surrounding the wreck.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Access via northwest coast operators. No shore entry is practical for this site.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Combine as a two-tank excursion with Mushroom Forest — this is how most operators run the site
- Look for garden eels on the sandy bottom around the wreck
- Small accessible areas allow a look inside the hull in appropriate conditions

## Safety Considerations

The wreck interior is limited and should be entered carefully. Surge can affect the shallow dive and reduce visibility. No penetration of confined spaces.

*No historical information found regarding vessel name origin, sinking date, or circumstances. The name "Catherine" is the OSM-assigned designation; the vessel's own history is undocumented in available sources.*
