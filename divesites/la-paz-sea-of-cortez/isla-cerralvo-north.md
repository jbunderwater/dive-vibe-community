## Isla Cerralvo North

Isla Cerralvo North is a reef dive site on the remote northern reaches of Isla Cerralvo, offering encounters with large groupers, sea fans, and pelagic species in a less-visited setting.

## Overview

Isla Cerralvo is a remote island in the southern Sea of Cortez, southeast of La Paz. Its northern diving area is less trafficked than the popular Espíritu Santo sites closer to the city, offering a quieter experience with productive reef communities shaped by the channel currents that sweep the island. Bajadiving.com describes La Punta Norte (the island's northern point) as a "maze-like reef teeming with life" with a maximum depth of 60 feet (18 m), featuring sea turtles, moray eels, barracuda, and sergeant major fish among coral heads. The northern tip is distinct from the La Reynita/La Reyna site cluster, which is a small western pinnacle with constant currents.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern end of Isla Cerralvo, Sea of Cortez
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Bajadiving.com specifically documents sea turtles, moray eels, barracuda, and sergeant majors at La Punta Norte. The broader northern Cerralvo area features gorgonians and brain corals as reef inhabitants, with conger/garden eels in adjacent sandy areas. Pelagic species including tuna, wahoo, and occasional hammerhead sharks pass through the Cerralvo channel. Giant Pacific manta rays are documented at La Reina (a separate site just north of the island); no site-specific source confirms manta ray encounters at the northern reef itself.

## Dive Profile

Reef structures from the surface to 18 meters, with sandy margins beyond. Moderate currents are typical given the channel position. Visibility is generally good. Conditions are less sheltered than the inner Espíritu Santo sites.

## Entry and Exit

Longer boat ride from La Paz than the Espíritu Santo sites — approximately 1.5–2 hours. Cabo Trek and La Paz operators with larger vessels run Isla Cerralvo diving days.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Best paired with La Reina for a two-tank Cerralvo day trip.
- July–August offers the best chance for giant Pacific manta ray encounters in this area.
- The remote location means fewer diver crowds — a benefit for wildlife encounters.

## Safety Considerations

The crossing from La Paz is longer and more exposed than Espíritu Santo routes. Check weather and sea state before departure. Currents can be moderate to strong; current experience is necessary. Bring sufficient hydration for the crossing.
