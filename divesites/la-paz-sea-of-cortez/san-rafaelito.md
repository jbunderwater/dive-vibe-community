## San Rafaelito

San Rafaelito is a rocky islet north of La Paz Bay offering beginner-friendly reef diving, a non-breeding sea lion colony, and active coral restoration.

## Overview

San Rafaelito is a small rocky islet located north of La Paz, close to Pichilingue beach, within the Balandra Protected Natural Area. The site features coral heads, small caves, and overhangs across a shallow reef in the protected inner bay waters. It is one of the most accessible dive sites from the city — and also one of the most ecologically active. San Rafaelito hosts an ongoing coral restoration program: more than 7,500 coral colonies have been transplanted at the site, with an additional 5,000 being hatched in a nursery for future reef regeneration (in collaboration with Baja Ferries and Efecto Arena). Sources disagree on the sea lion colony's character: the Sea of Cortez Guide (seaofcortez.guide) describes it as an "important breeding ground" with approximately 140 adults; Yatezzitos describes it as a "nursery" for 70–80 pups and juveniles; and other sources characterize it as a resting colony of males and juveniles. Swimming season is mid-October through May, with access restricted June through August. The site is a well-documented night dive location, where sleeping parrotfish and nocturnal invertebrates are easily observed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North of La Paz Bay, near Pichilingue, Sea of Cortez
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters

## Marine Life

The coral head structures support parrotfish, damselfish, various wrasse species, and other colorful reef fish. Sea lions from the resident colony approach divers in the water. Rays are documented in the sandy areas surrounding the reef. The deep side of the islet drops to over 60 feet (~18 m), offering a wider depth range than the shallow snorkel zone. Macro subjects are abundant on the coral heads and rocky surfaces. Sleeping parrotfish in their mucus cocoons are a highlight of night dives here.

## Dive Profile

Shallow reef diving over coral heads from the surface to 15–16 meters. Calm, protected conditions in the inner bay. Excellent beginner environment with minimal current and clear water. The site's accessibility from La Paz makes it popular for open-water certification dives and night dives.

## Entry and Exit

Short boat ride from La Paz (approximately 20–30 minutes from the marina area). Multiple La Paz operators use San Rafaelito as a training and day-trip site.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent night dive site — arrange with your operator for an evening trip to see sleeping parrotfish, octopus, and lobster.
- Approach the sea lion colony calmly. Access is restricted June–August; confirmed open season is mid-October through May.
- The coral restoration patches are a photography interest — healthy transplanted coral growth is visible.

## Safety Considerations

Depth and conditions are manageable for beginner divers. Being inside La Paz Bay means protection from ocean swell. Boat traffic in the Pichilingue area warrants use of a dive flag and awareness of surface conditions on ascent.
