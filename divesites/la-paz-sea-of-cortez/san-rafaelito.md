## San Rafaelito

San Rafaelito is a rocky islet north of La Paz Bay offering beginner-friendly reef diving, a non-breeding sea lion colony, and active coral restoration.

## Overview

San Rafaelito is a small rocky islet located north of La Paz, close to Pichilingue beach. The site features coral heads, small caves, and overhangs across a shallow reef in the protected inner bay waters. It is one of the most accessible dive sites from the city — and also one of the most ecologically active. San Rafaelito hosts an ongoing coral restoration program: more than 7,500 coral colonies have been transplanted at the site, with an additional 5,000 being hatched in a nursery for future reef regeneration. A small non-breeding colony of 40–80 California sea lions occupies the islet, offering interaction opportunities outside Los Islotes' seasonal closure periods. The site is a well-documented night dive location, where sleeping parrotfish and nocturnal invertebrates are easily observed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North of La Paz Bay, near Pichilingue, Sea of Cortez
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters

## Marine Life

The coral head structures support parrotfish, damselfish, various wrasse species, and other colorful reef fish. Sea lions from the resident non-breeding colony approach divers, particularly in water. Rays — including eagle rays — are documented in the sandy areas surrounding the reef. Macro subjects are abundant on the coral heads and rocky surfaces. Sleeping parrotfish in their mucus cocoons are a highlight of night dives here.

## Dive Profile

Shallow reef diving over coral heads from the surface to 15–16 meters. Calm, protected conditions in the inner bay. Excellent beginner environment with minimal current and clear water. The site's accessibility from La Paz makes it popular for open-water certification dives and night dives.

## Entry and Exit

Short boat ride from La Paz (approximately 20–30 minutes from the marina area). Multiple La Paz operators use San Rafaelito as a training and day-trip site.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent night dive site — arrange with your operator for an evening trip to see sleeping parrotfish, octopus, and lobster.
- Approach the sea lion colony calmly — this is a non-breeding group without the territorial aggression present at Los Islotes during pup season.
- The coral restoration patches are a photography interest — healthy transplanted coral growth is visible.

## Safety Considerations

Depth and conditions are manageable for beginner divers. Being inside La Paz Bay means protection from ocean swell. Boat traffic in the Pichilingue area warrants use of a dive flag and awareness of surface conditions on ascent.
