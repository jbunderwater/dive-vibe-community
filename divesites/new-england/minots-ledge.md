# Minots Ledge

A historic rocky ledge off Cohasset and Scituate, Massachusetts, home to the famous Minot's Ledge Lighthouse and documented as a productive cold-water dive with exceptional visibility.

## Overview

Minots Ledge is inseparable from one of the most dramatic stories in American maritime history. The ledge lies approximately 15 miles southeast of Boston, one mile offshore from Cohasset and Scituate. Between 1832 and 1841 alone, 40 wrecks occurred on this and neighboring reefs. The original Minot's Ledge Lighthouse — a spindly iron structure completed in 1850 — was destroyed in an April 1851 storm, killing the two assistant keepers who remained at their posts. The current granite tower, completed in 1860 after years of work in treacherous seas, is considered one of the most significant American engineering achievements of the 19th century.

As a dive site, Minots Ledge is documented but not commercially offered. A 2002 first-hand ScubaBoard report from a diver who accessed the site by anchoring a sailboat to the east and using a Zodiac describes exceptional visibility (starfish visible from the surface in 8 metres of water), mild current, depths ranging from 1.5 to 20 metres across the ledge, and a productive mix of large pollock schools, striped bass, winter flounder, and lobster. Wreck debris from the 1851 original iron lighthouse and the many historical shipwrecks here lies among the rocks.

The lighthouse tower has been privately owned since 2014 (purchased at auction). Access to the structure requires owner permission. The reef and surrounding water have no formal exclusion zone.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Cohasset/Scituate, South Shore Massachusetts — 15 miles southeast of Boston
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Reported excellent — one first-hand account states starfish visible from the surface at 8m depth
- **Current**: Mild and consistent (per 2002 ScubaBoard account); exposed outer position means conditions vary with weather
- **Water Temperature**: 7–20°C depending on season
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

The following species are documented in a 2002 first-hand ScubaBoard dive report at this specific site: large schools of pollock, striped bass, winter flounder (16 inches), starfish, and lobster.

Cold-water temperate species typical of the outer South Shore Massachusetts Bay position inhabit this site's rocky ledge habitat. No additional site-specific marine life surveys have been found in available sources.

## Dive Profile

Private boat required — anchor to the east of the ledge in approximately 14 metres of water. Use a Zodiac or dinghy to approach the lighthouse structure. The ledge depth range of 1.5–20 metres allows divers to work from shallower summit sections down to the base. Wreck debris from the 1851 iron lighthouse structure and historical shipwrecks is present among the rocks. Plan dives around slack tide. Deploy SMB before ascending in the exposed outer position.

## Entry and Exit

Private boat required — no commercial charter currently lists this site. Anchor in sand to the east of the ledge. The granite lighthouse tower (1860) is an unmissable surface navigation reference. The outer Cohasset/Scituate position is exposed to Massachusetts Bay conditions — only dive in settled weather.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Access requires your own private boat; no charter currently offers it commercially
- The lighthouse tower is privately owned (since 2014 by Bobby Sager) — accessing the structure requires owner permission
- Read about the 1851 lighthouse collapse and 1860 granite tower construction before diving — one of the most remarkable engineering stories in American history
- Iron beam debris from the original 1851 lighthouse structure lies on the seafloor; a 2007 Coast Guard archaeological dive located remains
- Plan conservatively around slack tide — the outer Massachusetts Bay position can have stronger conditions than inner South Shore ledge sites
- 7mm wetsuit or drysuit required

## Safety Considerations

The outer Cohasset/Scituate position is more exposed than inner South Shore ledge sites. Massachusetts Bay swells can create surge at shallower ledge sections. Open-water anchorage requires appropriate ground tackle. Deploy SMB before ascending. Cold water year-round.

## Photography

The 1860 granite lighthouse tower provides a remarkable surface backdrop. Historical wreck debris among the rocks — including iron beams from the 1851 lighthouse identified in 2007 — offers unique historical photography subjects. Pollock schools in clear Massachusetts Bay water above the ledge make dramatic wide-angle subjects.
