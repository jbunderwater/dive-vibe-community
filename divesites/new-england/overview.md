## New England

Historic wreck diving and rocky ledge explorations along the Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and Connecticut coastlines, where centuries of maritime history rest beneath cold Atlantic waters.

## Description

New England's dive sites span 48 documented locations with depths ranging from 2 to 55 meters, including 12 wreck sites and 36 reef/ledge sites. The waters off Cape Cod, Cape Ann, and the islands hold hundreds of shipwrecks dating from colonial schooners to WWII-era vessels, while boulder fields and rocky ledges teem with lobster, tautog, and seals. Stellwagen Bank National Marine Sanctuary protects critical habitat just north of Cape Cod, and Monomoy Island's seabed is scattered with wrecks of three-masted schooners and 500-foot freighters.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Shore Diving**: 2 accessible shore dive sites including the Ada K. Damon (intertidal wreck visible at low tide on Crane Beach, Ipswich) and the Frances (1872 wreck at Head of the Meadow Beach, North Truro)
- **Boat Diving**: 45 boat-accessible sites across Massachusetts Bay, Cape Cod, Rhode Island Sound, and Long Island Sound
- **Wreck Diving**: 12 documented wreck sites including the SS Pendleton (T2 tanker that broke apart during a 1952 nor'easter off Monomoy), SS James Longstreet (WWII Liberty ship used for aerial target practice off Brewster), and the Wanderer (sunk 1924 off Rhode Island)

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Boston Logan International Airport (BOS) is the primary gateway. T.F. Green Airport (PVD) in Providence serves Rhode Island sites. Cape Cod is approximately 90 minutes from Boston
- **Dive Operators**: Cape Ann Divers in Gloucester, Aqua Center in Sandwich, Aquarius Dive Center in Buzzards Bay, Sea Sports Divers in Hyannis, and Northern Atlantic Dive Expeditions operating across Massachusetts, Rhode Island, and New York. Note that no dedicated dive charter boats operate directly from Cape Cod — divers typically charter fishing boats or rent boats
- **Accommodation**: Extensive options from Boston hotels to Cape Cod vacation rentals, seasonal beach towns along the coast, and island lodging on Martha's Vineyard and Nantucket
- **Transportation**: A car is essential. Cape Cod is accessed via Route 6 and the Sagamore/Bourne bridges. Ferries serve the islands
- **Facilities**: Dive shops in Gloucester, Sandwich, Buzzards Bay, and Hyannis offer gear rental and fills. Don Ferris Dive Training on Cape Cod is a well-known local resource and author of regional diving guides

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperatures range from 4-21C (40-70F) seasonally. Summer surface temps can reach 21C (70F) but drop significantly at depth. Visibility varies from 3-15 meters (10-50 ft) depending on conditions and location, with offshore sites offering better clarity
- **Marine Biodiversity**: American lobster, tautog, striped bass, horseshoe crabs, squid, Atlantic torpedo rays, skates, harbor seals, grey seals, and blue sharks. Seasonal visitors include humpback whales, fin whales, and endangered Atlantic right whales in Stellwagen Bank
- **Conservation**: Stellwagen Bank National Marine Sanctuary protects 842 square miles of ocean off Massachusetts. Various state marine fisheries regulations govern lobster harvesting and protected species

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: June through September for warmest water and best conditions. July-August offers peak temperatures. Fall can bring excellent visibility as plankton blooms subside
- **Currency**: USD
- **Language**: English
- **Safety**: Drysuit recommended for most of the year; a 7mm wetsuit with hood is minimum for summer. Strong currents common around Cape Cod and the islands — plan dives around slack tide. Many wreck sites have restricted access or hazardous conditions. Cold-water rated regulators recommended
