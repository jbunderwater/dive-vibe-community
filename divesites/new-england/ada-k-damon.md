## Ada K. Damon

Historic intertidal wreck site at Crane Beach, Ipswich, Massachusetts.

## Overview

The Ada K. Damon is a 26-meter schooner that wrecked during a Christmas storm in 1909. This shallow intertidal site is primarily an archaeological and historical destination rather than a traditional diving wreck. The wreck remains half-buried in the shifting sands of Crane Beach at the base of Steep Hill and is accessible at low tide for inspection and photography. Located in Essex County, Massachusetts, this site offers insight into New England's maritime heritage.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Crane Beach, Ipswich, Massachusetts
- **Entry Type**: beach
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 2 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 1-3 meters (water variable, best at low tide)
- **Current**: Tidal surge
- **Best Time**: Low tide (planning essential)

## Marine Life

This shallow, cold-water environment supports typical New England temperate species including small fish, crustaceans, and mobile mollusks. The wreck structure provides shelter for winter flounder, juvenile fish, and lobsters.

## Dive Profile

This is not a traditional scuba dive site. Rather, visit at low tide to walk/wade to the exposed wreck structure (2 meters maximum depth). The wreck is identifiable by wooden planking and structural remains. Inspection is brief due to shallow depth and tidal constraints.

## Entry and Exit

Enter from Crane Beach and walk to the base of Steep Hill at low tide. The wreck area is approximately at the tide line. Exit before tide returns. Excellent for archaeological interest and historical photography.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Consult tide tables carefully—site is only accessible 1-2 hours on either side of low tide
- Wear suitable footwear for rocky/uneven beach terrain
- Bring camera for historical documentation
- This is an archaeological site—respect the heritage material, do not remove items
- Cold water waders recommended even for shallow approach
- Check tide times and weather conditions before visiting

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of cold water, surge, limited visibility in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Strong currents are possible — carry appropriate signaling devices and be prepared to abort the dive if conditions deteriorate. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

The wreck structure provides dramatic wide-angle subjects with natural light filtering through openings. A torch is essential for illuminating interior details and bringing out colors. Macro opportunities abound on the encrusted surfaces.
