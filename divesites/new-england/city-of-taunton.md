## City of Taunton

Historic Fall River Line freight steamship, shallow water wreck site in Mount Hope Bay.

## Overview

The City of Taunton is a 1890s-era Fall River Line freight steamship built in 1892. The vessel was abandoned and left to sink circa 1930, now resting on the western shore of Mount Hope Bay at Brayton Point in Somerset, Massachusetts. The wreck is easily visible at low tide, making it a unique accessible wreck site. Shallow water (4 meters or less) limits diving opportunity, but the site offers excellent archaeological and historical interest. The wreck remains identifiable with visible hull ribs and structural remains. This is an excellent beginner-friendly site for introducing wreck diving in New England's temperate, shallow-water environment.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Brayton Point, Somerset, Massachusetts (Mount Hope Bay)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 4 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 1-3 meters
- **Current**: Tidal, mild
- **Water Temperature**: 50-60°F (10-15°C)
- **Best Time**: Low tide (planning essential), summer months

## Marine Life

Shallow-water temperate species including small fish, juvenile striped bass, flounder, crabs, and other estuarine fauna. Tidal zone invertebrates common on wreck structure.

## Dive Profile

Low tide approach allows visual inspection of hull remains. Water depth rarely exceeds 4 meters directly on wreck. Wading possible with proper footwear. Brief underwater inspection (5-15 minutes) of accessible wreck structure. Not recommended for traditional scuba diving due to extreme shallowness and tidal constraints.

## Entry and Exit

Enter from Brayton Point shore at low tide. Wade to wreck structure. Exit before tide rises. Site is tidal-dependent and only accessible around low-tide windows (1-2 hours on either side). Consult tide tables before visiting.

## Tips and Recommendations

- MANDATORY: Consult tide tables—site only accessible at low tide
- Wear protective footwear for rocky/uneven bottom
- Water is very cold (waders/drysuit recommended)
- Excellent for historical documentation and photography
- This is a historic/archaeological site—respect the remains, do not disturb artifacts
- Not suitable for traditional scuba in most conditions due to depth
- Best as a supplementary visit combined with other nearby dives

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of cold water, surge, limited visibility in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Strong currents are possible — carry appropriate signaling devices and be prepared to abort the dive if conditions deteriorate. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

The wreck structure provides dramatic wide-angle subjects with natural light filtering through openings. A torch is essential for illuminating interior details and bringing out colors. Macro opportunities abound on the encrusted surfaces.
