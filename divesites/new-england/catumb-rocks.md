# Catumb Rocks

Submerged rocky outcrops off Watch Hill, Rhode Island, part of the Watch Hill Reef complex at the entrance to Fisher's Island Sound.

## Overview

Catumb Rocks lies in the Watch Hill area at the southwestern corner of Rhode Island, near the entrance to Fisher's Island Sound. It is confirmed as a distinct navigational feature within the Watch Hill Reef complex — a system that includes Watch Hill Reef, Sugar Reef, Catumb Rocks, and the passages near Lords and Wicopesset. Nautical sources specifically flag Catumb Rocks as a grounding hazard for boaters: one regional fishing source warns that "boaters can run aground on Sugar Reef and of course Catumb Rocks." The U.S. Coast Guard's coastal buoy modernization records list a "Catumb Passage Buoy 3C," confirming the rocks as a marked navigational hazard at the Watch Hill passage. The site sits at approximately 15 metres maximum depth, with rocky substrate typical of the Watch Hill coast. The strong tidal currents through the Watch Hill passage create the same productive fishing environment documented at nearby Watch Hill Reef.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Watch Hill area, southwestern Rhode Island, entrance to Fisher's Island Sound
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–12 meters (variable with tidal state)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong; Watch Hill tidal currents are significant
- **Water Temperature**: 8–20°C depending on season
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Cold-water temperate species typical of the Watch Hill area inhabit rocky habitat in this region. No site-specific dive reports documenting marine life at Catumb Rocks have been found. Regional fishing sources confirm that the Watch Hill Reef complex (of which Catumb Rocks is a part) hosts striped bass, bluefish, bonito, false albacore, scup, fluke, black sea bass, and blackfish (tautog) attracted by the strong tidal currents — species consistent with this rocky reef and tidal passage environment.

## Dive Profile

Boat access from Watch Hill area, Westerly, or eastern Connecticut operators near Stonington or Mystic. Descend to the rocky substrate at 10–15 metres and navigate the rock perimeter. The strong tidal currents in the Watch Hill area mean timing is critical — plan dives around slack water. Complete a 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat charter required. Watch Hill tidal currents are a primary planning consideration. A captain familiar with local tidal patterns is essential given the complex rock hazards in this area. Anchor in sand adjacent to rocks. Deploy SMB before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Plan dives strictly around Watch Hill slack tide — the currents here are among the strongest in Rhode Island Sound
- Catumb Rocks is a marked navigational hazard — a captain with local knowledge of the passage is essential
- Combine with Watch Hill Reef, Cormorant Reef, and Latimer Reef for a multi-site southern Rhode Island day
- Westerly and Watch Hill area operators have the best local knowledge

## Safety Considerations

Watch Hill tidal currents are strong and the passage between Fisher's Island Sound and Rhode Island Sound creates significant water movement. Plan all dives around slack water using Watch Hill tidal predictions — never attempt this site against a running current. Cold water below thermocline year-round — 7mm wetsuit or drysuit recommended. Deploy SMB clearly before surfacing.

## Photography

Rocky reef subjects at 15 metres require strobes for accurate color. Tidal passage environment with potential for fish attracted by current flow.
