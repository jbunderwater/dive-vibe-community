# Green Jacket Shoal

A 33-acre shoal in the Providence River, Rhode Island — Rhode Island's largest ship graveyard, containing 29 identified shipwrecks, and since 2018 a Marine Protected Area. Not a recreational scuba dive site.

## Overview

Green Jacket Shoal is a historically significant underwater site in the Providence River between Fox Point (Providence) and Bold Point (East Providence), Rhode Island. Named for the thick green eelgrass that once blanketed it, the shoal served from 1884 to 1919 as the effective "parking lot" for the Providence Dry Dock and Marine Railway Company — ships stripped of valuable machinery and hardware were towed here and abandoned at the end of their working lives.

In 2015, URI Graduate School of Oceanography marine archaeologist David Robinson identified 26 shipwrecks at the site (revised to 29 by 2018), making it Rhode Island's largest ship graveyard. Notable wrecks include the sidewheelers *Bay Queen* (182-foot sidewheeler operating 1865–1902) and *Mount Hope* (193-foot sidewheeler), along with 17 scow barges, 4 sailing ships, 3 harbor steamers, and other vessels. The site is a rare physical record of Providence's heyday as a maritime hub.

In 2018, the Rhode Island Coastal Resources Management Council created a Marine Protected Area to preserve the shoal, "creating a restricted perimeter and authorizing state and local law enforcement to take measures to ensure the graveyard is protected."

**This site is not a recreational scuba dive site.** No recreational dive documentation exists for Green Jacket Shoal in any source checked. The Providence River Boat Company operates surface glass-bottom boat tours of the shoal — this is the primary public access mode.

**The 18m depth field in the original OSM entry is incorrect.** The Providence River at this location is described as "shallow enough to be difficult for many ships to travel through" and has wood pilings protruding through the surface. Actual depths are estimated at 3–6 metres in the shoal area, not 18 metres.

## Historical Significance

- **1680**: India Point becomes Providence's first port
- **1787 onward**: John Brown family operates major trading enterprises
- **1884–1919**: Bold Point hosts Providence Dry Dock and Marine Railway Company
- **Late 1800s–early 1900s**: Immigrant steamships bring arrivals from Ireland, Cape Verde, Azores, Portugal
- **1930s–1940s**: Great Depression and 1938 hurricane damage India Point; area becomes scrap yard
- **1974**: India Point Park opens
- **2015**: URI marine archaeologist David Robinson identifies 26 wrecks
- **2018**: RI Coastal Resources Management Council designates site as Marine Protected Area
- **2018 revised count**: 29 shipwrecks documented

## Access and Tours

The Providence River Boat Company (providenceriverboat.com) operates guided boat tours of Green Jacket Shoal, led by marine archaeologist David Robinson. These are surface tours, not dives. The Marine Protected Area designation and shallow, debris-filled conditions make recreational scuba diving at this site inadvisable and potentially prohibited under the MPA restrictions.

## Research Notes

No scuba diving references found for this site on ScubaBoard, Zentacle, PADI, iDive New England Rhode Island listings, or any dive-focused resource. The 2001 U.S. Army Corps of Engineers study found "abundant" shellfish (soft-shell clams, oysters, mussels, periwinkles) in intertidal areas, confirming biological productivity, but these are not dive-accessible conditions.
