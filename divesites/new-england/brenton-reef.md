## Brenton Reef (LV39 Lightship)

Historic deep-water lightship wreck off Rhode Island—advanced technical dive only.

## Overview

Brenton Reef is the site of the LV39, a 387-ton wooden lightship that sank in the 1970s while being towed. The vessel lies upright and listing to port in 180 feet (55 meters) of water, providing approximately 20-25 feet of vertical relief. The wreck is accessible to the lower decks and may be penetrated by trained technical divers. This is an ADVANCED DEEP-WATER DIVE SITE requiring technical diving certification, extensive experience, and professional guidance. The depth exceeds recreational limits significantly. Brenton Reef is a landmark deep-water training and exploration destination in the Atlantic Northeast.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Brenton Reef, Rhode Island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 55 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-15 meters (variable with weather/season)
- **Current**: Variable, can be strong
- **Water Temperature**: 45-50°F (7-10°C)
- **Best Time**: June to September
- **Certification Required**: Advanced trimix or technical diving certification

## Marine Life

Deep cold-water Atlantic species. Limited visibility and depth restrict biological observation. Sparse encrusting fauna on wreck structure. This is not a primary diving goal for marine life observation.

## Dive Profile

This dive requires technical diving certification and experience with deep-water decompression, mixed gases (likely trimix), and extended bottom time management. Descent to 55 meters on wreck structure. Interior penetration possible for trained divers. Multiple decompression stops required: significant stops at depth (30+ meters), shallow stops (9-5 meters) for 20-50+ minutes. Total bottom time and decompression duration: 60-120+ minutes depending on profile.

## Entry and Exit

ADVANCED TECHNICAL DIVERS ONLY. Professional charter boat required (Northern Atlantic Dive Expeditions specializes in technical wreck diving). Controlled descent on line to wreck. Careful navigation and buddy separation monitoring essential. Return to ascent point and execute multi-stage decompression before surfacing.

## Tips and Recommendations

- PREREQUISITE: Advanced trimix or technical diving certification
- PREREQUISITE: 100+ technical deep-water dives minimum experience
- Dry suit and advanced thermal protection required (very cold water)
- Rebreather diving possible (technical training required)
- Multiple redundant air supplies and decompression gas required
- Dive computers with deep-water profiles and helium settings essential
- Professional guide/charter highly recommended (first-time divers)
- Plan dives well in advance—logistics and safety critical
- Not suitable for recreational diving or novice technical divers

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of cold water, surge, limited visibility in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Strong currents are possible — carry appropriate signaling devices and be prepared to abort the dive if conditions deteriorate. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
