# Abbot Rock

A submerged rocky ledge in the outer reaches of Salem Sound, north of Marblehead, Massachusetts. Not documented as a named recreational dive site.

## Overview

Abbot Rock is a submerged ledge system in the outer reaches of Salem Sound, north of Marblehead, Massachusetts. The rocky substrate drops to around 15 metres. The site is exposed to open ocean swells from the northeast. Salem Sound and its approaches include multiple submerged rocky hazards; this site sits in the outer sound zone used by vessels transiting between Beverly Harbor, Marblehead Harbor, and the Gulf of Maine.

Extensive research (June 2026) found no recreational dive documentation for Abbot Rock by name. The site does not appear in K1 Charters' Salem-area site listings, iDive New England's Massachusetts pages, North Shore Frogmen records, or ScubaBoard North Shore threads. iDive New England confirms Salem Sound (Beverly area) as an active dive area generally, and lists Halfway Rock as a nearby Beverly-area site — but Abbot Rock is not listed as a distinct named dive destination in any source found.

## Safety Considerations

Abbot Rock is exposed to open ocean conditions from the northeast. Currents can run strongly at peak tidal flow — plan dives around slack water. Cold water year-round. Deploy SMB before surfacing.
