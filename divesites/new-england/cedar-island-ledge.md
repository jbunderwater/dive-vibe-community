# Cedar Island Ledge

A submerged rocky ledge off the New Hampshire seacoast near the Isles of Shoals, exposed at low tide and submerged at high tide. Not documented as an active recreational dive destination.

## Overview

Cedar Island Ledge lies in the New Hampshire seacoast waters at approximately 42.97°N, 70.60°W, near the Isles of Shoals archipelago. The OSM data notes this feature is "submerged at high tide" — a tidal ledge, exposed at low water and covered at high tide. The ledge sits at 15 metres maximum depth in cold Gulf of Maine water north of Cape Ann.

The ledge has a documented maritime history: on January 15, 1813, a vessel struck Cedar Island Ledge near Star Island. Historical accounts in Isles of Shoals cruising guides note the ledge as unmarked, lying "to the north of Cedar Island, off the direct course." Coins are reported to have been recovered by scuba divers from around Cedar Island Ledge in connection with this wreck, per the Boston Sailing Center cruising guide for the Isles of Shoals area.

Extensive research (June 2026) found no recreational dive documentation for Cedar Island Ledge as an active, named dive destination: the site does not appear by name in Portsmouth Scuba's local dive guide, iDive New England's New Hampshire listings, or ScubaBoard New Hampshire area threads. The Isles of Shoals area generally is actively dived; Portsmouth Scuba confirms the broader area as diveable. Cedar Island Ledge is not, however, listed as a specific dive site by any operator or forum source found.

## Safety Considerations

The outer NH seacoast is exposed to Gulf of Maine swells and conditions. Cold water year-round in the Gulf of Maine — minimum 7mm wetsuit or drysuit. Moderate tidal current. Deploy SMB before surfacing in the open Gulf of Maine.
