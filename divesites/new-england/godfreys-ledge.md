# Godfrey's Ledge

A named rocky ledge off the New Hampshire seacoast near North Hampton, in the outer coastal zone south of the Isles of Shoals.

## Overview

Godfrey's Ledge is a submerged rocky ledge in the New Hampshire seacoast waters, located off North Hampton, NH at the southern end of the state's short coastline. The ledge name is associated with the Godfrey family of Hampton, NH. At coordinates placing it approximately 10 miles southwest of the Isles of Shoals (which lie at roughly 42.98°N, 70.62°W), this is a distinct outer coastal feature in the NH seacoast zone rather than a site in the Shoals group itself. The ledge sits in approximately 15 metres maximum depth in the cold waters of the Gulf of Maine, with rocky substrate typical of this stretch of coast. Portsmouth Scuba and other NH seacoast operators service this area, though published site listings from these operators do not specifically name Godfrey's Ledge among their listed sites.
