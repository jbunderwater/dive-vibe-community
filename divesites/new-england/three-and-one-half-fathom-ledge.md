# Three and One-half Fathom Ledge

A confirmed Boston Harbor boat dive site with sheer vertical walls dropping to 25+ metres — deeper and more dramatic than most South Shore ledges, with abundant marine life throughout.

## Overview

Three and One-half Fathom Ledge is a confirmed recreational dive site listed by both Boston SCUBA (bostonscuba.com) and iDive New England (idivenewengland.com/dive-sites/ma/3-1-2-fathoms-ledge). The name records an exact colonial-era depth sounding — three and a half fathoms (approximately 6.4 metres/21 feet) over the shallowest point, a depth that would have posed a serious hazard to larger commercial vessels.

Despite this historically shallow designation at the summit, the ledge descends significantly more: Boston SCUBA describes the site as starting at approximately 8 metres (25 feet) and dropping to over 25 metres (80 feet) "with shear vertical walls in many areas." This makes it one of the deepest and most topographically dramatic of the South Shore Boston Harbor boat dive sites. Both Boston SCUBA and iDive New England list it as an intermediate boat dive with good marine life.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South of Boston Harbor outer approaches, Massachusetts
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–10 metres (variable)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong; exposed outer position
- **Water Temperature**: 7–20°C depending on season
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Both Boston SCUBA and iDive New England describe "a lot of marine life around" this site. iDive New England classifies the site as supporting the characteristic species of the western Gulf of Maine cold-water reef community.

Cold-water temperate species typical of the outer Boston Harbor South Shore approach inhabit this rocky ledge habitat. The "sheer vertical walls" structure provides excellent encrusting organism habitat on shaded faces. No species-specific lists beyond the general operator descriptions have been found in available dive-specific sources.

## Dive Profile

Boat charter required. The shallowest section sits at approximately 6–8 metres (the historical 3.5-fathom sounding), then quickly drops to 25+ metres with sheer vertical walls in many areas. Divers can choose their depth along the ledge profile. The shallow top explores the historical summit zone; the mid-ledge walls at 10–20 metres are the primary dive zone; the base at 25+ metres is accessible for experienced divers. Complete a 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat charter from Boston area or South Shore operators. The outer position is more exposed than inner harbor sites — calm conditions and slack tide preferred. Anchor in sand adjacent to ledge. Deploy SMB before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The deepest of the South Shore outer Boston Harbor ledge cluster — plan dive time and gas accordingly for the 25+ metre sections
- The sheer vertical walls are unusual for the region and reward wide-angle photography
- Plan strictly around slack tide — the outer position can have significant current
- 7mm wetsuit or drysuit required; cold water year-round
- Combine with Tewksbury Rock or Martin's Ledge for a productive outer harbor multi-site day

## Safety Considerations

Sheer walls mean a diver ascending on the wall face can surface away from the boat — deploy SMB and plan ascent route. Cold water year-round. Strong current at the outer position. The deeper sections (20–25+ metres) are only for divers comfortable with these depths in cold-water conditions.

## Photography

The "sheer vertical walls" (per Boston SCUBA description) are unusual for Massachusetts reef diving and make this a compelling wide-angle site. Wall face portraits with encrusting life; the transition from kelp-covered shallow section to deep vertical wall is a strong compositional element.
