# Tewksbury Rock

A large rock pinnacle east of Outer Brewster Island, Massachusetts — one of the premier South Shore Boston Harbor boat dives, with a dramatic profile from 3 metres at the summit to 21 metres at the base.

## Overview

Tewksbury Rock is a confirmed recreational dive site listed by Boston SCUBA (bostonscuba.com) as a charter destination and described in multiple sources as one of the best multi-level dives accessible from Boston Harbor. The site lies east of Outer Brewster Island, approximately a 50-minute boat ride from a downtown Boston dive shop dock.

The rock's profile is dramatic: the shallowest summit sits at approximately 3 metres (10 feet) at average low tide, then the structure drops steeply to a maximum of around 21 metres (70 feet). The north side slopes gradually; the south side drops more steeply. The upper 10 metres is densely covered in kelp that gradually fades with depth. Huge boulders form walls, ledges, and numerous crevices throughout the structure — a first-hand dive blog (Liquid Origins, liquid-origins.com) describes "beautiful valleys and crevasses" in the deeper sections.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East of Outer Brewster Island, Boston Harbor, Massachusetts
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Approximately 6 metres (20 feet) at depth — considered good for the region
- **Current**: Strong — exposed position east of Boston Harbor islands; slack tide required
- **Water Temperature**: 7–20°C depending on season
- **Best Time**: June to October (calm conditions and slack tide required)

## Marine Life

The following species are documented in published first-hand dive accounts at this specific site: cunner (described as "big" specimens), tautog, American lobster, and scallops (at deeper sections around the pinnacle base).

The upper kelp zone supports typical North Shore reef species. Additional cold-water temperate species consistent with this outer Boston Harbor position likely inhabit the site, but only those listed above have been confirmed in site-specific sources.

## Dive Profile

Boat charter required — approximately 50-minute ride. The site is best dived at slack tide given its exposed outer harbor position. Descend to the summit at 3 metres and work down the ledge walls to the base at 21 metres. The north side offers a more gradual descent; the south side drops more steeply with larger wall sections. Explore the boulders and crevice faces for lobsters and tautog. Complete a 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Boat charter from Boston area operators (Boston SCUBA). The outer harbor exposure means this site requires calm surface conditions. Strong tidal current — plan strictly around slack water. Anchor or moor near the rock system. Deploy SMB before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This is one of Boston's best multi-level dives — the 3–21m profile allows divers to choose their depth
- Slack tide is essential — strong current makes this site uncomfortable or undiveable at peak tidal flow
- Combine with Martin's Ledge for a two-site Boston Harbor charter day
- The kelp cover in the upper zone (0–10m) is dense — good habitat for cunner and smaller reef fish
- 7mm wetsuit or drysuit required year-round
- Visibility of 6m/20ft is typical and considered good for this area

## Safety Considerations

Strong tidal current at the exposed outer harbor position. Plan strictly around slack water. The shallow summit (3m) can experience surface surge in any swell. Cold water year-round. Deploy SMB before surfacing in open harbor water.

## Photography

The dramatic boulder topography — described as "awesome rock formations" — makes wide-angle shooting compelling in the 8–15m zone. Cunner and tautog portraits on the kelp-covered ledge faces are primary subjects. Scallops at the base are unusual subjects in the Boston area.
