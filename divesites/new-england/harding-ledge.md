# Harding Ledge

A confirmed Boston Harbor boat dive site — a shallow, very rocky ledge 1.5 miles east of Point Allerton, Hull, Massachusetts, scattered with visible historic shipwreck debris.

## Overview

Harding Ledge is a confirmed recreational dive site listed by Boston SCUBA (bostonscuba.com) as a charter destination, with its own Wikipedia article and mention in multiple ScubaBoard Boston Harbor threads. The ledge lies 1.5 miles east of Point Allerton in Hull, Massachusetts, in the outer approaches to Boston Harbor.

Boston SCUBA's site description notes depths from 3 to 12 metres (10–40 feet), a very rocky bottom, and "lots of color and growths" throughout. The ledge has been the site of numerous shipwrecks throughout history — the Wikipedia article confirms this — and historic wreck debris remains visible in multiple areas across the site. This combination of shallow, accessible depth and the presence of wreck fragments makes it a distinctive South Shore dive.

A lighted bell buoy located 0.3 miles northeast of the shoal guides vessels safely around the outer edge.

## Site Information

- **Location**: 1.5 miles east of Point Allerton, Hull, Massachusetts
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Rocky ledge / reef with scattered historic wreck debris
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 metres (40 feet); shallowest 3 metres (10 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–10 metres (variable)
- **Current**: Moderate; outer Boston Harbor position
- **Water Temperature**: 7–20°C depending on season
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Boston SCUBA describes "lots of color and growths" on the very rocky bottom. The ledge structure with its shallow depth and rocky substrate supports encrusting life throughout. The scatter of wreck debris provides additional habitat complexity.

Cold-water temperate species typical of the outer Boston Harbor area inhabit this rocky ledge habitat. No site-specific species lists beyond the operator description above have been independently documented.

## Dive Profile

Boat charter required. The ledge is shallow and accessible: 3–12 metres (10–40 feet). The shallow maximum depth allows extended bottom time compared to deeper outer harbor sites. Navigate the ledge and examine visible wreck debris scattered throughout the rocky bottom. Multiple wreck fragments from historical groundings are present. Plan around tidal state. Complete a 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat charter from Hull, Scituate, or Boston area operators. Boston SCUBA includes this site in their charter schedule. Anchor in sand adjacent to the ledge. The navigational bell buoy (0.3 miles northeast) provides a useful reference for approach. Deploy SMB before surfacing.

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of the shallower South Shore boat dives — excellent bottom time at 10–40 ft
- Historic wreck debris throughout: this ledge has been wrecking ships for centuries, and scattered hull and machinery fragments remain
- Combine with Tewksbury Rock or Martin's Ledge for a mixed shallow/deep outer harbor charter day
- 7mm wetsuit or drysuit required
- The lighted bell buoy provides a useful surface reference

## Safety Considerations

Very rocky bottom can make anchoring challenging. Cold water year-round. Moderate current at the outer harbor position. Rocky bottom close to the surface at the shallowest sections — appropriate depth awareness needed. Deploy SMB before surfacing.

## Photography

Shallow depths (3–12m) allow good natural light without strobes in clear conditions. Historic wreck debris scattered on the rocky ledge is a distinctive photographic subject. "Lots of color and growths" on rock surfaces for close-up work.
