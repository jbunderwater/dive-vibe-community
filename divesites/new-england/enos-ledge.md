# Enos Ledge

A rocky ledge in the South Shore ledge cluster off Scituate and Cohasset, Massachusetts. Not documented as a named recreational dive site.

## Overview

Enos Ledge is part of the South Shore ledge cluster — a group of named submerged rocky ledges south of Boston Harbor in the waters off Scituate and Cohasset, Massachusetts. Along with Davis, Tobias, Mikes, Harding, Martin, Utonia, and Three-and-One-Half-Fathom Ledges, Enos forms part of a network of navigational hazards that appear on NOAA nautical charts for the approach south of the President Roads channel.

Extensive research (June 2026) found no recreational dive documentation for Enos Ledge by name. The ledge does not appear in Boston SCUBA, iDive New England, South Shore Divers, ScubaBoard South Shore threads, or any dive directory checked. The South Shore ledge cluster's confirmed recreational sites (Martin Ledge, Tewksbury Rock, Harding Ledge, Three-and-One-Half Fathom Ledge) are all documented by name; Enos does not appear alongside them in any source found.
