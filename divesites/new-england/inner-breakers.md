# Inner Breakers

A navigational hazard reef in the outer Salem Sound area off Marblehead, Massachusetts, where rocky shoals create breaking seas in a swell. Not documented as a named recreational dive site.

## Overview

Inner Breakers is a submerged rocky shoal in the outer Salem Sound area, east of Marblehead and west of the open Gulf of Maine approach. The name describes its navigational character — "breakers" form when ocean swells encounter shallow rocky ground, and the "inner" designation distinguishes this feature from outer breakers further seaward. Salem Sound and its approaches have a complex system of navigational hazards including rocky ledges and shoals that extend from Marblehead, Nahant, and the surrounding headlands.

Extensive research (June 2026) found no recreational dive documentation for Inner Breakers: the site does not appear in Salem/Marblehead area dive operator listings (K1 Charters, iDive New England, North Shore Frogmen), ScubaBoard North Shore threads, or dive directories. The rocky shoal structure and 14-metre depth are consistent with diveable terrain, but this site is not documented as a named recreational dive destination. Divers based in the Marblehead or Beverly area who wish to dive the outer Salem Sound approach should consult K1 Charters or similar operators for current site availability.
