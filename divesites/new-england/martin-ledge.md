# Martin Ledge

A rocky ledge east of Outer Brewster Island, Massachusetts — a confirmed Boston Harbor boat dive site with large pinnacles, deep crevices, and a profile dropping from 6 metres to over 21 metres.

## Overview

Martin Ledge is a confirmed recreational dive site listed by Boston SCUBA (bostonscuba.com) as a charter destination. The site lies east of Outer Brewster Island, in the same outer Boston Harbor area as Tewksbury Rock and Devil's Back Ledge. Boston SCUBA's site description notes the ledge begins at approximately 6–8 metres (20–25 feet) at the top and quickly drops to over 21 metres (70 feet). The rock formations are described as "awesome" with "large pinnacles, and deep crevices" on a very rocky bottom. Marine life is reliably present.

Multiple ScubaBoard Boston Harbor threads mention Martin's Ledge alongside Tewksbury Rock as the premier South Shore–accessible Boston Harbor boat dive combination.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East of Outer Brewster Island, Boston Harbor, Massachusetts
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–8 metres (variable; Boston Harbor typical range)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong — exposed outer harbor position; dive at slack tide
- **Water Temperature**: 7–20°C depending on season
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Boston SCUBA's site listing describes "usually a fair amount of marine life" at this site. The ledge structure with its large pinnacles and deep crevices provides abundant habitat. Species characteristic of outer Boston Harbor rocky ledges at this depth range are expected, but no site-specific species lists beyond the general description above have been found in available sources.

Cold-water temperate species typical of the outer Boston Harbor area inhabit similar rocky ledge/pinnacle habitat in this region. No site-specific marine life surveys have been independently documented beyond the general operator description.

## Dive Profile

Boat charter required. Top of ledge at 6–8 metres; maximum depth 21+ metres. Navigate the pinnacles and crevice faces from top to base. The dramatic topography — described as "awesome" rock formations — rewards a slow, exploratory approach. Combine with Tewksbury Rock on a two-site charter. Plan around slack tide. Complete a 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat charter from Boston area operators including Boston SCUBA. Exposed outer harbor position requires calm surface conditions. Plan dives strictly around slack water. Anchor or moor adjacent to ledge. Deploy SMB before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Combine with Tewksbury Rock for a premier outer Boston Harbor two-site day
- The deep crevices are the primary habitat for lobsters and larger tautog
- 7mm wetsuit or drysuit required
- Plan strictly around slack tide — outer harbor current can be strong
- The "large pinnacles" structure means this is a good site for wide-angle photography if visibility allows

## Safety Considerations

Strong to moderate current at the exposed outer harbor position. Plan around slack water. Cold water year-round. Deploy SMB before surfacing in open harbor water.

## Photography

The "large pinnacles, and deep crevices" topography (per Boston SCUBA site description) makes this an excellent wide-angle ledge dive when visibility allows. Rocky structure with encrusting life. Strobe lighting needed for color accuracy at 15–21 metres.
