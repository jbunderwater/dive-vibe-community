# Davis Ledge

A rocky ledge in the South Shore ledge cluster near Scituate, Massachusetts. Not documented as a named recreational dive site.

## Overview

Davis Ledge is one of a cluster of named submerged rocky ledges in the waters south of Boston Harbor, off the Cohasset and Scituate coastline of Massachusetts's South Shore. The ledges in this area — Davis, Enos, Harding, Martin, Mikes, Tobias, Three-and-One-Half-Fathom, and Utonia Ledges — appear on navigational charts as hazards south of the main shipping channels into Boston.

Extensive research (June 2026) found no recreational dive documentation for Davis Ledge by name. The ledge does not appear in Boston SCUBA, iDive New England, South Shore Divers, ScubaBoard South Shore threads, or any dive directory checked. The South Shore ledge cluster's confirmed recreational sites (Martin Ledge, Tewksbury Rock, Harding Ledge, Three-and-One-Half Fathom Ledge) are documented by name by Boston SCUBA; Davis Ledge does not appear in their site listing or in any other operator or forum source found.
