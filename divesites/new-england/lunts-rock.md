# Lunts Rock

A tidal rock obstruction in the Hampton/Seabrook NH coastal zone charted as a navigational hazard (foul ground). No dive-specific documentation found.

## Overview

Lunts Rock is a tidal rock at lat 42.821, lon -70.835 (Hampton/Seabrook NH coastal zone, Plum Island/Merrimack River approach area). It is recorded in the USGS Geographic Names Information System as feature ID 613703, confirming its status as a named geographic locality. OSM classifies it as `seamark:obstruction:category=foul_ground` and `seamark:rock:water_level=covers` (tidal). The site is within a cluster of similarly named navigational hazards including Black Rocks, Badgers Rock, Half Tide Rocks, and Sow and Pigs.

This site has been researched against five dive-operator and directory sources: iDive New England, Portsmouth Scuba, Zentacle, Boston SCUBA, and ScubaBoard. None list this site as a recreational dive destination. ScubaBoard discussions confirm the Newburyport/Plum Island area is primarily sandy with offshore rocky sites not documented as dive destinations; local divers travel to Cape Ann (Gloucester/Rockport) instead.

**This site is not currently documented as a recreational dive site.** The tidal rock exists as a charted nautical hazard, but no operator, guide, or diver forum has documented dives here.
