# Gangway Rocks

A permanently submerged rock hazard in the Hampton/Seabrook NH coastal zone. No dive-specific documentation found.

## Overview

Gangway Rocks is a permanently submerged rock obstruction at lat 42.814, lon -70.858 (Hampton/Seabrook NH coastal zone), classified as a maritime hazard (`hazard=yes`, `seamark:rock:water_level=submerged`, `tidal=no`). It sits approximately 250 metres from the adjacent Half Tide Rocks cluster (osm_id 322041507), which was historically also known as "Gangway Rock" — that cluster formerly contained a rock called Gangway Rock removed by US Army Corps contract in the 1880s. This separately named Gangway Rocks feature is a distinct obstruction that remains permanently submerged.

This site has been researched against five dive-operator and directory sources: iDive New England, Portsmouth Scuba, Zentacle, Boston SCUBA, and ScubaBoard. None list this site as a recreational dive destination. Hampton Beach NH ScubaBoard threads confirm predominantly sandy coastal bottom with offshore rocky sites not documented as dive destinations.

**This site is not currently documented as a recreational dive site.** The submerged rocks exist as charted nautical hazards, but no operator, guide, or diver forum has documented dives here.
