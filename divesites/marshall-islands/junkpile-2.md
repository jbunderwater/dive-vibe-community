## Junkpile-2

Junkpile-2 is the deepest of the three Junkpile wreck sites in Kwajalein Atoll's lagoon, with a maximum depth of 33 meters that places it firmly in advanced territory — but rewards the extra depth commitment with the most substantial and complex debris structures in the cluster.

## Overview

Sitting close to Junkpile-1 and Junkpile-3 in the inner lagoon, Junkpile-2 descends to 33 meters where the larger and heavier pieces of WWII military debris settled during post-battle cleanup and surplus disposal operations. The deeper position means the light quality is slightly reduced compared to the shallower sites, enhancing the atmospheric quality of the dive — a dive torch is essential here, both for navigation in the darker recesses and for bringing out the vivid colors of the encrusting sponges and corals that coat every surface.

At advanced difficulty, divers should plan their bottom time carefully — 33 meters eats through a tank quickly, and the most interesting debris is at depth. The approach is typically to spend maximum time at the deepest structures, then work progressively shallower as air permits, finishing the dive on the upper debris field and the sandy lagoon floor.

## What to Expect

The greater depth and more substantial debris at Junkpile-2 attract larger resident species. Larger moray eels occupy the deeper sections, and grey reef sharks occasionally cruise through the debris field at depth. The encrusting life here is more developed — large sponge colonies in orange, red, and purple dominate the surfaces, alongside sea fans that have managed to establish at these depths in the lagoon environment.

Lionfish are pervasive throughout all depth ranges. Look carefully for the highly camouflaged stonefish and flatfish resting on the sandy areas between debris pieces — passive mimicry specialists that are very easy to miss. Macro photographers will find the deep sections of Junkpile-2 particularly productive: nudibranchs, flatworms, and small crustaceans populate the encrusted debris surfaces.

## Practical Information

- **Depth Range**: 12–33 meters
- **Visibility**: 15–30 meters typical
- **Currents**: Generally mild; sheltered lagoon
- **Entry**: Boat dive from Kwajalein operators
- **Best For**: Advanced divers; bring a dive torch; careful air management required

## Marine Life Highlights

Grey reef sharks (occasional), large moray eels, lionfish, stonefish, flatfish, sponge colonies, sea fans, nudibranchs, flatworms, snapper, groupers, glassy sweepers.
