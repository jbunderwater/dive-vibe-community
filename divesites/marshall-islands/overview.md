## Marshall Islands

WWII wrecks, pristine coral atolls, and Pacific pelagic encounters.

## Description

The Marshall Islands is one of the Pacific's most historically significant and scenically remote diving destinations. Spread across two parallel chains of atolls — the Ratak (Sunrise) and Ralik (Sunset) chains — the archipelago encompasses Bikini Atoll, Kwajalein Atoll, and Kosrae, each with its own character. Bikini Atoll is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the location of 23 American nuclear weapons tests between 1946 and 1958, and home to an extraordinary fleet of sunken warships from Operation Crossroads. Kwajalein Atoll — the world's largest atoll by lagoon area — holds some of the Pacific War's most accessible WWII wreck diving. Beyond the wrecks, the outer reef walls, lagoon coral heads, and passes deliver pristine coral ecosystems rarely touched by mass tourism.

Diving here is serious business. Most sites are reachable only by liveaboard, and Bikini Atoll requires a dedicated liveaboard expedition. Visibility typically exceeds 30 meters in the open lagoon and outer walls. Water temperatures hold a comfortable 28–30°C year-round. Currents at pass and wall sites can be strong; divers should be comfortable navigating blue-water conditions.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Wreck Diving**: Bikini Atoll's warship wrecks — USS Saratoga, HIJMS Nagato, USS Arkansas, USS Gilliam, USS Anderson — are among the most famous in the world. Kwajalein hosts additional WWII Japanese and American wrecks.
- **Coral Head Diving**: Kwajalein Lagoon contains dozens of isolated coral pinnacles rising from the sandy lagoon floor, named after navigational buoys and local landmarks, with exceptional fish density.
- **Wall Diving**: Outer atoll drop-offs plunge hundreds of meters, draped in gorgonian fans and patrolled by grey reef sharks.
- **Drift Diving**: Pass diving at Kwajalein and Kosrae channels produces reliable encounters with schooling fish and pelagic species.
- **Night Diving**: Kwajalein's coral heads come alive after dark with hunting lionfish, feeding octopus, and bioluminescent plankton.

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: United Airlines operates the "Island Hopper" service connecting Honolulu, Kwajalein (Bucholz Army Airfield — military/DOD only), Kosrae, Pohnpei, Chuuk, and Guam. Civilian access to Kwajalein is through Ebeye Island. Bikini Atoll is accessible only via liveaboard expedition departing Majuro.
- **Dive Operators**: Kwajalein-based operators serve military and civilian divers on the lagoon sites. Bikini Atoll liveaboard expeditions run seasonally, typically April–October.
- **Accommodation**: Majuro has hotels and guesthouses. Ebeye near Kwajalein offers basic lodging. Bikini Atoll has no accommodation — all diving is from the liveaboard vessel.
- **Permits**: Bikini Atoll requires advance permits arranged through the liveaboard operator. Photography and artifact collection from wreck sites is strictly controlled.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Year-round temperatures of 27–30°C (81–86°F). Visibility is exceptional, typically 30–50 meters on outer reefs and 20–40 meters in the lagoon. Currents are moderate in the lagoon and can be strong at passes and outer walls.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Grey reef sharks, whitetip reef sharks, and occasional hammerheads patrol the outer walls. Napoleon wrasse, bumphead parrotfish, and large dogtooth tuna are common. Kwajalein's coral heads harbor dense populations of lionfish, moray eels, and nudibranchs. Hawksbill and green sea turtles are regular sightings.
- **Wreck Ecosystems**: The WWII wrecks at Bikini and Kwajalein are fully colonized by coral and sponge communities, hosting enormous populations of snapper, grouper, batfish, and glassy sweepers inside holds and along superstructures.
- **Conservation**: Bikini Atoll is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Dive operators enforce strict no-take and no-artifact policies across all wreck sites.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: April to November offers the calmest seas and best visibility. December–March sees more swell and reduced liveaboard scheduling for Bikini.
- **Currency**: US Dollar
- **Language**: Marshallese, English
- **Certification Requirements**: Advanced Open Water minimum for most Kwajalein wreck dives; deep wreck penetration dives at Bikini Atoll require wreck specialty certification or equivalent experience. Bikini liveaboards typically require Advanced certification and logged deep dive experience.
- **Safety**: The nearest hyperbaric chamber is at Kwajalein's hospital (military facility; access may be restricted for civilians) and at Guam Naval Base. Confirm emergency procedures with your liveaboard operator before departure. Bikini Atoll is 800km from Majuro — remote diving demands thorough pre-trip planning.
