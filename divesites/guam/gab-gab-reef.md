# Gab Gab Reef

A harbor reef dive inside Apra Harbor on Naval Base Guam — **access requires a valid military ID or a sponsored visitor pass**. Shore entry via stairs with handrail leads to a sloping coral reef dropping from 6 meters to beyond 30 meters. Exceptional visibility (23–30 meters / 75–100 feet) for a harbor site. Nurse sharks, whitetip reef sharks, green sea turtles, moray eels, and clownfish are commonly encountered.

## Overview

Gab Gab Reef is located inside Apra Harbor on Naval Base Guam, off Gab Gab Beach. **Critical access note**: the site is inside a U.S. Navy installation. Civilians require a valid military sponsor and must present authorized identification at the gate; base access policies are subject to change and should be confirmed before planning a trip.

The reef slopes from the beach entry at 6 meters down to beyond 30 meters along the outer edge. Visibility in Gab Gab's harbor waters is consistently far better than typical harbor environments — 23 to 30 meters (75–100+ feet) is the norm, with little wave action or current. The sheltered conditions and abundant marine life make this one of the most pleasant dives on Guam when access can be arranged.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gab Gab Beach, Naval Base Guam, Apra Harbor
- **Access**: Naval Base Guam — military ID or sponsored civilian access required; confirm current access policy before visiting
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 23–30 meters (75–100+ feet)
- **Water Temperature**: ~29 degrees C
- **Current**: Generally calm with no waves inside the harbor
- **Best Time**: Year-round; early morning recommended for the best shark encounters

## Marine Life

Nurse sharks are a highlight — divers commonly encounter them, including multiple individuals near feeding stations. Whitetip reef sharks are seen, particularly in early morning dives along the reef edge. Resident green sea turtles are observed on most dives, though they can be shy. Moray eels inhabit crevices throughout the reef. Clownfish and anemones are found at multiple locations on the reef. Large barracuda and ulua (giant trevally) patrol the water column. The site supports at least 50 recorded fish species. Plate corals, sponges, hard and soft corals are all present. Note: the "mini-mountain of cabbage leaf corals" description in earlier versions of this file has not been confirmed by primary sources reviewed; it may describe a specific feature within the broader reef or may be inaccurate.

## Dive Profile

Enter via the stairs from the parking lot and descend to the reef. Swim outward to where the slope steepens toward the outer edge. The main reef structure at 6–18 meters provides the most concentrated marine life. Deeper sections beyond 20 meters drop off sharply. Circles of the reef formation allow repeated passes over the coral and anemone beds. No waves or current typically — a relaxed dive suitable for extended observation.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Gab Gab Beach — walk to the stairs, enter via the handrail section, and step onto the submerged rock platform. The final step is notably slippery — proceed carefully. Facilities at the beach include showers, restrooms, and covered picnic tables. A designated swimming area with float markers is present; divers should be aware of swimmers.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Confirm current base access requirements and hours before visiting — policies can change with base command
- Early morning dives offer the best nurse shark and whitetip reef shark encounters
- The calm, high-visibility harbor water makes this an excellent photography site
- Bring a macro lens — clownfish and anemone pairings are abundant throughout the reef

## Safety Considerations

A generally gentle site. The harbor location means occasional submarine and boat traffic — maintain awareness of surface activity. The outer edge drops beyond recreational limits — monitor depth if venturing past 20 meters. The slippery entry step requires caution.
