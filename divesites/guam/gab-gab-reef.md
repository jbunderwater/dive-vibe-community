# Gab Gab Reef

A shore dive from Gab Gab Beach on the U.S. Naval Base Guam (ComNavMar), accessible to military-affiliated personnel and their guests. The reef drops from approximately 6 meters (20 feet) to over 30 meters (100 feet), with anemone beds, turtles, whitetip reef sharks, moray eels, and nurse sharks. Often called the "home reef" of Guam.

## Overview

Gab Gab Reef is located off Gab Gab Beach within the U.S. Naval Base Guam (formerly ComNavMar) on the eastern shore of Apra Harbor. It is generally considered Guam's informal "home reef" and is a primary training dive for military divers on the island. Access is restricted to those with base access — military personnel, their families, and sponsored guests. Non-military divers typically need to dive with a shop operating boat access to the site.

The reef slopes gradually from the beach entry at 6 meters (20 feet) to over 30 meters (100 feet). The structure includes plate corals, sponges, and assorted hard corals. Multiple anemone beds with resident clownfish are a highlight. Visibility, protected inside the harbor, is generally good at 22–30 meters (75–100 feet) — better than many harbor sites.

A second site, Gab Gab II, is a plateau reef at approximately 15 meters (50 feet) depth, accessible a short swim further out.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gab Gab Beach, U.S. Naval Base Guam, Apra Harbor
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 22–30 meters (75–100 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: ~28–29 degrees C
- **Current**: Generally mild inside the harbor
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Zentacle dive reviews confirm at Gab Gab: turtles, whitetip reef sharks, nurse sharks, moray eels, barracuda, batfish, clownfish (in anemone beds), and the large black ulua (giant trevally). Over 50 fish species have been reported by divers. Hard and soft corals including plate corals and sponges are present throughout. Multiple prominent anemone beds are a signature feature.

**Note on cabbage coral (Turbinaria)**: The "cabbage coral / underwater mini-mountain" description in the previous version was sourced from Michael McFadyen, but was not confirmed by the other independent sources reviewed in this QA pass. The reef is described as a sloping structure with multiple coral types — the "mini-mountain" characterization may apply to a specific section but is not widely corroborated. This specific claim has been removed pending additional confirmation.

## Dive Profile

Enter from Gab Gab Beach (easy entry with stairs and handrails at the marked entry point). Swim out to the white mooring buoy, approximately 5 minutes from shore. The reef begins at about 6 meters and drops progressively. Circle the reef structure exploring the coral formations and watching for turtles and reef sharks in the water column. Gab Gab II, the secondary plateau site, is accessible with a short further swim. The gentle depth profile allows generous bottom time.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Gab Gab Beach on Naval Base Guam. The marked entry point has stairs and a handrail. Secure base access before visiting.

## Safety Considerations

A gentle site with minimal hazards. Harbor location provides calm conditions. The base-access requirement means site conditions are generally well-maintained. Be aware of other beach users and any military vessel activity in the harbor. Moray eels are present — maintain distance from reef crevices.
