# Fish Eye Marine Park

A shallow protected reef in Piti Bay Marine Preserve, home to the only underwater observatory in Micronesia. Divers and snorkelers explore 200+ fish species and approximately 80 coral species in calm, shallow water (3–10 meters). Access via a walk-in entry from the sea bridge walkway.

## Overview

Fish Eye Marine Park is located in Piti Bay, within the Piti Bay Marine Preserve on Guam's western coast. The park's centrepiece is the only underwater observatory in Micronesia — a 10-meter-deep chamber reached by walking across a sea bridge and descending inside the structure. From inside, 26 viewing windows provide views of the surrounding reef without getting wet. Scuba divers and snorkelers explore the same reef from outside the observatory structure.

The Piti Bay Marine Preserve protects over 200 species of fish and approximately 80 species of coral. The protected designation has allowed reef life to flourish; fish populations are dense and relatively unafraid of divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Piti Bay Marine Preserve, Guam (located across from MDA dive shop on Marine Corps Drive)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 28–29 degrees C
- **Current**: Generally calm inside the bay
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The protected bay supports over 200 fish species and approximately 80 coral species. Colourful reef fish including butterflyfish, parrotfish, damselfish, and wrasses are present in large numbers. Barracuda, blacktip reef sharks, and sea turtles are occasionally sighted. Coral formations within the preserve are well-developed. No site-specific species lists beyond those noted above have been confirmed in sources reviewed.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the sea bridge area and explore the reef at 3–10 meters. The shallow maximum depth allows extended bottom time. The reef extends around the observatory structure, with the best coral and fish life within the preserved zone. Calm bay conditions make this ideal for relaxed exploration and underwater photography.

## Entry and Exit

Access via the Fish Eye Marine Park sea bridge walkway. Enter the water near the observatory structure. The park charges an admission fee (approximately USD 16 for adults). Exit at the same location.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The protected preserve status means fish are denser and more approachable than at unprotected sites
- Excellent for underwater photography beginners — calm water, abundant subjects, shallow depth
- Combine the dive with a visit to the underwater observatory for a non-diving perspective on the same reef
- Dolphin watching cruises and eco-snorkeling tours are also offered from the park

## Safety Considerations

An extremely gentle dive site. Calm bay conditions and shallow depth make this one of the safest dive locations on Guam. Be aware of observatory visitors and snorkelers sharing the water.
