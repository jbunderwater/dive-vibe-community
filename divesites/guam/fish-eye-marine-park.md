# Fish Eye Marine Park

A marine preserve in Piti Bay featuring Micronesia's only underwater observatory — a dry viewing chamber accessed via a 1,000-foot sea bridge, positioned approximately 9 meters (30 feet) below the surface. Divers and snorkelers explore the same protected reef from outside, in water home to over 200 fish species and 80 coral species. Natural "bomb holes" — percolation pits where fresh water filters into the lagoon — are a distinctive feature at 7–9 meters.

## Overview

Fish Eye Marine Park is located in Piti Bay on the western coast of Guam, within the Piti Bay Marine Preserve. The park's centerpiece is the Fish Eye Underwater Observatory, the only facility of its kind in Micronesia. The observatory extends approximately 1,000 feet from shore into Piti Bay; its viewing deck is positioned roughly 9 meters (30 feet) below the ocean surface and offers panoramic views through 24 large windows. Visitors can observe the reef without entering the water, while divers and snorkelers explore the same reef from the outside.

The Piti Bay Marine Preserve designation protects the surrounding reef, which supports over 200 fish species and approximately 80 coral species, making it among the most biodiverse shallow-reef environments on Guam. The "bomb holes" are distinctive natural percolation pits on the sandy bottom where fresh water filters through into the bay at depths of approximately 7–9 meters — an unusual geological feature that divers can explore. The observatory structure itself acts as an artificial reef and is an orientation point during dives.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Piti Bay Marine Preserve, Piti village, western Guam
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Bomb Holes**: ~7–9 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 28–29°C
- **Current**: Generally calm inside the bay
- **Best Time**: Year-round (park hours 9:00am–5:00pm)

## Marine Life

Over 200 fish species have been recorded in the preserve, including parrotfish, damselfish, triggerfish, and butterflyfish. Approximately 80 coral species are present. Sea turtles are occasionally observed passing through. The protected status allows fish to remain unafraid of divers. No specific species lists confirmed at the bomb holes site specifically — the above reflects the preserve-wide inventory.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the sea bridge walkway and explore the reef surrounding the observatory structure at 3–10 meters. Swim toward the bomb holes on the sandy bottom at 7–9 meters — these shallow percolation pits are a curious feature and worth locating. The observatory structure provides an orientation anchor throughout the dive. The shallow depth allows extended bottom time for photography.

## Entry and Exit

Access via the sea bridge walkway from the Fish Eye Marine Park visitor center (818 North Marine Corps Drive, Piti). Walk across the bridge and enter the water near the observatory. Exit at the same location. Park hours are 9:00am–5:00pm; admission charged separately from diving/snorkeling.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Combine the shore dive with a visit inside the observatory for an unusual perspective — observe the same reef from inside and outside
- The protected preserve means fish are dense and habituated to divers, making this excellent for underwater photography
- Seek out the bomb holes on the sandy bottom for a geological curiosity unique to this site
- Dolphin-watching cruises also depart from the park

## Safety Considerations

An extremely gentle dive site. Calm bay conditions and shallow depth make this one of the safest locations on Guam. Maintain awareness of snorkelers and observatory visitors sharing the water. Respect the marine preserve — no fishing, no coral contact.
