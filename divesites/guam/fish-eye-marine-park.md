# Fish Eye Marine Park

A shallow snorkel and dive site within the Piti Bomb Holes Marine Preserve — Guam's most ecologically diverse marine protected area — in Piti Bay. The park's centerpiece is the only underwater observatory in Micronesia, reached via a 300-meter (1,000-foot) wooden boardwalk. The percolation pits ("bomb holes") descend to approximately 9 meters (30 feet). The Preserve covers 900 acres and is confirmed to contain two mollusk species and one sea urchin species found nowhere else on Earth.

## Overview

Fish Eye Marine Park sits inside the Piti Bomb Holes Marine Preserve, established in 1999 as a marine protected area in Piti Bay on Guam's western coast. The "bomb holes" in the park's name refer not to actual bomb craters but to natural freshwater percolation pits where fresh water filters from the limestone into the shallow lagoon. The largest of these pits houses the Fish Eye Marine Park observatory, constructed in 1996. The preserve covers approximately 3.64 km² (900 acres) and receives over 200,000 visitors annually.

The underwater observatory is the only facility of its kind in Micronesia. Visitors walk 300 meters (1,000 feet) along a wooden sea bridge to reach the observation chamber, which sits at approximately 9 meters (30 feet) depth. Divers and snorkelers can explore the same reef from outside the observatory.

The Piti Bomb Holes Preserve is confirmed as the most ecologically diverse of Guam's five marine protected areas. It contains two mollusk species and one sea urchin species that are endemic to this location — found nowhere else on Earth.

**Marine life count note**: Promotional materials for Fish Eye Marine Park state "over 200 fish species" in the bay. This figure appears in visitor-oriented sources and has not been independently verified by a scientific species survey in the sources reviewed here. It is included as reported.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Piti Bay, western Guam (Piti Bomb Holes Marine Preserve)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters within the bay
- **Water Temperature**: 28–29 degrees C
- **Current**: Generally calm inside the bay
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The Piti Bomb Holes Marine Preserve contains two mollusk species and one sea urchin species endemic to this location. Visitor-oriented sources report over 200 fish species and abundant hard and soft coral within the protected bay. The marine preserve status has allowed fish populations to grow accustomed to divers. A resident spinner dolphin population is found offshore, and Fish Eye offers dolphin watching cruises.

Specific confirmed species from source research: colorful reef fish including butterflyfish, parrotfish, damselfish, and wrasses are typical of protected Indo-Pacific reef sites at this depth, but have not been independently catalogued in the sources reviewed. Regional marine life applies for species-level detail beyond the endemic invertebrates.

## Dive Profile

Access via the sea bridge walkway from the Fish Eye Marine Park visitor centre. The diving area surrounds the observatory structure at 3–10 meters depth. The shallow depth and calm conditions allow extended bottom time. The percolation pits (the "bomb holes") are the signature feature — explore these natural freshwater seep formations for the unique thermocline effects where fresh and salt water mix.

## Entry and Exit

Walk the 300-meter sea bridge to the observatory. Enter the water adjacent to the observatory platform. Exit at the same point. Guided snorkel tours are also available through Fish Eye Marine Park.

## Safety Considerations

An extremely gentle dive site. Calm protected bay, shallow depth, and well-maintained access infrastructure. Be aware of snorkelers and observatory visitors sharing the water. The site is a marine preserve — touching, collecting, or disturbing marine life or coral is prohibited.
