# Tokai Maru

A WWII Japanese passenger-cargo ship, 134 meters (440 feet) long and 8,365 GRT, torpedoed and sunk in Apra Harbor on 27 August 1943 by USS Snapper. Lies at approximately 37 meters (120 feet) on her port side, immediately adjacent to the WWI German raider SMS Cormoran — divers can position themselves between the two hulls and reach toward both World Wars simultaneously.

## Overview

The Tokai Maru was built in 1930 by Mitsubishi Zosen K.K. at the Nagasaki Shipyard for Osaka Shosen K.K. (now Mitsui O.S.K. Lines), originally operating as a fast luxury passenger-cargo vessel on the Tokyo–New York City service via the Panama Canal. She measured 134 meters (440 feet) long with a beam of 18 meters (60 feet) and a gross registered tonnage of 8,365 tons.

During World War II she was requisitioned by the Imperial Japanese Navy as a military transport. Her sinking came in stages:

- **26 January 1943**: USS Flying Fish (SS-229) hit her with two torpedoes in Apra Harbor, damaging but not sinking her.
- **5 May 1943**: USS Permit (SS-178) scored one further torpedo hit.
- **27 August 1943**: USS Snapper (SS-185) delivered two hits to her port side engine room, finally sinking her.

She came to rest on her port side at approximately 36–37 meters (120 feet), at an 85-degree list, immediately adjacent to the already-sunken SMS Cormoran. Torpedo and strafing damage is visible on the bow and in the No. 4 hold.

**Note on dimensions**: The X-Ray Mag article cites length as 134 meters, consistent with 440 feet. The existing description of 139 meters was incorrect — corrected to 134 meters (440 feet) per multiple sources.

**Note on contact point**: A first-hand dive report (Lakwatsero) and MDA documentation describe the two wrecks as separated by a small gap (approximately 2 meters) — divers can reach toward both wrecks simultaneously from this gap but the hulls do not physically touch in all accounts. Some promotional materials describe them as "touching." The critical experience is positioning between the hulls; the exact degree of physical contact is unresolved.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Apra Harbor, Guam
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 37 meters
- **Depth Range**: ~18–37 meters (60–120 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 feet (8–12 meters); harbor visibility is lower than open-ocean sites
- **Water Temperature**: ~26–29 degrees C
- **Note**: Unexploded ordnance may be present — do not touch or disturb any military artifacts
- **Current**: Generally mild inside the harbor
- **Best Time**: Year-round

No site-specific marine life survey data was found for this wreck. The hull carries coral and sponge encrustation typical of long-submerged steel structures in warm tropical water. Regional marine life applies; no site-specific species reports found.

## Dive Profile

The Tokai Maru's 134-meter length cannot be fully explored on a single dive. Plan to focus each dive on one section: bow area (with torpedo damage visible), mid-ship (where the two-wars experience is accessible), or stern. Descend from the shared mooring buoy with the SMS Cormoran. Deepest sections reach approximately 37 meters — plan deepest penetration first and work shallower as gas reduces. The point between the two wrecks where both can be reached is at approximately 30 meters. Nitrox is advised by MDA to extend bottom time.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only from Apra Harbor operators. Shared mooring buoy with SMS Cormoran provides the descent line. Always deploy an SMB during ascent due to harbor boat traffic.

## Safety Considerations

At 37 meters with harbor visibility under 12 meters, this is a challenging deep wreck dive. Gas management is critical — plan conservatively. Penetration requires wreck certification, a reel, and guideline deployment. The wreck is deteriorating with unstable hull sections — maintain excellent buoyancy. Nitrogen narcosis is significant at depth. Unexploded ordnance has been reported in Apra Harbor — do not touch or disturb any military artifacts. Always deploy SMB during ascent.
