# Tokai Maru

A WWII Japanese passenger-freighter, 134 meters long (440 feet), sunk by USS Snapper on 27 August 1943 in Apra Harbor adjacent to the WWI German raider SMS Cormoran. Together they form the only confirmed dive site in the world where a WWI and a WWII wreck touch each other on the ocean floor.

## Overview

The Tokai Maru was built in 1930 by Mitsubishi Heavy Industries and launched 16 May 1930 for Osaka Shosen Co. (now Mitsui O.S.K. Lines), originally designed for fast passenger-cargo service on the Tokyo–New York City route through the Panama Canal. She is 134 meters (440 feet) long and 18 meters (60 feet) wide. After Japan entered World War II, the vessel was requisitioned by the Imperial Japanese Navy as a military transport.

On 24 January 1943, USS Flying Fish attacked the Tokai Maru moored in Apra Harbor, scoring one torpedo hit that damaged but did not sink her. On 27 August 1943, USS Snapper fired four torpedoes; three struck the vessel and sank her. One torpedo struck the port side #3 cargo hold; the Tokai Maru came to rest on her port side at approximately 36–37 meters (120 feet), settling directly on top of the previously sunken SMS Cormoran with a small section of the two hulls in contact.

The wreck was listed on the U.S. National Register of Historic Places on 14 July 1988. **Safety note**: Four unexploded depth charges remain in the stern area of the wreck. Do not attempt to touch or move them.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Apra Harbor, Guam
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 37 meters
- **Depth Range**: 12–37 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–12 meters (25–40 feet) — variable with tide and harbor traffic
- **Water Temperature**: 28–29 degrees C
- **Current**: Generally mild inside the harbor
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The massive hull structure has become a significant artificial reef. No site-specific marine life lists have been confirmed in sources reviewed for this wreck alone; the site shares its marine community with the adjacent SMS Cormoran. The wreck complex is reported to attract sharks, turtles, and typical reef fish species as general Apra Harbor fauna.

## Dive Profile

Descend the shared mooring line to the wreck complex. The Tokai Maru's 134-meter length means a single dive cannot cover the entire vessel — focus on either the bow or stern section plus the two-wars contact point. Begin at depth (the keel at ~37 meters) while gas supply is full, then work shallower along the hull. The upper forward bridge at ~12 meters provides a long, productive safety stop. The contact point between the Tokai Maru and SMS Cormoran — where divers can touch both wrecks simultaneously — is in the mid-section at approximately 30 meters (95 feet). Four intact engines with catwalks are accessible in the engine room; torpedo and strafing damage is visible on the bow and in hold #4. Bed frames and truck frames remain in the cargo holds. Do not approach or touch the depth charges in the stern. Bottom time at 37 meters is severely limited on air; 32% nitrox is widely used here.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Apra Harbor operators. The shared mooring buoy with the Cormoran provides the descent line. Entry by giant stride. The harbor provides calm surface conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The two-World-Wars contact point is the signature experience — coordinate with your buddy for the photograph
- At 134 meters, this wreck requires at least two dives to cover both bow and stern sections
- Visibility in Apra Harbor averages 8–12 meters — carry a powerful torch for the cargo holds
- The torpedo hole in hold #4 is a photogenic penetration point for wreck-certified divers
- **Do not approach the depth charges in the stern** — they remain potentially active

## Safety Considerations

At 37 meters maximum depth with harbor visibility of 8–12 meters, this is a challenging deep wreck dive. Gas management is critical — plan conservatively. The wreck structure is deteriorating; avoid contact with hull plating. Interior penetration is for wreck-certified divers only. Four unexploded depth charges are present in the stern — maintain distance. The harbor location means boat traffic overhead — always deploy an SMB during ascent.
