# Hap's Reef

A celebrated fish-watching reef in Agat Bay — an upraised coral mound surrounded by sand flats, with depths ranging from 20 to 65 feet. Consistently praised for its exceptional density and variety of reef fish, with crevices, anemones, lionfish, octopus, pufferfish, butterflyfish, trumpetfish, soldierfish, and squirrelfish. One of Guam's most productive macro photography sites. Frequently dived as a second, shallower dive after Blue Hole.

## Overview

Hap's Reef is located in Agat Bay on Guam's southwest coast, accessible by boat. The reef is an upraised coral mound surrounded by flat sandy terrain, with the reef crest at approximately 20–30 feet and the surrounding sand flats dropping to 40–65 feet. The site is consistently described as one of Guam's easiest and most bountiful dives due to the exceptional abundance and variety of reef fish, excellent visibility (60–75 feet), and slight or absent currents.

The reef is particularly noted as a fish-watching destination rather than a topography dive — the mound structure, its crevices, and the surrounding sand host a remarkable cross-section of the small reef fish species found across the island. Dive operators frequently pair it with the deeper Blue Hole as a productive, relaxed second dive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agat Bay, southwest Guam
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Reef Crest**: ~20–30 feet (6–9 meters)
- **Sand Flat Depth**: ~40–65 feet (12–20 meters)
- **Typical Visibility**: 60–75 feet
- **Water Temperature**: 28–29°C
- **Current**: Slight
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Lionfish are present in the reef crevices. Squirrelfish shelter under ledges. Trumpetfish patrol in the water column. Soldierfish cluster in shaded areas. Butterflyfish of multiple species are abundant on the coral. Pufferfish are regularly seen. Octopus inhabit the reef crevices and surrounding sand. Anemones with resident clownfish are established on the mound. Almost all small reef fish species found on Guam have been recorded here.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the coral mound at approximately 20–30 feet. Circle the mound slowly, looking into crevices for lionfish, squirrelfish, and octopus. The sandy valleys surrounding the mound are worth scanning — sand-dwelling species and octopus venture onto the flat. The reef crevices reward patient, slow observation. Depth stays moderate throughout, allowing generous bottom time. The site is compact enough that multiple passes around the mound in a single dive are common.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from local operators. Entry by giant stride. The site is accessible from Agat Marina or by kayak from shore for experienced paddlers.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Slow down — the fish life here rewards patience and careful observation rather than covering distance
- Bring a macro lens: lionfish, squirrelfish, clownfish, and octopus are photogenic and approachable
- An excellent second dive after Blue Hole — the shallow depth helps off-gassing after the deep wall dive
- The sandy valleys surrounding the mound sometimes reveal sand-dwelling species on an outward foray

## Safety Considerations

A relaxed dive with minimal hazards. Currents are slight. The shallow depth keeps the dive well within recreational limits. Lionfish are present — do not touch. Maintain neutral buoyancy over the coral mound.
