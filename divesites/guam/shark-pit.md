# Shark Pit

An unusual dive along Orote Peninsula featuring a pinnacle that rises from approximately 30 meters (100 feet) to just 3–4 meters (12 feet) below the surface. WWII artifacts — tanks, trucks, and trailers driven off the cliff — are scattered around the base and are now coral-encrusted. Gorgonian sea fans grow at just 12 meters (40 feet), rare for Guam. Sea turtles are seen on most dives. Moderate currents; best for intermediate divers.

## Overview

Shark Pit is located along Orote Peninsula near the southern entrance to Apra Harbor. The site takes its name partly from its shape when viewed from above and partly because reef sharks are occasionally encountered, though sea turtles are the more reliable large-animal sighting.

The main feature is a pinnacle that rises from the sandy bottom at approximately 30 meters (100 feet) to within 3–4 meters (12 feet) of the surface. Gorgonian sea fans grow at approximately 12 meters (40 feet) — notably shallow for Guam waters, where sea fans typically occur at greater depth. WWII artifacts are documented at the site: tanks, trucks, and trailers that were driven off the cliff during or after World War II now lie scattered around the base of the pinnacle, encrusted in coral growth. Visibility is typically 20–30 meters.

Asia Dive Site describes the site as "particularly suitable for novices," but moderate currents at Orote Peninsula and the 30-meter base depth make this more appropriate for intermediate divers, consistent with the existing classification.

Earlier claims in this file that Shark Pit is "named for its turtle-like shape" and that "pilot whales have been reported" have not been confirmed by primary sources reviewed and have been removed. The WWII artifact details (tanks, trucks, trailers) are confirmed by Asia Dive Site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Orote Peninsula, Guam
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Depth Range**: 3–30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 28–29 degrees C
- **Current**: Moderate; variable at Orote Peninsula
- **Best Time**: December–April (calmer conditions)

## Marine Life

Sea turtles are observed on most dives. Gorgonian sea fans at 12 meters are a highlight — unusually shallow for Guam. Schools of barracuda and occasional reef sharks are reported in the area. The pinnacle surfaces provide macro photography opportunities. Fish life is described as plentiful in the surrounding reef.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the base of the pinnacle at approximately 30 meters. Observe the WWII artifacts (coral-encrusted tanks, trucks, and trailers) scattered at depth. Ascend along the pinnacle walls, observing the gorgonian sea fans at 12 meters. The pinnacle peak at 3–4 meters provides an extended, productive safety stop area. Turtles can be encountered at any depth along the pinnacle.

## Entry and Exit

Primarily by boat. Shore access from Naval Base Guam is technically possible but requires base access credentials. Entry by giant stride.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The gorgonian sea fans at 12 meters are a rare find at this depth on Guam — wide-angle lens recommended
- Look for the WWII artifacts (tanks, trucks, trailers) at the base of the pinnacle — they are coral-covered and easy to miss
- The pinnacle top at 3–4 meters makes an excellent extended safety stop with fish life to observe
- Follow your dive guide's assessment of current conditions before descending

## Safety Considerations

Currents at Orote Peninsula can be moderate and variable — do not exceed your planned bottom time at depth. Monitor depth when exploring the base of the pinnacle at 30 meters. The WWII artifacts are historically significant; observe but do not touch or disturb. Always deploy an SMB during ascent.
