# Shark Pit

An unusual dive along Orote Peninsula featuring a large haystack pinnacle rising from approximately 27–30 meters (90 feet) up to 4–5 meters (12–15 feet) below the surface, gorgonian sea fans at 12 meters, WWII artifacts scattered on the slope, and resident sea turtles. A popular drift dive with excellent visibility.

## Overview

Shark Pit is located along Orote Peninsula, in the same area as the Blue Hole and Barracuda Rock dives. MDA Guam, PADI, and SSI all describe it as "a very unusual dive with many artifacts from WWII." The steep slope surrounding the pinnacle is described as "littered with items dumped during the war," though the exact nature of these artifacts (military equipment, ordnance, vehicles) is not specified in the sources consulted; no source specifically confirms tanks or military vehicles at this site.

The site's defining feature is a large "haystack rock" pinnacle that rises from approximately 27–30 meters (90–100 feet) of water to a peak at about 4–5 meters (12–15 feet) below the surface. One of the distinguishing features is the presence of gorgonian sea fans at approximately 12 meters (40 feet) — notably shallow for Guam, where sea fans are typically found much deeper on outer walls.

Note on the site name: PADI and MDA identify the official name as "Sharks Pit." The JSON entry uses "Shark Pit" (without the 's'); both forms appear in sources.

Note on maximum depth: The JSON previously listed 20 meters, but PADI cites depths of 40–160 feet (12–49 meters) with the haystack pinnacle base at 90 feet (27 meters). Updated to 27 meters for the primary recreational dive zone.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Orote Peninsula, Guam (same dive corridor as Blue Hole and Barracuda Rock)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef / pinnacle with WWII artifacts
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate (drift conditions variable; depths to 27–30 meters)
- **Maximum Depth**: approximately 27–30 meters (90–100 feet) at pinnacle base
- **Pinnacle Peak**: approximately 4–5 meters (12–15 feet) below surface
- **Sea Fans**: at approximately 12 meters (40 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 18–30 meters (excellent; described as "super-clear water" by operators)
- **Water Temperature**: 28–29°C
- **Current**: Variable; typically a gentle drift
- **Best Time**: Year-round; also described as an excellent night dive

## Marine Life

Confirmed sightings at this specific site per MDA Guam, PADI, and SSI: sea turtles (seen on most dives, per MDA), schools of pyramid butterflyfish, arc-eyed hawkfish, and velvet hawkfish posing for photographers on the pinnacle. Gorgonian sea fans at 12 meters are a confirmed feature.

The original description mentioned "pilot whales" and "reef sharks" at this site — no source specifically confirms pilot whales at Shark Pit (Guam overall has had pelagic marine mammal sightings offshore, but these were not attributed to this site in sources consulted). Pilot whale references have been removed. The PADI listing for this site lists "sharks, whales, dolphins, turtles" as common sightings — this appears to be a generic regional Guam fauna list, not site-specific confirmation.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the pinnacle and begin exploring at the deeper sections (27–30 meters) where the WWII artifacts are scattered on the slope. Work upward along the pinnacle walls, observing the gorgonian sea fans at 12 meters, before ascending to the pinnacle peak for a safety stop. Sea turtles are encountered at various depths throughout the dive. The pinnacle top at 4–5 meters provides an ideal extended safety stop area. The gentle drift conditions along Orote Peninsula typically carry divers along the slope.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Agat or Cabras Marina area operators. Entry by giant stride. The drift conditions usually result in a boat pickup at the surface after the dive.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The sea fans at 12 meters are the shallowest on Guam — bring a wide-angle lens
- Slow down around the pinnacle base for macro subjects on hawkfish and other invertebrates
- Excellent night dive site per local operators — nocturnal species are more active after dark
- The WWII artifacts on the slope should be observed but not touched or disturbed; some may be hazardous

## Safety Considerations

Currents at Orote Peninsula are variable — always follow the dive guide's assessment. The pinnacle drops to 27–30 meters at the base; monitor depth when exploring the deeper sections. WWII artifacts may include ordnance — maintain a safe distance from any suspicious objects and do not touch them. Deploy a surface marker buoy for boat pickup.
