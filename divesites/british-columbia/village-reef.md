## Village Reef

Village Reef is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Village Reef is a boat dive site in Barkley Sound, positioned in the heart of one of BC's premier cold-water diving regions on the west coast of Vancouver Island. The reef supports the characteristic fauna of outer Barkley Sound — giant Pacific octopus, wolf eels, multiple rockfish species, lingcod, sea lions, and dense invertebrate communities of anemones, nudibranchs, and sea stars that thrive in the Pacific-influenced, nutrient-rich waters. Divers should plan for cold water requiring a drysuit and should check tidal and swell conditions before departing.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Barkley Sound reefs support giant Pacific octopus, wolf eels, lingcod, multiple rockfish species (tiger, china, quillback, copper), and cabezon. Invertebrate communities on the rocky substrate include giant plumose anemones, strawberry anemones, nudibranchs, orange-peel doris, sea cucumbers, sea pens, and encrusting sponges. Ratfish are encountered at depth. Harbour seals and Steller sea lions frequent the outer sound. Humpback whales and harbour porpoises are increasingly present in Barkley Sound.

## Dive Profile

Boat dive at 18 m maximum depth on a Pacific-influenced rocky reef in Barkley Sound. The reef terrain supports bull kelp in shallower zones giving way to dense invertebrate growth at depth. The nutrient-rich, current-swept environment supports more diverse life than sheltered east-coast Vancouver Island sites of equivalent depth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only from Bamfield or Ucluelet. Live-boat support is standard given the exposure of outer Barkley Sound. Local charter operators manage entry angles and current timing; independent navigation of the outer sound is not advisable without local knowledge.

## Tips and Recommendations

Visibility is best fall and winter (30+ m at depth); spring plankton blooms reduce near-surface clarity but improve significantly below 9 m. Water temperature 8–10 °C year-round — drysuit required. Barkley Sound operators run regular liveaboard and day-charter trips from Bamfield and Ucluelet; booking through them is the most reliable access route.

## Safety Considerations

Outer Barkley Sound is exposed to Pacific swells and weather can change quickly. Drysuit is non-negotiable. Always use a surface marker buoy on ascent; live-boat pickup is expected at all outer-sound sites.
