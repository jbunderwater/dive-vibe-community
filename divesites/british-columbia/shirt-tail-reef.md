## Shirt Tail Reef

Shirt Tail Reef is a rocky reef dive site in the southern Gulf Islands area of British Columbia, Canada, sitting in the tidal channels of the Active Pass approaches.

## Overview

Shirt Tail Reef lies in the complex of reefs and shoals east of the Gulf Islands in channels leading toward Active Pass, one of BC's most tidal-influenced marine corridors. These channels are among the most productive in the southern BC coast, with tidal mixing delivering nutrients that support outstanding underwater life. Water temperature is 7–12°C, and visibility typically 6–12 metres. The reef is subject to moderate tidal flows, making timing important. The unusual name likely refers to the elongated shape of the reef when viewed on a navigation chart.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Gulf Islands channels, British Columbia, Canada
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Rocky reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 6–12 metres
- **Current**: Moderate; approach at or near slack
- **Best Time**: July to October

## Marine Life

The Active Pass region is famous among BC divers for its lingcod populations, and Shirt Tail Reef reflects this reputation with resident large lingcod on the rocky structure. Copper rockfish, quillback rockfish, and kelp greenling are numerous. Dense plumose anemone colonies grow on exposed faces. Giant Pacific octopus maintain dens in the deeper boulder zones. Nudibranchs are diverse and prolific — this part of the Gulf Islands is a well-documented nudibranch hotspot. Harbour seals are constant companions.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef base and work along the structure at 12–18 metres, checking crevices and boulder piles for GPO and wolf eels. Spend significant time in the 8–12 metre zone where rockfish and lingcod concentrations are highest. Finish in the shallower kelp canopy for nudibranchs and safety stop. Total dive time 40–50 minutes at this depth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Sidney or Mayne Island nearby. Caution around Active Pass approaches — commercial ferry traffic is heavy in these channels and vessel wash is significant. Stay well clear of the BC Ferries route and deploy SMBs conspicuously on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Active Pass ferry traffic creates wake — anchor well clear of the navigational lanes
- Nudibranch diversity is outstanding — bring a macro lens or dual-system camera
- Lingcod tend to be large and bold at this site; move slowly for close approaches
- Late summer into autumn is optimal for visibility after summer plankton peaks

## Safety Considerations

The Active Pass approaches see very heavy commercial and recreational vessel traffic. Surface safety is the critical consideration — always use a brightly coloured SMB and look around carefully before fully surfacing. Dive with an experienced boatperson who is familiar with the local traffic patterns.

## Photography

Nudibranchs are the standout macro subjects in this area — bring your best macro setup. Lingcod portraits are excellent — the large resident animals sit boldly. Wide-angle anemone wall images on the current-swept sides of the reef are highly rewarding.
