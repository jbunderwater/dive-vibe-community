## Pope Rocks

Pope Rocks is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Pope Rocks is a reef site in Barkley Sound on the west coast of Vancouver Island, part of a Pacific Rim National Park Reserve region that National Geographic has recognised as one of the best cold-water dive destinations in the world. The open Pacific influence brings a distinct biological character — species including white-spotted rose anemones, giant mussels, sea otters, and large lingcod are commonly found alongside the wolf eels and giant Pacific octopus typical of inner BC waters. Rockfish diversity is exceptional here, with china, copper, yellowtail, and quillback rockfish all regularly encountered on the reef structure to 18 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America

## Marine Life

Barkley Sound's open-Pacific exposure delivers a biological character distinct from inner BC waters. Giant Pacific octopus, wolf eels, lingcod, and multiple rockfish species (china, copper, yellowtail, quillback) are the main fish highlights. White-spotted rose anemones and giant plumose anemones coat the reef structure alongside giant mussels and diverse nudibranchs including the orange-peel nudibranch and clown dorid. Sea otters have been reintroduced to the sound and are occasionally sighted. Puget Sound king crabs inhabit deeper sections, and sixgill sharks have been reported in the broader Barkley Sound area at depth.

## Dive Profile

The reef structure at Pope Rocks descends to around 18 metres, with the main invertebrate-encrusted rock faces distributed across the mid-range depths. Upwelling cold Pacific water delivers strong visibility — winter visibility in Barkley Sound regularly exceeds 30 metres, while summer algae blooms near the surface can reduce it to 10 metres. Water temperatures range from 8–10°C in winter to around 12–15°C at depth in summer.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only from Bamfield or Ucluelet-based charters. No shore access is available. Pope Rocks sits within the Pacific Rim National Park Reserve area; all diving must be conducted with respect for reserve regulations.

## Tips and Recommendations

Dive with a local Bamfield or Ucluelet charter operator who knows the tidal patterns in Barkley Sound, as currents can be strong around exposed rocky features. Winter months offer the best visibility; June–September brings the calmest sea states. The open-Pacific biological character makes Barkley Sound notably different from inner Strait of Georgia sites — expect more anemone diversity and a greater chance of pelagic encounters.

## Safety Considerations

Cold water (8–12°C) requires a dry suit or at minimum a 7 mm wetsuit with hood and gloves. Currents around exposed points in Barkley Sound can be substantial — time dives to slack water and use a surface marker buoy for ascent. Sea state can deteriorate rapidly on the outer coast; monitor conditions and have a weather contingency plan.
