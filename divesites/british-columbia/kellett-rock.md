# Kellett Rock

A remote submerged rock near Port San Juan at the western entrance to the Strait of Juan de Fuca.

## Overview

Kellett Rock is located near Port San Juan (Port Renfrew) at the western end of Vancouver Island's south coast, near the entrance of the Strait of Juan de Fuca from the open Pacific. This remote site sees the upwelling effects of the open ocean and experiences stronger swell and current than more sheltered locations. The rock offers productive diving in less-visited waters, with access from Port Renfrew or by liveaboard from Victoria. Water temperatures are 7–11°C, and visibility can reach 15–20 metres on good days with Pacific upwelling.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Port Renfrew, western Strait of Juan de Fuca, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–20 metres
- **Current**: Strong tidal currents and ocean swell — conditions-dependent site
- **Best Time**: Late summer to early autumn for calm conditions

## Marine Life

The ocean upwelling near Port Renfrew supports exceptional marine life. Giant Pacific octopus are well-established in rocky terrain of this type. Lingcod, cabezon, and tiger rockfish are all present and are typically large in these less-visited waters. Plumose anemones and encrusting sponges cover exposed surfaces. The rock's position near the open Pacific means occasional visits from Steller sea lions hunting in the area. Wolf eels inhabit deep crevices in the rock structure.

## Dive Profile

The rock rises to approximately 8–10 metres, with the perimeter dropping to 18 metres. Conditions at this site are highly variable — only dive when swell and current are minimal. At slack tide in calm conditions, work the rock's perimeter systematically. Current at this site can be strong — plan conservatively and begin ascending well before predicted current onset.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Port Renfrew harbour. Port Renfrew is a small community accessed by the West Coast Road from Victoria (approximately 2 hours). Liveaboard operators from Victoria occasionally include this area. The site is exposed to Pacific swell — conditions must be carefully assessed before departure. Do not attempt in swells above 1 metre or with developing weather systems.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Port Renfrew is a rapidly developing destination — check for current dive services in the area
- The Juan de Fuca Provincial Park area provides excellent above-water hiking along the Juan de Fuca Marine Trail
- Pacific upwelling in late summer and autumn can produce exceptional visibility in these waters
- This site rewards experienced divers with patience for waiting for ideal conditions

## Safety Considerations

Open Pacific exposure makes this site weather and swell dependent. Strong currents and significant swell require experienced boat handling. Only attempt this site with an operator familiar with west coast conditions. Cold water (7–11°C) demands drysuit proficiency and appropriate undergarment layering. Maintain strict current timing discipline.

## Photography

The open Pacific influence produces exceptional water clarity when upwelling conditions prevail. Wide-angle photography of the rock structure with large lingcod and encrusting life is outstanding. Less-dived sites like Kellett Rock often produce more natural animal behaviours — octopus in the open, lingcod hunting, and rockfish in undisturbed schools. Bring a full photography kit for this special site.
