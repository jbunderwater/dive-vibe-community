## Surprise Reef

Surprise Reef is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Surprise Reef is a reef site off the west coast of Vancouver Island in the vicinity of Barkley Sound, an area Jacques Cousteau once recognised as one of the world's top dive destinations due to its open Pacific biology and exceptional species diversity. The reef structure to 18 metres supports colorful encrusting invertebrates rarely seen in abundance on the calmer inner coast, including bryozoans, white-spotted rose anemones, and giant mussels alongside wolf eels, lingcod, and giant Pacific octopus. Sea lions, rockfish of multiple species, and the occasional sixgill shark are all possible encounters in these productive Pacific-influenced waters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Barkley Sound reef sites at this depth are known to support giant Pacific octopus, wolf eels in rocky crevices, lingcod on flat ledges, and multiple rockfish species including tiger rockfish, quillback rockfish, and China rockfish. Encrusting invertebrates typical of the outer sound include white-spotted rose anemones, fish-eating anemones (bright orange), neon yellow and other nudibranch species, and sun stars. Bull kelp forests provide habitat for smaller fish species. Sea lions and harbour seals are possible encounters in these productive Pacific-influenced waters. Ratfish are commonly encountered in Barkley Sound at these depths.

## Dive Profile

The reef structure descends to approximately 18 metres on a rocky substrate typical of outer Barkley Sound. Visibility in the outer sound is generally excellent (15–20+ metres) outside spring plankton bloom periods, though initial plankton layers in spring may clear quickly below the halocline. The site is exposed to Pacific swell and requires boat access and stable weather conditions.

## Entry and Exit

Boat-only access. Charter operators from Port Alberni (Rendezvous Dive Adventures) and Ucluelet provide the primary access to Barkley Sound reef sites. No shore access. Divers should confirm precise site coordinates with charter operators as "Surprise Reef" may be a locally-used name.

## Safety Considerations

- The outer Barkley Sound is exposed to Pacific Ocean swell — conditions can change rapidly.
- Drysuit essential year-round; water temperatures are 7–11°C.
- Tidal conditions in the sound influence current strength at exposed reefs; plan dives around slack.
- Remote location: ensure adequate surface support and emergency procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book through an established Barkley Sound operator (Rendezvous Dive Adventures, Olivia's Reef Dive Adventures) for accurate site navigation and tidal timing.
- Barkley Sound reef diving is generally combined with other nearby sites such as Kyen Point, Renate Reef, and Chup Point in multi-day liveaboard or lodge-based trips.
- Bring macro and wide-angle lenses: both nudibranch macro and rockfish / kelp forest wide-angle shots are productive.
