## Verbeke Reef

Verbeke Reef is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Verbeke Reef is a rocky reef dive site in Barkley Sound on the west coast of Vancouver Island, in a region where the open Pacific Ocean directly influences biology and produces some of BC's most diverse marine life. Divers regularly encounter giant Pacific octopus, wolf eels, lingcod, sea lions, and a variety of rockfish species, along with dense invertebrate growth including staghorn bryozoans, plumose anemones, and nudibranchs rarely found on the sheltered east coast of the island. The site is accessed by boat and requires a drysuit given Barkley Sound's cold Pacific water temperatures.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Barkley Sound reefs consistently host giant Pacific octopus, wolf eels (resident individuals up to 1.5 m), lingcod, tiger rockfish, china rockfish, quillback rockfish, and cabezon. Dense invertebrate growth on rocky substrates includes giant plumose anemones (white, nearly 60 cm tall), strawberry anemones, nudibranchs (orange-peel, alabaster, giant red), sea cucumbers, and sea pens. Ratfish and six-gill sharks are occasional visitors in deeper water. Harbour seals and Steller sea lions frequent the outer sound.

## Dive Profile

Boat dive in Barkley Sound's outer reaches where Pacific swells and cold upwelling produce exceptional nutrient-rich conditions. Maximum depth 18 m. The rocky reef terrain supports kelp forest in the upper zone giving way to encrusting invertebrate growth at depth. Extended bottom time is feasible given the relatively shallow profile.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only; the site lies within outer Barkley Sound and requires a charter from Bamfield or Ucluelet. Live-boat support is standard practice in these waters due to exposure and variable surface conditions. No shore access.

## Tips and Recommendations

Barkley Sound is a year-round destination, though summer and fall typically offer the calmest above-water conditions. Visibility is best in fall and winter — 30+ m below 9 m depth; spring plankton blooms may reduce near-surface clarity to 10 m but improve below that depth. Water temperature 8–10 °C at depth year-round; drysuit essential. Book through experienced Barkley Sound operators (Rendezvous Dive Adventures, Ucluelet dive charters) who know individual site entry angles and current patterns.

## Safety Considerations

Open Pacific exposure makes surface conditions a significant variable — swell can develop quickly. Cold water (8–10 °C) demands a properly fitted drysuit. Use a surface marker buoy when ascending; live-boat pickup is standard for this region.
