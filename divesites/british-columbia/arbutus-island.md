## Arbutus Island

Arbutus Island is a reef dive in the Gulf Islands of British Columbia, Canada.

## Overview

Arbutus Island sits in the northern Gulf Islands between Saltspring Island and the Saanich Peninsula, taking its name from the Pacific madrone trees — known locally as arbutus — that grow along the rocky shorelines of this region. The underwater terrain mirrors the rugged surface topography, with barnacle-covered rock shelves descending into boulder-strewn slopes and kelp-draped ridges. Maximum depth reaches 18 metres on the outer edges. Water temperature ranges from 8 to 11°C, making a dry suit the practical choice for comfortable bottom time. Visibility is typically 8 to 15 metres, with the best clarity in the summer months when plankton blooms subside.

## Marine Life

Arbutus Island's reef is home to the characteristic species of the inner Gulf Islands. Kelp greenling and copper rockfish patrol the rock edges and mid-water column. Lingcod are present year-round and become particularly visible in winter and early spring during spawning season, when males guard egg masses on exposed rock. Giant Pacific octopus use boulder crevices as dens; look for debris piles outside den entrances as telltale signs. Purple and red sea urchins cover many rock surfaces, and the encrusting fauna includes sponges, hydroids, and bright pink coralline algae. Nudibranchs such as the opalescent and sea lemon species are common.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the anchored boat to the reef crest and then work down the sloped edges to the maximum depth. The most productive zone for fish and invertebrates lies between 8 and 15 metres, where the rock faces are most varied. Work back shallower across the reef top, pausing in the kelp zone before beginning your ascent. A safety stop at 5 metres completes the dive.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. Anchor well away from delicate reef areas or use established mooring if available. Keep watch for other vessel traffic in the Gulf Islands, which can be considerable in summer. Deploy your SMB 5 to 6 metres before reaching the surface.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Dive at or near slack water for comfortable conditions
- Scan along kelp holdfasts for nudibranchs and small crustaceans
- Lingcod are most accessible in winter — dress for colder conditions in the 7-9°C range
- A dive light helps reveal colour in crevices and under ledges
- Check current predictions for the region before diving

## Safety Considerations

Boat traffic in the Gulf Islands is frequent, particularly on summer weekends. Ascend with a fully deployed SMB to ensure visibility from the surface. Tidal currents can build quickly — always plan dives for slack and be prepared to surface if conditions worsen. Cold water demands dry suit proficiency; ensure buoyancy control is solid before the dive.

## Photography

The reef and kelp combination at Arbutus Island supports both wide-angle and macro work. Rocky slopes colonised by sponges and anemones make compelling wide-angle subjects, while the sea floor and rock crevices yield nudibranchs and small invertebrates for macro lenses. Natural light quality is best from late spring through early autumn.
