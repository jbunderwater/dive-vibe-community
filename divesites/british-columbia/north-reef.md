## North Reef

North Reef is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

North Reef is a rocky reef site in the northern Gulf Islands near Sansum Narrows and Saltspring Island, an area well regarded by local divers for its cold, clear water and rich marine life. Sansum Narrows is one of the productive tidal passages of the Gulf Islands where currents deliver a steady supply of plankton to sessile filter feeders like plumose anemones and sponges, and rockfish, lingcod, and giant Pacific octopus make regular appearances along the reef. The site is accessed by boat and is best dived during the slacker phases of the tidal cycle.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Sansum Narrows and its surrounding passages are recognised as excellent Gulf Islands dive areas where tidal currents deliver plankton to dense communities of filter feeders. Plumose anemones and sponges colonise the rocky surfaces. Rockfish and lingcod occupy ledges and overhangs. Giant Pacific octopus are found in crevices throughout the reef. Mussels, rock scallops, and sea urchins appear on the rocky structure. Harbor seals are a regular presence in this area of the Gulf Islands.

## Dive Profile

North Reef is a rocky reef near Sansum Narrows and Saltspring Island, in a passage connecting Satellite Channel to the Strait of Georgia. Maximum depth reaches approximately 18 metres. Sansum Narrows is documented as one of the productive tidal dive passages in the Gulf Islands; DIVE BC lists it as a good boat dive accessible from Maple Bay or Cowichan Bay. Current-fed conditions make slack-water timing important.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only, typically from Maple Bay or Cowichan Bay on Vancouver Island. Local operators running charters to this area include dive shops in the Cowichan Valley region.

## Tips and Recommendations

Sansum Narrows experiences meaningful tidal current; plan dives around the slack using Canadian Hydrographic Service tables for the Sansum Narrows reference station. The site is a recognized drift dive option when current is running — experienced divers comfortable with drift may choose to ride the current along the reef. Best visibility is in winter and early spring.

## Safety Considerations

Water temperature is 7–12°C year-round — drysuit or 7 mm wetsuit with hood required. Currents through Sansum Narrows can be significant; assess conditions on descent and abort if flow is too strong for comfortable diving. Always ascend with an SMB in these boat-traffic waters.
