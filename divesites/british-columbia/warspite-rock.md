## Warspite Rock

Warspite Rock is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Warspite Rock is a reef dive site in the Discovery Passage region near Campbell River, one of the world's most productive cold-water dive areas due to its extreme tidal exchanges and resulting nutrient-rich currents. The rocky structure is decorated with the colourful invertebrate life characteristic of the passage — strawberry anemones, sponges, hydroids, and tubeworms — while crevices shelter lingcod, tiger rockfish, red Irish lord sculpins, wolf eels, and giant Pacific octopus. Diving is limited to slack tide windows and is best arranged through local Campbell River charter operators.

## Marine Life

Discovery Passage's extreme tidal exchanges create cold, oxygen-rich water that supports exceptionally dense invertebrate communities. Rocky structure in this region is carpeted with strawberry anemones, plumose anemones, cup corals, sulphur sponges, finger sponges, feather stars, tubeworms, and green and purple sea urchins — all competing for position in the current stream. Crevices shelter wolf eels, tiger rockfish, red Irish lord sculpins, lingcod, and giant Pacific octopus. Kelp greenlings and cabezon are common in the upper zone.

## Dive Profile

Boat dive at 18 m maximum in the Discovery Passage region near Campbell River. The current-swept rocky terrain produces the characteristic vertical surface coverage that makes this area world-renowned. Dive windows are restricted to slack tide; the actual dive duration at many Discovery Passage sites is limited by the short calm window between tidal flows.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only; access through Campbell River charter operators (Abyssal Diving Charters, Big Animal Encounters, Oceanfix). A knowledgeable skipper is essential — local expertise in reading the tides here is as important as the published tide tables. Live-boat support required.

## Tips and Recommendations

Plan around the published slack tide but arrive early — current can lag or lead the tables. Visibility in Discovery Passage rarely drops below 9 m and is often 15–20 m at depth. Water temperature 8–10 °C year-round. Drysuit required. The Campbell River area offers over a dozen world-class dive sites accessible on the same charter, including the HMCS Columbia wreck.

## Safety Considerations

Tidal currents in Discovery Passage can reach 15 knots at maximum flow — diving outside the slack window is extremely dangerous. A knowledgeable local skipper is essential, not optional. Cold water (8–10 °C) requires a drysuit. Deploy surface marker buoy before ascending so the boat can track your position.
