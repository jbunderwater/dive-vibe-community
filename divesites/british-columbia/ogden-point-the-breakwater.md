## Ogden Point (The Breakwater)

Ogden Point Breakwater is Victoria's most accessible shore dive and is considered one of the best shore dives in Canada, offering excellent access to a unique granite breakwater habitat just minutes from downtown Victoria.

## Overview

Located at 199 Dallas Road in downtown Victoria, the breakwater extends 800 meters into the ocean, creating an ideal marine habitat rich in biodiversity. The site features stacked granite blocks and concrete structures that form a distinctive diving environment unlike typical reef or wreck sites. From newly certified divers to advanced level divers, there are depths and challenges for all skill levels. The site is famous for both its accessibility and the abundance of iconic Pacific species.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Downtown Victoria (199 Dallas Road), British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 8-12°C (cold water diving required)

## Site Characteristics

The breakwater consists of 800 meters of stacked granite blocks and concrete creating:
- Vertical walls and drop-offs
- Crevices and ledges
- Natural habitat structure for marine life
- Multiple entry/exit points
- Progressive depth opportunities for skill levels

Sunken plaques line the breakwater at various depths—a "scavenger hunt" aspect adds interest for divers.

## Marine Life

This site is exceptional for encounters with iconic Pacific species:
- **Wolf eels** (resident, commonly sighted)
- **Giant Pacific octopus** (present year-round, can be elusive)
- **Ling cod** and various rockfish
- **Puget Sound king crab**
- **Swimming scallops**
- **Sea urchins and nudibranchs**
- **Seals** (occasional visits)
- **Kelp** and marine vegetation
- **Decorator crabs and anemones**

## Dive Profile

Multiple profiles possible:
- **Shallow exploration** (5-12m): Scavenger hunt plaques, kelp forest, invertebrates
- **Moderate depth** (12-18m): Wolf eel sightings, rocky structure exploration
- **Deeper sections** (18-25m): Extended bottom time, larger structure complexity
- Typical dive 30-45 minutes depending on profile

All depths and challenges accessible from single entry point.

## Entry and Exit

**Direct Shore Access**
- Granite stepping blocks on left side of breakwater accessed via staircase
- Walk out along granite blocks until bull kelp visible at surface
- Easy entry marks start of dive
- Same location for exit

**Facilities**
- $2 parking (paid lot nearby)
- Picnic tables for gear setup/breakdown
- Stairs directly to water
- Fresh water shower at top of concrete stairway
- Ogden Point Dive Centre onsite with:
  - Emergency equipment
  - Tank fills
  - Post-dive freshwater shower
  - Gear rental/sales

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Easy, popular site**: Expect other divers, especially weekends
- **Visibility**: Best in winter and early spring; summer can see plankton blooms
- **Iconic species**: Wolf eels are common; photograph ethical—maintain distance
- **Photography**: Excellent wide-angle and macro opportunities
- **Scavenger hunt**: Look for plaques at various depths (historic markers)
- **Multiple dives**: Site structure supports multiple dives with different focuses
- **Beginning divers**: This is a excellent site for freshly certified divers (with guidance)
- **Advanced divers**: Deeper exploration and complex navigation available

## Safety Considerations

This is an Intermediate site suitable for trained recreational divers:
- Cold water (8-12°C) requires proper thermal protection
- Entry/exit via granite blocks requires careful foot placement
- Surge can be present, especially on windy days
- Maintain excellent buoyancy to avoid disturbing bottom silt
- Navigate carefully on uneven structures
- The breakwater is actively used by boat traffic—use appropriate signaling
- Carry surface marker buoy (SMB) for boat awareness
- Always dive with a buddy
- Exit carefully, watching for surge zones near shore
- The site is safe and accessible but cold water remains a factor year-round
