# Louisa Rock

A productive submerged rock in the Saanich Inlet area offering accessible cold-water diving.

## Overview

Louisa Rock sits in the waters around the Saanich Peninsula, in the channels between the peninsula and the Gulf Islands. Saanich Inlet and its surrounding waters are among BC's most accessible and well-dived areas, making sites like Louisa Rock familiar to generations of Victoria-area divers. The rock provides hard-bottom reef habitat in waters known for dense octopus populations and consistent marine life. Water temperatures are 8–11°C and visibility averages 6–12 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Saanich Peninsula area, near Victoria, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Submerged rock / reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 6–12 metres
- **Current**: Moderate tidal currents
- **Best Time**: Late spring through autumn

## Marine Life

Louisa Rock hosts a productive Salish Sea community. Giant Pacific octopus are found in crevices throughout the rock structure — the Victoria and Saanich Inlet area is renowned for its octopus density. Wolf eels are occasionally encountered at this type of rocky site. Lingcod are present as territorial predators. Copper and quillback rockfish inhabit the rock faces. Plumose anemones and encrusting sponges colonise exposed surfaces. Kelp greenlings and painted greenlings are common. Harbour seals are abundant in the area.

## Dive Profile

The rock provides diving from approximately 6–8 metres at the summit to 18 metres at the base. Work the deeper perimeter zone first, exploring crevices for octopus, then ascend to the rock's summit zone for the safety stop. The transition area between rock and sand at the base is particularly productive for large octopus dens.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Brentwood Bay, Sidney, or other Saanich area launches. GPS required. Anchor on sand away from the rock. The protected waters of Saanich Inlet make this a good site in most weather conditions. Deploy SMB before ascending — recreational boat traffic in the area is moderate.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Victoria has a large and active dive community — local knowledge is easily accessible
- The Saanich area has multiple accessible reef sites within a short boat ride
- Saanich Inlet is one of BC's top octopus diving areas — morning dives can be most productive for octopus sightings
- Victoria dive shops regularly run trips to Saanich Inlet and surrounding reef sites

## Safety Considerations

Tidal currents in the Saanich area are moderate and manageable. Cold water (8–11°C) requires drysuit protection. Always deploy SMB before ascending. Maintain buddy contact. Check conditions before departure.

## Photography

Octopus portraiture is the photographic priority at Louisa Rock — the Victoria area's dense octopus population provides exceptional opportunities. Wide-angle shots of octopus on rocky substrate are outstanding. Wolf eel portraits in den entrances are classic Pacific Northwest images. Macro work on encrusting surfaces finds nudibranchs and colonial invertebrates.
