## Beacon Rock

Beacon Rock is a boat-access reef dive in the Strait of Georgia off Nanaimo, British Columbia, Canada.

## Overview

Beacon Rock is a submerged navigational hazard in the Strait of Georgia that has been converted by biology into a prime dive site. The rock is well known to local Nanaimo-area divers and sees regular charter boat traffic from the city's active dive community. Rocky substrate drops from near-surface to 18 metres, with varied terrain of ridges, crevices, and sandy patches at the perimeter. Cold Strait of Georgia water — typically 8 to 11°C — keeps the rock richly colonised. Visibility averages 8 to 12 metres and can improve significantly during summer when conditions are calm.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Strait of Georgia, Nanaimo area, British Columbia, Canada
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8-12 meters (25-40 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 8-11°C (46-52°F) — dry suit recommended
- **Current**: Light to moderate; stronger on tidal exchanges
- **Best Time**: April to October

## Marine Life

Beacon Rock hosts a solid representation of Strait of Georgia temperate marine life. Lingcod are a consistent presence on the rock surface, and giant Pacific octopus use the deeper crevices as dens. Schools of perch hover in the shallower zone, while copper rockfish occupy mid-depth ledges. The rock face is carpeted with plumose anemones in denser sections and colonised by encrusting sponges and bryozoans throughout. Sea stars, including the large sunflower sea star, move across the rock. Nudibranchs are numerous — look for the yellow-striped Janolus and the large white alabaster nudibranch.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the rock base and begin exploring the deeper terrain, checking crevices carefully for GPO dens and lingcod. Work shallower along the rock face, noting the changing community as depth decreases. The reef crest at 5 to 8 metres often has the highest fish density. Complete a safety stop at 5 metres and deploy your SMB before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Nanaimo's waterfront via charter or private vessel. The rock is marked on charts and GPS coordinates are widely shared among local dive operators. Anchor or drift depending on current conditions; ensure the boat provides watch during the dive.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Nanaimo has active dive charter operators that run regular trips to Beacon Rock
- Best conditions are typically on neap tides with lighter currents
- A bright torch reveals colour and detail on the anemone-covered rock face
- Watch for boat traffic on the surface — deploy SMB well before surfacing
- Winter months often produce clearer water in the Strait of Georgia

## Safety Considerations

The Strait of Georgia is a busy commercial shipping route. Be visible at the surface at all times — a brightly coloured SMB is non-negotiable. Current can pick up on tidal exchanges; monitor conditions during the dive and surface with reserve gas. Cold water increases fatigue and air consumption — plan conservative dive profiles.

## Photography

Beacon Rock suits wide-angle shooting of the rock structure and schooling fish. The perch and rockfish communities are photogenic and accustomed to diver presence. Macro opportunities include nudibranchs and small crustaceans on the rock face. Morning dives often have the best ambient light in the Strait.
