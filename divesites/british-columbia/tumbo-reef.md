## Tumbo Reef

Tumbo Reef is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Tumbo Reef lies off the eastern tip of Tumbo Island in the southern Gulf Islands, near Saturna Island and Reef Harbour — an area noted for abalone, unusual invertebrates, and some of the clearest waters in the Strait of Georgia. The reef sits close to the BC-Washington border in a zone frequented by orcas, and divers can expect lingcod, rockfish, Dungeness crabs, sea urchins, and dense anemone growth on the rocky structure. Access is by boat from Saturna or nearby islands, and conditions can be affected by weather exposure from the Georgia Strait.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

The waters around Saturna Island and Tumbo Island are noted for abalone and "unusual coral" — the southernmost Gulf Islands sit in some of the clearest water in the Strait of Georgia. Rocky reef sites at this depth in the outer Gulf Islands support lingcod, multiple rockfish species, Dungeness crab, sea urchins, and dense anemone growth. Giant Pacific octopus are common throughout Gulf Islands reefs. The proximity to the BC-Washington border and the open southern Strait places this area in waters frequented by transient orcas, as well as harbour seals and harbour porpoises.

## Dive Profile

Rocky reef to 18 metres off the eastern tip of Tumbo Island near Reef Harbour, between Tumbo and Cabbage islands (part of Gulf Islands National Park Reserve). The reef is exposed to the southern Strait of Georgia and subject to weather influence from open strait fetch. Visibility is reported as among the best in the Strait of Georgia in this area, particularly in autumn through winter.

## Entry and Exit

Boat-only access. Tumbo Island is part of Gulf Islands National Park Reserve; access is by boat from Saturna Island, Mayne Island, or other nearby Gulf Islands. Reef Harbour (between Tumbo and Cabbage islands) provides 10 mooring buoys for visiting boats. No permanent dive services at the island.

## Safety Considerations

- Exposed outer southern Gulf Islands location — weather and swell from the open strait can develop quickly.
- Tidal currents in the southern Gulf Islands can be strong; plan dives around slack water.
- Cold water (7–12°C) year-round; drysuit recommended.
- Gulf Islands National Park Reserve regulations apply; check current park rules.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Reef Harbour mooring buoys provide a convenient base for diving the reef without anchoring.
- Combine with Saturna Island and Cabbage Island snorkelling/diving in a multi-day Gulf Islands boat trip.
- Autumn and early winter (October–January) offer peak visibility in these southern Gulf Islands waters.
