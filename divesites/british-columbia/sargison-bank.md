## Sargison Bank

Sargison Bank is a shallow offshore bank near Barkley Sound on the west coast of Vancouver Island, British Columbia, Canada, offering reef diving in exposed Pacific Ocean conditions.

## Overview

Located off the outer coast of Vancouver Island near the entrance to Barkley Sound, Sargison Bank sits in truly exposed Pacific water. Swells, surge, and upwelling influence conditions significantly — dives here require a good weather window and an experienced skipper. When conditions cooperate, the bank rewards with clear, cold Pacific water (7–11°C), visibility up to 15–20 metres, and a biodiversity characteristic of the outer Vancouver Island coast. This is open-ocean diving far from the sheltered Gulf Islands environment.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Outer Barkley Sound, Vancouver Island, British Columbia, Canada
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Offshore reef / bank
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–20 metres
- **Current**: Variable; tidal and oceanic
- **Best Time**: July to September (weather-dependent)

## Marine Life

The outer coast environment brings strong Pacific upwelling, supporting kelp-blanketed rocky substrates with dense populations of sea urchins, sunflower sea stars, and large anemone communities. Lingcod and cabezon are resident predators. Harbour porpoise and Dall's porpoise are occasionally sighted in surface waters near the bank. Pacific halibut may be encountered in sandy transition zones around the reef edges. Steller sea lions from nearby colonies are a regular presence.

## Dive Profile

Work the shallowest sections of the bank first to allow eyes to adjust to conditions, then explore the deeper slopes. The terrain is varied — rocky pinnacles, kelp-covered ridges, and sandy plains between the high points. Maximum recommended depth is 18 metres given the surge and currents present. Keep dives conservative with plenty of gas for a controlled ascent.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only, typically from Bamfield or Ucluelet. Sea conditions on the outer coast can change rapidly — monitor marine forecasts closely. Drift entries and pickups may be necessary when current is running. This site is not suitable for inexperienced boat crews.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Only dive in calm window conditions — west coast swell can arrive with little warning
- Bring a wetsuit hood even in summer; upwelling brings very cold water to the surface
- Halibut spotting is possible in sandy zones between rock formations
- Binoculars for marine mammal watching on the surface between dives

## Safety Considerations

Open Pacific Ocean exposure is the defining safety factor at Sargison Bank. Surge can be significant, and sea state can deteriorate quickly. Always dive with an experienced local guide or charter. Emergency services are distant — conservative diving practices are essential. Carry a signal mirror and personal locator beacon (PLB) in addition to an SMB.

## Photography

The outer coast's cleaner water and kelp environments produce vivid underwater imagery. Wide-angle with kelp forest context shots are stunning in good visibility. Sea lions making passes at the reef provide excellent opportunities. Halibut camouflage photography on sandy substrate requires patience and keen eyes.
