## Oregon Rock

Oregon Rock is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Oregon Rock is a submerged rocky reef in the Strait of Georgia offshore from central Vancouver Island, lying in the productive waters between Nanaimo and the northern Gulf Islands. The site is characteristic of this part of the strait, where cold, nutrient-rich water supports lingcod and rockfish on rocky structure, giant Pacific octopus in crevices, and nudibranchs and sea urchins across the reef surface. Visibility in this region of the Strait of Georgia is generally favorable, and the area sees lighter boat traffic than the more heavily dived southern Gulf Islands sites.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

The central Strait of Georgia between Nanaimo and the northern Gulf Islands supports a representative cold-water reef community. Lingcod and rockfish — including quillback and copper rockfish — are reliable finds on rocky structure. Giant Pacific octopus are commonly encountered in crevices. Plumose anemones and encrusting sponges colonise exposed rock surfaces. Nudibranchs, sea urchins, and cabezon are typical at this depth. Harbor seals are regular companions on boat dives in this part of the strait.

## Dive Profile

Oregon Rock is a submerged rocky reef in the Strait of Georgia, positioned in the productive waters between Nanaimo and the northern Gulf Islands. Maximum depth reaches approximately 18 metres. Light to moderate currents are typical; conditions should be checked on descent. Visibility in this part of the strait is generally favourable and can reach 15+ metres outside of the spring plankton bloom. The site sees lighter boat traffic than the more heavily dived southern Gulf Islands.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. Nanaimo Dive Outfitters operates local charters and can provide guidance on reaching Strait of Georgia rocky reef sites in this area.

## Tips and Recommendations

Avoid dives during the spring plankton bloom (typically April–June) when surface visibility can be limited. Water clarity is best in winter and late summer. A drysuit is appropriate year-round. Deploy an SMB on ascent — BC Ferries and commercial shipping traffic pass through the central Strait of Georgia.

## Safety Considerations

Water temperature is 7–12°C year-round — drysuit or 7 mm wetsuit with hood required. Boat traffic in the Strait of Georgia near Nanaimo includes commercial shipping and BC Ferries routes; always ascend with a deployed SMB. Check current conditions on descent.
