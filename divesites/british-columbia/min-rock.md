## Min Rock

Min Rock is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Min Rock is a submerged rocky reef in Discovery Passage near Quadra Island, one of the most current-swept and biologically productive marine corridors on the BC coast. The powerful tidal flows that race through the passage deliver a constant supply of nutrients, coating the rock surfaces in thick growths of plumose anemones and encrusting invertebrates, with lingcod, rockfish, and giant Pacific octopus sheltering in the structure. Diving requires careful timing around slack water, as the passage's currents can make off-slack conditions dangerous even for experienced divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Discovery Passage's high-nutrient, high-current environment produces some of the densest invertebrate life on the BC coast. Plumose anemones — some reaching half a metre in length — cover exposed rock surfaces. Strawberry anemones and encrusting sponges colonise every available substrate. Lingcod and multiple rockfish species shelter in the rocky structure. Giant Pacific octopus occupy crevices throughout the reef. Wolf eels, kelp greenling, and cabezon are regular sightings. Harbour seals patrol the shallower areas and spiny dogfish are common at depth.

## Dive Profile

Min Rock is a submerged rocky reef in Discovery Passage near Quadra Island. Maximum depth reaches approximately 18 metres. The reef sits within a tidal corridor where current speeds can exceed 15 knots in Seymour Narrows and remain significant throughout the passage — diving is only safe during slack tide windows. Visibility in Discovery Passage rarely drops below 9 metres due to constant upwelling of clear water, and can exceed 30 metres during optimal conditions.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. Charters operate from Campbell River and Quadra Island; operators include Abyssal Diving Charters and Oceanfix Dive Centre. The site is typically reached within 30 minutes from Campbell River's Fisherman's Wharf. Precise slack-water timing from the local tide tables is essential — divers should plan to be in the water only during slack and exit before current begins to build.

## Tips and Recommendations

Check the Canadian Hydrographic Service tide tables specifically for Discovery Passage — the slack at Min Rock may differ from the reference station times. Dive duration is dictated by the slack window, often 20–40 minutes. Local charter operators have the most accurate knowledge of when slack occurs at specific sites and are strongly recommended for visitors unfamiliar with the passage.

## Safety Considerations

Currents in Discovery Passage can reach dangerous speeds with very little warning as the tide turns. Only dive during confirmed slack water; abort if current begins to build before planned exit. Water temperature is 7–12°C year-round — a drysuit is strongly recommended. Brief your dive plan with the boat crew before descending, and ensure the crew is monitoring conditions from the surface.
