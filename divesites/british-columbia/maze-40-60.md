## Maze 40-60'

Maze 40-60' is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

The Maze is one of the dive plans available at Madrona Point in Nanoose Bay, widely regarded as one of the best dive sites on central Vancouver Island near Parksville. Divers explore intricate rock formations at depths of 40 to 60 feet (12 to 18 meters), where resident wolf eels and giant Pacific octopus tuck into crevices and nudibranchs cover the rock surfaces. Visibility on this side of the Strait of Georgia is consistently good, often exceeding 15 meters, making it a favorite site for underwater photographers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

The Maze dive area at Madrona Point shares the same cold, nutrient-rich Strait of Georgia environment as the adjacent Main Wall, and produces strong marine life encounters at its shallower depths. Giant Pacific octopus are found in dens within the rock formations — the intricate boulder maze is ideal octopus habitat. Wolf eels occupy crevices in the rocky formations. Nudibranchs in many species — including brightly colored forms — cover sponge-encrusted surfaces throughout. Lingcod appear on elevated rocks. White and orange plumose anemones, painted anemones in various color forms, pink crimson anemones, and encrusting soft corals decorate the rock surfaces. Boot sponges and cloud sponges are present. Sea lions from the nearby log dump may approach divers here as well.

## Dive Profile

The Maze consists of intricate rock formations at depths of 40 to 60 feet (12 to 18 meters) — the shallow intermediate-depth complement to the deeper Main Wall. Divers navigate through a labyrinth of boulders and low-relief rock structures rather than a single wall face. The top of the Maze begins at roughly 12 meters and the maximum depth is 18 meters, making it suitable for Open Water certified divers. It is encountered en route to the Main Wall from the right beach entrance, making it the natural second dive after descending Main Wall on a multi-dive day, or a standalone dive for intermediate-level divers. Visibility averages 9–15 meters and is best in winter.

## Entry and Exit

Shore dive from Madrona Drive in Nanoose Bay (same entry point as Main Wall). The Maze rock formations are reached by navigating on a compass heading offshore from the right beach entrance — divers typically pass through the Maze area while route-finding to the Main Wall, or intentionally stay in this zone for a shallower dive. No facilities on-site; nearest services in Parksville.

## Tips and Recommendations

Search rock crevices carefully — octopus dens are numerous and residents can be found on most dives. Move slowly and look into every gap in the boulders at multiple depth levels. This is an excellent second dive after the deeper Main Wall, using remaining air and bottom time efficiently. A torch dramatically improves invertebrate finds in the darker crevices.

## Safety Considerations

Water temperature 8–12°C year-round; drysuit required. No significant tidal current at Madrona Point. Maximum depth 18 m is within recreational limits for all certification levels. Surface navigation to the site via compass heading recommended in reduced visibility.

## Photography

The Maze is one of the best sites in BC for nudibranch macro photography — diverse species are concentrated on sponge-covered rock surfaces. Octopus den encounters suit close-focus wide-angle. The multi-layered rock formation provides interesting compositional elements for ambient-light wide angle. Visibility is consistently adequate for photography in fall and winter.
