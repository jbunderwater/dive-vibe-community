## Money Maker Reef

Money Maker Reef is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Money Maker Reef is a rocky reef site in the Gulf Islands, lying in the southern Strait of Georgia in the waters between Saltspring Island and the Saanich Peninsula. The reef attracts concentrations of lingcod and rockfish that hold on the rocky structure, along with giant Pacific octopus, nudibranchs, and plumose anemones thriving in the cold, nutrient-rich water. Tidal currents in this area of the Gulf Islands can be variable, so checking conditions before descending is advisable.

## Marine Life

The rocky reef structure in this area of the Gulf Islands harbours the full complement of cold-water Pacific Northwest species: lingcod and quillback rockfish on the bottom, giant Pacific octopus in crevices and under ledges, wolf eels in deeper den openings, and plumose anemones carpeting exposed rock surfaces. Nudibranchs, sea urchins, and sea cucumbers occupy the reef surface. Harbor seals are regular companions on boat dives throughout the Gulf Islands.

## Dive Profile

Money Maker Reef is a rocky reef site in the southern Strait of Georgia, in the waters between Saltspring Island and the Saanich Peninsula. Maximum depth reaches approximately 18 metres. Tidal currents in the Gulf Islands can be variable at this location; checking conditions on descent is advisable. Visibility in the southern Gulf Islands typically ranges 9–20 metres and is best in winter and early spring before the plankton bloom.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. Local operators based in Sidney, Victoria, or the Gulf Islands service this area; Frank White's Dive Store and Ocean Quest Dive Centre operate charters in the southern Gulf Islands. Mooring or anchoring at the reef is standard.

## Tips and Recommendations

Dive around slack water and check current conditions on descent before committing to the dive plan. Winter months generally offer the best visibility in this part of the Gulf Islands. A drysuit is appropriate given year-round water temperatures of 7–12°C.

## Safety Considerations

Tidal currents in the Gulf Islands can build quickly; monitor conditions throughout the dive. Water temperature demands proper thermal protection — a drysuit or a sealed 7 mm wetsuit with hood is standard for comfortable BC diving. Boat traffic in the Strait of Georgia can be significant; deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) on ascent.
