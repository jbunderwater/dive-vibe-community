# Centre Reef

A submerged reef in the Nanaimo area of the Strait of Georgia supporting vibrant cold-water communities.

## Overview

Centre Reef sits in the central Strait of Georgia near Nanaimo, in the productive mid-channel waters that see consistent tidal upwelling. The reef supports the characteristic temperate cold-water community of the Salish Sea, with a particular abundance of filter feeders on exposed current-swept faces. Nanaimo is one of BC's most active dive destinations, with several established operators servicing sites like Centre Reef. Water temperatures are 8–11°C year-round, and visibility typically reaches 8–15 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Strait of Georgia, Nanaimo area, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 metres
- **Current**: Tidal currents present — dive near slack
- **Best Time**: Late spring through autumn

## Marine Life

Giant Pacific octopus are regularly encountered at Centre Reef, drawn to the rocky habitat for shelter and hunting grounds. Lingcod occupy prominent positions on the reef structure. Copper and quillback rockfish are the dominant fish, with black rockfish often schooling in the water column above. Plumose anemones coat the reef faces with white and orange columns. Encrusting sponges and bryozoans provide habitat for numerous nudibranch species. Puget Sound king crab are found along the reef base, and harbour seals visit frequently.

## Dive Profile

The reef rises to about 8 metres, with flanks dropping to 18 metres. A systematic perimeter swim of the reef provides the best coverage. Spend the first half of the dive at depth on the outer reef faces where current and invertebrate growth are greatest, then transition to the shallower crest for the safety stop zone. The interior of the reef structure often harbours the most octopus and fish.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Nanaimo's boat basin or launching facilities. Locate the reef by GPS. Anchor on sand well clear of the reef summit. Deploy an SMB before ascending — BC Ferries routes pass through the southern Strait of Georgia and vessel traffic is constant. Have the boat tender maintain watch.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Nanaimo has a thriving dive community — local knowledge about exact reef structure and current timing is valuable
- The Nanaimo area supports multiple dive sites suitable for day-trip multi-dive days
- Autumn typically provides the clearest visibility throughout the Strait of Georgia
- Consider combining Centre Reef with a nearby artificial reef dive such as the HMCS Saskatchewan or HMCS Cape Breton

## Safety Considerations

Tidal currents require careful timing. Always consult Nanaimo or Ballenas Island tide tables and plan to dive at slack. Surface with a brightly coloured SMB visible from a distance. Cold water demands drysuit proficiency. Keep buddy separation to a minimum in any current.

## Photography

Centre Reef's invertebrate growth provides excellent photographic subjects. Wide-angle reef panoramas with plumose anemones in the foreground and rockfish in the background are classic Pacific Northwest images. Macro work on encrusting sponges reveals nudibranchs, sea spiders, and colonial tunicates. Octopus hunting or investigating divers makes compelling behavioural photography.
