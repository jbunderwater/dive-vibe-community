## Neville Rock

Neville Rock is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Neville Rock is a rocky reef site in the Strait of Georgia north of Powell River on BC's Sunshine Coast, in an area known locally for accessible cold-water diving with outstanding marine diversity. The nutrient-rich waters of this part of the strait support rockfish, lingcod, giant Pacific octopus, and sea cucumbers on the rocky bottom, while harbor seals are frequent companions on the dive. The upper Sunshine Coast sees relatively light dive traffic, and the clarity of the water here is typically excellent for photography.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

The northern Sunshine Coast and upper Strait of Georgia support abundant cold-water marine life on rocky substrate. Lingcod and quillback rockfish are reliably found on the reef structure. Giant Pacific octopus inhabit crevices at depth. Sea cucumbers, rock scallops, and plumose anemones colonise the rocky surfaces. Wolf eels appear in den openings throughout the site. Harbor seals are common companions on boat dives in this area.

## Dive Profile

Neville Rock is a rocky reef site in the Strait of Georgia north of Powell River, at coordinates placing it in the upper Sunshine Coast diving zone. Maximum depth reaches approximately 18 metres. Water clarity in this part of the strait is typically excellent, with visibility often exceeding 15 metres outside the spring plankton bloom period. The area receives substantially less dive traffic than southern Gulf Islands sites.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only from Powell River or Lund. Local operators including Salish Sea Dive in Powell River run charters to Strait of Georgia sites in the upper Sunshine Coast region.

## Tips and Recommendations

Powell River is renowned as a premier winter diving destination on the BC coast due to exceptional water clarity during the cooler months. Plan dives around slack water and check surface conditions before departure. A drysuit is recommended for year-round comfort given water temperatures of 7–12°C.

## Safety Considerations

Water temperature is 7–12°C year-round — drysuit or 7 mm wetsuit with hood required. Boat traffic in the upper Strait of Georgia is lighter than the southern areas but still present; deploy an SMB on ascent. Weather in the strait can deteriorate rapidly.
