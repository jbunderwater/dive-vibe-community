## Musgrave Rock

Musgrave Rock is a reef dive site in British Columbia, North America.

## Overview

Musgrave Rock is a rocky outcrop in Satellite Channel off the southwest coast of Saltspring Island, a passage linking Saanich Inlet to the wider Gulf Islands. The site sits near Musgrave Point at the southern end of Saltspring, where tidal currents through the channel support a diverse assemblage of plumose anemones, rockfish, and giant Pacific octopus on the rocky substrate. Harbor seals are common in the area, and the cold, clear water of the channel provides good visibility for exploring the reef structure.

## Site Information

- **Location**: British Columbia, North America
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Satellite Channel's tidal flows sustain an abundant invertebrate community. Plumose anemones cover exposed rock surfaces. Giant Pacific octopus occupy crevices throughout the rocky substrate, and wolf eels appear in den sites along the reef. Rockfish and lingcod hold on the structure. Sea urchins, rock scallops, and sea cucumbers are present across the reef surface. Harbor seals are common in the area and may approach divers in the water.

## Dive Profile

Musgrave Rock sits at the southern tip of Saltspring Island in Satellite Channel, a passage with notable tidal current connecting Saanich Inlet to the broader Gulf Islands waterways. Maximum depth reaches approximately 18 metres. Satellite Channel experiences meaningful tidal current — visibility ranges 6–18 metres depending on conditions and season, with 20–30 feet (6–9 metres) being typical. Diving should be planned around slack water.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only from Maple Bay, Cowichan Bay, or the Saltspring Island side. Local operators including Ocean Quest Dive Centre and Frank White's Dive Store run charters to Gulf Islands sites in this area.

## Tips and Recommendations

Current timing is critical — Satellite Channel should be dived only at slack water. Check Canadian Hydrographic Service tide tables for the Satellite Channel reference station. Visibility can deteriorate with increased current and during spring plankton bloom periods. A drysuit is recommended for year-round comfort.

## Safety Considerations

Water temperature in Satellite Channel is 7–12°C year-round — appropriate thermal protection is essential. Currents can build rapidly; plan your exit before the slack ends. The Musgrave Landing area has some boat traffic; deploy an SMB on ascent.
