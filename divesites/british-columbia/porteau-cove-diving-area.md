## Porteau Cove Diving Area

Porteau Cove Provincial Park is a premier shore diving destination in Howe Sound featuring multiple shipwrecks, artificial reef structures, and excellent training conditions for divers of all levels.

## Overview

Porteau Cove is located on the east side of Howe Sound, 24 km north of Horseshoe Bay and 40 km north of Vancouver alongside Highway 99. The 14-hectare provincial marine park provides excellent shore access with professional facilities and a diversity of wrecks and artificial structures. The site is managed by BC Parks and serves as both a recreational destination and professional diving training facility. Visibility is best in winter (up to 14m) but varies seasonally. The site gently slopes to approximately 18m at the deepest point.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Howe Sound, Porteau Peninsula, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Muck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-14 meters (best in winter)
- **Current**: Generally mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to February (best visibility)
- **Water Temperature**: 8-12°C (cold water diving required)

## Featured Wrecks

**The Granthall** (primary attraction)
- 28-meter steel tug
- Scuttled March 11, 1992
- Depth: 10-15 meters
- Ideal for novice divers due to shallow depth and intact structure
- Heavily encrusted with anemones and decorator crabs
- Easily explored without penetration training

**The Nakaya**
- Located in 20-30 meter depths
- Advanced state of decomposition
- Lies partially outside park boundary
- Accessible for experienced divers only

**Centennial Wreck**
- Part of artificial reef network
- Densely populated with anemones
- Shallow to moderate depth

## Artificial Reef Structures

Multiple artificial reef structures were positioned in the 1980s-90s to create a diverse, park-like diving environment:
- Concrete block reefs
- Jungle gym structures
- Rocky outcrops
- Creating excellent habitat for kelp and marine life

## Marine Life

The site supports diverse marine communities:
- Anemones (abundant on wrecks)
- Decorator crabs
- Wolf eels
- Lingcod
- Rockfish (multiple species)
- Giant Pacific octopus
- Sea urchins
- Kelp and marine vegetation
- Seasonal nudibranchs

## Dive Profile

Beginners: Start on Granthall wreck in 10-15m, explore external features and cutaway sections (20-30 minutes), ascend with safety stop.

Intermediate: Explore multiple wrecks and reef structures, reach 20-25m on secondary sites, plan 40-50 minute dives with extended safety stops.

Advanced: Deeper wreck penetration (20-30m), complex navigation between structures, potential for staged dives.

## Entry and Exit

A concrete stairway provides direct water access. Entry best during high tide (easier stairway access). At low tide, significant walking distance over rocks. Diver facilities include:
- Change room with showers
- Picnic tables for gear setup/breakdown
- Diver shower (top of stairway)
- Large parking lot
- Nearby campground
- Ferry dock alternative access

## Tips and Recommendations

- Time entry for high tide if possible
- Check winter for best visibility (up to 14m)
- Beginners should start with Granthall (10-15m, easy navigation)
- Winter visibility can reach 14 meters—excellent for photography
- Summer plankton/algae can reduce visibility significantly
- The park-like arrangement allows structured progression through depths
- Excellent site for training due to varied terrain and controlled conditions

## Safety Considerations

This is an Intermediate site suitable for trained recreational divers:
- Cold water (8-12°C) requires proper thermal protection
- Entry can be challenging at low tide
- Surge and surge zone hazards near shore
- Wreck penetration of Granthall is safe without technical training, but stay external unless trained
- The Nakaya lies partially outside park boundary; respect boundaries
- Always dive with a buddy
- Use surface marker buoy for boat communication
- Plan air management carefully for multi-structure dives
- Carry proper signaling devices

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
