# Buckeye Shoal

A shallow shoal in the southern Strait of Georgia offering productive cold-water reef diving.

## Overview

Buckeye Shoal is located in the southern Strait of Georgia near the US-Canada border in the waters around the southern Gulf Islands. Shoals in this area form productive dive sites where tidal upwelling concentrates nutrients and supports healthy invertebrate communities. Water temperatures are cold throughout the year at 8–12°C, and visibility ranges from 6–12 metres with the best conditions in late summer. Drysuit diving is standard for this region.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Strait of Georgia, Gulf Islands area, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 6–12 metres
- **Current**: Tidal currents present — plan around slack
- **Best Time**: Late spring through autumn

## Marine Life

Buckeye Shoal hosts the typical cold-water assemblage of the southern Strait of Georgia. Giant Pacific octopus are found among the rocky terrain. Lingcod are a dominant predator and are regularly seen resting on the bottom or hunting among the rocks. Copper, quillback, and black rockfish occupy different depth zones. Plumose anemones and encrusting coralline algae cover exposed rock surfaces. Dungeness crab forage on sandy areas adjacent to the shoal. Harbour seals and occasional Steller sea lions visit the site.

## Dive Profile

The shoal rises to a high point of approximately 6–8 metres below the surface, dropping to sand at 18 metres. Work the rocky terrain at mid-depth during the main portion of the dive. The transition zone between rocky reef and sand bottom often harbours the largest octopus and lingcod. End the dive on the shoal's shallowest section for a comfortable safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by boat from Sidney, Roche Harbor (Washington State), or nearby Gulf Islands communities. GPS coordinates are required to locate the submerged shoal. Anchor well clear of the reef peak to avoid damage. Deploy a highly visible SMB during ascent due to boat traffic in this area of the Strait.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Buckeye Shoal is relatively shallow, making it an excellent second dive of the day
- The site is close to the US border — ensure you have the appropriate documentation for cross-border boat trips
- Autumn months tend to provide the clearest visibility on southern Strait reefs
- Local Gulf Islands resorts and dive guides can point you to the exact location

## Safety Considerations

Boat traffic in the southern Strait of Georgia is heavy during summer. Always surface with a visible SMB. Tidal currents around the southern Gulf Islands are variable and can be strong. Cold water (8–12°C) demands drysuit protection. Carry a dive flag on the boat.

## Photography

Wide-angle shots of the rocky shoal structure with plumose anemones and orbiting rockfish are the signature images from this type of site. Macro photographers should examine encrusting sponges and bryozoans for nudibranchs. Octopus interactions can yield exceptional behavioural photography. Bring adequate artificial lighting.
