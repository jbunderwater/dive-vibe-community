# Forest Island

A small island in the southern Gulf Islands with productive kelp forest diving accessible by boat.

## Overview

Forest Island is a small island in the central Gulf Islands between Salt Spring Island and the Saanich Peninsula. The island's rocky perimeter supports bull kelp forest habitat, creating an accessible and productive dive site in the shallow depths characteristic of kelp diving. The site is named for its forested above-water landscape, which extends underwater as bull kelp fronds reaching toward the surface. Maximum depth is 12 metres, making it suitable for extended bottom times. Water temperatures are 8–11°C and visibility averages 6–12 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Central Gulf Islands, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Bull kelp forest
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 6–12 metres
- **Current**: Moderate tidal currents in the Gulf Islands
- **Best Time**: Late spring through early autumn when kelp is densest

## Marine Life

The kelp forest around Forest Island supports a diverse community. Kelp greenlings are abundant among the stipes, and their vibrant colouring makes them memorable sights. Pile perch and striped seaperch school in the open areas within the kelp. Giant Pacific octopus forage in the rocky holdfasts and along the island's rocky perimeter. Painted greenlings, cabezon, and small lingcod are present in the shallower zones. Harbour seals use the kelp for cover and are reliably present. Bull kelp beds attract black-tailed deer that swim between Gulf Islands — their occasional underwater appearances are a memorable sighting.

## Dive Profile

Maximum depth of 12 metres allows long, unhurried dives exploring the kelp canopy. Begin at the rocky perimeter of the island and work outward into the kelp bed. Move slowly and deliberately through the kelp stipes to avoid entanglement. The holdfasts at the bottom of individual kelp plants harbour small invertebrates including brittlestars and worms. The shallow depth makes a relaxed ascent through the kelp canopy possible.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Sidney or Ganges Harbour, Salt Spring Island. Anchor carefully on sand outside the kelp zone — never anchor in the kelp bed. Navigate to the island's perimeter by boat, then enter the water. Carry a knife accessible on your forearm in case of kelp entanglement. Exit back to the boat away from the dense kelp fronds.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Move slowly and keep arms tucked when navigating through kelp stipes
- Morning light through the kelp canopy provides the best natural-light photography conditions
- Gulf Islands kelp forests peak in late summer — visit before autumn storms begin to break down the kelp
- Forest Island is an excellent site for Open Water Divers looking for a more natural BC dive experience

## Safety Considerations

Kelp entanglement is the primary hazard — always carry a cutting tool. Tidal currents in the Gulf Islands require planning. Boat traffic in the area requires careful SMB deployment — surface away from the densest kelp growth where possible. Cold water (8–11°C) requires drysuit protection.

## Photography

Looking upward through the kelp stipes toward the surface canopy creates cathedral-like natural light images — one of BC's signature underwater shots. Wide-angle or fisheye lenses are ideal for kelp forest interior photography. Kelp greenlings and other fish among the stipes make great subjects. Morning light provides the best natural illumination.
