# Cypress Reef

A natural rocky reef in the southern Salish Sea near Cypress Island, supporting the cold-water species typical of the San Juan Islands and southern BC Gulf Islands.

## Overview

Cypress Reef lies in the waters between the BC Gulf Islands and the San Juan Islands of Washington State, in the vicinity of Cypress Island. The reef sits at the BC-Washington border area, where the currents and cold, productive waters of the Salish Sea support dense marine communities. The OSM data originally classified this site as a wreck; however, the underlying OSM tags identify it as `natural=reef`, and no wreck identity could be confirmed from available sources. The site is treated here as a natural reef.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Salish Sea, near Cypress Island, BC/Washington border area
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 8–12°C year-round

## Marine Life

The Cypress Island area of the Salish Sea is documented as habitat for rockfish (copper, quillback, and others), lingcod, and kelp greenling. Strong currents and rocky reef structure in this region support dense sessile invertebrate communities including plumose anemones and encrusting sponges. No site-specific species reports for Cypress Reef itself were found — the Cypress Island Aquatic Reserve (Washington DNR) documents the broader habitat quality of this area.

## Dive Profile

Rocky reef structure at depths to approximately 18 metres. Cold Salish Sea water with tidal current influence — timing around slack water is recommended. Visibility is typically 6–12 metres and best in autumn and early winter.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access. The site lies in the cross-border waters near Cypress Island. Operators from Sidney, BC or Bellingham, Washington area serve this part of the Salish Sea.

## Safety Considerations

Cold water (8–12°C) requires a drysuit or well-fitted semidry. Tidal currents in the San Juan/Gulf Islands region can be significant — plan around slack. Boat traffic is active in this waterway.
