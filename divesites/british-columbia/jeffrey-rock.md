# Jeffrey Rock

A submerged rock on the central Sunshine Coast with excellent Pacific Northwest marine life.

## Overview

Jeffrey Rock is located in the Strait of Georgia off the central Sunshine Coast, between the mainland and Texada Island. The Sunshine Coast is known for its accessible boat diving, with sites spread between Gibsons in the south and Powell River in the north. Jeffrey Rock is in the mid-Sunshine Coast zone and represents the area's typical productive cold-water reef diving. Water temperatures are 8–11°C and visibility averages 8–15 metres with autumn being the peak visibility season.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Strait of Georgia, central Sunshine Coast, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 metres
- **Current**: Tidal currents in the Strait — plan around slack
- **Best Time**: Late summer and autumn

## Marine Life

Jeffrey Rock hosts the productive cold-water community of the central Strait of Georgia. Giant Pacific octopus are regularly encountered. Lingcod are large and territorial. Copper, quillback, and black rockfish are the dominant fish. Plumose anemones and encrusting sponges cover the rock's exposed faces. Kelp greenlings and painted greenlings inhabit the shallower portions. Puget Sound king crab are found on the rocky base. Harbour seals are consistent visitors.

## Dive Profile

The rock rises to approximately 8–10 metres below the surface, with the base at 18 metres on sand. Work the deeper perimeter of the rock during the main dive, then ascend to the summit for safety stop time. The transition from rock to sand around the base typically harbours the largest crustaceans and occasionally the biggest octopus.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Pender Harbour, Egmont, or Sechelt. GPS required. Anchor on sand clear of the rock. Deploy SMB before ascending — vessel traffic in the central Strait includes pleasure boaters and the Sunshine Coast ferry route. Live boat pickup is preferred.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Combine Jeffrey Rock with other Sunshine Coast sites for a full dive day
- The Sunshine Coast north of Sechelt is less dived than the southern Gulf Islands — marine life tends to be less disturbed
- Local Sechelt and Pender Harbour operators know this rock well
- Pender Harbour is an excellent base for multi-day Sunshine Coast diving

## Safety Considerations

Tidal currents in the Strait of Georgia require planning. The Sunshine Coast ferry route passes through this area — SMB deployment is critical. Cold water (8–11°C) demands drysuit protection. Weather in the central Strait can deteriorate quickly.

## Photography

Sunshine Coast reef photography captures the same Pacific Northwest species as the Gulf Islands but often in less-visited settings. Octopus in natural behaviour, rockfish among encrusting sponge, and lingcod territorial displays make strong documentary images. Wide-angle reef portraits are highly effective.
