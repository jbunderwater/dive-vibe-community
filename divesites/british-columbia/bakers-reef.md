## Bakers Reef

Bakers Reef is a boat-access reef dive near the San Juan Islands boundary in the southern Gulf Islands, British Columbia, Canada.

## Overview

Bakers Reef lies in Boundary Pass, the international waterway between the southernmost Gulf Islands of British Columbia and the American San Juan Islands. This position exposes the reef to consistent tidal exchange through the pass, which carries cold, oxygen-rich water from the Pacific and keeps the rock surfaces heavily colonised with filter feeders. The reef rises to near-surface depths and drops to 18 metres, with boulder and bedrock habitat throughout. Water temperatures typically range from 8 to 10°C, and visibility runs 8 to 15 metres in clear conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Boundary Pass, southern Gulf Islands, British Columbia, Canada
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8-15 meters (25-50 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 8-10°C (46-50°F) — dry suit required
- **Current**: Moderate to strong on tidal exchanges
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Bakers Reef supports the rich temperate marine life typical of the Boundary Pass area. Lingcod are prominent residents on the deeper edges, and giant Pacific octopus use the boulder fields extensively. Schooling black rockfish frequent the mid-water above the reef, while perch species pick invertebrates from the rock face. Dense colonies of plumose anemones festoon the current-swept ledges. Steller sea lions from nearby haulouts occasionally investigate divers. Nudibranchs, including the brightly coloured opalescent and alabaster varieties, are found throughout the site.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deeper edge of the reef and explore the boulder terrain at 15 to 18 metres before working your way up across the reef crest. The most dense anemone and invertebrate coverage is typically on the current-facing walls between 10 and 16 metres. Finish the dive in the shallower zone before ascending. Complete a 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres, and deploy your SMB well in advance.

## Entry and Exit

Boat only. Boundary Pass has active shipping and ferry traffic — ensure the captain maintains a proper watch and that divers ascend with bright SMBs clearly visible. Anchor away from the reef crest to avoid anchor drag damage.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Plan the dive for slack water on Boundary Pass; currents can reach 4+ knots on springs
- Steller sea lion encounters are more likely in cooler months (October to March)
- A wide-angle lens captures the anemone walls effectively
- The international border runs through Boundary Pass — remain in Canadian waters
- Depth sounder or chartplotter helps confirm reef position

## Safety Considerations

Heavy vessel traffic through Boundary Pass creates a genuine surface hazard. Ascend with a brightly coloured, fully deployed SMB. The captain should monitor vessel traffic closely and position the boat to protect ascending divers. Current can build rapidly after slack — monitor conditions and surface promptly if current strengthens.

## Photography

Anemone walls at Bakers Reef offer dramatic wide-angle compositions. Position against the rock face with the anemone colonies filling the background for the best results. Black rockfish and perch species make active wide-angle subjects in the water column. Macro opportunities include nudibranchs, shrimp, and encrusting bryozoans.
