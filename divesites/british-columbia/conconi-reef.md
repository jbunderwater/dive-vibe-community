# Conconi Reef

A reef in the southern Gulf Islands frequently dived for its rockfish communities and invertebrate life.

## Overview

Conconi Reef is located in the southern Gulf Islands between Salt Spring Island and the eastern Saanich Peninsula, in the waters that see regular tidal exchange between the Strait of Georgia and Haro Strait. The reef is a popular destination for Gulf Islands boaters and divers, offering the region's characteristic mix of cold-water invertebrate communities and Pacific Northwest fish species. Water temperatures hold at 8–11°C and visibility averages 8–15 metres in good conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Gulf Islands, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 metres
- **Current**: Moderate tidal currents — plan around slack
- **Best Time**: Late spring through autumn

## Marine Life

Conconi Reef is particularly noted for its rockfish populations. Copper, quillback, and black rockfish are all present, with black rockfish often schooling conspicuously in the mid-water column. Lingcod are resident predators. Giant Pacific octopus are commonly found in crevices. Plumose anemones and encrusting sponges coat exposed surfaces. Painted greenlings and kelp greenlings are abundant in the shallower rocky zones. Nudibranchs are found throughout the reef on sponge-covered surfaces.

## Dive Profile

The reef rises to approximately 6–8 metres, with the surrounding terrain dropping to 18 metres. Work the deeper outer reef first, then transition toward the shallower crest as the dive progresses. Schools of black rockfish in the mid-water column are best observed by ascending slowly through the water column and watching the fish respond. Complete a 3-metre safety stop before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Sidney, Swartz Bay, or nearby Gulf Island communities. GPS required. Anchor on sand clear of the reef. Monitor current conditions before entry and watch for tide reversal during the dive. Deploy an SMB clearly before surfacing — ferry traffic in the region is significant.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Midwater observation of schooling black rockfish is a highlight of this site
- The area around Conconi Reef is excellent for whale-watching at the surface — resident orca are sometimes seen nearby
- Gulf Islands sunsets with dive boats at anchor make this a full-day destination
- Sidney area dive charters offer regular trips to southern Gulf Island reef sites

## Safety Considerations

Currents in the southern Gulf Islands can be significant, particularly during spring and fall tides. Always check tide tables before departure. BC Ferries routes pass through this area. Surface with a highly visible SMB. Cold water (8–11°C) requires drysuit protection.

## Photography

Schooling black rockfish photographed from below against a bright surface canopy create striking silhouette images. The reef structure with plumose anemones makes fine wide-angle photography. For macro work, examine sponge surfaces carefully for nudibranchs. Pacific octopus colour change is always spectacular to document.
