## Critter Corner

Critter Corner is St. Vincent's flagship muck dive, sitting in the shadow of Fort Duvernette on the eastern edge of Young Island, roughly 600 feet off Villa Beach.

## Overview

The site is a seagrass bed at around 35 feet (10.7m) of volcanic sand, and is the dive most associated with St. Vincent's "Critter Capital of the Caribbean" reputation, historically led by local dive operator Bill Tewes of Dive St. Vincent. PADI's dive-site database lists this location under a generic "Reef" category with boilerplate marine-life tags ("Sharks, Whales, Dolphins, Turtles") that don't match the shallow, sheltered muck character described by multiple dive-specific sources — that generic PADI categorization has been disregarded in favor of the muck-diving description.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Muck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters

## Marine Life

Two independent sources (Bucket Tripper and Bluewater Dive Travel) both confirm flying gurnards, frogfish, goldspotted eel, arrow shrimp, and blenny/goby species here. Bucket Tripper additionally reports yellowhead jawfish, nimble spray crabs, snake eels, fanworms, razorfish, and the locally prized red-banded lobster in the seagrass.
