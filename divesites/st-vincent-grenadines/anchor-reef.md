## Anchor Reef

Named for an 18th-century anchor buried in its coral, Anchor Reef is one of St. Vincent's best-known dives and has been repeatedly cited among the top 100 dive sites in the world.

## Overview

The reef starts around 9 meters and drops into a wall most sources describe as reaching about 36 meters (120ft), riddled with swim-throughs; theDiveGlobe is an outlier, listing a shallower 30m maximum with an 18m average and rating the site Beginner rather than Intermediate — the depth and difficulty here follow the majority of sources. The wall face is decorated with gorgonians and black corals.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 36 meters

## Marine Life

Bluewater Dive Travel and local dive-shop descriptions confirm eels, squid, and seahorses living among the gorgonians, along with frogfish in the reef's crevices and overhangs. The anchor itself — the reef's namesake — remains embedded in the coral, though no source gives a verified date or origin for it beyond "18th century."
