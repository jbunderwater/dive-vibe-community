## The Wall (Bequia)

The Wall (Bequia) is an Advanced-only wall dive off West Cay at Bequia's southern tip, dropping to as deep as 40 meters.

## Overview

The dive begins with a swim over a shallow ledge at West Cay before the reef descends steeply into open water; local operator Dive Bequia lists the wall's depth at 40m (130ft), though theDiveGlobe separately reports a shallower maximum of 30m with an 18m average — sources disagree on exactly how deep the site runs. Swim-throughs are found at the base of the wall. When the current runs in the right direction, divers can follow it around the tip of West Cay and down its southern side toward Middle Cay (also called Big Cay), usually finishing on a shallow shelf at the southernmost tip where currents converge; on rare current-free days it's possible to continue all the way around the rock. Currents, waves, and swell can make the site genuinely challenging, and a minimum Advanced certification is required.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters

## Marine Life

Dive Bequia reports barracuda, spiny lobsters, lionfish, green moray eels, turtles, reef sharks, Atlantic spadefish, nurse sharks, black jack, and eagle rays along the wall, plus soft and hard coral cover. Along the southern stretch toward Middle Cay, schools of tarpon and seahorses have been reported. TripAdvisor visitor accounts corroborate the barracuda and tarpon sightings.
