## New Guinea Reef

New Guinea Reef on St. Vincent's leeward coast is celebrated for an unusual diversity of black coral — three species in six different colors — draped over a long terraced slope.

## Overview

Also known via the Questelles shore-access point, the reef's terraced slope starts around 9 meters (30ft) in the inner bay and descends, in stages, to roughly 45 meters (150ft) at a sandy bottom thick with branching vase sponges — a considerably deeper range than earlier data on this site suggested. The point can carry current up to about 2 knots, though conditions are calm most of the time. Because of the depth range and current, this is rated Intermediate rather than Beginner; most recreational diving happens on the shallower terraces in the 9-20m range, with the deeper sections better suited to more experienced divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters

## Marine Life

The site's defining feature is its black coral diversity — three species in six colors, confirmed by multiple sources — draping columnar overhangs alongside bright pink and scarlet encrusting sponges and large branching vase sponges on the sandy bottom. We did not find a source confirming specific fish species (e.g., parrotfish or turtles) at this particular site, so those claims have been removed pending further research.
