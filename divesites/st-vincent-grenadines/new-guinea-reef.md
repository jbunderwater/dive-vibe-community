## New Guinea Reef

New Guinea Reef is a reef dive site in St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Caribbean.

## Overview

New Guinea Reef (also known as Questelles Reef) on St. Vincent's leeward coast is celebrated for an extraordinary diversity of black coral. Columnar overhangs encrusted in bright pink and scarlet sponges drop from around 9 meters near the shore entry and mooring toward a sandy bottom. This listing covers the shallower, beginner-friendly mooring area; the same site also has a deeper "point" that sources describe dropping to roughly 45 meters (150ft) in a current that can reach 2 knots — that section is advanced-only and not reflected in the depth/difficulty fields here.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. Vincent and the Grenadines, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 meters

## Marine Life

New Guinea Reef is exceptional for black coral diversity, alongside bright pink and scarlet encrusting sponges on the sandy bottom. Sources describe generally sparse large-fish numbers here due to regional fishing pressure, with small reef fish and "beautiful small" coral formations making up most of what divers see. Turtles and moray eels are reported in St. Vincent's shallower reef sites generally, but are not confirmed by name at this specific reef.
