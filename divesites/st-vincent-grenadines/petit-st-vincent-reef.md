## Petit St. Vincent Reef

The reefs around the private island of Petit St. Vincent (PSV) sit within a Marine Conservation Area adjacent to the St. Vincent and the Grenadines Mission Blue Hope Spot.

## Overview

Note on naming: St. Vincent and the Grenadines' official tourism board names the two principal dive sites near PSV as "Mopion Reef" and "Pineese Reef" — "Petit St. Vincent Reef" as used on this page is a descriptive label for the island's reef diving generally rather than a single official site name. More than a decade of fishing and anchor restrictions around PSV, backed by a partnership between the Philip Stephenson Foundation, The Nature Conservancy, CLEAR Caribbean, and Sustainable Grenadines, has produced a documented, steady increase in reef biomass, including an elkhorn coral restoration garden. In November 2017, Mission Blue founder Dr. Sylvia Earle convened a marine-conservation gathering on PSV tied to the Hope Spot designation. Diving is operated by a PADI Five Star dive center on the island, run by Jean-Michel Cousteau's Diving Caribbean Centre — one of only two Cousteau-branded dive centers in the world.

Expect general Eastern Caribbean reef fauna, benefiting from the area's long-standing no-fishing protections, but treat specific species claims as unconfirmed until a site-specific source is found.
