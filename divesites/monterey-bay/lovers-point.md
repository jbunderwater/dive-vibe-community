## Lovers Point

Lovers Point offers one of Monterey Bay's most comfortable and underappreciated shore dives, featuring large pinnacles, excellent biodiversity, and one of the region's most consistent octopus sightings.

## Overview

Located in Pacific Grove along Ocean View Boulevard, Lovers Point is a dual-entry site offering two very different diving experiences on either side of the point. The site features large pinnacles with impressive biodiversity in shallow water. Despite being overshadowed by nearby Cannery Row attractions, Lovers Point provides a rewarding and often less-crowded alternative for divers of all skill levels.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Pacific Grove, Ocean View Boulevard (36.625°N, 121.915°W)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters (30-65 feet), can reach 40 feet on best days
- **Water Temperature**: 10-13°C (50-55°F) — requires 5-7mm wetsuit
- **Parking**: Free on Ocean View Boulevard (2-hour limit) or small parking lot
- **Entry Points**: East side (protected from swell, beginner-friendly), West side (more exposed, rougher)
- **Seasonal Restrictions**: May 1 through September 30, diving prohibited on east side Friday-Monday and certain holidays
- **Best Time**: Fall (September-November) for calmer conditions and good visibility

## Marine Life and Features

Lovers Point is one of the few places where octopuses may be consistently found during the day, often discovered on sand bottoms, kelp holdfasts, or swimming mid-water. Within the nooks and crannies of the rocks hide an assortment of small fish, nudibranchs of exceptional variety, and colorful sponges. The pinnacles create diverse microhabitats supporting varied marine life.

## Two Different Dive Areas

**East Side**: Well-protected from swell, popular with beginners. Shallower depths, gentle topography, minimal current. Good for training and building skill.

**West Side**: More exposed to ocean swell, normally rougher and more surgy than the east side. Greater challenge for intermediate divers, more interesting deeper pinnacle structures.

## Dive Profile

Enter from stone stairs at either the east or west side. The east side offers a gentler entry and exploration at 5-15 meters. The west side provides more dramatic terrain. Navigate along pinnacles exploring the diverse rock formations and small-animal habitat. Expect to spend time looking for octopuses, nudibranchs, and small fish species. Ascend gradually while exploring the shallower structures near the surface.

## Entry and Exit

**East Side Entry**: Designated stone stairs provide safe water entry on protected days. Straight entry to shallow rocky terrain. Exit at same location.

**West Side Entry**: Rock and sand beach entry with more surge and swell exposure. Suitable for intermediate divers with current and swell experience. Exit at same location using careful approach to avoid surge impact.

Check conditions before choosing which side to dive. East side requires good weather conditions but offers easier diving.

## Parking and Amenities

- Parking on Ocean View Boulevard (free, 2-hour limit)
- Small parking lot south of point for easy beach access via stone stairs
- No facilities at beach itself
- Restaurants and shops nearby in Pacific Grove

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of Monterey's most underappreciated sites—expect fewer crowds than Cannery Row dives
- Octopus hunting is best during calm mornings on sand bottom areas
- East side is excellent for training and building cold-water skills
- Fall dives (September-November) offer best conditions and visibility
- Bring macro cameras for excellent nudibranch and octopus photography
- Respectful close observation allows excellent wildlife photography

## Cold Water Considerations

Monterey's 50-55°F water even at shallow depths requires proper thermal protection (5mm+ wetsuits). The pinnacle depth variation (5-21 meters) allows divers to choose their thermal challenge level.

## Seasonal Access Restrictions

From May 1 through September 30, the east side is closed to diving on Fridays through Mondays and certain holidays (protecting breeding octopuses and marine life). Plan dives accordingly during these peak summer months. West side and winter east-side diving remain available.

## Safety Considerations

Monitor conditions before entering—west side can be rough in swell. Always use an SMB on ascent. Buddy contact is essential, particularly when focused on octopus hunting. The shallow depths and protected east-side option make this an excellent choice for safer, more comfortable Monterey diving.
