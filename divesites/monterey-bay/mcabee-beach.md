## McAbee Beach

McAbee Beach offers an easy, beginner-friendly kelp forest dive on Cannery Row with historical artifacts and diverse marine life, allowing escape from the crowded Breakwater area.

## Overview

Located on Cannery Row between Hoffman and Prescott Streets in Monterey, McAbee Beach provides one of the most protected entries in Monterey Bay. The site offers easy escape from Breakwater crowds while providing access to a large kelp bed just minutes from shore and several old pipelines creating distinctive artificial reef structures.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cannery Row, Monterey (between Hoffman and Prescott Streets)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Kelp
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-15 meters (15-50 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 10-13°C (50-55°F) — requires 5-7mm wetsuit
- **Current**: Minimal, well-protected entry
- **Parking**: El Torito's Mexican Restaurant parking (all day parking $10) or Wave St parking structure
- **Facilities**: Restrooms (no showers) on Wave St & Hoffman Ave
- **Best Time**: Year-round, calm days

## Marine Life and Features

The site features a large kelp bed just minutes from shore with diverse marine life including rockfish, halibut, hermit crabs, kelp bass, giant jellyfish, rubber lip surfperch, black-eye gobies, and sailfin sculpins. The site's defining feature is the network of old sardine-pumping pipelines running from shore to approximately 40 feet, which were used to pump sardines from offshore fishing boats to the canneries. Rockfish now hide in the pipes with halibut in the nearby sand—a fascinating historical and ecological feature.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the sandy beach, which offers one of Monterey's easiest entries. Quickly reach the large kelp bed just offshore. Navigate along the pipe system or explore the natural reef structures. The variety of reef, kelp forest, and sandy bottom types provides diverse dive options. Most interesting features are at 10-15 meters. Ascend through the kelp canopy observing its structure and inhabitants.

## Entry and Exit

Sandy beach entry is straightforward even for beginners. Gear up on the beach, enter at a comfortable pace, and orient toward the kelp bed visible from shore. Exit the same way, watching for any incoming surge on final approach to shore.

## Parking and Amenities

Parking options:
- El Torito's Restaurant: All-day parking $10 (convenient to beach)
- Wave St & Hoffman Ave parking structure with public restrooms (no showers)

The restaurant location makes this highly accessible for a quick dive between dining or shopping.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent beginner-friendly alternative to crowded Breakwater
- The historical pipe structures are unique—worth exploring and photographing
- Large kelp bed offers excellent kelp navigation practice
- Expect diverse fish life—bring cameras for macro opportunities
- Less crowded than Breakwater despite good accessibility
- Easy entry makes this ideal for building confidence in kelp diving

## Cold Water and Kelp Navigation

Monterey's 50-55°F water even at shallow depths requires 5mm+ thermal protection. Kelp forests can disorient less-experienced divers—stay near the pipe structures or use compass bearings. Buddy contact is essential in kelp. The good visibility at this site (for kelp diving) helps with navigation.

## Historical Context

The pipeline system represents Monterey's sardine fishing heritage. During the peak of the canning industry (1920s-1950s), these pipelines pumped sardines directly from fishing boats to the canneries. Today they create artificial reef structures supporting thriving marine life—an unintended ecological gift from industrial history.

## Safety Considerations

Monitor your air consumption carefully—kelp diving increases air density effects. Always use an SMB on ascent. Maintain buddy contact, especially in the kelp. Watch for unexpected current changes. The easy entry conditions and shallow depth make this an excellent site for building cold-water diving confidence.
