## Metridium Fields

Metridium Fields is a spectacular intermediate dive site featuring giant white anemones covering rocky reefs at San Carlos Beach, offering a world away from the crowded nearby Breakwater.

## Overview

While located at the same beach as the often-crowded Breakwater, Metridium Fields provides an entirely different diving experience. The site is defined by enormous white metridium anemones—pure white, flower-like anemones standing 2+ feet tall—covering the rocky reef structure. Navigation is straightforward: follow a large offshore pipe until it ends, then head due north for a short distance to reach the metridium-covered rocks.

## Site Information

- **Location**: San Carlos Beach, South of Cannery Row (same entry as Breakwater)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-15 meters (15-50 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 10-13°C (50-55°F) — requires 5-7mm wetsuit
- **Current**: Moderate, generally manageable
- **Best Time**: Year-round, calm to moderate conditions

## Marine Life and Features

The defining feature is the dense coverage of giant white metridium anemones — pure white, 60-centimeter-tall plume anemones covering low-profile rocky outcrops. These create an otherworldly underwater garden unique in Monterey. Beyond the metridiums, sources confirm rockfish, juvenile wolf eels, nudibranchs (sea lemons, clown dorids, hermissendas), swell sharks in rock crevices, and clusters of orange sunflower sea stars. A small school of surfperch is often present near the anemone fields.

## Dive Profile

Begin at San Carlos Beach and navigate to the pipe structure. Follow the pipe northward and downward, using it as your primary landmark. At roughly 10-16 meters, the metridium fields begin in earnest. The rocks are covered with these white anemones in various states of extension and contraction, creating a dramatic visual landscape. Use the pipe as your navigation anchor for the return ascent. Excellent macro photography potential.

## Entry and Exit

Enter via San Carlos Beach Park with good parking and facilities. The entry is identical to the Breakwater entry—straightforward sandy beach access. Current and swell are usually moderate. Exit in the same location, watching for surge on the final approach to shore.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Less crowded than the Breakwater despite same entry point
- Bring multiple batteries for macro/wide-angle cameras—this site demands photography
- Navigate by the visible pipe system to locate the metridium fields
- Best diving on moderate days when metridiums are extended
- Excellent for intermediate divers building kelp and structure navigation skills
- The site offers quieter, more contemplative diving than the nearby Breakwater

## Cold Water Diving

Monterey's 50-55°F water year-round demands proper thermal protection. Wear 5-7mm wetsuits or drysuits for this depth and duration. Cold water reduces buoyancy stress but increases hypothermia risk on extended dives.

## Safety Considerations

Maintain awareness of the current, which can increase with depth. Monitor your air consumption on intermediate dives—the cold water increases air density effects. Stay near the pipe and rocky structures to avoid disorientation. Always use an SMB on ascent. Buddy contact is essential, particularly when distracted by photography.
