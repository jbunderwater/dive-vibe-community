## Bluefish Cove (Point Lobos)

Bluefish Cove within Point Lobos State Reserve is one of Monterey's premier wall dives featuring drop-offs exceeding 100 feet, deep canyons, and some of Central California's finest hydrocoral specimens.

## Overview

Bluefish Cove is technically a distinct section within the greater Point Lobos State Reserve diving area, offering intermediate divers access to dramatic wall terrain without requiring the advanced skills of Monastery Beach. The wall is steep with deep canyons and valleys, and the rock surfaces display exceptional colorful encrusting marine life including bright pink and purple hydrocoral specimens—some of the largest in the region. Fish are exceptionally friendly to divers, having never experienced spearfishing.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Point Lobos State Reserve, Carmel Bay (accessed from Whalers Cove)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters (30-80 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 10-13°C (50-55°F) — requires 5-7mm wetsuit
- **Current**: Moderate, variable
- **Access Restrictions**: Maximum 15 buddy pairs per day; permits required; $10 diver registration + $10 car + $5 boat launch fee
- **Best Time**: September-November (calmer conditions, improved visibility), winter for nesting fish behavior

## Marine Life and Features

Bluefish Cove is famous for its outstanding fish watching and photography. Huge lingcod can be photographed at close range. During winter months, both lingcod and cabezon guard their nests on the wall, providing exceptional behavioral photography opportunities. The wall is blanketed with pink and purple hydrocoral creating a jeweled underwater landscape. Rockfish, wolf eels, nudibranchs, anemones, and sponges complete the vibrant ecosystem.

## Dive Profile

Enter at Whalers Cove and navigate to the Bluefish area. The bottom drops gradually then steeply to over 100 feet. Plan your dive between 20-35 meters where hydrocoral coverage and fish life are concentrated. The canyons and valleys carved into the wall provide endless points of interest. Ascend along the wall with careful buoyancy control.

## Entry and Exit: Point Lobos Access

Point Lobos State Reserve requires:
- Entry through main gate ($10/car)
- Diver registration ($10/diver)
- All entries/exits at Whalers Cove ramp only
- Compliance with 15 buddy pair daily maximum
- Proof of certification and valid diving permits

Whalers Cove's ramp entry is typically straightforward with good water access.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book permits early—summer months fill quickly with 15 buddy pairs per day limit
- This is one of Monterey's best fish photography sites—bring multiple cameras
- Winter dives offer nesting behavior and gray whale sightings along migration route
- The wall provides endless exploration—plan dives with deliberate focus areas
- This site is ideal for intermediate divers seeking wall experience without advanced-only dangers

## Cold Water and Depth Considerations

Monterey's 50-55°F water at 35 meters demands proper thermal protection (5-7mm wetsuits). Nitrogen narcosis begins at 35 meters—maintain buddy contact and exercise conservative depth limits. The vertical wall structure can lead to unintended rapid descent—monitor your depth gauge.

## Safety Considerations

Strictly comply with Point Lobos reserve regulations and daily buddy pair limits. Always use an SMB on ascent. Maintain buddy contact at depth and through surface conditions. Watch for current changes along the wall. Respect the reserve's marine sanctuary status.
