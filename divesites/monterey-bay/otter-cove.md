## Otter Cove

Otter Cove is a quiet, underutilized site offering solitude while diving, featuring one of Monterey's thickest and healthiest kelp beds and a large raft of sea otters that often forage in the area.

## Overview

Located at Ocean View Boulevard and Sea Palm Avenue in Pacific Grove, about 1/4 mile north of Lovers Point, Otter Cove is a bit north of popular Lovers Point and the famed dive sites along Cannery Row yet is a very underutilized site. This makes it an excellent choice for those seeking solitude while diving in Monterey Bay. The site features one of the thickest and healthiest kelp beds in Monterey Bay and a large raft of sea otters that often forages here.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ocean View Boulevard and Sea Palm Avenue, Pacific Grove
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Kelp
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-15 meters (15-50 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 10-13°C (50-55°F) — requires 5-7mm wetsuit
- **Current**: Minimal to moderate
- **Parking**: Free, unlimited parking on Ocean View Boulevard
- **Tidal Note**: Best diving at high tide for easier surface swimming
- **Best Time**: Year-round on calm days; high tide dives

## Marine Life and Features

The site's name comes from the large raft of sea otters that often forage here—one of Monterey Bay's most reliable otter populations. The rocky area features red strawberry anemones, orange cup corals, giant Metridium anemones, and a multitude of colorful sponges. The kelp bed is one of the thickest and healthiest in Monterey Bay, creating dense underwater forest structure. The combination of dense kelp and rich shallow-water invertebrate life creates exceptional ecosystem diversity.

## Dive Profile

Enter via one of two sets of stone steps to beach. Very shallow close to shore (2-4 meters), so divers should check tide tables and only dive at high tide for easier surface swimming. The best and deeper diving is found at the distant kelp forest (8-14 meters). Navigate to the kelp bed offshore, then explore the forest structure and surrounding rocks. The thick kelp canopy and metridium coverage create a labyrinthine underwater landscape. Ascend through the kelp while observing the canopy structure.

## Parking and Amenities

- Free, unlimited parking on Ocean View Boulevard
- Two sets of stone steps provide beach access
- No facilities at beach

## Tidal Considerations

**Critical**: Divers should check tide tables and only dive at high tide. Low and mid-tide conditions can make the shallow approach very difficult. High tide significantly improves surface swimming comfort and safety.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent beginner-friendly kelp diving site with minimal crowds
- One of the least crowded sites despite good kelp and marine life
- The large sea otter raft provides excellent wildlife observation
- High tide dives are essential for good conditions
- Bring cameras for macro nudibranch and anemone photography
- This site offers solitude—worth seeking out for those wanting quiet diving

## Why It's Underutilized

Despite proximity to Lovers Point and good diving conditions, Otter Cove remains less visited because:
- Tidal sensitivity (requires high tide planning)
- Less accessible parking than Cannery Row sites
- Location is less famous than adjacent Lovers Point

This is a benefit for divers seeking quieter experiences.

## Cold Water and Kelp Navigation

Monterey's 50-55°F water requires 5mm+ thermal protection even at these shallow depths. Kelp forests can disorient less-experienced divers—this site's generous depth range allows staying in shallow, familiar territory while exploring the kelp canopy. Buddy contact is essential in the thick kelp.

## Safety Considerations

**Only dive at high tide**—low tide conditions are dangerous. Monitor current, though usually minimal. Kelp forests demand excellent buoyancy control to avoid entanglement. Always use an SMB on ascent. Maintain buddy contact in the kelp. The beginner-friendly conditions and generally calm environment make this an excellent choice for building kelp diving confidence.
