## Cathedral Reef

Cathedral Reef is a cave dive site in Mauritius, Africa.

## Overview

La Cathédrale (the Cathedral) is widely regarded as Mauritius' most famous dive site, located five minutes by boat from Flic en Flac on the west coast. A drop-off combined with massive boulders has created a cave-like chamber where sunlight penetrates through rifts in the rock, producing cathedral-like shafts of light. The dive begins at around 12–18 metres and descends to around 28 metres, where the large cavern connects via arches, swim-throughs, and a chimney. Lobsters, moray eels, and reef fish including wrasses, sweetlips, and groupers inhabit the cracks and ledges. Whitetip reef sharks have been reported at the site in winter months. The dramatic topography makes this a sought-after dive for experienced divers and underwater photographers.

**Note on depth:** Most sources cite a maximum around 28–30 metres, though some aggregate databases list up to 37 metres. The 28m figure is used here as it reflects the majority of local operator descriptions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Flic en Flac, Mauritius
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
