# Dick

A drift dive along a flat-topped ridge reef west of Cape Liguan, where gorgonias, huge barrel sponges, and nudibranchs cover the reef top, and the western wall drops vertically past 40 meters.

## Overview

Dick is a ridge reef located west of Cape Liguan in the Rabaul area of East New Britain Province. The reef has a distinctive flat top adorned with gorgonian sea fans, huge barrel sponges, nudibranchs, flatworms, and anemones. The western side of the ridge transitions to a wall that drops more than 40 meters, with vertical sections providing dramatic topography. The dive is typically conducted as a drift, carried along the ridge by the current. Visibility is excellent, typically 25-30 meters, and the combination of macro life on the reef top and wall diving on the western flank makes Dick a satisfying two-in-one dive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West of Cape Liguan, Rabaul, East New Britain Province, Papua New Guinea
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-30 meters
- **Current**: Moderate (drift dive)
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest conditions April to November

## Marine Life

The flat reef top is rich in macro subjects. Gorgonian sea fans are plentiful, and huge barrel sponges dot the ridge -- some large enough to accommodate a diver. Nudibranchs and flatworms are found throughout, making this a productive site for critter hunters. Anemones with resident clownfish are common. On the wall face, soft corals and additional gorgonias cling to the vertical surfaces. The current that enables the drift also brings nutrients, supporting a healthy and diverse reef community.

## Dive Profile

Enter the water upstream and descend to the flat-topped ridge. The reef top provides the macro hunting ground -- spend time here examining gorgonias, barrel sponges, and reef surfaces for nudibranchs and flatworms. As the drift carries you along the ridge, move to the western edge where the wall drops vertically past 40 meters. Explore the wall face at a comfortable depth before ascending back to the reef top for the shallower portion of the dive. The current provides a comfortable pace along the ridge.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Rabaul-based operators. The boat positions upstream of the ridge based on current direction. Entry is by giant stride. The drift carries divers along the ridge. Deploy an SMB for pickup at the end of the drift.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring both macro and wide-angle setups if possible -- the reef top rewards macro and the wall rewards wide-angle
- Examine barrel sponges closely -- some are enormous and make excellent photographic subjects
- Slow down on the reef top to search for nudibranchs and flatworms hiding among the coral
- Watch your depth on the western wall -- the vertical drop can pull you deeper than planned

## Safety Considerations

The drift is typically manageable for intermediate divers, but current strength can vary. The western wall drops to significant depth, so monitor your depth gauge carefully. Carry an SMB, whistle, and signalling devices. Stay with your buddy and guide throughout the drift.
