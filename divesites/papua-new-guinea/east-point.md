# East Point

A wall dive at the eastern point of Urara Island where a gentle slope gives way to a vertical wall plunging 40+ meters, adorned with gorgonias, huge sponges, and colourful soft corals.

## Overview

East Point is located at the eastern extremity of Urara Island in the Rabaul area of East New Britain Province. The dive begins on a slope that gradually steepens before transitioning to a vertical wall dropping more than 40 meters. The wall and slope are covered in gorgonian sea fans, huge sponges, anemones, and soft corals, creating a richly textured underwater landscape. Fish life includes unicornfish, red bass, and sweetlips, with schools of fish concentrated along the wall edge. Visibility is typically around 20 meters, and the site is accessible to intermediate divers comfortable with wall diving.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Eastern point of Urara Island, Rabaul, East New Britain Province, Papua New Guinea
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40+ meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest conditions April to November

## Marine Life

The wall and slope support diverse marine life. Gorgonian sea fans are plentiful on the wall face. Huge sponges, including barrel sponges, anchor to the slope and wall. Anemones with clownfish dot the reef. Schools of unicornfish cruise along the wall edge. Red bass (two-spot red snapper) and sweetlips are commonly encountered. Soft corals add splashes of colour throughout, particularly on the deeper wall sections.

## Dive Profile

Descend on the slope near the eastern point of Urara Island. The terrain begins as a gentle slope, steepening as you move toward the point where it transitions to a vertical wall. Follow the wall face at a comfortable depth -- the most productive zone for gorgonias and fish life is typically between 15 and 30 meters. The wall drops beyond 40 meters, so maintain depth awareness. Work your way back toward the slope for a gradual ascent and safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Rabaul-based operators. Entry is by giant stride near the eastern point. The dive follows the wall and returns to the slope. Surface near the point and signal the boat for pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Start deep and work shallow to make the most of your bottom time on the wall
- Look for red bass sheltering under overhangs and ledges on the wall face
- The huge sponges make excellent wide-angle photography subjects when shot against the blue water backdrop
- The slope section near the top is good for a relaxed safety stop among the soft corals

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops to significant depth, so monitor your depth gauge carefully. Current is generally mild but can increase around the point. Carry an SMB for safety stops away from the reef. Stay within your depth limits and plan your gas management to allow a comfortable ascent.
