# Christmas Tree

An advanced wall dive outside Port Moresby's barrier reef, past Basilisk Passage -- named for a bommie resembling a Christmas tree, with a wall dropping past 55 meters, a gully to 45 meters with large gorgonias, and grey reef sharks.

## Overview

Christmas Tree is located outside the barrier reef near Port Moresby, past Basilisk Passage. The site is named after a large coral bommie whose shape resembles a Christmas tree. The main feature is a wall that drops past 55 meters, with Mark's Ravine -- a gully cutting into the wall face -- descending to 45 meters and lined with large gorgonian sea fans. Grey reef sharks patrol the wall edge, and humpheaded (Napoleon) wrasse are regular visitors. The exposed open-water location and depth make this an advanced dive, but the combination of dramatic wall topography, deep gully exploration, and shark encounters rewards those with the experience and gas management skills to dive it safely. Visibility is typically around 25 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Outside barrier reef past Basilisk Passage, Port Moresby, Papua New Guinea
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 55 meters (Mark's Ravine to 45 meters; wall continues beyond)
- **Typical Visibility**: 25 meters
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: Year-round; sea conditions may limit access

## Marine Life

Grey reef sharks are the headline species, patrolling the wall edge and the blue water beyond. Humpheaded (Napoleon) wrasse are regularly encountered. The gorgonian sea fans lining Mark's Ravine are large and photogenic. The wall itself supports the typical Port Moresby outer-reef community of hard and soft corals, sponges, and reef fish. The depth and exposure attract pelagic species that are less commonly seen on shallower, sheltered sites.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wall and locate the Christmas Tree bommie as your reference point. Mark's Ravine is the key feature -- a gully cutting into the wall face that descends to approximately 45 meters, lined with large gorgonias. Exploration of the gully requires careful depth management, as the wall beyond continues past 55 meters. Spend your deep time in the ravine observing the gorgonias and watching for grey reef sharks, then ascend along the wall face where the coral and fish life continues. The Christmas Tree bommie at a shallower depth serves as a good reference for navigation and a productive area for your safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Port Moresby, transiting past Basilisk Passage to the outer reef. The exposed location means sea conditions may prevent access on rough days. Entry is by giant stride. Deploy an SMB for ascent and boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This is a deep dive requiring proper gas management -- plan your bottom time at depth carefully
- Mark's Ravine is the highlight; prioritize it while you have the air and no-deco time for the deeper section
- Grey reef sharks may approach closely -- remain calm and enjoy the encounter
- Nitrox can extend safety margins but watch your MOD at depth
- Ask your operator about sea conditions before committing -- the exposed location can be rough

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops well past 55 meters, and Mark's Ravine reaches 45 meters -- this is firmly advanced diving territory. No-decompression time is severely limited at these depths. Monitor depth, time, and gas supply constantly. Grey reef sharks are generally not aggressive but should be treated with respect. Current can be significant at the outer reef -- carry an SMB and signalling devices. The boat transit past the barrier reef means surface conditions may be challenging for boat re-entry.
