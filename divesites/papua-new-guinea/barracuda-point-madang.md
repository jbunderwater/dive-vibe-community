# Barracuda Point Madang

One of the best dives around Pig Island in Madang -- a current-swept point named for the large schools of military seapike (barracuda) that gather here in groups of thirty or more.

## Overview

Barracuda Point is widely regarded as one of the top dives in the Pig Island area of Madang Lagoon. The site takes its name from the resident schools of chevron barracuda -- locally called military seapike -- that form silvery columns of thirty or more fish above the reef edge. The reef top sits at 3-5 meters, dropping away along a sloping wall to deeper water. Current is always present at this site; the direction and strength shift with the tidal cycle, and the boat crew will assess conditions before choosing the entry point and drift direction.

The combination of reliable current, a healthy shallow reef top, and an impressive roster of large pelagic visitors makes this a standout site in the Madang area. Divers should be comfortable in moderate to strong flow and able to manage buoyancy near a shallow reef crest.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Pig Island, Madang Lagoon, Papua New Guinea
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate to Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Reef Top**: 3-5 meters
- **Current**: Always present; direction varies with tide
- **Best Time**: April to November (dry season), though diveable year-round

## Marine Life

The headline attraction is the barracuda -- schools of thirty or more chevron barracuda (military seapike) that hang in the current along the reef edge. Blacktip reef sharks and whitetip reef sharks patrol the deeper sections of the wall. Huge double-headed Maori wrasse (humphead or Napoleon wrasse) are a regular sighting here, with individuals reaching up to two meters in length. Hump-headed parrotfish move in groups across the reef, and large schools of batfish drift in the mid-water.

Mackerel and trevally hunt in the current upwelling along the point. Clown triggerfish add color to the reef slope. The shallow reef top at 3-5 meters supports healthy hard coral growth with the usual complement of reef fish -- damselfish, anthias, butterflyfish, and anemones with resident clownfish.

## Dive Profile

The dive is conducted as a drift, with the direction determined by the prevailing tidal current. The boat drops divers up-current of the point and picks them up on the other side. Descend quickly to the reef top at 3-5 meters and move along the edge, dropping deeper along the wall as conditions and air supply allow. The barracuda schools and sharks are typically found along the current-facing side of the point in mid-water between 10 and 25 meters. The deeper wall offers encounters with larger pelagics but reduces bottom time significantly at 30-40 meters.

Work back to the reef top for your safety stop, where the coral garden provides plenty to see during the final minutes of the dive. Deploy an SMB before surfacing so the boat can track your position.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Madang-based operators, with a short ride to Pig Island. Entry is by giant stride into the current. The boat crew will brief the drift direction and pickup plan before each dive. Carry an SMB and whistle -- in current, surface visibility from the boat is essential. Exit is by pickup from the trailing boat.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Listen carefully to the boat crew's briefing on current direction -- conditions change with the tide and the dive plan adjusts accordingly
- Carry a reef hook if you want to hold position in the current to watch the barracuda schools or sharks
- The shallow reef top at 3-5 meters is excellent for an extended safety stop and is often where the Maori wrasse are spotted
- Wide-angle lens is essential for the barracuda schools and large pelagics
- Nitrox extends bottom time for those wanting to spend longer at the 15-25 meter range

## Safety Considerations

Current is always present and can be strong, particularly on spring tides. This site is not suitable for divers who are uncomfortable in current. The shallow reef top at 3-5 meters means swell can create surge near the surface -- maintain awareness when ascending. Stay with your buddy and the group, as separation in current can result in being carried away from the pickup area. Always carry an SMB, whistle, and ideally a signaling mirror. The maximum depth of 40 meters limits bottom time on air -- plan gas management conservatively.

## Photography

Wide-angle is the priority here. The barracuda schools make dramatic subjects when backlit against the surface or shot from below with the reef edge as backdrop. The Maori wrasse are habituated to divers and often approach closely, offering excellent portrait opportunities. Blacktip and whitetip sharks in the blue water beyond the reef edge reward patient photographers with clear backgrounds. The reef top at 3-5 meters provides good natural light for hard coral and clown triggerfish shots without strobes.
