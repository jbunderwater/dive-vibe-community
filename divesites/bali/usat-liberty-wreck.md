## USAT Liberty Wreck

One of the world's most accessible wreck dives — a WWII-era US Army cargo ship torpedoed on 11 January 1942, now lying parallel to shore just 25 metres offshore at Tulamben, accessible without a boat.

## Overview

The USAT Liberty was a US Army cargo ship launched on 19 June 1918 at the Federal Shipbuilding Company in Kearny, New Jersey. On 11 January 1942, it was torpedoed by the Japanese submarine I-66 while carrying railway parts and rubber from Australia to the Philippines, approximately 10 nautical miles southwest of the Lombok Strait. Two destroyers (USS Paul Jones and HNLMS Van Ghent) towed the stricken vessel toward Singaraja, but she was taking on too much water and was instead beached on the black-sand shore at Tulamben, Bali. The ship remained on the beach until tremors from the 1963 eruption of Mount Agung caused it to slide off into the sea, where it now lies.

Wikipedia records the vessel's length as 411 ft 6 in (approximately 125 metres); dive operator descriptions commonly give the figure as 120 metres. The wreck lies parallel to shore roughly 25 metres out, spanning 3 to 30 metres depth, with the upper structure at 3–5 metres. The hull is completely encrusted in soft corals, gorgonian fans, and sponges, making this one of the world's great shore dives and one of Indonesia's most-visited dive sites.

- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
