# Staghorn Crest

Extensive staghorn coral formations blanket the reef crest above a soft-coral-draped wall, creating one of Sipadan's healthiest and most colourful shallow reef environments

## Overview
Staghorn Crest is named for the dense fields of staghorn coral (Acropora) that dominate the reef crest before the wall drops away. This site showcases Sipadan's reef health at its finest — the staghorn formations are extensive and pristine, creating a complex three-dimensional habitat that teems with reef fish. The shallow coral gardens transition to a wall below where soft corals take over, providing a striking contrast between the hard coral plateau and the soft coral wall. Turtles graze among the staghorn fields, and the healthy reef supports a rich community of butterflyfish, angelfish, and damselfish. The moderate depth and generally manageable currents make this an accessible and visually rewarding dive.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Sipadan Island, Sabah, Malaysia (western side)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 metres
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; can be stronger in sections
- **Best Time**: Year-round; afternoon light conditions are particularly good for photography

## Marine Life
The staghorn coral fields are the foundation of the ecosystem here. These branching formations provide shelter for thousands of small fish — damselfish, chromis, and anthias swarm above the coral, creating clouds of colour. Red seawhip corals and black coral formations (reported up to 2 metres in width on the deeper wall sections) add to the structural complexity. Barrel sponges are common. Angelfish, groupers, nudibranchs, triggerfish, shrimp, and gobies are all confirmed here. Green and hawksbill turtles are regularly sighted, resting on ledges and grazing on the crest. Whitetip reef sharks pass along the wall edge. The deeper sections may on occasion produce sightings of hammerhead or thresher sharks, though such encounters are infrequent and not guaranteed at this specific site.

## Dive Profile
Begin on the reef crest exploring the staghorn coral fields and their associated marine life. Work gradually toward the wall edge where the hard coral transitions to the wall face, featuring red seawhip corals and black coral formations. Descend along the wall — the site features a plateau at around 15 metres with many cracks and ledges full of life, followed by a wall that continues well beyond recreational limits. A multi-level profile works well: spend time on the wall, then ascend to the shallow coral gardens for the safety stop phase. Afternoon dives here have particularly good natural light conditions on the western-facing wall sections. The site can also be dived as a gentle drift in moderate current.

## Entry and Exit
Boat dive from Mabul or Kapalai island resorts. Giant stride entry onto the reef crest area. The moderate conditions and shallow profile make entry and exit straightforward. Deploy an SMB during ascent for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Spend ample time on the reef crest — the staghorn coral gardens are the highlight and are best appreciated slowly
- Excellent buoyancy is essential in the coral gardens — the staghorn formations are brittle and easily broken by fin strikes
- Look for hawkfish perched on sea fans and gorgonians along the wall — they hold still for photographers
- Sipadan permits (176 per day) are required and must be arranged through your resort
- The shallow profile makes this an ideal late-afternoon dive when no-decompression limits are reduced from earlier deep dives

## Safety Considerations
The shallow depth and moderate currents make this one of Sipadan's most forgiving sites, but care is still needed. The brittle staghorn coral formations can cause cuts if a diver makes contact, and the damage to the coral is disproportionate — a single fin kick can destroy years of growth. Maintain a safe distance above the reef at all times. An SMB and signalling device are standard equipment. The nearest hyperbaric chamber is in Kota Kinabalu.

## Photography
The staghorn coral fields photographed from above with natural light create striking reef-scape images. Afternoon dives here benefit from particularly good natural light conditions on the western-facing sections. Wide-angle with a slow shutter for ambient light works well in the shallow, bright conditions. Turtles resting on ledges provide strong foreground subjects. Macro opportunities include gobies on coral heads, nudibranch details, and the resident shrimp. Red seawhip corals on the wall make vivid close-focus wide-angle subjects when lit by strobe.
