# Hanging Gardens

A vertical wall draped in cascading soft corals and enormous gorgonian sea fans — widely regarded as the most beautiful coral wall on Sipadan

## Overview
Hanging Gardens takes its name from the spectacular display of Dendronephthya alcyonarian soft corals that adorn the terraced wall face like a lush vertical garden. Located on Sipadan's northeastern side, this section of wall drops to around 70 metres before the wall face transitions to an abyss. The alcyonarian soft corals cascade in pastel shades of lemon, lavender, and antique rose from overhang to overhang — quite different from the vivid colours seen on well-lit tropical reefs. Enormous gorgonian fans extend into the current to filter-feed at depth. The visual impact of the terraced wall is widely regarded as the most photogenic on the island. The marine life is typical Sipadan calibre: turtles, reef sharks, Napoleon wrasse, and schooling fish, all set against an extraordinarily colourful backdrop.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Sipadan Island, Sabah, Malaysia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 metres
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: April to December for best visibility

## Marine Life
The coral growth is the primary attraction — dense fields of Dendronephthya alcyonarians in pastel shades of lemon, lavender, and rose overflow from terrace to terrace, while massive gorgonian sea fans, black coral bushes, and barrel sponges create a vertical reef garden unlike anything else on the island. Green and hawksbill turtles cruise along the wall and rest on ledges between the coral formations. Whitetip reef sharks patrol the deeper wall sections. Napoleon wrasse are regularly sighted, their curiosity often bringing them close to divers. Anthias, damselfish, and butterflyfish swarm around the coral heads. Pygmy seahorses have been found on gorgonian fans by sharp-eyed guides. Crinoids in brilliant yellows and reds perch on the sea fans.

## Dive Profile
The wall begins just over a metre from the surface and descends through a series of terraces to around 70 metres before dropping into the abyss. Drift along the wall starting at 25-30 metres where the largest gorgonian fans and the most dramatic Dendronephthya alcyonarian displays are found on the deeper terraces. Rise gradually along the wall face, taking in the changing coral composition at different depths. The shallow reef crest at 3-5 metres offers hard coral gardens with turtles and reef fish for the safety stop. Current here is typically milder than at the exposed points, allowing more time to hover and appreciate the coral formations. Buoyancy control is paramount — the delicate alcyonarian soft corals are easily damaged by contact.

## Entry and Exit
Boat dive from Mabul or Kapalai island resorts. Giant stride entry. The northeastern aspect of this site means it is often sheltered from prevailing currents, providing more relaxed diving conditions. Deploy an SMB during ascent for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Spend time at 20-30 metres where the most spectacular soft coral and gorgonian displays are found
- Ask your divemaster to point out pygmy seahorses on the gorgonian fans — they are present but extremely difficult to spot
- Maintain perfect buoyancy control throughout — the hanging corals are extremely fragile and a single fin kick can destroy decades of growth
- Sipadan permits (176 per day) must be arranged through your resort in advance
- This is widely regarded as the best site on Sipadan for coral wall photography — bring a wide-angle lens and allow plenty of dive time at depth where the alcyonarians are at their finest

## Safety Considerations
Current conditions are generally more moderate than at the island's exposed points, but can increase without warning. The vertical wall still demands careful depth management — it is easy to drift below planned depth while focused on the coral displays. An SMB and signalling device are essential. Avoid all contact with the wall — the soft corals and sea fans are slow-growing and extremely delicate. The nearest hyperbaric chamber is in Kota Kinabalu.

## Photography
This is widely regarded as Sipadan's premier photography site. Wide-angle is essential for capturing the scale of the gorgonian fans and the cascading alcyonarian soft coral terraces — position a diver or turtle in the frame for scale against the wall. Close-focus wide-angle works beautifully for individual coral heads with the blue water background. Macro photographers can search the gorgonians for pygmy seahorses and the crinoids for commensal shrimp. The depth of the best alcyonarian coral displays (20-30m) means artificial light from strobes is critical to render the pastel colours faithfully — at these depths, natural light shifts strongly toward blue and the lemon and lavender tones appear as shades of grey without strobe illumination.
