# Mid Reef

A current-swept wall between Barracuda Point and South Point where upwellings attract pelagics and schooling fish above a coral-covered reef crest

## Overview
Mid Reef sits on Sipadan's eastern wall between the island's two most famous current-exposed points — Barracuda Point to the north and South Point to the south. The site benefits from the strong currents that sweep along this stretch of coastline, creating nutrient-rich upwellings that attract pelagic visitors and sustain dense schools of reef fish. The reef crest features healthy coral gardens before the wall drops away, offering a combination of reef-top colour and blue-water encounters. Dived as a drift, the current does most of the work, carrying divers along the wall while marine life parades past.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Sipadan Island, Sabah, Malaysia (eastern wall, between Barracuda Point and South Point)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters (wall descends well beyond; safety stop over 6-metre hard coral flat)
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 metres
- **Current**: Moderate to strong — can also push upward due to cold water thermoclines rising from depth
- **Best Time**: April to December

## Marine Life
The current-driven conditions bring higher densities of fish than many comparable Sipadan sites. Large schools of moorish idols, redtooth triggerfish, unicornfish, and bannerfish race along the wall. Forster's hawkfish are commonly seen perching on gorgonians. Five-lined cardinalfish and harlequin sweetlips move furtively under ledges and overhangs. Sulu fangblennies form aggregations among the gorgonian fans. Large green and hawksbill turtles — very accustomed to divers — are abundant throughout the dive. Whitetip reef sharks and Napoleon wrasse are seen regularly. The hard coral flat at the top of the wall (around 6 metres) hosts dense coral growth including knotted navy sea fans, orange gorgonian fans, black coral, and staghorn, table, and cabbage corals, making it one of the most productive safety-stop zones on the island. Schools of bumphead parrotfish are sometimes encountered on the coral flat in groups of 40 or more.

## Dive Profile
This is a drift dive along the eastern wall, between Barracuda Point to the north and South Point to the south. Enter and descend along the wall, drifting with the current. Be aware that currents here can push upward as well as laterally when cold water thermoclines rise from depth — monitor your buoyancy accordingly. The harder fish life is in the 15-30 metre range along the wall, while the coral flat at 6 metres rewards an extended safety stop. Spend time scanning the blue water off the wall for sharks and pelagics, then work up to the reef crest. The current can accelerate in sections — maintain awareness and be ready for pickup.

## Entry and Exit
Boat dive from Mabul or Kapalai island resorts. Giant stride entry into the current. The divemaster will assess current direction and place the entry accordingly for a productive drift. Deploy an SMB during ascent for boat pickup — the current can move divers quickly along the wall. The boat follows the divers' bubbles.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Relax into the drift and let the current carry you — fighting it wastes air and energy
- Scan the blue water constantly as pelagics can appear from any direction in the current
- The reef crest coral gardens are worth extended exploration during the shallow phase of the dive
- Sipadan permits (176 per day) must be arranged through your resort in advance
- This site pairs well with Barracuda Point or South Point for a two-dive morning session along the eastern wall

## Safety Considerations
Currents can be stronger than expected given the intermediate rating, particularly in sections where the wall geometry funnels the flow. Maintain buddy contact and carry an SMB, whistle, and signalling mirror. Do not fight the current — if separated from the group, ascend safely and deploy your SMB. The nearest hyperbaric chamber is in Kota Kinabalu.

## Photography
Wide-angle for reef-scape compositions along the coral-covered wall edge, with schooling fish and the blue water background. Turtles on the reef crest are reliable subjects. The drift conditions make steady shooting challenging in stronger current — brace yourself or use the reef structure (without touching coral) to stabilize. The shallow reef crest in good light produces vibrant ambient-light images of the coral gardens.
