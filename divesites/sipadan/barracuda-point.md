# Barracuda Point

Sipadan's most iconic dive site, where a massive tornado of over a thousand chevron barracuda spirals against a wall dropping 600 metres into the Celebes Sea

## Overview
Barracuda Point occupies the northern tip of Sipadan Island, where converging currents sweep nutrient-rich water along a vertical wall that plunges from a shallow reef crest at 5 metres to beyond 600 metres. This exposed promontory acts as a natural aggregation point for pelagic life, and the site is defined by the enormous resident school of chevron barracuda — often exceeding a thousand individuals — that form the iconic tornado formation Sipadan is famous for worldwide. The currents here are among the strongest on the island, creating an exhilarating drift dive that regularly delivers encounters with whitetip reef sharks, grey reef sharks, green and hawksbill turtles, and massive schools of bigeye jacks.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Sipadan Island, Sabah, Malaysia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-40 metres
- **Current**: Strong to very strong — often the most current-exposed site on the island
- **Best Time**: April to December (calmest seas), though barracuda are resident year-round

## Marine Life
The chevron barracuda school is the headline attraction. Hundreds to over a thousand fish form a swirling vortex that can extend from the reef top down along the wall, shifting shape as the current changes. Schools of bigeye jacks — sometimes numbering in the thousands — create their own shimmering formations nearby. Whitetip reef sharks and grey reef sharks patrol the wall edge, particularly in the deeper sections below 20 metres. Green and hawksbill turtles are seen on virtually every dive, resting on wall ledges or cruising along the reef crest. Bumphead parrotfish pass through in groups during early morning dives. Napoleon wrasse, giant trevally, and occasional eagle rays round out the pelagic cast. The wall itself hosts soft corals, sea fans, and crinoids at depth.

## Dive Profile
This is typically dived as a drift dive along the wall. Enter in the blue water off the point with a negative or rapid descent to avoid being swept past the site by surface current. The barracuda tornado is usually encountered between 10 and 25 metres — approach slowly and allow the school to envelop you rather than swimming into it. Drift along the wall checking the blue water for sharks and jacks. The wall is sheer and featureless in places below 30 metres, so most of the action is in the 10-25 metre range. Ascend gradually along the wall and perform your safety stop over the reef crest where turtles graze.

## Entry and Exit
Boat dive from Mabul or Kapalai island resorts, approximately 15 minutes by speedboat. Giant stride entry. The divemaster will assess current direction and strength before choosing the entry point. Deploy an SMB during your safety stop — currents can carry divers well past the point, and boat pickup in the blue is common. Listen carefully to the briefing on current management.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Do not chase or swim into the barracuda tornado — stay still and let the school circle around you for the best experience and photographs
- Dive early morning for the best chance of bumphead parrotfish passing through
- Carry a reef hook if your operator permits — useful for holding position in strong current while watching the barracuda
- Sipadan permits are limited to 176 divers per day and must be arranged through your resort well in advance
- Nitrox is recommended to extend bottom time in the 15-25 metre zone where the best encounters occur

## Safety Considerations
Strong and unpredictable currents are the primary hazard. Down-currents along the wall can pull divers to depth rapidly — monitor your computer constantly and be prepared to fin away from the wall into open water if caught in a downward pull. An SMB, whistle, and mirror are essential signalling devices. The nearest hyperbaric chamber is in Kota Kinabalu (approximately one hour by air from Semporna). Never fight a current — signal the boat and drift if necessary. This site is not suitable for inexperienced divers.

## Photography
Wide-angle is essential. The barracuda tornado against the deep blue wall backdrop is the signature Sipadan image and one of the most photographed underwater scenes in the world. Position yourself inside or just below the vortex for dramatic compositions. Schools of bigeye jacks provide additional wide-angle opportunities. The wall ledges with resting turtles and patrolling sharks offer strong subject-and-background compositions. Strobes are useful for bringing out the silver of the barracuda but ambient light shots can also be striking for the tornado silhouette.
