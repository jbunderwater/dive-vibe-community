## K2

K2 is a submerged seamount on the northeast side of Gili Banta (Banta Island), close to the boundary of Komodo National Park. The seamount peaks at approximately 7 metres below the surface and descends to 25–40 metres, forming a coral garden that attracts large pelagics on the current-swept sides.

## Overview

K2 is a seamount dive distinct from the exposed, current-raked conditions of nearby Rollercoaster. The top of the mount sits at around 7 m, forming a coral garden plateau before the flanks drop into deeper water. Strong currents sweeping past the seamount concentrate pelagic species: barracuda, dogtooth tuna, Spanish mackerel, and sharks are regularly encountered on the current-facing sides. The coral gardens on the plateau and upper flanks support a dense community of soft and hard corals, angelfish, butterflyfish, parrotfish, and fusiliers. Intermediate divers can work the shallower plateau while more experienced divers explore the deeper flanks. Access by liveaboard or day trip from Kalimaya Dive Resort (~40 minutes) or other east Sumbawa operators. Best season April to November.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northeast of Gili Banta, Sumbawa / Komodo border
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (liveaboard or day charter)
- **Site Type**: Reef / seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 metres (seamount peak ~7 m)
- **Currents**: Moderate to strong on current-facing sides
- **Best Season**: April–November

## Marine Life

Barracuda, dogtooth tuna, Spanish mackerel, sharks, angelfish, butterflyfish, parrotfish, fusiliers, soft and hard corals.

## Dive Profile

Ascend to the plateau at 7 m and explore the coral gardens before dropping to the flanks. Pelagics are concentrated on the upcurrent side. Max depth 25 m for intermediate; deeper possible on flanks.
