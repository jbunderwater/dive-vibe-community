## Los Cabos

Diving at the tip of the Baja Peninsula where the Pacific Ocean meets the Sea of Cortez, offering everything from calm reef dives and underwater sand waterfalls to offshore seamounts with schooling hammerhead sharks.

## Description

Los Cabos encompasses the twin towns of Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo at the southern tip of the Baja California Peninsula. The convergence of the Pacific Ocean and the Sea of Cortez creates a unique marine environment where tropical, subtropical, and temperate species overlap. Dive sites range from 2 to 40 meters depth, with beginner-friendly reef dives along the protected Corridor coastline, dramatic walls and sandfalls in the Cabo San Lucas Marine Park, and world-class pelagic diving at the offshore Gordo Banks seamount. Jacques Cousteau called the Sea of Cortez "the world's aquarium," and the Los Cabos area lives up to that reputation with sea lions, mobula rays, hammerhead sharks, whale sharks, and dense reef life.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Cabo San Lucas Marine Park**: The core dive area — Pelican Rock (the most popular site, beginner friendly), Sand Falls (Cousteau-famous underwater sand waterfall, advanced), Neptune's Finger (largest sandfall plus wall dive), North Wall (excellent night diving), and Land's End at El Arco (sea lion colony where the Pacific meets the Cortez)
- **The Corridor**: A stretch of coastline between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo with protected reef sites — Whale's Head (eagle rays, cleaning station), Blow Hole (boulders and gorgonians), Santa Maria Bay (marine sanctuary), Chileno Bay (finger reefs with whitetip sharks), Gavilanes (healthy coral ridges), and Palmilla (twin reef systems with gorgonians)
- **San Jose del Cabo**: El Salinar Shipwreck — a shallow wreck split into three sections, good for beginners and macro photography
- **Offshore**: Gordo Banks seamount, approximately 11–13 km (7–8 miles) offshore — schooling hammerhead sharks, silky sharks, tuna, and marlin. Advanced only with strong currents and blue water descents

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Los Cabos International Airport (SJD) receives direct flights from most major US and Canadian cities, plus connections via Mexico City. The airport is approximately 30 minutes from Cabo San Lucas and 15 minutes from San Jose del Cabo
- **Dive Operators**: Numerous PADI and SSI dive shops operate from the Cabo San Lucas marina, with boats departing daily for marine park and Corridor sites. Gordo Banks trips run weather-dependent, typically requiring advance booking
- **Accommodation**: Full range from budget hotels in San Jose del Cabo to luxury resorts along the Corridor and in Cabo San Lucas
- **Transportation**: Airport shuttles, taxis, and rental cars serve the area. Most dive operators offer hotel pickup. The Corridor connects the two towns via a four-lane highway
- **Facilities**: Full tourist infrastructure including hospitals, dive shops with rental equipment, international ATMs, and restaurants. The nearest hyperbaric chamber is in the Cabo San Lucas area

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature ranges from 20°C (68°F) in winter to 28°C (82°F) in summer, warmest July through October. Visibility varies from 10 to 30 meters depending on site and season. The Pacific side of Land's End has more surge and current than the sheltered Sea of Cortez side
- **Marine Biodiversity**: California sea lions (resident colony at Land's End), spotted eagle rays, cow-nose rays, mobula rays (seasonal), green sea turtles, whitetip reef sharks, nurse sharks, guitarfish, moray eels, Cortez angelfish, king angelfish, pufferfish, schools of jacks and snappers, garden eels, octopus, and spiny lobsters. At Gordo Banks: schooling hammerhead sharks, silky sharks, yellowfin tuna, marlin, and occasional whale sharks (sightings not predictable)
- **Conservation**: The Cabo San Lucas Marine Park protects the bay and surrounding waters. Santa Maria Bay is a designated marine sanctuary. Fishing is prohibited within the marine park boundaries

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: July to October for warmest water, best visibility, and calmest conditions. October to March for humpback whale sightings. Hammerhead sharks at Gordo Banks run August to January (peak September–December). Year-round diving is possible with most Corridor sites diveable in all seasons
- **Currency**: Mexican Peso (MXN). US Dollars widely accepted in tourist areas but at unfavorable exchange rates
- **Language**: Spanish; English widely spoken in tourist areas and by dive operators
- **Safety**: Marine park and Corridor sites are suitable for all levels, with many beginner-friendly options. The Pacific side of Land's End and Gordo Banks require intermediate to advanced experience due to currents and depth. Jellyfish can be present seasonally. Boat traffic is heavy around the marina and El Arco — always deploy an SMB before surfacing
