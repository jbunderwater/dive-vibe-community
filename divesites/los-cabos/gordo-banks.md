## Gordo Banks

A pair of submerged seamounts (Inner and Outer Gordo) located approximately 7-10 miles offshore from the Puerto Los Cabos marina in San Jose del Cabo — roughly 80-90 minutes by boat each way. The Inner Bank's peak rises to around 30-35 meters below the surface; the Outer Bank is deeper and beyond recreational limits for most dives. This is the premier big-animal site in Los Cabos, where schooling hammerhead sharks, silky sharks, yellowfin tuna, wahoo, and marlin patrol the open water around the seamount. The rocky surface is covered in black coral and wire coral. The dive involves a blue water descent with no wall or line to follow, strong and unpredictable currents, and a remote open-ocean environment. Advanced certification and solid deep-water experience are required; most operators mandate Advanced Open Water or higher with Nitrox.

## Site Information

- **Location**: 7-10 miles offshore from Puerto Los Cabos marina, San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters (open ocean)
- **Current**: Strong and unpredictable
- **Best Time**: Late August to January for hammerhead sharks; whale sharks visit seasonally

## Marine Life

Schooling scalloped hammerhead sharks are the main attraction — best season is late August through January. Silky sharks, yellowfin tuna, wahoo, and marlin are regularly encountered at the seamount. Manta rays and giant mobula rays also visit. Large schools of jacks, snappers, creole fish, and bonito aggregate around the structure. Humpback whales and dolphins pass through seasonally. Whale sharks have been reported at this site.

## Dive Profile

The boat anchors or drifts over the seamount. Entry is a blue water descent with no visual reference to follow down. Descend quickly to the seamount top at 30-35 meters and position yourself where the current channels pelagic traffic past the structure. Bottom time is short at this depth. Ascent is in open blue water with no wall to follow — deploy an SMB before ascending for pickup. Nitrox is required by most operators.

## Safety Considerations

This is a challenging open-ocean dive with no shelter, no shallow areas, and strong currents that change unpredictably. Blue water descents and ascents require composure and experience. Narcosis at 35-40 meters is a real concern. The boat ride is long and can be rough in swell. Not suitable for divers without significant open water and deep diving experience. Carry an SMB, whistle, and mirror. Dive with a computer and agree on strict turn pressures before entering. Most operators require AOW certification minimum and Nitrox certification.
