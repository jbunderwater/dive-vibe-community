## Gordo Banks

A submerged seamount system offshore from San Jose del Cabo. The seamount's highest peak sits at approximately 30–31 meters below the surface. Multiple sources describe the distance from San Jose del Cabo marina as approximately 7 miles (11–13 km), though some operators describe the site as farther offshore depending on which bank is being dived. This is the premier big-animal dive in Los Cabos — schooling scalloped hammerhead sharks, silky sharks, yellowfin tuna, marlin, and wahoo patrol the seamount. The dive is an open blue water descent with no visual reference and strong, unpredictable current.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Offshore from San Jose del Cabo marina (approximately 7 miles / 11–13 km)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 12-30 meters (open ocean)
- **Current**: Strong and unpredictable
- **Best Time**: August to January for scalloped hammerheads; September to December for peak conditions overall

## Marine Life

Scalloped hammerhead sharks are the primary attraction, with the best season from late August to January. Silky sharks, yellowfin tuna, wahoo, marlin, and large schools of jacks aggregate around the seamount. Mobula rays gather in large formations, particularly June–August and November–December. Whale sharks visit occasionally. Humpback whale songs can be heard underwater during the winter migration (December through March). Orcas and pelagic manta rays are rare but documented.

## Dive Profile

The boat drifts or anchors over the seamount. Entry is a blue water descent — no wall or line to follow down. Descend directly to the seamount top at approximately 30 meters and position where current delivers pelagic traffic past the structure. Bottom time is limited at depth. Ascend in open blue water and deploy an SMB for pickup. Nitrox is strongly recommended to extend bottom time safely. Most operators require a minimum of Advanced Open Water certification and a checkout dive before joining this trip.

## Safety Considerations

An exposed open-ocean dive with no shelter, no shallow areas, and strong currents. Blue water descents and ascents require composure and open water experience. Narcosis at 35–40 meters is a real concern. The boat ride is 45–90 minutes each way depending on conditions and operator. Not suitable for divers without significant open water and deep diving experience. Carry an SMB, audible signal, and mirror. Dive with a computer and agree on strict turn pressures. This is described as a "hit or miss" dive — large animals are never guaranteed.
