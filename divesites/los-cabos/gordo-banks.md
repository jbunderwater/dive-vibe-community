## Gordo Banks

A submerged seamount approximately 11–13 kilometers (7–8 miles) offshore from San Jose del Cabo. The peak rises to 31–35 meters below the surface from deep open ocean. This is the premier big-animal dive in Los Cabos — schooling hammerhead sharks, silky sharks, yellowfin tuna, and marlin patrol the seamount. The dive involves a blue water descent with no visual reference, strong currents, and no shelter. Advanced certification and open water experience are mandatory. Depths from 31 to 40 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: 11–13 km (7–8 miles) offshore from San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters (open ocean)
- **Current**: Strong and unpredictable
- **Best Time**: August to January for hammerheads (peak September–December)

## Marine Life

Schooling scalloped hammerhead sharks are the main attraction, with peak aggregations from August through January. Silky sharks, yellowfin tuna, wahoo, and marlin are regularly encountered. Large schools of jacks and other pelagics aggregate around the seamount. Mobula rays visit the site. Manta rays are occasional visitors. Whale sharks are occasional visitors; sightings are not predictable at this site.

## Dive Profile

The boat anchors or drifts over the seamount. Entry is a blue water descent — no wall or line to follow down. Descend quickly to the seamount top at 31–35 meters and position yourself where the current brings pelagic traffic past. Bottom time is short at this depth. Ascend to a safety stop in open blue water and deploy an SMB for pickup. Nitrox strongly recommended.

## Safety Considerations

This is an exposed open-ocean dive with no shelter, no shallow areas, and strong currents. Blue water descents and ascents require experience and composure. Narcosis at 35-40 meters is a real concern. The boat ride is 45-60 minutes each way and can be rough. Not suitable for divers without significant open water and deep diving experience. Carry an SMB, whistle, and mirror. Dive with a computer and agree on strict turn pressures.
