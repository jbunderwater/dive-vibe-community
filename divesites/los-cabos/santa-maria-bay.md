## Santa Maria Bay

A protected horseshoe-shaped cove designated as a marine sanctuary along the Corridor. Two large granite rocks shelter the bay from swell and wind, creating calm conditions year-round. The reef runs along sandy corridors between rocky strips, with sea fans, coral growth, and a variety of reef fish filling the structure. Depths range from 6 to 18 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, marine sanctuary (approximately 20 minutes from Cabo San Lucas marina)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef (rocky reef with sand corridors)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Minimal — protected cove
- **Best Time**: Year-round; mornings typically have best visibility

## Marine Life

The reef supports Cortez angelfish, king angelfish, sergeant majors, yellowtail snappers, wrasses, and damselfish. Moray eels and octopus hide in the reef structure. Bat rays and butterfly rays cruise the sandy areas between reef strips. Sea turtles are occasionally seen. Nudibranchs appear on the rocky substrate, making this a good macro site.

## Dive Profile

A gentle reef dive with no significant hazards. Explore the sandy corridors between reef strips, checking overhangs for moray eels and octopus. The horseshoe shape of the bay allows a natural loop back to the entry point. Works well for training dives, photography, and snorkeling; snorkelers share the bay so maintain awareness on ascent.

## Safety Considerations

One of the calmest and most protected sites in Los Cabos. Suitable for all levels including discover scuba participants. Minimal current and surge. Snorkelers frequent the site, so watch for swimmers on ascent.
