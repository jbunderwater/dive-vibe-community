## Santa Maria Bay

A protected horseshoe-shaped cove with confirmed marine sanctuary status (visitloscabos.travel and multiple official sources). One of the few Corridor sites accessible by both boat and shore — a public parking area off the coastal highway at Km 13 leads to a boardwalk that provides direct beach access, making this unique among Corridor dive sites.

Shallow coral gardens fill the bay in calm, sheltered water. Depths range from 6 to 18 meters (20–60 ft), with a maximum around 21 meters (70 ft) at the outer edges. The protected status keeps the reef in good condition and the fish population dense.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, marine sanctuary at approximately Km 13
- **Entry Type**: boat or shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Minimal — well-protected cove
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Sea turtles and bat rays are frequently sighted over the sandy areas. Sea bass, snapper, angelfish (Cortez and king), hogfish, and wrasses inhabit the coral gardens. Nudibranchs, moray eels, and sea fans (gorgonians) are confirmed by multiple sources. The sheltered reef supports an abundance of smaller tropical species throughout the year.

## Dive Profile

A gentle reef dive with no significant hazards. Explore the coral garden in the center of the cove, then work along the rocky edges where moray eels and nudibranchs shelter. The horseshoe shape allows a natural loop back toward the entry point. Well suited for training dives, discover scuba programs, and underwater photography.

## Safety Considerations

One of the calmest and most protected sites in Los Cabos. Suitable for all levels including discover scuba participants. Minimal current and surge. Snorkelers share the bay — watch for swimmers on ascent. Shore entry via boardwalk is straightforward but check surf conditions before entering from the beach.
