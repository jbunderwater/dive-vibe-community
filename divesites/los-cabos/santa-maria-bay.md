## Santa Maria Bay

A small, protected horseshoe-shaped cove designated as a marine sanctuary along the Corridor. The bay starts shallow and slopes to about 18 meters, with coral-encrusted rocky reef structures covering the majority of the bottom. The protected status and calm water make it a reliable site year-round and a popular choice for discover scuba participants.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, marine sanctuary
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Minimal — protected cove
- **Best Time**: Year-round; mornings tend to be calmer and less crowded

## Marine Life

The coral-encrusted rocky reef supports moray eels, octopus, snappers, and hogfish. Bat rays and butterfly rays are frequently seen over sandy patches. The protected environment attracts nudibranchs and flatworms — the site is noted as a good macro destination alongside larger reef encounters. The horseshoe shape of the cove concentrates fish life throughout.

## Dive Profile

Begin in the shallower central section of the cove and work along the rocky edges where moray eels and octopus shelter. The horseshoe shape provides a natural loop — divers can work along one side and return along the other near the entry point. Depth is open-water friendly throughout, making this suitable for certification dives and wide-angle photography.

## Safety Considerations

One of the calmest and most protected sites in Los Cabos. Suitable for all levels including discover scuba participants. Minimal current and surge. Snorkelers also use the bay, so maintain awareness on ascent.
