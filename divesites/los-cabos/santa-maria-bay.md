## Santa Maria Bay

A protected horseshoe-shaped cove within the Los Cabos tourist corridor marine sanctuary. The bay's reef features coral-encrusted rocky structures with gorgonian sea fans, creating a calm, sheltered environment well within Open Water depth limits. The site is 20 minutes from the marina and is one of the more frequently visited Corridor sites for beginner divers. Depths range from 3 to 18 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, within the Los Cabos marine sanctuary (20 minutes from marina)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Minimal — protected cove
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Coral gardens support parrotfish, angelfish, sergeant majors, and large hawkfish. Moray eels and octopus hide in the reef structure. Bat rays and butterfly rays are frequently seen over the sandy areas between reef sections. Whitetip reef sharks patrol the outer reef edge. Sea turtles are common. Lobsters shelter in rock crevices. Snake eels and nudibranchs make this a productive spot for macro photography.

## Dive Profile

A gentle reef dive with no significant hazards. Explore the coral garden in the center of the cove, working along the rocky edges where moray eels and octopus shelter. The horseshoe shape of the bay means you can make a loop and return near your entry point. Suitable for training dives and underwater photography.

## Safety Considerations

One of the calmest and most protected sites in Los Cabos. Suitable for all levels including discover scuba participants. Minimal current and surge. Snorkelers share the bay, so watch for swimmers on ascent.
