## Palmilla

Two reef systems blended with coral canyons near the eastern end of the Corridor, not far from San Jose del Cabo. Soft corals and vibrant gorgonian sea fans color the reef, while schools of jacks and porkfish move through the channels. Cleaning stations are distributed across the site. Green sea turtles are regular visitors. Coral coverage here is considered among the best in the region by local operators. Depths range from 5 to 18 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, near San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Schools of yellowtail jacks and porkfish are a consistent highlight. Gorgonian sea fans and soft corals dominate the reef structure. Green sea turtles are frequently encountered grazing on the reef. Cortez angelfish, king angelfish, and various wrasse species are resident reef fish. Cleaning stations add to the diversity of fish behavior observable here.

## Dive Profile

Start on one reef system, cross the sandy channel, and explore the second. The coral canyons between and within the two reefs provide the most interesting terrain. Shallow depth allows long bottom times. Good site for photography given the colorful gorgonians and approachable turtle population.

## Safety Considerations

Calm, shallow site suitable for all levels. Mild current possible. The dual reef structure provides natural shelter.
