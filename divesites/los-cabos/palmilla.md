## Palmilla

A large reef system near the eastern end of the Corridor, close to San Jose del Cabo. Local operators describe two interconnected reef areas with coral canyons running between them, covered in soft corals and gorgonian sea fans. The reef begins shallow and offers flexibility for different experience levels. Green sea turtles are a consistent presence, and the site is noted for ray cleaning stations.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, near San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef (large reef system with coral canyons)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Schools of yellowtail jacks and porkfish are a highlight. Gorgonian sea fans and soft corals dominate the reef structure. Green sea turtles graze the reef and are frequently encountered throughout. Cortez angelfish, king angelfish, and various wrasse species are common. Ray cleaning stations bring additional visitors to the site. Moray eels and octopus shelter in the coral canyons.

## Dive Profile

Start on the reef system, cross the sandy channel between sections, and explore the second reef area. The coral canyons between and within the reef sections provide the most interesting terrain. Shallow depth allows extended bottom times. A good site for photography given the colorful gorgonians and approachable turtle population.

## Safety Considerations

Calm, shallow site suitable for all levels. Mild current possible. The dual reef structure provides natural shelter.
