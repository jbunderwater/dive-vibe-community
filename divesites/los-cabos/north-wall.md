## North Wall

A sloping rock-covered wall on the north side of Pelican Rock, close to the marina (4 minutes by boat). The dive begins over a shallow sandy bottom where colonies of garden eels sway in the current. At around 12 meters, large boulders appear, sheltering moray eels, lobsters, scorpionfish, and pufferfish. The rocks give way to a cliff face at depth. Known as one of the better macro photography sites in the marine park, with seahorses, frogfish, and nudibranchs regularly reported. Depths from 3 to 27 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cabo San Lucas Marine Park (north side of Pelican Rock, 4 minutes from marina)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually mild — minimal currents make this a reliable macro site
- **Best Time**: Year-round; night dives particularly rewarding

## Marine Life

Garden eels carpet the sandy shallows. Green moray eels, spiny lobsters, spotted scorpionfish, and guineafowl pufferfish live among the boulders. Seahorses appear at the site and are a reliable draw for macro photographers. Frogfish, nudibranchs, shrimps, and fan-tail pipefish are documented here. Cow-nose rays and mobula rays pass through. At night, octopus, crabs, and basket starfish emerge, making this a well-regarded night dive.

## Dive Profile

Start in the sandy shallows at 3-6 meters to observe garden eels, then move over the boulder field at 12 meters. Continue to the wall edge if your certification and air supply allow. The varied topography makes the site rewarding at every depth — beginners can stay in the boulder zone while more experienced divers explore the wall. Slow, methodical swimming with attention to crevices is the right approach for finding macro subjects.

## Safety Considerations

Mild conditions make this suitable for beginners, though the wall drops deep beyond the 27-meter mark. Scorpionfish blend into the rocks — avoid placing hands on the substrate. For night dives, carry a primary and backup light.
