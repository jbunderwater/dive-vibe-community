## Gavilanes

A sheltered reef site situated between Chileno Bay and Santa Maria Bay in the Corridor. The topography features a series of ridges and canyons — some sources describe small chambers in the rocky structure — at depths of 12 to 18 meters. The reef here is noted by local operators for its relatively healthy coral cover and lush soft corals.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Chileno Bay and Santa Maria Bay
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef (ridges and canyons)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The healthy coral coverage supports diverse reef life. Cortez angelfish, king angelfish, large groupers, and schools of grunts and snappers are common. Spiny lobsters shelter in the crevices between ridges. Whitetip reef sharks patrol the canyons. Moray eels, scorpionfish, and octopus also use the rocky structure. Sea turtles and rays visit the site.

## Dive Profile

Follow the ridges and explore the canyons between them. The parallel ridge structure provides natural navigation — swim along one ridge, cross a sandy channel to the next, and return along a different route. Check crevices and overhangs in each canyon for lobsters and resting sharks. The varied terrain between ridges rewards systematic exploration.

## Safety Considerations

Sheltered location with mild conditions. Suitable for beginners. Light current possible but rarely strong enough to affect the dive significantly.
