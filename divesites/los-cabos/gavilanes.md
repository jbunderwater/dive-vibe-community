## Gavilanes

Situated between Chileno Bay and Santa Maria Bay along the Corridor, confirmed by five or more independent sources. Boat access only — unlike the neighboring bays, there is no public beach at Gavilanes. The site is noted as the most sheltered Corridor dive during south swells (caboprivateguide.com), making it a reliable option when other sites are exposed. Depths are very consistent at 12 to 18 meters (40–60 ft).

A series of ridges and canyons covered in hard and soft corals form the main topography. Mobula rays have been documented here by a first-hand ScubaBoard trip report (December 2020), and whale songs are audible underwater during winter months according to the same report.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Chileno Bay and Santa Maria Bay
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner to Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; most sheltered during south swells

## Marine Life

Cortez angelfish, king angelfish, large groupers, and lobsters are confirmed residents of the ridges and canyons. Whitetip reef sharks patrol the site and are reported by multiple operator sources. Mobula rays have been documented by first-hand trip reports. In winter, whale songs are audible underwater — an unusual and memorable feature of the dive.

## Dive Profile

Follow the ridges and explore the canyons between them. The parallel ridge structure provides natural navigation — swim along one ridge, cross to the next, and return along a different channel. Check the crevices and overhangs for lobsters and resting sharks. Consistent depths of 12–18 meters make air management straightforward.

## Safety Considerations

One of the most sheltered sites along the Corridor. Suitable for beginners. Light to moderate current possible but rarely strong enough to significantly affect the dive. Boat access only — coordinate with your operator for pickup.
