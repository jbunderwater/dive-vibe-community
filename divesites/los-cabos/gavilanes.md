## Gavilanes

Situated between Chileno Bay and Santa Maria Bay, Gavilanes is cited by several local operators as having one of the healthiest coral reefs in the Los Cabos area. It is also noted as the most sheltered dive site in the Corridor, making it a good option when south swells affect other sites. The topography features a series of ridges and canyons covered in hard and soft corals, with depths of 3 to 18 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Chileno Bay and Santa Maria Bay
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; particularly useful during south swells when other Corridor sites are exposed

## Marine Life

Moray eels are reported in abundance in the crevices between ridges. Scorpionfish, octopus, sea turtles, and rays are commonly encountered. Cortez angelfish, king angelfish, large groupers, and schools of grunts inhabit the reef. Spiny lobsters shelter in the crevices. Whitetip reef sharks patrol the canyons between ridges. Cleaning stations attract larger species throughout the site.

## Dive Profile

Follow the ridges and explore the canyons between them. The parallel ridge structure provides natural navigation — swim along one ridge, cross to the next, and return along a different channel. Check the crevices and overhangs in each canyon for lobsters, moray eels, and resting sharks.

## Safety Considerations

Sheltered location with mild conditions. Suitable for beginners. Light to moderate current possible but rarely strong enough to significantly affect the dive. The shelter from south swells makes this a reliable fallback site.
