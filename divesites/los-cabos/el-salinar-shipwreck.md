## El Salinar Shipwreck

A shallow shipwreck split into three large sections resting on a sandy bottom near San Jose del Cabo. The wreck has been colonized by marine life, creating an artificial reef. The shallow depth and calm conditions make it accessible to all certification levels. The identity, history, and original vessel name of this wreck have not been verified across independent sources.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The wreck structure attracts reef fish including grunts, snappers, and sergeant majors. Moray eels occupy cavities in the corroded hull. The sandy bottom around the wreck sections hosts garden eels and various flatfish. Coral encrustation on the metal surfaces supports smaller invertebrates, making this a productive macro photography site. The site is noted as accessible for snorkelers in calm conditions.

## Dive Profile

Visit each of the three wreck sections, starting with the largest. The sections are spread across a sandy bottom, so navigation between them is straightforward. The shallow depth allows extended bottom time. External exploration only — the corroded wreck structure is not appropriate for penetration.

## Safety Considerations

Shallow, calm site with minimal hazards. The wreck's corroded metal can have sharp edges — maintain neutral buoyancy and avoid contact. No penetration should be attempted.
