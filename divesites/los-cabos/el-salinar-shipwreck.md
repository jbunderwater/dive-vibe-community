## El Salinar Shipwreck

A shipwreck of unidentified origin resting in three large sections on a sandy bottom near San Jose del Cabo. The vessel's name, history, and sinking date have not been documented in available sources. The wreck has been colonized extensively by marine growth, and the three separated sections and surrounding sand provide a habitat that local operators describe as good for macro photography. Maximum depth is 12 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The wreck structure and surrounding sandy bottom attract macro life concentrated around the three sections. Coral encrustation on the metal surfaces supports small crustaceans and other invertebrates. No site-specific marine life reports from independent sources were found beyond operator descriptions of general macro life and reef fish around the wreck sections.

## Dive Profile

Visit each of the three wreck sections in sequence, starting with the largest. The sections are spread across a sandy bottom, so navigation between them is straightforward. The shallow depth allows extended bottom time for photography. External exploration only — the structural integrity of the wreck sections has not been independently assessed, and penetration should not be attempted.

## Safety Considerations

Shallow, calm site with minimal hazards. The wreck's corroded metal can have sharp edges — maintain neutral buoyancy and avoid contact with the hull. No penetration should be attempted.
