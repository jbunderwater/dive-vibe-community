## Neptune's Finger

Home to what multiple operators describe as the largest sandfall in the Cabo San Lucas Marine Park — a claim well-supported by operator consensus across divecabo.com, loscabosguide.com, and diveninjaexpeditions.com. A dramatic pinnacle and wall formation, the site descends from a reef top at around 9 meters to a wall face where corals, gorgonian sea fans, and sponges thrive between 18 and 30 meters. The sandfall begins at approximately 30 meters (100 ft) and cascades down the wall face. This is also a confirmed ray cleaning station — the primary such site in the Marine Park — where spotted eagle rays and bat rays hover while attended by cleaner wrasses. Green sea turtles, large groupers, and occasional mobula rays are seen here. Maximum depth reaches approximately 37 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cabo San Lucas Marine Park
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 37 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: July to October

## Marine Life

The ray cleaning station is the highlight — spotted eagle rays and bat rays hover while cleaner wrasses work them over. Green sea turtles, large groupers, Cortez angelfish, and king angelfish are common. Mobula rays are seen seasonally. The wall itself supports dense gorgonian growth and colorful sponges.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top around 10-12 meters and follow it to the wall edge at 18 meters. The wall drops steeply, with the best coral coverage between 18 and 28 meters. Look for the ray cleaning station along the wall. The sandfall is visible cascading down the wall face. Watch your depth — the wall continues well beyond recreational limits.

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops deep, so monitor your depth carefully. Currents can push divers along the wall face. The site is appropriate for intermediate divers comfortable with wall diving and depth management.
