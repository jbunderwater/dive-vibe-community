## Neptune's Finger

A towering rock formation named for its resemblance to an upright finger, rising from the Cabo San Lucas Marine Park seabed. The site features a dramatic vertical wall beginning at around 8 meters that drops past 30 meters, with the deepest sections extending well beyond recreational limits. Beautiful fan coral fields develop at depth. A ray cleaning station is one of the site's highlights. Depths range from 8 to 33 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cabo San Lucas Marine Park (6 minutes from marina)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: July to October

## Marine Life

The ray cleaning station attracts cow-nose rays and spotted rays that hover while cleaner fish work them over. Green sea turtles, large groupers, and snappers are common. Mobula rays are seen seasonally. The deeper wall sections (around 30 meters) host dense gorgonian sea fan growth. Guitarfish and stingrays have been reported resting on sandy areas. Schools of sardines and tropical reef fish inhabit the shallower sections.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top and follow it down to the wall. The best coral coverage is in the mid-section of the wall between 15 and 28 meters. Look for the ray cleaning station while working along the wall face. The wall continues well beyond recreational limits — monitor depth carefully. The site is rated Advanced due to the depth and wall diving nature.

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops deep; monitor your depth carefully. Currents can push divers along the wall face. This site is marked Advanced on most operator listings — appropriate for divers comfortable with wall diving and depth management at 30+ meters.
