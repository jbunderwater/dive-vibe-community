## Neptune's Finger

A steep rocky pinnacle rising above the bay surface, capping a dramatic wall that drops from roughly 10 meters to well beyond recreational limits. The wall face between 18 and 33 meters holds dense gorgonian sea fans, hard corals, and sponges. A ray cleaning station along the wall draws spotted eagle rays that hover while cleaner wrasses work them over. The site sits at the southern end of the South Wall system that runs from Pelican Rock, and is about 6 minutes from the marina.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cabo San Lucas Marine Park
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (approximately 6 minutes from the marina)
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate (Advanced for deep sections below 30 m)
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters (wall continues deeper)
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: September to November

## Marine Life

The ray cleaning station is the highlight — spotted eagle rays and bat rays hover at the station while small cleaner wrasses service them. Green sea turtles, large gulf groupers, Cortez angelfish, and king angelfish are common. Mobula rays and tuna pass through seasonally. The wall supports dense gorgonian growth and colorful encrusting sponges.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top around 10–12 meters and follow the wall edge south. The best coral coverage is between 18 and 28 meters. Look for the cleaning station mid-wall. The pinnacle visible above the surface provides a reliable orientation landmark throughout the dive. Watch your depth — the wall continues well beyond 33 meters. Return to the shallows for your safety stop using the pinnacle as a reference point.

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops deep, so monitor depth carefully. Currents can push divers along the wall face. The site is appropriate for intermediate divers comfortable with wall diving and depth management; deeper sections below 30 meters are better suited to advanced divers.
