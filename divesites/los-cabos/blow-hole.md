## Blow Hole

Named for a surface blowhole visible on the adjacent cliff where surge forces seawater through a rock cavity. The underwater site features massive boulders, deep crevices, and a boulder-strewn reef with high-profile rocky structures. The backside of the main rock formation creates a wall covered in gorgonian sea fans. Schools of bigeye jacks swirl around the pinnacle tops. Depths range from 12 to 30 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/rocky reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Moderate — can be strong on the exposed side
- **Best Time**: July to October

## Marine Life

Dense schools of bigeye jacks are a consistent highlight. Gorgonian sea fans and black coral cover the rock walls. Green sea turtles, guineafowl pufferfish, and large groupers are common. Whitetip reef sharks rest under ledges and in crevices. Small nurse sharks and guitarfish have been reported on sandy areas between boulders, though encounters are irregular. Look in the crevices for moray eels and lobsters.

## Dive Profile

Descend among the boulders and navigate through the crevices and overhangs. The gorgonian-covered wall on the backside of the main rock formation is the centerpiece — spend time here before exploring the surrounding boulder field. Depth varies significantly across the site, so watch your gauge when moving between features. Reduced diver traffic compared to the main marine park sites often makes for quieter encounters.

## Safety Considerations

Current can be strong, especially on the exposed side of the rock formations. The boulder terrain creates overhead-like environments in places — maintain good buoyancy and avoid entering tight spaces without proper overhead training. Intermediate certification recommended.
