## Blow Hole

Named for a natural blowhole in the coastal cliffs nearby. The underwater terrain features massive boulders, deep crevices, and a rock wall on the backside of the main formation that is heavily draped in gorgonian sea fans. Located approximately 20 minutes from the marina in the Corridor, the site offers more current exposure than the bay sites and a chance of seeing nurse sharks and guitarfish resting between the boulders. Depths range from 12 to 30 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle with rocky reef and boulders
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters
- **Current**: Moderate — can be strong on the exposed side
- **Best Time**: July to October

## Marine Life

Dense schools of bigeye jacks are a highlight. Gorgonian sea fans cover the rock walls. Green sea turtles, guineafowl pufferfish, and large groupers are common. Nurse sharks and banded guitarfish are occasionally spotted resting on the sand between boulders. Whitetip reef sharks use the site in warmer months. Bat rays and eagle rays pass through. Look in the crevices for moray eels and spiny lobsters.

## Dive Profile

Descend among the boulders and navigate through the crevices and overhangs. The gorgonian-covered wall on the backside of the main rock is the centerpiece — spend time here before exploring the surrounding boulder field. Depth varies significantly across the site, so watch your gauge when moving between features. Check sandy pockets between boulders for nurse sharks and guitarfish resting on the bottom.

## Safety Considerations

Current can be strong, especially on the exposed side of the rock formations. The boulder terrain creates confined passages in places — maintain good buoyancy and avoid entering tight spaces without appropriate training. Intermediate certification recommended.
