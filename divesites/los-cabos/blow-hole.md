## Blow Hole

Named for a small blowhole on the nearby shore. The underwater terrain features massive boulders and deep crevices along a rocky reef and wall combination. The backside of the main rock formation presents a gorgonian-covered wall face — one of the more dramatic wall sections along the Corridor. Depths range from 12 to 30 meters (40–100 ft), with some deeper sections reaching around 35 meters.

Whitetip reef sharks are regularly reported here, particularly during warmer months. In winter, guitarfish (banded guitarfish) and giant electric rays (bullseye electric rays) appear seasonally — confirmed by operator reports and at least one ScubaBoard trip account. Schools of jacks, groupers, and sea turtles are consistent across multiple sources. Nurse sharks have been claimed by one operator but are not confirmed by trip reports at this specific site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate to Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters
- **Current**: Moderate — can be strong on exposed side
- **Best Time**: Year-round; warm months for whitetip reef sharks, winter for guitarfish and electric rays

## Marine Life

Dense schools of bigeye jacks are a consistent highlight. Gorgonian sea fans cover the wall face. Green sea turtles, guineafowl pufferfish, large groupers, and spotted eagle rays are reported by multiple sources. Whitetip reef sharks are more commonly observed during warmer months. In winter, guitarfish and giant electric rays appear seasonally. Moray eels and lobsters occupy the crevices between boulders.

## Dive Profile

Descend among the boulders and navigate through the crevices and overhangs. The gorgonian-covered wall on the backside of the main rock formation is the centerpiece — plan to spend time here before exploring the surrounding boulder field. Depth varies significantly across the site, so monitor your gauge when moving between features.

## Safety Considerations

Current can be strong on the exposed side of the rock formations. The boulder terrain creates overhead-like environments in places — maintain good buoyancy and avoid entering tight spaces without proper overhead training. Intermediate to Advanced certification recommended given the depth range and potential for current.
