## Blow Hole

Named for a blowhole in the cliff face above that erupts with seawater when surge rolls in. The underwater terrain features massive boulders, deep crevices, and a wall on the backside of the main rock formation covered in gorgonian sea fans. Dense schools of bigeye jacks swirl around the pinnacles. Depths range from 8 to 30 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo (20 minutes from marina)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Moderate — can be strong on the exposed side
- **Best Time**: July to October

## Marine Life

Dense schools of bigeye jacks are a consistent highlight. Gorgonian sea fans cover the rock walls. Green sea turtles, guineafowl pufferfish, large groupers, and snapper schools are common. Whitetip reef sharks are reported in the warmer months. Nurse sharks and guitarfish are occasionally spotted resting on sandy patches between boulders in cooler water. Giant electric rays have been seen in colder months. Look in the crevices for moray eels and lobsters.

## Dive Profile

Descend among the boulders and navigate through the crevices and overhangs. The gorgonian-covered wall on the backside of the main rock is the centerpiece. Depth varies significantly across the site — the boulder zone is 8-20 meters while the deepest areas reach 30 meters. Watch your gauge when moving between features.

## Safety Considerations

Current can be strong, especially on the exposed side of the rock formations. The boulder terrain creates overhead environments in places — maintain good buoyancy and avoid entering tight spaces without proper training. Intermediate certification recommended.
