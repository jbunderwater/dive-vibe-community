## Land's End

The southernmost tip of the Baja Peninsula, where the Pacific Ocean meets the Sea of Cortez at the famous El Arco rock arch. A resident colony of California sea lions regularly approaches divers, making this one of the most entertaining dives in Cabo. The scattered remains of the cargo ship *Harry Lundeberg* — a Canadian-built Park Ship that ran aground here on February 8, 1954, while carrying gypsum plaster from Isla San Marcos — lie across a football-field-sized area of seafloor, buried for decades and partially exposed by Hurricane Odile in 2014. Depth ranges from 8 to 22 meters across the site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cabo San Lucas Marine Park, at the tip of the Baja Peninsula
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (approximately 7 minutes from the marina)
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/rocky headland
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters
- **Current**: Can be strong — this is the meeting point of two bodies of water
- **Best Time**: September to November for best visibility and calmest conditions

## Marine Life

California sea lions are the star attraction and frequently approach divers directly. Schools of cow-nose rays, snappers, and baitfish are common. Moray eels, octopus, and pufferfish occupy rocky crevices. Mobula rays pass through seasonally.

## Dive Profile

The site surrounds the granite rock formations at the very tip of the peninsula. Surge and current are common due to the convergence of the Pacific and the Sea of Cortez. Start on the sheltered bay side and work toward the point as conditions allow. The wreck remains of the *Harry Lundeberg* are scattered between 12 and 30 meters — look for corroded hull sections and encrusted metal among the boulders. Strong surface current can develop quickly; carry an SMB.

## Safety Considerations

Currents and surge can intensify without warning, particularly on the Pacific side. Not recommended for inexperienced divers in rough conditions. Boat traffic is heavy in the area — deploy an SMB before surfacing.
