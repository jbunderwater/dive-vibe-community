## Whale's Head

Named for a distinctive rock formation visible above the surface, whose profile resembles a whale surfacing from the sea. The site marks the start of the Corridor — the stretch of coastline between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo — and is a 10–15 minute boat ride from the marina. A thriving coral reef at depths of 2–15 meters is the main draw, with spotted eagle rays a reliable feature. The shallow depth and calm conditions suit beginning divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Start of the Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round; humpback whales audible December to March

## Marine Life

Spotted eagle rays are the main draw, frequently encountered near the reef. Pufferfish, Cortez angelfish, sergeant majors, needlefish, and butterflyfish inhabit the rocky reef areas. Sea turtles pass through occasionally. Sandy patches yield garden eels and stingrays. During winter months (December–March), humpback whale songs can sometimes be heard underwater.

## Dive Profile

A straightforward reef dive over mixed sand and coral terrain at shallow depth. Explore the rocky reef areas and check the sandy edges for ray and turtle encounters. Maximum depth is only 15 meters, so bottom time is generous. A good site for newer divers to build experience before progressing to deeper Corridor sites.

## Safety Considerations

Shallow, calm conditions with minimal hazards. Mild current possible. Boat traffic along the Corridor requires an SMB on ascent.
