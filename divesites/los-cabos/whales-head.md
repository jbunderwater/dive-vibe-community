## Whale's Head

Named for a large rock formation whose profile resembles a surfacing whale. Located at the Cabo San Lucas end of the Corridor, about 15–20 minutes from the marina, this site is well known for spotted eagle ray encounters over a mixed sandy bottom and rocky reef at depths of 2 to 15 meters. Channels and rocky clearings with soft coral make the topography more varied than the shallow depth suggests.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Start of the Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Spotted eagle rays are the main draw and are frequently encountered over the sandy patches and rocky areas. Bat rays also use the site. Pufferfish, Cortez angelfish, needlefish, and sergeant majors inhabit the rocky areas. Sea turtles pass through occasionally.

## Dive Profile

A straightforward dive over mixed sand and rock terrain. Navigate through the underwater channels and rocky clearings, checking sandy patches for eagle rays feeding on the bottom. Maximum depth is only 15 meters, allowing generous bottom time. A suitable site for newer divers building confidence before progressing to deeper Corridor sites.

## Safety Considerations

Shallow, calm conditions with minimal hazards. Mild current is possible. Boat traffic along the Corridor requires an SMB on ascent.
