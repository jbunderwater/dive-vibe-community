## Whale's Head

Named for a distinctive rock formation above the surface that resembles a surfacing whale's head, this site marks the start of the Corridor — the stretch of coastline running between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo. Below the surface, a mix of sandy bottom and rocky reef creates habitat favored by spotted eagle rays. A ray cleaning station operates here regularly. Shallow depths of 2 to 15 meters make it accessible to a range of diver levels. Humpback whale song can sometimes be heard underwater during the winter months.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Start of the Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (10-15 minutes from marina)
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; winter for whale song

## Marine Life

Spotted eagle rays are the main draw, regularly seen at the cleaning station and cruising the sandy patches. Angelfish, porcupinefish, damselfish, and butterflyfish inhabit the rocky areas. Sea turtles pass through occasionally. The sandy areas yield garden eels and stingrays. Needlefish frequent the shallows. Humpback whales pass the Corridor during winter, and their songs are occasionally audible on the dive.

## Dive Profile

A straightforward dive over mixed sand and rock terrain. Explore the rocky reef areas for ray encounters and cleaning station activity. Maximum depth is only 15 meters, so bottom time is generous. The site is listed as Intermediate on operator sites due to mild current, which is more common here than at the more sheltered bay sites closer to Cabo. Good site for building confidence with current before progressing to deeper Corridor sites.

## Safety Considerations

Mild current possible and more regular here than at the marine park sites. Boat traffic along the Corridor requires an SMB on ascent.
