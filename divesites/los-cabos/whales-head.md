## Whale's Head

Named for a whale-shaped rock formation visible above the surface. A shallow mixed sand and rocky reef site at the start of the Corridor — the coastline stretch between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo — approximately 10–15 minutes by boat from the marina. Depths range from 2 to 15 meters (5–50 ft), making it one of the most beginner-accessible sites in Los Cabos.

Spotted eagle rays are the primary attraction and have been confirmed by multiple independent sources as frequent visitors. Sea turtles, octopus, moray eels, and tropical reef fish round out the marine life. Neptune's Finger is the confirmed ray cleaning station site in the Marine Park; whether Whale's Head functions as a cleaning station has not been confirmed by multiple independent sources.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Start of the Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner to Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Spotted eagle rays are a reliable highlight, confirmed by dive operators and trip reports alike. Sea turtles pass through regularly. Octopus, moray eels, and an array of tropical reef fish — including Cortez angelfish, pufferfish, and sergeant majors — inhabit the rocky areas. Sandy patches support garden eels and stingrays.

## Dive Profile

A straightforward shallow dive over mixed sand and rock. The rocky reef sections are the main focal point for eagle ray encounters. Maximum depth of 15 meters provides generous bottom time. An excellent site for newer divers to build confidence and reef-reading skills before progressing to deeper Corridor sites.

## Safety Considerations

Shallow, calm conditions with minimal hazards. Mild current is possible. Boat traffic along the Corridor requires an SMB on ascent.
