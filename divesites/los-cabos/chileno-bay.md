## Chileno Bay

One of the most accessible and species-rich reef sites along the Corridor. Chileno Bay is a public beach with confirmed shore dive access, as well as regular boat service — making it one of two Corridor sites (along with Santa Maria) reachable without a charter. Calm, protected waters shelter a maze of finger-shaped reef formations decorated with sea fans and coral trees. Depths of 8 to 18 meters (20–60 ft) over varied terrain make it rewarding for beginners and experienced divers alike.

Whitetip reef sharks resting under ledges and among boulders are a confirmed highlight, documented by five or more independent sources including first-hand ScubaBoard trip reports. Nurse sharks are reported by three operator sources but have not yet been confirmed by first-hand trip accounts at this site specifically.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, public beach between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: boat or shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner to Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Whitetip reef sharks resting under ledges and boulders are a reliable and well-documented sighting. Green sea turtles are common throughout the site. Schools of jacks, snapper, and grunts patrol the water column (confirmed by ScubaBoard reports). Gulf grouper, Cortez angelfish, sea fans, and coral trees provide habitat for smaller reef species — hawkfish, wrasses, and damselfish among them. Lobsters shelter in the crevices. Spotted eagle rays are occasional visitors.

## Dive Profile

Navigate through the finger reef channels, checking under overhangs for resting sharks and turtles. The maze-like structure gives the site a sense of exploration despite the moderate depth. Work systematically through the channels to cover ground. The varied topography also makes it good natural navigation practice for newer divers.

## Safety Considerations

Protected and generally calm. Suitable for beginners. Whitetip reef sharks are non-aggressive and pose no threat when left undisturbed. Snorkelers use the bay — ascend with awareness. Mild current possible along the outer reef edge.
