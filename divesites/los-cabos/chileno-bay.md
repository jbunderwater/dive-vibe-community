## Chileno Bay

One of the most accessible reef sites along the Corridor, known for healthier coral than the shallower marine park sites closer to Cabo San Lucas. The bay shelters a maze of finger-shaped reef formations decorated with sea fans. Whitetip reef sharks rest under ledges in the finger reefs. Green sea turtles and schooling jacks are regular residents. Depths of 2 to 18 meters over varied terrain suit beginners and experienced divers alike.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Whitetip reef sharks rest under ledges in the finger reefs. Green sea turtles are common. Schools of jacks, snappers, and grunts are consistently present. Spotted eagle rays, large gulf grouper, and Cortez angelfish are reported. The finger reef channels and overhangs provide habitat for nurse sharks, starfish, urchins, and jellyfish. Sea fans and coral trees support hawkfish, wrasses, and damselfish.

## Dive Profile

Navigate through the finger reef channels, checking under overhangs for resting sharks and turtles. The maze-like structure gives the site a sense of exploration despite moderate depth. Work systematically through the channels to cover ground, following from the shallower inner bay to the deeper outer reef edges. Good natural navigation practice for newer divers.

## Safety Considerations

Protected and calm. Suitable for beginners. Whitetip reef sharks are docile and pose no threat if left undisturbed. Snorkelers use the bay, so ascend with awareness. Mild current possible along the outer reef edge.
