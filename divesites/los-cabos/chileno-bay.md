## Chileno Bay

One of the most accessible and lively reef sites along the Corridor. A maze of finger-shaped reef formations covered in sea fans and coral trees provides habitat diversity, with the site about half a mile from shore. The protected location keeps conditions calm and marine life abundant. Depths of 8 to 18 meters over varied terrain make it suitable for all certification levels.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Whitetip reef sharks rest under ledges in the finger reefs — a consistent draw for divers at this site. Green sea turtles are common, along with spotted eagle rays, large gulf grouper, nurse sharks, and Cortez angelfish. The coral trees and sea fans provide habitat for starfish, sea urchins, sponges, and various smaller reef fish. Mobula rays have been reported passing through.

## Dive Profile

Navigate through the finger reef channels, checking under overhangs for resting sharks and turtles. The maze-like structure gives the site a sense of exploration despite moderate depth. Work systematically through the channels to cover more ground. Check under ledges and overhangs — this is where the whitetip sharks are most reliably found.

## Safety Considerations

Protected and calm. Suitable for beginners. Whitetip reef sharks are docile and pose no threat if left undisturbed. Snorkelers use the bay, so ascend with awareness. Mild current possible along the outer reef edge.
