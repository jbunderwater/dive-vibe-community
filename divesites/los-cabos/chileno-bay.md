## Chileno Bay

One of the most popular reef sites along the Corridor, known for relatively healthy coral compared to higher-impact areas closer to Cabo San Lucas. Calm, protected waters shelter a maze of finger-shaped rocky reef formations decorated with sea fans and coral. Depths of 8 to 18 meters over varied terrain make it accessible for beginners while still offering enough structure to reward experienced divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef (finger reefs with sand channels)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Whitetip reef sharks rest under ledges and boulders within the finger reefs — check the deeper side of each channel overhang. Green sea turtles are common throughout the site. Spotted eagle rays and schools of jacks and snapper move through the channels. Large gulf grouper and Cortez angelfish are resident. The coral trees and sea fans provide habitat for hawkfish, wrasses, and damselfish.

## Dive Profile

Navigate through the finger reef channels, checking under overhangs for resting sharks and turtles. The maze-like structure provides a sense of exploration despite the moderate depth. Work systematically through the channels rather than swimming in open water over the reef — the most life is found in and around the rocky structure. Good natural navigation practice for newer divers.

## Safety Considerations

Protected and calm. Suitable for beginners. Whitetip reef sharks are docile and pose no threat if approached calmly without chasing. Snorkelers use the bay; ascend with awareness. Mild current possible along the outer reef edge.
