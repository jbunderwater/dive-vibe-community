## Chileno Bay

One of the most accessible and lively reef sites along the Corridor. Calm, protected waters shelter a maze of finger-shaped reef formations decorated with sea fans and coral trees. Green sea turtles, whitetip reef sharks, spotted eagle rays, large sea bass, and nurse sharks all frequent the site. Depths of 12 to 18 meters over varied terrain make it a rewarding dive for beginners and experienced divers alike.

## Site Information

- **Location**: The Corridor, between Cabo San Lucas and San Jose del Cabo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Whitetip reef sharks rest under ledges in the finger reefs. Green sea turtles are common, along with spotted eagle rays, large gulf grouper, and Cortez angelfish. Nurse sharks appear occasionally. The coral trees and sea fans provide habitat for smaller reef species including hawkfish, wrasses, and damselfish.

## Dive Profile

Navigate through the finger reef channels, checking under overhangs for resting sharks and turtles. The maze-like structure gives the site a sense of exploration despite moderate depth. Work systematically through the channels to cover more ground. Good natural navigation practice for newer divers.

## Safety Considerations

Protected and calm. Suitable for beginners. Whitetip reef sharks are docile and pose no threat if left undisturbed. Snorkelers use the bay, so ascend with awareness. Mild current possible along the outer reef edge.
