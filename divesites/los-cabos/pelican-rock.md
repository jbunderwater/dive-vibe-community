## Pelican Rock

One of the most popular and accessible dive sites in Cabo San Lucas, located on the north side of the bay 5 minutes from the marina. The reef slopes gradually from a shallow rocky top down to the canyon edge, with sandy patches supporting a sandfall that cascades over the wall. Sea fan-covered slopes and dense baitfish schools define the shallower sections, while the wall drops steeply toward the canyon in the deeper zones. Depths range from 5 to 27 meters depending on how far toward the canyon edge you venture.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cabo San Lucas Marine Park, inside the bay
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (5 minutes from marina)
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Minimal — protected within the bay
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest June to October

## Marine Life

Green moray eels, guineafowl pufferfish, scorpionfish, and Cortez angelfish are resident. Mobula rays and cow-nose rays cruise the deeper sections. The shallow rocky areas act as habitat for frogfish, seahorses, and nudibranchs — this is also a productive macro site. Whitetip reef sharks patrol the deeper reef edge. Lobsters shelter under ledges.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the boat on the bay side. Explore the shallow reef and sea fan areas first, then descend along the slope toward the canyon edge for more advanced terrain. The sandy chute near the canyon rim creates a localized sandfall visible from the reef top. The deeper sections near the canyon edge are better suited to intermediate divers. The site is close to Sand Falls, and experienced divers sometimes combine both in one dive — though Sand Falls requires Advanced certification for the deeper viewing depth.

## Safety Considerations

The bay provides excellent shelter, making this one of the calmest dive sites in the area. Watch depth near the canyon edge — the bottom drops away steeply. Boat traffic within the bay requires an SMB on ascent.
