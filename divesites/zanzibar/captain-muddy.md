## Captain Muddy

Captain Muddy is a reef site accessible from Nungwi on northern Zanzibar Island, one of the regular dive sites on the Nungwi rotation known for its resident sea turtles and accessible intermediate depth profile. The unusual name is attributed to local dive guide lore and has stuck as a recognizable site designation.

## Overview

Despite the name, Captain Muddy is typically a clear and pleasant dive with visibility averaging 15-20 meters. The site features a coral reef slope from 5 meters at the crest to 20 meters at the sand base, with a diverse coral community including Acropora formations, Porites heads, and table corals at depth. The western aspect of the Nungwi reef area provides shelter from the northeast monsoon, making Captain Muddy diveable in most conditions. Water temperatures are a warm 26-29°C year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Nungwi, North Zanzibar Island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-20 meters (50-65 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round at Nungwi; October-March for best visibility

## Marine Life

The resident turtle community at Captain Muddy is well-established — green turtles are reliably present and have become accustomed to respectful divers, feeding calmly on algae and sponges on the reef top. Large parrotfish in their terminal (bright rainbow) phase are prominent. Schools of surgeonfish patrol the reef edge. Barracuda and trevally cruise above the reef. Moray eels occupy the deeper crevices at the base of the slope.

## Dive Profile

A relaxed intermediate reef dive with a comfortable slope profile. Descend to the sand base at 18-20 meters and work back up the slope, spending time at the most productive coral zones at 10-15 meters. Finish with an extended shallow section on the reef top where turtle encounters are most likely. Total dive times of 45-55 minutes are typical.

## Entry and Exit

Boat departure from Nungwi beach, 5-10 minutes travel time. Captain Muddy is frequently paired with Barracuda Point or Big Wall for a two-tank morning. Giant stride entry; SMB deployment for ascent given Nungwi boat traffic.

## Tips and Recommendations

- An excellent second dive or "refresher" site at the start of a Zanzibar trip
- Green turtle encounters are best approached with slow, unhurried movement — kneeling in the sand or hovering very low without finning strongly
- The intermediate certification level is appropriate given the 20-meter depth and occasional current; recently certified beginners should dive this site only with a guide
- Evening dives here can be rewarding — lionfish become active at dusk and octopus emerge to hunt

## Safety Considerations

Generally calm conditions make this a forgiving intermediate site. Current can increase on the channel-facing sections — check with the guide which aspect of the reef is calmer on the day of diving. Standard SMB deployment required for ascent. Fire coral and sea urchins present throughout the reef.

## Photography

Green turtle portraits are the signature photography subject at Captain Muddy. The combination of good visibility, calm fish behavior, and clear Zanzibar water provides excellent conditions for natural-light turtle photography at 5-10 meters. The deeper reef sections at 15-20 meters benefit from strobes for color accuracy.
