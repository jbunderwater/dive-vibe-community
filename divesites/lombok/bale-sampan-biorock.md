# Bale Sampan Biorock

A Biorock coral restoration structure off Gili Trawangan that doubles as a beginner dive site teeming with juvenile reef fish.

## Overview

Bale Sampan Biorock is one of more than 120 Biorock structures in the Gili Islands, the second-largest Biorock project in the world. The structure sits in shallow water off Gili Trawangan, with the frame starting at around 5 meters and extending to 18 meters. Biorock technology — first deployed in the Gilis in 2004 — passes a safe low-voltage electrical current through submerged steel frames, causing calcium carbonate to form on the structure and allowing coral fragments to attach and grow at three to five times their natural rate. The result is a young but thriving artificial reef that has proven more resistant to bleaching events than surrounding natural reef. This site is ideal for beginners due to its shallow depths, calm conditions, and dense concentration of fish life drawn to the structure.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Gili Trawangan, Lombok, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Artificial reef (Biorock structure)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters (60 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters
- **Current**: Usually mild around the structure
- **Water Temperature**: 26–30°C (79–86°F)

## Marine Life

Biorock structures attract and shelter juvenile fish in high concentrations. Expect dense clouds of glassfish, anemonefish in resident anemones, juvenile groupers, lionfish perching on frame edges, pipefish threading through growing coral, and occasional cuttlefish hovering nearby. Flat worms and nudibranchs colonize the encrusted metal surfaces. Reef octopus are regular visitors. Turtles are commonly sighted in the shallower areas surrounding the structure. The site offers a snapshot of active coral restoration, with visible coral growth from fragments attached by reef gardeners.

## Dive Profile

Begin at the surface and descend to the shallowest part of the Biorock frame. Explore the structure slowly, checking each section for macro life hiding among growing coral. Work gradually deeper toward 18 meters, then ascend back along the frame for a safety stop in the shallows. Total dive times of 50–60 minutes are typical given the shallow depth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Gili Trawangan. The structure is close to the island, with a short boat ride of just a few minutes. Multiple dive operators on Gili Trawangan include Biorock sites in their beginner and fun-dive itineraries.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Slow down and look closely — the structural complexity hides small macro life easily overlooked at speed
- Maintain neutral buoyancy throughout to avoid disturbing growing coral fragments
- Morning dives tend to have the best visibility before boat traffic stirs sediment
- Ask your dive operator about the current status of coral planting programs — some operators offer participation opportunities

## Safety Considerations

Conditions at this site are generally calm and predictable, making it suitable for newly certified divers. Maintain buoyancy control to avoid contact with the structure and its fragile coral growth. Avoid touching any part of the frame or coral.

## Photography

The Biorock structure provides close-up opportunities with corals growing in geometric patterns alongside the steel frame — an unusual compositional subject. Macro work is excellent: pipefish, nudibranchs, and anemonefish offer classic reef portraits. Wide-angle shots showing the full structure with divers demonstrate the scale of the conservation effort.
