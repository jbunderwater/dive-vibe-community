## Gili Divers Biorock

A Biorock coral restoration structure associated with the Gili Divers operation on Gili Trawangan's east coast.

## Overview

Gili Divers Biorock is named after Gili Divers, an operator confirmed active in the Gili Islands (gilidivers.com). It forms part of the east coast Gili Trawangan Biorock cluster supported by the Gili Eco Trust, which started the Biorock program in 2004. The structures use low-voltage electrical current to stimulate accelerated limestone mineral accretion on steel frames, creating substrate that encourages accelerated coral colonisation. Gili Divers lists "Bio Rocks" as a collective dive experience on their site.

As a named individual dive site, "Gili Divers Biorock" does not appear as a distinct listing in published dive site databases, which list the east coast structures collectively as "BioRocks."

## Site Information

- **Location**: East coast Gili Trawangan, accessible from Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 m
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Anthias, wrasse, pufferfish, lionfish, and sweetlips resident on the structure. Turtles visit regularly. Nudibranchs and cleaner shrimps on coral growth. Sandy substrate nearby supports stingrays and garden eels.

Regional Coral Triangle species diversity applies to the broader east coast reef.

## Safety Considerations

Do not touch the metal frame. Maintain buoyancy. Lionfish venomous — observe without contact. Low-voltage current is safe for divers.
