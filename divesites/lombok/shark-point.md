# Shark Point

The northwest corner of Gili Trawangan — a popular drift site with daily blacktip and whitetip reef shark sightings, a sunken tug wreck, and abundant turtles.

## Overview

Shark Point sits on the northwestern corner of Gili Trawangan at the edge of the Lombok Strait, covering a large area from the shallow turtle-filled reef top at 5 meters down through coral terraces to a drop-off exceeding 50 meters. The site earns its name from consistent blacktip and whitetip reef shark encounters, though local divemasters note it might more accurately be called "Turtle Point" — the shallow zone between 5 and 12 meters hosts huge numbers of green sea turtles grazing on seagrass and coral bommies. A small sunken tug boat wreck at 30 meters adds an additional attraction for Advanced divers. The Glenn Nusa shipwreck near the start of the site's canyon system was intentionally sunk and is now home to schools of black snapper and sweetlips, with a resident whitetip shark.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest corner, Gili Trawangan, Lombok, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters (82 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters
- **Current**: Medium to strong; stronger on the outer drop-off
- **Water Temperature**: 26–30°C (79–86°F)

## Marine Life

Green sea turtles are extraordinarily abundant in the shallows — this is arguably the best single site in the Gili Islands for turtle encounters. Blacktip and whitetip reef sharks are sighted on essentially every dive, both in the deep canyons and surprisingly in the shallows. Blue-spotted stingrays and ribbon-tail rays rest in the canyon floors at depth. Humpback snapper schools congregate between the canyon walls. Anemonefish, ribbon eels, octopus, and scorpionfish occupy the shallower coral bommies. When strong current runs, batfish, giant trevally, Spanish mackerel, and dogtooth tuna sweep past the outer edge.

## Dive Profile

Begin in the shallow zone at 5–8 meters among the coral bommies and turtles. Work along the reef following the current before descending into the canyon system at 15–25 meters. The canyon entry typically involves a drop through three progressively deeper gullies. The Glenn Nusa wreck is at 30 meters near the canyon entrance and can be included by Advanced divers. Ascend back to the shallows for a safety stop above the turtle zone. Total dive time approximately 50 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Gili Trawangan. The northwest location means this site is exposed to swell when the northwest monsoon is active. On moderate current days, this is a relaxed drift; on strong current days, it requires confident drift diving skills. SMB essential for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Spend meaningful time in the 5–12 meter turtle zone before descending — this is some of the best turtle watching in Indonesia
- The Glenn Nusa wreck at 30 meters requires Advanced certification — discuss this with your operator in advance
- Strong current days bring manta rays and larger pelagics past the outer canyon walls
- Morning dives tend to have the best turtle activity and visibility before afternoon boat traffic

## Safety Considerations

The outer reef drop and canyon system at 25+ meters require confident depth management. Current can accelerate rapidly at the canyon entrance. Never ascend through the open water beyond the reef edge — always return to the reef and ascend with an SMB in the shallows. Be aware of boat traffic at this popular site.

## Photography

The turtle zone in the shallows offers some of the most accessible and consistently good turtle photography in the Gili Islands. Wide-angle shots of multiple turtles on a single frame are achievable. Blacktip reef sharks in the shallows make striking portraits against the white sandy patches. Canyon topography with fish schools suits wide-angle with strong backscatter control.
