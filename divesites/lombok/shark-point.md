## Shark Point

The northwest tip of Gili Trawangan — blacktip and whitetip reef sharks, sea turtles in the sea grass shallows, and the intentionally scuttled Glenn Nusa tugboat at 28 m.

## Overview

Shark Point is on the northwest headland of Gili Trawangan, where a sloping reef at 8–18 m gives way to deeper ridges and canyons. Blacktip and whitetip reef sharks cruise the site consistently, and large numbers of green turtles rest in the sea grass and coral bommies at 5–12 m. The current sweeps across the point, concentrating schools of jacks, trevally, humpback snapper, and bumphead parrotfish.

The Glenn Nusa tugboat lies on the sandy bottom at 28 m between Halik and Shark Point. It is an old tugboat that was cleaned and intentionally scuttled in February 2016 by a collaborative project of the Gili Islands dive centres and the Gili Eco Trust, to create a new fish habitat and artificial reef. The vessel is 22 m long, 6 m high, and 7 m wide, and lies upright. Large openings allow wreck penetration dives for certified wreck divers or wreck specialty course participants. Schools of batfish and snapper, whitetip sharks, blue-spotted stingrays, pipefish, leaf scorpionfish, and nudibranchs have been confirmed at the wreck.

Access to the Glenn Nusa requires Advanced Open Water certification (minimum 30 m depth rating) and involves an extra charge at some operators, as it is beyond the other Shark Point diving.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest Gili Trawangan, accessible from Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 m
- **Current**: Strong; always a drift dive
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Blacktip and whitetip reef sharks throughout the site — consistently present. Green turtles in large numbers on the shallow reef and sea grass at 5–12 m — one of the highest-density turtle areas in the Gili Islands. Bumphead parrotfish schools on the reef. Humpback snapper schools in the canyon heads. Schools of jackfish (bigeye trevally) at the headland. Blue-spotted stingrays on sandy patches.

At the Glenn Nusa wreck (28 m): schools of batfish and snapper on the hull, whitetip sharks, blue-spotted stingrays, pipefish, leaf scorpionfish, and nudibranchs — all confirmed by Dive Central Gili and Terumbu Divers.

*Species listed above are confirmed at this specific site by multiple local dive operators.*

## Dive Profile

Begin on the sloping reef at 8–18 m and follow the current. Visit the turtle zone at 5–12 m in the sea grass. If Glenn Nusa is on the agenda (Advanced divers only), descend to 28 m on the sandy bottom. Ascend via the reef slope and complete the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Lombok or Gili Trawangan. Always a drift dive — enter upstream, pickup downstream. SMB essential.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The 5–12 m turtle zone is outstanding and accessible to all diver levels as part of the main dive
- Glenn Nusa requires Advanced Open Water minimum and is dived as a separate dive or extension — confirm with your operator
- Dive on neap tides for more manageable current at the canyon entry
- Intermediate divers should stay on the shallower reef sections; advanced divers can explore deeper

## Safety Considerations

Strong current is always present. Advanced divers only for the deep canyons and Glenn Nusa wreck. Do not attempt wreck penetration without a wreck diving certification. Always carry an SMB. Coordinate the drift pickup point with the boat before descent.

## Photography

Reef sharks resting on the reef slope. Schools of black snappers around the Glenn Nusa wreck suit wide-angle. Turtle portraits in the shallow sea grass zone with natural light. Bumphead parrotfish schools on the reef.
