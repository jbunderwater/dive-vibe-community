## Trawangan Wall

The northwest wall of Gili Trawangan — also referred to as Halik Reef — a drift wall with barrel sponges, gorgonian fans, eagle rays, and reef sharks to 40 meters.

## Overview

Trawangan Wall is the northwest wall of Gili Trawangan, a site that overlaps with what some operators call "Halik Reef." Blue Marlin Dive records Halik as having a shallow section (15–22 m) and a deeper section (30–40 m) with bommies and gorgonian fans. Oceans 5 Dive describes a steep slope with gullies, excellent for drift diving. Gili Divers confirms eagle rays, turtles, barrel sponges, reef sharks, and cuttlefish. Trawangan Dive confirms eagle rays, bumphead parrotfish (at full moon), giant trevally, and snapper schools. The site is dived as a drift from the northwest wall.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest Gili Trawangan, accessible from Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Current**: Moderate to strong; consistent drift
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Confirmed by multiple operators: eagle rays (Trawangan Dive, Gili Divers), reef sharks (Gili Divers), giant trevally, snapper schools, bumphead parrotfish at full moon (Trawangan Dive), barrel sponges, turtles, cuttlefish, ribbon eels, moray eels. Lionfish in overhangs. Anemones with clownfish.

## Safety Considerations

Drift current is consistent. Always carry an SMB. Deep sections (30–40 m) require Advanced certification.
