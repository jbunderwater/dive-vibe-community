## Deep Turbo

A northeast Gili Trawangan pinnacle site — coral sea mounds rising from a sandy bottom to 10–15 m, with sea fans, leopard sharks, and strong currents for advanced divers.

## Overview

Deep Turbo sits on the northeast side of Gili Trawangan, where coral pinnacles (sea mounds) rise from a sandy bottom. The site features several large structures with overhangs, small caves, and dense soft coral and gorgonian sea fan growth. Maximum depth is 40 m, though the most productive diving occurs at 20–30 m. Strong and sometimes unpredictable currents are the defining characteristic of this site — they concentrate the fish life but require advanced buoyancy and drift skills. Nitrox is commonly used here to extend bottom time at depth.

Shallow Turbo, to the south of this site, covers the same reef system at shallower depth and serves as the beginner-accessible version.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northeast Gili Trawangan, accessible from Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 m (current-swept)
- **Current**: Strong to very strong; variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Giant sea fans on the pinnacle structures — a defining visual feature of the site. Leopard sharks (also called zebra sharks) reported on the sandy bottom around the mounds. Whitetip and blacktip reef sharks. Barracuda in mid-water. Schooling batfish and butterfly fish. Scorpionfish and lionfish in the overhangs and small caves. Garden eels in the sandy bottom around the mound bases. Turtles on the shallower sections.

*Species listed are confirmed by local dive operators for this site. Pelagic encounters (trevally, tuna) are possible in strong current but not guaranteed.*

## Dive Profile

Boat entry upstream. Descend quickly and locate the nearest pinnacle structure. Use the mounds as reference to maintain depth in the current. Explore the overhangs and caves for resident fish. Ascend the pinnacle face and deploy SMB before drifting off. Do not exceed planned no-decompression limits — gas consumption increases significantly in strong current.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Lombok or Gili Islands operators. Entry timing based on current conditions. Drift pickup required. SMB mandatory. Nitrox recommended.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced Open Water certification minimum; proven drift and deep experience required
- A reef hook is useful for stationing at the upcurrent face
- Nitrox significantly extends viable bottom time at 25–30 m on this site
- Only dive with operators who know this site's current patterns
- Shallow Turbo is a good warm-up dive on the same system before attempting Deep Turbo

## Safety Considerations

Strong current is the primary hazard. Be prepared to abort the descent if conditions exceed your ability. Do not exceed planned depth while focused on fish activity. Coordinate with the boat on current direction and drift pickup plan before descending. Always carry an SMB.

## Photography

Large sea fans in profile against the blue water suit wide-angle. Leopard sharks resting on sand around the mound bases. Barracuda in current-swept water require fast shutter speeds. The cave/overhang interiors suit strobe-lit wide-angle.
