## Deep Turbo

An offshore sea mound north of Gili Trawangan with strong currents, bumphead parrotfish schools, pygmy seahorses, and big pelagics — one of the top advanced dives in the Gili Islands.

## Overview

Deep Turbo is a sea mound (pinnacle) north of Gili Trawangan, reserved for advanced divers comfortable with strong current conditions. The mound rises from the sandy bottom to 18 m at its shallowest and drops steeply, with advanced dive depths reaching 30–35 m. Strong currents concentrate marine life — bumphead parrotfish schools, schooling batfish, eagle rays, reef sharks, and sleeping turtles are among the confirmed attractions. Giant gorgonian sea fans host pygmy seahorses. When conditions allow drifting between mounds on medium current, the experience is outstanding. The site is best experienced with Nitrox to maximise bottom time at depth. Approximately 25 minutes by boat from Gili Air operators.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North of Gili Trawangan, offshore channel
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/sea mound
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30–35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 m (current-swept)
- **Current**: Strong; always present; medium current days optimal
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Schools of bumphead parrotfish confirmed at this site. Schools of batfish. Spotted eagle rays in sandy areas. White and blacktip reef sharks patrolling the reef. Pygmy seahorses in giant gorgonian sea fans. Green turtles sleeping in coral on medium current days. Schooling fusiliers and redtooth triggerfish. Moray eels, scorpionfish, and lionfish in the reef structure. Leopard shark reported by Blue Marlin Dive.

## Dive Profile

Boat entry upstream. Descend quickly and locate the mound. On medium current days, drift between mounds for the most productive dive. Maintain depth using the pinnacle as reference while observing pelagics. Deploy SMB at depth before surfacing away from the mound.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Gili Islands operators who schedule this site based on conditions. Timing based on current. Drift pickup required. SMB mandatory. Site may be unavailable on strong current or wave days.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced Open Water certification required; Nitrox recommended to maximise bottom time
- Medium current days produce the best combination of pelagic activity and safe diving
- Examine gorgonian fans carefully for pygmy seahorses
- A reef hook is useful for stationing at the upcurrent face
- Only dive with operators who know current patterns at this site

## Safety Considerations

Strong current is the primary hazard. Be prepared to abort if conditions exceed your ability. Do not descend beyond planned depth while focused on pelagics. Coordinate with the boat on current direction and pickup plan. Always carry an SMB.

## Photography

Bumphead parrotfish schools suit wide-angle and fast shutter speeds. Eagle rays in current against blue water. Pygmy seahorses on gorgonian fans require dedicated macro lens. Reef sharks with schooling fish in context.
