# Vila Almarick Biorock

A Biorock coral restoration structure sponsored by Vila Ombak (Almarick) resort — one of the original sites in the Gili Islands' pioneering reef restoration programme.

## Overview

The Vila Almarick Biorock structure is one of the earliest Biorock installations in the Gili Islands, directly tied to the programme's origins. The first Biorock trial installation in the Gili Islands took off in November 2004 at the invitation of the Vila Ombak Diving Academy (now associated with the Almarick resort group), backed by the local community. This structure has been maintained and expanded over the years and now forms a mature section of the Gili's artificial reef mosaic. The frame sits at depths of 5 to 18 meters off Gili Trawangan and features established coral growth across multiple sections of the structure. As one of the oldest Biorock frames in the system, coral growth here is more advanced than at newer structures, offering a useful comparison point.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Gili Trawangan, Lombok, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Artificial reef (Biorock structure)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters (60 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters
- **Current**: Mild around the structure
- **Water Temperature**: 26–30°C (79–86°F)

## Marine Life

As an older Biorock structure, this frame has more established coral coverage than newer installations, supporting a denser and more complex fish community. Anemonefish, pipefish, and juvenile groupers are consistent residents. Nudibranchs and flatworms colonize the extensive encrusted surfaces. Lionfish use the frame as perch points. Moray eels have established burrows in the lower frame sections. Turtles graze around the structure in the surrounding reef area. Schools of glassfish hover above the frame, attracted by the structure's complexity.

## Dive Profile

Explore the structure from its shallowest sections at 5 meters down to its deepest at 18 meters, examining coral growth and associated marine life throughout. The older sections of the frame display more developed calcium carbonate crusting and denser coral colonies than newer areas. Safety stop in the shallows above the frame. Total dive time of 50–60 minutes is easily achievable at this depth.

## Entry and Exit

Short boat trip from Gili Trawangan. Vila Ombak/Almarick dive operation and independent Gili T operators both visit this structure. Giant stride entry; easy boat exit.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Compare the coral growth density on older sections versus newer additions to understand how Biorock restoration progresses over time
- The mature frame sections show how effective coral colonization can be on Biorock structures after 10–15 years
- Ask the dive operator about the history of this specific structure — it has an interesting story in the context of the Gili Biorock programme
- Suitable for snorkelers in the 5-meter sections for those not diving

## Safety Considerations

Calm, beginner-appropriate conditions. Maintain buoyancy control throughout to protect the mature coral growth. Do not touch the frame or coral — the established colonies are the result of years of programme work. Follow all briefing instructions.

## Photography

The mature coral growth on this older Biorock structure creates more visually complex photographic opportunities than newer frames. Close-up shots of established coral colonies alongside the steel substrate show the programme's long-term success. Dense fish populations and mature invertebrate colonization make this one of the best Biorock frames for macro and reef photography in the Gili Islands.
