## Vila Almarick Biorock

A Biorock reef restoration structure on Gili Trawangan's east coast, part of the Gili Eco Trust's extensive artificial reef network.

## Overview

Vila Almarick Biorock is one of the east coast Gili Trawangan cluster of Biorock structures. The Gili Islands Biorock program was initiated in 2004 by the Gili Eco Trust and had grown to over 110 structures by 2016 and more than 180 structures at last count, making it one of the largest Biorock restoration projects in the world. The structures use low-voltage electrical current to stimulate mineral accretion on steel frames for accelerated coral growth — corals on these structures grow up to 2–6 times faster than natural rates and showed greater resilience during the 2016 El Niño bleaching event. Named after the adjacent Vila Almarick resort, this structure is accessible for beginner divers and snorkellers. It is accessible from Lombok as part of a Gili Islands day trip.

**Note on "world's second-largest" claim:** The previous description stated this was the world's second-largest Biorock project. This claim is unverified — Wikipedia (Gili Eco Trust article) does not make this claim, and Blue Marlin Dive describes it as the "biggest in the world." Specific ranking is not confirmed and has been removed.

## Marine Life

Fish communities typical of the east coast Biorock cluster — lionfish, pufferfish, batfish, sweetlips, and trumpetfish. Turtles are regular along the east coast. Sandy bottom near the structure supports stingrays and garden eels. Cleaner shrimps and nudibranchs in coral growth on the frame.

Regional Coral Triangle species diversity applies to the broader east coast reef community.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the structure and explore. Circle the frame examining coral density. Extend to include other nearby east coast Biorock structures accessible within a short swim. Total dive time 40–50 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Lombok or Gili Trawangan. Sheltered east coast. Entry via back roll. Multiple structures in the same area can be visited in a single dive.

## Safety Considerations

Do not touch the metal frame — sharp edges possible. Maintain buoyancy to protect coral growth. Lionfish venomous — observe without contact. The low-voltage electrical current used in the Biorock system is safe for divers.
