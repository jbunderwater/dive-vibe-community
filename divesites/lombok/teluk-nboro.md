## Teluk NBoro

A bay dive on Lombok's northwest coast in the Teluk Nara/Naro area — silt and rubble bottom with rare critters for confident intermediate divers.

## Overview

Teluk NBoro corresponds to the Teluk Nara (Naro) harbour area on Lombok's northwest coast, approximately 25 minutes by boat from the Gili Islands. The site features a soft, silty sea floor under a pier structure, and the silt bottom is the primary critter habitat. Good buoyancy is essential — stirring the silt collapses visibility instantly. The pier chains and nearby structures are covered in sponges and sea fans, providing habitat for frogfish and rare nudibranchs. This is a challenging but rewarding muck dive best suited to confident intermediate divers with good buoyancy control.

- **Location**: Teluk Nara/Naro area, northwest Lombok coast
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Muck dive

- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Low — silty conditions
- **Current**: Generally mild inside the bay
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Rare mimic octopus and coconut octopus on the silt bottom — confirmed by Dive Central Gili as specific to the Teluk Nara harbour area. Blue-ringed octopus has been reported here (Dive Central Gili). Painted frogfish on pier chains and sea fan structures. Rare nudibranchs. Scrawled filefish around the pier.

*All species listed are confirmed by Dive Central Gili for the Teluk Nara harbour muck diving site. Only 1 source found — treat specific species claims with caution pending additional sources.*

## Dive Profile

Descend and move slowly over the silt bottom, maintaining neutral buoyancy at all times. Focus on the pier chains and their attached growth for frogfish and nudibranchs. The silt floor must not be disturbed. Night dives here extend the species list with active octopus and other nocturnal critters.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Gili Islands or Lombok northwest coast operators. Short transit from Gili Air. Entry via back roll. Calm conditions typically.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent buoyancy is mandatory — the silt collapses visibility immediately if disturbed
- A primary and backup torch for peering into structure recesses
- Night dives reveal active critters not visible by day
- Combine with Frog Fish Point for a full macro/muck day from Lombok or Gili Air
- Blue-ringed octopus are present — admire from safe distance, never handle

## Safety Considerations

Low visibility from silt requires attention to depth and direction. Do not stir the silt. Blue-ringed octopus at this site are extremely venomous — never pick them up. Standard buoyancy care essential.

## Photography

Mimic octopus in action on the silt floor. Blue-ringed octopus (extreme care — photograph safely at distance). Frogfish on pier chains with surrounding growth context. Nudibranchs on sea fans suit macro with diopter.
