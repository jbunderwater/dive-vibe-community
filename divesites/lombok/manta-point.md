## Manta Point

A gently sloping reef on the south side of Gili Trawangan where reef manta rays are seasonally encountered — reef sharks, sweetlips, and turtles on every dive.

## Overview

Manta Point is located on the south coast of Gili Trawangan. The site features a gently sloping reef with hard and soft corals, bommies, and large table corals from 8 m down to approximately 24 m. Gentle current occasionally makes this a pleasant drift dive. The reef has consistent populations of reef sharks, sweetlips, turtles, and reef fish regardless of season. Manta rays are the bonus encounter: the best season is March to May, when plankton-rich water draws them to feed, though they can appear year-round in smaller numbers. At peak season, up to 17 mantas have reportedly been seen at one time at this location.

Stingrays, cuttlefish, and octopus are also regularly encountered on the sandy patches between bommies.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South coast of Gili Trawangan, accessible from Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 m
- **Current**: Light to moderate; gentle drift
- **Best Time**: March–May for manta rays; May–October for best general conditions

## Marine Life

Reef manta rays — seasonal encounters, best March to May. In season, multiple mantas possible in one dive (up to 17 reported at one time). Whitetip reef sharks resting around bommie corals. Sweetlips and batfish around coral heads. Green and hawksbill turtles throughout the reef. Blue-spotted stingrays on sandy patches. Cuttlefish and octopus.

*Manta ray encounters are seasonal and not guaranteed. Reef sharks, sweetlips, and turtles are confirmed reliable sightings at this site. Species list based on reports from local dive operators at this specific site.*

## Dive Profile

Enter upstream of the reef. Follow the gentle slope between 10–20 m, scanning both the reef and open water for mantas. Spend time around the bommies for reef shark and sweetlips encounters. Turtles are found throughout. Complete a safety stop before surfacing. Drift pickup by boat if current is running.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Lombok northwest coast or Gili Islands operators. Light drift entry. SMB for pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Treat manta sightings as a welcome bonus rather than a guaranteed occurrence
- The reef and turtle encounters make this worth diving regardless of manta activity
- Check with your operator for recent manta sightings before specifically requesting this site
- March to May is the highest-probability window for mantas at this site
- Combine with Air Wall or Bounty Wreck for a varied Lombok diving day

## Safety Considerations

Standard drift dive precautions apply. Maintain SMB carry. Manta rays require a 3 m minimum distance and should never be touched.

## Photography

Manta rays in shallow water produce excellent natural light shots. Reef shark portraits at rest around bommies. Turtle wide-angle in the drift reef.
