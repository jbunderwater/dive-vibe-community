# Manta Point

The best spot in the Gili Islands for manta ray encounters during the wet season, with pink cabbage corals and abundant reef fish year-round.

## Overview

Manta Point is situated off the south coast of Gili Trawangan, in the area where the Indonesian Throughflow — the massive Pacific-to-Indian-Ocean current that runs through the Lombok Strait — generates nutrient upwellings. During the wet season, typically April to November, when plankton concentrations are at their highest, manta rays, devil rays, and eagle rays gather here to feed. From 10–15 meters, divers drift through surge above beautiful pink cabbage corals while scanning the blue water for approaching rays. Even outside manta season, the site holds whitetip reef sharks, schooling sweetlips, snappers, and fusiliers in the water column above the reef. The gentle surge in the shallows makes this primarily a drift dive with variable conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South coast, Gili Trawangan, Lombok, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters (66 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters (lower during plankton blooms)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong; surge in shallows
- **Water Temperature**: 26–30°C (79–86°F)

## Marine Life

Manta rays (oceanic and reef species) and devil rays are the seasonal highlight, most frequently sighted April through November during plankton-rich periods. Eagle rays visit year-round. Whitetip reef sharks cruise the reef edge at 15–20 meters. The shallow zone features swaying pink cabbage coral with schools of sweetlips, snappers, fusiliers, and chromis. Blue-spotted stingrays rest on sandy patches. Lionfish and spiny lobsters inhabit the deeper sections. Turtles are present year-round in the shallower areas.

## Dive Profile

The dive follows a drift from the southern approach to the site, hovering at 10–15 meters above the cabbage coral zone. Keep one eye on the reef and one scanning the blue water beyond the reef edge. When mantas are present, experienced divemasters will signal to the group and position divers to let the rays approach naturally. The deeper 15–20 meter zone is explored during the early or late portions of the dive. Ascend with the current and deploy an SMB for boat pickup.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Gili Trawangan. The south coast involves a longer boat ride than the northern sites. Entry conditions depend heavily on swell — the southern exposure means this site may be skipped by operators when Indian Ocean swell is running. Giant stride entry; SMB ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Visit April through November for the best chance of manta encounters
- Visibility may be reduced to 8–12 meters during strong plankton blooms — this is normal and sign of good manta conditions
- Stay calm and horizontal when mantas approach — sudden movements cause them to veer away
- The pink cabbage corals in the surge zone at 10 meters are a distinctive feature worth slow exploration even without rays

## Safety Considerations

The southern exposure makes this site susceptible to Indian Ocean swell, which can create surge near the reef. The divemaster team assesses conditions daily and will cancel if unsafe. During strong current, maintain reef contact or a known depth reference. Always carry an SMB at Manta Point — currents can separate divers from the boat.

## Photography

Manta encounters at Manta Point are among the best wide-angle shooting opportunities in the Gili Islands. Stay low and let the rays pass overhead for dramatic silhouette shots. The pink cabbage corals make attractive ambient-light wide-angle subjects when mantas are absent. Blue-water background shots of ray silhouettes work well in plankton-rich conditions.
