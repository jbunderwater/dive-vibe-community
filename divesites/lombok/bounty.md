## Bounty

The Gili Islands' most accessible wreck dive — a pontoon on Gili Meno's western slope fully overgrown with soft corals and sheltering diverse marine life.

## Overview

The Bounty Wreck lies on the western or southwestern side of Gili Meno. The pontoon served as a floating pier and sank during a storm, with the most commonly cited date being 2001 (sources vary: one cites 1999, one the early 2000s, one 2002 — the exact year is not fully consistent across independent sources). The structure is 30 m long and 15 m wide, with its highest point at 8 m and maximum depth at 18 m, making it accessible throughout its length for Open Water level divers. Over the years the structure has been completely colonised by soft and hard corals and now functions as a thriving artificial reef. It was among the first artificial reef structures in the Gili Islands / Lombok area. Frogfish, scorpionfish, angelfish, ghost pipefish, drummer fish, and damselfish are confirmed residents.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West/southwest side of Gili Meno, Gili Islands / Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate (accessible to all certified divers)
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 m
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Frogfish in various positions on the coral-encrusted hull. Scorpionfish camouflaged against the encrusted surface. Drummer fish (convict surgeonfish) and damselfish in resident groups. Ghost pipefish among soft coral growth. Moray eels inside the structure. Angelfish species throughout the wreck. Turtles cruise past the shallow sections. Whitetip reef sharks occasionally visit.

Regional Coral Triangle species diversity applies to the broader reef community surrounding the wreck. Pygmy seahorses are mentioned in some destination overviews for the Gili Islands but are not specifically confirmed as a regular resident by wreck-specific source pages.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deeper tip of the pontoon at 18 m and work your way up to the shallowest section at 8 m. Search the coral growth methodically for frogfish and scorpionfish. The entire wreck can be circled multiple times given the generous air at this depth. Complete the safety stop above the shallow section.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Gili Meno or Gili Trawangan operators. Mooring on the wreck. Entry via back roll. Minimal current on most days. Ascent from the wreck structure for the safety stop.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Frogfish are cryptic; look for irregular shapes on the encrusted surfaces
- Scorpionfish are extremely well-camouflaged on the hull — never rest hands or knees on the wreck surface
- A torch reveals colour on the soft corals that is lost without artificial light
- Combine with Meno Wall for a comprehensive Gili Meno diving day

## Safety Considerations

The wreck structure may have sharp metal edges — do not touch it. Do not attempt interior penetration without wreck diving training. Scorpionfish are extremely well-camouflaged on the hull. Always carry an SMB.

## Photography

Frogfish portraits in their cryptic positions on the wreck suit macro. Scorpionfish camouflaged on encrusted hull. Wide-angle captures the complete pontoon structure with accompanying fish life. The coral-covered hull makes stunning close-focus wide-angle subjects.
