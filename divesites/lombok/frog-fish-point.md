## Frog Fish Point

A muck and macro dive site near the east side of Gili Air — multiple frogfish species, seahorses, ghost pipefish, and leaf scorpionfish in a sandy rubble and coral habitat.

## Overview

Frog Fish Point (also referenced as the Gili Air harbour area dive site) is a dedicated muck and macro site on the eastern side of Gili Air. The site is characterised by sandy substrate, rubble patches, sparse coral, and the macro-rich conditions typical of harbour-adjacent muck diving. Multiple frogfish species are the signature find — warty, giant, and hairy frogfish have all been reported here by local operators. Seahorses, ghost pipefish, and leaf scorpionfish are also regularly found. The site is calmer and shallower than the current-driven reef sites in the Gili Islands circuit, making it a popular complement to deeper dives.

The coral block on the east side of Gili Air drops from 5–18 m with anemone-covered coral and surrounding garden eels, with sandy areas extending toward the harbour.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East side of Gili Air (harbour area), Gili Islands / Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Muck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–18 m
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Multiple frogfish species reported at this specific site: warty frogfish, giant frogfish, and hairy frogfish. Seahorses and pipefish in rubble and sparse algae zones. Ghost pipefish among debris. Leaf scorpionfish in rubble. Black-spotted moray eels. Nudibranchs throughout the rubble zones. Mantis shrimps at burrow entrances. Blue-spotted stingrays on the sand. Garden eels in the sandy patches. Spearers (mantis shrimp) also reported.

*All frogfish species and critter sightings are confirmed by local dive operators for this specific site.*

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sandy substrate at 10–20 m and move extremely slowly across the site. Your guide will typically have pre-scouted known frogfish positions. Examine each piece of rubble, sparse coral fragment, and algae tuft systematically. A full 55-minute dive is easily spent on this site.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Gili Air (very short transit). Entry via back roll or giant stride. Minimal current. Return to mooring or operator pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Ask your guide specifically about current frogfish locations before the dive — they are pre-scouted
- Move extremely slowly — frogfish are spotted by shape irregularity, not colour
- The Gili Air harbour mandarin fish display is worth a separate dawn or dusk dive
- A dedicated macro camera setup rewards with detailed frogfish portraits
- Combine with Air Wall for a varied Gili Air diving day

## Safety Considerations

Maintain positive buoyancy above the sand at all times — stonefish and scorpionfish can be present. Do not stir the sandy substrate — visibility collapses instantly and critters are disturbed. Standard muck site precautions apply.

## Photography

Frogfish in their full cryptic context are the priority subject. Each colour morph and species presents a different photographic challenge. Ghost pipefish and seahorses suit a 100mm macro lens. Nudibranchs reward macro with diopter for maximum detail.
