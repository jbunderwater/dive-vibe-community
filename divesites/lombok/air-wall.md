## Air Wall

Gili Air's signature wall dive on the island's west side — gorgonian sea fans, bumphead parrotfish, and a vertical drop to 28 m.

## Overview

Air Wall runs along the west (southwest) side of Gili Air, dropping from a shallow reef crest at 5–10 m down to 24–28 m on a steep face covered with soft corals, sea fans, and coral bommies. The wall holds some of the rarer gorgonian sea fans in the Gili Islands, and the sandy slope above the wall top is where bumphead parrotfish are regularly spotted feeding in schools. A cleaning station around 22 m, marked by a large coral block, draws leaf fish and glassfish. Turtles frequent the shallow upper reef. The consistent current makes this a comfortable drift dive for intermediate divers, and the sheltered position of the wall gives it reliably good visibility.

**Note on location:** Multiple dive operators place Air Wall on the west or southwest side of Gili Air. Earlier versions of this description incorrectly stated the eastern side — corrected here based on sources.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West/southwest side of Gili Air, Gili Islands / Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 m
- **Current**: Light to moderate; consistent along the wall face
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Bumphead parrotfish are spotted on the sandy slope at the top of the wall and on the upper reef crest — a reliable and striking sighting. Gorgonian sea fans throughout the wall face, noted as rare and impressive for the Gili Islands. Hawksbill turtles feed along the reef crest. Leaf fish and glassfish at the cleaning station around 22 m. Moray eels, cuttlefish, scorpionfish, and octopus in wall crevices. Clownfish in anemone fields. Nudibranchs, wire coral gobies, and orangutan crabs in the shallower coral zone. Whitetip sharks have been reported in the deeper sections.

*No site-specific marine survey data exists for this site. Species listed are confirmed sightings reported by local dive operators.*

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wall face at 10–15 m and follow it. Stay between 15–22 m to observe the gorgonians and main wall features. Visit the cleaning station at around 22 m. Watch the sandy slope at the top of the dive for bumphead parrotfish before or after the wall section. Ascend gradually to the shallow coral crest and complete a safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Gili Air (short transit). Entry via back roll or giant stride. Current along the wall is manageable for intermediate divers. SMB for drift ascent if current is running.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Scan the gorgonian fans carefully for pygmy seahorses — they have been reported at Gili Air sites
- Arrive early to catch bumphead parrotfish activity on the upper reef
- Combine with a Frog Fish Point macro dive for a varied Gili Air day
- Mandarin fish are found in the Gili Air harbour area — worth a separate dawn/dusk visit

## Safety Considerations

Current can increase on the open wall face. Be aware of depth on the wall sections. Always carry an SMB. Reef sharks in the deeper sections are non-aggressive.

## Photography

Gorgonian sea fans suit wide-angle close-focus with strobes. Bumphead parrotfish schools require wide-angle and fast shutter. Leaf fish and glassfish at the cleaning station suit macro. Turtles in the shallow natural light suit wide-angle.
