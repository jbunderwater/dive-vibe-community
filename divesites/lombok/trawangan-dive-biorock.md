# Trawangan Dive Biorock

A Biorock electrolytic reef restoration structure maintained by Trawangan Dive — one of the Gili Islands' premier coral conservation organisations.

## Overview

The Trawangan Dive Biorock structure is part of the globally significant Gili Islands reef restoration network, maintained by Trawangan Dive, one of Gili Trawangan's longest-established PADI 5-Star dive centres. Trawangan Dive has been at the forefront of the Gili Islands' Biorock programme since its inception in 2004, running public education sessions, training volunteers, and hosting International Reef Restoration Training Workshops. This specific structure sits off Gili Trawangan at depths of 5 to 18 meters, featuring active coral growth on an electrified steel frame. The programme uses low-voltage current to accelerate coral growth rates three to five times above normal while significantly increasing resistance to thermal bleaching events. The Gili Islands as a whole host more than 120 Biorock structures, the second-largest concentration in the world.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Gili Trawangan, Lombok, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Artificial reef (Biorock structure)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters (60 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–25 meters
- **Current**: Mild around the structure
- **Water Temperature**: 26–30°C (79–86°F)

## Marine Life

The Biorock frame attracts and shelters fish in high densities relative to its size. Anemonefish inhabit anemones on the structure. Pipefish are common among the growing coral branches. Lionfish perch on frame edges throughout the day. Nudibranchs and flatworms colonize the frame surface between coral patches. Juvenile groupers and damselfish use the structure as territory. Reef octopus visit from the surrounding reef. Turtles are regularly seen in the shallows adjacent to the structure.

## Dive Profile

Shallow dive with the entire structure accessible between 5 and 18 meters. Start at the surface and explore the upper frame sections first. Work methodically across the structure, checking each section for marine life. Descend to the deepest sections only if comfortable at 18 meters. Ascend and complete a safety stop above the structure. Multiple passes of the structure are possible given the shallow depth and generous dive time.

## Entry and Exit

Short boat trip from Gili Trawangan's eastern beach. Trawangan Dive and other Gili T operators include this in beginner and fun-dive programs. Giant stride entry.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Trawangan Dive runs a dedicated Introduction to the Biorock Process eco-course — worth taking if you want to understand the science before diving
- Look at the physical frame closely — areas where calcium carbonate has accumulated show a whitish mineral crust on the steel
- Compare different sections of the structure to see variation in coral growth and colonization
- Morning dives offer cleaner water before afternoon boat traffic

## Safety Considerations

This is one of the safest and most accessible dive sites in the Gili Islands. Maintain buoyancy to avoid contact with the frame and coral fragments. Do not touch the frame even though the current is safe — it's the coral you need to protect. Follow all briefing instructions about approach distance.

## Photography

Macro photography on the frame surface reveals the colonization process in detail: mineral deposits, coral polyp attachment, and micro-organisms all make compelling images. Fish portraits in context with the structure tell the conservation story. Wide-angle shots showing the full frame with divers and surrounding reef create effective educational images.
