## Simons Reef

A deep advanced reef north of Gili Meno — sea mounds in a figure-eight formation with massive gorgonian fans, pygmy seahorses, schooling fish, and a sandy bottom at 35 m.

## Overview

Simon's Reef (also written Simons Reef) lies north of Gili Meno. The site consists of 8 major sea mounds (takats) arranged roughly in a figure-eight pattern, rising from a sandy bottom at 35 m. Each mound is sculpted with canyons, overhangs, and diverse coral growth including brain corals, gorgonian fans, and table corals. Pygmy seahorses have been reported in the gorgonian fans. Schools of fish and pelagic species including barracuda, tuna, and trevally are encountered in the channelling currents. Sea snakes have also been reported at the site. The site is for advanced divers only given the depth profile.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North of Gili Meno, Gili Islands / Lombok
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 m (current-swept)
- **Current**: Strong; channelling through sea mound system
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Pygmy seahorses in gorgonian fans — reported at this site by Gili Divers. Schools of snapper, fusiliers, and jack in the currents between mounds. Large barracuda and tuna in open water. Trevally. Sea snakes reported. Barrel sponges and diverse soft and hard coral varieties throughout.

Regional Coral Triangle species diversity applies. Source confirmation for pygmy seahorses comes from Gili Divers site listing; a second independent source specifically for this site was not found for pygmy seahorses during this QA pass.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sea mound system and navigate between the takats. Spend time examining the large gorgonian fans for pygmy seahorses. Allow channelling current to carry you between sections. Ascend to shallower sections before the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Lombok or Gili Islands operators. Entry timing based on current conditions. Drift pickup. SMB essential.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced Open Water with canyon and current experience
- The figure-eight formation rewards multiple dives — each visit reveals new passages
- One of the best deep dives accessible from Lombok's northwest coast
- Combine with Deep Turbo or Takat Malang for a full advanced north Gili area day

## Safety Considerations

Channelling currents between the mounds can be strong and unpredictable. Know your exit plan before entering each canyon section. Do not exceed planned depth. Always carry an SMB. Dive with experienced local guides.

## Photography

Massive gorgonian fans are the feature subject — wide-angle captures their scale. Pygmy seahorses require macro lens and skilled guide. Schooling fish in the currents suit wide-angle.
