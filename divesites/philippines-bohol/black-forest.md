# Black Forest

The most popular dive site at Balicasag Island, named for its dense forest of black coral (Antipathes) on a steep slope from 8 to 40+ meters. The black coral -- actually green in colour, with black internal skeleton -- creates dramatic formations at depth alongside barracuda schools, trevallies, and sea turtles.

## Overview

Black Forest is located on the northeast side of Balicasag Island, a small, nearly perfectly circular island just 600 meters in diameter, about 30 minutes by boat from Alona Beach on Panglao Island. Declared a marine sanctuary since 1985, Balicasag's reefs have benefited from nearly four decades of active protection, and Black Forest is considered the premier dive site on the island.

The site features a steep slope that drops from coral heads in the shallows at 8 meters to depths exceeding 40 meters. The name comes from the dense growth of black coral (Antipathes) found at 35-45 meters -- though the coral is actually green in its living state, with the distinctive black colour visible only in the internal skeleton. Gorgonian sea fans, schooling barracudas, trevallies, and occasional sea turtles make this a varied and exciting dive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northeast side, Balicasag Island, Bohol, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (30 min from Alona Beach)
- **Site Type**: Steep slope/wall
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 26-30 degrees C
- **Current**: Can be strong; drift diving possible
- **Best Time**: June to October (calmest seas)

## Marine Life

Schools of barracuda and trevally are regularly encountered along the slope. Colourful anthias cloud the reef face. Wrasses, snappers, batfish, triggerfish, surgeonfish, Moorish idols, and angelfish are common. Garden eels colonise the sandy patches at depth. Sea turtles are occasionally seen. The black coral forest at depth hosts small invertebrates among the branches. Gorgonian sea fans grow on the steeper sections.

## Dive Profile

Descend along the slope from the coral heads at 8 meters. The upper slope features hard corals, gorgonians, and the densest fish life. The black coral forest begins around 35 meters and extends to 45+ meters -- only accessible to deep-certified divers. Most of the dive is best spent between 15-30 meters where the fish schools concentrate. Watch for barracuda and trevally in the blue water off the slope. Ascend along the slope to the shallow coral heads for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Panglao Island (Alona Beach area), approximately 30 minutes. Entry by giant stride. Note: the municipality of Panglao limits divers at Balicasag to 150 per day with advance booking required at least 24 hours ahead.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book your Balicasag dive slot at least 24 hours in advance -- the 150-diver daily limit can fill up
- The black coral forest is at depth; only attempt if deep-certified and plan gas management carefully
- The barracuda schools are the visual highlight for most divers -- hover at 15-20 meters and let them approach
- Strong currents at Black Forest can turn this into a drift dive -- follow your guide's lead

## Safety Considerations

Currents can be strong at this site, requiring drift diving experience. The steep slope makes depth management critical -- the temptation to follow the black coral deeper is real. The 40-meter maximum depth demands careful gas planning. Follow the dive guide's briefing on current conditions and dive plan.
