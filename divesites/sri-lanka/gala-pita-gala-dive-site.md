## Gala pita gala dive site

Gala Pita Gala is a boat-accessed rock pinnacle off Unawatuna with a small natural cave cutting through it — one of the area's most consistently recommended dive sites across local operators.

## Overview

Gala Pita Gala (also spelled Galapita Gala) is a rock formation roughly 10-15 minutes by boat from Unawatuna, described consistently across four independent sources as "rock on top of rock" rising from the seabed to within about 10 meters of the surface, with a maximum depth around 25 meters at its base. A naturally formed cave, reported at approximately 3 meters long, runs through part of the formation — PADI and local operators note that while the site as a whole suits all levels, entering the cave itself is recommended only for Advanced-certified divers. This is a boat dive, not a shore dive as previously listed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sri Lanka, Asia (Unawatuna)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters

## Marine Life

Multiple operators (PADI, Sun Diving, Pearl Divers) report nudibranchs ("slugs"), sea worms, and both black and soft/fan corals on the rock surfaces. Large lobsters, stingrays, blue-ring angelfish, and lionfish are specifically named as sightings at this site. One source additionally lists trigger fish, parrotfish, giant trevally, scorpionfish, and occasional hammerhead sharks, though hammerhead reports were not corroborated across multiple sources and should be treated as an occasional/less certain sighting rather than a reliable encounter.
