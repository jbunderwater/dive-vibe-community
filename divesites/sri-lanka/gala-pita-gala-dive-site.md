## Gala pita gala dive site

Gala Pita Gala is a rock pinnacle off Unawatuna — an "assortment of rocks one on top of another," not a coral reef.

## Overview

Gala Pita Gala (coordinates matched to within ~150m against the PADI dive site database) is a large underwater rock pinnacle off Unawatuna on Sri Lanka's south coast. Divers circle the formation on a multi-level route, working from around 10 meters at the top of the rock down to about 25 meters at its base. The rock is covered in soft coral, fan coral and sponges, and multiple sources independently describe it as having "more fish than corals" — a fish-rich site rather than a classic coral garden, which is why this listing has been reclassified from "reef" to "pinnacle."

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sri Lanka, Asia
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters

## Marine Life

Divers report nudibranchs, several shrimp species, angelfish, butterflyfish, triggerfish, parrotfish, giant trevally and scorpionfish at this specific site. One operator additionally reports a small (~3m) cave within the formation with manta ray sightings nearby. We have deliberately left out "sharks, whales, dolphins, turtles" — the same phrase appears verbatim on multiple unrelated Sri Lanka PADI site listings and reads as regional boilerplate rather than a site-specific report.
