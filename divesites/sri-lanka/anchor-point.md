## Anchor Point

Anchor Point is a pair of rocky reef sites off Kalpitiya on Sri Lanka's northwest coast, split into a shallower and a deeper section by local dive operators.

## Overview

Anchor Point is a boat-accessed rocky reef site in the Kalpitiya dive area, reached as part of the region's standard two-tank boat trips. Local operators (Kalpitiya Diving Center, Kalpitiya Discovery Diving) list it as two distinct sections: "Anchor Point Reef 1" at around 14 meters and "Anchor Point Reef 2" at around 17 meters, both on a stone/rocky reef bottom. No source found confirms the origin of the site's name or the presence of actual ship's anchors on the seabed — a previous version of this page's claim about "old anchors and anchor chain fragments from the spice trade" was unsupported and has been removed.

Kalpitiya's wider reputation for spinner dolphin aggregations and a seasonal (roughly November–March) whale shark presence is well documented at the destination level, though these are area-wide phenomena rather than confirmed sightings tied specifically to this reef.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sri Lanka, Asia (Kalpitiya)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 17 meters

## Marine Life

Kalpitiya dive operators describe general reef fish ("aquarium fish") at both Anchor Point sections, without naming individual species specific to this site. Parrotfish, grouper, and sea turtles are commonly reported across Kalpitiya's rocky reefs generally. Spinner dolphins and, seasonally (November-March), whale sharks are a well-documented feature of the wider Kalpitiya area rather than a confirmed sighting at this specific reef.
