## Bar Reef

Bar Reef is Sri Lanka's largest coral reef system, a shallow marine sanctuary off Kalpitiya recording 156 coral and 283 reef fish species.

## Overview

Bar Reef is Sri Lanka's largest coral reef system — a marine sanctuary off Kalpitiya on the northwest coast, roughly 3 nautical miles long, reachable by about an hour's boat ride from shore. It is composed of shallow patch reefs of plate coral interspersed with sandstone reef and seagrass, and is one of the most heavily documented reefs in the country: surveys record 156 coral species and 283 reef fish species within the sanctuary. The reef sits in just 2-8 meters of water, making it a beginner-friendly, largely snorkel-depth site, though currents can pick up with the tides. Some shallow sections have been damaged by crown-of-thorns starfish, dynamite fishing and boat anchors, and the reef went through bleaching events in 1998 and 2016, but divers still report healthy coral cover in less-disturbed areas. Best diving season runs late November to early May, when the northeast monsoon brings calm conditions.

## Marine Life

Operators report black-tip and white-tip reef sharks, manta rays, and green, hawksbill and olive ridley turtles at Bar Reef, alongside a wide range of tropical reef fish drawn from the sanctuary's 283 recorded species. The 156 recorded coral species, dominated by plate coral, provide habitat across the sandy channels between reef patches.
