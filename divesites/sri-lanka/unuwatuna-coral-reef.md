## Unuwatuna Coral Reef

Unuwatuna Coral Reef is a shore-accessible reef dive site located 4 kilometers south of Galle town in southern Sri Lanka. The site is accessible from Unuwatuna beach, a popular tourist destination known for its relaxed atmosphere and excellent diving infrastructure.

## Overview

Unuwatuna offers accessible reef diving with a mix of hard and soft coral formations. The area is famous for nearby wreck diving, including the SS Rangoon wreck (32 meters deep). Water temperatures range from 27-30°C year-round, with best visibility (10-25 meters) occurring during the dry season from December to March. The site is suitable for intermediate divers with shore access capabilities.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Unuwatuna, Galle District, southern Sri Lanka
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Depth Range**: 5-18 meters (can access deeper areas)
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters (33-82 feet)
- **Current**: Variable, moderate to strong in outer sections
- **Water Temperature**: 27-30°C year-round
- **Best Time**: December to March (optimal conditions); October to April (diving season)

## Marine Life

Unuwatuna's coral formations support diverse marine life including Napoleon wrasse, black coral formations, and sea turtles. The reef system provides habitat for reef sharks, rays, lionfish, moray eels, and numerous colorful reef fish species. Seasonal visitors include whale sharks (January-March) and manta rays. The healthy coral ecosystem supports a wide variety of invertebrate species including nudibranchs and seahorses.

## Dive Profile

Unuwatuna's reef begins at the shoreline and slopes gradually to 18-25 meters. Start in shallow water (5-8 meters) to familiarize yourself with local conditions, then descend along the reef slope. Most marine life and coral formations are concentrated between 5-18 meters. The site allows exploration of both reef structure and deeper sections based on diver certification and experience. Plan 45-60 minute dives with adequate reserve for ascent and safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from Unuwatuna Beach via shore entry. Wade from the beach into deeper water, staying clear of other swimmers and boats. Established entry points are used by local dive centers. Navigate seaward using landmarks (Galle Fort to the north) or compass. The dive site is reached within 10-15 minutes of water entry. Exit by returning to the beach at the same location. Be alert to wave surge during exit and approach the shoreline diagonally to minimize impact.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Shore entry allows for flexible scheduling — no boat dependency
- Several dive centers operate in Unuwatuna (Pearl Divers, Sun Diving, Unawatuna Diving Centre) offering training and guided dives
- The nearby SS Rangoon wreck (32m) is a popular companion dive for experienced divers
- Best underwater photography occurs morning when light angles are optimal
- During whale shark season (January-March), inform dive operators of interest in species-specific dives
- The area includes other nearby reef and wreck sites (SS Orestes, Galle Ship Wreck) allowing multiple-site trips
- Strong currents possible during monsoon transition (May); avoid off-season diving if inexperienced

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of strong currents, jellyfish, sea urchins in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Strong currents are possible — carry appropriate signaling devices and be prepared to abort the dive if conditions deteriorate. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
