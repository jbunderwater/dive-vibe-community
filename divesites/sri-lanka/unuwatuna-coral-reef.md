## Unuwatuna Coral Reef

A shallow fringing reef inside Unawatuna's sheltered bay near Galle, supporting over 60 recorded hard coral species despite repeated bleaching stress.

## Overview

Unawatuna Coral Reef lies within the sheltered, crescent-shaped bay of Unawatuna near Galle on Sri Lanka's southwest coast, offering accessible shore diving through a shallow fringing reef down to around 18 meters. The bay's calm conditions and warm water (27-30°C) make it one of the country's most accessible dive and snorkel sites. Sources describe the reef as supporting over 60 species of hard coral, though — like Sri Lanka's other shallow reefs — it has been repeatedly stressed by coral bleaching events, including in 1998 and 2016, and conservation efforts are ongoing.

A wreck locally associated with the area, the SS Rangoon, lies nearby but is a physically separate dive site (not part of this reef itself) — sources describe it as a 19th-century British steamship in 18-30 meters of water; because it is not one of this database's tracked sites, we have not researched its history in depth here.

## Marine Life

Green and hawksbill turtles are commonly reported gliding over the reef or feeding on seagrass/sponges in the bay. General Unawatuna-reef sources also report lionfish, parrotfish, moray eels and nudibranchs. We have not repeated more specific claims (e.g., "large schools of chromis," "porcupinefish") from the prior version of this page, as we could not confirm them against a site-specific source.
