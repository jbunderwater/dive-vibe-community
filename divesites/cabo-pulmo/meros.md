# Meros

## Overview
Meros is a northern reef bar paired with El Bajo, featuring meandering coral and rock formations that give the site a varied, exploratory character. Named for the groupers ("meros" in Spanish) that congregate here, the site also attracts transiting whale sharks and mobula rays, along with regular sea turtle sightings. During winter months (December through March), humpback whale songs are audible underwater — a surreal addition to an already rich dive. The 9-18 meter depth range and moderate conditions make Meros accessible to intermediate divers while still delivering encounters with the park's larger marine residents.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park, Baja California Sur (northern reef)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (5-10 min from Cabo Pulmo village)
- **Site Type**: Reef bar / pinnacle
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: December-March for whale songs and mobula rays; year-round for reef life

## Marine Life
Grouper congregations (the site's namesake), transiting whale sharks, mobula rays, sea turtles, humpback whale songs audible underwater in winter. The meandering reef structure supports diverse resident fish populations.

## Dive Profile
Depths from 9 to 18 meters along the reef bar. Follow the meandering coral formations, checking overhangs for resting groupers. The reef connects to the El Bajo system to the south. The 45-minute park dive limit applies.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Dive in winter for the chance to hear humpback whale songs underwater
- Often combined with El Bajo on the same boat trip
- Look under overhangs and in crevices for the groupers the site is named after
- Keep an eye on the blue water — whale sharks and mobulas transit through without warning
