# El Cien

## Overview
El Cien ("One Hundred," named for its approximate depth in feet) is a deep outer reef site with a dramatic drop-off, offering Cabo Pulmo's most exposed pelagic diving. Also referred to locally as Mario's Reef. Bull sharks and nurse sharks patrol the deeper sections, while large groupers, dog snappers, and bigeye trevally aggregations move through the site. Sea turtles are regular visitors. The 30-meter depth and exposure to open-ocean currents — which can be strong and unpredictable — make this advanced divers only. El Cien and La Esperanza are often paired on the same boat trip. Tiger sharks have been listed as a possible encounter at this outer reef site, but no confirmed sighting reports were found for this specific location.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park, Baja California Sur (outer reef)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Moderate to strong; unpredictable
- **Best Time**: October to May for peak pelagic activity

## Marine Life
Bull sharks (most reliably November–May), nurse sharks, large groupers, dog snappers, bigeye trevally, sea turtles, mobula rays (seasonal). The drop-off edge is where most pelagic encounters occur. Tiger sharks are listed as a possible species at outer Cabo Pulmo reef sites; no site-specific confirmed sighting reports were found for El Cien.

## Dive Profile
Advanced certification required. The reef drops to 30 meters and currents can shift without warning. Descend to the reef edge and scan the blue water for pelagic traffic. Monitor depth and air carefully — deep profiles with current reduce safe bottom time below the 45-minute park limit. Carry an SMB.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Often paired with La Esperanza on the same boat trip
- Position yourself near the drop-off edge for best pelagic viewing
- Wide-angle setup essential
- Current strength varies significantly between dives — listen to the guide's briefing carefully
