# Las Casitas

## Overview
Las Casitas ("Little Houses") is a distinctive site within Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park dominated by several large rocks — some barely submerged — featuring caves, crevices, and swim-through passages. The "casitas" are the rock formations themselves, creating sheltered chambers that harbour resting sea turtles, moray eels, and diverse reef fish. Hard and soft corals cover the rock surfaces. Garden eels extend across the surrounding sand flats, retreating into their burrows as divers approach. The site can be reached from Mermaid Beach (Los Chopitos) when currents allow. The easy depth profile (averaging around 12 meters, max 15–16 meters) and sheltered conditions make this suitable for all levels including beginners and resort course participants. Note: no source confirmed cup corals (Tubastraea spp.) as a specific feature of this site.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park, Baja California Sur
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life
Sea turtles (resting in the cave formations), garden eels on surrounding sand, moray eels, groupers, snappers, starfish, and diverse tropical reef fish sheltering in the caves and crevices. Hard and soft corals cover the rock surfaces. The rock formations create microhabitats distinct from the park's reef bar sites. Regional marine life includes whale sharks as occasional Cabo Pulmo park visitors; no site-specific whale shark reports were found for Las Casitas.

## Dive Profile
Maximum depth of 15 meters. Navigate between and through the rock formations, peering into caves and crevices. No overhead environment diving certification required — the formations are open cavern-style with visible exits. Approach the sand flats slowly to observe garden eels. The 45-minute park dive limit applies.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Bring a torch to illuminate the caves and crevices for resting turtles and moray eels
- Move slowly near the sand flats for garden eel observation
- The sheltered rock formations make this a good choice on days with current at outer sites
- Despite the "cave" site type, this is open cavern diving — visible exits throughout
- Can be accessed from Mermaid Beach when surface currents permit
