# Los Morros

## Overview
Los Morros is a broken reef section adjacent to El Bajo, stretching roughly 400 yards in length. This is the iconic site for Cabo Pulmo's signature spectacle: the jack tornado. Massive schools of bigeye trevally form swirling columns so dense they can block out the sunlight overhead. The high fish concentration here is a direct result of the park's 30+ years of marine protection. The broken reef structure creates channels and pockets where fish aggregate, making nearly every dive an encounter with schooling life on a scale rarely seen elsewhere in the Sea of Cortez.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park, Baja California Sur
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; jack tornado most reliable October-May

## Marine Life
Bigeye trevally (jack tornado), dense schooling fish of multiple species, reef fish in the broken coral structure. The sheer biomass at this site is what makes it exceptional — quantity as much as variety.

## Dive Profile
Maximum depth of 18 meters along the broken reef. Most of the action happens in mid-water as the jack schools form and reform around divers. Stay neutrally buoyant in the water column to enjoy the spectacle without disturbing the reef below. The 45-minute park dive limit applies.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Wide-angle lens essential — this is the site for the classic jack tornado shot
- Stay still and let the school come to you rather than chasing
- Often combined with El Bajo on the same boat trip
- Morning dives tend to have the best jack tornado formations
