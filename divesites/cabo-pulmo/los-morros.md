# Los Morros

## Overview
Los Morros is a series of massive rock formations covered in sea fans and corals, located north of El Bajo and further offshore than the central reef sites. This is one of Cabo Pulmo's most iconic sites for the park's signature spectacle: the jack tornado. Massive schools of bigeye trevally form swirling columns so dense they can block out the sunlight overhead. The rocky formations feature tunnels, crevices, and sandy patches full of garden eels. The site can experience cooler water and stronger current than inshore sites — conditions that experienced divers rate this as a more demanding dive than its 18-meter maximum depth suggests. The high fish concentration is a direct result of the park's 30+ years of marine protection.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park, Baja California Sur
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; jack tornado most reliable October-May

## Marine Life
Bigeye trevally (jack tornado formations), large groupers including Gulf groupers and Goliath groupers, schooling snapper, stingrays, moray eels, garden eels in sandy patches between formations. The sheer biomass at this site is what makes it exceptional — quantity as much as variety. Sea lions are occasionally observed transiting the area.

## Dive Profile
Maximum depth of 18 meters along the broken reef. Most of the action happens in mid-water as the jack schools form and reform around divers. Stay neutrally buoyant in the water column to enjoy the spectacle without disturbing the reef below. The 45-minute park dive limit applies.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Wide-angle lens essential — this is the site for the classic jack tornado shot
- Stay still and let the school come to you rather than chasing
- Often combined with El Bajo on the same boat trip
- Be prepared for cooler water and potential current at this offshore site
- Check the tunnel and crevice formations for resting groupers
