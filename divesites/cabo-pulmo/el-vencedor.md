# El Vencedor

## Overview
El Vencedor is the most famous dive site in Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park and the park's only wreck. A wooden-hulled Mexican tuna trawler, El Vencedor struck the reef and sank to a sandy bottom at 14 meters. Sources disagree on exact dimensions and date: most operators describe the vessel as approximately 27–30 meters (90–100 feet) in length, while sinking dates cited range from 1978 to the mid-1980s — no single authoritative record has been confirmed. Because the hull was primarily wood, little of the original structure survives; what remains are scattered metal components including the engine, propeller shaft, and machinery spread across the sand. The site now functions as an artificial reef. It is the park's primary site for bull shark encounters, with schools of yellow snapper and graybar grunt permanently colonising the wreckage. Giant moray eels shelter among the machinery, pufferfish and golden groupers patrol the structure, and thousands of garden eels populate the surrounding sand flats.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park, Baja California Sur
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to May for bull shark season; year-round for marine life

## Marine Life
Bull sharks (present much of the year, most reliably November through May), large schools of yellow snapper and graybar grunt, giant moray eels, golden groupers, pufferfish, amberjacks, garden eels on surrounding sand. During the October–November sardine migration, fish activity increases markedly. The scattered wreckage acts as an artificial reef concentrating marine life within the broader park ecosystem.

## Dive Profile
Descent to the wreck on sand at 14 meters. The hull is accessible for external exploration along its full length. Spend time hovering above the sand flats on approach to observe garden eels before they retreat. The 45-minute park dive limit applies. Do not attempt penetration without proper training and equipment.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Bring a torch for peering into the hull where morays shelter
- Approach garden eels slowly — they retract into the sand quickly
- During bull shark season, stay calm and maintain a low profile near the wreck
- Maximum group size of 6 divers per guide (park rule)
