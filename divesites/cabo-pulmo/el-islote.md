# El Islote

## Overview
El Islote ("The Islet") is a rocky island promontory at the southern end of Pulmo Bay, rising from a sandy seabed with coral growth, sea fans, and diverse reef fish colonising its flanks. The rock creates wall-like faces in places, descending to a sandy base at roughly 18 meters where boulder formations create sheltered pockets. Divers circle the formation starting from shallow water (around 1.5 meters) and work their way down to the sandy bottom. At the northern deeper face, rare golden grouper are sometimes found. Sardine schools periodically swarm the site in bait-ball formations. The site's relatively easy conditions and diverse scenery make it one of the best beginner-friendly sites in Cabo Pulmo, though intermediate divers will also find the marine variety rewarding.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Cabo Pulmo National Marine Park, Baja California Sur (southern end)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life
Sea fans, hard corals, multiple eel species (green, zebra, fine-spotted, jewel eels confirmed by local operators), moray eels, African pompano, groupers, snappers, sea turtles, lobsters, nudibranchs, scorpionfish. Sardine schools periodically form bait balls around the rock. Golden grouper occasionally found at the deeper northern face. Regional marine life including bigeye trevally may transit the site; no site-specific jack tornado reports were found for El Islote.

## Dive Profile
Circle the seamount from shallow coral areas down to the sandy base at 18 meters. The boulder formations at the base are worth exploring for morays and sheltering fish. Conditions are generally calm, making this suitable for newer divers. The 45-minute park dive limit applies.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Good first-dive-of-the-trip site to get comfortable before deeper park sites
- Check the boulder formations at the base for moray eels
- Sea fans on the upper slopes make excellent wide-angle subjects with blue water backgrounds
