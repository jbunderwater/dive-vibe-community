# El Natural Wreck

A shore-accessible vessel wreck in the waters near Rincón — a compact, well-encrusted artificial reef site offering beginner divers on Puerto Rico's northwest coast a straightforward introduction to wreck diving at accessible depth.

## Overview

El Natural Wreck is a deliberately sunk vessel lying off the northwest coast of Puerto Rico in the Rincón area. The wreck rests at 24 metres maximum depth with its upper structure at shallower levels, making it accessible to Open Water certified divers for exterior exploration. The vessel has been in place long enough to develop a productive encrustation of sponges, corals, and algae on its hull, and the structure now serves as an aggregation point for a diverse reef fish community. The northwest coast's clear water gives the site typical visibility of 15–25 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C year-round.

## Marine Life

The wreck structure has attracted a typical Caribbean artificial reef community. Schools of French grunts and yellowtail snappers occupy the superstructure. Spotted moray eels reside in the hull crevices. Caribbean reef octopus are found around the exterior hull and debris field. Southern stingrays rest on the sandy bottom adjacent to the wreck. Hawksbill turtles visit the wreck periodically. Large barracuda patrol the open water column above the structure.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry via established access point and surface swim or subsurface navigation to the wreck. Descend to the upper structure at 15–18 metres and work progressively deeper to the maximum at 24 metres. Explore the wreck's exterior circumference before ascending to the upper structure for the safety stop. Total dive time 40–50 minutes within NDL limits.

## Entry and Exit

Shore dive from the Rincón area coast. The specific entry access point varies — local operators and guides will provide current directions as access conditions change seasonally. Fins on in waist-deep water; surface navigation to the descent point.

## Tips and Recommendations

A guide familiar with the site is recommended for first visits — the underwater navigation to the wreck from shore can be disorienting for new visitors. The 24-metre maximum depth approaches recreational limits for some certifications — plan the dive conservatively with a turn-around time to ensure adequate air for the ascent and safety stop. Carry a torch for the darker sections of the hull.

## Safety Considerations

The deeper sections at 24 metres require attention to NDL limits and air consumption. Do not enter enclosed spaces without wreck diving training. DSMB recommended for the ascent. Exit the water carefully at the shore entry point if surge is present.

## Photography

The accessible depth and compact scale of El Natural Wreck make it suitable for wide-angle hull photography and close-focus work on the encrusted surfaces. Natural light at 15–20 metres on the upper structure is adequate for hull shots in morning conditions. Moray portraits and fish community compositions are the main subjects.
