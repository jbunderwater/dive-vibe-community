## Escambrón Reef

Escambrón Reef (officially Parque Marino del Escambrón / La Poza del Escambrón) is a protected marine park and shore dive site on the north coast of Puerto Rico, immediately adjacent to Escambrón Beach in metropolitan San Juan. It is one of the most accessible urban dive sites in the Caribbean, located minutes from Old San Juan and served by Scuba Dogs, a full-service PADI dive shop located on-site at Parque Nacional del Tercer Milenio.

## Overview

The marine park was established to protect and restore nearshore habitat adjacent to one of San Juan's busiest public beaches. The park features natural reef formations, over 200 artificial "Taino Reef" structures, a sunken Fish Protection Wall, swim-throughs and cavern formations in Labyrinth Reef, and an underwater "City of Atlantis" with Roman columns, statues, benches, and a ship-shaped structure. Fish at the site are highly accustomed to divers, making close-up encounters common. The shallow depth and protected water make it ideal for certification training and refresher dives.

## Marine Life

Yellowtail snappers, damselfish, grunts, blue tangs, and parrotfish are abundant throughout the park. Green turtles and hawksbill turtles are regularly sighted, and manatees have been reported in the area. Tarpon congregate around structure in lower-light conditions. Sergeant majors and wrasse are common on the reef.

## Dive Profile

The protected inner bay (La Pocita) offers very shallow, calm water ideal for beginners and snorkellers. Labyrinth Reef on the outer edge of the bay features swim-throughs and natural caverns at slightly greater depth. Maximum depth across the site is approximately 9 metres (30 feet). Visibility is best during settled weather; heavy rain or onshore swell can reduce visibility significantly. Calmer conditions prevail from late spring through early fall.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Escambrón Beach, one of San Juan's primary public beaches. Scuba Dogs provides gear rental, guided dives, and training directly on-site, eliminating the need to transport equipment.

## Safety Considerations

Visibility can drop sharply after rainfall or strong onshore swell. Winter cold fronts can make entries rough on the north coast. Boat traffic in the bay area requires awareness on ascent.
