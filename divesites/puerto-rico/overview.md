## Puerto Rico

Remote Desecheo and Mona Island walls, La Parguera's 22-mile wall dropping 1,500 feet, and diverse Caribbean reef ecosystems across the archipelago.

## Description

Puerto Rico offers 11 documented dive sites ranging from 8 to 40 meters depth, spread across five distinct diving regions from San Juan to the remote offshore islands. The Parguera Wall on the southwest coast stretches 22 miles along a dramatic shelf drop exceeding 450 meters, while Desecheo Island — a National Wildlife Refuge 12 miles off Rincon — harbors some of Puerto Rico's best-preserved coral reefs with visibility reaching 30 meters. Mona Island, 50 miles offshore, rewards the journey with caves, pristine corals, and encounters with sharks and pelagics.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: 6 boat-accessible sites including Desecheo Island, La Parguera Wall, and offshore reef systems
- **Shore Diving**: 5 shore-accessible sites including Blue Beach and Crash Boat Beach, suitable for training and recreational dives
- **Wall Diving**: 3 wall sites anchored by the Parguera Wall — one of the Caribbean's longest continuous wall dives
- **Reef Diving**: 8 reef sites across the island including Culebra, Fajardo's Cordillera Nature Reserve, and Vieques

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Luis Munoz Marin International Airport (SJU) in San Juan is the main gateway with direct flights from the US mainland and Caribbean connections. Regional airports at Aguadilla (BQN) serve the west coast near Rincon and Desecheo. Ferries connect to Vieques and Culebra from Ceiba.
- **Dive Operators**: Sea Ventures Dive Center (Fajardo), Taino Divers (Rincon, specializing in Desecheo trips), West Divers and Parguera Divers (La Parguera), Aqua Adventures and Scuba Dogs (San Juan and Culebra)
- **Accommodation**: Full range from San Juan hotels to guesthouses in Rincon, La Parguera, and the offshore islands of Vieques and Culebra
- **Transportation**: Rental cars essential for reaching dive regions outside San Juan. Ferries to Vieques and Culebra; charter boats for Desecheo and Mona.
- **Facilities**: PADI-certified dive centers across all regions with full equipment rental. Limited facilities on remote islands — operators provide all gear for Desecheo and Mona trips.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 26-29°C year-round, visibility 15-30 meters (up to 30+ meters at Desecheo), trade wind swells affect the north coast while the south coast remains calmer
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Tropical reef fish, nurse sharks, barracudas, stingrays, spotted eagle rays, sea turtles, moray eels, and octopi. Mona Island adds shark encounters and seasonal humpback whale sightings. La Parguera is renowned for bioluminescent bay night experiences.
- **Conservation**: Desecheo Island is a National Wildlife Refuge with restricted access. La Cordillera Nature Reserve protects reefs off Fajardo. Mona Island is managed as a natural reserve with limited visitor permits.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: Year-round diving; December to April offers the driest weather. Summer months bring warmer water and whale shark possibilities. Hurricane season runs June to November.
- **Currency**: US Dollar (USD)
- **Language**: Spanish and English (both official)
- **Safety**: Recompression chambers available in San Juan. Strong currents at Mona Island and Desecheo require advanced experience. La Parguera Wall depths demand proper planning. No US passport required for American citizens.
