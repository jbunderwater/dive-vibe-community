# Parker Reef

A protected rocky reef in the northern San Juan Islands, part of the San Juan Islands National Wildlife Refuge, with exceptional cold-water marine life.

## Overview

Parker Reef is located within the San Juan Islands National Wildlife Refuge and National Monument in the northernmost part of the archipelago — an area with very low recreational boating pressure that contributes to the site's excellent marine life density. The reef structure offers a combination of sloping rock faces and boulder fields typical of the best Pacific Northwest diving, with cold, nutrient-rich water (7–11°C) sustaining dense communities of fish and invertebrates. The protected status of the area means that fish sizes tend to be notably larger than at more heavily fished sites.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern San Juan Islands, Washington (San Juan Islands National Wildlife Refuge)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Rocky reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 7–11°C
- **Current**: Tidal; plan for slack
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

The protected status of Parker Reef contributes to a fish community with notably large individuals. Lingcod here are reported to reach impressive sizes, and copper and quillback rockfish are abundant. Giant Pacific octopus in the boulder fields can be very large — arm spans of 2 metres or more have been recorded in this part of the archipelago. Wolf eels are present, and harbour seals from local rookeries on the refuge islands regularly investigate divers. Invertebrate life is exceptional: plumose anemone walls, Puget Sound king crabs, nudibranchs, encrusting sponges, and rock scallops characterise every surface.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top and work progressively deeper, paying attention to the most interesting boulder sections and vertical faces. The 18-metre maximum keeps the dive within comfortable no-decompression limits for extended bottom time. Return via the shallow reef crest for a safety stop before surfacing with a deployed SMB.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. The northern San Juan Islands are reached by charter from Anacortes or Friday Harbor. Anchor on the sheltered side of the reef. Back-roll or giant stride entry. Deploy an SMB before the final ascent and surface upwind of the vessel.

## Tips

- The protection afforded by the wildlife refuge means larger, bolder fish — approach slowly for best viewing
- Visiting in midsummer (July–August) coincides with salmon runs and the potential for orca sightings in nearby waters
- Respect the wildlife refuge by staying off rocks above the waterline where seabird and seal habitat is protected
- Carry extra warmth — water in the northern Sound can be colder than southern sites

## Safety

The northern San Juan Islands are more exposed to open-water conditions than the protected southern reaches. Check wind and swell forecasts in addition to tidal information. Cold water requires dry suit diving. Always carry and deploy an SMB. Wildlife refuge rules apply; follow all posted guidelines.

## Photography

The large fish at Parker Reef make outstanding portrait subjects. Close-focus wide-angle shots of large lingcod and rockfish near the anemone-covered rock faces are a signature Pacific Northwest image. Macro work on nudibranchs and encrusting organisms is equally productive. A torch and strobe are essential for accurate colour at depth.
