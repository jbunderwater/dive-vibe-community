## Wreck of a PB4Y bomber

The PB4Y-2 Privateer wreck is a legendary technical dive in Lake Washington — a four-engine patrol bomber that crashed during takeoff in 1956 and remains one of the most distinctive wreck dives in the Pacific Northwest.

## Overview

A Consolidated PB4Y-2 Privateer (a naval variant of the B-24 Liberator) crashed in Lake Washington on August 26, 1956, after the crew failed to properly configure the flaps for takeoff from NAS Sand Point (now Warren G. Magnuson Park). The 11-man crew evacuated safely via life rafts before the aircraft sank. A Navy salvage operation subsequently raised the plane, but a shackle pin failed during the recovery attempt, and the aircraft sank a second time. The wreck has remained on the lake bottom ever since.

**Depth note (conflict flagged):** Sources disagree on the exact depth. Pacific Northwest Diving's first-person dive account places the wreck line at approximately 90 feet fresh water (ffw), with the approach starting around 30 ffw. Other sources, including the Bureau of Aircraft Accidents Archives, cite depths closer to 150 feet (45 metres), consistent with the OSM node value of 47m. The discrepancy may reflect the difference between the approach line depth and the aircraft's deepest resting point, or fresh-water vs. salt-water gauge calibration. Divers planning this dive should consult local technical divers for current line conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Lake Washington, off Warren G. Magnuson Park (formerly NAS Sand Point), Seattle
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 4–10°C
- **Note**: Lake Washington, not Puget Sound proper — freshwater, different visibility and biota

## Wreck Condition

Key sections visible include the cockpit area, nose turret, starboard outboard engine, tail turret, and tail numbers. GUE Seattle has conducted photogrammetry of the wreck and produced detailed 3D documentation. The aircraft's large footprint requires careful navigation. The fuselage and major assemblies are recognizable but deteriorating.

## Visibility and Conditions

Visibility is "almost always shrouded in terrible visibility" (Pacific Northwest Diving). Silt disturbance from finning makes conditions worse. Navigation relies on following guide lines rather than visual references. Divers use scooters to cover the distance from the descent point to the wreck.

## Dive Profile

This is a technical dive requiring decompression planning, redundant equipment, and appropriate gas blending. First-person accounts describe a "healthy deco obligation and nearly 2-hour runtime." The site is not accessible to recreational sport divers without technical training and certification. Route: descent to approach line, scooter to wreck, circumnavigate clockwise, ascend on decompression schedule.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Lake Washington launches (Magnuson Park area). GPS coordinates: 47° 40.57' N, 122° 14.6' W (Pacific Northwest Diving). The site is not marked with a permanent buoy — guide line access is necessary.
