## Pacific Queen

The MV Pacific Queen is a historic refrigerated fishing vessel that was beached north of Picnic Point near Edmonds. While listed in historical records as a wreck, this vessel is NOT an active dive site due to location on a private beach and minimal submersion.

## Overview

The MV Pacific Queen was a large wooden-hulled refrigerated vessel built during World War II as one of eight Auxiliary Rescue and Salvage (ARS) vessels constructed in 1942-1943. It was engaged in freezing and transporting salmon from Alaska to Puget Sound ports. Rather than being wrecked in traditional maritime fashion, the vessel was intentionally run aground on a private beach north of Picnic Point in the 1950s for salvage operations. The ship's remains are visible from Picnic Point Park but the site is not suitable for diving due to private beach access restrictions.
