## Equator

The Equator is a historic schooner with an extraordinary maritime legacy, immortalized in literature by author Robert Louis Stevenson. Built in San Francisco in 1888, this vessel was chartered by Stevenson in 1889 for voyages to the Gilbert Islands, adventures that became the basis of his travel book "In the South Seas." However, the Equator is NOT an active dive site. It has been preserved as a historical monument and resides in a protected shed on the Everett waterfront.

## Overview

The Equator is a 1888-built pygmy schooner that served as a coconut trading vessel, Arctic whaler, and Puget Sound tug before being salvaged and preserved in 1967. Rather than being a diving wreck, this vessel was deliberately placed on a breakwater at Jetty Island in 1956 and later moved to the Everett waterfront where it now sits in a dry-dock shed. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1972. This site is a historical landmark viewable from shore, not suitable for diving.
