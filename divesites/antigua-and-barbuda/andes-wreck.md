# Andes Wreck

**Note: This entry and the Deep Bay Wreck (Andes) entry both refer to the same vessel at the same location.** The authoritative entry is Deep Bay Wreck (Andes); see that page for full details.

## Overview

The Andes was a three-masted steel barque built in England in 1874. She sailed from Trinidad on 5 June 1905 carrying 1,330 barrels of pitch destined for road paving in Chile. Poorly stowed cargo caused friction, igniting a fire that burned the vessel and sent her to the bottom of Deep Bay on 9 June 1905. The wreck lies in approximately 7.5–9 metres (25–30 feet) of water in Deep Bay, accessible from shore. She is protected under Antigua's Marine Areas Act of 1972. The wooden elements have long since decayed, but iron structural elements remain visible, heavily encrusted with coral and marine growth.

The wreck is also listed as Deep Bay Wreck (Andes) in this database — both entries describe the same site. See the Deep Bay Wreck (Andes) page for a full dive description.
