# Barbuda Reef

The reef systems of Barbuda — Antigua's less-visited northern island — encompassing South Ridge, Palaster Reef (east coast barrier reef), and Palmetto Point, accessed by day trip from Antigua in the near-absence of local dive infrastructure.

## Overview

Barbuda is a low, flat coral island approximately 28 km north of Antigua with a resident population of only a few hundred. It receives far fewer divers than Antigua, which benefits the reef systems. The island's coast is largely surrounded by shallow reef shelving, providing accessible diving. The best-regarded dive area is South Ridge, a long reef extending from the island's southern tip. On the east coast, Palaster Reef is a barrier reef running along the coast of an area known locally as the Highlands, with depths from 10 metres down to shallower sections and significant marine life concentration. Palmetto Point on the southwest coast features colonies of staghorn and brain corals. There are no dedicated dive centres on Barbuda; divers must arrange equipment and guidance through Antigua-based operators, who transport tanks and gear by boat or air.

The database entry here represents the general Barbuda reef diving area centred on the southwest coast — coordinates have low confidence. Specific named sites (South Ridge, Palaster Reef) are better researched but lack verified GPS coordinates.

## Marine Life

Confirmed marine life in the Barbuda reef system includes sea turtles (which nest on Barbuda's beaches and are present in the water), stingrays, eagle rays (reported at Palmetto Point), and large schools of reef fish. The Antigua and Barbuda government notes the Barbuda reefs contain an enormous number of wrecks, most yet to be explored. Previous descriptions of this site claimed "Caribbean reef sharks and nurse sharks as consistent sightings" — this was a regional generalisation not confirmed to a specific dive spot in available sources, and has been removed. The low-traffic environment does support generally healthier fish populations than comparable Antigua sites.

## Dive Profile

Diving in Barbuda depends heavily on which operator is leading and which site area is targeted. South Ridge dives typically involve exploring the long reef at 8–15 metres depth. Palaster Reef is a longer barrier reef dive with entry points along the coast. Boat dive from the Antigua–Barbuda crossing vessel. Total dive: 45–60 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

All Barbuda diving is by boat, requiring either a day trip from Antigua (approximately 45 minutes crossing each way, weather-dependent) or a stay at one of Barbuda's limited lodges. No commercial dive centre operates on the island; Jolly Dive (Antigua) has offered Barbuda day trips by prior arrangement. The channel crossing can be rough in northeasterly conditions — verify weather before committing.

## Tips and Recommendations

Barbuda diving is a full-day commitment from Antigua and requires advance arrangement. The investment yields access to reef systems that see very few divers. Post-Hurricane Irma (2017) recovery has been ongoing on the island itself; check with operators on current reef condition and accessibility before booking. The ruins of wrecked vessels visible in various states around the coastline are an additional historical interest.

## Safety Considerations

The open-water channel crossing from Antigua to Barbuda can be rough. Remote location means emergency support is significantly further away than at Antigua sites. Dive conservatively. DSMB required.
