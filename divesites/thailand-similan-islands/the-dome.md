## The Dome

The Dome is a submerged dome-shaped rock formation in the northern Similan Islands area, Phang Nga Province, Thailand, located in close proximity to Twin Peaks and often dived in combination with that site on liveaboard northern routes.

## Overview

The formation takes its name from its smooth, rounded profile — a single dome-shaped granite structure that rises from approximately 30 meters to the near-surface. Unlike the jagged, complex topography of the boulder sites, The Dome is a relatively clean, uncomplicated structure that allows divers to circumnavigate the entire feature in a single dive. Its surfaces are carpeted with soft corals and encrusting growth, and it sits in open water where current exposure supports a healthy ecosystem around the structure. The site tends to be calmer than Twin Peaks and is often recommended as a first dive for divers adjusting to the northern current-exposed sites.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern Similan Islands area, Phang Nga Province, Thailand
- **Entry Type**: liveaboard
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–35 meters
- **Current**: Moderate; generally more manageable than nearby pinnacle sites
- **Best Time**: November to May

## Marine Life

Soft coral coverage on the dome flanks is among the denser expressions of Dendronephthya and Chironephthya in the area, feeding on the current passing over the rounded surface. Schooling fish — fusiliers, surgeonfish, and trevally — orbit the dome in loose formations. Whitetip reef sharks rest on sand at the base. Sea turtles are regular visitors. The current-facing side of the dome hosts sea fans at depth with associated crinoids. Moray eels inhabit the deeper crevices where the granite is more fractured. Reef fish diversity is high throughout the shallower portions of the dome (8–15 m).

## Dive Profile

A full circumnavigation of the dome is achievable in a single dive. Begin the descent on the current-upstream side, allow the current to carry you around one flank at mid-depth (15–22 m), work back to the dome summit (6–10 m), and circuit to the other side before ascending along the mooring or on a free ascent/SMB. The dome top in the shallows provides excellent decompression stop time in good light with active reef fish.

## Entry and Exit

Exclusively liveaboard. Zodiac entry with descent on a temporary mooring or freefall if current is gentle. Open-water ascent on SMB if current is running at the surface. The site is typically dived as part of a northern Similan or Surin itinerary.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The dome's uncomplicated shape makes it an excellent site for practising the circumnavigation technique before attempting the more demanding Twin Peaks
- Soft coral on the current-facing flanks is best photographed in the first morning light before boat traffic stirs sediment
- Check the base of the dome structure where it meets the sand for resting whitetip reef sharks and, occasionally, leopard sharks
- Carry a reef hook but use it conservatively — attach only to bare rubble, never to encrusted surfaces or live coral
- This site is a useful first dive of the day to let divers calibrate buoyancy with fresh cylinders before progressing to more challenging sites

## Safety Considerations

The Dome is more forgiving than the adjacent pinnacle sites but still demands Advanced-level open-water competence. Current can pick up rapidly and an inattentive diver can be swept off the formation. Always carry and know how to deploy an SMB in current. Briefings will cover the zodiac pick-up protocol for separated divers — attend them carefully.

## Photography

The dome's soft coral coverage is a primary photographic draw. Wide-angle images looking along a current-swept flank with a diver and the soft coral banks in the same frame represent the classic composition. Shoot with strobe positioned at 30–45 degrees to illuminate the soft coral without washing out the blue ambient background. The dome top in good morning light is productive for ambient-light wide-angle reef photography without flash.
