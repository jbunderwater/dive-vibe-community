## USS Indra (Wreck)

The USS Indra (ARL-33) is a 338-foot landing craft repair ship deliberately sunk in 1992 as part of North Carolina's artificial reef program, only 10 miles from Beaufort Inlet. Its combination of shallow depth — 60 feet (18 meters) at maximum — light current, and large, navigable interior spaces makes the Indra one of North Carolina's best beginner and training wreck dives, and a favorite site for Open Water and Advanced Open Water certification dives.

## Overview

The Indra was a Achelous-class landing craft repair ship used by the U.S. Navy in World War II and Korea. After decommissioning, she was cleaned, prepared, and scuttled to create an artificial reef just offshore of the Crystal Coast. Unlike the deep offshore wrecks of the Outer Banks, the Indra offers diving accessible without venturing far into the Gulf Stream. The relatively protected location inside Beaufort Inlet means calmer seas and predictable conditions. The wreck is largely intact, upright, and stable, with large openings that allow safe swim-throughs even for newer divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: 10 miles from Beaufort Inlet, Crystal Coast, North Carolina
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Top of Wreck**: ~12 meters (40 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 6–15 meters (20–50 feet)
- **Current**: Light to none — sheltered location compared to offshore wrecks
- **Water Temperature**: 22–28°C (72–82°F) in summer; cooler fall and winter
- **Best Season**: Year-round — short boat ride makes weather windows more accessible

## Marine Life

The Indra hosts a classic artificial reef community. Octopus are reliably found tucked into crevices and machinery throughout the wreck. Moray eels patrol the interior passageways. In late summer, tropical species including yellow tang, damselfish, and butterflyfish appear with warmer Gulf Stream water. Grouper, sea bass, and spadefish are year-round residents. The relatively shallow depth and abundant light support colorful encrusting sponges and algae growth on all exposed surfaces.

## Dive Profile

The wreck sits upright on a sandy bottom. Beginning divers typically explore the exterior deck, smokestack, and superstructure while maintaining contact with the hull. The large cargo holds and main deck openings provide beginner-accessible swim-throughs with good ambient light. More experienced divers can explore the interior spaces and machinery spaces. Maximum depth is 18 meters — a comfortable recreational dive even for newly certified divers.

## Entry and Exit

Charter boats run from Beaufort and Morehead City, with a short 10-mile transit. Discovery Diving Company is the primary local operator and runs regular trips to the Indra. The site has mooring buoys. A giant stride or back roll entry from the dive boat is standard. Deploy an SMB for the safety stop and boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- An excellent site for a first wreck dive or to practice skills in a forgiving environment
- Large interior openings allow swim-throughs without specialized wreck training
- Octopus are reliably found — look carefully in recessed areas under machinery and debris
- Visibility can vary; check with operators for current conditions before booking
- The Indra is often paired with a second shallow site — ask your operator about multi-site trips
- A 3mm wetsuit is sufficient in summer; a 5mm is more comfortable in spring and fall

## Safety Considerations

The Indra's shallow depth and calm conditions make it one of North Carolina's most forgiving dive sites. Standard buddy diving protocols apply. Current is minimal compared to offshore sites. The dive boat is only 10 miles from shore, shortening any emergency response compared to outer wrecks.

## Photography

The large interior spaces with ambient light make the Indra an accessible wide-angle subject for newer underwater photographers. Octopus encounters make excellent macro subjects. The bow and stern sections photographed from the exterior show the wreck's impressive 338-foot length in context.
