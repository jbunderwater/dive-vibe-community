## U-352 (Wrack)

U-352 (Wrack) is a historic wreck dive in North Carolina, North America.

## Overview

U-352 is one of the most celebrated dive sites on the US East Coast — a 220-foot (67.1 m) German Type VIIC submarine sunk by the US Coast Guard Cutter Icarus on 9 May 1942 and sitting with a 45-degree starboard list in approximately 115 ft (35 m) of water about 26 miles off Morehead City, the conning tower visible at roughly 90 ft. The outer hull has deteriorated but the sub remains structurally intact, with the cracked bow exposing forward torpedo tubes and the deck gun still visible; the interior is a protected war grave and penetration is prohibited by law. Sand tiger sharks are regularly reported cruising along the hull. The wreck was discovered in 1975 by George Purifoy, founder of Olympus Dive Center, which still runs charters from Morehead City today.

## Marine Life

Sand tiger sharks are the signature attraction — individuals regularly patrol the hull, unfazed by divers. Olympus Dive Center trip reports and Discovery Diving's site notes describe dense schools of Atlantic spadefish and amberjack over the conning tower, with black sea bass on the lower hull sections. No site-specific scientific marine life survey was found; species accounts are sourced from local dive operator reports and diver accounts.

## Wreck History

U-352 was a German Type VIIC submarine, 67.10 m (220 ft 2 in) in length, commissioned 28 August 1941 under Kapitänleutnant Hellmut Rathke. On 9 May 1942 she was sunk by depth charges from the US Coast Guard Cutter Icarus approximately 26 miles south of Morehead City. Of 48 crew, 33 survived and were taken prisoner; 15 perished. The wreck was discovered in 1975 by George Purifoy, founder of Olympus Dive Center in Morehead City, using Loran-C navigation. She lies in approximately 115 ft (35 m) of water with a 45-degree starboard list and was listed on the National Register of Historic Places on 12 November 2015. Penetration of the interior is prohibited — the wreck is a war grave.

## Dive Profile

Bottom depth is 110 ft (33.5 m); the conning tower rises to approximately 90 ft. The wreck is 218 ft long and sits nearly intact with a starboard list. Divers typically descend the mooring line to the conning tower area, then work fore and aft along the hull. The bow section exposes the pressure hull and forward torpedo tubes. Bottom time at this depth limits dives to approximately 20–30 minutes on air; nitrox extends bottom time. The site is visited on full-day charter trips by Olympus Dive Center and Discovery Diving, both operating from Morehead City/Beaufort, approximately 26–28 nautical miles offshore.

## Entry and Exit

All access is by charter boat from Morehead City or Beaufort. Olympus Dive Center (Morehead City) operates full-day trips visiting U-352 two to three times per week during the dive season (April–October). Discovery Diving (Beaufort, 414 Orange St) also runs regular charters. The mooring line is attached near the conning tower. No shore access is possible given the 26-mile offshore position.

## Tips and Recommendations

Olympus Dive Center requires Advanced and Nitrox certifications plus experience diving from a boat to depths greater than 70 ft. Book multiple dive days when planning a visit — weather cancellations are common given the 26-mile offshore distance. Peak shark activity runs June through September. Visit early in the season for clearer water before summer thermoclines develop.

## Safety Considerations

Depth of 110 ft puts this at the recreational limit. Nitrox is strongly recommended. Strong currents can develop off the North Carolina coast — always confirm conditions with the charter operator before booking. Water temperature ranges from approximately 60°F (15°C) in winter to over 80°F (27°C) in summer at the surface; thermoclines can drop temperatures significantly at depth. This is a designated war grave — wreck penetration is prohibited by law.
