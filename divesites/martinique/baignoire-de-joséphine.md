## Baignoire de Joséphine

Baignoire de Joséphine — Josephine's Bathtub — is one of Martinique's most beloved and quirky snorkel and swim sites, a natural sandbar in the Atlantic where the sea is so shallow (just 1–2 meters) that you can literally stand in the middle of the ocean, far from shore. Named in romanticised association with Napoleon's Martinique-born empress Joséphine de Beauharnais, the site is a beloved day-trip destination for both visitors and locals.

## Overview

Located off the northeast coast of Martinique near La Trinité, Baignoire de Joséphine is a submerged sandbar in the Atlantic Ocean where water depth is typically only 1–2 meters at its shallowest. The sandbar creates a natural wading and snorkel area with remarkable biodiversity for such a thin water column — the sandy substrate and seagrass patches support juvenile fish, sea turtles, and a range of invertebrates. The site is accessible only by boat and reached during calm conditions. The clear turquoise water and the surreal experience of standing in the open ocean are the primary appeal.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off La Trinité, northeast Martinique (Atlantic side)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 2 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Variable; Atlantic swells can affect the site
- **Best Time**: December to April (calmer Atlantic conditions)

## Marine Life

Despite the extreme shallowness, Baignoire de Joséphine supports interesting marine life. Sea turtles are frequently seen grazing on seagrass in the surrounding area. Juvenile reef fish use the seagrass beds as nursery habitat, and starfish and sea cucumbers are scattered across the sand. The site is primarily a snorkel and wade experience rather than a full scuba dive, but the biodiversity rewards careful observation.

## Dive Profile

This is not a conventional scuba dive site — the depth of 2 meters makes it unsuitable for standard air consumption patterns. It is most appropriately experienced as a snorkel, wade, or confined-water-style scuba practice. Divers visiting Martinique often include a stop here during a multi-site boat tour of the northeast coast.

## Entry and Exit

Access exclusively by boat from La Trinité or tour operators running Atlantic coast excursions. Entry via ladder or water slide depending on the vessel. Conditions must be calm — the Atlantic side is more exposed than the Caribbean.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The "standing in the ocean" experience is genuinely special and photogenic — bring a waterproof camera
- Visit on a calm day in the dry season (December to April) when Atlantic conditions cooperate
- Combine with a snorkel at the adjacent Presqu'île de la Caravelle for a varied half-day
- The site is very popular with day-trippers — arrive early for a quieter experience

## Safety Considerations

Atlantic swells can raise suddenly and make wading uncomfortable or dangerous. Follow the operator's instructions about when to return to the boat. The site is shallow enough that boat propellers can pose a hazard — stay clear of boats in the area. Sunburn is a real risk when wading in exposed open water.

## Photography

Baignoire de Joséphine offers one-of-a-kind photography: humans standing in the open ocean with the horizon stretching all around. Underwater, the crystal-clear 2-meter column of water lit from above creates luminous images of the sandy and seagrass floor. Split-level above/below shots are particularly effective here.
