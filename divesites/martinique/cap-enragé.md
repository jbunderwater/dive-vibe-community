## Cap Enragé

Cap Enragé — the Enraged Cape — lives up to its name as one of Martinique's more challenging reef sites, located on the exposed south coast where stronger currents and more open-ocean conditions create a dynamic dive environment suited to intermediate divers comfortable with current.

## Overview

Positioned on a headland on Martinique's southwestern coast, Cap Enragé features a reef that extends from shallow rocky slopes down to a sandy bottom at 22 meters. The topography is characterised by rocky outcrops, coral formations, and short wall sections created by the cape's geological structure. Exposure to prevailing currents brings richer water and better marine life diversity than the calmer inner-reef sites. Visibility averages 20–30 meters. Water temperature is the usual 26–28°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southwest coast, near Le Diamant, Martinique
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Moderate to strong; site is current-dependent
- **Best Time**: Slack tide periods, November to May

## Marine Life

The current-influenced environment supports a richer mix of species than the calmer southern banks. Caribbean reef sharks patrol the cape's deeper sections, and hammerhead sharks are occasional visitors at depth. Eagle rays and large barracuda are common. The rocky reef structure supports spotted morays, large spiny lobsters, and diverse sponge communities. Schools of creole wrasse sweep along the cape face during favourable current.

## Dive Profile

Dive planning at Cap Enragé must account for current direction. On a flood tide, drift along the cape face heading in the prevailing direction; on slack, explore the deeper rocky base. Maximum depth is 22 meters. The most biologically rich zone is the cape face at 10–18 meters where current and reef structure create a productive interface. Allow adequate air for a full safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only; operators time the dive to coincide with manageable current. Live-boat pickup is typical. Giant stride entry; SMB deployment before ascending is essential.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Only dive with a local operator who knows current timing at this site
- The name "Enragé" warns you — check conditions carefully before committing to the site
- Current diving experience is a genuine prerequisite for this site
- The combination of rocky topography and current makes this Martinique's most exciting reef dive for intermediate divers ready for a challenge

## Safety Considerations

Current is the primary hazard. Never attempt to dive against a strong current at this site — it is a drift dive. All divers must carry SMBs and know how to deploy them in moving water. Live-boat pickup is the standard approach; stay with the group. Dive within your training and experience.

## Photography

The open-water character and current conditions suit wide-angle photography of free-swimming species. Reef shark encounters on the cape face, barracuda schools, and eagle ray passes are the highlight shots. A fast shutter speed is needed in current conditions to capture sharp images of moving subjects.
