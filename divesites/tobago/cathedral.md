## Cathedral

Cathedral (also referenced locally as Flying Manta) is a reef dive in Tobago's Speyside area — dramatic rocky overhangs and ledges give the site its name, and the site is within the northeast corridor known for manta ray encounters.

## Overview

Located in the Speyside area on Tobago's northeast coast, Cathedral takes its name from the dramatic rocky overhangs and ledges that create a cathedral-like underwater atmosphere. The site is part of the larger Speyside dive zone that benefits from the Guiana Current and upwelling from the South American continental shelf. Depths extend to approximately 37 metres on the deeper portions of the site. The site is also sometimes referred to as Flying Manta in local usage, reflecting the northeast coast's association with manta ray sightings. Visibility in the Speyside area is typically Tobago's best, reaching 25–35 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C, with cooler upwelling possible.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Speyside, northeast Tobago
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 37 meters

## Marine Life

Cathedral sits within the northeast Tobago manta ray zone. Manta ray encounters are associated with this area, with the best season generally reported as March to July (April and May being mating season). Angelfish, butterflyfish, parrotfish, hawksbill turtles, and barracuda are documented reef residents of the Speyside zone. Note: specific species claims ("nurse sharks," "larger snappers to ledges") and specific depth attributions for manta encounters at this site require individual source confirmation — general northeast coast marine life applies.
