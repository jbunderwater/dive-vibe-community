## Arnos Vale

Arnos Vale is a beautiful shore-entry reef dive on Tobago's leeward (Caribbean) coast, situated in front of the historic Arnos Vale Hotel beach. The site offers easy access and a relaxed reef experience that contrasts with the current-driven drift diving on Tobago's windward northeast coast.

## Overview

The beach at Arnos Vale is one of Tobago's most protected stretches of Caribbean coast, backed by lush forest and framed by rocky headlands. The reef starts directly off the beach and extends to 12 meters, featuring mixed coral growth on a rocky and sandy substrate. Tobago's combination of Caribbean and Atlantic influence creates unusually productive water even on the sheltered coast, and the marine life diversity at Arnos Vale exceeds what the modest depth and calm conditions might suggest. Visibility averages 15–25 meters. Water temperature holds at 26–28°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Arnos Vale, Caribbean coast, western Tobago
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Very mild; sheltered Caribbean coast
- **Best Time**: Year-round (Caribbean coast is dived year-round)

## Marine Life

Arnos Vale is confirmed as a nursery area with juvenile reef fish seeking refuge in the rocky shelf. The bay is a protected area where juvenile and small reef species are well-represented. Squid and moray eels are active at night. Note: "Spotted eagle rays passing regularly" and "occasional manta ray" were previous attributions not confirmed by primary sources for this specific site — the maritime ocean collection source describes this as a "bay nursery, rocky shelf" without pelagic claims.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the beach and follow the slope to the reef. The profile is gentle and predictable. Most marine life is concentrated in the 5–12 meter zone. Extended bottom times are possible at this depth — Arnos Vale rewards slow, contemplative diving rather than coverage of distance.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry over sand and small rocks near the Arnos Vale beach. Navigate to the reef before descending. Exit at the same beach. The site is accessible year-round and suitable for solo diving by experienced divers, though a buddy is always advisable.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Best dived early morning when light conditions are optimal and the beach is empty
- Stay near the rocky reef margins for the best macro life encounters
- Eagle ray sightings are most common in the first hour after dawn
- Arnos Vale pairs well with the Maverick wreck or Mt. Irvine Wall for a varied two-dive day on the Caribbean coast

## Safety Considerations

Standard shore dive precautions. Watch for boat traffic from the nearby hotel jetty. Sea urchins are present in rocky areas near shore. The site is calm enough to dive independently, but always inform someone of your dive plan.

## Photography

The combination of calm, clear water and relaxed marine life makes Arnos Vale one of Tobago's best shore photography sites. Eagle ray portraits in the early morning, turtle encounters over seagrass, and the lush green Caribbean coastal backdrop above water all make for distinctive images.
