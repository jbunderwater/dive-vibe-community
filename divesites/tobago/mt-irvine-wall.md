## Mt Irvine Wall

Mt Irvine Wall is one of Tobago's most popular Caribbean coast dive sites — a shallow wall accessible from shore at Mt Irvine Beach, descending from 25 feet to about 60 feet (18 metres) through crevices and ledges where moray eels, spiny lobsters, crabs, scorpionfish, and flying gurnards congregate. Two cannons from a Dutch East India Company vessel that ran aground in the 17th century rest on the wall.

## Overview

Mt Irvine Wall lies off Mt Irvine Beach on Tobago's Caribbean coast. The wall runs from about 25 feet (7.5 metres) to 60 feet (18 metres), with interesting crevices through the rocky structure. Multiple sources describe this as one of the most popular Caribbean coast sites in Tobago, accessible from shore and suitable for all experience levels. A pair of historical cannons associated with a Dutch East India Company vessel that ran aground in the 17th century adds historical interest. Scorpionfish, flying gurnards, and seahorses are documented species in the crevice habitat.

## Marine Life

Moray eels, spiny lobsters, crabs, scorpionfish, and flying gurnards occupy the crevices and ledges. Two historical cannons on the wall are a distinctive feature. The rocky wall structure supports a diverse invertebrate community.
