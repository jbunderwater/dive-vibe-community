## Tobago

World-class drift diving at Speyside with the largest brain coral in the western hemisphere, nutrient-rich currents from the Orinoco and Amazon rivers, and over 700 documented reef fish species.

## Description

Tobago, the smaller sibling of Trinidad and Tobago, punches far above its weight as a dive destination. Dive sites range from 12 to 40 meters depth, spanning reefs, caves, walls, pinnacles, and wrecks. What makes Tobago exceptional is its position at the confluence of Caribbean and Atlantic waters — nutrient-rich currents from the Orinoco and Amazon rivers feed the reefs, creating marine biodiversity unmatched elsewhere in the Caribbean. Speyside on the northeast coast is the primary dive hub, with 30-40 dive sites within 10 minutes of the jetty, including Kelleston Drain which hosts the largest brain coral in the western hemisphere at over 5 meters wide.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Reef Diving**: Reef sites benefiting from nutrient-rich waters that produce dense coral growth and abundant fish life. Japanese Gardens off Speyside is one of the most popular, with hard and soft coral gardens descending along sloping terrain.
- **Boat Diving**: Boat-accessible sites, mostly concentrated around Speyside and Little Tobago island in the northeast.
- **Shore Diving**: Shore-accessible sites on the island's sheltered Caribbean coast.
- **Drift Diving**: Speyside is renowned for drift diving, with currents carrying divers along reef walls past schooling fish and pelagics.
- **Wreck Diving**: Wreck site — the Maverick Wreck.
- **Cave Diving**: Cave site with swim-through formations in the volcanic rock.
- **Wall Diving**: Wall dive along a steep volcanic drop-off.

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: ANR Robinson International Airport (TAB), formerly Crown Point Airport, receives direct flights from London Gatwick (seasonal), Toronto, and inter-island connections from Trinidad (25-minute flight from Piarco International Airport). Trinidad serves as the main regional hub with connections to North America, Europe, and other Caribbean islands. Caribbean Airlines operates the Trinidad-Tobago route frequently.
- **Dive Operators**: Tobago Dive Experience (Speyside, founded by Sean Robinson, PADI and NAUI certified), Blue Waters Dive'n (PADI 5-Star Gold Palm at Blue Waters Inn, Speyside), and ERIC Tobago. Most serious dive operators are based in Speyside on the windward coast.
- **Accommodation**: Speyside offers small hotels and guesthouses catering to divers, including Blue Waters Inn. Crown Point and Scarborough in the southwest have a wider range of tourist accommodation but are further from the northeast dive sites.
- **Transportation**: Rental cars or taxis. The drive from Crown Point to Speyside takes approximately 1.5-2 hours along winding coastal roads. Many dive operators offer accommodation-to-dive transfers.
- **Facilities**: The island has basic but adequate tourism infrastructure. Medical facilities in Scarborough.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 24-27°C (75-80°F), cooler January through March (24°C) and warmer June through October (27°C). A 3mm to 5mm wetsuit is recommended. Visibility ranges from 12-30 meters (40-100 ft), with Speyside generally offering the best clarity. Currents are common, particularly at Speyside drift sites.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Giant manta rays (historically resident at Speyside, most reliably seen March-July with April-May being mating season, though sightings have become less frequent in recent years), whale sharks (occasional migratory visitors), nurse sharks, stingrays, barracuda, tarpon, hawksbill turtles, parrotfish, angelfish, and over 700 species of reef fish documented in Tobago's waters. The giant brain coral at Kelleston Drain measures approximately 5.3 meters wide by 3 meters high.
- **Conservation**: The Main Ridge Forest Reserve (the oldest legally protected forest reserve in the western hemisphere) extends its watershed protection to offshore reefs. No spearfishing is permitted in Tobago. The Buccoo Reef Marine Park protects the southwest coast reefs.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: Year-round diving. January through May offers the driest weather. Manta ray season peaks March through July. Visibility is generally best outside the rainy season (June-December). Hurricane risk is low — Tobago sits at the southern edge of the hurricane belt.
- **Currency**: Trinidad and Tobago Dollar (TTD); US dollars accepted at most dive operations.
- **Language**: English (official); Tobagonian Creole English widely spoken.
- **Safety**: The Tobago Hyperbaric Facility in Roxborough operates 24 hours a day and is reachable from anywhere on the island in approximately 30 minutes. DAN insurance recommended. Drift dive sites at Speyside require current awareness — always follow your divemaster's briefing.
