## Buccoo Reef

Buccoo Reef is the largest coral reef complex in Tobago and a designated RAMSAR wetland site. This protected marine park features accessible shallow diving and snorkeling with vibrant coral formations.

## Overview

Buccoo Reef is one of the Caribbean's most accessible coral reef systems, spanning 7 square kilometers with five distinct reef flats. The reef was designated as a protected marine park in 1973 and recognized as a RAMSAR site in 2005. The shallow reef flats reach depths of up to 25 meters, while the fore-reef slopes to 15-30 meters. This beginner-friendly site is perfect for divers of varying experience levels, with healthy hard coral growth that distinguishes this reef system. The reef is also accessible to snorkelers and glass-bottom boats.

## Marine Life

Buccoo Reef supports diverse marine ecosystems with abundant fish populations including snappers, groupers, parrotfish, angelfish, butterfly fish, and damselfish. The reef also hosts green and hawksbill sea turtles, southern stingrays, nurse sharks, and eagle rays. The healthy hard coral formations create diverse habitats.

## Dive Profile

Begin your dive on the shallow reef flats and gradually work deeper as conditions allow. Most marine life is concentrated in the shallower reef areas (5-15 meters). The site is ideal for photographers and macro enthusiasts. Plan your dive within your certification limits and allow adequate air for a safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of boat traffic in this popular reef area. The protected marine park status means fishing and damaging coral is prohibited. Dive within your certification limits and always dive with a buddy. Respect the conservation regulations.

## Conservation

Buccoo Reef is a protected marine park area. Anchoring is restricted, fishing is prohibited, and divers are expected to follow all conservation guidelines to help preserve this important reef system.
