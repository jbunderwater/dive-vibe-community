# Gorgonia Valley

A sheer wall at Gadji Pass on the north of Isle of Pines, dropping from 10 to 35+ meters, covered in a virtual forest of gorgonian sea fans and colourful soft corals. Features a heart-shaped swim-through, pygmy seahorses, leopard sharks, and a resident blacktip reef shark documented at the site for over eight years.

## Overview

Gorgonia Valley (Vallee des Gorgones in French) is one of the signature dives of Kunié Scuba Center's almost 20 sites in the Bay of Gadji at the northern end of the Isle of Pines (Ile des Pins). The site sits at Gadji Pass, where the reef forms a sheer wall dropping from approximately 10 meters at the reef top to more than 35 meters. Numerous faults and a heart-shaped swim-through cut through the reef structure.

The wall is covered in a dense forest of gorgonian sea fans in vivid colours, complemented by soft corals that give the site extraordinary visual richness. The Isle of Pines' relatively remote location and the Gadji Bay reserve status (access only with Kunié Scuba Center) have kept the reef in excellent, intact condition. A blacktip reef shark has reportedly inhabited the site for approximately eight years, exhibiting territorial behavior during dives -- one of the few verifiably documented resident individuals in New Caledonian waters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gadji Pass, northern Isle of Pines (Ile des Pins), New Caledonia. Approximately 15-minute boat ride from the resort area.
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-27 degrees C
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: Year-round; September to December for optimal conditions

## Marine Life

A resident blacktip reef shark has been documented at the site for over eight years. Leopard sharks (Stegostoma tigrinum) are documented at the Bay of Gadji sites. Pygmy seahorses are found among the gorgonian fans -- one of the highlights for macro photographers. Abundant reef fish populate the wall and swim-throughs. Clouds of anthias school around the gorgonian fans.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top at approximately 10 meters. Follow the sheer wall down, exploring the heart-shaped swim-through and the numerous faults in the reef structure. The wall drops to 35+ meters -- monitor depth carefully. The most dense gorgonian growth and best marine life concentration is typically between 12-25 meters. Keep an eye in the blue water off the wall for the resident blacktip and any passing pelagics.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat with Kunié Scuba Center from the Isle of Pines (approximately 15 minutes). The Bay of Gadji is a protected area -- diving here requires a guide from Kunié Scuba Center. Entry by giant stride.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The heart-shaped swim-through is the centrepiece of the dive -- carry a torch to illuminate the coral inside
- Macro photographers should scan the gorgonian fans carefully for pygmy seahorses
- The resident blacktip reef shark is territorial but not aggressive -- maintain a respectful distance
- This site pairs naturally with Jardin d'Eden (Garden of Eden), the adjacent site in the Gadji Bay network

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops to significant depths -- monitor depth carefully. The swim-through is an overhead environment; maintain awareness of exit points. Variable currents are possible in the pass. Dive with Kunié Scuba Center guides who know the resident shark's behavior patterns.
