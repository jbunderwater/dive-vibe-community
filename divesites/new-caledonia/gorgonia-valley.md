# Gorgonia Valley

A sheer wall on Gadji Reef off the northwest corner of Isle of Pines, dropping from 10 to 35+ meters. Swim-throughs in the reef structure, healthy coral growth, abundant fish life, and a resident blacktip reef shark.

## Overview

Gorgonia Valley is located on Gadji Reef off the northwest corner of the Isle of Pines (Ile des Pins), the stunning island at the southern end of New Caledonia's lagoon. The site features a sheer wall dropping from approximately 10 meters at the reef top to 35 meters and beyond, with swim-throughs cutting through the reef structure at various depths.

The wall is decorated with healthy coral growth including gorgonian sea fans that give the site its name. Fish life is abundant along the wall and in the swim-throughs, and a resident blacktip reef shark patrols the site. The Isle of Pines' remote location and limited diving traffic mean the reef is in excellent condition. Visibility typically ranges from 20-40 meters in the clear lagoon waters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gadji Reef, NW corner of Isle of Pines, New Caledonia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-27 degrees C
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: September to December

## Marine Life

A resident blacktip reef shark is regularly encountered at this site. The wall supports abundant fish life -- reef species shelter in the crevices and swim-throughs while larger pelagics cruise the blue water beyond. The gorgonian sea fans and coral growth on the wall provide habitat for a diverse invertebrate community.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top at approximately 10 meters. Follow the wall down, exploring the swim-throughs as they present themselves. The wall drops to 35+ meters, so monitor depth carefully. The best coral growth and fish concentration is typically between 12-25 meters. Look for the resident blacktip reef shark in the blue water off the wall. Ascend along the wall face to the reef top for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Isle of Pines operators. The Gadji Reef location is on the northwest corner of the island. Entry by giant stride.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The swim-throughs are the highlight of this site -- carry a torch to illuminate the coral inside
- Watch for the resident blacktip reef shark in the blue water off the wall
- The Isle of Pines' remote location means excellent reef condition -- one of New Caledonia's best dive areas

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops to significant depths -- monitor depth carefully. Swim-throughs are overhead environments; maintain awareness of exit points. Variable currents are possible.
