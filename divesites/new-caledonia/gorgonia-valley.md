# Gorgonia Valley

A sheer wall on Gadji Reef off the northwest corner of Isle of Pines, dropping from 10 to 35+ meters, with a v-shaped canyon and heart-shaped swim-through. A virtual forest of gorgonian fans and soft corals lines the canyon walls, with a resident blacktip reef shark, leopard sharks, and eagle rays.

## Overview

Gorgonia Valley is located on Gadji Reef off the northwest corner of the Isle of Pines (Ile des Pins), at the southern end of New Caledonia's lagoon. The site features a sheer wall dropping from approximately 10 meters at the reef top to 35 meters and beyond, with a distinctive v-shaped canyon running through the reef structure and a heart-shaped swim-through as its centrepiece.

The canyon and wall are lined with a virtual forest of gorgonian sea fans — giving the site its name — plus colourful soft corals. A territorial blacktip reef shark patrols the site regularly; leopard sharks and eagle rays are also reported weaving between the fans. The Isle of Pines' limited diving traffic means the reef is in excellent condition. Visibility typically ranges from 20-40 meters in the clear lagoon waters. The Kunie Scuba Centre, the island's dive operator, runs guided dives at this site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gadji Reef, NW corner of Isle of Pines, New Caledonia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-27 degrees C
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: September to December

## Marine Life

A territorial blacktip reef shark is regularly encountered at this site. Multiple sources report leopard sharks and eagle rays weaving through the gorgonian fans. The v-shaped canyon and heart-shaped swim-through concentrate reef fish. The gorgonian sea fans and soft coral growth support a diverse invertebrate community. No site-specific species lists with verified sightings were found beyond these accounts from operator sites.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top at approximately 10 meters. Follow the wall down, exploring the swim-throughs as they present themselves. The wall drops to 35+ meters, so monitor depth carefully. The best coral growth and fish concentration is typically between 12-25 meters. Look for the resident blacktip reef shark in the blue water off the wall. Ascend along the wall face to the reef top for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Isle of Pines operators. The Gadji Reef location is on the northwest corner of the island. Entry by giant stride.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The swim-throughs are the highlight of this site -- carry a torch to illuminate the coral inside
- Watch for the resident blacktip reef shark in the blue water off the wall
- The Isle of Pines' remote location means excellent reef condition -- one of New Caledonia's best dive areas

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops to significant depths -- monitor depth carefully. Swim-throughs are overhead environments; maintain awareness of exit points. Variable currents are possible.
