# False Pass

A drift dive through a break in the barrier reef near Poum, 410 km north of Noumea. Pristine reef with a 25-meter tunnel exiting at 32 meters, a forest of very large colourful gorgonian sea fans, and encounters with turtles, tuna, trevally, and grouper.

## Overview

False Pass is located near Poum at the far northern tip of Grande Terre, approximately 410 kilometres north of Noumea. This remote location -- well off the tourist circuit -- means the reef is in pristine condition, rarely dived, and teeming with marine life.

The site is a break in the barrier reef that is dived as a drift. The highlight is a 25-meter long tunnel through the reef wall that enters at a shallower depth and exits at approximately 32 meters onto the outer wall. Beyond the tunnel, the reef is decorated with a forest of very large gorgonian sea fans in vivid colours -- some of the most impressive gorgonian growth in all of New Caledonia. Turtles, tuna, trevally, and large grouper are regularly encountered in and around the pass.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Poum, northern tip of Grande Terre, New Caledonia (410 km N of Noumea)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 32 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-27 degrees C
- **Current**: Moderate (drift dive)
- **Best Time**: September to December

## Marine Life

Green sea turtles are regularly seen in and around the pass. Tuna and trevally patrol the deeper water beyond the tunnel exit. Large grouper shelter in the reef structure. The gorgonian sea fan forest hosts small crustaceans and invertebrates among the fan branches.

## Dive Profile

Enter upstream of the pass and drift with the current. The tunnel entrance appears in the reef wall -- enter and swim through the 25-meter passage, exiting at approximately 32 meters onto the outer reef face. Here, the large gorgonian sea fan forest stretches along the wall. Drift along the wall at 25-32 meters, admiring the fans and watching for pelagics in the blue water, before ascending to the reef top for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access requires a dive boat from Poum or a liveaboard -- this is an extremely remote dive site. Entry by giant stride. Exit by SMB pickup after the drift.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The tunnel swim-through is the centrepiece of the dive -- bring a wide-angle lens with strobes for the gorgonian forest at the exit
- This site requires significant travel from Noumea -- typically accessed on multi-day dive trips to the north
- The remote location means the reef condition is outstanding -- among the best in New Caledonia
- The gorgonian sea fans are very large and colourful -- some of the finest specimens in the country

## Safety Considerations

The tunnel is an overhead environment -- maintain visual contact with the exit light throughout. The 32-meter exit depth requires careful gas management. Current through the pass is moderate but consistent -- stay with the group. The remote location means medical facilities are far away; dive conservatively.
