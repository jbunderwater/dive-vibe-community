# Forna Point

The first headland beyond Wied il-Għasri — sheer cliff walls riddled with caves and covered in sponges and hydroids.

## Overview

Forna Point is the first headland beyond Wied il-Għasri on Gozo's north coast. From here onward, the north coast becomes "a bastion of sheer cliffs, the base of which is riddled with sea caves." The main feature is Għar tal-Margun (Cormorant Cave) with its huge entrance. Two caverns off to the east have walls lined with spectacular corals. The near-vertical cliff walls are literally covered with sponges, spiral worms, calcareous growths, and orange hydroid colonies on the undersides of ledges and boulders. Steep vertical drop-offs on both sides reach 30–32 metres. The site is rarely dived, meaning visibility averages 25 metres and big species encounters are more likely due to low diver traffic.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North coast Gozo, Malta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 32 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Tips and Recommendations

- Carry a torch for cave interiors
- Dive within your certification limits
- Check conditions with local operators before diving
