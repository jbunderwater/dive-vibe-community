# Submarine cave

A deep cave dive at Xwejni Bay on Gozo's north coast with a double arch feature.

## Overview

Submarine Cave is located at Xwejni Bay on Gozo's north coast. The site combines cave, reef, and wall diving with an average depth of 30 metres and maximum depth exceeding 45 metres. A nearby double arch adds to the site's topographical interest.

The site is part of a broader dive route along Gozo's north coast. Shore access is possible but difficult — boat access is preferred. The cave requires experienced-level certification.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Xwejni Bay, north coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Marine life reports are mixed. The surrounding reef and wall support typical Gozo species.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the cave at approximately 30 metres. Explore the cave interior, then visit the double arch feature nearby. The wall drops beyond 45 metres — monitor depth carefully.

## Entry and Exit

Shore access is possible but medium difficulty. Boat access preferred. Located at Xwejni Bay on Gozo's north coast.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The double arch nearby adds variety to the dive
- Part of the broader Xwejni Bay / north coast dive area
- Boat access is recommended over the difficult shore entry
- Experienced divers only due to depth

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification required for 45+ metre depth. The cave environment at depth requires careful gas management. Carry a torch and SMB.

## Photography

The double arch provides a natural frame for wide-angle compositions. The cave interior at depth requires powerful lights for effective photography.
