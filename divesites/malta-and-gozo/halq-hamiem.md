# Halq Hamiem

A massive cave with a shaft reaching the cliff top — rarely dived, with excellent big fish sightings.

## Overview

Halq Hamiem is a massive cave above the waterline at a sharp indent in the cliff off Hekka Point on Gozo's west coast. A big shaft in the ceiling goes right to the top of the cliff, creating dramatic light effects. The cave entrance is at only 6 metres depth, with a rocky ledge outside dropping to 20 metres. The seabed is covered with massive boulders.

This is a rarely dived site, which means increased chances of encountering abundant marine life and big fish sightings due to low diver traffic.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Hekka Point, west coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Double-banded bream, seahorses, common octopus, tuna, dentex, and saddled bream. The low diver traffic means marine life is more abundant and less skittish than at popular sites.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the cave entrance at 6 metres. Enter the massive cavern and look up to see the shaft reaching the cliff top. Exit and explore the rocky ledge and boulder field to 20 metres. The rarely-dived nature of the site rewards patient observation.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators. Rarely visited — may need to request specifically.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Rarely dived — excellent chance of big fish sightings
- The ceiling shaft to the cliff top creates dramatic light effects
- Seahorses have been reported — look carefully among the boulders
- The cave entrance at 6m makes this accessible despite the remote location
- One of Gozo's hidden gems — ask your operator specifically for this site

## Safety Considerations

Intermediate level. The cave is above the waterline with good natural light. The main risk is the remote location — ensure your boat operator knows the site. The boulder field can be disorienting in reduced visibility.

## Photography

The ceiling shaft creates a natural spotlight effect — position yourself beneath it for dramatic overhead illumination. The rarely-dived boulder field offers excellent macro opportunities. Big fish encounters are more likely here than at busier sites.
