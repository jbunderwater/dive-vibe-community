# Wied Bassasa

A dual-corridor tunnel through a headland with the option to enter from inside — always crystal-clear water.

## Overview

Wied Bassasa features a good-sized cave and tunnel system that passes through a small headland on Malta's south coast. Two corridors both lead to the same area, creating a natural circuit. The cave entrance goes above the surface, so divers can start from within the cave itself.

The highlight is the play of sunlight and the colours of sponges and marine growth on the cave walls. A plateau at approximately 10 metres drops rapidly to 40+ metres outside. The wall beyond the cave has crevices filled with marine life. The site is known for consistently outstanding visibility.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Wied Bassasa, south coast Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–40 meters (outstanding)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The cave walls are covered in colourful sponges and marine growth. The wall crevices outside shelter grouper, moray eels, and octopus. The deeper sections attract pelagic visitors.

## Dive Profile

Enter the cave from above the surface or descend from outside. Explore both corridors of the tunnel system. Exit on the far side of the headland and descend the wall to explore crevices. The plateau at 10 metres provides an excellent safety stop area.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry. The cave entrance is accessible from above water level. Advanced certification required for the deeper wall sections.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The dual-corridor tunnel creates a natural circuit — no need to backtrack
- Enter from inside the cave for a unique start to the dive
- Visibility is consistently outstanding — one of Malta's clearest sites
- The colourful sponges on cave walls glow with torch illumination
- The wall beyond the cave drops rapidly — watch your depth

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification required for the deep wall (40+ metres). The cave itself is relatively shallow and well-lit. The rapid depth change from 10 metres plateau to 40+ metres wall requires careful depth monitoring.

## Photography

The consistently excellent visibility makes this one of Malta's best sites for photography. Sunlight in the tunnel creates dramatic effects on the colourful sponge-covered walls. Wide-angle for the tunnel; macro for the sponges and wall crevice life.
