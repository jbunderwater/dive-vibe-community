## MV Cominoland

MV Cominoland has a longer history than most Malta dive sites. The vessel was built in 1942 by Philip & Son Ltd in England as Miner VI, an M-class minelayer for the Royal Navy's harbour defence operations during World War II (295 GRT, 34 m long, 8 m beam). Sold in 1966 to E. Zammit & Sons, she was converted to a passenger ferry and began serving the Valletta–Comino route, eventually taking the name MV Cominoland by 1976. Captain Morgan Cruises acquired the vessel in 1982 and operated her on tourist day cruises until decommissioning. The Gozo Tourism Association scuttled MV Cominoland on 12 August 2006 alongside MV Karwela. Before sinking, the hull paint was stripped as a controlled experiment to compare marine life settlement rates between the bare-hulled Cominoland and the painted Karwela.

## Overview

The Cominoland rests upright on a sandy seabed approximately 60 metres east of the MV Karwela at Xatt l-Ahmar, south-east Gozo. The wreck is compact at 34 metres in length — significantly smaller than the adjacent Karwela. The deck sits at approximately 32–35 metres and the deepest sections reach 41 metres. The vessel has two decks accessible for penetration via openings cut before scuttling. Minelaying equipment near the stern is an unusual historical feature. The wreck sits upright and is in stable condition for wreck diving.

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine life reports found. The wider Malta/Gozo waters support grouper, moray eels, octopus, bream, barracuda, and Mediterranean corals.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deck at 32–35 metres and begin your exterior survey. Swim the length of the 34-metre vessel and examine the stern area where minelaying equipment from the vessel's original Royal Navy service remains visible. Enter the two accessible decks via the pre-cut openings. Monitor depth as the keel approaches 41 metres. Ascend with adequate reserve for a safety stop at 5 metres. The adjacent Karwela can be combined if gas and NDL permits.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Malta and Gozo operators serve the wreck site. Entry by giant stride. The paired Cominoland–Karwela wreck dive is one of Malta's signature dive experiences. Deploy an SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Cominoland–Karwela combination is one of the great paired wreck dives in the Mediterranean
- Penetration requires wreck diving certification and a torch
- Look for the minelaying equipment near the stern — an unusual historic detail from the vessel's WWII Royal Navy service
- The stripped hull (no paint) creates a different texture of marine growth compared to the Karwela

## Safety Considerations

The 41-metre maximum depth demands careful gas management. Avoid fin kicks near the bottom inside the wreck — silt reduces visibility rapidly. Always carry a primary and backup torch for penetration. Do not enter confined spaces without wreck training. Buddy diving is mandatory.
