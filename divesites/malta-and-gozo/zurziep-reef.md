# Zurziep Reef

A surreal underwater playground of gullies, overhangs, and fireworks anemones near Xlendi.

## Overview

Zurziep Reef is located beneath the Wardija cliffs between Wardija Point and Kercem village on Gozo's south coast, approximately 10 minutes by boat from Xlendi. Despite its name, the site features south-facing caverns alongside a small colourful reef.

A series of gullies form a shallow channel between the reef and cliff face, with multiple overhangs and pass-throughs. Boats anchor on the reef at 6 metres. The gentle drop-offs and varied topography have been described as "a surreal underwater playground." The site is accessible to various experience levels and excellent for photography and snorkelling.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Wardija cliffs, near Xlendi, south Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Numerous cardinalfish and various anemone species including the distinctive fireworks anemone (Pachycerianthus multiplicatus). The sheltered gullies support colourful marine communities.

## Dive Profile

Anchor on the reef at 6 metres. Explore the gullies and channels between the reef and cliff face. Navigate the overhangs and pass-throughs. The gentle terrain allows relaxed exploration at a comfortable depth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Xlendi operators (approximately 10 minutes). The anchoring area at 6 metres provides sheltered access.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Described as 'a surreal underwater playground' by divers
- Look for fireworks anemones — distinctive and photogenic
- Gentle depths allow extended bottom time
- Good for both diving and snorkelling
- Combine with Xlendi Bay or Dawra Tas-Sanap nearby

## Safety Considerations

Suitable for intermediate divers. Gentle depths and sheltered conditions. The gullies and pass-throughs are wide and accessible.

## Photography

Fireworks anemones are the standout macro subject. The gullies and pass-throughs create natural framing for wide-angle compositions. The shallow depth allows natural light photography throughout.
