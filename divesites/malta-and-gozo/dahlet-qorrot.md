# Dahlet Qorrot

A picturesque Gozo inlet with fishermen's boathouses in cliff caves — stingrays and flying gurnards in crystal-clear water.

## Overview

Dahlet Qorrot is a small inlet nestled between the northern cliffs of Gozo near Nadur. A pebbly beach with a quayside and rocks on either side leads into clear deep water. Local fishermen use caves at the cliff base as traditional boathouses, adding character to the surface scenery. Underwater, the terrain consists of extensive Posidonia neptune grass beds among small sandy patches at a maximum depth of 10 metres. Visibility averages 30 metres and can reach 40 — exceptional for a shallow bay. The site is best dived in west or south wind conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dahlet Qorrot, near Nadur, east coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–40 meters (avg 30m)
- **Current**: None to light
- **Best Time**: Year-round (best in W/S winds)

## Marine Life

Stingrays, lizardfish, common octopus, ornate wrasse, Turkish wrasse, flying gurnards, Mediterranean damselfish, and rainbow wrasse. The Posidonia meadows support rich shallow-water biodiversity.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Exceptional visibility for a shallow bay — often 30m+
- Look for stingrays and flying gurnards on the sandy patches
- The fishermen's boathouses in cliff caves are photogenic above water
- Easy shore entry with parking, boat ramp, and toilets
- Best in west or south wind conditions
