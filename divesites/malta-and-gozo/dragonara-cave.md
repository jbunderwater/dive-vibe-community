# Dragonara cave

An open-top collapsed cave forming a coral lagoon with shimmering light effects — actually located at L'Ahrax Point, not St. Julian's.

## Overview

Dragonara Cave is located at L'Ahrax Point on Malta's north coast (not to be confused with the terrestrial Grotta di Dragonara near St. Julian's). The dive site features an open-top collapsed cave that forms an almost circular coral lagoon. An underwater tunnel entrance at 6 metres leads to a small inland sea where divers can surface and view high cliff walls with a sky-blue ceiling above.

The shimmering sunlight bouncing off the cave walls into crystal-clear water creates an enchanting light display. The cave interior features golden cup corals, red sponges, false coral, and tubeworms. Water temperature is slightly higher inside the cave, encouraging coral growth. The surrounding reef extends to approximately 18 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: L'Ahrax Point, north Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Generally calm
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Golden cup corals, red sponges, false coral, tubeworms, and starfish inhabit the cave interior. The slightly warmer water temperature inside encourages coral growth. The surrounding reef supports typical Mediterranean species.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the tunnel entrance at 6 metres. Follow the tunnel into the enclosed coral lagoon — divers can surface inside and view the dramatic cliff walls and open sky. Explore the cave interior, then exit and dive the surrounding reef to 18 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from north Malta operators. L'Ahrax Point is Malta's northernmost point. Open Water certification sufficient.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The coral lagoon with sunlight effects is uniquely photogenic
- Surface inside the cave to appreciate the cliff walls and open sky
- Water temperature is slightly warmer inside — noticeable on the return
- Popular with underwater photographers for the light effects
- Not to be confused with the terrestrial Grotta di Dragonara near St. Julian's

## Safety Considerations

Open Water certification sufficient. The cave is well-lit with natural light — no overhead environment risk. The tunnel entrance at 6 metres is straightforward.

## Photography

The shimmering light effects on the cave walls are the main photographic attraction. Ambient light photography works beautifully. Shoot upward from inside the cave for dramatic sky-through-water compositions. Wide-angle essential.
