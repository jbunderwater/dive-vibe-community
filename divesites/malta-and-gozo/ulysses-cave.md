# Ulysses Cave

A large cavern with a freshwater spring creating a visible halocline — cold non-salty water inside a Mediterranean cave.

## Overview

Ulysses Cave is a large cavern carved out of vertical cliffs northwest of Xlendi Bay on Gozo's south coast. A small inner cave just fits two divers at the entrance. The highlight is a freshwater spring inside that creates a cold freshwater environment with a visible halocline — the separation layer between fresh and salt water.

The site has an average depth of 10+ metres with a maximum exceeding 45 metres on the surrounding wall. The freshwater spring makes this a cold, non-salty dive inside the cave — an unusual experience for a Mediterranean site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: NW of Xlendi Bay, south coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light inside
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The cave's freshwater environment supports a different community than the surrounding marine habitat. The outer walls and reef host typical Mediterranean species.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the cavern entrance. The large outer cavern is spacious — explore the walls and formations. The inner cave is narrow (fits two divers). The freshwater halocline is visible as a shimmering layer. The wall outside drops to 45+ metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators. Advanced certification required.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The freshwater spring creates an unusual cold, non-salty environment inside
- The halocline (salt/fresh water boundary) is visually striking
- The inner cave is tight — only two divers at a time
- Decent cavern but there are better cave options in Gozo if time is limited
- Combine with Xlendi Bay or Zurziep Reef nearby

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification required. The freshwater environment inside the cave is noticeably colder than the sea — be prepared. The inner cave is tight — do not enter without proper training. The wall outside drops to extreme depths.

## Photography

The halocline effect creates a unique visual distortion that photographs beautifully — especially in video. The contrast between the dark cave interior and the blue Mediterranean entrance is a classic composition.
