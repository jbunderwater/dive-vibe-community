# Inland Sea

An 80-metre tunnel connecting an enclosed lagoon to the open Mediterranean — one of the most unique dives in the Mediterranean.

## Overview

The Inland Sea (Qawra tal-Dwejra) is a small enclosed lagoon approximately 60 metres wide on Gozo's west coast at Dwejra, connected to the open Mediterranean by an 80-metre-long underwater tunnel through the cliff. The tunnel starts at 4–6 metres depth inside the lagoon, progressively deepening to 15 metres mid-tunnel, and reaching 22–26 metres at the seaward exit. The lagoon itself is only 2 metres deep.

Swimming through the cliff from the calm enclosed lagoon to the open sea — with its dramatic deep indigo blue panorama at the exit — is an unforgettable experience. The tunnel has vertical walls and a pebble bottom. On the open sea side, the seabed drops to approximately 50 metres with additional caves including Whale Cave to the north and Old Man Cave (accessible via a left route, ~20 minutes in, at 18 metres).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dwejra, west coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light inside; variable outside
- **Best Time**: Year-round (best April to November)

## Marine Life

Nudibranchs (including Regal Doris), John Dory, combers, wrasse, sea hares, and tube anemones inhabit the tunnel and surrounding area. The open-sea side supports larger species adapted to the deeper environment.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the shore at the Inland Sea lagoon. Descend to 4–6 metres and enter the tunnel. The tunnel deepens progressively — maintain good buoyancy as boats pass overhead during summer. At the seaward exit (22–26 metres), explore the caves and wall outside. Return through the tunnel or ascend outside if boat pickup is arranged.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the Inland Sea lagoon — easy access with steps. The lagoon is used by small traditional fishing boats (luzzu) — be aware of boat traffic overhead in the tunnel during summer months.

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of the most unique dives in the Mediterranean — swimming through a cliff
- The stunning blue light at both tunnel entrance and exit is the highlight
- Good buoyancy control is essential — boats pass overhead in the tunnel
- Explore Old Man Cave on the left route outside (18m, ~20 min from exit)
- The Inland Sea is part of the Dwejra complex — combine with Blue Hole and Azure Reef

## Safety Considerations

Good buoyancy control required due to boat traffic overhead in the tunnel. The seaward exit opens to deeper water — monitor depth carefully. Conditions outside can differ significantly from the sheltered lagoon. Carry a torch for the tunnel.

## Photography

The tunnel creates spectacular light effects — shoot towards both the lagoon entrance and sea exit for contrasting blue tones. The moment of emerging from the tunnel into the open Mediterranean is the signature shot. Wide-angle lens essential.
