# HMS Trusty Star

A WWII naval minesweeper drifter lost to a mine in 1942, discovered in 2016 at 85–90 metres off Fort St. Elmo — designation is HMD (His Majesty's Drifter), not HMS.

## Overview

HMD Trusty Star (His Majesty's Drifter) was a steel-hulled fishing drifter of approximately 26 metres length built in 1919 by Ouse Shipbuilding. The vessel had several names: originally HMT Groundswell, then renamed FV Elie Ness when used in the Scottish fishing industry, before being requisitioned by the Admiralty in 1939 and renamed HMD Trusty Star. The vessel was fitted with magnetic mine-sweeping equipment on 9 April 1941 and had considerable success keeping Grand Harbour approaches clear despite heavy Axis mine-laying operations.

On 10 June 1942, while clearing mines laid by German S-boats outside Grand Harbour, Trusty Star struck a mine herself and sank approximately 3 km off Fort St. Elmo, Valletta. One Maltese seaman was injured; the rest of the crew were rescued unhurt.

The wreck was discovered in September 2016 and opened to divers on 1 May 2019 (managed by Heritage Malta). She lies at 85–90 metres depth, quite intact but covered in silt, resting at approximately 45 degrees on her starboard side on a sandy seabed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~3 km off Fort St. Elmo, Valletta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (Extreme Technical)
- **Maximum Depth**: 90 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable at depth
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The silt-covered wreck supports limited marine life at this depth. Some encrustation and deep-water species are present. No site-specific marine life reports are documented. The wider Malta/Gozo waters support grouper, moray eels, octopus, bream, barracuda, and Mediterranean corals.

## Dive Profile

Extreme technical dive at 85–90 metres. The vessel lies at a 45-degree angle on her starboard side — orient to the hull and explore along its 26-metre length. The intact condition rewards close inspection of the minesweeping vessel's features. Bottom time is measured in minutes at this depth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Heritage Malta permit required since May 2019 — must dive through an approved centre registered with Heritage Malta's Underwater Cultural Heritage Unit. Technical diving qualifications with deep trimix or rebreather experience mandatory.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The correct designation is HMD (His Majesty's Drifter), not HMS — she was a requisitioned fishing vessel, not a formal warship
- The vessel had three names: HMT Groundswell → FV Elie Ness → HMD Trusty Star
- The wreck is heavily silted — maintain excellent buoyancy to avoid reducing visibility
- She survived the mining with only one casualty — most crew escaped
- Heritage Malta permit required — arrange through approved operator

## Safety Considerations

Extreme technical dive at 85–90 metres. CCR or heavy trimix mandatory. Heritage Malta permit required. The wreck's silty covering means careful buoyancy control is essential. Full technical support team required.

## Photography

The intact hull at a dramatic 45-degree angle makes a compelling subject. Silt disturbance is the main photography challenge — approach carefully and shoot before disturbing the area. Powerful lights required at 85+ metres.
