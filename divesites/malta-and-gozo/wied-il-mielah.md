# Wied il-Mielah

A massive arch resembling the collapsed Azure Window — with two caverns, barracuda schools, and even mola mola sightings.

## Overview

Wied il-Mielah on Gozo's north-west coast is characterised by a massive natural arch jutting from high sheer cliff walls, often compared to the collapsed Azure Window. Underneath the arch lie two caverns and a tunnel. One cavern offers an air chamber where divers can surface.

A pass-through at 18 metres depth connects a shallow reef (12–30 metres long) to the cliff wall. The recreational depth range is 15–30 metres, with the wall dropping to 50+ metres for technical divers. The site is known for barracuda schools at 30–35 metres, amberjack, and occasional mola mola (ocean sunfish) — a rare and exciting Mediterranean sighting.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Wied il-Mielah, NW coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 50 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Barracuda schools (30–35 metres), amberjack, mola mola (ocean sunfish), octopus, tuna, Mediterranean cardinalfish, and dentex. Colourful sponges cover the boulders. The diversity of marine life here is notable even by Gozo standards.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the arch area. Explore the two caverns — surface in the air chamber. Navigate the pass-through at 18 metres between the shallow reef and cliff wall. The barracuda schools are typically found at 30–35 metres along the wall. Monitor depth carefully as the wall continues well beyond recreational limits.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Resembles the Azure Window before it collapsed — dramatic arch formation
- Barracuda schools at 30–35m are a regular highlight
- Mola mola (ocean sunfish) have been sighted — keep watching the blue
- Surface in the cavern air chamber for a unique experience
- Beginner-friendly cavern exploration plus advanced wall dive options

## Safety Considerations

Intermediate for the caverns and pass-through. Advanced for the deeper wall sections (30+ metres). The wall drops beyond 50 metres — careful depth monitoring required. Carry an SMB.

## Photography

The massive arch provides a dramatic wide-angle frame. Barracuda schools at 30–35 metres are photogenic with a wide lens. The colourful sponge-covered boulders offer macro subjects. If mola mola appear, they are an extraordinary photographic opportunity.
