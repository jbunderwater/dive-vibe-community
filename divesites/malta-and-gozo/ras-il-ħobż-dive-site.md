# Ras il-Ħobż (Dive Site)

A huge underwater column rising from the deep to just 8–12 metres below the surface — wall dropping beyond 100 metres.

## Overview

Ras il-Ħobż (also known as Middle Finger) is a dramatic pinnacle dive site off Gozo. A huge column rises from 40+ metres to just 8–12 metres below the surface. A narrow channel 35 metres deep separates the pinnacle from the coast. The seaward side drops beyond 100 metres into the abyss. The site is sheltered from north-west winds.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Gozo coast
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 100 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Lobsters, octopus, and moray eels inhabit the pinnacle. The deep walls attract pelagic species.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The pinnacle rises from 40m+ to just 8–12m below surface — dramatic
- Seaward side drops beyond 100m — extreme depth management required
- Also known as 'Middle Finger'
- Sheltered from NW winds
