# Billinghurst Cave

One of the largest caves in the Maltese islands — a 60-metre penetration with an air pocket at the back, near Reqqa Point.

## Overview

Billinghurst Cave is one of the largest caves in the Maltese Islands, located near Reqqa Point on Gozo's north coast. The large cave faces due north and sees almost no direct sunlight. It extends 60 metres over fine sand and shingle, with an upward slope leading to a massive second cave containing an air pocket where divers can surface. Entry is via a ladder directly above the cave entrance. The impressive colours created by light at the entrance contrast with the dark interior. The exit may not always be visible from deep inside — cave navigation skills are recommended.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Reqqa Point, north coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Various eels and shrimp inhabit the cave. The entrance area supports sponges and encrusting organisms illuminated by available light.

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of the largest caves in the Maltese Islands
- The air pocket in the second cave allows surfacing inside the cliff
- Entry via ladder directly above the cave entrance
- The exit may not be visible from deep inside — maintain orientation
- Combine with Reqqa Point wall and Anchor Reef
