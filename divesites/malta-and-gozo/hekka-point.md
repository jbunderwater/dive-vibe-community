# Hekka Point

A brilliantly coloured surface cavern leading to a massive submerged cave with consistent seahorse sightings.

## Overview

Hekka Point on Gozo's west coast features two cave environments: a brilliantly coloured surface cavern open to the sky at approximately 15 metres depth, and a massive completely submerged cave accessed via a steep wall beyond the surface cavern, full of pristine corals.

The submerged cave offers excellent chances of seahorse sightings. Lobsters and shrimp are also reported. The dramatic transition from the illuminated surface cavern to the expansive submerged cave makes this a varied and rewarding dive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Hekka Point, west coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Seahorses are consistently sighted in the submerged cave. Lobsters and shrimp inhabit the cave interior. The surface cavern walls are covered in brilliant colours from sponges and encrusting organisms.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the surface cavern at 15 metres — open to the sky with brilliant colours. Continue down the steep wall to the entrance of the submerged cave. Explore the pristine coral formations inside. Search carefully for seahorses on the cave walls.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators. Advanced certification required for the submerged cave section.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Consistent seahorse sightings in the submerged cave — look carefully on the walls
- The surface cavern's brilliant colours make an excellent first impression
- The transition from open cavern to submerged cave is dramatic
- Pristine coral formations inside the submerged cave
- Lobsters visible in cave crevices — bring a torch

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification required due to 30-metre depth and submerged cave environment. The transition from surface cavern to submerged cave involves a steep wall — maintain buoyancy. Carry a torch for the submerged section.

## Photography

The brilliantly coloured surface cavern with natural overhead light is the most photogenic feature. Seahorse portraits require macro lens and patience. The transition from open cavern to dark submerged cave creates dramatic wide-angle compositions.
