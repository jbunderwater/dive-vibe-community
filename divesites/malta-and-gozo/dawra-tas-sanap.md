# Dawra Tas-Sanap

An interesting cavern below Sanap Cliffs with five huge anchors among the boulders and vivid sponge colours.

## Overview

Dawra Tas-Sanap is located below the Sanap Cliffs on Gozo's south-west coast. The site features an interesting cavern plus a second cave to the east of the main ledge, flanked by small reefs on each side. A vertical wall drops from a ledge at approximately 8 metres.

The cavern walls are decorated with vivid sponges, anemones, and soft corals — a torch is recommended to bring out the colours. Among the boulders on the slope below, five huge anchors have been found, adding historical intrigue to the dive. The site is sheltered from north-westerly winds.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Below Sanap Cliffs, SW Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 65 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Amberjacks, Mediterranean cardinalfish, nudibranchs, common octopus, tuna, discodoris, and yellow cluster anemones. The vivid sponges, anemones, and soft corals on the cavern walls are a highlight.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the ledge at 8 metres. Enter the main cavern and explore the colourful walls. Visit the second cave to the east. Descend the wall to find the five large anchors among the boulders. The wall continues to 40+ metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators. Sheltered from north-westerly winds.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Five huge anchors among the boulders — historical artefacts on a cave dive
- Bring a torch to illuminate the vivid sponge and soft coral colours
- Two caves to explore — the main cavern and a second to the east
- Sheltered from NW winds — a good option when other sites are too exposed
- The vertical wall from the 8m ledge provides dramatic depth changes

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification recommended due to depth potential (40+ metres). The caverns are well-lit. The wall drops steeply — monitor depth. Carry an SMB.

## Photography

The vivid sponges and soft corals on the cavern walls are best photographed with a torch or strobe to restore colour. The five anchors among boulders make unusual wide-angle subjects. The vertical wall provides dramatic depth-perspective compositions.
