# San Dimitri Point

Dramatic wall dive beneath 80-metre cliffs on Gozo's NW corner — one of the best pelagic encounter sites on the island.

## Overview

San Dimitri Point (Tirxa) is a wall dive site beneath 80-metre-high cliffs on Gozo's north-west corner. A shallow reef platform at 6 metres (used for boat anchoring) gives way to a gentle drop-off reaching massive boulders at 18+ metres. An interior gully runs between the reef and shore with a passage beneath large boulders. The remote location and dramatic cliff scenery are complemented by excellent pelagic encounters — this is one of the best big-fish sites on Gozo. Local legend tells of a chapel that collapsed into the sea here.

## Site Information

- **Location**: San Dimitri Point, NW corner Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Groupers, dentex, large shoals of barracuda, tuna, and amberjack. One of the best pelagic encounter sites in Gozo. The remote location means fish populations are robust.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
