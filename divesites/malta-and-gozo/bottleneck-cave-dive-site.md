## Bottleneck Cave (Dive Site)

Bottleneck Cave is a cavern dive on the northwest coast of Gozo near the Dwejra/Reqqa area, named for its distinctive narrow entrance that widens into a larger interior chamber. The tight entry section requires calm buoyancy and a measured approach, making this a more engaging cavern experience than many of Gozo's more open cave sites.

## Overview

The cave is accessed through a narrow bottleneck entrance — the feature that gives the site its name — which opens into a more spacious interior chamber. The cave is accessible to recreational divers but requires good buoyancy control to navigate the constricted entry without disturbing the encrusted walls. The interior is illuminated by natural light from the entrance and any additional torch light. Maximum depth reaches approximately 20 metres. The clear northwest Gozo waters provide exceptional visibility.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest Gozo, Malta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–25 meters
- **Current**: Generally calm inside; exposed coast outside
- **Best Time**: April to November

## Marine Life

The cave interior shelters spiny lobster, conger eels, and small fish adapted to low-light conditions. The narrow entrance discourages larger fish, making the interior a quieter refuge. Outside the entrance, the reef supports grouper, sea bream, and barracuda. Moray eels inhabit the crevices in the adjacent rock face.

## Dive Profile

Approach the narrow entrance and carefully transit through it, maintaining controlled buoyancy to avoid contact with the walls. Explore the interior chamber. Exit back through the bottleneck and explore the surrounding reef at 10–20 metres. Complete a safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Gozo operators access the northwest coast. Entry by giant stride. The exposed northwest coast can experience significant swell — confirm conditions before diving.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Practice your buoyancy control in open water before attempting a confined cave entry
- Remove any equipment that might snag on the narrow entrance — trail gauges close to your body
- A torch is essential inside the cave
- Only intermediate and above divers with solid buoyancy skills should attempt this site
- Combine with Reqqa Point for a superb west Gozo dive day

## Safety Considerations

The narrow entrance requires careful buoyancy management. Never force your way through — if it feels too tight, exit and discuss with the dive guide. Maintain orientation to the entrance throughout. Carry a torch and dive with a buddy. The exposed coast outside requires sea state assessment before entry.

## Photography

The bottleneck entrance creates a dramatic circular frame for shots of divers or the cave interior beyond. The contrast between the narrow dark entrance and the illuminated interior is a compelling subject. A torch is essential for interior shots.
