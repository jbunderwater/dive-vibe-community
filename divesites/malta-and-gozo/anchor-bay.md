# Anchor Bay

Shallow reef diving beneath the Popeye Village film set — with large boulders, swim-throughs, and the Scorpion Cave.

## Overview

Anchor Bay is a small inlet between two cliffs on Malta's north-west coast, famous as the location of the 1980 Popeye movie film set (still standing as a tourist attraction). Underwater, the site features large boulders, swim-throughs, rocky reefs, and seagrass meadows at a maximum depth of 12 metres. A large anchor rests on the seabed at 8 metres. The adjacent Scorpion Cave (a separate site entry) adds a cavern option. Shore entry is via a concrete jetty on the south side of the bay opposite Popeye Village, with ladders on the bay side.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Anchor Bay, NW Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Wrasses, scorpionfish, morays, groupers, parrotfish, and octopus. The seagrass meadows support rich biodiversity. Good for night dives.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
