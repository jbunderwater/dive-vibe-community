# Tug 2

A 1975 tugboat built in Malta for the Chinese government, scuttled in June 2013 off Sliema — shore accessible with penetration opportunities at 22 metres, rotated 90 degrees by a 2016 storm.

## Overview

Tug 2 (original Chinese name: Tuo Lun Er Hao) is a tugboat (approximately 30 metres long, 7.5-metre beam, 141 gross tons, with a 15.24-metre mast height) built in 1975 by Malta Drydocks for the Chinese government. The vessel worked in Malta's Grand Harbour during the China Dock project before being decommissioned. Bezzina Marine Services purchased her in 2000.

Tug 2 was scuttled on 20 June 2013 as an artificial diving reef, positioned upright on sand at 22 metres depth approximately 250 metres off Exiles shoreline, Sliema — a joint project of the Professional Diving Schools Association (PDSA) and the Malta Tourism Authority. In October 2016, a tropical storm moved the wreck approximately 30 metres and rotated it 90 degrees, crashing the bow into a nearby reef. The wreck remains largely intact in this new position. The wheelhouse and engine room offer safe penetration with multiple swim-throughs. Shore accessible from Exiles.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~250 m off Exiles, Sliema
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate

- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Fish schools congregate around the wreck structure. The wheelhouse and hull cavities shelter grouper, moray eels, and octopus — confirmed by multiple dive operators. The surrounding reef (damaged by the 2016 storm displacement) supports additional marine life. The wider Malta waters support bream, barracuda, and Mediterranean corals.

## Dive Profile

Enter from Exiles shore and swim approximately 250 metres to the wreck. Descend to the deck and explore the wheelhouse — safe to enter with a clear exit. Move to the engine room for another penetration opportunity. Multiple swim-throughs are available. Average depth approximately 15 metres, maximum 22 metres. The moderate depth allows generous bottom time.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Exiles, Sliema. Approximately 250-metre surface swim. Also accessible by boat. Watch for boat traffic, especially during summer weekends.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Shore accessible — no boat needed
- The wheelhouse and engine room offer penetration with clear exits — basic wreck training recommended
- The wreck was displaced 30 metres and rotated 90 degrees by a tropical storm in October 2016; the bow is now crashed into a reef
- Watch for heavy boat traffic on summer weekends
- Combine with the nearby Exiles reef dive

## Safety Considerations

Intermediate level dive at 22 metres average. Penetration of wheelhouse and engine room is straightforward but requires basic wreck training. Watch for boat traffic during the surface swim and safety stop — carry an SMB. Summer weekends are busiest.

## Photography

The intact tugboat at moderate depth with good natural light makes excellent wide-angle subjects. The wheelhouse interior provides atmospheric penetration shots. Fish congregating around the wreck add life to compositions. The storm-displaced position — bow into reef — adds visual interest.
