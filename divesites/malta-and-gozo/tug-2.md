# Tug 2

A 1975 Chinese-registered tugboat scuttled in 2013 off Sliema — shore accessible with easy penetration at 22 metres.

## Overview

Tug 2 is a tugboat (30 metres long, 7.5-metre beam, 15.24-metre height with mast, 141 gross tons) built by Malta Drydocks in 1975 for the Chinese government. Originally registered as Tuo Lun Er Hao in Tianjin, China, she worked in Malta's Grand Harbour on the China Dock project before being decommissioned and purchased by Bezzina Marine Services in 2000.

Scuttled on 20 June 2013, the wreck was positioned upright on sand at 22 metres depth approximately 250 metres off Exiles shoreline, Sliema. In October 2016, a tropical storm moved the wreck approximately 30 metres and rotated it 90 degrees, crashing the bow into a reef. The wreck remains largely intact. The wheelhouse and engine room are safe for penetration with multiple swim-throughs available. Shore accessible from Exiles with a moderate surface swim.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~250m off Exiles, Sliema
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Fish schools congregate around the wreck structure. The wheelhouse and hull cavities shelter grouper, moray eels, and octopus. The surrounding reef (damaged by the storm displacement) supports additional marine life.

## Dive Profile

Enter from Exiles shore and swim approximately 250 metres to the wreck. Descend to the deck and explore the wheelhouse — safe to enter with a clear exit. Move to the engine room for another penetration opportunity. Multiple swim-throughs are available. Average depth 15 metres, maximum 25 metres. The moderate depth allows generous bottom time.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Exiles, Sliema. Approximately 250-metre surface swim. Also accessible by boat. Watch for boat traffic, especially during summer weekends.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Shore accessible — no boat needed
- The wheelhouse and engine room are safe for penetration with clear exits
- The wreck was displaced 30 metres by a 2016 tropical storm and now sits rotated 90 degrees
- Watch for heavy boat traffic on summer weekends
- Combine with the nearby Exiles reef dive

## Safety Considerations

Intermediate level dive at 22 metres average. Penetration of wheelhouse and engine room is straightforward but requires basic wreck training. Watch for boat traffic during the surface swim and safety stop — carry an SMB. Summer weekends are busiest.

## Photography

The intact tugboat at moderate depth with good natural light makes excellent wide-angle subjects. The wheelhouse interior provides atmospheric penetration shots. Fish congregating around the wreck add life to compositions. The storm-displaced position (bow into reef) adds visual interest.
