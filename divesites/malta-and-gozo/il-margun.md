# Il-Margun

Two enormous caves stretching from cliff top to below the waterline — rarely dived, with rich marine encounters.

## Overview

Il-Margun (Għar tal-Margun / Cormorant Cave) is located at a sharp indent in the cliff off Forna Point on Gozo's north coast. Two massive caves stretch almost from the cliff top down to the surface and beyond, with the cave bottoming out at approximately 24 metres. The overall maximum depth is 40 metres on a sloping seabed completely covered with boulders.

The site is not well known and rarely visited, which means marine life encounters are quite prolific with a high chance of rare species. The enormous scale of the two caves — extending from above the waterline nearly to the cliff top — is impressive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Forna Point, north coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Quite prolific marine life due to infrequent diver traffic. High chance of encountering rare species. Dusky grouper, octopus, and typical Mediterranean reef species. The boulder field provides extensive habitat.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the cave entrances. Explore both massive caves — they extend from above the waterline to 24 metres depth. The surrounding boulder field slopes to 40 metres. The low diver traffic means patient observation is rewarded.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators (approximately 30 minutes from charter point). Rarely offered — may need to request specifically.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Rarely dived — marine life is more prolific than at popular sites
- Two caves of enormous scale — from cliff top to below waterline
- The boulder field around the caves supports rich biodiversity
- Request this site specifically — it's not on most operators' standard rotations
- Combine with nearby Forna Point wall for a varied dive day

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification required due to 40-metre maximum depth. The boulder-covered seabed can be disorienting — maintain orientation to the cliff. Carry a torch for the cave interiors.

## Photography

The enormous cave entrances with natural light from above create dramatic wide-angle compositions. The rarely-dived boulder field offers undisturbed marine life subjects. The scale of the caves — visible from cliff top to underwater — is difficult to capture but impressive in video.
