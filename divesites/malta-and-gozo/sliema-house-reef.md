# Sliema House Reef

An urban shore dive off Sliema promenade with surprisingly good marine life — caverns, tunnels, and arches at shallow depths.

## Overview

Sliema House Reef is the collective name for the reef system off Sliema's seaside promenade, encompassing both Fortizza Reef and Coral Gardens. The shallow reef features caverns, tunnels, arches, overhangs, valleys, and gullies at a maximum of 15 metres depth. Shore access is directly off the promenade — one of Malta's most accessible urban dives. Despite the urban location, the marine life is surprisingly diverse and the site is excellent for photography.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sliema promenade, Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Nudibranchs, cuttlefish, octopus, moray eels, and various Mediterranean fish species. Good macro opportunities due to shallow depth and light.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
