# Sanap Cliffs

A vertical wall dropping from an 8-metre ledge to 40+ metres — vivid sponges, soft corals, and yellow cluster anemones.

## Overview

Sanap Cliffs on Gozo's south-west coast features a vertical wall dropping from an 8-metre ledge (used for anchoring and safety stops) to 40+ metres. An interesting cavern is accessible from the wall, plus a second cave to the east with small reefs on each side. The wall is decorated with vivid colours from sponges, anemones, and soft corals — a torch brings them to life. Water can get choppy at this exposed location.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sanap Cliffs, SW Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Amberjack, Mediterranean cardinalfish, nudibranchs, common octopus, tuna, discodoris. Yellow cluster anemones on the wall. Vivid sponges, anemones, and soft corals provide rich wall colours.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
