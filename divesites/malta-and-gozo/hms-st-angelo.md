# HMS St. Angelo

A WWII harbour tugboat mined in 1942 outside Valletta — sitting upright at 54 metres in a busy shipping channel.

## Overview

HMS St. Angelo was a tugboat (~24 metres long) used to transport personnel from the Royal Navy base at Fort St. Angelo to warships moored in Grand Harbour during WWII. The vessel also served for rescue, towing, and minesweeping operations. On 30 May 1942 she struck a mine and sank outside Valletta.

The wreck lies approximately 1.5 km off the Grand Harbour entrance at 54 metres depth on a sandy bottom, sitting upright and reported in very good condition. The site's location in the main maritime channel — with busy commercial shipping traffic in and out of Valletta — makes it one of Malta's more hazardous dive locations.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~1.5 km off Grand Harbour entrance, Valletta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 54 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Variable; shipping channel
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The intact upright wreck provides good shelter for grouper, moray eels, and octopus. The sandy surroundings host bream and other Mediterranean species.

## Dive Profile

Descend via shotline to the deck of the upright wreck at approximately 45–50 metres. The compact 24-metre vessel can be surveyed in a single pass. Note the good condition of the hull and superstructure. Maximum seabed depth 54 metres. Gas management critical.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. The site is in an active maritime shipping channel — dive boats must coordinate with port authorities and maintain appropriate flags and signals.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The site is in an active shipping channel — listen for engine noise during safety stops
- The wreck's good condition makes it rewarding despite the depth
- Named after Fort St. Angelo in Grand Harbour, the Royal Navy's Malta headquarters
- Combine with other deep wrecks in the Grand Harbour approach area

## Safety Considerations

Advanced technical certification required for 54-metre depth. The site's location in an active shipping channel is the primary hazard — large vessels transit this area continuously. Dive boats must display appropriate signals. Deploy SMB well before surfacing. Listen for engine noise during decompression stops.

## Photography

The upright, well-preserved wreck makes an excellent wide-angle subject. The compact size allows the entire vessel to be captured in a single frame with a wide lens. Shoot upward to silhouette the vessel against ambient light.
