# Tower wreck

An ancient cargo scatter of Punic-era amphorae (~3rd century BC) in Xlendi Bay, Gozo — part of the world's first Deepwater Archaeological Park, inaugurated August 2023.

## Overview

The Tower Wreck is located in Xlendi Bay, south-west Gozo, named for its proximity to the 17th-century Xlendi coastal watchtower that guards the bay entrance. The site was first detected in 1993 during a deep-water submarine survey, and extensively surveyed in 2006–2008 by Heritage Malta researchers.

The site presents as a large scatter of Punic-era amphorae (including Maltese Punic ovoid amphorae with looped handles and flat-bottomed Sagona types) alongside bowls, urns, jugs, and lead anchor stocks, spread across approximately 67,000 square metres of seabed at depths of 105–115 metres. Whether the deposit represents one shipwreck or multiple loss events has not been definitively established by archaeological investigation. The cargo is dated to approximately the 3rd century BC (Punic period), contemporary with the Punic Wars.

Heritage Malta designated the site as an Archaeological Zone at Sea in 2020 and declared it the world's first Deepwater Archaeological Park in 2022. The park opened for technical diver access in summer 2023, funded by the Malta Airport Foundation. Two designated diving routes are available through approved Heritage Malta operators.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Xlendi Bay, SW Gozo (near Xlendi watchtower)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 105 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable at depth
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: Summer months (when park is accessible)

## Marine Life

At 105–115 metres, marine life is limited. The amphorae and rocky outcrops of extinct coral reefs provide shelter for deep-water invertebrates. No site-specific marine life surveys are documented. The wider Gozo waters support grouper, moray eels, octopus, bream, barracuda, and Mediterranean corals.

## Dive Profile

Extreme technical dive at 105–115 metres following one of two designated Heritage Malta diving routes. The cargo scatter of amphorae and ceramics is the primary feature. Bottom time is measured in single-digit minutes. The photogrammetry 3D model of the site is available via Heritage Malta's Virtual Museum for pre-dive familiarisation.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Heritage Malta permit mandatory. Part of the Xlendi Underwater Archaeological Park (opened August 2023). Two designated diving routes available — follow the designated route only. Technical diving qualifications and CCR or heavy trimix required.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Heritage Malta permit mandatory — dive through an approved operator only
- Part of the world's first Deepwater Archaeological Park, inaugurated August 2023
- Whether the deposit is one shipwreck or several loss events is still under archaeological investigation — this is a cargo scatter, not a ship hull
- The amphorae date to approximately 300 BC (Punic period), near-contemporary with the Punic Wars
- The nearby Phoenician Shipwreck at ~110 metres (separate site) adds to Xlendi's deep archaeological significance
- A 3D virtual museum tour of the site is available online from Heritage Malta

## Safety Considerations

Extreme technical dive at 105–115 metres. CCR or very heavy trimix mandatory. Heritage Malta permit required. Protected archaeological site — no artefact disturbance or removal permitted. Follow the designated diving route only.

## Photography

The amphorae scatter in the Deepwater Archaeological Park setting is the primary subject. Heritage Malta has produced photogrammetric 3D documentation of the site. Follow the chartered route to avoid disturbing the delicate silty seabed. Extremely powerful lighting required at 105+ metres.
