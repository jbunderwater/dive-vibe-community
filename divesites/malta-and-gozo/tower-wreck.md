# Tower wreck

A 2,300-year-old ancient trading vessel in Xlendi Bay, Gozo — part of Heritage Malta's Deepwater Archaeological Park.

## Overview

The Tower Wreck is a 3rd-century BC ancient trading vessel located in Xlendi Bay, south-west Gozo, near the tower guarding the bay entrance. The wreck lies at 105 metres depth on a fine sandy and silty seabed with rocky outcrops.

Detected via a deep-water submarine survey, the site presents as a dense scatter of amphorae alongside bowls, urns, and jugs dating to approximately 2,300 years ago. The wreck forms part of Heritage Malta's Deepwater Archaeological Park, which was opened in 2024 with two chartered diving routes for technical divers.

Heritage Malta manages the site and a diving permit is required.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Xlendi Bay, SW Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 105 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable at depth
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

At 105 metres, marine life is limited. The amphorae provide some shelter for deep-water invertebrates.

## Dive Profile

Extreme technical dive at 105 metres following one of two chartered Heritage Malta diving routes. The cargo scatter of amphorae and ceramics is the primary feature. Bottom time is measured in single-digit minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Heritage Malta permit mandatory. Part of the Deepwater Archaeological Park (opened 2024). Two chartered diving routes available. Technical diving qualifications required.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Heritage Malta permit mandatory
- Part of the Deepwater Archaeological Park opened in 2024
- Two chartered diving routes are available — follow the designated route
- Approximately 2,300 years old — contemporary with the Punic Wars
- Combined with the Phoenician Shipwreck, Xlendi hosts two of the world's oldest known wrecks

## Safety Considerations

Extreme technical dive at 105 metres. CCR or very heavy trimix mandatory. Heritage Malta permit required. Protected archaeological site — no artefact disturbance permitted. Follow the designated diving route.

## Photography

The amphorae scatter in the Deepwater Archaeological Park setting is the primary subject. Document the site following the chartered route for archaeological consistency. Extremely powerful lighting required at 105 metres.
