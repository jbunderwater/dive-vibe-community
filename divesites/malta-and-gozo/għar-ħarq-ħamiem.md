# Għar Ħarq Ħamiem

Malta's only fully submerged terrestrial cavern — with endemic albino shrimp found nowhere else on Earth.

## Overview

Għar Ħarq Ħamiem is the only known fully submerged terrestrial cavern in the Maltese Islands, located on the north side of St. George's Bay, St. Julian's. Two chambers on different levels are connected by a narrow corridor. The water surface inside the cave is 16 metres below sea level. The cavern has been described as "as large as the Mosta Dome."

Water transitions from freshwater at the surface to brackish at mid-depths to seawater at the bottom. The cave contains **endemic albino shrimp** — a species found only in this specific cavern, nowhere else on Earth. The site is scheduled as a Level 1 Area of Ecological Importance and Site of Scientific Importance.

This is not a standard recreational dive. Closed-circuit equipment, trimix, and proper cave diving certification are required. Lines are installed but the cave is extremely silty with poor visibility.

## Dive Profile

This is a serious cave dive requiring full cave certification. Enter from shore on the north side of St. George's Bay. Navigate through the freshwater layer to the brackish and saltwater zones. Follow installed guidelines through the silty environment. Two chambers on different levels connected by a narrow corridor. The cave surface is 16 metres below sea level.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the north side of St. George's Bay, St. Julian's. Access is difficult. Full cave diving equipment required including closed-circuit rebreather and trimix.
