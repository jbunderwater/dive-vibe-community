# MV Imperial Eagle

A former Malta–Gozo ferry scuttled in 1999 as an artificial reef — sitting perfectly upright at 42 metres off Qawra Point.

## Overview

The MV Imperial Eagle was originally built in 1938 by John Crown & Sons Ltd as the excursion ship New Royal Lady (length ~45 metres, beam 9 metres, twin diesel engines). Requisitioned during WWII for transport, she later operated as the Crested Eagle on Thames cruises before being sold to Malta in 1958, where she served as a Malta–Gozo ferry carrying approximately 70 passengers and 10 cars.

Purchased by the diving community in 1995, she was scuttled on 19 July 1999 approximately 500 metres off Qawra Point as an artificial reef. The wreck sits perfectly upright on a sandy seabed with the deck at 32 metres and maximum depth of 42 metres. Wooden components have largely decomposed while metal structures remain. Penetration is possible through rooms and corridors. Notable nearby features include Qawra Reef (25-metre top), a natural rock arch, a 1600s anchor, and a Statue of Christ positioned 30 metres from the bow.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~500m off Qawra Point, NE Malta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 42 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The wreck has developed into a productive artificial reef over 25+ years. Grouper, barracuda, amberjack, and bream patrol the structure. Moray eels and octopus inhabit the interior spaces. Schools of damselfish cluster around the superstructure.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deck at 32 metres. Explore the metal superstructure — the bridge area retains only the wheel. Penetration through rooms and corridors is possible for appropriately trained divers. Maximum seabed depth is 42 metres at the keel. On ascent, visit the nearby Statue of Christ (30 metres from the bow) and the 1600s anchor. Safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Buġibba or St. Paul's Bay operators. The wreck is well-marked and regularly visited.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Statue of Christ 30 metres from the bow is worth visiting
- A 1600s anchor lies nearby — easy to combine on the same dive
- Qawra Reef (25m top) is adjacent for a second shallower dive
- Penetration is possible but wooden structures have decayed — watch for sharp metal edges
- The only remaining bridge feature is the wheel

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification required for 42-metre depth. Penetration requires wreck diving training — decomposed wooden structures leave sharp metal edges. Carry a torch and SMB. Monitor gas consumption at depth.

## Photography

The upright wreck sitting on clean sand with good visibility makes a classic wide-angle subject. The bridge wheel is iconic. The Statue of Christ nearby provides a unique second subject. Interior passages offer atmospheric penetration photography.
