# Jesus of Qawra

A 3-metre statue of Christ blessed by Pope John Paul II — resting at 25 metres near the MV Imperial Eagle wreck.

## Overview

Jesus of Qawra (Kristu tal-Baħħara / Statue of Christ) is a 3-metre-tall concrete and fibreglass statue by sculptor Alfred Camilleri Cauchi, blessed by Pope John Paul II on 26 May 1990. Originally placed near St. Paul's Island, it was relocated to Qawra Point on 17 May 2000, approximately 30 metres from the bow of the MV Imperial Eagle wreck (scuttled 1999). The statue rests at approximately 25 metres depth. A natural rock arch in the reef wall and a 1600s-era anchor wedged between rocks are additional features nearby.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Qawra Point, NE Malta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The surrounding reef and nearby wreck support grouper, bream, and barracuda. Schools of damselfish congregate around the wreck structure.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
