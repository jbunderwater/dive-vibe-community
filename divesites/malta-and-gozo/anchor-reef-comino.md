# Anchor reef (Comino)

A submerged rocky ledge northwest of Cominotto with steep walls dropping to 36 metres — a WWII anchor sits among colourful boulders.

## Overview

Anchor Reef (Comino), also known as Lautier's Reef (Sikka Tal Lautier), is a submerged rocky ledge northwest of Cominotto island. The reef starts a few metres wide and expands to around 150 metres toward the east. Steep walls drop from a shallow 5–15 metre plateau to meet the ocean floor at 25–38 metres, where massive boulders, small caves, and overhangs characterise the deeper sections. A WWII-era anchor sits near colourful boulders at depth. The shallow portions are relatively featureless, but the site improves significantly at depth with excellent macro photography opportunities.

## Site Information

- **Location**: NW of Cominotto island, Comino
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Schools of barracuda, parrotfish, octopuses, nudibranchs (dotted sea slugs), sponges, and soft corals on the deeper boulders. The reef improves significantly with depth.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The deeper sections (25m+) are far more interesting than the shallow plateau
- Look for the WWII-era anchor among the boulders
- Excellent macro photography on the encrusted boulders
- Boat access recommended — shore access is difficult
- Part of the Comino dive day — combine with P31 and Santa Maria Caves
