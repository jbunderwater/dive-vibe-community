# Xlighter 127

A WWI-built Royal Navy lighter sunk during WWII in 1942 — lying at a dramatic angle with bow at 5 metres and stern at 25 metres.

## Overview

Xlighter X127 is a British water lighter originally built as a landing craft in 1915 for the Royal Navy during WWI (24 metres long, 7-metre beam), later converted to a fuel lighter. She sank on 6 March 1942 during WWII operations in Marsamxett Harbour off Manoel Island at Lazzaretto Creek entrance.

The wreck was unidentified for decades, known locally as "Carolita" or "Coralita." A 2003 underwater survey initially identified it as X-131; the correct identification as X-127 was confirmed in 2006. The wreck lies at a dramatic steep angle on a slope — bow at 5 metres and stern at 22–25 metres depth. Despite decades underwater, the vessel is in exceptionally good condition.

Note: Visibility at this site is consistently poor — the location in Marsamxett Harbour means high silt content. A Land Rover wreck lies on the seabed along the coast nearby.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Marsamxett Harbour, off Manoel Island
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 3–8 meters (consistently poor)
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Octopuses and groupers inhabit the wreck structure. The harbour environment limits overall marine diversity compared to open-water sites.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore and locate the wreck on the harbour slope. Start at the shallow bow at 5 metres and descend along the hull to the stern at 22–25 metres. The steep angle provides a dramatic perspective. The excellent preservation allows detailed exploration of the lighter's construction. A Land Rover wreck lies along the coast nearby for additional interest.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the Manoel Island area. The harbour location provides calm, sheltered conditions. The poor visibility is a constant at this site regardless of weather.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Visibility is always poor — 3–8 metres is normal; don't expect Malta's usual crystal-clear water
- The wreck was misidentified for decades — known locally as 'Carolita'
- A Land Rover wreck lies along the coast nearby
- The steep bow-to-stern angle (5m to 25m) is dramatic and unusual
- The shallow bow section is excellent for beginners; the stern suits more experienced divers

## Safety Considerations

The shallow bow section (5 metres) is suitable for beginners. The deeper stern at 25 metres requires more experience. Poor visibility is the primary challenge — maintain buddy contact and carry a torch. The sheltered harbour location means calm conditions.

## Photography

The dramatic steep angle is the wreck's most distinctive feature — shoot from below to emphasise the slope. The poor visibility creates atmospheric, moody images. A torch is essential for illuminating detail in the low-visibility water.
