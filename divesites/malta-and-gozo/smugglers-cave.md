# Smugglers Cave

A simple shallow cave with swimthrough at the base of the Marfa Ridge cliffs — beginner-friendly and rarely crowded.

## Overview

Smugglers Cave is a shallow cave at the base of the cliffs of Rdum l-Aħmar on the north side of Marfa Ridge. The cave features a small swimthrough at approximately 3 metres depth, with the surrounding reef and sandy slope extending to a maximum of 18 metres.

The gentle terrain beyond the cave features a rocky bottom with boulders housing diverse marine life. The site is simple but effective as an introduction to cave environments for less experienced divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Rdum l-Aħmar, Marfa Ridge, north Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 3 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The rocky bottom with boulders beyond the cave supports diverse Mediterranean marine life. Wrasse, bream, and octopus are commonly seen.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the shallow cave at 3 metres. Swim through the short passage, then explore the surrounding rocky reef and boulder formations extending to 18 metres. The gentle terrain allows relaxed exploration.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. The site is at the base of cliffs on the north side of Marfa Ridge.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Good introduction to cave environments for beginners
- Very shallow — extended bottom time available
- The surrounding boulders often harbour more marine life than the cave itself
- Rarely crowded — off the main dive site circuit

## Safety Considerations

Beginner-friendly. Very shallow cave with easy exit. No overhead environment risk. The surrounding reef extends gently to 18 metres.

## Photography

The swimthrough provides a framed composition. The shallow depth and natural light allow ambient light photography throughout. The boulder formations beyond the cave offer macro opportunities.
