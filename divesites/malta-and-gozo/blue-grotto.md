# Blue Grotto

A complex of seven sea caves along Malta's south-east coast, including a main arch over 30 metres high — one of Malta's most dramatic natural sites.

## Overview

The Blue Grotto (Taħt il-Ħnejja) is a complex of seven sea caves along Malta's south coast near Wied iż-Żurrieq, including the main Blue Grotto arch (over 30 metres high), Honeymoon Cave, Cat's Cave, Reflection Cave, and the Elephant Foot formation. The caves face the uninhabited islet of Filfla.

On sunny days, reflection of sunlight on the white sandy seafloor lights up the caves in brilliant blue hues. Different colours of red, mauve, green, orange, and yellow are visible on the cave walls. The underwater terrain features dramatic arches, tunnels, and drop-offs created by centuries of wave action. The actual Blue Grotto cave is approximately 1 km east of the Wied iż-Żurrieq entry point.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Wied iż-Żurrieq, south coast Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light; can be exposed to south swells
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The caves and surrounding reef host typical Mediterranean species including groupers, moray eels, octopus, and schools of bream. John Dory are sometimes sighted. The cave walls support colourful sponges and encrusting organisms.

## Dive Profile

Access the cave system by boat. The shallow cave interiors (5–10 metres) allow extended exploration time. The surrounding reef drops to 30 metres with additional cave features. Natural light penetrates deeply into the main arch, making it accessible for all certification levels.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Wied iż-Żurrieq. The caves can also be reached by a 40-minute surface swim from shore, but boat access is strongly recommended. Tourist boats also operate in the area — coordinate with dive operators.

## Tips and Recommendations

- On sunny days the cave lighting is spectacular — plan your dive for morning or early afternoon
- The main arch is over 30 metres high — one of the largest natural arches in Malta
- Seven distinct caves offer variety for multiple dives
- Combine with the nearby West Reef and East Reef for a full day
- The tourist boat ride through the caves is worth doing as surface reconnaissance

## Safety Considerations

Beginner-friendly depths in the caves. The surrounding area can be exposed to southerly swells — check conditions. Boat traffic from tourist operators is common. Carry an SMB when outside the caves.

## Photography

The interplay of sunlight and the white sandy bottom creates the famous blue glow — a natural light photographer's dream. Wide-angle is essential for the massive arch formations. The colour variety on cave walls rewards close-up work with a torch.
