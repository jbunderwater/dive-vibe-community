# Santa Marija Caves (Dive Site)

An impressive maze of interconnecting tunnels and caverns on Comino's NW coast — multiple exits always visible.

## Overview

Santa Marija Caves on Comino's north-west coast is an impressive collection of interconnecting tunnels and small caverns. Gullies, swim-throughs, and interconnected caves create a maze-like environment, but many exit points are always visible for safety. Anchoring is in 10 metres of water. Sea bream accustomed to feeding, octopuses, and lobsters inhabit the site. Excellent for underwater photography.

## Site Information

- **Location**: NW coast, Comino
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 19 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Sea bream (accustomed to feeding and approachable), octopuses, and lobsters. The crystal-clear Comino waters make for excellent marine life encounters.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A maze of caves with exit points always visible — safe exploration
- Sea bream are accustomed to divers and very approachable
- Excellent for underwater photography in Comino's clear water
