# HMS Maori

Malta's shallowest warship wreck — a Tribal-class destroyer famous for helping sink the Bismarck, bombed in Grand Harbour in 1942.

## Overview

HMS Maori was a Tribal-class destroyer launched in 1937, famous for her role in the sinking of the German battleship Bismarck. She was bombed by the Luftwaffe in Valletta's Grand Harbour in February 1942 and sank beside Fort St. Elmo. Now lying half-buried in sand at 8–14 metres depth, she is Malta's shallowest warship wreck. Much of the vessel lies beneath the sand — the exposed sections include hull plates and structural elements. Visibility at this harbour site is typically poor and a DSMB is required due to harbour boat traffic.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Fort St. Elmo, Grand Harbour, Valletta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–15 meters (often poor)
- **Current**: Light; harbour boat traffic
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Bream schools, goatfish, and occasional seahorse. The harbour environment limits diversity compared to open-water sites.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Malta's shallowest warship wreck — accessible to Open Water divers
- HMS Maori helped sink the Bismarck — significant naval history
- Visibility is typically poor — this is a harbour site
- DSMB mandatory due to harbour boat traffic
- Much of the wreck is buried in sand — manage expectations
