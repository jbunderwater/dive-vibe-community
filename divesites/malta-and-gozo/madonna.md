# Madonna

An underwater Madonna statue placed in a natural cave at 18 metres — part of the legendary Cirkewwa dive complex.

## Overview

The Madonna dive site features an underwater statue of the Holy Mary placed in a small natural cave at 18 metres depth, halfway along the Cirkewwa reef wall. The statue was placed by the Amphibians Diving Club in November 1987. The site is part of the larger Cirkewwa dive complex, which also includes the famous Arch, the P29 and Rozi wrecks, and Susie's Pool. The reef wall features caves, tunnels, and swim-throughs extending beyond the Madonna's location. Shore entry at Cirkewwa is easy with stairs and railings.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cirkewwa, north Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Octopus, barracuda, and breams inhabit the reef wall. The cave sheltering the Madonna supports small invertebrates and crustaceans.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
