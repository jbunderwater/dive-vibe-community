# Wied ir-Raheb

Three caves along a cliff drift dive — including the unique 'House Cave' with its door-and-window architecture.

## Overview

Wied ir-Raħeb (The Sacristan's Valley) is a boat dive along the cliff face on Gozo's west coast featuring three distinct caves explored as a drift dive. The gorge starts hundreds of metres inland and ends 25 metres above the surface at the cliff face.

Cave 1 is open to the surface at 15 metres depth, with walls and bottom covered in colourful sponges and calcified coral. Cave 2 — the House Cave (Ta' Wiereb) — is the highlight: a distinctive rectangular entrance resembling a clean-cut door in the cliff wall with a round window exit above the entrance. Inside, a very dark but wide chimney leads to water level with a small enclosed air pocket. Cave 3 is open to the surface at 25 metres with similar colourful decoration.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 70 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Fish populations are reportedly less abundant than expected despite the dramatic boulder formations. The cave walls are richly decorated with colourful sponges and calcified coral.

## Dive Profile

This is a drift dive along the cliff, visiting three caves in sequence. Start at Cave 1 (15m, open to surface), then drift to the House Cave (25m, the highlight), and finish at Cave 3 (25m, open to surface). The cliff wall drops much deeper — monitor your depth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators. The site is along Gozo's west coast cliffs.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The House Cave's door-and-window architecture is unique in Gozo
- This is a drift dive — three caves visited in sequence
- The colourful sponges and calcified coral on cave walls are exceptional
- The gorge (Sacristan's Valley) starts far inland — dramatic cliff scenery
- The chimney inside the House Cave has a small air pocket

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification recommended due to 25-metre depth and drift dive format. The House Cave chimney is dark — carry a torch. The cliff wall extends much deeper than the caves — careful depth management required.

## Photography

The House Cave's rectangular entrance is an extraordinary composition — a perfectly framed door in the cliff. The colourful sponge-covered walls in all three caves reward macro and wide-angle. Shoot into the House Cave window for dramatic backlighting.
