## MT Hephaestus

MT Hephaestus was a bunkering oil tanker built in 1965 by Bröderna Jonssons Torrdocka (Kållansdsö Varv AB) in Lidköping, Sweden (595 GRT, 61.33 m long, 7.93 m beam). On 10 February 2018, the vessel ran aground near Qawra, Malta during severe weather when the crew lost control and the ship beached on rocks near St Paul's Bay. All seven crew members evacuated safely; the vessel carried no cargo at the time. The owners deemed repair costs uneconomical and the tanker was towed to Marsa for storage. The Malta Tourism Authority subsequently acquired the vessel and scuttled it on 29 August 2022 at Xatt l-Ahmar, Gozo, in collaboration with the Professional Diving Schools Association (PDSA) and the Ministry for Gozo, adding to the cluster of artificial reef wrecks in the area that includes the Karwela, Xlendi, and Cominoland.

## Overview

The tanker rests upright on a sandy seabed at Xatt l-Ahmar with the deepest sections approaching approximately 46–47 metres. The wreck was thoroughly cleaned of pollutants and large entry holes were cut through the hull before sinking to create safe penetration access. The vessel is relatively new on the seabed and its marine community is still developing. It sits approximately 30 metres offshore, making it accessible by both shore and boat.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Xatt l-Ahmar, south-east Gozo, Malta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 47 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–25 meters
- **Current**: Generally light (eastward drift in the area)
- **Best Time**: Year-round (best April to November)

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine life reports found. The wreck is relatively new (scuttled 2022) and its marine community is still developing. The wider Malta/Gozo waters support grouper, moray eels, octopus, bream, barracuda, and Mediterranean corals.

## Dive Profile

Descend the anchor line or mooring chain to the wreck. Conduct an exterior survey of the hull before entering through the pre-cut access holes. Monitor depth carefully — the deepest accessible sections approach 47 metres and NDL limits are quickly reached. Ascend with adequate reserve for a 5-metre safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Dive operators from Malta and Gozo serve the site by boat. Shore diving is also possible for experienced divers with appropriate equipment. The site is suitable for Advanced Open Water certified divers for the exterior; penetration requires wreck certification. Deploy an SMB on ascent for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Gas management is critical at 47 metres — plan turn pressure conservatively
- The wreck is relatively new and still establishing its resident marine community
- Penetration requires wreck diving certification and a torch
- Shore diving requires DPV and twinset with stages according to local operator guidance

## Safety Considerations

The 47-metre maximum depth places this dive at the advanced recreational limit. Gas management, NDL discipline, and ascent rate control are all critical. Never penetrate the wreck without wreck diving training and appropriate torches. Always dive with a buddy. Deploy an SMB on ascent.
