# Ta Cenc

Diving beneath Gozo's highest cliffs — massive boulder swim-throughs and a small cavern on the south coast.

## Overview

Ta' Ċenċ is a wall dive site beneath Gozo's highest cliffs on the south coast, in a small inlet neighbouring Mġarr ix-Xini. The near-vertical and sometimes overhanging wall is full of small holes and fissures. Huge boulders between 20–30 metres depth create impressive swim-throughs.

A small platform at 8 metres serves as a reference point and safety stop area. A small shallow cavern serves as the turning point of the dive. The cliffs continue underwater to approximately 30 metres before giving way to the boulder field.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South coast Gozo, near Mġarr ix-Xini
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Nudibranchs, lobsters (between boulders), shoals of damselfish, bream groups, grouper, and parrotfish. The holes and fissures in the wall harbour diverse invertebrate life.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the 8-metre platform. Follow the wall down through the fissures and holes. Explore the massive boulder swim-throughs at 20–30 metres. Visit the small cavern as a turning point. Ascend using the 8-metre platform for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Primarily a boat dive. Shore entry is possible — cliff jump near the Ta' Ċenċ hotel area with a steel ladder back up (for experienced divers only).

## Tips and Recommendations

- You're diving beneath Gozo's highest cliffs — dramatic surface scenery
- The massive boulder swim-throughs at 20–30m are the highlight
- Look for lobsters between the boulders
- The cliff-jump shore entry is only for experienced divers
- A good companion dive to Mġarr ix-Xini (adjacent inlet)

## Safety Considerations

Intermediate to Advanced. The wall can be overhanging — maintain buoyancy. The boulder swim-throughs require good spatial awareness. Shore entry via cliff jump is risky and not recommended for most divers.

## Photography

The massive boulder swim-throughs provide dramatic framing for wide-angle compositions. Nudibranchs on the wall are excellent macro subjects. The dramatic cliff backdrop above water adds context to surface shots.
