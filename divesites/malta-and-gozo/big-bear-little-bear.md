## Big Bear-Little Bear

Big Bear-Little Bear is a reef dive near the western coast of Gozo, named for two large rock formations rising from the seabed that resemble bears in outline when viewed from underwater. The site lies in the open water off Gozo's exposed western coast, which offers dramatically clear Mediterranean water.

## Overview

The two rock formations — "Big Bear" (the larger) and "Little Bear" (the smaller) — rise from a sandy seabed at approximately 15–20 metres. The pinnacle-like rocks are encrusted with sponges, sea fans, and encrusting corals, and serve as focal points around which the dive revolves. The clear waters of western Gozo, with visibility regularly exceeding 25 metres, allow the full spectacle of the formations to be appreciated from a distance.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West Gozo, Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Variable; exposed west-facing coast
- **Best Time**: April to November

## Marine Life

The rock formations attract diverse fish life. Grouper hold territories around the larger formation. Sea bream, wrasse, and barracuda orbit the pinnacles. Moray eels inhabit the crevices in the rock faces. Octopus are seen in the surrounding sandy areas. In summer, schools of barracuda and amberjack may gather around the exposed formation.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sandy bottom at 18–20 metres near the formations and circle the Big Bear first. Then navigate to the Little Bear for comparison. Spend time examining the encrusted surfaces carefully before ascending. The sandy bottom between the formations provides a pleasant interlude. Safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Gozo operators access this western coast site. Entry by giant stride. The west-facing coast is exposed to the prevailing westerly swells — confirm sea state before diving. Deploy an SMB on ascent for boat pickup in the open water.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The site is exposed to western swell — only dive in settled weather
- The distinctive formation shapes are best appreciated from a slight distance — hover back to take in the full scale
- Bring a torch for the caves and crevices in the formation faces
- Combine with nearby Reqqa Point or Double Arch for a spectacular west Gozo day

## Safety Considerations

The exposed west Gozo location requires careful sea state assessment. Swell can make boat entry and exit challenging. Always carry an SMB. Dive with a buddy and maintain awareness of the boat position in the open water.

## Photography

The two formations rising from the sandy bottom make distinctive wide-angle subjects. Shooting from below and slightly away captures the scale of the "bears." The clear Gozo water provides exceptional visibility for both natural light and strobe photography.
