# Victorian Guns

Three Victorian-era cannons in a collapsed sea cavern at Fort Ricasoli — a unique snorkeling and shallow dive site at just 2–3 metres.

## Overview

The Victorian Guns site consists of three Victorian-era cannons lying in a sea cavern on the seaward side of Fort Ricasoli, Kalkara, on Malta's eastern coast near the entrance to Grand Harbour. Two smaller cannons (~3 metres long, ~1 metre diameter) and one larger cannon (~4.5 metres long, ~1.2 metres diameter) are associated with the Victorian-era fortifications at Fort Ricasoli.

The cavern formed when the roof collapsed, taking the cannons with it. A hole in the roof lets natural light into the cave interior, creating a dramatic lighting effect. The cannons rest at just 2–3 metres depth inside the cavern, with the surrounding reef at approximately 12 metres.

This is one of Malta's most unusual dive sites — a combination of military history, cavern environment, and extreme shallowness. It is suitable for snorkeling as well as diving.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Fort Ricasoli, Kalkara, eastern Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 3 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Light; sheltered cavern
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The cavern and surrounding reef host typical Mediterranean inshore species. The shallow, sheltered environment supports damselfish, wrasse, and small grouper. Octopus are commonly found in the rocky surroundings.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore and navigate to the seaward side of Fort Ricasoli. The cavern entrance leads to the three cannons at 2–3 metres depth. Natural light enters through the collapsed roof, illuminating the interior. Explore the cannons, then exit to the surrounding reef at approximately 12 metres for additional diving. The extreme shallowness allows essentially unlimited bottom time.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the Fort Ricasoli area. The cavern is accessed by swimming along the seaward wall of the fort. Located near Grand Harbour entrance — beware of boat traffic in the approach area.

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of Malta's most unique dive sites — cannons in a natural cavern
- Suitable for snorkeling as well as diving due to 2–3 metre depth
- The collapsed roof opening creates beautiful natural light effects
- The larger cannon is 4.5 metres long — impressive at this shallow depth
- Located near Grand Harbour entrance — carry an SMB when outside the cavern

## Safety Considerations

Beginner-friendly due to extreme shallowness. The primary hazard is boat traffic near Grand Harbour entrance — stay close to shore and carry a DSMB when outside the sheltered cavern. The cavern has natural light from the roof opening.

## Photography

The natural light streaming through the collapsed roof onto the Victorian cannons is an extraordinary photographic subject. Ambient light photography works beautifully here. The contrast between the dark cavern walls and the light beam creates dramatic compositions. Wide-angle lens essential.
