# HMS Russell

A WWI pre-dreadnought battleship sunk by mines in 1916 — Malta's deepest major warship wreck at 115 metres.

## Overview

HMS Russell was a Duncan-class pre-dreadnought battleship built by Palmers Shipbuilding and Iron Company in Jarrow. Launched 19 February 1901, she measured 432 feet (132 m) long with a 75 ft 6 in (23 m) beam and displaced 13,270–13,745 long tons (normal). Principal armament comprised four 12-inch (305 mm) 40-calibre guns in twin-gun turrets fore and aft, supplemented by twelve 6-inch guns, ten 12-pounder guns, and four submerged 18-inch torpedo tubes.

On 27 April 1916, HMS Russell was approaching Malta when she struck two naval mines laid by the German submarine U-73, which had arrived off Malta on 25 April and laid mines outside the Grand Harbour entrance. A fire broke out in the after section of the ship and the vessel gradually sank. Sources vary on casualties: Wikipedia records 125 personnel lost; the Imperial War Museums records 27 officers and 99 men. The wreck was first examined in 2003 by a British dive team and is managed by Heritage Malta.

The wreck lies at approximately 115 metres depth (63 fathoms), roughly 3.2 nautical miles (6 km) from the Delimara peninsula, lying completely upside down on a sandy seabed. The stern section is destroyed from the mine blast. Large 12-inch (305 mm) guns are visible on the sand beneath the inverted hull.

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine life reports found. The wider Maltese waters support Mediterranean species; at 115 metres depth the environment is beyond recreational diving range.

## Dive Profile

Extreme technical dive at 115 metres requiring closed-circuit rebreather or heavy trimix. The inverted hull presents an enormous wall of steel. The 12-inch guns lying on the sand beneath the bow are the primary exploration objective. The destroyed stern section shows the catastrophic mine damage. Bottom time is measured in minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Heritage Malta permit required. Technical diving qualifications with deep trimix or rebreather experience mandatory. Full support team required.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Heritage Malta permit required
- This is a WWI wreck (27 April 1916), not WWII
- The 12-inch guns on the sand beneath the inverted hull are an extraordinary sight
- Casualty figures vary across sources: approximately 125 personnel lost (Wikipedia); IWM records 27 officers and 99 men
- Treat as a war grave with appropriate respect
- Only accessible to experienced technical divers with deep-diving capability

## Safety Considerations

Extreme technical dive at 115 metres. CCR or heavy trimix mandatory. Heritage Malta permit required. Designated war grave. No artefact removal permitted. Full technical support team and appropriate bailout gases required.
