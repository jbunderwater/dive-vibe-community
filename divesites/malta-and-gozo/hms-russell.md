# HMS Russell

A WWI pre-dreadnought battleship sunk by mines in 1916 — Malta's deepest major warship wreck at 115 metres.

## Overview

HMS Russell was a Duncan-class pre-dreadnought battleship built by Palmers Shipbuilding, Jarrow, launched February 1901 (132 metres long, 23-metre beam). On 27 April 1916 she struck two naval mines laid by German submarine U-73 while manoeuvring outside Valletta harbour. A fire broke out in the stern followed by an explosion of the 305mm gun turret. The ship capsized and sank within approximately 20 minutes. The disaster killed 27 officers and 98 ratings.

Located approximately 6 km east of Fort St. Elmo at 115 metres depth, the wreck was discovered in July 2003. She lies completely upside down on a sandy seabed with the stern section missing where the mines struck. The area around the wreck is littered with gun casings, and large 12-inch (305mm) guns are visible on the sand beneath the inverted bow section. Heritage Malta manages the site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~6 km east of Fort St. Elmo, Valletta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive

- **Typical Visibility**: Variable at depth
- **Current**: Can be strong
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

John Dory fish have been observed around the wreck. Coral growth is present on the hull surfaces. The massive inverted hull creates a habitat island on the sandy seabed.

## Dive Profile

Extreme technical dive at 115 metres requiring closed-circuit rebreather or heavy trimix. The inverted hull presents an enormous wall of steel. The 12-inch guns lying on the sand beneath the bow are the primary exploration objective. The missing stern section shows the catastrophic mine damage. Bottom time is measured in minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Heritage Malta permit required. Technical diving qualifications with deep trimix or rebreather experience mandatory. Full support team required.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Heritage Malta permit required
- This is a WWI wreck (1916), not WWII
- The 12-inch guns on the sand beneath the inverted hull are an extraordinary sight
- 125 lives were lost — treat as a war grave with appropriate respect
- Only accessible to experienced technical divers with deep-diving capability

## Safety Considerations

Extreme technical dive at 115 metres. CCR or heavy trimix mandatory. Heritage Malta permit required. 125 crew died — designated war grave. No artefact removal. Full technical support team and appropriate bailout gases required.

## Photography

The massive inverted hull and 12-inch guns make extraordinary subjects but require powerful lights at 115 metres. The scale of the wreck — 132 metres long — is difficult to convey but the guns provide a sense of proportion.
