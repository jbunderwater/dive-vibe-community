# Ta'Camma caves

The floor drops from 8 to 30 metres inside Gudja Cave — with a halocline effect from freshwater mixing and a massive exit arch.

## Overview

Ta'Camma Caves (Gudja Cave) is the most famous cave in the Ta'Camma system on Gozo's west coast. Entry is at 8 metres depth and 6 metres above sea level. After a few metres, the floor drops abruptly from 8 to 30 metres depth. A massive arch stretches from 20 metres to the seabed at 34 metres.

The passage narrows and gradually shallows until a 6-metre-deep crack is encountered, leading to a small air chamber. A freshwater source at the back creates stunning halocline effects where fresh and seawater mix. The mesmerizing blue surroundings and dramatic depth changes make this one of Gozo's most impressive cave dives.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light inside; variable outside
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Nudibranchs, damselfish, and sea breams inhabit the cave. Colourful sponges decorate the cave walls. The halocline zone where fresh and salt water mix creates a visually distinctive environment.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the cave entry at 8 metres. The dramatic floor-drop to 30 metres is immediate — maintain buoyancy. Navigate through the massive arch (20–34 metres), then follow the narrowing passage to the air chamber. Return the same way. The depth changes require careful gas management.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators. Advanced certification required.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The floor-drop from 8m to 30m is dramatic and immediate — be prepared
- The halocline effect from freshwater mixing is visually stunning
- The massive exit arch (20–34m) is one of Gozo's most impressive features
- The air chamber at the back has a freshwater source
- Advanced buoyancy skills essential for the depth changes

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification required. The abrupt depth change from 8 to 30 metres is the primary hazard — maintain buoyancy control. The narrowing passage toward the back requires careful navigation. Carry a torch. Monitor gas consumption at depth.

## Photography

The halocline effect creates a unique visual distortion that photographs beautifully. The massive arch is a dramatic wide-angle subject. Shoot from inside the cave looking out for the classic blue-water framing. The depth changes provide dramatic perspective compositions.
