# Cirkewwa - Right Arch (Dive Site)

One of Malta's most visited dive sites — the natural arch, walls, boulders, and swim-throughs at Cirkewwa.

## Overview

Cirkewwa is rated as one of the most visited dive sites in the Maltese Islands. The Right Arch site features a natural arch 12 metres below the surface and 8 metres above the seabed, surrounded by walls, boulders, flat platforms, and swim-throughs. The Sugarloaf rock formation is nearby. The site serves as a gateway to the P29 wreck, Rozi wreck, and the wider Cirkewwa reef complex. Shore entry is easy with stairs and railings.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cirkewwa, north Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Moray eels, octopus, grouper, parrotfish, rays, jacks, barracuda, cuttlefish, nudibranchs, and seahorses.

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of Malta's most visited dive sites
- Gateway to P29, Rozi wreck, and the wider reef complex
- Shore entry with stairs and railings — very accessible
