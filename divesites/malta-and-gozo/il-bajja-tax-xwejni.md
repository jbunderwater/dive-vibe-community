## Il-Bajja tax-Xwejni

Il-Bajja tax-Xwejni — Xwejni Bay — is a coastal bay dive on the northwest shore of Gozo, a short distance from the famous Qbajjar salt pans. The bay is one of the more sheltered entry points along this exposed coastline, offering relatively protected water that makes it accessible even when the outer Dwejra and Reqqa sites are too rough to dive. The Xwejni area is known above water for its ancient salt evaporation pans, still worked by Gozitan families using traditional methods.

## Overview

The dive takes place along the rocky limestone coastline of the bay, descending over stepped reef to a sandy bottom at approximately 20 metres. The site's position in a partial bay provides some shelter from the northwest swell that closes the more exposed sites nearby. The bay walls are colonised by sponges, anemones, and encrusting organisms, while the sandy floor between the reef sections supports flatfish and goatfish. Visibility is typically 20–25 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest Gozo, Malta

## Marine Life

The rocky bay walls support moray eels and octopus. Sea bream and wrasse are plentiful throughout the reef. Grouper occupy the deeper crevice zones at 15–20 metres. The sandy floor areas host goatfish, flatfish, and occasionally stingrays. The coastal rocks above the dive site are habitat for the Maltese wall lizard, visible from the boat.

## Dive Profile

Enter the bay and descend along the rocky wall to 15–20 metres. Explore the reef base and sandy floor before ascending along the stepped limestone terrain. The bay's shallower inner zone provides a pleasant finish to the dive with good light penetration. Safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Gozo northwest coast operators serve this site. The bay offers more sheltered entry and exit than the fully exposed Dwejra and Reqqa sites. Even so, sea state should be assessed before diving. The old salt pans above provide an interesting surface interval context.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A more sheltered alternative when northwest swell prevents diving at Reqqa Point
- The surrounding coastal landscape — salt pans, limestone karst, dry-stone walls — is a characteristic part of the Gozo experience
- Look carefully under ledges for moray eels and octopus in the crevice zones
- Combine with nearby Qbajjar Reef and Xwejni Bay reef for a full day in this area
- Torch useful for examining the underside of limestone overhangs

## Safety Considerations

While more sheltered than adjacent sites, the northwest Gozo location means conditions can change rapidly. Monitor sea state throughout the dive and surface in the most sheltered part of the bay. Always carry an SMB and dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The shallow bay setting with good natural light penetration creates attractive wide-angle reef shots. The stepped limestone terrain and sandy floor provide varied compositions. Moray eels and octopus in the crevice zones are strong macro subjects.
