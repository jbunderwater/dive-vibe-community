# Anchor reef (Gozo)

A T-shaped reef with vertical wall, swim-throughs, and a large anchor — between Reqqa Point and Double Arch.

## Overview

Anchor Reef (Gozo) is a submerged rocky ledge between Reqqa Point and Double Arch on Gozo's north coast. The T-shaped reef starts narrow and expands to approximately 150 metres toward the east. A vertical wall on one side drops from a shallow 5–8 metre plateau (with holes and overhangs) to 30–40+ metres. Two swim-throughs add topographical interest. A large anchor in the deeper section gives the site its name. Also known as 'Landslide Reef.'

## Site Information

- **Location**: Between Reqqa Point and Double Arch, north coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Schools of barracuda, parrotfish, octopus, and moray eels. The vertical wall attracts pelagic visitors.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
