# Ghar Lapsi

A multi-site dive complex on Malta's south coast — cave system, finger reef, middle reef, underwater nativity scene, and Black John.

## Overview

Għar Lapsi is a rocky inlet on Malta's south coast near Siġġiewi featuring multiple dive sub-sites. The 40-metre-long cave system has multiple cracks letting in sunlight. Finger Reef is a finger-shaped reef with a wall and cave (exit hole in roof, 20 metres max). Middle Reef has several reefs surrounded by sand, seagrass, and boulders (12 metres). The Crib is an underwater nativity scene at 22 metres beneath a mini overhang with life-size wooden figures. Black John is an offshore reef reaching 38 metres with interesting rock formations and a halocline effect. Shore entry is from the small cove — steep hill with steps. Parking, café, and toilets available.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Għar Lapsi, south coast Malta (Siġġiewi)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Breams, salema, jellyfish, octopus, cuttlefish, nudibranchs, wrasse, cardinal fish, groupers, moray eels, flying gurnards, occasional parrotfish and stingrays. Strong currents not unusual.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
