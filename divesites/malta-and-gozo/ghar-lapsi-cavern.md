# Ghar Lapsi cavern

A 40-metre-long tunnel with six exit points flooding it with light — one of Malta's brightest and most colourful cave dives.

## Overview

The Għar Lapsi Cavern is a 40-metre-long shallow tunnel system with six exit points and multiple crevices allowing abundant light penetration. Unlike most cave dives, the entire tunnel is bright and colourful throughout. The tunnel runs at 3–6 metres depth with a maximum of 8 metres on the sea side.

A second, lesser-known cavern lies approximately 20 minutes' swim to the left. Outside the main tunnel, a sand patch within 10 metres of the exit regularly hosts stingrays, flying gurnards, and cuttlefish. The site also features an underwater nativity scene ("the Crib") at 22 metres depth beneath a mini overhang, with life-size wooden figures.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Għar Lapsi, south coast Malta (Siġġiewi)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Can be strong
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Stingrays, flying gurnards, octopuses, and cuttlefish are commonly found on the sand patch just outside the tunnel exit. Inside, backlighting reveals sponges and marine growth on the cave walls. Mediterranean sea bream, moray eels, grouper, red starfish, and damselfish inhabit the surrounding area.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the shore at the small rocky inlet. The cave system is immediately accessible in shallow water. Swim through the 40-metre tunnel — the six exit points mean you're never more than a few metres from open water. Outside, explore the sand patch for marine life, then optionally descend to the Crib nativity scene at 22 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the small Għar Lapsi cove. Access involves a steep hill with steps — physically demanding with gear. Parking, a café, and toilets are available. An alternative entry near the reverse osmosis plant provides access to the deeper Black John reef.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The six exit points make this one of the brightest cave dives anywhere
- Check the sand patch outside for stingrays and flying gurnards
- The underwater nativity scene (Crib) at 22m is worth the detour
- A second cave system lies 20 minutes' swim to the left
- Strong currents are not unusual — check conditions

## Safety Considerations

Beginner-friendly for the tunnel (3–6 metres, always lit). The deeper areas and Crib at 22 metres require more experience. Strong currents can occur — check conditions. The steep hill access is physically demanding.

## Photography

The tunnel with its six light sources creates unique multi-directional backlighting effects. Silhouette shots through the exit points are dramatic. The sponges and marine growth on cave walls glow with backlighting. The Crib nativity scene at 22 metres is a unique photographic subject.
