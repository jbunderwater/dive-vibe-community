# Scotscraig

A 1950s Scottish river ferry that starred in the Popeye movie — now a beginner-friendly wreck dive at 21 metres near Anchor Bay.

## Overview

The Scotscraig was a small British river ferry (25 metres long) built in 1951 by Caledon Shipbuilding & Engineering, Dundee, Scotland. Originally carrying up to 6 cars and passengers, she operated the Tay Ferry Service between Dundee and Newport-on-Tay until 1966 when the Tay Road Bridge opened.

The vessel was brought to Malta and used as a supply and filming vessel during production of the 1980 Popeye movie (starring Robin Williams) at Anchor Bay. She sank around 1981 — reportedly taking on water and breaking her tow ropes during adverse weather while being relocated.

The wreck lies approximately 500 metres northwest of Anchor Bay at 21 metres depth, sitting upright on seagrass, shingle, and sand. The steel hull is breaking down but otherwise quite intact. Lower deck penetration is possible. Marine life includes moray eels, conger eels, groupers, octopuses, and occasional stingrays. Visibility at this site is not as good as elsewhere around Malta.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~500m NW of Anchor Bay, NW Malta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 meters (below Malta average)
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Moray eels and conger eels inhabit the hull interior. Groupers and octopuses are resident around the wreck. Occasional stingrays visit the sandy surroundings. The seagrass bed supports a diverse community of Mediterranean species.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wreck sitting upright at 21 metres. Circumnavigate the 25-metre hull, then explore the lower deck through penetration points. The steel hull provides multiple openings. The seagrass bed surrounding the wreck rewards exploration for rays and marine life. At 21 metres, bottom time is generous.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from nearby operators. The site is close to Anchor Bay (Popeye Village). Can be combined with shore diving at Anchor Bay.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The wreck was used in the 1980 Popeye movie starring Robin Williams
- Visibility is below Malta's average — don't expect the crystal-clear water found at Gozo sites
- Lower deck penetration is possible with appropriate training
- Look for stingrays in the surrounding seagrass
- Popeye Village above water makes for a fun pre/post-dive visit

## Safety Considerations

Intermediate level dive at 21 metres. Lower deck penetration should only be attempted with wreck training. Visibility is reduced compared to other Malta sites — maintain buddy contact. The breaking steel hull has sharp edges.

## Photography

The intact upright wreck on seagrass provides an atmospheric wide-angle subject. Reduced visibility can create moody, atmospheric images. Marine life in and around the wreck — especially moray eels and groupers — provides macro and portrait opportunities.
