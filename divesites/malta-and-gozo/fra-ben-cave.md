# Fra Ben cave

A shallow cavern at Qawra Point with an open ceiling and blowhole — rays of light dance inside on sunny days.

## Overview

Fra Ben Cave is a shallow cavern on the north side of the Qawra Point peninsula. The cave extends approximately 15 metres into the cliff with an open ceiling that forms a small inland sea with a large rock in the middle. Divers swim around the central rock and can surface in a blowhole.

The cave entrance is at 6 metres depth, reached by a 100-metre swim from the shore entry point. On sunny days, rays of light dance around inside the cavern, creating beautiful lighting effects. The overall site extends to 18 metres depth on the surrounding reef.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Qawra Point, north Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The cave shelters typical Mediterranean inshore species. The surrounding reef at Qawra Point supports bream, wrasse, and octopus.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore and swim approximately 100 metres along the coast to the cave entrance at 6 metres. Enter the cavern, swim around the central rock, and surface in the blowhole. Exit and explore the surrounding reef to 18 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Qawra Point. Easy access. The 100-metre surface swim is straightforward in calm conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Best dived on sunny days when light rays penetrate the open ceiling
- Surface in the blowhole for a unique perspective
- The central rock inside the cave is the main feature to navigate around
- Combine with other Qawra Point sites (L-Cave, North Reef)
- Simple but photogenic — good for all levels

## Safety Considerations

Beginner-friendly. The cave has natural light from the open ceiling — no overhead environment risk. The site is shallow and sheltered.

## Photography

The rays of light streaming through the open ceiling are the signature shot. Best on bright sunny days. The blowhole surfacing point provides a unique above/below perspective. Ambient light photography works well at this shallow depth.
