# Mellieha bay

A shallow training area on Malta's largest sandy beach — calm conditions for courses and beginners.

## Overview

Mellieħa Bay is Malta's largest sandy beach, used primarily as a training area by dive centres. The bay area includes Slugs Bay (a shallow reef with rock arch, seagrass, and sand at a maximum of 12 metres) which is the most commonly dived sub-site. The shallow, calm conditions make it ideal for discover scuba diving courses, try dives, and beginner training.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mellieħa Bay, north Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The Slugs Bay reef and seagrass meadows support abundant marine life. Small fish, octopus, and invertebrates are common in the shallows.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
