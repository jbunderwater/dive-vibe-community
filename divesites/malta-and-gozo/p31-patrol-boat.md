# P31 - Patrol Boat

A shallow Comino wreck visible from the surface in crystal-clear water — penetrable from stem to stern at just 18–20 metres.

## Overview

P31 (ex-Pasewalk, GS05) was a Kondor I-class minesweeper built at Peenewerft shipyard in Wolgast, East Germany. Laid down 12 December 1968, launched 18 June 1969, and commissioned 18 October 1969 into the East German Volksmarine. The vessel measured 51.98 m in length with a 7.12 m beam and displaced 361 tons. After the reunification of Germany and the disbandment of the Volksmarine, Malta acquired the vessel on 1 July 1992 and renamed it P31, operating it as a patrol vessel in the Offshore Command of the Maritime Squadron of the Armed Forces of Malta. The boat was decommissioned in 2004 and bought by the Malta Tourism Authority. After cleaning and preparation, P31 was scuttled on 25 August 2009 off the west coast of Comino, halfway between Lantern Point and Cominotto, at 18 metres depth.

At 18–20 metres depth on fine white sand, the wreck allows extended bottom time for all levels of certified diver. In Comino's clear waters, the wreck is visible from the surface. Divers can penetrate from stern to bow through the interior.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast of Comino, between Lantern Point and Cominotto
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (crystal clear)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The wreck attracts schools of bream and damselfish. Octopus and moray eels shelter in the hull. The surrounding sand supports flatfish.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Visible from the surface in Comino's crystal-clear water
- Penetrable from stern to bow — excellent introduction to wreck diving
- The top of the wreck is only 7 metres from the surface — suitable for snorkellers
- Combine with Santa Maria Caves and Lantern Point for a Comino dive day
