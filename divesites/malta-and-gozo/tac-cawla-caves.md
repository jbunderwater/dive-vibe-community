# Tac-Cawla Caves

Four caves with two penetrating 120–130 metres into the cliff — enclosed air pockets reachable only by scuba.

## Overview

Tac-Cawla Caves on Gozo's west coast is a system of four caves. Two massive caves visible from the surface penetrate 120 and 130 metres into the cliff — extraordinary penetration distances. Two completely submerged narrow caves start at 20 metres depth and extend 40 and 50 metres into the rock.

The two large surface caves both have air pockets at the back, totally enclosed in rock. One air pocket is only reachable after a 10-minute scuba dive through the cave. Brilliantly blue and immaculately clear water is visible when exiting the caves. The cave bottoms are very silty — experienced divers with good buoyancy are essential.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters (outside); reduced inside
- **Current**: Light inside
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Fish life is historically limited inside the caves for unknown reasons. The surrounding reef supports typical Gozo marine life.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat and navigate to the cave entrances. The two surface caves (120m and 130m penetration) are the primary objectives. Follow the cave to the enclosed air pockets at the back. The submerged caves at 20 metres are shorter (40–50m) but require more experience. Cave bottoms are very silty — maintain distance from the floor.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only from Gozo operators. The caves are accessible from the water only.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Two caves penetrate 120–130 metres into the cliff — extraordinary distance
- The enclosed air pockets are only reachable by scuba — surface and chat
- Cave bottoms are extremely silty — perfect buoyancy is essential
- The blue water visible on exit is brilliantly clear
- The submerged caves at 20m are narrower and require more experience

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification required. The extreme penetration distances (120–130 metres) mean thorough gas planning and navigation skills are essential. Silty bottoms can reduce visibility to zero if disturbed — experienced cave/cavern divers only. Always maintain a clear line of sight to the exit or carry a guideline.

## Photography

The brilliantly blue water visible from inside the caves looking out is the signature shot. The contrast between the dark cave interior and the luminous entrance creates dramatic compositions. The air pocket provides a unique above/below surface opportunity inside the cliff.
