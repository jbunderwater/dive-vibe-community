# Scorpion Cave

A three-chambered cave shaped like a scorpion — surface in air pockets and look back at the brilliant blue Mediterranean.

## Overview

Scorpion Cave in Anchor Bay is named for its shape — a main "body" with two side "claws." The wide entrance opens into a large bowl where divers can surface in an air pocket. Left and right passages lead to two smaller caverns, each with their own smaller air pockets.

The cave entrance is at 10 metres depth, with the interior shallowing to 8 metres toward the back. The wide entrance allows intense sapphire blue light inside, with shafts of sunlight. The exit is always clearly visible from inside. The site is reached by a 150-metre swim from the concrete jetty in Anchor Bay (approximately 20 minutes).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Anchor Bay, NW Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The sheltered cavern supports small fish and invertebrates. The surrounding Anchor Bay reef hosts wrasses, scorpionfish, morays, groupers, and octopus.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the concrete jetty at Anchor Bay and swim approximately 150 metres to the cave entrance at 10 metres. Enter the main chamber and surface in the air pocket. Explore the left and right passages (the 'claws') with their own air pockets. Exit and explore the surrounding reef.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the concrete jetty on the south side of Anchor Bay, opposite Popeye Village. Ladders available on the bay side. The 150-metre surface swim takes approximately 20 minutes.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Surface in the main air pocket and look back — the brilliant blue entrance is 'truly glorious'
- Explore all three chambers (body + two claws) for the full experience
- The cave is suitable for all levels — exit always visible
- Combine with the Scotscraig wreck and Anchor Bay reef
- Popeye Village above water makes for a fun pre/post-dive visit

## Safety Considerations

Beginner-friendly. The wide entrance means the exit is always visible — no overhead environment risk. Shallow depth (8–10 metres) allows extended bottom time.

## Photography

The sapphire blue light flooding through the wide entrance is the signature shot. Surface in the air pocket and shoot back toward the entrance for dramatic blue-water compositions. Each of the three chambers offers different lighting conditions.
