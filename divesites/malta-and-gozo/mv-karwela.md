## MV Karwela

MV Karwela was built in 1957 by Jos L. Meyer in West Germany as MS Frisia II (497 GRT, 50 m long, 8 m beam). The vessel passed through several name changes — MV Nordpaloma (1977) — before arriving in Malta in 1986 as MV Karwela. It was subsequently purchased by Captain Morgan Cruises and operated as a tourist cruise ferry until decommissioning in 2002. The Gozo Tourism Association scuttled MV Karwela on 12 August 2006 alongside the MV Cominoland as part of Malta's artificial reef programme. Before sinking, 18 tonnes of fuel and oil were removed and large openings were cut through the hull to make the wreck safe for divers to penetrate. Buoyancy tanks were used during the scuttling to ensure the vessel settled upright.

## Overview

The Karwela sits upright on a sandy seabed off the south-east coast of Gozo at Xatt l-Ahmar. The top deck (including the captain's cabin) lies at approximately 30 metres, the bow at 39 metres, and the stern at 41 metres. The vessel has three decks and is penetrable via the openings cut before scuttling. A photogenic staircase in the centre of the wreck descends to the lower deck, which is illuminated by portholes. The hull has accumulated considerable marine growth since 2006. The adjacent MV Cominoland lies approximately 60 metres away, and the MV Xlendi and MT Hephaestus are also nearby.

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine life reports found. The wider Malta/Gozo waters support grouper, moray eels, octopus, bream, barracuda, and Mediterranean corals.

## Dive Profile

Descend the anchor line to the top deck at approximately 30 metres. Conduct an exterior circuit of the vessel before any penetration. The bridge, passenger saloon, and engine room are the main penetration targets — large holes have been cut along the sides of the first two decks. The photogenic central staircase leads to the lower deck. Monitor NDL carefully as the keel approaches 41 metres. Ascend with a 5-metre safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Malta and Gozo operators serve this site. Entry by giant stride. The Karwela and Cominoland are typically offered as a paired wreck dive. Always deploy an SMB on ascent for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Wreck certification required for interior penetration
- The central staircase leading to the lower deck is a distinctive navigational feature
- The combination with the adjacent Cominoland is a signature Gozo dive experience
- Nitrox extends bottom time meaningfully at this depth profile

## Safety Considerations

Maximum 41-metre depth demands gas management discipline. Interior penetration requires wreck training and equipment. Silt can be disturbed inside the vessel, reducing visibility rapidly. Always maintain directional awareness inside the wreck. Buddy diving is mandatory.
