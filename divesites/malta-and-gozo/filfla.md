# Filfla

A nature reserve island with outstanding ray encounters — former military bombing target with unexploded ordnance on the seabed.

## Overview

Filfla is a tiny uninhabited island 5 km south of Malta, rising 60 metres above the sea with dramatic limestone cliffs. Underwater, drop-off reefs surround the island with a twin peaks reef near the islet of Filfoletta. A nature reserve since 1971 (Filfla Natural Reserve Act 1988), special permission is required to dive here — no mooring or anchoring is allowed.

The marine life is outstanding, particularly for rays — divers report 10+ rays per dive of multiple species including large stingrays free-swimming in groups. Angel sharks are present on the sandy seabed. Spiny and slipper lobsters are common. Notably absent are grouper and barracuda compared to protected areas.

Filfla was formerly used as a British military bombing target — **unexploded ordnance remains on the seabed. Do not touch any unidentified objects.**

## Site Information

- **Location**: Filfla island, 5 km south of Malta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 70 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

OUTSTANDING for rays — 10+ rays per dive of multiple species. Angel sharks on sandy bottoms. Spiny and slipper lobsters. Notable absence of grouper/barracuda. One of the best ray dive sites in the Maltese archipelago.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
