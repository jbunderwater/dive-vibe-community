## MV Xlendi

MV Xlendi was built in 1955 at Helsingør Værft in Denmark as a train ferry (1,123 GRT, approximately 77 m long, 12 m beam). The vessel underwent several name changes — MV Royal Sheeba (1987), MV Borgshorn (1988) — before operating as MV Xlendi from 1990, serving the Gozo Channel route until 1997. The Gozo Tourism Association scuttled the vessel on 12 November 1999, making it Gozo's first purpose-sunk artificial reef dive site, predating the Karwela and Cominoland by seven years.

## Overview

The Xlendi lies on its starboard side on a sandy slope, with the upward-facing port side of the hull at approximately 25–30 metres and the keel at 44 metres. The two propellers — one at each end, as the vessel was double-ended — sit at approximately 32 metres. This capsized orientation is the wreck's defining characteristic and creates a profile unlike any other site in the cluster. Wreck penetration is not recommended due to the unstable and unsafe structure of the capsized interior. The wreck is approximately 60 metres from the MV Cominoland.

## Marine Life

Large resident grouper have been documented at the Xlendi wreck. Cuttlefish and octopus are found in crevices around the hull. Amberjack school above the site during summer months.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the hull at approximately 25–30 metres and begin exploration of the exposed port-side hull. Follow the curve of the hull down to the keel at 44 metres. Note the double-ended ferry configuration — twin propellers at either end of the vessel, both accessible at around 32 metres. Do not attempt interior penetration — the capsized structure is unstable. Ascend with a safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Malta and Gozo operators serve the site. Entry by giant stride. The Xlendi is frequently combined with the adjacent Karwela and Cominoland. Nitrox is commonly used for combined deep wreck dives. Deploy an SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The wreck lies on its side — not upright — giving it a very different profile from the Karwela and Cominoland
- Penetration is NOT recommended due to the unstable capsized structure
- Both propellers are accessible at approximately 32 metres — distinctive for a double-ended ferry
- Large resident grouper are reliable subjects at this site
- This is the longest-established of the Xatt l-Ahmar wreck cluster

## Safety Considerations

At 44 metres maximum depth, this wreck pushes the advanced recreational diving limit. Gas management and NDL discipline are critical. Do not enter the capsized interior — the structure is unstable and unsafe. Always buddy dive and deploy an SMB on ascent.
