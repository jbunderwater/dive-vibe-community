## MV Xlendi

MV Xlendi was a Maltese passenger and cargo ferry named after the picturesque bay on Gozo's southwest coast, scuttled on 12 November 1999 to create an artificial reef dive site — predating the nearby Karwela and Cominoland scuttlings by seven years. She was one of the earlier examples of Malta's commitment to purposeful wreck creation as a dive tourism asset and has had the longest period to develop her marine community of the modern Maltese ferry wrecks.

## Overview

The Xlendi rests at 30–44 metres, with the main deck accessible at 30 metres and the deepest sections at 44 metres. Over 26 years on the seabed, the wreck has developed a mature marine community. The hull surfaces are thickly encrusted with sponges, hydroids, and encrusting corals, and large grouper and conger eels have established long-term territories within the vessel. The wreck sits close to the Karwela and Cominoland, and all three can theoretically be visited on a day's diving. Visibility in the area is typically 20–25 metres.

## Marine Life

Large grouper — some of impressive size — have been resident on the Xlendi for many years and are notably unafraid of divers. Conger eels of significant length occupy the lower compartments. Dense moray eel populations inhabit the pipe work and structural cavities. The outer hull is a rich macro diving environment with nudibranchs, sea slugs, and small crustaceans throughout. Barracuda and amberjack are regular visitors to the water column above.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deck at 30 metres and spend time on the exterior circuit before any penetration. The mature fish life around the exterior rewards time spent without entering the wreck. Penetration of the passenger spaces and engine room follows for those with wreck certification. The deepest accessible sections at 44 metres require careful NDL management. Ascend with a full safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Malta and Gozo operators serve the site. Entry by giant stride. The Xlendi is frequently combined with the adjacent Karwela and Cominoland. Nitrox is commonly used for these combined deep wreck dives. Deploy an SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The longest-established ferry wreck in this cluster — the marine growth is notably more mature
- Large resident grouper are the Xlendi's most famous inhabitants — approach slowly
- Wreck certification required for interior penetration
- Nitrox strongly recommended for maximising bottom time in the 30–44-metre range
- The three Malta ferry wrecks together represent a full day of world-class wreck diving

## Safety Considerations

At 44 metres maximum depth, this wreck pushes the advanced recreational diving limit. Gas management and NDL discipline are critical. The mature silt inside the wreck compartments reduces visibility rapidly if disturbed. Wreck training and equipment are required for penetration. Always buddy dive.

## Photography

26 years of marine growth on the Xlendi's hull creates a spectacular encrusted subject. The large resident grouper are among Malta's most reliably photographed large fish subjects. Interior shots require a torch and careful buoyancy. The upward-looking bow shot with the vessel silhouetted against blue water is a classic composition.
