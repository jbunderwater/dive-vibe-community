# Xatt I-Ahmar

A giant 'ice cream scoop' cavern in the cliff with a drop-off to a second cave at 34 metres — gateway to Gozo's scuttled wrecks.

## Overview

Xatt l-Aħmar (Red Bay) on Gozo's south coast features a large cavern described as "a giant ice cream scoop taken out of the cliff side" with a sandy sloping bottom. At 20 metres depth, a drop-off leads to 34–36 metres where a second cave and massive overhang are found.

The bay is also the starting point for Gozo's famous scuttled wrecks (MV Karwela, MV Cominoland, MV Xlendi, and the MT Hephaestus scuttled in 2022). The wide shallow plateau (5 metres) with large boulders and Posidonia seagrass fields provides easy shore access. Xatt l-Aħmar is the second most popular diving destination in Malta after Dwejra.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Xatt l-Aħmar (Red Bay), south coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Plentiful marine life across the site. Octopus, seahorses (on the reef), barracuda, and tuna on the deeper drop-off. The diverse habitats — reef, cavern, wall, sand — support rich biodiversity.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore across the shallow 5-metre plateau. Navigate to the 'ice cream scoop' cavern in the cliff face. Explore the sandy interior, then continue to the drop-off at 20 metres. The second cave at 34–36 metres is for experienced divers. Return via the shallow plateau. The scuttled wrecks are accessed separately from the same bay.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the bay — easy and shallow access. Medium difficulty walk to the entry point. The bay is also the departure point for wreck boat dives.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Gateway to four scuttled wrecks — MV Karwela, MV Cominoland, MV Xlendi, and MT Hephaestus
- The 'ice cream scoop' cavern formation is visually distinctive
- Look for seahorses on the shallow reef
- Second most popular dive destination in Malta after Dwejra
- Suitable for introduction dives on the shallow plateau

## Safety Considerations

The shallow plateau (5m) is suitable for all levels. The deeper cavern sections (34–36m) require Advanced certification. The bay is sheltered and conditions are generally calm.

## Photography

The distinctive cavern formation provides dramatic wide-angle subjects. The shallow reef with seahorses rewards patient macro work. The variety of environments — cavern, reef, wall, wreck — makes this a versatile photography location.
