# West Reef/Caves

Bell Tower Cave with three stacked entrances and Mouse Hole Cave — plus a diving helmet statue on the route to Um El Faroud.

## Overview

West Reef/Caves is a dive site in the Wied iż-Żurrieq area on Malta's south coast, often confused with the Blue Grotto (which is 1 km east). The site features two main caves: Bell Tower Cave, with three entrances stacked on top of each other (deepest at 27 metres), and Mouse Hole Cave (named for its narrow entrance) at 25 metres.

The reef includes drop-offs, ledges, gullies, boulders, seagrass, and sandy areas. A notable feature is a full-size mock-up of an old brass diving helmet mounted on a concrete block, resting on the sand at approximately 28 metres. The site is on the route to the Um El Faroud wreck.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Wied iż-Żurrieq, south coast Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The caves and reef support groupers, moray eels, octopus, and schools of bream. John Dory and Spanish dancers (in winter) have been reported. Hunting fish congregate in summer.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore at Wied iż-Żurrieq. Navigate to Bell Tower Cave and explore the three-tiered entrance system. Visit Mouse Hole Cave at 25 metres. Find the diving helmet statue on sand at 28 metres. The site is on the route to Um El Faroud — combine on the same dive day.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Wied iż-Żurrieq with ladders at multiple entry points. The site can also be reached by boat.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bell Tower Cave's three stacked entrances are architecturally unique
- The diving helmet statue at 28m is a local landmark — worth finding
- Mouse Hole Cave is named for its narrow entrance — not for claustrophobes
- On the route to Um El Faroud — good warm-up or second dive
- Often confused with Blue Grotto — this is 1 km west

## Safety Considerations

Intermediate level for the deeper caves (25–28 metres). The reef shallows are suitable for beginners. Watch for currents. Carry an SMB.

## Photography

Bell Tower Cave's three-tiered entrance creates unique multi-level framing compositions. The diving helmet statue makes a quirky detail shot. The cave interiors reward both wide-angle and ambient light techniques.
