# Sirens

A shallow shore-accessible training reef in St. Paul's Bay — easy access with handrails, ladders, and rock-cut pools.

## Overview

Sirens is a shallow reef close to shore in St. Paul's Bay, used primarily as a training and practice site for courses and try dives. The rocky and sandy bottom features boulders and Posidonia seagrass. The waterfront has multiple entry points with handrails, ladders, stairs, and rock-cut pools. Also good for night diving, photography practice, and navigation skills training.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. Paul's Bay, Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Moray eels, octopus, cuttlefish, scorpionfish, wrasses, and breams. The quay wall supports additional marine life.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
