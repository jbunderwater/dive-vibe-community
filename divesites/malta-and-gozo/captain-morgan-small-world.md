# Captain Morgan Small World

A former party and excursion boat operated by Captain Morgan Cruises, scuttled at 103 metres as a deep-water dive attraction — one of Malta's deepest intentionally sunk wrecks, rarely visited.

## Overview

The Captain Morgan Small World was a former passenger excursion and party vessel operated by Captain Morgan Cruises in Malta. When the vessel exceeded its operational lifespan, it was deliberately sunk as a deep-water dive attraction. The vessel size and exact scuttling date have not been confirmed from independent sources.

Resting at 103 metres depth in open sea, this is one of Malta's deepest intentionally scuttled wrecks. As of 2016, Dive Systems Malta was the only operator confirmed to have conducted recorded expeditions to the site. Tuna and John Dory have been reported in the area around the wreck. Very limited information is available about the vessel's current condition, precise location, or exact history.

Tuna and John Dory have been observed near the wreck by Dive Systems Malta. At 103 metres, the wreck provides a habitat island on the deep seabed. No site-specific marine life surveys are documented. The wider Malta/Gozo waters support grouper, moray eels, octopus, bream, barracuda, and Mediterranean corals.
