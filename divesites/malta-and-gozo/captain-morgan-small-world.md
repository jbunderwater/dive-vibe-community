# Captain Morgan Small World

A former party boat scuttled at 103 metres — one of Malta's deepest intentionally sunk wrecks, rarely dived.

## Overview

The Captain Morgan Small World was a former party and excursion boat operated by Captain Morgan Cruises in Malta. When the vessel exceeded its operational lifespan, it was intentionally sunk as a deep-water dive attraction.

Resting at 103 metres depth in open sea, this is one of Malta's deepest intentionally scuttled wrecks and is very rarely dived. As of 2016, only Dive Systems Malta had conducted recorded expeditions to the site. Tuna and John Dory fish have been reported in the area around the wreck. Very limited information is available about the vessel's current condition.

## Marine Life

Tuna and John Dory have been observed near the wreck. At 103 metres, the wreck likely provides a habitat island on the deep seabed.

## Dive Profile

Extreme technical dive at 103 metres. Very limited information about the wreck's condition or layout. Plan for a reconnaissance-style dive with maximum bottom time constraints.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Technical diving qualifications with deep trimix or CCR experience mandatory. Very few operators offer this dive.

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of Malta's deepest intentionally scuttled wrecks
- Very rarely dived — few reports on current condition
- Dive Systems Malta was the only operator known to have visited (as of 2016)
- Not managed by Heritage Malta — but technical dive support is essential

## Safety Considerations

Extreme technical dive at 103 metres. CCR or heavy trimix mandatory. Full support team required. The wreck's rarely-dived status means current conditions are largely unknown — plan for unknowns.

## Photography

The scarcity of visitors means the wreck may offer untouched photographic subjects. Extremely powerful lighting required at 103 metres. Document your findings — very few images of this wreck exist.
