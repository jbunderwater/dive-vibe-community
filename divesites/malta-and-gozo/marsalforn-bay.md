# Marsalforn Bay

Gozo's main dive hub — a shallow bay with Posidonia seagrass, rocky sections, and excellent night diving right in front of the dive shops.

## Overview

Marsalforn Bay on Gozo's north coast is a shallow training and night dive site directly in front of several dive operators in Marsalforn village. The underwater terrain features Posidonia seagrass beds, rocky sections, sandy bottom areas, and wave breaker structures at a maximum of 11–12 metres depth. The diverse terrain types in a compact shallow area make it ideal for training dives, discover scuba sessions, and particularly good night dives where flying gurnards and other nocturnal species emerge.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Marsalforn, north coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Flying gurnards, octopus, sole fish, lizardfish, mullets, and pipefish. The Posidonia meadows hide various small marine creatures that emerge at night.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent night dive site — flying gurnards and nocturnal species
- Right in front of Marsalforn dive shops — maximum convenience
- Diverse terrain (seagrass, rock, sand) in a compact area
- Primary training site for Gozo-based operators
