## Double Arch Reef

Double Arch Reef is a wall and arch dive on the northwest coast of Gozo near the Dwejra headland, named for two natural limestone arches that perforate the cliff face above and below water. The site sits in the same dramatic geological zone as Reqqa Point and Calypso Cave, where the open Mediterranean has carved the limestone into a series of arches, passages, and overhangs that define Gozo's reputation as one of the Mediterranean's premier dive destinations.

## Overview

The site features a wall descending to approximately 30 metres, interrupted by two arch formations that allow passage through the limestone reef. The arches are well-lit by natural light and are large enough to swim through comfortably. The wall surfaces are colonised by encrusting sponges and sea fans. The northwest Gozo location provides the clear, deep blue water visibility typical of this coastline — regularly 20–30 metres in settled conditions. The site can be combined with Reqqa Point for a comprehensive northwest Gozo dive day.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest Gozo, Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Variable; exposed coast position
- **Best Time**: April to November

## Marine Life

The arch interiors shelter conger eels, moray eels, and spiny lobster. Grouper occupy territories along the wall. Encrusting sponges and sea fans on the wall surface provide habitat for nudibranchs and small crustaceans. Sea bream and wrasse school in the open water above the reef. Barracuda are frequently sighted in summer in the blue water off the wall edge.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deeper arch at approximately 20–25 metres and transit through it, then ascend along the wall to the shallower arch. Explore both arch structures and the intervening wall surface for marine life. Continue along the wall top in the 8–12 metre zone before completing the dive. Safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Gozo operators access the northwest coastline. Entry by giant stride. The exposed northwest Gozo coast requires sea state assessment before diving — do not attempt in swell greater than 1 metre. Deploy an SMB on ascent for boat pickup in the open water.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Swim through both arches for the full experience — each has a distinct character
- Natural light in the arches is best when the sun is positioned behind the opening
- The wall below the arches rewards slow exploration with a torch
- Combine with Reqqa Point for a spectacular back-to-back northwest Gozo dive day
- Depth discipline is important — the wall can draw divers deeper than planned

## Safety Considerations

The northwest Gozo location is exposed to prevailing westerly swell. Assess conditions carefully. Current can run along the wall, especially on the tidal change. Monitor depth continuously on the wall face. Always carry an SMB and dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The double arch structure offers rare dual-frame wide-angle compositions. Positioning outside one arch and shooting through to the other creates depth and symmetry. Diver silhouettes in the arch openings add scale. The wall surface below the arches provides macro subjects with a torch.
