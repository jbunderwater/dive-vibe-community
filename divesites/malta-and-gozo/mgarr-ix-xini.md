# Mgarr-ix-Xini

A protected inlet ('harbour of galleons') with exceptional macro diving — seahorses, stargazers, and 80-minute dives.

## Overview

Mġarr ix-Xini (meaning 'harbour of galleons') is a protected inlet on Gozo's south coast offering some of the best macro and critter diving in Malta. The sheltered bay has a sandy bottom valley with seagrass, rocky reef walls with hiding places, and two caves along the right-hand wall at 6 and 16 metres. The exceptionally diverse marine life includes lizardfish, goatfish, seabream, scorpionfish, octopus, cuttlefish, flounder, baby rays, gurnards, seahorses, pipefish, weaver-fish, sea-hare, stargazers, and moray eels. The gentle gradient (max 15–20 metres) allows long relaxed dives — 80+ minutes have been reported. Shore entry is easy with shallow 1–2 metre initial access.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mġarr ix-Xini, south coast Gozo
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Exceptionally diverse: lizardfish, goatfish, seabream, scorpionfish, octopus, cuttlefish, flounder, baby rays, flying gurnards, seahorses, pipefish, weaver-fish, sea-hares, stargazers, moray eels. Excellent macro/critter diving.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for overhangs and cave features
- Dive within your certification limits
