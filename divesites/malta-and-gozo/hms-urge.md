# HMS Urge

A WWII U-class submarine lost with all hands in 1942 — discovered in 2019, now a protected war grave at 130 metres.

## Overview

HMS Urge was a U-class British submarine (58 metres long, 4.9-metre beam) that left Grand Harbour on 27 April 1942 bound for Alexandria, Egypt. She never arrived. A mine struck the bow, and the resulting explosion broke off the bow section. All 32 crew members and 12 passengers were lost — there were no survivors.

The wreck was discovered in 2019 and identified by Heritage Malta divers in April 2021. She stands upright on the seabed at approximately 130 metres depth with her deck gun still facing forward. The bow is badly damaged from the mine strike. Colourful fan corals have colonised the hull. The relatively small size of the U-class submarine allows a comprehensive visual survey even at extreme depth.

HMS Urge is a designated **war grave** — 44 people died aboard. Heritage Malta manages the site.

## Marine Life

Colourful fan corals have colonised the hull over 80+ years. The submarine structure provides shelter for deep-water species.

## Dive Profile

Extreme technical dive at 130 metres — among the deepest diveable wrecks in Malta. The upright submarine with forward-facing deck gun is visible on descent. The damaged bow tells the story of the fatal mine strike. Extensive decompression required. Bottom time measured in single-digit minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only. Heritage Malta permit mandatory. This is a designated war grave. Only accessible to the most experienced technical divers with appropriate CCR or heavy trimix capability.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Heritage Malta permit mandatory
- 44 people died aboard — this is a war grave requiring solemn respect
- The deck gun facing forward is the most recognisable feature
- The damaged bow shows where the mine struck
- Fan coral growth adds colour to the hull

## Safety Considerations

Extreme technical dive at 130 metres. CCR or very heavy trimix mandatory. Full technical support team required. Heritage Malta permit mandatory. This is a war grave — 44 crew and passengers lost. No artefact removal or disturbance permitted under any circumstances.

## Photography

The upright submarine with deck gun is an iconic subject at extreme depth. Fan corals provide colour. The damaged bow section documents the fatal mine strike. Requires very powerful lighting systems at 130 metres.
