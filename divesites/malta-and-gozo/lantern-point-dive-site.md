## Lantern Point (Dive Site)

Lantern Point is a reef dive on the southern tip of Comino Island, at the point where the island's limestone cliffs meet the open channel between Comino and the main island of Malta. The "lantern" refers to the small navigational light mounted on the headland above, a reference point for boats navigating the Santa Marija channel. The site is part of the cluster of Comino reef and cave dives clustered around the island's southern extremity.

## Overview

The reef descends over stepped limestone terrain to a sandy bottom at approximately 20 metres, with the point's position providing exposure to clear channel water on the southern face. The limestone surfaces are colonised by sponges, anemones, and encrusting organisms. Visibility is regularly 20–25 metres in the Comino area — among the best in the central Mediterranean. The site can be combined with Lantern Point West and other Comino sites for a full dive day in this area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Comino, Malta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–25 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate; exposed channel position
- **Best Time**: April to November

## Marine Life

Grouper occupy the deeper crevice sections at the base of the reef. Sea bream, wrasse, and barracuda school in the open water above the reef top. Moray eels and conger eels inhabit the crevices in the lower limestone. Octopus are common around the rocky terrain. The sandy areas near the reef base support goatfish and sand-dwelling flatfish.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deeper reef section at 15–20 metres and work along the southern face of the point. Explore the stepped limestone terrain and crevice zones before ascending the reef slope to the shallower reef top. The headland position means the dive can be conducted in either direction depending on conditions. Safety stop at 5 metres with an SMB deployed.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Gozo and Malta operators include Comino south as part of day trip programmes. Entry by giant stride. The channel-facing position means current should be assessed before entry. Deploy an SMB for ascent in the open water approach to the point.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check current direction on the south-facing channel side before committing to a direction
- Combine with Lantern Point West for a paired dive around the southern end of Comino
- The clear Comino channel water produces excellent natural light photography
- A torch helps with the crevice zones at the reef base
- Look carefully under the reef overhangs for resident grouper

## Safety Considerations

The channel position at Lantern Point means current can be encountered, particularly on tidal changes. Deploy an SMB on ascent. Boat traffic in the Malta–Comino channel requires careful surfacing. Always dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The clean Mediterranean water and stepped limestone terrain produce excellent wide-angle reef shots. The channel visibility allows full-reef compositional perspectives. Grouper portraits, moray eels, and schooling sea bream all make strong subjects here.
