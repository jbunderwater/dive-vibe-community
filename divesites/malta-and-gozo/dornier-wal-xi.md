# Dornier Wal XI

Remnants of a 1932 German-designed flying boat in the shallows of Gnejna Bay — a popular freediving site that may no longer exist.

## Overview

The Dornier Wal XI (registration I-AZEA) was a German-designed twin-engine flying boat (Dornier Do J, length ~17m, wingspan ~22m) built in Italy and operated by Italian airline SANA. On 16 February 1932, an engine fire forced an emergency landing in Gnejna Bay on Malta's northwest coast. Passengers and crew were rescued by HMS Brillant, and the aircraft was towed to Malta.

The wreck lies in the middle of Gnejna Bay at only 5–12 metres depth on a sandy seabed. However, only partial fuselage remains with sponsons and some cockpit controls (including a rudder pedal) were ever visible. Recent diver reports from 2025 suggest the wreck may no longer exist due to decades of deterioration and wave action. When accessible, the site was popular with freedivers and featured cuttlefish, octopuses, and rays in the surrounding area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gnejna Bay, NW coast Malta
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Generally calm in bay
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

When the wreck was intact, cuttlefish, octopuses, and rays were commonly seen in the surrounding sandy area. The shallow bay environment supports typical Mediterranean inshore species.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the shore at Gnejna Bay and swim to the middle of the bay. The wreck lies at 5–12 metres on sand. Due to advanced deterioration, little recognisable structure may remain. The shallow depth allows extended bottom time for searching the area.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Gnejna Bay beach. Easy access in calm conditions. The bay can be exposed to northwest swells.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This is NOT a WWII wreck — it's a 1932 civilian aviation accident
- The wreck may no longer exist due to wave action and deterioration
- Best combined with snorkeling or freediving in Gnejna Bay
- Check recent local diver reports before planning a dedicated trip

## Safety Considerations

Shallow and calm conditions make this a beginner-friendly site. The bay can be affected by NW swells — check conditions. No overhead environment risks.

## Photography

If any wreckage remains, the shallow depth and natural light provide good conditions for ambient light photography. The sandy bay setting with scattered remains makes for atmospheric wide-angle shots.
