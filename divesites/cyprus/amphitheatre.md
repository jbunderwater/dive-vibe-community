# Amphitheatre

A natural amphitheatre carved by sea currents at 9 metres — one of the most popular dive sites in Paphos, with dramatic arches and Atlantic Triton shells.

## Overview

The Amphitheatre takes its name from a natural rock formation carved by sea currents at approximately 9 metres depth, shaped like a classical amphitheatre. Surrounding it are rock formations, holes, arches, and overhangs, with a second amphitheatre and a 'stage' area featuring a skylight opening. The site ranges from 6 to 22 metres. Shore entry is approximately a 10-minute drive from Paphos dive centres.

A notable marine feature is the Atlantic Triton's Trumpet (Charonia tritonis) — a large sea snail whose shells can reach 45 cm, among the largest shells in the Mediterranean. They are found along the walls of the formation.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Coral Bay area, Paphos, Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

Groupers, moray eels, trumpet fish, octopus, cuttlefish, flounders, sea bream, and wrasse have been reported at this site. Atlantic Triton's Trumpet shells (up to 45 cm) are a distinctive find along the rock walls.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The natural amphitheatre at 9m is the signature feature
- Atlantic Triton's Trumpet shells can reach 45cm here — notable for the Mediterranean
- A second amphitheatre and 'stage' with skylight opening add variety
- Shore entry approximately 10 minutes from Paphos dive centres
