# Amphitheatre

A natural amphitheatre carved by sea currents at 9 metres — one of the most popular dive sites in Paphos with arches and Atlantic Triton shells.

## Overview

The Amphitheatre is one of the most popular diving sites in Paphos. A natural amphitheatre formation carved by sea currents at approximately 9 metres depth is surrounded by rock formations, holes, arches, and overhangs. The site also features a second amphitheatre and a "stage" area with a skylight opening. Atlantic Triton's Trumpet shells (up to 45 cm) have been found here — among the largest shells in the Mediterranean. Maximum depth is 22 metres. Located a 10-minute drive from Paphos dive centres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Coral Bay area, Paphos, Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

Groupers, yellow-spotted moray eels, flute fish, trumpet fish, octopus, cuttlefish, flounders, sea bream, wrasse, and blennies. Atlantic Triton's Trumpet shells (up to 45 cm) are a notable find.

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of the most popular Paphos dive sites
- The natural amphitheatre at 9m is the signature feature
- Atlantic Triton's Trumpet shells here reach 45cm — impressive
- A second amphitheatre and 'stage' area add variety
- 10 minutes from Paphos dive centres
