# White River

A 25–30 metre swim-through shaped by ancient river erosion — distinctive geological formations on the northwest Paphos coast.

## Overview

White River features a prominent swim-through described as 25–30 metres long (sources vary slightly), formed by ancient river erosion that carved gullies, overhangs, and curved rock faces into the seabed. Natural light illuminates the passage, making it suited for photography. The site is on the northwest Paphos coast near the Akamas Peninsula and is accessed from shore.

Maximum depth is 12 metres. The geological character — eroded river-bed topography — distinguishes this site from the typical cave formations further north near Cape Greco.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest Paphos coast, Cyprus (near Akamas Peninsula)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine life reports found. Regional Mediterranean fauna applies.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Natural light illuminates the swim-through — a torch is useful for overhangs but not essential
- Check sea conditions before entry — shore entry can be affected by swell
- Dive within your certification limits
