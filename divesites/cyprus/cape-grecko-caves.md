# Cape Grecko Caves

Over a dozen naturally illuminated caves in Cape Greco National Park — including The Cathedral, Love Bridge, and the Blue Room.

## Overview

Cape Greco Caves within the National Forest Park feature a network of natural caves, tunnels, and swim-throughs carved into the lava rock coastline. Most cave passages sit between 3–8 metres depth, with some extending to 12–15 metres. All named passages are naturally illuminated — no cave diving training is required for the standard routes.

Named features confirmed by multiple sources include:
- **The Cathedral**: A larger cavern with light shafts entering through cracks above
- **Love Bridge Cave**: A swim-through passage beneath the Love Bridge natural rock arch (a surface landmark near the car park)
- **Blue Room**: Named for the ethereal blue light filling the chamber

Shore entry is from rocky platforms near the Love Bridge car park, requiring some scrambling over rock and reef shoes. Boat access from Ayia Napa dive operators is also available.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cape Greco National Park, east Cyprus (near Love Bridge car park)
- **Entry Type**: Shore or boat
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: May–October (calmest conditions); April–November diveable

## Marine Life

Damselfish, painted combers, octopus, sea urchins, and moray eels are confirmed at this site. No large pelagic species are typical — this site is notable for its geological environment rather than big-animal encounters.

## Tips and Recommendations

- All named cave passages are naturally lit — safe for Open Water divers with cavern/swim-through experience
- Rocky shore entry from Love Bridge car park area — reef shoes essential
- Over a dozen cave passages in the area — multiple dives needed for comprehensive exploration
- The Cathedral, Love Bridge Cave, and Blue Room are the three most distinctive named features
