# Liberty Wreck

A small Russian cargo vessel scuttled in 2009 — the first artificial reef wreck in the Protaras area, now thriving with marine life.

## Overview

The Liberty Wreck is a small Russian cargo vessel (originally carried frozen products), 37 metres long and 10 metres wide. Purposely sunk in May 2009 as the first artificial reef wreck in the Protaras area. She sits upright at 27–28 metres depth with the shallowest point at 22 metres, approximately 1 km from shore.

Features include two easy swim-throughs via door-like openings on deck and a large cargo loading hatch. Located just 90 metres from the Nemesis Wreck. The area is closed to boat traffic to allow the marine ecosystem to flourish.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Protaras, east Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–40 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

Groupers, moray eels, jacks (seasonally), turtles, rays, nudibranchs, triggerfish, and octopus. The closed boat traffic area supports a thriving ecosystem.

## Tips and Recommendations

- First artificial reef wreck in Protaras — scuttled 2009
- Nemesis Wreck is just 90m away — combine on one dive
- Area closed to boat traffic — healthier marine ecosystem
- Advanced Open Water with Nitrox recommended by local operators
