# Dinosaur Bay

Underwater rock formations resembling dinosaur bones — swim over them to find caves and sinkholes beyond.

## Overview

Dinosaur Bay near Peyia is named for its distinctive underwater rock formations that resemble dinosaur bones. Divers swim out over these bone-shaped rocks, then find lots of small caves and sinkholes to explore beyond. Navigation can be tricky on the return as the terrain becomes flat and featureless — taking a compass bearing on the way out is recommended. Shore entry is from a semi-circular bay (turning opposite Spilies bar).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Peyia, Paphos, Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

Mediterranean reef species in the cave and sinkhole habitats.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for cave and overhang exploration
- Dive within your certification limits
