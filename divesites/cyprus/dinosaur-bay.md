# Dinosaur Bay

Named for distinctive bone-shaped rock formations — with small caves and sinkholes beyond and navigational challenges on the return.

## Overview

Dinosaur Bay near Peyia (in the Paphos area) is named for its distinctive underwater rock formations described as resembling dinosaur bones. Divers swim out over these formations and then find small caves and sinkholes to explore beyond. The site ranges from approximately 5 to 20 metres depth and is accessible from shore (entry from a semi-circular bay).

Dive operators note that navigation on the return can be tricky once the distinctive bone-like formations are behind you, as the terrain becomes relatively flat and featureless. Taking a compass bearing on the way out is recommended. DiveAdvisor lists this as suitable for more experienced divers in the Paphos area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Peyia, near Paphos, Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine life reports found. Mediterranean cave and crevice species regional to the Paphos coast apply.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Take a compass bearing on entry — navigation back can be disorienting
- Small caves and sinkholes beyond the main formations reward exploration
- The distinctive bone-shaped rocks are the landmark for orientation
