# Costandis

A Soviet trawler scuttled in 2014 alongside a passenger vessel — home to a friendly resident grouper nicknamed 'George.'

## Overview

The Costandis was originally a Soviet bottom trawler named Zolotets, built in 1989 in the USSR, approximately 30 metres long. Registered on the Cyprus Ships Register in 1997, she was intentionally sunk on 22 February 2014 in the Dasoudi Marine Protected Area off Limassol as an artificial reef, alongside the passenger vessel Lady Thetis (which sits nearby at 18 metres).

The wreck sits upright with open spaces created for easy swim-throughs of the engine room, navigational bridge, and decks. She is considered an ideal introduction to wreck diving. A notable resident is a large friendly grouper nicknamed "George" who approaches divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dasoudi Marine Protected Area, off Limassol, Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

Huge shoals of amberjacks, two-banded bream, saddle bream, damselfish, trumpet fish, morays, turtles, and occasional dolphins. A large friendly resident grouper nicknamed 'George' approaches divers regularly.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Look for 'George' the friendly resident grouper
- Lady Thetis wreck sits nearby at 18m — two wrecks on one dive
- Prepared with easy swim-throughs — ideal introduction to wreck diving
- In the Dasoudi Marine Protected Area — healthy marine life
