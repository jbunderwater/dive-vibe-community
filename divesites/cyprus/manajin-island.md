# Manajin Island

A small island you can dive all the way around — with old masonry, statues, and a blowhole 'champagne cork' exit.

## Overview

Manajin Island is a small island near Paphos that is compact enough to dive multi-level all the way around. The seabed is strewn with old masonry, statues, and other artefacts. Features include a rocky bottom with swim-throughs, a sandy patch, pink Peyssonnelia algae, a bowl formation, and a notable blowhole exit that creates a 'champagne cork' effect. Depth ranges from 6 to 25 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Paphos, Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

Typical Paphos reef species. The varied terrain with old masonry provides habitat for groupers, octopus, and moray eels.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check conditions with local operators before diving
- Carry a torch for cave and overhang exploration
- Dive within your certification limits
