# Manajin Island

A compact island you can dive all the way around — with old masonry artefacts, swim-throughs, and a blowhole 'champagne cork' exit.

## Overview

Manajin Island (also spelled Manijin Island) is a small island near Paphos compact enough to circumnavigate in a single multi-level dive. The seabed around the island is strewn with old masonry and artefacts. Features include a rocky bottom with swim-throughs, a sandy patch, pink Peyssonnelia algae, a bowl formation, and a notable blowhole exit described by dive operators as a 'champagne cork' effect. Depth ranges from 6 to 25 metres, with shore entry accessible from the nearby coast. Multiple operators in the Paphos area list this as a standard site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Paphos, Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef/rock formation
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

The varied terrain with old masonry provides habitat for groupers, octopus, and moray eels. No site-specific species surveys available.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Compact enough to dive all the way around in one dive
- The blowhole 'champagne cork' exit is a unique feature — ask operators for the route
- Old masonry scattered on the seabed makes this a distinctive site among Paphos reefs
