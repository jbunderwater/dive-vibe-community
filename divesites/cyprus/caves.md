# Caves

A network of well-illuminated caves and tunnels in Cape Greco National Park — visibility regularly exceeds 30 metres.

## Overview

The Caves at Cape Greco National Park feature a network of natural underwater caves and tunnels off the cliffs. Caves reach lengths of up to 30 metres but are remarkably well-illuminated, eliminating the need for torches. Features include swim-throughs, the Horse Shoe Cave, and the Blow Hole exit. The terrain includes lava rock formations. Visibility typically 25–40 metres — some of the clearest water in Cyprus.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cape Greco National Park, east Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (exceptional)
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

Bream, damselfish, blue-spotted cornet fish, jacks, lionfish, bearded fire worms, ornate wrasse, groupers, octopus, moray eels, and sea turtles.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Caves are well-illuminated — torches not essential but recommended
- Horse Shoe Cave and Blow Hole exit are highlights
- Some of the clearest water in Cyprus — 25–40m visibility
- Shore entry with rocky giant stride
