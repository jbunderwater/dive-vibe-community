# Caves

A network of well-illuminated caves and tunnels in Cape Greco National Park — exceptional visibility and a Blow Hole exit.

## Overview

The Caves at Cape Greco National Park feature a network of natural underwater caves and tunnels carved into the lava rock cliffs. Caves reach lengths of up to 30 metres but remain well-illuminated with natural light entering through cracks and openings, making them accessible without specialist cave diving training. Named features within the area include the Horse Shoe Cave and a Blow Hole exit. Visibility at this site is consistently among the best in Cyprus, typically 25–40 metres.

Shore entry requires a rocky giant stride from the cliff platforms. The site is part of the Cape Greco area that also includes the Cape Grecko Caves site a short distance away.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cape Greco National Park, east Cyprus
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (exceptional)
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: March to November

## Marine Life

Bream, damselfish, cornet fish, jacks, lionfish, groupers, octopus, moray eels, and sea turtles have been reported at the site. Bearded fire worms are present in the cave habitats.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Cave passages are naturally lit — torches useful for darker sections but not essential for the main passages
- Horse Shoe Cave and the Blow Hole exit are signature features
- Among the clearest water in Cyprus — 25–40m visibility typical
- Rocky shore entry — reef shoes and controlled giant stride recommended
