## Mana Crack

Mana Crack is an 11-mile sunken fringing reef off the Na Pali Coast — the furthest offshore dive site in the state of Hawaii — always dived as a live-boat drift due to strong currents and the absence of any moorings.

## Overview

Mana Crack runs along the Na Pali coastline as a lengthy submerged reef with many distinct drop points. Operators select different sections for the first and second dives. The first dive typically runs 60–100 feet (18–30m); the second dive is done shallower at 45–80 feet (14–24m) on a different drop point, with a 90-minute surface interval in between spent exploring the Na Pali coast's sea caves, waterfalls, and hidden beaches.

The wall itself features massive black coral stalks swaying in the current and ancient coral formations with deep crevices. There are no moorings anywhere along Mana Crack, so all dives are live-boat entries: divers drop into the water, drift with the current along the reef, and are picked up by the support vessel downcurrent. ScubaBoard contributors describe the site as "the 'Niihau of Kauai'" for its relative remoteness and concentration of large fish species compared to the south shore sites.

## Site Information

- **Location**: 11-mile sunken fringing reef along the Na Pali Coast, Kauai (west/northwest shore)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (live boat; no moorings)
- **Site Type**: Wall (sunken fringing reef with drift)
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (live boat only, strong currents, remote location)
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters (100 feet, first dive; 24m/80 feet second dive)
- **Depth Range**: First dive 60–100 feet (18–30m); second dive 45–80 feet (14–24m)
- **Water Temperature**: 24–26°C (75–79°F)

## Marine Life

Confirmed species from operator sites and diver reports include: large barracuda, spotted eagle rays, grey reef sharks, white-tip reef sharks, java moray eels, rainbow runners, stripe-belly puffers, black surgeonfish, and wringtail wrasse. ScubaBoard contributors report grouper (mu), trevally (ulua), and the rare spotted knifejaw. Hammerhead sharks are cited by Explore Kauai Scuba as a possible encounter. Spinner dolphins are reported as nearly guaranteed sightings on the transit. Humpback whales are commonly encountered during winter months (November–April).

Mana Crack is also noted as an excellent invertebrate site — nudibranchs are plentiful, though operators humorously caution divers not to stare at macro life for too long given the volume of large fish passing through.

## Tips

- A full-day charter (approximately 8 hours) is the standard format; budget accordingly
- Sea conditions can be rough on the open-water crossing; consider seasickness precautions
- Live boat diving requires practiced buoy/DSMB deployment for end-of-dive pickup
