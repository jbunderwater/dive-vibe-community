## General Store

General Store is a South Shore boat dive named for its historically "well-stocked" fish life, built around a horseshoe-shaped lava inlet that shelters the remains of the steam schooner Pele — one of Kauai's few accessible historic wrecks.

## Overview

The General Store site sits in a horseshoe inlet with small lava tubes on either side, providing structure for a large variety of fish species to shelter. Depths run 65–90 feet inside the inlet, with open water outside exceeding 90 feet. The site is frequently dived as a current-influenced drift, though the inlet itself provides a refuge when surface currents pick up.

The wreck remains of the steam schooner Pele — which struck the sunken rock Kalanipuao on March 24, 1895, while carrying 200 tons of coal and general freight from Koloa to Makaweli — lie at this site. No lives were lost in the sinking. After more than 130 years underwater, very little of the vessel remains: the boiler, scattered hull plates, and a couple of anchors are the identifiable remnants. The name "General Store" predates common use of the wreck as the primary draw; the site is primarily dived for its abundant fish and macro life.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Poipu area, South Shore, Kauai
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck (steam schooner Pele, 1895) within lava reef system
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters (90 feet, outer edge of inlet)
- **Depth Range**: 65–90 feet (20–27m)
- **Water Temperature**: 24–27°C (75–81°F)

## Wreck Details

- **Vessel**: Steam schooner Pele
- **Sank**: March 24, 1895
- **Cause**: Struck sunken rock Kalanipuao, approximately three-quarters of a mile from shore between Koloa and Eleele
- **Remains**: Boiler, hull plates, anchors; minimal structural integrity after 130+ years

Sources: The Garden Island newspaper (2000 historical ships list and 2015 dedicated article on the Pele) confirm the vessel name, sinking date, and cause. Tonnage and vessel dimensions are not confirmed in available sources.

## Marine Life

Whiskered boarfish are seen here on a regular basis (Fathom Five notes this as one of their consistent sightings). White-tip reef sharks, sea turtles, and octopus are common. Lemon butterflyfish, milletseed butterflyfish, pennant butterflyfish, and helmet conch are typical reef fish at this site. Black coral stands attract wrasse and parrotfish. Lobsters and large crabs occupy the small lava caves. Dolphins are frequently reported passing through the outer water column.
