## Kukuiula Bay

Kukuiula Harbor's sheltered horseshoe bay offers a calm, beginner-friendly shore dive on Kauai's South Shore, with abundant marine life and on-site facilities — and doubles as the primary boat launch for south shore and Niihau charters.

## Overview

The harbor's protective breakwater keeps large swells out of the bay, creating some of the most consistently calm water for shore diving on Kauai's south coast. The large horseshoe-shaped bay provides two distinct dive zones within the same entry point, making it well-suited to a two-tank shore excursion. Maximum depth reaches approximately 50 feet (15m) in the deeper sections of the bay.

Fathom Five Divers operates guided shore dives from this site, noting that the reef here is large enough to sustain multiple dives without repeating the same ground. The harbor also serves as the departure point for all south shore boat charters and Niihau day trips (May–September).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Poipu area, 12 miles southwest of Lihue Airport, South Shore, Kauai (between Spouting Horn and Po'ipu Beach)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 25–28°C (77–82°F)
- **Facilities**: Restrooms, showers, picnic tables; no lifeguard

## Marine Life

Sea turtles, moray eels, and large schools of reef fish are confirmed by Fathom Five and local divers as regular residents of the bay. The protected waters attract species typical of Kauai's south shore reef community. Specific species documentation at this site beyond the above is limited in available sources.
