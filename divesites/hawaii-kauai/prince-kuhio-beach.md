## Prince Kuhio Beach

Prince Kuhio Beach (also called Prince Kuhio Park) is a very shallow south shore shore dive best known for its reliable sea turtle encounters in 3–21 feet of water.

## Overview

The entry is a rocky lava shore — booties or reef shoes are essential. Divers step off the lava into approximately 2 feet of water, put on fins, and proceed to the reef. The main reef area is a long swim from the entry point. The site is very shallow throughout: maximum depth runs 3–21 feet (1–6m), with most of the diving done in less than 10 feet. The Scuba Directory notes that the reef is not particularly fish-rich or coral-dense compared to nearby sites, but the site's appeal is primarily the reliable presence of Hawaiian green sea turtles resting and grazing in the shallows.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Koloa, South Shore, Kauai (approximately 4.8 miles south of Koloa town on Poipu Road west fork)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 6 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 25–28°C (77–82°F)

## Marine Life

Hawaiian green sea turtles are the primary draw and are frequently observed resting and feeding in the shallows. Whitemouth moray eels, trumpetfish, and Picasso triggerfish (humuhumunukunukuapua'a) are confirmed at this site. General reef fish are present, though fish density and coral coverage are described by The Scuba Directory as limited compared to nearby Koloa Landing.

## Tips

- Bring reef shoes or booties — the lava entry is rough
- The reef is a long surface swim from the entry; plan your air accordingly
- Suitable as a warm-up dive alongside a Koloa Landing trip rather than a primary destination
