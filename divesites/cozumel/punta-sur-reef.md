# Punta Sur Reef

Cozumel's southernmost dive, an advanced wall and cavern system where dramatic geography meets strong open-ocean currents.

## Overview

Punta Sur sits at the southern tip of Cozumel, where the island's wall system meets the open Caribbean and currents converge from multiple directions. The site is characterised by a vertical wall dropping well past sport diving limits, intersected by a famous cavern system known as Punta Sur Caves — a series of connected chambers with names like "The Cathedral" that create dramatic, light-filled interiors. The surrounding reef is in exceptional condition due to its remote location and reduced dive traffic compared to the central sites. Visibility regularly exceeds 30 meters, and the siting at the exposed tip means strong current is the norm rather than the exception. This is not a site for beginners; local divemasters are essential to navigate the unpredictable current patterns.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern tip of Cozumel, Quintana Roo, Mexico
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (80–130 feet)
- **Current**: Strong and variable — exposed headland with converging currents
- **Best Time**: November to April; summer months can have stronger, less predictable currents

## Marine Life

The open-ocean positioning attracts large pelagics that don't appear at calmer central sites. Spotted eagle rays are frequently encountered in groups at depth. Caribbean reef sharks patrol the wall edge, and the occasional nurse shark rests in sand channels inside the caverns. Hawksbill turtles nest on the adjacent beaches and are reliably seen underwater. Large midnight parrotfish and rainbow parrotfish forage along the coral. The wall sponge community is exceptional — massive azure vase sponges and orange elephant ear sponges create a riot of color. Schools of Atlantic spadefish and horse-eye jacks congregate in the water column at the wall edge.

## Dive Profile

Punta Sur requires a precisely timed boat entry to match the current cycle. Descend quickly once in the water to avoid being swept off the reef by surface currents. The divemaster will lead you along the wall and into the cavern entrances — the Cathedral chamber requires care but is passable without specialist cave training as it has natural light throughout. Maximum depth on the wall is 35 meters; most divers spend the majority of their time between 18 and 28 meters in the cavern sections. Ascent is typically done in the water column away from the wall with an SMB due to current.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by boat only — a lengthy ride from the main dive pier in San Miguel, often taking 30–45 minutes. Only operators with solid familiarity with Punta Sur's current patterns should be chosen. Boat captains will position dynamically during the dive and monitor for current shifts. An SMB is absolutely required for the ascent.

## Tips

- Discuss current conditions thoroughly in the briefing before descending
- The Cathedral cavern is the highlight — ensure your air budget includes time to explore it
- Stay close to your divemaster on this site; current can disorient even experienced divers
- Carry both an SMB and a whistle — surface pickups here can be complicated
- Macro photographers can find unusual nudibranchs and cleaning stations on the wall sponges

## Safety

This is an advanced site requiring strong buoyancy control and current-diving experience. Attempting to fight Punta Sur's currents will rapidly exhaust your air and create risk. If you arrive and conditions exceed your experience level, abort — no dive is worth an emergency at a site this remote from recompression facilities. The recompression chamber in San Miguel is approximately 45 minutes away by boat. Ensure your dive insurance is current before diving this site.

## Photography

The Cathedral chamber interior is one of Cozumel's most dramatic photo subjects — a wide-angle lens captures the shafts of natural light filtering through openings with divers silhouetted against the blue. The wall's sponge life rewards macro shooters who can manage buoyancy in current. Eagle rays at depth appear against the deep blue open water — bring a fast lens and be ready to react quickly.
