# El Islote

A deep-south Cozumel reef near the Punta Sur ecotourist zone, offering pristine coral in one of the least-visited sections of the island's dive corridor.

## Overview

El Islote (The Islet) is located at the southern end of Cozumel's leeward reef system, close to the Punta Sur ecological reserve. The site name likely refers to a prominent isolated coral formation that serves as a navigation landmark. The reef system here benefits from being adjacent to one of Cozumel's most protected zones — boat traffic is lower than at central sites, and the coral formations are in corresponding health. The 20-meter maximum depth and moderate currents make this accessible to intermediate divers making the longer boat trip to the south. The site serves as an alternative or complement to the more famous Palancar sections for divers wanting something less trafficked.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Deep south Cozumel, near Punta Sur, Quintana Roo, Mexico
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (80–130 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate southward drift near the point
- **Best Time**: November to April; summer conditions at the southern tip can be unpredictable

## Marine Life

The isolation of the deep south reefs produces exceptional wildlife density. Spotted eagle rays are extremely common in this zone, frequently appearing in small groups near the sandy channels. Caribbean reef sharks are regular visitors from the outer wall. Hawksbill turtles nest on nearby Punta Sur beaches and dive throughout these southern reefs; sightings are almost guaranteed. The deep south sponge fauna is extraordinary — massive barrel sponges over a meter in diameter, giant azure vase sponges, and tube sponge aggregations create a habitat as visually rich as the coral itself. Moray eels are abundant in larger reef structures.

## Dive Profile

The deeper south location and moderate current require confident buoyancy and navigation skills. The dive typically follows the reef top at 8–15 meters before descending along prominent coral formations to the 20-meter maximum. The current in the southern zone tends to sweep divers along the reef more decisively than central sites — plan your route along rather than against it. End the dive with a gradual ascent to the crest for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Long boat ride from San Miguel — 35–50 minutes. Operators serving the southern sites are specialists with Punta Sur-area knowledge. Expect full drift-dive protocol including mandatory SMB deployment and water-column ascents.

## Tips

- Budget the longer travel time — the dive quality in this underpopulated southern zone justifies the additional boat time
- The sponge formations here rival Palancar's in size and variety; bring appropriate lens choices for wide-angle sponge compositions
- Scout the reef edge seaward for eagle ray sightings before committing to the deeper sections
- Southern sites are often better visited in the afternoon when conditions at the northern and central sites are more crowded
- Pairing El Islote with Chun Chacaaab Reef makes an excellent full southern day

## Safety

The remote position of this site magnifies the standard safety considerations for Cozumel drift diving. Emergency response time from San Miguel is extended. All divers should carry a fully inflated SMB throughout the dive, ready to deploy without hesitation. Current assessment by the boat captain before entry is critical. Recompression chamber access requires a 40–50 minute boat return to San Miguel.

## Photography

The southern Cozumel reefs offer some of the most dramatic sponge photography in the Caribbean. Massive barrel sponges photographed from below with a diver silhouetted against the surface light create striking compositional images. Eagle ray groups near the sandy channels reward pre-positioned wide-angle shooting. The exceptional visibility in the southern reef zone — regularly exceeding 35 meters — allows for unusually clean backgrounds in all types of photography.
