# El Islote

An Advanced-rated southeast Cozumel reef site near the Punta Celarain lighthouse — a large patch coral island formation with strong currents and depths to 24 meters, outside the usual leeward dive corridor.

## Overview

El Islote ("The Islet") is positioned on the southeastern end of Cozumel near the Punta Celarain lighthouse, beyond the main concentration of leeward dive sites. Sources describe it as a large patch coral island — broad ridges of pristine hard and soft coral separated by sandy channels — with depths ranging from 18 to 40 meters and strong current conditions. The remote location relative to San Miguel and the Advanced difficulty rating mean far fewer dive boats visit compared to leeward sites, leaving the coral in good condition. Operators rate this Advanced due to current strength and depth.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southeast Cozumel, near Punta Celarain lighthouse, Quintana Roo, Mexico
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Good when conditions allow
- **Current**: Strong — primary reason for Advanced rating
- **Best Time**: When current conditions are assessed as manageable; consult operator

## Marine Life

Large pelagics, sharks, and eagle rays have been reported at El Islote due to its open-ocean exposure and stronger currents. The coral ridges and sandy channels support typical Caribbean reef species. Sources note "good" marine life ratings. Specific species confirmed at this exact site are limited by available source data.

## Dive Profile

The dive follows the broad coral ridges with the current dictating pace and direction. Depth range of 18–40 meters means careful dive planning is required; recreational divers should set conservative turn pressures. The sandy channels between ridges provide navigation structure. SMB deployment is essential before ascending due to the remote location and surface current.

## Entry and Exit

Long boat trip from San Miguel — among the longest transits for Cozumel dive sites due to the southeast location (approximately 45–60 minutes). Drift dive protocol with SMB ascent applies. The captain must assess current conditions before entry.

## Tips

- Confirm current conditions with your operator before booking; this site is dived selectively
- Advanced certification and current-diving experience are genuine prerequisites
- The pristine condition of the coral, consequence of lower diver traffic, rewards those who make the effort

## Safety

Strong currents and the remote location combine to make this one of Cozumel's more demanding sites. Emergency response from San Miguel takes significantly longer than at leeward sites. All divers must carry SMBs and audible signalling devices. Do not attempt this site without a guide familiar with southeast Cozumel current patterns.
