## Manta divesite

Manta divesite is a sandy reef plateau at 18–21 m off Nosy Be, known primarily as a cleaning station where manta rays congregate to be serviced by cleaner wrasse.

## Overview

This site sits on a shallow sandy seabed scattered with coral blocks and small reef patches. Manta rays visit the cleaning stations here to have parasites removed by wrasse, and divers settle on or near the bottom to observe from below as the rays circle overhead.

Scuba Nosy Be describes the site as bottoming out at 18–21 m with small reefs dotted throughout providing habitat for cleaner wrasse. Manta rays are present year-round, and mobula rays are also frequently reported at the site. Groupers, turtles, and tuna are regularly seen in the area. Visibility is generally good when currents are mild.

Note on depth: one source (urlaub-auf-madagaskar.com) places a manta cleaning station at 25–28 m in the same general area; it is unclear whether this refers to the same site or a nearby location. The 18–21 m figure is consistent with data from Scuba Nosy Be and the site's OSM coordinates.

The site is accessible to Intermediate divers. Conditions are generally calm, but the boat ride requires open-water comfort.
