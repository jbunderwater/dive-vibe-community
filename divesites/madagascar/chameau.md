## Chameau

Chameau ("Camel") is a two-hump shoal approximately 14 kilometres offshore from Nosy Be, named for its distinctive underwater profile. A small unnamed vessel was deliberately sunk on the northwest side as an artificial reef (reported by the local operator as April 2007; this date has not been independently verified).

## Overview

The site takes its name from the two-hump shape of the shoal, which lies precisely along the SE-to-NW tidal current axis. The standard dive profile runs with a falling tide: divers descend to the southeast end of the first hump at around 22 metres, then ascend to 13 metres as the current carries them to the second hump. Reef passages between the humps are lined with gorgonians in the shallower sections.

At high tide with a northwest current, a deeper route starts at approximately 33 metres on a northern shoal, tracking southeast past the reef passages and the wreck exterior before finishing on the second hump.

The artificial reef on the northwest side — a small fishing vessel sunk by the local operator — sits at around 30–33 metres and is accessible only during the high-tide deep-route profile. The vessel has no recorded name, build date, or sinking history in any published source beyond the operator's own dive site description. An independent 2013 diver trip report with photographs confirms the wreck exists and is clearly visible on the seafloor, describing it as a small fishing vessel.

Confirmed marine life from diver reports: lobsters, sea turtles, and lionfish at depth. The reef sections support gorgonians and barracuda. The wreck interior should not be assumed to be safely penetrable without appropriate training and equipment.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Nosy Be, northwest Madagascar (approximately 14 km offshore)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
