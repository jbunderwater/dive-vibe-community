## Chameau

Chameau ("Camel" in French) is a current-fed reef shoal about 14 km offshore from Nosy Be, named for its distinctive double-hump formation, with a secondary wreck dive on its northwest flank.

## Overview

Chameau is a shoal formed by two distinctive humps whose orientation runs with the prevailing tidal current, southeast to northwest. The standard dive profile follows a falling tide: starting at 22 metres on the first (southeast) hump and drifting progressively shallower to 13 metres on the second. An alternative high-tide profile begins at 33 metres on the northwest side, where divers explore the exterior of a small vessel sunk in April 2007 before riding the current across to the second hump. Gorgonian sea fans are present on the upper reef sections. The site's current-dependent nature requires intermediate-level buoyancy control and tidal awareness. Navigation is straightforward once the tidal direction is understood.

### Wreck Component

A fishing vessel was intentionally sunk in April 2007 on the northwest side of the Chameau formation at approximately 33 metres. Multiple sources confirm this sinking date and location. The vessel's name, length, and tonnage are not documented in available sources and are not stated here. The wreck serves as a secondary dive objective: a large Napoleon wrasse is reported to have been resident in the boat prior to sinking. The wreck can only be reached at high tide when a north-west current is running. The wreck exterior can be viewed; penetration capability is not documented by sources consulted.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Madagascar, Africa (Nosy Be area, ~14 km offshore)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters

## Marine Life

Sources confirm at the Chameau site: lobsters, sea turtles, lionfish, jacks, angelfish, crocodilefish, blue-spotted stingrays, nudibranchs, leaffish, scorpionfish, and white-tip reef sharks. Large schools of fish are associated with the wreck. Gorgonian sea fans are present on the reef sections. Whale shark sightings are noted as an occasional possibility at this offshore location but are not guaranteed.
