## Nosy Tanikely

Nosy Tanikely is Madagascar's best-known beginner dive site, a protected marine reserve 15 minutes by boat from Nosy Be with some of the region's highest coral diversity.

## Overview

Nosy Tanikely is a tiny volcanic island (approximately 550 × 450 metres) surrounded by a marine and coastal protected area. The reserve is commonly described as a natural aquarium — coral in excellent condition, exceptional visibility of 10–30 metres, and abundant marine life accessible to divers of all experience levels. Three distinct dive sites ring the island: the north face ("Petit pain de sucre"), which features gorgonians and trevallies in a gentle drift at 15–24 metres; the south face ("Small Drop-off"), a drift through beautiful coral architecture with blue-spotted stingrays and occasional guitar rays to 20 metres; and the east "beach" side, a shallow dive to 20 metres with hawksbill turtles, groupers, scorpionfish, moray eels, and parrotfish. Turtle encounters are considered near-certain at this site. Leopard sharks and whitetip reef sharks are present on the north and south faces.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Nosy Tanikely Marine Reserve, south of Nosy Be, Madagascar
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef (marine reserve)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 23 meters
