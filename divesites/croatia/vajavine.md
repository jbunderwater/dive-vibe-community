## Vajavine

Vajavine is a house reef wall dive at Vrbnik on Krk Island, also known in English as "100 Steps" — a reference to the 100 stairs descending from the town to the sea, where a dive ladder provides water entry. The site is operated as a house reef by the dive centres at Vrbnik and is accessible by shuttle bus from the facilities. The name *Vajavine* refers to the bay itself.

## Overview

The dive ladder at the base of the 100 steps provides a direct entry to a colourfully covered steep rock face that drops to 30 metres and beyond. The wall sections are described by local operators as having varied crustaceans, octopus, schooling fish, and occasional seahorses. A one-way dive from Vajavine to Silent Beach is a popular option offered by the local dive centres. Despite the shore access via stairs, the diving itself is a quality wall dive in the clear Kvarner water.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Vajavine Bay (100 Steps), Vrbnik, Krk Island, Kvarner, Croatia
- **Entry Type**: Shore dive (via dive ladder at base of 100 stairs)
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30+ meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters
- **Current**: Variable; check locally
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Local dive operators report: various crustaceans, octopus, and schooling fish on the steep face sections. Occasional seahorse sightings. No additional site-specific species claims made.

## Dive Profile

Entry via dive ladder at the base of the 100 steps. Descend onto the steep wall face and explore down to 30 metres. A one-way dive to Silent Beach can be arranged with the operator. Ascend slowly along the wall face and complete a safety stop at 5 metres. Deploy an SMB for boat pickup if doing the one-way option.

## Entry and Exit

Shuttle bus from the Vrbnik dive centre to the site. Entry and exit via the dive ladder. If completing the one-way dive to Silent Beach, coordinate pickup with the dive centre beforehand.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The one-way dive to Silent Beach is a popular programme — check availability with the dive centre when booking
- A torch enhances the colours of the sponge-covered steep face sections
- The stairs (100 steps) require reasonable fitness — factor this into planning with less mobile divers
- The site pairs naturally with the adjacent Vrbnik Cave and Silent Beach for a multi-site day

## Safety Considerations

The wall extends to 30m and beyond — maintain awareness of depth. Current can be present. Always dive with a buddy and carry an SMB. Entry and exit via the dive ladder requires composure with equipment in choppy conditions.

## Photography

The colourful steep face with crustaceans and octopus provides strong wide-angle and macro subjects. The clear Kvarner water allows effective strobe work. The one-way option to Silent Beach allows a long photographic dive on the wall.
