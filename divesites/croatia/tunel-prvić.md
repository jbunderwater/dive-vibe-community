## Tunel Prvić

Tunel Prvić is an underwater tunnel at the northern tip of Prvić island in the Kvarner, south of Šilo on Krk. Note: this site is in the Kvarner region, not the Šibenik area of central Dalmatia as some records suggest. The tunnel cuts through a rocky promontory at approximately 12 metres entrance depth, extends for roughly 35 metres, and has a diameter of approximately 3 metres — straightforward to navigate. Exiting the tunnel reveals what local dive centres describe as one of the most beautiful rock faces in the whole area.

## Overview

The tunnel runs from north to south through the tip of Prvić island. With the entrance at 12 metres, the tunnel is lit at both ends by natural light and is easy to navigate in a single direction. Beyond the exit, the adjacent wall face begins immediately, providing a seamless transition from tunnel to wall diving. A separate feature — the "Pillar-Cave" — lies approximately 40 metres from the tunnel exit and can be combined in the same dive. The site is also known for a high probability of large fish, including tuna, in the open water area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern tip of Prvić island, Kvarner (near Šilo, Krk), Croatia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/tunnel
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters
- **Current**: Can be present through the tunnel; check with operators
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Local dive operators note high probability of large fish (specifically tuna) in the open water near the tunnel exit. No further site-specific species confirmed.

## Dive Profile

Enter the tunnel at 12 metres and transit the approximately 35-metre passage. Natural light at both ends keeps orientation clear. Exit onto the rock face and explore the adjacent wall. The nearby Pillar-Cave 40m away can be included in the same dive. Maximum depth 20 metres. Safety stop at 5 metres before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Šilo (Krk) or Vrbnik operators access Prvić by boat. Enter on one side of the island at the tunnel entrance. Exit on the other side — coordinate with the dive boat for pickup at the correct location before the dive. SMB deployment on ascent is important for boat coordination.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Brief thoroughly with the local dive guide on current direction and pickup location before the dive
- A torch is useful for examining the tunnel walls even though natural light is present throughout
- The adjacent rock face after the tunnel exit is worth spending time on
- Check for current direction before entering — the tunnel can have flow
- The nearby Pillar-Cave 40m from the tunnel exit rewards a combined dive

## Safety Considerations

Through-tunnel dives carry added responsibility: confirm current direction, ensure all divers have torches, and agree on the exit plan before entering. If current inside the tunnel proves unexpectedly strong, remain calm and decide whether to continue or exit back the way you came. Always carry an SMB and dive with a buddy.

## Photography

Shooting along the length of the tunnel toward the light-filled exit — with a diver as the focal point — creates a dramatic image. The circular entrance framing blue water is a classic composition. The rock face beyond the tunnel exit provides strong wide-angle subjects.
