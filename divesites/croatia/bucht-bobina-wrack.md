## Bucht Bobina (Wrack)

Bucht Bobina is a sheltered bay site near Krk Island in the northern Kvarner region of Croatia, taking its name from the small cove ("Bucht" is German for bay) that provides the launch point. A historical wreck lies on the sandy bottom of the bay, its scattered remains forming an artificial reef in otherwise open water.

## Overview

The wreck sits at around 20–25 metres on a sandy seabed. While the original structure has broken down considerably over the years, the hull plating, ribs, and scattered debris are well encrusted with Mediterranean invertebrates and provide habitat for a variety of fish species. The sheltered bay location means conditions are typically calm, making this a pleasant dive in the clear Kvarner waters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bobina Bay, Krk Island area, Kvarner, Croatia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Generally minimal in the sheltered bay
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The wreck debris field attracts octopus that hide beneath hull plates, conger eels in the deeper recesses, and scorpionfish sitting camouflaged on encrusted metal. Grouper and sea bream are common visitors, and the sandy bottom around the wreck often hosts hermit crabs, sea urchins, and small flatfish.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wreck at 20–25 metres and explore the scattered debris field. The dive is relatively relaxed — swim the length of the structure, poke torch beams into dark crevices, and finish with a shallow water exploration of the bay edges. Allow gas for a 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats access the site from Krk-area operators. Entry by giant stride. Ascend via a shot line or the anchor line and deploy an SMB for open-water portions of the ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A torch is essential for investigating the dark spaces between hull plates
- Combine with a nearby reef dive for a well-rounded day in the Kvarner archipelago
- Visibility can be reduced in spring when planktonic blooms occur in the Kvarner
- A 5–7 mm wetsuit is recommended in spring and autumn

## Safety Considerations

Depth is within standard recreational limits. The site is straightforward, but divers should monitor gas consumption carefully when exploring the debris field. Always carry an SMB. Dive within your certification level.

## Photography

Encrusted metal framework, octopus in the debris, and colourful sponge growth make this a productive macro site. Wide-angle shots can capture the scattered hull structure against the sandy bottom. Morning dives often offer the best natural light penetration.
