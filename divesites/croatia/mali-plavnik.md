## Mali Plavnik

Mali Plavnik ("Little Plavnik") is a small uninhabited islet in the Kvarner archipelago. Local dive operators describe the underwater terrain as "Underwater Mountains" — a complex formation of rock towers that extend above and below the waterline, with drops reaching 40 metres and beyond. This is a notably different character from the surrounding reef terrain, making it a distinctive site in the area.

## Overview

Multiple submerged rock towers extend downward from the islet's base, dropping steeply into the blue. An 18-metre-deep cave in one of the tower faces contains a reported monkfish of approximately one metre in size. The area also supports blue-striped squat lobsters and large scorpionfish. The exposed position of the islet in open Kvarner water generates conditions that attract open-water pelagic species. Kvarner visibility is typically 15–20 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mali Plavnik islet, Kvarner (between Krk and Cres), Croatia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters
- **Current**: Variable around the islet; can be stronger on exposed sides
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Local dive operators confirm: large scorpionfish, blue-striped squat lobsters, and a monkfish (approximately 1 metre) resident in the cave at 18m depth. No additional site-specific species claims made.

## Dive Profile

Boats typically anchor on the lee side of the islet. Descend to the base of the towers and explore the wall faces, working down to 40 metres on the deeper sides. The cave at 18m is a worthwhile feature on the ascent. The islet's perimeter is too large for complete circumnavigation in one dive — plan which section to focus on. Ascend with deliberate stops; deploy an SMB on ascent.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Cres, Krk, or Lošinj operators access this islet. Entry by giant stride. The exposed position of the islet requires careful current assessment. Deploy an SMB on ascent for boat coordination.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The cave at 18m is a notable feature — have your torch ready as you approach
- Current around exposed sides of the islet can be strong — check conditions before moving from the sheltered side
- The formation rewards multiple dives, each exploring a different face of the towers
- This is a site suited to divers comfortable in open water at depth

## Safety Considerations

The islet's exposed position can generate current on some faces. Check conditions with the captain before each dive. The depth extends beyond standard recreational limits — plan and manage accordingly. Always carry an SMB.

## Photography

The submerged rock towers with their dramatic vertical faces provide excellent wide-angle subjects. The cave at 18m and its resident monkfish are striking portrait subjects. The open blue-water setting creates opportunities for wide-angle shots with large resident fish.
