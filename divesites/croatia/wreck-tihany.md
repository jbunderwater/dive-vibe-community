## Wreck Tihany

The Tihany is a cargo steamship that sank in 1917 near the small island of Školjić in the waters off Unije island (Lošinj area of the Kvarner). Note: the wreck lies near Unije island in the northern Kvarner — not near Pag island as some records suggest. The vessel is 60 metres long and rests at a depth of approximately 35 metres. Notably, ancient amphorae fragments have been found in the immediate vicinity of the stern, indicating this stretch of water has been a shipping hazard for millennia.

One source (ScubaSpace) records the sinking date as February 12, 1917; another source describes the vessel as an Austro-Hungarian cargo steamship that stranded due to bad weather and poor navigation. Exact circumstances require further verification from maritime records.

## Overview

The Tihany rests on the seabed at approximately 35 metres, providing a substantial wreck dive at the upper end of recreational limits. At 60 metres length, the wreck can reward multiple dives exploring different sections. The presence of amphora fragments nearby adds archaeological interest. Visibility in Lošinj area waters typically reaches 10–30 metres. The site is recommended for advanced divers.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the highest point of the wreck — typically the superstructure — then work down to 35 metres along the hull. Complete a circuit of the accessible sections with a torch. Ascend via the shot line and complete a minimum 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres. Gas management is important at this depth.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Mali Lošinj or Unije operators access this site. Entry by giant stride or back roll. Ascend via shot line or deploy SMB for free ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A torch is essential for inspecting the wreck interior
- Good buoyancy control will prevent silt disturbance
- The amphora fragments near the stern are historically significant — do not disturb them
- This should be the deepest dive of the day given the 35m depth

## Safety Considerations

The 35-metre maximum depth is at the upper recreational limit. Gas management and careful ascent are important. Never penetrate enclosed spaces without proper training and equipment. Carry an SMB and dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The wreck structure at 35m provides wide-angle subjects in the clear Kvarner water. The amphora fragments near the stern are historically significant macro subjects. A torch and strobe combination reveals the encrusting organisms on the hull.
