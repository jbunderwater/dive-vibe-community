## Zabodaski

Zabodaski is a reef dive site in the Pag Island area of northern Dalmatia, in the channel between Pag and the Zadar Archipelago. The site offers a rocky reef with typical Dalmatian topography in the clear open waters of this scenic island chain.

## Overview

The reef at Zabodaski descends over rocky limestone terrain to approximately 25 metres. The site features the characteristic boulders, crevices, and sloping faces typical of the Dalmatian coast, colonised by encrusting sponges, anemones, and sea fans. Posidonia seagrass is present in the shallower sections. The clear northern Dalmatian water provides visibility typically reaching 15–20 metres in the main season.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Pag Island area, Northern Dalmatian Coast, Croatia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Rocky reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Grouper are resident in the boulder sections. Moray eels and octopus inhabit the rocky crevices throughout the reef. Sea bream, ornate wrasse, and sardinella are abundant in the water column. The Posidonia shallows provide habitat for seahorses and pipefish. Nudibranchs and starfish are scattered across the reef surfaces.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the upper reef and work down to 25 metres, exploring the varied terrain as you go. The 10–18 metre zone typically offers the most varied marine life. Ascend the reef face and complete a 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Pag or Zadar-area operators serve this site. Entry by giant stride or back roll. Deploy an SMB on ascent for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Posidonia meadows in the shallows are worth time at the start or end of the dive
- A torch enhances the deeper crevice sections where eels and lobster shelter
- Combine with a wreck or cave dive from the Pag or Zadar area for a varied programme
- The northern Dalmatian coast offers excellent diving with relatively few crowds

## Safety Considerations

Standard intermediate reef diving. Monitor depth on the sloping terrain. Carry an SMB and dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The excellent visibility in the Dalmatian open water makes this a productive wide-angle site. Resident grouper, schooling sea bream, and the Posidonia meadow with its seahorses provide diverse photographic subjects.
