## Silent Beach

Silent Beach is a sheltered bay dive on the northern coast of Krk Island in the Kvarner region, adjacent to the similarly named Secret Beach and Vajavine wall sites. The name reflects the calm, serene character of the cove — a peaceful diving environment with productive marine life and minimal disturbance from boat traffic or current.

## Overview

The cove at Silent Beach provides access to a rocky reef descending to approximately 25 metres in typically calm conditions. The limestone terrain features boulders, stepped terraces, and sandy patches at depth. The shelter of the bay promotes undisturbed populations of marine life across all depth zones. The Kvarner's characteristic clear water — typically 15–20 metres visibility — allows full appreciation of the site's marine community.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern Krk Island, Kvarner, Croatia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters
- **Current**: Minimal; sheltered bay position
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The calm, sheltered conditions support a mature and undisturbed marine community. Grouper hold territories in the boulder sections, often less wary than at more frequently visited sites. Moray eels inhabit crevices throughout the reef. Octopus are regularly seen hunting. Sea bream and ornate wrasse are abundant. The sandy patches at depth attract flatfish, and the shallower rocky areas may yield seahorses and pipefish.

## Dive Profile

Enter the water and descend into the tranquil bay environment. Explore the reef from the shallows to 25 metres, spending time in each depth zone. The "silent" quality of the site is part of the experience — move slowly and allow marine life to approach. Ascend gently and complete a safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats from Krk island operators include this site in their programme. Entry by giant stride or back roll into the calm bay. Surface conditions are gentle and exit is straightforward.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Embrace the site's character — slow, quiet movement rewards with undisturbed wildlife encounters
- This is an outstanding site for macro photography given the calm conditions
- Combine with the adjacent Vajavine wall site for contrasting dive experiences in the same area
- Visit early morning for the calmest conditions and most active marine life
- The sandy areas at depth are worth checking for flatfish and burrowing species

## Safety Considerations

The sheltered bay provides some of the most benign diving conditions in the Kvarner. Standard intermediate parameters apply. Carry an SMB and dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The calm, clear conditions make this one of the best photography sites in the Krk Island area. Unhurried, quiet diving reveals marine life at close range. The sandy-bottom sections create bright, open frames for fish portraits. Macro subjects on the reef surfaces can be approached without disturbance.
