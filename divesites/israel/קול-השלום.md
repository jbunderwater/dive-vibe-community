## קול השלום (Voice of Peace)

קול השלום — "Voice of Peace" in Hebrew — is the wreck of the MV Peace, the 170-foot Dutch cargo ship that served as the broadcasting vessel for Abie Nathan's legendary offshore pirate radio station. The ship was deliberately scuttled on 28 November 1993 in international waters approximately 15 nautical miles off the Israeli coast.

## Overview

The Voice of Peace radio station operated from 19 May 1973 until November 1993, broadcasting popular music and peace-advocacy programming from the MV Peace anchored off Tel Aviv. Founded by Israeli peace activist Abie Nathan, the station was heard throughout the Middle East. Nathan scuttled the ship after the Oslo Accords were signed, believing the station's mission of peace had been fulfilled. The ship sailed to a position in international waters, the hull plugs were removed, and the MV Peace sank beneath the Mediterranean at 19:33 on 28 November 1993.

The vessel — originally built in the Netherlands in April 1940 as the Rolf — was a 570-ton general cargo ship measuring approximately 52 metres (170 feet) in length, with a 49-metre (160-foot) aerial mast. The wreck lies in deep international waters. Its exact depth and accessibility for recreational diving have not been confirmed by published sources. The coordinates in this database (31.9685, 34.5547) place it approximately 15 nautical miles offshore, consistent with historical accounts.

## Dive Profile

If accessible, this would be a deep technical dive in open water far offshore. The wreck's exact resting depth is not confirmed in public diving literature. Dive planning should treat this as a minimum Advanced/Technical dive given the distance from shore and probable depth. No recreational dive operator is known to offer guided dives to this site.

## Safety Considerations

This site is in international waters with no shore-based support. The depth is unconfirmed and likely exceeds recreational limits. A seaworthy dive vessel, surface support, and appropriate technical dive training are required. Always verify current conditions and legal access before planning a dive to this location.

## Historical Note

A memorial plaque on the Tel Aviv boardwalk at Gordon Beach commemorates the Voice of Peace, playing recordings of the station's call sign in Abie Nathan's voice. Abie Nathan died in Tel Aviv on 27 August 2008 at the age of 81.
