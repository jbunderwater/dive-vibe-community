## קיסריה - עוגנים (Caesarea Anchors)

One of the most distinctive dive spots within the Caesarea Underwater Archaeological Park, named for the collection of ancient stone anchors visible on the seabed. These anchors range from simple stone slabs with a single hole to more refined designs, tracing the development of anchor technology from Canaanite through Roman periods.

## Overview

The Caesarea Underwater Archaeological Park, Israel's first underwater archaeological national park, protects the sunken remains of Sebastos — the massive artificial harbor built by Herod the Great (22-10 BCE). The Anchors site within the park showcases one of the most tangible archaeological features: stone anchors of various ages that were lost or discarded in the harbor over centuries of maritime activity.

The park is administered with four underwater trails marked by ropes, numbered signposts, and waterproof maps distributed to each diver. The site is shallow and well-protected within the harbor basin.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Caesarea National Park, harbor anchorage area
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 6 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-12 meters
- **Current**: Generally light within the protected harbor
- **Best Time**: Spring and autumn

## What Divers See

Stone anchors from multiple historical periods, representing the evolution of anchor design through antiquity. Ancient harbor infrastructure including stone blocks, quay remains, and column fragments. Marine life including sea bream, octopus, grouper, moray eels, and invertebrates on the reef structures.

## Dive Profile

Very shallow site, ideal for beginners and snorkelers. Follow the numbered trail with the park's waterproof map. No technical skills required; all certification levels welcome.
