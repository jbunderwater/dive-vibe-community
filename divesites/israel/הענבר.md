## הענבר (The Amber)

הענבר — "The Amber" — is a natural reef site off the Ashkelon coast in the southern Mediterranean. The name ("amber" in Hebrew) likely refers to the colour of the rock formation or the sandy-amber seabed characteristic of this coastal zone.

## Overview

This site lies within a cluster of natural rock outcrops on the sandy seabed offshore Ashkelon, close to Natural Rock 1 and Natural Rock 2. The rocky formations create localised reef habitat for Mediterranean species in an area otherwise dominated by flat sandy bottom. Depth in this area is estimated at 10–15 metres based on coastal bathymetry.

No published dive-specific description for this site has been found. It is likely dived alongside the nearby Ashkelon rock sites and the archaeological anchor site as part of a multi-site boat day.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Offshore Ashkelon, southern Mediterranean coast of Israel
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–10 metres (variable; seasonal)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

Typical Mediterranean reef species: grouper, sea bream, salema, wrasse, moray eels, octopus, and cuttlefish (seasonally). Sandy patches around the rock may host stingrays, flatfish, and sand-dwelling gobies.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Autumn and winter conditions are preferred for visibility
