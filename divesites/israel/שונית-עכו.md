## שונית עכו (Acre Reef)

A canyon and wall dive approximately 1.6 km off the ancient port city of Acre (Akko) — one of northern Israel's notable reef structures.

## Overview

The Acre Reef (שונית עכו, Shonit Akko) is situated approximately 1 mile (1.6 km) off the shore of ancient Acre (Akko/عكا), a UNESCO World Heritage city on the northern Israeli coast. The reef takes the form of a canyon and wall, with the wall face starting at around 8 metres and dropping to approximately 26 metres. The wall is approximately 70 metres long, featuring numerous small niches and caves along its face.

The site is located near the Rosh Hanikra-Achziv Marine Reserve, which provides some broader ecosystem protection. Access is by boat through local dive operators in the area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Approximately 1.6 km off Acre (Akko), northern Israel
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–12 metres (seasonal variation)
- **Current**: Moderate; can be variable along the wall
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The niches and caves in the wall harbour groupers, moray eels, sea bream, and Mediterranean parrotfish. Octopus are common. The wall face supports sponges and sea anemones, attracting nudibranchs and small crustaceans.

## Dive Profile

Boat dive, descending to the top of the wall at approximately 8 metres, then following the wall to depth. The 70-metre length of the wall allows exploration in both directions. The cave niches reward careful inspection with a torch.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Access through dive operators in Acre or Nahariya
- The wall runs approximately 70 metres — plan to cover it systematically
- Torch useful for illuminating cave niches
- The nearby ancient port city of Acre offers cultural sightseeing to complement the dive
