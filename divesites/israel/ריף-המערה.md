## ריף המערה (Cave Reef)

"Cave Reef" (ריף המערה) is a shore dive site in the Herzliya area, featuring a rocky reef with a cave or overhang formation. The site is classified in OSM data as a cave-type dive site and lies just offshore at approximately 32.17°N, 34.79°E — in the waters off Herzliya's southern coastline, near Sidna Ali beach and the Apollonia area.

## Overview

The Herzliya coastal area has several named reef dive sites that are served by the Reef Israel Diving and Surfing Club at the Dan Acadia Hotel. Cave Reef is one of these, featuring a natural cave or tunnel section within the rocky reef. The cave provides shelter for species that prefer low-light, enclosed environments.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Herzliya coast, central Israeli Mediterranean
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-12 meters
- **Current**: Generally light
- **Best Time**: Autumn and spring

## Marine Life

Cave-dwelling Mediterranean species: grouper sheltering in the cave entrance, moray eels, octopus, sea bream, scorpionfish, nudibranchs, and sponges on cave walls.

## Safety Considerations

Cave section requires a dive light. Do not exceed training level when entering confined spaces. Check with local operators (Reef Israel Diving Club, Herzliya) for current conditions and safe access points.
