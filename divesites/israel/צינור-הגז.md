## צינור הגז (Gas Pipe)

"Gas Pipe" (צינור הגז) is a dive site off the southern Tel Aviv / Jaffa coastline named for an underwater gas pipeline that runs along or crosses this section of the seabed. Pipelines in the Mediterranean quickly become artificial reefs, with marine life colonizing the hard substrate along the pipe's length.

## Overview

Located at approximately 32.09°N, 34.75°E — in waters off Jaffa — this site is known to local Tel Aviv-area divers as a dive along an underwater pipe structure. Pipeline dive sites offer distinctive linear topography and reliable fish aggregations, as the pipe provides both shelter and a navigation guide for divers.

The "Old Oil Channel" — a retired oil transport pipe — was mentioned in Tel Aviv dive guides as a site at approximately 15 meters depth housing rare marine creatures. This may be the same or a similar site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Jaffa / south Tel Aviv, Israeli Mediterranean
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: Approximately 12-15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-10 meters
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: Autumn and spring

## Marine Life

Grouper, sea bream, moray eels, octopus, and encrusting organisms along the pipe structure. Pipelines create a linear habitat that can be navigated from one end to the other.
