## DOLPHIN

DOLPHIN is a shore-entry dive site adjacent to Eilat's Dolphin Reef facility and the EAPC (Katza) Beach area, offering one of the best opportunities in Israel to dive alongside semi-wild bottlenose dolphins.

## Overview

The DOLPHIN site corresponds to the Katza / EAPC Beach area immediately adjacent to the Dolphin Reef attraction on Eilat's southern shore. The EAPC Beach (formerly belonging to the Eilat Ashkelon Pipeline Company) was opened to the public for diving and swimming around 2016-2017. The defining feature of this dive is the presence of bottlenose dolphins — residents of the adjacent Dolphin Reef facility — that move freely in and out of the open sea and regularly visit the area around the old EAPC oil pipeline pillars. The submerged pier pillars are covered in soft corals, hard corals, and sponges, creating a productive artificial reef structure.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Eilat, adjacent to Dolphin Reef facility and EAPC (Katza) Beach, Red Sea, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters
- **Current**: Light; sanctuary zone rules apply
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round; mornings give the highest chance of dolphin encounters

## Marine Life

The site is most famous for encounters with Black Sea bottlenose dolphins that live at the adjacent Dolphin Reef. These dolphins are not trained performers and interact with divers voluntarily. Beyond dolphins, the pipeline pillars are home to lionfish, moray eels, parrotfish, and sergeant majors. Pufferfish, sea snakes, and anglefish with cleaner shrimp are commonly reported. The soft corals and sponges on the pillars attract nudibranchs and invertebrates.

## Dive Profile

Entry from the shore at EAPC Beach. The pipeline support pillars begin at approximately 6 feet (2 meters) and extend to 30 meters (the fifth pillar at the edge of the swim area is at approximately 30 feet/10 meters). The swimming distance from the first to the fifth pillar spans about 100 meters. Diving around the pillars between 6 and 20 meters offers the richest marine life. This is a sanctuary zone — touching the seabed is prohibited.

## Entry and Exit

Free shore entry from EAPC Beach (no entrance fee required, unlike the Dolphin Reef facility). Public transport access is available nearby. The beach has parking. Note: this is a sanctuary zone where touching the seabed is not permitted — buoyancy control is mandatory.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Arrive early in the morning for the best chance of dolphin encounters (most active at dawn)
- You have over 90% chance of encountering dolphins during sunrise dives according to local reports
- The sanctuary rules prohibit touching the seabed — practice excellent buoyancy before diving here
- Shore entry is free; the adjacent Dolphin Reef facility charges for entry to swim with dolphins in a controlled environment

## Safety Considerations

Sanctuary zone: no touching the seabed. Buoyancy control is strictly enforced. Be aware of occasional boat traffic near the Dolphin Reef facility. The dolphins are wild animals that interact voluntarily — do not chase or try to touch them.
