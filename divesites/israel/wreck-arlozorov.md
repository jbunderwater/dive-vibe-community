## Wreck Arlozorov

A historically significant Ma'apilim (illegal Jewish immigration) ship wreck in shallow water off Bat Galim beach, Haifa — a touchstone of pre-State of Israel history.

## Overview

The Chaim Arlozorov (originally a vessel renamed to honour the assassinated Zionist leader Chaim Arlosoroff) was a Ma'apilim ship used to transport Jewish Holocaust survivors and refugees to British-Mandate Palestine in defiance of British immigration restrictions. On 27 February 1946, the ship was intercepted by British forces as it approached Haifa carrying approximately 1,398 refugees, including 500 young women who had survived Nazi concentration camps. After a several-hour confrontation, passengers were carried ashore by British soldiers. The vessel subsequently struck a shoal and sank in shallow water northeast of Bat Galim beach, where it has remained ever since.

The wreck lies within easy reach of shore in up to 10 metres of water, spread across the seabed northeast of Bat Galim. The site is accessible from Bat Galim public beach. There is ongoing debate among researchers about whether the visible remains are definitively those of the Arlozorov or a different vessel, as metal scavengers were active in the area after the vessel's sinking.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bat Galim beach, Haifa, Mediterranean
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 3–8 metres (affected by harbour conditions and seasonal variation)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Typical shallow Mediterranean species inhabit the wreckage: sea bream, groupers, moray eels, octopus, and sea urchins. The broken structure provides substrate for sponges and encrusting organisms.

## Dive Profile

A shallow, accessible dive suitable for intermediate divers. Enter from Bat Galim beach and navigate northeast toward the wreck remains. The site is spread across a relatively wide area. Depth is modest at approximately 10 metres, making this suitable for longer bottom times. A compass and awareness of boat traffic in Haifa Bay are advisable.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The site has historical significance — treat it with respect
- Visibility can be limited due to proximity to the harbour — morning dives typically have better conditions
- Check with Haifa local dive operators for current site conditions and exact entry point
- Be aware of potential boat traffic in the Haifa Bay area

## Safety Considerations

Haifa Bay has commercial shipping traffic. Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB) and surface with it deployed. Visibility can drop significantly in poor conditions. The wreck debris is scattered and angular — watch for sharp metal edges.
