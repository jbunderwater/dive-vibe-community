## צוללת U559 (Submarine U-559)

U-559 was a German Type VIIC U-boat whose sinking on 30 October 1942 became one of the most significant intelligence events of World War II.

## Overview

On 30 October 1942, U-559 was hunted to the surface in the eastern Mediterranean approximately 70 miles north of the Nile Delta, at a position of approximately 31°47'N 33°24'E. After the German crew abandoned ship, three Royal Navy sailors from HMS Petard — Lieutenant Anthony Fasson, Able Seaman Colin Grazier, and canteen assistant Tommy Brown — boarded the sinking submarine and recovered Enigma codebooks and cipher machine components before the vessel sank beneath them. Fasson and Grazier drowned; both were posthumously awarded the George Cross. The recovered material significantly advanced Bletchley Park's ability to break the naval Enigma cipher.

**Note on site accessibility:** The OSM-recorded position (32.315°N, 33.2°E) is approximately consistent with the historical sinking area, but this location is in open international waters well offshore — not in Israeli coastal waters. No recreational dive operations at this specific site are known to exist. The wreck is at depth beyond recreational limits. This entry serves as a historical record point.

## Historical Significance

The cryptographic material recovered from U-559 is documented as a pivotal contribution to breaking the four-rotor naval Enigma cipher, helping turn the tide in the Battle of the Atlantic. Fasson and Grazier's sacrifice is commemorated at the National Memorial Arboretum and in multiple published historical accounts.
