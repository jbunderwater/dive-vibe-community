## פלמחים - הרוקי (Palmachim - The Rocky)

פלמחים - הרוקי — "Palmachim - The Rocky" — is a shallow rocky reef dive off Palmachim Beach, within the Palmachim National Park area south of Tel Aviv. The site takes its name from the rocky substrate that characterises the shallows along this stretch of coast.

## Overview

Palmachim is one of the most accessible shore-dive destinations on Israel's central Mediterranean coast. The rocky bays along Palmachim Beach form shallow reef ecosystems at depths of just 2–7 metres, making this site ideal for beginners, students, and divers seeking easy, relaxed dives on natural Mediterranean rock formations.

The rocky shoreline provides shelter in crevices for small crabs, worms, octopus, cuttlefish, and other Mediterranean invertebrates. Stingrays and guitarfish are occasionally reported in the sandy channels between the rock formations.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Palmachim Beach, south of Tel Aviv (Palmachim National Park)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–12 metres (variable; better in autumn and winter)
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

Common sightings include octopus, cuttlefish, small crabs, sea bream (sparidae), moray eels, gobies, and various Mediterranean invertebrates. Guitarfish and stingrays are occasional visitors to sandy areas adjacent to the rocky reef.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Palmachim Beach. Easy entry and exit over rocks or sand. Check national park rules and any access restrictions before diving. The site is within Palmachim National Park and some areas may have seasonal wildlife protection closures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent site for training dives and first open-water experiences
- Morning dives typically offer better visibility
- Combine with the nearby barge wreck (הדוברה בפלמחים) for a full dive day
- Carry an SMB even in shallow water due to boat traffic
