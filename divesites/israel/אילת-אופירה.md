## אילת - אופירה (Eilat - Ophira)

אילת - אופירה (Eilat - Ophira) is a shore-entry coral reef dive site in southern Eilat near the Coral Beach Nature Reserve and Lighthouse Beach area, named after the Hebrew place name "Ophira" associated with this section of coast.

## Overview

"Ophira" (אופירה) is a Hebrew name with historical resonance — it was the name used for the Eilat/Aqaba area in Biblical references and was also the official name of Eilat's airport (now Ovda) and the town itself in some historical Israeli designations. In the context of this dive site at lat 29.502, the site sits in the southern section of Eilat's diving coast, between the Coral World Underwater Observatory and the Lighthouse Beach area. This part of the coast falls within or adjacent to the Coral Beach Nature Reserve zone, which features the world's northernmost shallow coral reef. The reef begins meters from shore and the slope descends to recreational depths within a short swim.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Eilat, near Coral Beach Nature Reserve, Red Sea, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Current**: Very light to none
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The southern Eilat reef zone between the Coral World Observatory and Lighthouse Beach is one of the most productive sections of the Israeli Red Sea coast. Hard corals including Acropora tables, brain corals, and massive Porites dominate the reef structure. Fish life includes parrotfish, butterflyfish, angelfish, anthias, grouper, and moray eels. Napoleon wrasse are resident in the area. Sea turtles patrol the reef edge. Lionfish are commonly encountered, as are pufferfish, octopus, and nudibranchs in the rubble zones.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from the southern beach zone, close to the Coral World Observatory. The reef starts shallow and the slope descends to 25+ meters. The most productive zone is between 5 and 20 meters along the coral wall. Excellent visibility throughout the year. The site benefits from the gentle southerly currents that maintain water clarity in this area.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the southern Eilat beach, possibly within or adjacent to the Coral Beach Nature Reserve (entrance fee may apply within reserve boundaries). The Lighthouse Beach is a short distance further south. The area is well-served by dive centers operating from the nearby Coral Beach hotels.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The proximity to the Coral World Underwater Observatory makes this a logical add-on to a surface visit to the aquarium
- Excellent visibility and calm conditions year-round make this one of Israel's most reliable dive sites
- The site is excellent for dive training given the controlled conditions
- Night diving is highly recommended in this area — invertebrates become active after dark

## Safety Considerations

No significant hazards — this is one of Israel's most benign diving environments. Light currents, no surge, and excellent visibility combine to make this an ideal dive site for all levels. Stay within reserve boundaries and follow no-touch rules for coral protection.
