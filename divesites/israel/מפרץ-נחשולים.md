## מפרץ נחשולים (Nachsholim Bay)

A sheltered Mediterranean bay with shallow reef diving and ancient ruins at the foot of the Carmel mountains — accessible from Dor-Nachsholim beach.

## Overview

Nachsholim Bay (מפרץ נחשולים) sits at the southwestern edge of the Carmel mountain range on the Mediterranean coast of Israel, near the ancient site of Dor (Tel Dor). The bay features a natural rock breakwater creating a lagoon with relatively calm, clear water. The shore dive follows the reef line northward, passing the ruins of ancient Dor — a Phoenician port city — which creates a unique combination of natural reef and archaeological diving.

Maximum depth is approximately 8 metres. The reef is riddled with crevices, and a natural hole carved by currents provides additional interest. The sandy areas between reef sections yield encounters with rays and guitar fish.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dor-Nachsholim, Carmel Coast, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 3–8 metres (seasonal variation)
- **Current**: Mild — bay provides some protection
- **Best Time**: May to October
- **Facilities**: Restaurant, bathrooms, changing rooms, and showers at Dor beach

## Marine Life

Crabs, octopus, squid, nudibranchs, and various sea bream inhabit the reef crevices. Guitar fish and electric rays are found in sandy areas. Sea urchins are abundant on rocky substrate. Sea turtles are occasionally observed.

## Dive Profile

Enter from Dor beach and navigate along the natural reef line heading north. The dive follows the ancient coastal ruins of Dor, where stone structures are visible on the seabed. The reef crevice system rewards slow, careful exploration. Depths of 3–8 metres allow long bottom times.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Access requires purchasing a day ticket at Dor beach
- Bring a macro lens — nudibranchs and small crustaceans are highlights
- Combine with a visit to Tel Dor archaeological site above water
- The nearby Nachsholim Museum covers the maritime history of the Dor area
