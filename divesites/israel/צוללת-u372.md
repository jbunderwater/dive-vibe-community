## צוללת U372 (Submarine U-372)

U-372 was a German Type VIIC U-boat that served in the Kriegsmarine during World War II. Commissioned on 19 April 1941, the submarine operated in the Atlantic before transferring to the Mediterranean. U-372 is notably responsible for sinking HMS Medway (a British submarine depot ship) on 30 June 1942 off Alexandria.

## Overview

On 4 August 1942, U-372 was hunted to exhaustion and sunk southwest of Haifa by a combined Royal Navy and RAF operation: depth charges from HMS Sikh, HMS Zulu, HMS Croome, and HMS Tetcott, coordinated with attacks from an RAF Wellington bomber (221 Squadron). The wreck lies in the eastern Mediterranean at approximately 32°28'N, 34°37'E.

The submarine's dimensions were 67.10 m in length with a displacement of 769 tonnes surfaced / 871 tonnes submerged. No crew survived. The wreck's precise accessible depth is not publicly documented in available sources; given its offshore location, it is likely at a depth requiring advanced or technical diving.

## Dive Profile

The wreck is a rare opportunity to dive a WWII-era German submarine in the Mediterranean. The Type VIIC was the most common U-boat class, with a length of 67 m. Penetration of the submarine structure requires specialist training and equipment. External wreck swim-around provides views of the pressure hull, conning tower remains, and possibly torpedo tube openings.

## Safety Considerations

Deep wreck diving requires appropriate certification (Advanced or higher), suitable gas planning, and dive computer use. Penetration is not recommended without specific wreck-diving training. Metal edges may be sharp and visibility inside the hull is typically poor.
