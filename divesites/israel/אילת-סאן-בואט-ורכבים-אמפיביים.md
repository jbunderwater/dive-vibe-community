## אילת - סאן בואט ורכבים אמפיביים (Eilat - Sun Boat and Amphibious Vehicles)

The Sun Boat and Amphibious Vehicles site is a purposely-sunk wreck cluster in northern Eilat, forming an accessible artificial reef at 15 meters depth on Israel's Red Sea coast.

## Overview

The Sun Boat (סאן בואט) was a 30-meter liveaboard dive vessel that operated across the Red Sea before being intentionally sunk in 2010 to create an artificial reef. The wreck stands upright on a sandy bottom at approximately 14-15 meters depth, with its bow facing north. Connected by cables to the main wreck are several smaller amphibious vehicles and metal structures, creating a multi-component artificial reef complex. The site is located in northern Eilat, approximately 280 meters from shore, reached with a 10-15 minute swim from HaDatiyim Beach. The site can be seen by most beginner divers, though the swim distance requires good fitness.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern Eilat, HaDatiyim Beach area, Red Sea, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable — can be reduced in this area
- **Current**: Generally light
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round; morning for better visibility

## Marine Life

Schools of glassfish have colonized the interior of the Sun Boat in dense numbers, sometimes reducing visibility inside the wreck. Soft corals have established on the hull railings and fences. Small schools of tuna are reported circling the wreck. Lionfish and moray eels inhabit the structural elements. The connected amphibious vehicles provide additional habitat for encrusting organisms and reef fish. Rays occasionally visit the sandy bottom around the wreck cluster.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from HaDatiyim Beach (free parking nearby). Swim approximately 280-330 meters, using the yellow buoy as a reference point. The wreck sits at 15 meters on a flat sandy bottom. Explore the exterior of the 30-meter hull, swim through the open sections, and visit the connected amphibious vehicles via the guide cables. The wreck is accessible in one extended dive. A safety stop can be made at the mast if it reaches shallower depth.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from HaDatiyim Beach in northern Eilat. Public transport access is approximately 650 meters away. The swim distance is the main challenge — approximately 280-330 meters each way. Good navigation is required to locate the wreck. Follow the yellow buoy marker as a reference. Note that surface visibility may be limited near the wreck.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring an underwater torch: the interior of the wreck is colonized by dense glassfish schools that create stunning visual effects
- The amphibious vehicles are connected by cables — follow the cables from the main wreck to find them
- Morning dives offer the best visibility at this site
- The swim distance makes this unsuitable for beginner divers with poor fitness or air consumption

## Safety Considerations

The 280-330 meter shore swim in open water requires good fitness and navigation. Do not attempt if surface conditions are rough (north winds affect this area). Wreck penetration into enclosed spaces requires proper training and equipment. The sandy seagrass habitat in the approach area is ecologically sensitive — avoid dragging fins through it.
