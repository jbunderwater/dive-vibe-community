## קיסריה דרום (Caesarea South)

The southern section of the Caesarea diving area, adjacent to the Underwater Archaeological Park. This area lies along the southern extent of the ancient Herodian harbor of Sebastos and offers shallow reef diving with potential access to archaeological remains.

## Overview

The Caesarea Underwater Archaeological Park encompasses approximately 50 acres (200,000 sq. m) of sunken harbor remains, built by Herod the Great between 22 and 10 BCE. The southern area provides access to the outer harbor structures and reef formations that have developed on the ancient stonework over the centuries. The site is managed by the Old Caesarea Diving Center, which offers guided access to multiple marked trails within the park.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Caesarea National Park, southern harbor area
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-12 meters
- **Current**: Generally light
- **Best Time**: Spring and autumn

## Marine Life

Typical Mediterranean reef species including sea bream, grouper, octopus, moray eels, scorpionfish, and nudibranchs on and around the ancient stone structures.

## Dive Profile

Shallow site suitable for beginner divers. The ancient masonry provides interesting topography — blocks, collapsed walls, and column fragments — all colonized by Mediterranean marine life. Follow any marked trails and use the park's waterproof map.
