דוברה הפוכה — "Inverted Barge" — is a wreck site in the Ashdod offshore zone of the southern Mediterranean coast. The name describes a barge that came to rest upside-down (inverted) on the seabed.

## Overview

Inverted or capsized barge wrecks are a recognisable category of Mediterranean coastal wrecks. This site lies at approximately 31.795°N 34.579°E, around 8–10 kilometres offshore in the area between Ashdod and Ashkelon. The inverted hull orientation creates a distinctive dive profile — the underside of the hull becomes the highest point of the wreck, with the upturned gunwales and bottom plates resting on the seabed.

The site likely sits in approximately 20–25 metres of water based on local bathymetry, consistent with other offshore wreck sites in this zone. No specific depth has been confirmed in published diving sources.

Inverted barge wrecks provide excellent artificial reef habitat: enclosed spaces under the upturned hull shelter grouper, sea bream, and moray eels, while encrusting growth covers the exterior plating.
