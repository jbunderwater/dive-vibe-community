## מערת הצמיגים (Tire Cave)

"Tire Cave" — a locally known dive site in the Bat Yam / Jaffa coastal area, named for tires that were placed in or around an underwater cave formation, creating an artificial reef structure within a natural cave.

## Overview

Located at approximately 32.04°N, 34.74°E — in the Bat Yam or southern Jaffa area of the Tel Aviv metropolitan coast — this site features a natural cave in the rocky reef that has been enhanced with tire placements. Used tires placed underwater colonize quickly with encrusting organisms and provide shelter for marine life, combining with the natural cave setting to create a distinctive dive site.

The site is accessible from shore and is a known training and recreational dive site for Tel Aviv-area dive operators and clubs.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bat Yam / south Jaffa coastline, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-10 meters
- **Current**: Generally light
- **Best Time**: Autumn and spring

## Marine Life

Cave-dwelling species including grouper, moray eels, sea bream, octopus, and invertebrates colonizing the tire surfaces. The combination of natural cave ceiling and artificial tire substrate creates varied habitat.

## Safety Considerations

Cave diving requires awareness of overhead environments. Carry a dive light. Do not exceed your training level when entering cave sections.
