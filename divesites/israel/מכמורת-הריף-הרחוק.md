## מכמורת הריף הרחוק (Michmoret Far Reef)

The farther of two reef dive sites off Michmoret village, located approximately 500 meters further offshore than the Near Reef. The greater distance from shore means a longer surface swim but typically rewards divers with slightly deeper water and less disturbance than the nearshore site.

## Overview

Michmoret is a small fishing village and beach resort north of Netanya where the Alexander River meets the Mediterranean. The two offshore reefs provide the primary scuba diving in this part of the central Israeli coast. The Far Reef sits in slightly deeper water, generally in the 12-20 meter range.

Like others along the central Israeli coast, the reef is a rocky limestone formation with sandy channels between reef sections. The Mediterranean here has seasonal visibility changes — clearer in autumn and winter, reduced in summer due to plankton blooms.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Michmoret village, north of Netanya, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: Approximately 15-20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-12 meters
- **Current**: Moderate; stronger offshore conditions possible
- **Best Time**: Spring and autumn

## Marine Life

Sea bream, grouper, moray eels, octopus, scorpionfish, nudibranchs, and invertebrates typical of the central Israeli Mediterranean coast. Slightly deeper water may yield larger grouper and more pelagic visitors.

## Dive Profile

Longer surface swim than the Near Reef. Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB) for the return swim. Intermediate certification recommended.
