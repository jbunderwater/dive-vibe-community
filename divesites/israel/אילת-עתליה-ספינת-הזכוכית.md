## אילת - עתליה - ספינת הזכוכית (Eilat - Atalia - Glass Bottom Boat)

The Atalia Glass Bottom Boat site is a deep wreck in the Atalia area of Eilat's central Red Sea coastline, associated with a sunken tourist vessel with glass viewing panels at a significant depth.

## Overview

This site is listed in OSM data as "אילת - עתליה - ספינת הזכוכית" — "Eilat - Atalia - Glass Bottom Boat." The "Atalia" reference may indicate a specific neighborhood or beach area (Atalia is a Hebrew name associated with the coastline in this part of Eilat). The coordinates at lat 29.519 place this site approximately 500 meters north of the ספינת הזכוכית site to the south, in a slightly different location along the reef slope. It is possible this represents a second entry point or navigation reference for the same or a nearby wreck, or a distinct submerged structure in the Atalia section of the coast. Given the wreck designation in OSM and the consistent "Glass Bottom Boat" name, this site involves a wrecked tourist vessel at advanced depth on the central Eilat reef slope.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Eilat, Atalia area, central Red Sea coastline, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 55 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-35 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

At depth, the wreck structure is likely colonized by sponges, soft corals, and encrusting organisms. The Atalia section of the coast is within the broader Coral Beach zone, meaning the reef above the wreck depth is rich in hard corals, anthias, parrotfish, and reef fish typical of the Eilat marine environment. Larger species including grouper and barracuda pass through the deep zone.

## Dive Profile

Boat access recommended for this depth. Descent along the reef slope to the wreck at approximately 48-55 meters. Bottom time is strictly limited by decompression requirements. The reef wall above the wreck offers excellent diving in the 15-40 meter range for the ascent and safety stops.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access is recommended. Contact local Eilat dive operators for guided access to this site. This dive is not suitable for unsupervised diving by recreational-only certified divers.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This site pairs well with shallow reef dives in the same area at 5-20 meters for a two-dive day
- The reef slope above the wreck is part of the broader Coral Beach ecosystem and is worth exploring in its own right
- Technical divers can plan extended bottom time using trimix

## Safety Considerations

**Depth warning: 48-55 meters exceeds recreational diving limits.** Advanced certification and recent deep diving experience are required. Nitrogen narcosis is a significant concern at this depth on air. Proper decompression planning is essential. Do not dive this site alone or without appropriate gas planning. Wreck penetration requires technical wreck/cave certification.
