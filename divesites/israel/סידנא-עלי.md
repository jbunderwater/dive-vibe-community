## סידנא עלי (Sidna Ali)

Sidna Ali is a beach and dive site on Israel's central Mediterranean coast, named for the nearby historic 14th-century mosque (Masjid Sidna Ali) at Herzliya. One of the historically significant early dive sites in Israel, the Sidna Ali area was home to one of Israel's first diving schools, with a Mediterranean Diving Centre established at the beach in the early days of Israeli sport diving.

## Overview

The Sidna Ali beach lies just north of Herzliya proper, below the coastal cliffs. The dive site features a shallow rocky reef accessible directly from the beach. The site is close to the Apollonia National Park area and is part of the same general coastal diving zone served by the Reef Israel Diving and Surfing Club at the Dan Acadia Hotel in Herzliya.

Diving conditions are typical of the central Israeli Mediterranean coast: visibility 5-12 meters, water temperature 16-28°C seasonally, with the reef populated by typical eastern Mediterranean species.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sidna Ali Beach, Herzliya area, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-12 meters
- **Current**: Generally light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Autumn and spring for best visibility

## Marine Life

Sea bream, grouper, octopus, moray eels, mullet, scorpionfish, nudibranchs, and invertebrates in rock crevices. The shallow reef is well-suited for macro photography.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from Sidna Ali beach. Rocky reef begins close to shore. Suitable for Open Water and Intermediate level divers. Contact local operators (Reef Israel Diving Club, Herzliya) for guided dives in the area.
