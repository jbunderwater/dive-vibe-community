## שדות ים - הגרנד קניון (Sdot Yam Grand Canyon)

A notable dive site near Kibbutz Sdot Yam (adjacent to Caesarea), featuring a canyon or gorge formation in the rocky reef. The name "Grand Canyon" refers to the dramatic underwater terrain — a deep crack or channel running through the rocky seabed close to the Caesarea coastline.

## Overview

Sdot Yam sits immediately south of Caesarea and north of Michmoret. The diving here is managed in part by the Haifa University Morris Kahn Marine Research Station, which maintains a diving center at Kibbutz Sdot Yam. The "Grand Canyon" feature is a locally prominent cave or canyon formation in the rocky Mediterranean reef, offering more dramatic topography than typical flat sandy-bottom sites in the area.

The site classified as a cave/canyon in OSM data, consistent with the name. It is accessible from shore near the kibbutz.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kibbutz Sdot Yam, south of Caesarea, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Maximum Depth**: Approximately 15-20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-12 meters
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: Spring and autumn

## Marine Life

Mediterranean rocky reef species: grouper, sea bream, octopus, moray eels, nudibranchs, scorpionfish, and various invertebrates within the canyon walls.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry leads to the reef edge, then into the canyon feature. The canyon walls provide interesting topography and shelter for marine life. Intermediate level recommended due to the cave/canyon navigation and potential currents.

## Safety Considerations

Canyon diving requires maintaining awareness of overhead environments. Carry a dive light. Do not enter enclosed spaces without appropriate training and equipment.
