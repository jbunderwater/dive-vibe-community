## הגוררת איתנה (Tug Eitana)

A former Haifa harbour tugboat deliberately sunk on 13 March 1996 as an artificial reef, lying on its side at approximately 30 metres depth.

## Overview

The Eitana was a civil tugboat that worked Haifa Harbour for many years before being decommissioned. On 13 March 1996, it was deliberately scuttled as an artificial reef to provide a dive site and marine habitat. The wreck lies on its side on the seabed at approximately 30 metres depth, slowly becoming encrusted with Mediterranean sponges and soft corals.

The seabed around the wreck is silty, and visibility can be easily compromised by careless finning — buoyancy control is important at this site. The wreck is near the Rosh Hanikra area based on its coordinates, though exact access arrangements should be confirmed with local operators.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern Israeli Mediterranean (Rosh Hanikra coastal area)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–12 metres (silty seabed — good buoyancy essential)
- **Current**: Moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The tugboat hull is progressively colonising with sponges and encrusting organisms. Groupers, sea bream, and moray eels are attracted to the wreck structure. Octopus and sea urchins inhabit the lower wreck and nearby seabed. The silty bottom around the wreck hosts garden eels and sand-dwelling species.

## Dive Profile

Boat dive, descend directly to the wreck at 30 metres. Explore the hull, wheelhouse, and engine area. Bottom time at this depth is limited — plan carefully. Maintain excellent buoyancy to avoid disturbing the silty seabed. Ascend along the wreck superstructure for a multi-level profile, with safety stop at 5 metres.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent buoyancy control is essential — the silty bottom clouds instantly
- Access and IDF clearance requirements should be confirmed locally due to proximity to Lebanese border area
- A torch illuminates the interior of the wheelhouse and lower sections
- The 13 March 1996 sinking date makes this a purpose-built artificial reef with decades of marine growth

## Safety Considerations

At 30 metres, recreational divers have limited no-decompression time. Plan conservatively. The silty bottom can reduce visibility rapidly. Carry an SMB.
