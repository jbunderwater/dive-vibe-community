## ריף שקמונה (Shikmona Reef)

A rocky reef in shallow water off the western tip of the Haifa Bay, at the site of ancient Tel Shikmona — an accessible shore dive at the foot of the Carmel ridge.

## Overview

Shikmona Reef (ריף שקמונה) lies off the western point of Haifa Bay, adjacent to Tel Shikmona — an ancient Phoenician archaeological mound located near Israel's National Institute of Oceanography. The reef is a ridge of stones extending from the shoreline, with depths ranging from 4 to 9 metres. It begins approximately 300 metres from shore, making it accessible as a shore dive.

The site offers a gentle introduction to Mediterranean diving in Israel's northern coast. The shallow rocky reef provides habitat for a variety of Mediterranean species, and crevices in the reef structure harbour interesting macro life.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Western Haifa Bay, Shikmona area, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 4–10 metres (affected by harbour conditions and season)
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Sea bream, groupers, wrasse, octopus, moray eels, and sea urchins inhabit the rocky reef structure. Nudibranchs and small crustaceans can be found in crevices. Sea turtles are occasionally seen.

## Dive Profile

A relaxed shallow dive — enter from shore and navigate approximately 300 metres to the reef edge. The reef provides a clear structure to follow in either direction. Depths of 4–9 metres allow extended bottom times. Suitable for Open Water level divers in calm conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check sea conditions before entry — Haifa Bay can be exposed in westerly weather
- The proximity to the National Institute of Oceanography means marine research activity in the area
- Good macro photography opportunities in reef crevices
- A wetsuit is recommended even in summer due to thermoclines
