## חדרה - כרישים (Hadera Sharks)

One of Israel's most extraordinary dive experiences, this site near the Orot Rabin power plant's warm-water discharge outlet draws large aggregations of sandbar sharks (Carcharhinus plumbeus) and dusky sharks (Carcharhinus obscurus) every winter. The warm water effluent — several degrees above ambient Mediterranean temperature — attracts primarily pregnant female dusky sharks and male sandbar sharks, creating an unusual seasonal congregation in the eastern Mediterranean.

## Overview

The sharks arrive reliably from November through March, with peak numbers typically in December and January. The phenomenon is studied by the Morris Kahn Marine Research Station at the University of Haifa, which has documented growing shark numbers over the years. Dives are conducted via boat from the Old Caesarea Diving Center, which offers guided shark dives as a dedicated package including two boat dives.

**Important note:** Following a fatal shark attack in April 2025, the site has been subject to changing access restrictions. Check with local dive operators for current status before planning a visit.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Hadera coast, near Orot Rabin power plant, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: Approximately 15-20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-10 meters (visibility is often reduced in this area)
- **Current**: Can be moderate to strong near the discharge outlet
- **Best Season**: November to March (shark aggregation season)
- **Prerequisites**: Minimum 30 logged dives; at least 2 Mediterranean shore dives or similar. Advanced Open Water certification or equivalent required.

## Marine Life

The primary draw is the shark aggregation: mostly sandbar sharks and dusky sharks — neither species is typically aggressive toward divers. Outside of shark season, the area supports typical Mediterranean species including grouper, sea bream, octopus, and moray eels.

## Safety Considerations

Advanced diver certification and meaningful logged dive experience are required by operators. Strong currents are possible near the warm discharge. Maintain calm behavior around sharks, avoid sudden movements, and follow your guide's instructions at all times. Do not attempt to touch or herd sharks. Check current operator requirements and access status, as restrictions have changed following the 2025 incident.

## Photography

Wide-angle photography is well suited to this site during shark season. The shallow, relatively murky conditions can challenge autofocus; manual override and strobe use are recommended.
