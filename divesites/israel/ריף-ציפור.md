## ריף ציפור (Bird Reef / Bird Head Canyon)

Bird Reef, also known locally as "Bird Head Canyon" or Mezizim Reef, is a shallow shore dive just north of the Gordon Reef area in Tel Aviv, named after a bird statue that stood on top of the hotel on the adjacent beach. The site features a natural crack running through the reef from east to west, creating a small canyon system.

## Overview

Located off Mezizim Beach (north Tel Aviv), this site offers beginner-friendly diving on a shallow rocky reef. The defining feature is a natural crack or channel through the reef running roughly east-west, wide enough for divers to swim through at depths of 9-12 meters. The canyon walls support a variety of Mediterranean marine life.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mezizim Beach area, north Tel Aviv / south Herzliya, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: Approximately 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-10 meters
- **Current**: Generally light
- **Best Time**: Autumn and spring

## Marine Life

Crown squirrelfish, cuttlefish, stingrays, sea bream, octopus, and typical Mediterranean shallow reef species. The canyon walls offer shelter for small cave-dwelling invertebrates.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from Mezizim Beach. Swim to the reef edge and locate the east-west running crack. Follow the channel through the reef. Depth is limited — a good site for new divers and students.
