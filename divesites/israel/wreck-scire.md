## Wreck Scire

The Scirè is a WWII Italian Navy submarine lying at 33 meters depth in Haifa Bay — one of the most historically significant dive sites on the Israeli Mediterranean coast.

## Overview

The Italian Regia Marina submarine Scirè (also spelled Scire) was an Adua-class submarine launched on 6 January 1938 at La Spezia, Italy. It achieved remarkable wartime success, participating in attacks on 28 Allied ships including the famous December 1941 raid on Alexandria harbour where its human torpedo (SLC) teams sank the British battleships HMS Queen Elizabeth and HMS Valiant. On 10 August 1942, the Scirè was detected by British intelligence as it approached Haifa Bay and was sunk by depth charges dropped by the British naval trawler HMS Islay, approximately 11 km from Haifa port. All crew perished.

The wreck lies on the seabed tilted approximately 25–30 degrees to port. It measures approximately 60 metres in length and displaces around 680 tonnes. Despite damage from depth charges and cannon fire — particularly visible at the bow — the wreck is in remarkably intact condition. Notable features include stern torpedo launch tubes with their doors intact, twisted metal debris, the forward keel, and three unexploded depth charges nearby. IANTD research expeditions (2008, 2011, 2015, 2017) in collaboration with the University of Haifa's Leon Recanati Institute of Maritime Studies have produced 3D reconstructions of the wreck. In 1984, the remains of 42 crew members were recovered from the rear torpedo room and interred in Bari, Italy.

In 2023, the wreck was declared Israel's first official underwater historical site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Haifa Bay, Israel, Mediterranean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–15 metres (conditions vary seasonally)
- **Current**: Variable; can be strong
- **Best Time**: May to October (calmer conditions)
- **Special Requirements**: Advanced certification required; technical diving for penetration

## Marine Life

The wreck is colonised by Mediterranean sponges, sea anemones, and encrusting organisms. Groupers, sea bream, and moray eels are commonly encountered around the structure. Octopus inhabit crevices in the wreckage.

## Dive Profile

At 33 metres, this site sits at the bottom of sport diving range. Divers should plan a direct descent to the wreck, allowing time to inspect the stern torpedo tubes, the bridge area, and the bow damage before ascending. Avoid disturbing silt. Penetration of the interior requires proper technical wreck training and must not be attempted recreationally. Plan for a safety stop at 5 metres and carry an SMB.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Boat diving only — no shore access to this site
- Dive with operators familiar with the specific wreck location
- IDF Navy clearance may be required — coordinate in advance
- Bring a dive torch for examining structural details
- The wreck is an officially protected historical site — nothing may be removed

## Safety Considerations

Three unexploded depth charges have been documented near the wreck — do not approach or disturb them. The 33-metre depth leaves limited bottom time on air. Decompression dive planning is recommended. Currents in Haifa Bay can be unpredictable. Visibility can deteriorate quickly if silt is disturbed.

## Photography

The Scirè offers exceptional wide-angle photographic opportunities. Torpedo tubes, the bridge silhouette, and the overall hull profile make iconic subjects. Bring a torch to illuminate interior details from outside penetration points.
