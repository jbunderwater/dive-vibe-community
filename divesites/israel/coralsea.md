## CORALSEA

CORALSEA is a shore-entry coral reef dive site in Eilat situated in the Coral Beach area, near the Coral World Underwater Observatory and within the protected marine corridor of Israel's Red Sea coast.

## Overview

CORALSEA sits in the heart of Eilat's premier diving zone, close to the Coral World Underwater Observatory at approximately lat 29.516. This area lies within or adjacent to the Coral Beach Nature Reserve, which spans 1.2 kilometers of the world's northernmost shallow coral reef. The reserve is home to over 100 species of coral and more than 650 species of marine life. The reef begins just meters from shore, making this one of the most accessible reef environments anywhere in the world. Visibility regularly exceeds 20 meters, and the Gulf of Aqaba's calm conditions persist year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Eilat, Coral Beach area, Red Sea, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Current**: Very light to none
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The Coral Beach zone is among Israel's richest marine environments. Divers encounter hard and soft corals of exceptional diversity, including Acropora, Porites, and table corals. Fish life includes lionfish, parrotfish, butterflyfish, anthias, grouper, and moray eels. The area adjacent to the Coral World Observatory has been protected for decades, supporting healthy reef fish populations. Sea turtles, octopus, and sea snakes are regularly reported. Nudibranchs and other invertebrates thrive in the rubble zones.

## Dive Profile

Entry from the shore along the protected reef zone. The reef starts at 3 meters deep and the slope descends to 20 meters and beyond. The shallower sections (3-10 meters) are rich in hard coral formations. Between 10 and 20 meters, the slope features larger coral structures and more open-water fish species. The Coral World Observatory structure itself, visible from the water, provides a dramatic reference point.

## Entry and Exit

If entering within the Coral Beach Nature Reserve, an entrance fee is required. Reserve entrances are controlled and staffed. Alternative free entry is possible from the adjacent Almog Beach section to the north. Facilities including showers, lockers, and a dive shop are available within the reserve grounds.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Combine with a visit to the Coral World Underwater Observatory for surface-level reef viewing
- Early morning entry gives best light for photography and highest fish activity
- The shallow sections (under 5m) are excellent for snorkelers and introductory divers
- Night diving is a highlight here — reef predators become active after dark

## Safety Considerations

The reserve enforces no-touching and no-collecting rules. Buoyancy control is essential to protect the coral. Fin carefully in shallow areas. Boat traffic is restricted within the reserve but watch for boats near the boundaries.
