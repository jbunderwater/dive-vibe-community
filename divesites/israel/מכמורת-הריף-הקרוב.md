## מכמורת הריף הקרוב (Michmoret Near Reef)

The closer of two reef dive sites off the village of Michmoret, a small fishing and beach community north of Netanya on Israel's Mediterranean coast. The "Near Reef" designation distinguishes this from the more distant "Far Reef" site approximately 500 meters further offshore.

## Overview

Michmoret sits at the mouth of the Alexander River and is known for its natural beaches and small-boat harbor. The rocky reefs offshore offer accessible Mediterranean diving. The Near Reef is the closer of the two, making it suitable for divers looking for a shorter swim from shore.

The reefs here are typical of the Israeli Mediterranean central coast: rocky limestone formations with sandy channels, hosting a characteristic assemblage of eastern Mediterranean marine life.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Michmoret village, north of Netanya, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: Approximately 10-15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-12 meters (Mediterranean variable)
- **Current**: Generally moderate; can strengthen in weather
- **Best Time**: Spring and autumn

## Marine Life

Sea bream, grouper, salema, octopus, moray eels, scorpionfish, nudibranchs, mullet, and invertebrates typical of the central Israeli Mediterranean coast.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from Michmoret beach, swimming out to the reef. The Near Reef is accessible with moderate surface swim. Good site for intermediate divers exploring the central coast.
