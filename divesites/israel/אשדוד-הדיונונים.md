## אשדוד - הדיונונים (Ashdod - The Cuttlefish)

אשדוד - הדיונונים — "Ashdod - The Cuttlefish" (דיונונים = cuttlefish in Hebrew) — is a shallow rocky reef site off the Ashdod coast. The name indicates this site is known for its cuttlefish encounters, which are frequently reported along Israel's central Mediterranean coast, particularly near the rocky areas south of Ashdod port.

## Overview

This site lies in the shallow coastal waters near Ashdod, where rock patches and sandy substrate create habitat for cephalopods, small reef fish, and invertebrates. Cuttlefish (Sepia officinalis) are seasonally common in the eastern Mediterranean and frequently gather near rocky substrate in the 5–12 metre depth range. The site is accessible from shore and suitable for beginner to intermediate divers.

The Ashdod coastline offers several small rocky reef areas alongside wreck sites. The southern breakwater of Ashdod port also creates artificial reef habitat nearby.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ashdod coast, central Mediterranean coast of Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–10 metres (variable; seasonal Mediterranean)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

Cuttlefish are the signature species at this site. Common Mediterranean reef fish include sea bream, grouper, salema, and wrasse. Octopus are frequently encountered in rocky crevices. Stingrays and guitarfish may be found on sandy patches. Mediterranean moray eels inhabit deeper rock formations.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the Ashdod coastal area. Access point requires local knowledge — contact a local dive operator in Ashdod for current entry point and conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Autumn and winter are peak cuttlefish season on Israel's Mediterranean coast
- Buoyancy control is important to avoid disturbing sand and reducing visibility
- Carry an SMB for surface visibility near the shipping lanes approaching Ashdod port
- Visibility improves significantly after summer — autumn to spring diving is recommended
