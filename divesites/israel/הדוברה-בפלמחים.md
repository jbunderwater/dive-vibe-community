## הדוברה בפלמחים (The Barge at Palmachim)

הדוברה בפלמחים — "The Barge at Palmachim" — is a shallow wreck dive located off Palmachim Beach, south of Tel Aviv. Palmachim is known for its rocky shallow reefs and two small old barge wrecks accessible from shore, making it one of the most approachable wreck dives on Israel's Mediterranean coast.

## Overview

This site is one of two barge wrecks in the Palmachim area. The wreck lies at very shallow depth — reported at no more than 7 metres — on the sandy bottom near the rocky shoreline of Palmachim National Park. The site is suitable for beginners and snorkelers as well as certified divers. The Palmachim coast features rocky bays protected within a national park, and the reef areas host a variety of Mediterranean marine life including octopus, crabs, worms, and squid in the crevices of the rocks surrounding the wreck.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Palmachim Beach, south of Tel Aviv
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–12 metres (variable; Mediterranean south coast)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

The Palmachim area is known for cuttlefish, octopus, small crabs, sea bream, and occasionally stingrays and guitarfish that frequent the rocky national park bays nearby. The shallow wreck provides shelter and artificial reef habitat.

## Entry and Exit

Enter from Palmachim Beach. Shore access is straightforward. Check national park regulations before diving — parts of the coastline may have access restrictions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Suitable for Open Water divers and even confident snorkelers
- Bring an SMB for surface visibility
- Dive conditions are best in autumn and winter when visibility is higher
- Pair this dive with the nearby second barge wreck in the same area
