## המערה הכחולה (Blue Cave)

A shallow sea cave accessible from the Dor-Habonim coastal trail, named for the brilliant blue light effects inside its water-filled interior.

## Overview

The Blue Cave (המערה הכחולה) is a natural sea cave located along the Dor-Habonim coastal trail, approximately 1 km south of Tel Dor near Nachsholim Beach. The cave was formed over thousands of years by wave erosion, dissolution, and partial collapse of the coastal limestone and sandstone. It takes its name from the striking blue light effects created by sunlight refracting through the underwater entrance and lighting up the interior pool.

The cave is accessible by swimming from the rocky coastline at the base of the Dor Habonim cliffs. Maximum depth is approximately 6–8 metres. The interior features the characteristic blue light that gives the site its name, visible from underwater and at the surface inside the cave. No specific-source confirmed diving records have been found — the site is known primarily as a snorkelling and swimming destination.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dor-Habonim coastal area, south of Tel Dor, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 3–8 metres
- **Current**: Mild; wave surge can be present
- **Best Time**: May to October (calm summer conditions)

## Marine Life

Typical rocky-coast Mediterranean species: sea bream, wrasse, sea urchins, octopus, and hermit crabs in and around the cave entrance.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Access via the red-marked Dor-Habonim coastal hiking trail
- Best visited in calm conditions — the cave entrance can be hazardous in swell
- Morning light reportedly creates the most dramatic blue interior lighting effects
- Combine with snorkelling in the lagoons at Nachsholim Bay nearby

## Safety Considerations

Cave entrance is submerged — assess swell and surge conditions before entry. The cave is small and visibility inside depends on light conditions and swell. Dive with a buddy.
