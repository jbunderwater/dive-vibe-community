## BRIDGE N

BRIDGE N is a shore-entry coral reef dive site in the southernmost section of Eilat's coastline, in the area of Neptune's Tables near the Princess Hotel bridge and Taba border crossing.

## Overview

BRIDGE N is located at the southern end of Eilat's diving coast, near where the Princess Hotel footbridge extends over the beach — one of the access points to the Neptune's Tables dive area. This southern zone, running from the Princess Hotel bridge area to the Taba border crossing, is part of one of Eilat's most extensive reef sections. Neptune's Tables — characterized by wide flat Acropora coral tables at varying depths — dominate this part of the coast. The site is open to all certification levels and is known for its excellent visibility, fed by gentle southerly currents that keep the water clear year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Eilat, near Princess Hotel bridge, Red Sea, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Current**: Gentle southerly drift; generally mild
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The southern reef areas are known for their diversity. Garden eels are commonly seen on sandy patches between coral formations. Butterflyfish, angelfish, parrotfish, and grouper are resident reef species. Napoleon (humphead) wrasse have been reported in the area. Sea turtles occasionally pass through. Stingrays rest on sandy areas between the coral tables.

## Dive Profile

Entry from shore near the bridge structure. A short swim leads to the reef edge where the slope descends gradually. The distinctive flat Acropora tables appear at depths between 6 and 25 meters. Divers can explore the plateau at shallower depths or follow the slope to deeper sections. Excellent for navigational diving as the bridge and shoreline provide clear reference points.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the beach near the Princess Hotel bridge. Note that access to the southernmost beaches in Eilat is unorganized — no lifeguard or facilities at this location. The beach is close to the Egyptian Taba border; bring identification if diving near the border zone.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Arrive early morning for best visibility and calmer surface conditions
- The flat coral tables provide dramatic wide-angle photography subjects
- Garden eel colonies on sandy patches are excellent for patient underwater photographers
- Check local conditions before diving as southern winds can occasionally affect surface conditions

## Safety Considerations

The southernmost Eilat sites are unorganized beaches with no lifeguard service. Dive with a buddy and carry an SMB. Be mindful of the proximity to the Egyptian border — diving zones end at the international border crossing.
