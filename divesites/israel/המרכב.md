## המרכב (The Markab)

המרכב is the dive site name for the Markab — an ancient Lebanese merchant ship scattered in the shallow waters near the Rosh Hanikra Marine Nature Reserve, its timbers long rotted away, leaving amphora and pottery fragments embedded among the rocky reef.

## Overview

The Markab (מרכב = vessel/ship in Arabic; also spelled Merkav) is an ancient Lebanese merchant ship that was making its way to the old port at Achziv when it foundered on the rocky reef near Rosh Hanikra. The ship's wooden structure has completely rotted away over the centuries, but the cargo remains: ancient amphora have fused with the surrounding rock, and pottery shards provide shelter for small marine creatures. The site sits in shallow water at about 8 metres depth within the confines of the Rosh Hanikra Marine Nature Reserve.

The site is listed by the Putsker Diving Club of Nahariya as one of the notable dive sites in the Rosh Hanikra region. Access requires coordination with local dive operators and IDF Navy clearance given proximity to the Lebanese border.

**Important conservation note:** Touching or removing any artefacts is strictly forbidden. The site is protected under Israeli antiquities law.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Rosh Hanikra, northern Israeli Mediterranean coast
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–12 metres
- **Current**: Mild; can increase with swell
- **Best Time**: May to October
- **Special Requirements**: IDF Navy clearance required; dive with licensed local guide

## Marine Life

The ancient amphora and pottery fragments provide microhabitat for a wealth of crustaceans, octopus, little crabs, and small reef fish. Typical northern Israeli Mediterranean species inhabit the surrounding rocky reef: sea bream, grouper, moray eels, and sea urchins.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Arrange access through Putsker Diving Club (Nahariya) or Indigo Diving Club (Shavei Zion)
- The amphora have become one with the rock — do not attempt to move or photograph them in a way that disturbs them
- Combine with the Rosh Hanikra Grottoes dive on the same boat trip for a full northern Israeli experience
