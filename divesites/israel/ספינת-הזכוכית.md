## ספינת הזכוכית (Glass Bottom Boat)

ספינת הזכוכית (Glass Bottom Boat) is a deep wreck dive site in Eilat's central reef zone, located approximately 500 meters north of the Coral World Underwater Observatory at a depth of 48-55 meters — beyond standard recreational diving limits.

## Overview

The Glass Bottom Boat (ספינת הזכוכית in Hebrew) is the wreck of a former tourist vessel fitted with glass panels in its hull for underwater viewing. The wreck lies at approximately 48-55 meters depth, close to the buoy of the Japanese Gardens. At this location — adjacent to the world's northernmost coral reef and near the landmark Coral World Underwater Observatory — the wreck rests in the deep zone of the reef slope. This depth range is beyond recreational diving limits (40 meters maximum for Advanced Open Water) and requires either technical diving training or a supervised deep specialty dive. The site is referenced in local diving sources and represents one of the more challenging wreck dives along Eilat's coast.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Eilat, near Coral World Underwater Observatory and Japanese Gardens buoy, Red Sea, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 55 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-35 meters
- **Current**: Light
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round (weather permitting for boat operations)

## Marine Life

At 48-55 meters, the wreck is in the deep reef zone where the character of marine life changes. The structure is likely colonized by sponges, soft corals, and encrusting organisms typical of deeper Red Sea habitats. Larger pelagic species including barracuda and jacks occasionally pass through the deep zone. The proximity to the Japanese Gardens means the surrounding reef is rich in biodiversity above the wreck's depth range.

## Dive Profile

Boat access is recommended. Descent along the reef slope or anchor line to 48-55 meters. Time at this depth is severely limited by decompression requirements — a brief exploration of the wreck exterior followed by a controlled ascent with appropriate stops. This profile requires Advanced or technical dive training. Gas planning must account for the deep stop and staged decompression if dived on air or nitrox.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access is strongly recommended given the depth and distance from shore. Coordinate with an Eilat dive operator experienced in deep diving. This site is not appropriate for independent shore diving given its depth.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Technical diving (trimix, decompression diving) extends bottom time significantly at this depth
- A free descent on the reef wall adjacent to the Japanese Gardens allows viewing of shallow and mid-water reef before committing to the deep wreck
- Dive on a calm day to minimize surface interval challenges for the boat crew
- The nearby Japanese Gardens provides an excellent decompression stop area on the reef wall above 20 meters

## Safety Considerations

**Depth warning: 48-55 meters is beyond recreational diving limits.** This dive requires Advanced Open Water certification at minimum, with a current deep dive log entry. Technical diving certification (tec-rec or equivalent) is strongly recommended. Narcosis is significant at this depth on air. This wreck should not be penetrated without technical cave or wreck certification. Always dive with a properly planned gas supply and a buddy experienced in deep diving.
