## תל אביב - ריף גורדון (Tel Aviv Gordon Reef / Gordon Caverns)

The Gordon Caverns (also called Gordon Reef) is one of Tel Aviv's best-known dive sites, lying just offshore of Gordon Beach at the northern end of Tel Aviv's beach strip. The site features a system of rocky caves and crevices in the Mediterranean reef, accessible via shore entry.

## Overview

The reef runs along the rocky shoreline near Gordon Beach. Large boulders and rocky outcrops have formed a small cave and tunnel system, creating interesting topography for divers and snorkelers. Depths range from the shallows to approximately 12 meters. The site is managed by local dive clubs, and visitors should ask for directions at the dive club inside the adjacent marina.

This is a popular beginner and training site due to its shallow, sheltered nature and easy shore access. It is also the starting point for Tel Aviv-area dive operators exploring the reef.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gordon Beach area, Tel Aviv, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-10 meters (can be reduced in summer)
- **Current**: Generally light; can increase in storm conditions
- **Best Time**: Autumn and spring for best visibility

## Marine Life

Octopus, lobsters, crabs, sea bream, moray eels, mullet, and various cave-dwelling species. The rock crevices shelter colorful species including crown squirrelfish and cuttlefish.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry at Gordon Beach. Swim out to the rocky reef and follow the boulders. Small cave systems at 6-12 meters depth. Suitable for Open Water level divers.

## Safety Considerations

Watch for surge in rough conditions. The site is close to a busy urban beach — be aware of swimmers, especially in summer. Always check conditions before entry.
