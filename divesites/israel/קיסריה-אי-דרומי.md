## קיסריה אי דרומי (Caesarea South Island)

A reef dive within the Caesarea Underwater Archaeological Park, located near the southern portion of the ancient Herodian harbor complex. This is part of Israel's first and only underwater archaeological national park, opened in 2006.

## Overview

The Caesarea Underwater Archaeological Park encompasses the remains of Sebastos, the artificial harbor built by Herod the Great between approximately 22 and 10 BCE. At the time of its construction, it was the largest artificial harbor in the Roman world. The harbor gradually subsided and sank over the following centuries, preserving the ancient infrastructure on the seabed.

The south island area features shallow reef structures with accessible archaeological remains. The park offers four marked underwater trails with numbered underwater signposts, and divers are equipped with waterproof maps. Depths in the main park area are generally 3-7 meters, making it accessible for snorkelers and entry-level divers alike.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Caesarea National Park, south harbor area
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-12 meters (Mediterranean variable)
- **Current**: Generally light within the protected harbor area
- **Best Time**: Spring and autumn

## What Divers See

Ancient harbor infrastructure including basalt slabs, concrete breakwater blocks, ashlar stone blocks, and piers from the Herodian period. Stone anchors representing the development of anchor design through antiquity. Typical Mediterranean marine life including sea bream, octopus, moray eels, grouper, and nudibranchs has colonized the ancient structures.

## Dive Profile

Shallow, protected reef suitable for beginners with certification. Follow the numbered trail markers with the waterproof map provided by the dive center. Contact Old Caesarea Diving Center (tel: 04-6265898) for guided dives.
