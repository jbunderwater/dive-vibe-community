## אמיר פארוק (Emir Farouk)

אמיר פארוק is the wreck of the Egyptian naval sloop El Amir Farouq (also written Emir Farouk), the former flagship of the Egyptian Navy, sunk on 22 October 1948 during Israel's War of Independence. The sinking is considered one of the most significant naval actions of the 1948 Arab-Israeli War.

## Overview

The El Amir Farouq was originally launched in 1926 as a British-built passenger cargo vessel before being converted to military service in 1936, when it became the flagship of the Egyptian Navy. During Operation Yoav on 22 October 1948, Israeli naval forces attacked the ship using explosive motorboats — the precursor to Israel's naval commando forces. The vessel was struck by two boats and sank within approximately five minutes with heavy casualties.

The wreck lies in the Mediterranean Sea off the southern Israeli/Gaza coast. Its precise resting depth is not confirmed in published diving sources; the coordinates here (31.5914, 34.3240) suggest a position well offshore in relatively deep water. No established recreational dive operation to this wreck has been documented in available sources.

## Historical Significance

The sinking of the Emir Farouk on 22 October 1948 marked a turning point in the 1948 war. Egyptian naval recruitment suffered significantly in the aftermath. The explosive motorboat tactics used in the attack directly influenced the formation of Israel's naval commando unit (Shayetet 13). The ship was built on the River Clyde in Great Britain.

## Safety Considerations

This site is far offshore in open water. Depth is unconfirmed but likely in the Advanced range. Verify current accessibility and legal status (waters near Gaza) before planning any dive. A capable dive vessel with surface support is required. No commercial recreational diving operation to this wreck is known to be currently operating.
