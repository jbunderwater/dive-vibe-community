## LightHouse

LightHouse (Migdalor Beach) is a shore-entry coral reef and cave dive site in the southern section of Eilat's Red Sea coast, named for the lighthouse overlooking the beach and known for its shallow cave formations and colorful pinnacle reef.

## Overview

LightHouse — known in Hebrew as Migdalor Beach — is located approximately 8 kilometers from central Eilat near the International University Institute for Marine Sciences. The site takes its name from the lighthouse on the hillside above. The diving character here is distinctive: a shallow rocky shore gives way to a broad plain at 3-5 meters depth, where a series of natural cave and arch formations provide shelter for diverse reef fish. At the edge of the shallow plain, the bottom drops away at a steep angle toward 30 meters and beyond. Nearby, the "Three Sisters" — three coral pinnacles approximately 100 meters apart, each about 5 meters tall — offer additional diving interest. The site is accessible from shore and is suitable for beginners in the shallower sections.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Eilat, Migdalor (Lighthouse) Beach, Red Sea, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-35 meters
- **Current**: Light; gentle southerly drift common in this area
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The cave and arch formations at 3-5 meters are home to a rich community of smaller reef fish: cardinalfish, glassfish, and squirrelfish shelter in overhangs, while pufferfish and lionfish cruise the surrounding reef. The slope supports grouper, parrotfish, and snapper. The Three Sisters coral pinnacles are particularly good for spotting stingrays resting on the sandy patches between them. The nearby University beach area hosts marine research equipment and additional reef habitats. Octopus are commonly seen in the rocky entry zone.

## Dive Profile

Entry from the rocky shore. The first notable feature is a large shallow plain at 3-5 meters, where cave formations (protected by netting) and arch structures provide excellent marine life density. From the edge of the plain, the slope drops steeply toward 30 meters. For a shallower dive, explore the caves and then swim laterally along the plain. For a deeper profile, follow the slope down and return along the 15-20 meter depth band.

## Entry and Exit

Rocky shore entry — water shoes or booties are recommended. The entry point is on the rocky side of Migdalor Beach (adjacent to the restaurant/buffet side). The University dive site is accessed from the other side of the same beach. Facilities (including a beach bar/restaurant) are available at the beach.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The cave section at 3-5 meters is excellent for beginners and snorkelers
- Bring a torch for exploring the arch and cave formations
- Visit Three Sisters by swimming 100-200 meters south of the main entry
- The site connects to the University dive site on the northern rocky shore section

## Safety Considerations

The rocky entry can be slippery — take care getting in and out of the water. The cave section is protected by netting; do not attempt to enter the netted areas. Be careful with buoyancy near the cave formations to avoid damage to coral. The deeper slope requires careful air management.
