## סטי"ל אח"י כידון (INS Kidon Missile Boat)

The Hebrew-language entry for the INS Kidon missile boat wreck — a decommissioned Israeli Navy Sa'ar 4.5-class missile boat sunk as an artificial reef and war memorial at approximately 26 metres depth.

## Overview

סטי"ל אח"י כידון is the Hebrew designation for the INS Kidon (אח"י = Ach"i = Israeli Navy Ship; כידון = Kidon = "javelin"; סטי"ל = patrol missile boat). This Hebrew-language site entry corresponds to the same wreck as "Wreck Kidon," located approximately 3 km offshore near Nahariya.

The INS Kidon was deliberately sunk after decommissioning to create an artificial reef and to serve as an underwater memorial to twelve IDF Naval Commandos (Shayetet 13) killed in a 1997 Hezbollah ambush near Ansariya, Lebanon. Alongside the sunken vessel, a monument of twelve chairs arranged in bat-wing formation — each bearing the name of one of the fallen — rests at the bow at 26 metres depth.

See also: Wreck Kidon.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern Mediterranean coast, Israel (near Nahariya/Shavei Zion area)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–12 metres
- **Current**: Moderate
