## אפולוניה (Apollonia)

Apollonia (Arsuf) is an ancient coastal site just north of Herzliya, featuring both a well-preserved Crusader fortress on the clifftop and underwater reef structures below the shoreline. The site sits within the Apollonia National Park.

## Overview

The underwater area off Apollonia consists of shallow rocky reefs immediately below the coastal cliffs. Divers and snorkelers can explore the natural rock formations along the base of the cliff, where typical Mediterranean species inhabit crevices and ledges. The Reef Israel Diving and Surfing Club is located near Apollonia National Park at the Dan Acadia Hotel in Herzliya and operates dives in this area.

The onshore Apollonia fortress dates from the Crusader period (12th–13th centuries), built on the site of earlier Phoenician, Hellenistic, and Roman settlements. The ancient harbor structures are partially submerged, and limited archaeological remains may be visible in the shallows.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Herzliya, below Apollonia National Park cliffs
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-15 meters
- **Current**: Generally light; can increase in strong weather
- **Best Time**: Spring and autumn for best visibility

## Marine Life

Typical Mediterranean shallow reef species: octopus, moray eels, sea bream, grouper, mullet, scorpionfish, nudibranchs, and invertebrates in rocky crevices.

## Dive Profile

A gentle shore entry leads to rocky reef structures in the shallows. The site is accessible to Open Water level divers. Explore the rock ledges and crevices along the cliff base. Maximum depth is limited by the natural topography.

## Safety Considerations

Watch for surge near the cliff base in rough weather. Check conditions before entering — this is an exposed coastal site. Be aware of any boating traffic in the area.
