## BUOY 2

BUOY 2 is a shore-entry coral reef dive site in Eilat's central diving corridor, marked by a navigation buoy that serves as a reference point for divers along the Coral Beach area.

## Overview

BUOY 2 is one of several numbered buoy marker positions along Eilat's central Red Sea coastline, falling roughly in the area between the Coral Beach Nature Reserve and the northern resort zone. In Eilat, numbered buoys delineate swimming zones and serve as underwater navigation landmarks. The site sits in the Coral Beach corridor where the reef slope drops off close to shore. Like most of Eilat's central coastal strip, this area features coral gardens on a sand-and-rubble slope that can be reached within minutes of shore entry. The Gulf of Aqaba's calm, current-free conditions make buoy-marked sites accessible to beginners and experienced divers alike.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Central Eilat coastline, Red Sea, Israel (Coral Beach corridor)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-35 meters
- **Current**: Very light; Gulf of Aqaba has minimal tidal movement
- **Water Temperature**: 21°C (winter) to 26°C (summer)
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

The central Eilat reef corridor supports a broad range of Red Sea species. Coral formations host anthias, chromis, and damselfish. Larger reef fish including grouper, snapper, and parrotfish are common. Lionfish perch in coral overhangs. Octopus and moray eels inhabit crevices in the reef structure. Sea turtles are occasionally sighted on the reef slope.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore and follow the sandy approach out to the reef edge. The buoy marks a fixed reference point that helps with navigation and surface orientation. The reef slope descends from approximately 3 meters to 20 meters. Most diving is conducted along the slope between 5 and 15 meters where coral cover is densest.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the central Eilat beach zone. Use the numbered buoy as a surface navigation marker. Water conditions are generally calm year-round. Exit at the same entry point, using the buoy as a reference from underwater.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The buoy itself often attracts small fish congregating around its line
- Good site for night dives given calm conditions and proximity to facilities
- Bring a compass for underwater navigation between buoy markers
- Combine with nearby sites for a full day of diving in the central corridor

## Safety Considerations

Watch for occasional surface boat traffic near buoy markers. Stay within the designated swim zones. Currents are minimal but an SMB is always recommended for ascents.
