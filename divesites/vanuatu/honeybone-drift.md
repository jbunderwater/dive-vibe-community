# Honeybone Drift

A relaxed drift dive on the incoming tide along the outer southern side of Mele Bay, running from the Honeybone reef to the Twin Bommies near Port Vila Harbour. Gorgonians, nudibranchs, and reef fish in warm, clear water.

## Overview

Honeybone Drift follows the incoming tidal current from the Honeybone reef area to the Twin Bommies, located outside Port Vila Harbour on the southern side of Mele Bay. The dive covers a stretch of reef decorated with gorgonian sea fans and inhabited by a rich variety of invertebrate and fish life.

The highlight of the drift is reaching the Twin Bommies -- two large coral formations that rise from approximately 10 meters depth to within 5 meters of the surface. The bommies feature overhangs that shelter fish and invertebrates, making them an excellent spot to linger before ascending. Visibility averages around 20 meters and water temperature sits between 26-28 degrees C year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Outside Port Vila Harbour, Mele Bay, Efate, Vanuatu
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: ~20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 26-28 degrees C
- **Current**: Mild to moderate (incoming tide)
- **Best Time**: April to November; dive timed with incoming tide

## Marine Life

The reef along the drift route hosts gorgonian sea fans, nudibranchs on the coral surfaces, crocodilefish resting on sandy patches, anemones with clownfish, and titan triggerfish. The Twin Bommies concentrate fish life around their overhangs, including schools of small reef fish and the occasional larger predator.

## Dive Profile

Enter upstream at the Honeybone reef area and drift with the incoming tidal current. The bottom ranges from 10-27 meters along the route, with most of the interesting terrain between 10-20 meters. The drift terminates at the Twin Bommies, where the formations rise to 5 meters and provide an excellent location for an extended safety stop while exploring the overhangs.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Port Vila operators. The boat drops divers upstream at the Honeybone reef, then follows the group or repositions to pick up at the Twin Bommies. Deploy an SMB during ascent for boat pickup. The dive must be timed with the incoming tide for proper current direction.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a macro lens for the nudibranchs and crocodilefish along the drift route
- The overhangs at Twin Bommies are excellent for close-up photography with natural light filtering from above
- Best done as a morning dive when light penetrates the east-facing sections of the reef

## Safety Considerations

The current on this drift is generated by tidal flow and is generally mild to moderate. Maintain good buoyancy to avoid contact with the reef. Always carry an SMB for signaling the pickup boat.
