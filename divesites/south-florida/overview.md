## South Florida

Year-round warm-water diving along the Southeast Florida Reef Tract, from the Blue Heron Bridge macro paradise in Palm Beach to Gulf Stream drift dives off Fort Lauderdale and over 100 reef and wreck sites.

## Description

South Florida features 12 documented dive sites spanning from Key Biscayne north to Palm Beach County, with depths ranging from 3 to 24 meters. The region sits along the Southeast Florida Reef Tract, the only living coral reef system in the continental United States, running parallel to shore with reef lines remarkably close to the beach. Lauderdale-by-the-Sea is recognized as the "Shore Diving Capital of South Florida" with living reef just 100-400 feet from the beach. The Gulf Stream current passes close to shore, delivering warm, clear water and enabling signature drift diving where divers float along the reef while the boat follows their bubbles.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Wreck Diving**: 5 wreck sites including the SS Inchulva/Delray Wreck (1903 steamship at 7.6m), Lofthus (1898 shipwreck on the National Register of Historic Places), SS Copenhagen (popular underwater preserve with 10,000 annual visitors), Tenneco Towers (five decommissioned oil platforms at 18-61m), and the Deerfield Beach Artificial Reef complex
- **Reef Diving**: 6 reef sites including Blue Heron Bridge (world-class macro diving at Phil Foster Park), Anglins Pier Reef (shore-accessible living reef), Breakers Reef (drift dive off Palm Beach), Haulover Ledge, Neptune Memorial Reef (underwater memorial designed as Lost City of Atlantis), and Lauderdale-by-the-Sea Beach
- **Shore Diving**: 3 shore-accessible sites at Blue Heron Bridge, Anglins Pier Reef, and Lauderdale-by-the-Sea Beach, with the SS Inchulva also reachable from shore
- **Boat Diving**: 9 boat-access sites with short rides due to the reef tract's proximity to shore
- **Drift Diving**: Prevailing Gulf Stream currents run parallel to the coastline, making drift diving the standard technique at most boat sites

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport (FLL) is centrally located for the region's dive sites. Palm Beach International Airport (PBI) serves the northern sites including Blue Heron Bridge and Breakers Reef. Miami International Airport (MIA) is an alternative for southern sites.
- **Dive Operators**: South Florida Diving Headquarters in Pompano Beach operates a fleet of three boats including two 45-foot catamarans accommodating up to 35 divers. Sea Experience in Fort Lauderdale was voted one of the Best Dive Operators in North America by Scuba Diving Magazine in 2026. Pura Vida Divers in Riviera Beach specializes in Blue Heron Bridge and Palm Beach reef dives. Jupiter Dive Center offers reef, wreck, and shark dives. Scuba Network in Fort Lauderdale has over 40 years of experience.
- **Accommodation**: Fort Lauderdale and Lauderdale-by-the-Sea offer the widest selection of lodging within minutes of both shore and boat diving. Riviera Beach is the closest base for Blue Heron Bridge and Palm Beach County sites. Deerfield Beach and Pompano Beach are centrally located alternatives.
- **Transportation**: Rental car recommended for accessing sites spread across the region, though many shore dives are walkable from nearby hotels. Fort Lauderdale boat operators are clustered near Hillsboro Inlet (Pompano Beach/Deerfield) and Port Everglades.
- **Facilities**: Extensive dive shop infrastructure throughout the region. Air fills, nitrox, equipment rental, and certification courses available at numerous locations. Blue Heron Bridge at Phil Foster Park has free parking, restrooms, and pavilions.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature ranges from 22-29°C (72-84°F) year-round, remaining above 24°C (75°F) most of the year due to Gulf Stream influence. Visibility ranges from 10-30 meters (33-98 feet), best in summer when the Gulf Stream flows closest to shore. Drift diving is standard procedure at most boat sites due to consistent northward current. Blue Heron Bridge is best dived at high-tide slack water, providing 45 minutes to 2 hours of calm conditions.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Blue Heron Bridge is a world-renowned macro photography destination with seahorses, pipefish, frogfish, batfish, flying gurnards, and mimic octopuses. Reef sites support nurse sharks, Caribbean reef sharks, sea turtles, eagle rays, stingrays, barracuda, groupers, snappers, hogfish, and blue tangs. Tenneco Towers attracts bull sharks and large barracuda schools. Tarpon are common around the shallow wreck sites. Lobster, scorpionfish, and angelfish are abundant across the reef tract.
- **Conservation**: The Southeast Florida Reef Tract is the only shallow coral reef system in the continental United States. Broward County operates a comprehensive artificial reef program with over 76 deployed artificial reef sites. The Lofthus wreck is designated as Florida's eighth Underwater Archaeological Preserve. Neptune Memorial Reef combines memorial services with habitat creation.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: Year-round diving is South Florida's greatest advantage. Summer (June-September) offers the warmest water and best visibility. Winter (December-March) brings slightly cooler water but coincides with lobster season and typically good conditions. Blue Heron Bridge is diveable year-round but always plan around high tide for best conditions.
- **Currency**: US Dollar (USD)
- **Language**: English
- **Safety**: Gulf Stream currents can be strong and variable; drift diving requires surface marker buoy (SMB) deployment. Blue Heron Bridge requires a dive flag by law and divers must avoid the boat channel running 30 meters from shore. Tenneco Towers dives to 60+ meters require Advanced or Technical certification. Shore entries at Lauderdale-by-the-Sea can have surge during swell events. Multiple hyperbaric chambers available in the region including at Broward Health Medical Center.
