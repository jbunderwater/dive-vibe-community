## Nama Point

Nama Point (Namalatu) is a scenic wall dive named after a local beach whose name means "Name of the King." This coral reef wall is covered with sea fans and gorgonians, creating a stunning visual landscape.

## Overview

Nama Point is a beautiful wall dive site featuring a vertical coral wall covered extensively with sea fans and gorgonians. The name Namalatu comes from words meaning "Nama" (Name) and "Latu" (King). The site is located on a beach named after these words, and the underwater topography mirrors the dramatic land formations. Visibility typically ranges from 15-25 meters, with water temperatures averaging 26-28°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ambon Bay (eastern area near beach)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: March-April and September-December (dry season)

## Marine Life

Densely covered in sea fans and gorgonians providing shelter for reef fish. Typical inhabitants include reef sharks, trevally, grouper, snapper, wrasse, and various smaller reef fish. The wall structure and coral coverage attract crustaceans and other invertebrates.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the top of the wall at approximately 5-8 meters depth. Follow the wall profile downward, exploring the sea fan-covered slopes between 8-18 meters depth. The gorgonians and sea fans are concentrated on the upper portions of the wall. Plan dives to explore different sections of the wall across multiple visits.

## Entry and Exit

Boat entry via giant stride or back roll to the wall top area. Alternatively, wade entry from the beach may be possible depending on conditions. Follow the dive guide to the wall structure and descend along its slope. Ascend along the wall and surface near the boat or beach for exit.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent wide-angle photography opportunities with sea fan formations
- Move slowly to observe fish behavior within the coral
- Macro photography in coral crevices yields interesting subjects
- The site is excellent for identifying coral species
- Respect the delicate gorgonians and sea fans—avoid contact
- Multiple dives reveal new areas of the wall
- Good visibility and calm conditions during dry season

## Safety Considerations

Moderate current requires good buoyancy control, but conditions are generally favorable. Watch depth on the wall to avoid exceeding Intermediate limits. Maintain awareness of your position relative to the boat or shore. The healthy coral makes this a natural buoyancy control practice site. Always dive with a buddy and maintain good communication.

## Photography

Wall dives offer stunning wide-angle opportunities with dramatic depth perspectives. The interplay of light and shadow along the wall creates compelling compositions. Look for macro subjects in wall crevices and overhangs.
