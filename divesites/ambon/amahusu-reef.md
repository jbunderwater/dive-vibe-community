## Amahusu Reef

Amahusu Reef is a colorful coral reef dive site in Ambon Bay, famous for its resident colony of photogenic Saron shrimp with distinctive Captain America-style markings.

## Overview

Amahusu Reef is a coral reef dive site in Ambon Bay featuring healthy coral structures and a special colony of Saron shrimp (cleaner shrimp). These rarely seen shrimp are excellent photography subjects with their striking Captain America red, white, and blue color patterns. The reef offers gentle diving conditions with good visibility for a Beginner-level site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ambon Bay, near Laha area
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Minimal to light
- **Best Time**: March-April and September-December (dry season)

## Marine Life

The site's primary attraction is the colony of Saron shrimp (cleaner shrimp) with their distinctive Captain America-style red, white, and blue markings. These are rarely seen and highly photogenic shrimp. The reef also hosts typical reef fish including wrasse, snappers, grouper, anthias, and various smaller reef species. Sea fans and coral formations provide habitat diversity.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the shallow reef at 5-8 meters depth and explore the coral formations where the Saron shrimp colony resides. The entire site is relatively shallow and compact, suitable for 45-50 minute dives. Focus on careful observation and photography of the shrimp and coral structures rather than covering large distances.

## Entry and Exit

Sandy shore entry with minimal surge. Descend to the reef and navigate by coral formations and landmarks. The site is close to shore, making navigation simple. Exit at the same location.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent photography site for the rare Saron shrimp
- Bring macro lens to capture the shrimp details and markings
- Go slowly and look carefully within coral crevices for shrimp
- The shrimp are resident—multiple dives offer consistent encounters
- Beginner-friendly for building reef diving skills and coral ID
- Early morning dives offer best light and clearest water
- Respect the delicate coral structures—avoid contact

## Safety Considerations

Minimal current and shallow depth make this very safe for Beginner divers. Watch for sea urchins and sharp coral. Use proper buoyancy to avoid damaging coral formations. The calm conditions make this an excellent site for building basic diving skills. Always dive with a buddy and follow established entry and exit routes.

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
