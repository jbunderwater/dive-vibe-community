## Pintu Kota

Pintu Kota — "Gate of the City" — is Ambon's most iconic south-coast dive, a lava-honeycombed headland where a spectacular underwater arch at 17 meters, draped in gorgonians and sea fans, creates cathedral-like chambers to swim through.

## Overview

Pintu Kota sits on a small headland on Ambon's south coast, approximately one hour by boat from Ambon Bay, and is typically paired with Hukurila Cave for a full south-coast dive day. The defining feature is a large natural underwater arch at approximately 15–17 meters depth — one of several archways that honeycomb the headland, creating the impression of "large gates." Above water, matching rock arches frame the sea; below, the equivalent formations are draped in gorgonian fans and soft corals and lit by shafts of filtered light from the surface.

The dive profile works down to the arch at 17 meters, enters the cathedral-like chamber, then works back up to the arch's highest point at approximately 7 meters to finish the dive at a shallow depth. The slope on the far side of the arch drops steeply. Visibility on the south coast is typically much better than inner Ambon Bay — 20–30+ meters is reported on good days (starfish.ch documents 30+ meter visibility possible at this site).

Sources consistently classify Pintu Kota as an arch/cave type site rather than an open reef, though the surrounding slope is coral-covered. This entry is corrected from the prior "reef" classification.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South coast of Ambon Island, approximately 1 hour from Ambon Bay by boat
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters (south coast, Indian Ocean water)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; can strengthen at the headland
- **Best Time**: Dry season (October to April); weather-dependent access

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at Pintu Kota from independent sources: Napoleon wrasse, gorgonian sea fans (colonising the arch), nudibranchs on the arch surfaces, schools of reef fish (snappers, fusiliers, jacks) aggregating around the arch opening. Bottles-up-diving.com records occasional manta ray encounters in the area around the headland. The outer headland is exposed to larger pelagic influence from the Indian Ocean side. Inside the arch chamber, the gorgonian growth and light conditions attract dense aggregations of small fish. No site-specific confirmed records of whale sharks have been found in independent sources.

## Dive Profile

Boat approach from Ambon City. The dive works from the reef top down to the arch at 17 meters, through the arch chamber — the "city gates" — and back up to the shallower exit at approximately 7 meters. The steep slope on the far side of the arch must be watched carefully to avoid drifting deep. Allow adequate air for the return traverse and a safety stop at 5 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive; entry by giant stride or back roll. The south coast is only accessible in calm sea conditions — check weather before committing. A mooring may be available; if not, drift approach. Surface with SMB for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- South-coast access is weather-dependent — have a backup bay dive plan
- Combine with Hukurila Cave for a full south-coast day
- The midday sun angle creates the best light shafts through the arch into the chamber
- Wide-angle photography is the primary draw — bring your biggest dome port
- Look for nudibranchs on the gorgonian surfaces inside the arch

## Safety Considerations

Headland location means currents can strengthen unpredictably. The steep slope on the far side of the arch makes depth awareness critical — monitor depth throughout the arch swim-through. South-coast sea state can make boat handling challenging; coordinate with operators experienced on this route.

## Photography

Pintu Kota is one of Indonesia's great wide-angle dive photography destinations. The archway framing, combined with natural light shafts and gorgonian-draped walls, makes every composition dramatic. Shoot divers silhouetted in the arch opening, or aim toward the surface light for backlit gorgonian shots. The exterior reef offers macro opportunities on the sponge-covered rock faces.
