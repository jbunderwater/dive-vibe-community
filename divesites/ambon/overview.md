## Ambon

The muck diving capital of the Banda Sea, home to the psychedelic frogfish found virtually nowhere else on Earth and world-class macro photography on volcanic black sand.

## Description

Ambon Bay in Maluku Province is one of the world's premier muck diving destinations, rivaling Lembeh Strait for critter diversity. The database documents 10 dive sites with depths ranging from 8 to 35 meters. The bay's volcanic black sand slopes and rubble fields support an extraordinary density of rare macro life, including the endemic psychedelic frogfish first described by science in 2009. Beyond muck, Ambon offers a cave dive, a WWII-era wreck, wall diving, and healthy reef sites.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Muck Diving**: 5 sites (Laha, Rhino City, Psychedelic Frogfish Site, Air Manis, Twilight Zone) on volcanic sand slopes, the core of Ambon's world-class macro diving
- **Shore Diving**: 6 sites accessible from shore, primarily along the Laha coastline of inner Ambon Bay
- **Wreck Diving**: 1 site (Duke of Sparta Wreck) at 35 meters depth with corals and sponges colonizing the hull
- **Cave Diving**: 1 site (Hukurila Cave) on the outer coast at 20 meters
- **Wall Diving**: 1 site (Nama Point) at 18 meters
- **Reef Diving**: 2 sites (Pintu Kota, Amahusu Reef) offering contrast to the muck sites

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Fly to Pattimura Airport (AMQ) in Ambon, with direct flights from Jakarta, Makassar, and Surabaya. Ambon is well-connected by Indonesian domestic airlines including Garuda, Lion Air, and Sriwijaya Air.
- **Dive Operators**: Dive Into Ambon and Mikumba Diving are the primary operators specializing in muck diving. Several liveaboard vessels use Ambon as an embarkation point for Banda Sea itineraries.
- **Accommodation**: Ambon city has a range of hotels from budget to mid-range. Dive resorts are located near the Laha dive sites on the inner bay.
- **Transportation**: Dive sites along Laha are reached by short boat rides or shore entry. Outer sites like Hukurila Cave and Pintu Kota require longer boat transfers.
- **Facilities**: Ambon is a provincial capital with hospitals, ATMs, and standard amenities. No hyperbaric chamber on Ambon; nearest recompression facilities are in Makassar.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature ranges from 26-29C year-round. Visibility on muck sites is typically 10-25 meters, with October generally offering the best conditions. The sheltered inner bay provides calm diving conditions most of the year.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: The psychedelic frogfish (Histiophryne psychedelica) is Ambon's signature species, most reliably spotted around October. The bay supports multiple frogfish species, flamboyant cuttlefish, Rhinopias scorpionfish, ghost pipefish, pygmy seahorses, mandarinfish, blue-ringed octopus, mimic octopus, weedy scorpionfish, and an extraordinary variety of nudibranchs, crustaceans, and cephalopods. The Duke of Sparta wreck hosts lionfish and scorpionfish around its debris field.
- **Conservation**: Ambon Bay's dive sites are informally protected by the dive operators who maintain site stewardship. The bay faces pressures from development and fishing but the dive sites remain productive.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: March to May and September to November offer the best conditions. October is historically the peak month for psychedelic frogfish sightings. December to February brings the rainy season with reduced visibility. Ambon is diveable year-round due to the sheltered bay.
- **Currency**: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
- **Language**: Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia) and Ambonese Malay
- **Safety**: Most muck sites are shallow and suitable for beginners, making Ambon accessible to a wide range of divers. The Duke of Sparta wreck at 35 meters and Hukurila Cave require intermediate to advanced skills. Standard tropical diving precautions apply. Ambon has better medical infrastructure than most remote Indonesian dive destinations, though no hyperbaric chamber.
