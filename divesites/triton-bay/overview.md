## Triton Bay

Indonesia's remote soft coral frontier in West Papua, where whale sharks gather at traditional fishing platforms and endemic species thrive in nutrient-rich waters.

## Description

Triton Bay lies within the Kaimana Marine Protected Area in West Papua, one of the last true frontiers of Indonesian diving. Dive sites range in depth across the bay, with depths ranging from 8 to 35 meters. First surveyed for diving in 2006, the area continues to yield new sites each year. Triton Bay is renowned for its extraordinary soft coral growth fueled by nutrient-rich upwellings, whale shark encounters at bagan fishing platforms, and endemic species including the Triton Bay walking shark and flasher wrasse found nowhere else.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: All sites are boat-access, reached from Triton Bay Divers resort or liveaboard vessels
- **Wall Diving**: Sites including Namatota Channel, Iris Wall, and Aiduma Island Wall, featuring deep drop-offs draped in black coral and soft corals
- **Drift Diving**: Site (Whale Shark Bagan) in open blue water near traditional bagan fishing platforms, best at new moon from October to April
- **Reef Diving**: Sites ranging from shallow coral gardens (The Corals Garden at 12m) to boulder reefs with strong currents (Little Komodo at 25m)

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Fly to Kaimana (KNG) in West Papua, typically via Jakarta or Makassar with connections through Ambon or Sorong. From Kaimana, a boat transfer reaches the dive sites. This is a genuinely remote destination with limited flight frequency.
- **Dive Operators**: Triton Bay Divers is the only dedicated dive resort in the area, situated in Kaimana Regency. Several liveaboard operators include Triton Bay on Raja Ampat-to-Kaimana itineraries.
- **Accommodation**: Triton Bay Divers offers the sole resort-based accommodation. Kaimana town has basic hotels. Liveaboard vessels provide the main alternative.
- **Transportation**: Boat transfers from Kaimana to dive sites. No road access to the diving areas.
- **Facilities**: Very limited. The resort is self-contained. There is no hyperbaric chamber in Kaimana; the nearest is in Makassar or Manado.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature ranges from 27-29C. Visibility is often reduced to 10-15 meters due to the nutrient-rich plankton that feeds the bay's extraordinary soft coral growth and attracts whale sharks. Currents range from mild to strong depending on site and tidal conditions.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Triton Bay sits within the Coral Triangle and supports endemic species including the Triton Bay epaulette shark (walking shark) and several flasher wrasse species. The bay hosts whale sharks (both adults and juveniles) that feed around bagan fishing platforms, pygmy seahorses, Napoleon wrasse, giant groupers, eagle rays, schooling barracuda and jacks, and dense soft coral communities including massive black coral bushes.
- **Conservation**: The Kaimana Marine Protected Area covers over 597,000 hectares, established with support from Conservation International. Local communities participate in conservation through traditional fishing practices.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: October to early June (the dive season). Whale sharks are most reliably seen October to April, with best encounters around new moon periods. The bay is generally closed to diving from mid-June through September.
- **Currency**: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
- **Language**: Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia), with local Papuan languages spoken in villages
- **Safety**: Strong currents at wall and channel sites require intermediate to advanced skills. The remote location means limited medical facilities; the nearest hyperbaric chamber is a long flight away. Divers should carry dive insurance with evacuation coverage.
