## Iris Wall

Iris Wall is an Advanced wall dive in the Iris Strait separating Aiduma Island from Triton Bay's mainland. This site features a dramatic vertical drop adjacent to Saruenus Island, with nutrient-rich currents that concentrate fish life and support exceptional soft coral growth on the wall structure.

## Overview

Iris Wall is located in the Iris Strait, the channel between Aiduma Island and the mainland where Triton Bay's most nutrient-rich waters flow. The site features a vertical wall that drops from shallow exploration depths to 35+ meters, with the most interesting diving concentrated between 12-35 meters. The currents that create challenging conditions at depth are actually ecological life-support systems—nutrient-rich water flowing through the strait supports the prolific fish life and soft coral gardens characteristic of Triton Bay. This is an Advanced site requiring strong depth management and current awareness.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Iris Strait between Aiduma Island and mainland, Triton Bay, West Papua
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters (30-65 feet)
- **Current**: Medium to strong (nutrient delivery system)
- **Best Time**: October to April
- **Water Temperature**: 27-30°C

## Marine Life

The nutrient-rich water concentrates fish life exceptionally densely. Expect schools of jacks, barracuda, trevally, snappers, and countless reef fish. Larger specimens include giant groupers, Napoleon wrasse, hunting predators, and black coral bushes providing habitat for ambush hunters. The wall structure supports soft coral growth and crevice-dwelling species. Eagle rays, manta rays, and occasional whale sharks are possible. The concentrated fish biomass is visually remarkable compared to shallow sites.

## Dive Profile

Begin the dive at the wall top (10-12 meters) where fish concentration is greatest. Descend the wall in controlled stages, exploring crevices and coral formations between 15-35 meters. The wall typically descends beyond 35 meters, but stay within conservative depth limits. Most interesting features and marine life are found between 12-32 meters. The middle section of your dive is ideal for deeper exploration while air consumption remains manageable. Reserve the final section for shallower reefs and safety stops.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by dive boat. Entry is via giant stride or back roll. Descend along the wall structure rather than free-descending into the blue. Strong currents are characteristic—follow guide direction for descent and buoyancy control. Expect to work against current; maintain awareness of current direction and depth. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop. Maintain constant buddy contact and communication with boat crew.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced certification with strong depth management skills is essential
- Pre-dive briefing on current direction is critical; understand the flow
- Monitor depth gauge continuously; the wall encourages unintentional deep descents
- Scout wall crevices and coral features for fish activity
- Bring wide-angle equipment—the dramatic wall and dense fish schools reward photography
- Plan profiles carefully; conserve air for the deeper wall exploration (you'll use more at depth)
- Understand buoyancy control requirements in current
- This site is best reserved for experienced Advanced divers

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification is required. Strong currents are characteristic of this site; they are the reason the site is biodiverse but also represent a hazard. Maintain active depth management and stay in frequent visual contact with your buddy. Monitor air consumption carefully—deeper dives consume air faster. Be prepared to ascend if current exceeds pre-briefing parameters or if you exceed comfortable depth/air margins. Avoid diving if conditions deteriorate beyond briefing expectations. Always carry appropriate signaling devices (SMB). This is a rewarding but technically demanding Advanced dive site.

## Photography

Wall dives offer stunning wide-angle opportunities with dramatic depth perspectives. The interplay of light and shadow along the wall creates compelling compositions. Look for macro subjects in wall crevices and overhangs.
