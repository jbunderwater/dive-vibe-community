# Sabang Point

A current-affected site on eastern Sabang Bay with sand and coral outcrops, turtles, large pufferfish, porcupinefish, garden eels, and a reliable selection of nudibranchs and octopus.

## Overview

Sabang Point sits on the eastern side of Sabang Bay, where currents sweep around the headland and bring nutrients that support a varied community of marine life. The bottom is a mix of sand and coral outcrops, providing different habitats in close proximity. Turtles are regularly encountered here, along with impressively large pufferfish and porcupinefish. The deeper sandy areas host colonies of garden eels, while the coral outcrops harbour nudibranchs, egg cowries, and octopus. Visibility is typically around 15 meters, and the site can experience noticeable current.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Eastern Sabang Bay, Puerto Galera, Mindoro Island, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive (reef and sand)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: ~15 meters
- **Current**: Variable, often present
- **Water Temperature**: 28-29°C

## Marine Life

Turtles are one of the most reliable sightings at Sabang Point, often seen cruising between coral outcrops. Large pufferfish and porcupinefish patrol the reef edges. At greater depth, garden eels extend from the sandy bottom, swaying with the current and retracting as divers approach. Nudibranchs are found across the coral outcrops, and egg cowries -- with their distinctive mantles covering the shell -- can be spotted on soft coral. Octopus hide in crevices among the outcrops.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sand and coral outcrops, working with any current present. Visit the deeper sandy areas to observe garden eels, approaching slowly to avoid spooking them back into their burrows. Explore the coral outcrops for nudibranchs, egg cowries, and octopus. Watch for turtles, pufferfish, and porcupinefish throughout the dive. Maximum depth reaches approximately 27 meters. Work gradually shallower through the coral outcrops for the second half of the dive.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Approach garden eel colonies slowly and stay low to the bottom for better viewing
- Check coral outcrops carefully for egg cowries and octopus
- Current can be variable -- be prepared with an SMB for drift situations
- The mix of sand and coral habitats means both macro and wide-angle subjects are available
- Turtles are often seen mid-dive -- keep scanning ahead
