## Edge of the World

Edge of the World is the deepest and most exposed wall dive on Molokini Crater's outer back wall, where a large ledge at around 15 metres gives way to a sheer vertical drop reaching roughly 76 metres into open ocean.

## Overview

Edge of the World occupies the deepest section of Molokini's back wall — the southernmost extremity of the crater — where a prominent shelf juts out at approximately 15 metres and the wall beneath it plunges to around 76 metres (250 feet), making it the deepest drop-off on the backside. Much of the site sits in near-constant shadow regardless of the time of day, a condition that suppresses the usual reef fish density and instead draws larger pelagic and deep-water species. Sharks and rays are the primary encounters here. The dive is conducted as a live-boat drift: divers enter, descend to the shelf, and work the wall face while the captain follows the surface markers. Buoyancy control, a powerful dive light, and solid air management are essential given the depth, shadow, and lack of a mooring.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Molokini Crater, Maui, Hawaii
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 46 meters

## Marine Life

No site-specific species list has been confirmed by independent sources beyond general accounts of sharks and rays drawn by the upwelling and shadow. The broader Molokini back wall hosts whitetip reef sharks, grey reef sharks, eagle rays, moray eels, slipper lobsters, and occasional manta rays — but sightings at this specific outer point are condition-dependent and reported infrequently in dive logs. No regional species list is attributed to this site specifically.

## Tips

- Requires Advanced Open Water certification and recent drift diving experience; most operators require a skills assessment before conducting back wall dives.
- Morning departures are strongly recommended — afternoon swells on the exposed south face can make re-boarding dangerous.
- Bring a powerful primary dive light: the permanent shadow means colour-rendering is poor without artificial light.
- Surface marker buoys are mandatory; no mooring exists and boat pick-up depends on your SMB.
