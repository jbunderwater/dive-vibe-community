## Reef's End

Reef's End marks the western tip of Molokini Crater's submerged rim, where a series of terraced reef steps descend from 3 metres down to the outer edge, dropping into open water beyond 100 metres.

## Overview

Reef's End is the westernmost point of Molokini's underwater crescent, where the inside sand channel meets the exposed outer face. A typical dive profile begins in the shallow sand channel (around 3 metres), moves out along a series of stair-stepping reef terraces to around 40 metres at the outer edge, then crosses back across the sand channel to finish on the mid-reef hard corals. The sand channel itself hosts a confirmed garden eel colony, and the terraced shelves descend through antler coral and cauliflower coral zones. Strong currents are present at the outer point; conditions on the inside of the rim are calmer and suitable for less experienced divers. The outer edge drops away to approximately 107 metres (350 feet) of open water, making it strictly a recreational-limit dive on the outer shelves.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Molokini Crater, Maui, Hawaii (western tip of crater rim)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters

## Marine Life

Independently confirmed species at Reef's End include: garden eels (in the sand channel), whitetip reef sharks, grey reef sharks, moray eels, giant trevally, white pyramid butterflyfish, wrasses, unicornfish, Moorish idols, trumpetfish, lobsters, and antler coral. Manta rays visit the area and spinner and bottlenose dolphins have been reported. Whale sharks are occasionally sighted near the outer edge. Humpback whales are seasonally audible and sometimes visible from Molokini (December–May) but are not specifically documented at this site.

## Tips

- The outer tip of Reef's End is exposed to open-ocean current; know your limits and stick to the inside rim if conditions are unfavourable.
- The garden eel patch in the sand channel rewards slow, hovering approaches — fin movement at a distance will cause the eels to retract.
- Visibility at Reef's End is among the most consistent in the crater: operators cite 30–60 metres as typical.
