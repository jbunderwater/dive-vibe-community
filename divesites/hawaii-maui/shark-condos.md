## Shark Condos

Shark Condos is the name given to a series of narrow lava shelf formations and cavern-like voids at around 40 metres depth at Molokini Crater, where whitetip reef sharks congregate to rest during the day.

## Overview

Shark Condos sits near the deeper end of Molokini's inner crater, accessed as a deep extension of a crater-side dive. At approximately 40 metres (130 feet), the volcanic substrate creates a series of horizontal shelves and recesses — narrow, cave-like voids that cut back into the lava. Whitetip reef sharks rest here in numbers, using the low-flow conditions of the enclosed spaces to remain stationary. The site requires solid no-decompression limit awareness given the depth and the time typically spent at 35–40 metres during a single-tank recreational dive. Molokini's exceptional visibility — reported at 30–50 metres inside the crater — means divers can often see the silhouettes of the resting sharks against the blue before reaching the ledges.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Molokini Crater, Maui, Hawaii (deep inner crater)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters

## Marine Life

Multiple sources independently confirm whitetip reef sharks as the primary species at Shark Condos, resting in the lava shelf recesses during daylight hours. Boarfish, giant trevally, and lobsters are also reported at this site by multiple sources. Grey reef sharks are documented at Molokini generally but are not specifically attributed to Shark Condos in site-specific accounts reviewed.

## Tips

- This site is offered only to advanced divers by Molokini operators; most require Advanced Open Water certification and will assess your dive log before conducting this dive.
- NDL management at 40 metres is critical — at this depth on air, bottom time is short (roughly 20 minutes on most dive computers before NDL warning). Plan your descent to minimise time before reaching the condos.
- The sharks are habituated to divers but remain wild animals; do not approach or reach into the ledges.
