## Hawaii - Maui

World-famous Molokini Crater diving with dramatic wall drops, lava formations, and seasonal humpback whale encounters in warm Hawaiian waters.

## Description

Maui offers 8 documented dive sites concentrated around the Molokini Crater marine preserve, a half-submerged volcanic caldera located off South Maui. The sites range from intermediate reef dives at shallow depths to advanced wall dives along Molokini's outer edge, where the crater wall drops steeply into deep blue water. Visibility regularly exceeds 30 meters (100 feet), particularly from late summer through fall. From December through April, humpback whales migrate to Maui's waters, and their songs can often be heard underwater during dives.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Wall Diving**: 4 wall dive sites including Edge of the World, Flying Sea Cliffs, Enenue, and The Back Side along Molokini's dramatic outer crater walls, rated Advanced
- **Reef Diving**: 3 reef sites including Middle Reef, Reef's End, and Tako Flats at intermediate level, featuring healthy coral formations inside the crater
- **Cave Diving**: 1 cave site at Shark Condos, known for whitetip reef shark resting areas in lava overhangs
- **Boat Diving**: All 8 sites are boat-access only, departing from Kihei and Ma'alaea Harbor

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Kahului Airport (OGG) is Maui's main airport with direct flights from the US mainland and interisland connections. Ma'alaea Harbor is the primary departure point for Molokini boat dives, approximately 20 minutes from the airport.
- **Dive Operators**: Maui Dreams Dive Co. is South Maui's only 5-Star PADI Instructor Development Center, based in Kihei. Banyan Tree Divers offers personalized shore diving tours near Lahaina and Ka'anapali. Other operators include Dive Maui, Scuba Shack, and Maui Pacific Divers.
- **Accommodation**: Kihei and Wailea on the South Shore offer the closest lodging to Molokini departure points. Ka'anapali and Lahaina on the West Side are popular alternatives with direct access to shore diving at sites like Black Rock.
- **Transportation**: Rental car recommended for accessing different dive departure points across the island. Ma'alaea Harbor is centrally located between South and West Maui.
- **Facilities**: Full-service dive shops in Kihei, Lahaina, and Ka'anapali offer equipment rental, air fills, and certification courses.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature ranges from 24-28°C (75-82°F) year-round, with peak temperatures around 28°C (82°F) in October. Visibility at Molokini frequently exceeds 30 meters (100+ feet). Conditions are calmest from April through October; winter months can bring swell and occasional cancellations.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Green sea turtles are common on nearly every dive. Whitetip reef sharks rest in cave formations at Shark Condos. Manta rays are encountered on deeper dives. Reef fish include racoon butterflyfish, yellowfin goatfish, saddle wrasse, and endemic Hawaiian species. Octopus (tako) are frequently spotted at Tako Flats. Seasonal humpback whales (December-April) can be seen and heard during dives.
- **Conservation**: Molokini Crater is a State Marine Life Conservation District with strict no-take regulations. Anchoring is prohibited; all boats use mooring balls. Over 25% of Hawaiian reef fish species are endemic, found nowhere else on Earth.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: April through October for the calmest ocean conditions and best visibility. October is considered peak season with 28°C water and 30+ meter visibility. December through April for humpback whale season, though seas can be rougher.
- **Currency**: US Dollar (USD)
- **Language**: English
- **Safety**: All Molokini sites are boat-access only with open ocean conditions. Advanced wall dives along the outer crater have significant depth and current exposure. Conditions can change rapidly; operators cancel trips during high surf or strong wind advisories. No hyperbaric chamber on Maui; nearest is on Oahu.
