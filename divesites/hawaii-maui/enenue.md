## Enenue

Enenue is a gently sloping reef on the northeast (inner) side of Molokini Crater, named after the Hawaiian chub — the rudderfish (enenue or nenue) — that schools in the shallows here.

## Overview

Enenue occupies the eastern inner face of Molokini Crater, offering a gradual slope from the surface down to around 18 metres (60 feet) before the terrain steepens into deeper water. It is one of the more approachable sites on the inner crater, with calm conditions and good visibility typical of the protected interior. The shallower sections of the slope are colonised by antler coral formations and attract dense schools of butterflyfish. At the base of the slope, where the reef meets the sand, divers encounter moray eels and, with patience, whitetip reef sharks resting in reef ledges. The site can be dived from an anchored boat or as a drift, depending on current conditions. Unlike the outer back wall sites, Enenue does not require drift experience, though good buoyancy is needed to avoid damaging the antler coral at depth.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Molokini Crater, Maui, Hawaii (northeast inner wall)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Multiple sources confirm the following species at or directly adjacent to Enenue: butterflyfish (schools in the shallows), antler coral, moray eels, whitetip reef sharks (in ledges at the base of the slope), and occasional rays. Manta rays and grey sharks are also reported at Molokini generally but are not specifically documented at this inner-wall site. Sources note that turning the corner toward the back wall adds shark ledge access, but that is treated as a separate dive profile.

## Tips

- The antler coral formations near the bottom of the slope are fragile — maintain neutral buoyancy before descending to the 18-metre zone.
- Schools of butterflyfish congregate in the 5–10 metre range and are the site's most photogenic feature; plan your air for shallower time.
- Less exposed to swell than the outer back wall sites; suitable when back wall conditions are unfavourable.
