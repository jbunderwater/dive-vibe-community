## Socorro Islands (Revillagigedo Archipelago)

One of the world's premier big-animal liveaboard destinations — giant Pacific manta rays, multiple shark species, humpback whales, and interactive bottlenose dolphins in remote Pacific waters.

## Description

The Revillagigedo Archipelago — commonly called the Socorro Islands — is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and Mexico's most remote marine reserve, located approximately 400 km (250 miles) southwest of Cabo San Lucas. The archipelago consists of four volcanic islands: Socorro, San Benedicto, Roca Partida, and Clarion (closed to divers). All diving is liveaboard-only; there is no day-trip or shore access. Vessels depart from Cabo San Lucas (Los Cabos International Airport, SJD) and require approximately 30 hours of transit to reach the dive sites.

The diving character here is defined by large pelagic encounters in open Pacific water, not coral reefs. The volcanic seafloor provides the structural backbone — lava pinnacles, walls, and canyon formations — but the draw is the animals that aggregate around them. Giant Pacific manta rays (Mobula birostris) with wingspans up to 6.7 meters are the signature species, and encounters are among the most intimate in the world; the animals are habituated to divers and actively seek out interaction. Up to seven species of sharks have been recorded in a single dive, including scalloped hammerheads (sometimes in schools of hundreds), silky sharks, Galapagos sharks, tiger sharks, oceanic whitetips, and whale sharks. A resident bottlenose dolphin pod at Cabo Pearce and Socorro Island actively seeks out divers in behavior found at very few other destinations globally. Humpback whales visit to breed from January through March and are regularly heard underwater — in-water encounters have been documented at Punta Tosca.

### Dive Sites

Sites are spread across three islands:

**San Benedicto Island**: The Boiler (manta cleaning station pinnacle, regarded as one of the world's best sites for giant manta interactions), San Benedicto Canyon/El Canyon (horseshoe volcanic canyon with two cleaning stations and high shark diversity), The Aquarium (shallow macro and fish-density site), and Fondeadero (checkout dive on three lava block formations near the anchorage).

**Socorro Island**: Cabo Pearce (wall dive with manta cleaning station and the famous interactive bottlenose dolphin pod), Punta Tosca (lava finger wall, the archipelago's best site for tiger shark encounters and humpback whale in-water encounters), and O'Neal Rock/Roca O'Neal (pinnacle nicknamed "Hammerhead Central" with plateau at 10–12 meters and a photogenic cavern below).

**Roca Partida**: The isolated volcanic pinnacle approximately 64 miles west of San Benedicto — regarded as one of the best big-animal dive sites on the planet. Its vertical walls and sheer remoteness concentrate whitetip reef sharks (stacked in resting recesses), schooling hammerheads, oceanic whitetips, and multiple manta ray species around a structure small enough to circumnavigate in a single dive.

### Diving Conditions

- **Water Temperature**: 22–26°C (72–79°F); thermoclines can push temperatures lower at depth
- **Visibility**: 20–30+ meters at open pinnacle sites (best at Roca Partida); 10–15 meters at sheltered sites (Fondeadero)
- **Currents**: Moderate to strong; Roca Partida and Cabo Pearce can experience ripping current from any direction
- **Entry**: Giant stride from zodiac tenders; surface marker buoy (SMB) mandatory for all open-water ascents
- **Wetsuit**: 5mm minimum; 7mm recommended for extended multi-dive days

### Accessibility

Liveaboard-only. Vessels depart from Cabo San Lucas; trip durations range from 8–12 days. All operators require a minimum Advanced Open Water certification and typically 50 or more logged dives. Some operators require evidence of current diving experience. Nitrox is available aboard all major liveaboards and is strongly recommended given multi-dive daily profiles and depths at El Canyon and Roca Partida.

### Best Season

November to June. The islands are accessible year-round but summer conditions can be challenging. Peak manta concentration occurs January through April. Humpback whales visit January through March. Whale sharks are possible November through April. Some operators suspend trips during summer months.

### Conservation

The Revillagigedo Archipelago became a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2016. It is a fully no-take marine reserve — no fishing, no anchoring on coral, strict no-touch rules for all marine life. Gloves are prohibited throughout the marine park. All diving is supervised by liveaboard guides familiar with the specific conditions at each site.

## Additional Information

- **Currency**: Mexican Peso (MXN)
- **Language**: Spanish
- **Nearest Emergency Care**: Cabo San Lucas (approximately 30 hours transit); nearest hyperbaric chamber is in La Paz or Cabo San Lucas
