## Roca Partida

Roca Partida is an isolated volcanic pinnacle in the Socorro Islands (Revillagigedo Archipelago), approximately 64 miles west of San Benedicto. The name means "split rock" — the narrow pinnacle splits into two peaks at the surface, its guano-covered tops rising just above the waterline in the middle of the open Pacific.

## Overview

Roca Partida is widely regarded as one of the best big-animal dive sites in the world, ranking alongside Wolf and Darwin in the Galapagos. The pinnacle erupts from the seafloor at around 50 meters and offers dramatic vertical walls on all sides with virtually no shelter from ocean surge and current. Its total isolation in open water makes it a magnet for pelagic life — everything from stacked whitetip reef sharks to schooling hammerheads, manta rays, and occasional whale sharks converges here.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Revillagigedo Archipelago, Pacific — approximately 64 miles west of San Benedicto
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 50 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–30+ meters (often the best visibility in the archipelago)
- **Current**: Strong, often ripping — can arrive from any direction
- **Best Time**: November to June; January–April for oceanic whitetips and hammerheads

## Marine Life

Roca Partida's signature spectacle is its whitetip reef sharks. On the eastern face of the pinnacle, a series of rock ledges and recesses serve as daytime resting platforms — divers regularly find these crevices packed with whitetips stacked on top of each other, their sandpaper skin abraded smooth against the volcanic rock. At depth around 20 meters, small caves house lobsters and additional shark species.

Above the resting sharks, the open water around the pinnacle fills with clouds of creolefish and jacks, often pursued by dolphins, tuna, and wahoo. Scalloped hammerheads school here, sometimes in groups that divers can watch sweep past from a sheltered position among the rocks. Galapagos sharks patrol the walls alongside silky sharks and oceanic whitetips. Giant Pacific manta rays circle the pinnacle, and whale sharks pass through seasonally. Humpback whales visit from January through March and are regularly heard — and sometimes seen — during dives.

## Dive Profile

Divers descend quickly along the walls to find shelter from the current in the rock structure, typically settling in recesses at 15–25 meters to observe the stacked whitetips and await the hammerhead schools. The pinnacle is small enough to circumnavigate in a single dive, but conditions demand careful air management. Safety stops are conducted in blue water, often with additional pelagic encounters. No natural anchor points exist — conditions can deteriorate rapidly.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard only; there is no day-trip access. Entry is via giant stride from the zodiac tender. Ascent requires a surface marker buoy (SMB) for zodiac pickup in open water. Roca Partida has no protected anchorage, so conditions determine whether diving is possible.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Descend fast and get behind the rocks immediately to escape the full force of the current
- Position yourself in a sheltered recess at depth and let the animals come to you — chasing sharks here is futile and exhausting
- Bring a reel and SMB — zodiac pickups in open water at Roca Partida require it
- A 5mm wetsuit minimum; water temperature ranges 22–26°C depending on season
- Nitrox is strongly recommended given multi-level profiles at depth
- Early morning dives typically offer the calmest conditions

## Safety Considerations

Roca Partida is the most exposed and challenging site in the Revillagigedo Archipelago. Strong surge presses divers against barnacle-encrusted walls — gloves are prohibited in the marine park, so technique matters. Currents can reverse direction mid-dive. The site is 64 miles from any support infrastructure. Divers must have solid buoyancy control, current diving experience, and 50+ logged dives. This site is not suitable for newly certified Advanced divers.

## Photography

Roca Partida is one of the premier big-animal wide-angle destinations on the planet. The stacked whitetip reef shark shots are iconic. Shoot upward from below the resting sharks with the pinnacle walls as background. Hammerhead silhouettes against the blue require fast shutter speeds. Mantas circling in open water demand wide-angle and fast response. Visibility is consistently the best in the archipelago, often exceeding 30 meters.
