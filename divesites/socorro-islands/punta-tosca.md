# Punta Tosca

The archipelago's best site for tiger shark encounters and in-water humpback whale experiences along a volcanic lava finger wall

## Overview
Punta Tosca is a series of lava fingers extending from Socorro Island's west coast into the Pacific. The site is best known as the Socorro archipelago's premier location for tiger shark encounters, with Nautilus Liveaboards reporting more tiger shark sightings here than at any other site in the islands. Pods of bottlenose dolphins frequent the lava fingers and are described by operators as the most interactive dolphins in the archipelago. From January through March, Punta Tosca is also the best site in the Revillagigedo for in-water humpback whale encounters, as breeding whales frequent these waters — a single humpback was documented hovering near a divemaster for approximately 20 minutes at this site. The volcanic substrate includes caves, arches, and swim-throughs, and Nautilus operators describe Punta Tosca as the most colourful corner of the archipelago compared to the harder volcanic walls at The Boiler and Cabo Pearce.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Socorro Island, Socorro Islands, Pacific
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 metres
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: November to June (humpback whales January to March)

## Marine Life
Tiger sharks are the headline species at Punta Tosca, confirmed by Nautilus Liveaboards as appearing with greater frequency here than at any other site in the archipelago. These large sharks patrol along the wall and in the blue water. Bottlenose dolphins are a major draw — pods frequent the lava fingers and are described by operators as among the most interactive in the islands. Giant oceanic manta rays (Mobula birostris) visit regularly. Whitetip reef sharks rest along the wall. Galapagos sharks, silky sharks, and silvertip sharks cruise the deeper water. From January through March, humpback whales are present to breed — their song is audible underwater, and in-water visual encounters have been documented at this specific site. Moray eels, lobsters, sea turtles, and schools of jacks inhabit the volcanic caves and arches. The site's varied topography — lava fingers, caves, and swim-throughs — supports a greater diversity of reef organisms than the more exposed pinnacle sites.

## Dive Profile
Descend along the wall and work along the lava finger formation, scanning the blue water for tiger sharks and other pelagics. The wall drops steeply, so careful depth management is essential. Most encounters occur between 12-25 metres. During whale season, keep ears open for humpback song — whales may pass at the edge of visibility. The volcanic rock offers overhangs and recesses worth investigating for resting sharks and marine life.

## Entry and Exit
Zodiac tender from the liveaboard. Giant stride entry. Conditions can involve moderate to strong currents and surge. Deploy an SMB during ascent for boat pickup. Be prepared for current changes during the dive.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Tiger sharks often approach from behind or below — maintain 360-degree awareness
- During whale season (January to March), listen for humpback song — encounters can occur unexpectedly
- Do not attempt to approach or chase tiger sharks or whales; remain calm and let them control the interaction
- Gloves are prohibited throughout the marine park
- A 5-7mm wetsuit is recommended; water temperatures can be cool at this site

## Safety Considerations
Tiger shark encounters require composure and situational awareness. Maintain eye contact with approaching sharks and do not turn your back. Strong currents along the wall can develop. The nearest hyperbaric chamber is approximately 30 hours away in Cabo San Lucas or La Paz. Carry appropriate signalling devices and be prepared to abort the dive if conditions deteriorate.

## Photography
Wide-angle is essential for tiger shark and whale encounters. Tiger sharks often make close, deliberate passes that provide dramatic portrait opportunities against the volcanic wall backdrop. During whale season, wide-angle with natural light captures humpback encounters effectively. The volcanic topography provides strong compositional elements.
