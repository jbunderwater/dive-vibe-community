## South Lagoona Reef

South Lagoona Reef is a drift reef dive on the western side of Tiran Island in the Strait of Tiran, Red Sea — a short boat ride from Sharm el-Sheikh. It is the southern section of the Laguna Reef system, which local fishermen know as Marsa Shabir. The area offers the best mooring shelter in the Tiran strait and is a popular overnight anchorage for dive boats.

## Overview

The outer side of South Lagoona is an interesting drift dive between the North Laguna and South Laguna navigation beacons. Tidal currents strongly influence dive direction here — dives are best done in the afternoon and will run either north or south depending on conditions. South of the South Laguna beacon, a wide plateau at 15–25 m carries large table corals (Acropora sp.) and narrows northward into a steep, fauna-rich wall.

## Marine Life

The hard coral wall supports numerous fish species including schools of butterflyfish. Gorgonian fan corals are found on the deeper plateau sections. Divers drifting with the current have opportunities to spot pelagic species cruising the wall — larger fish are common along this exposed reef edge. Glassfish and anthias inhabit the reef's crevices and overhangs.

## Dive Profile

The main lagoon averages 10–12 m in depth. The southern plateau drops to 15–25 m. Tidal currents determine dive direction; suitable for all experience levels with proper drift dive awareness. Best in afternoon sessions. Boat access from Sharm el-Sheikh.
