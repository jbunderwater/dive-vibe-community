## شرم الناقة

شرم الناقة (Sharm el-Naga, meaning "Bay of the She-Camel" in Arabic) is a reef dive site on the Egyptian Red Sea coast between Hurghada and Port Safaga. The site sits within a protected bay that gives it the sheltered conditions and clean water associated with the best Egyptian reef diving.

## Overview

Sharm el-Naga is part of the stretch of coastline south of Hurghada that includes several notable diving areas. The bay here forms a natural shelter that reduces swell and current, creating a calm environment for reef diving. The reef structure drops from a shallow coral flat to approximately 25-30 meters with good hard coral coverage and a resident community of reef fish. The site is accessible from both Hurghada and Port Safaga operators and sees regular day-boat traffic. Some sections of the reef are accessible from shore, though boat access provides the best route to the most productive reef zones.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sharm el-Naga Bay, Red Sea coast between Hurghada and Port Safaga, Egypt
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters (65-100 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round (best September to May)

## Marine Life

Sharm el-Naga is considered one of the better reef fish diversity sites in the Hurghada area. humphead wrasse are regularly encountered. Sea turtles are a consistent sighting, particularly hawksbill turtles in the shallower reef zones. Schools of barracuda and jacks cruise the open water adjacent to the reef. Moray eels are plentiful in the crevices. Triggerfish and parrotfish dominate the upper reef zones. Lionfish are abundant in the overhangs. Colorful nudibranchs and flatworms can be found in the rubble zones for attentive macro divers.

## Dive Profile

Boat drop at the reef mooring; descend to the reef slope in 20-30 meters. Work along the reef face, ascending gradually from the deepest sections. The mid-reef zone from 10-20 meters has the richest community structure. The reef flat at 5 meters provides a relaxed safety stop with good natural light. Allow enough bottom time to cover the full reef traverse from deeper slope to shallows.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Hurghada or Port Safaga operators, typically on full-day or half-day trips. Standard back roll or giant stride entry. Shore access is possible in certain areas of the bay. Boat pickup after the safety stop with SMB deployment.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Sharm el-Naga is a good choice for divers looking for calm conditions and reliable reef quality in the Hurghada area
- Bring a macro lens or diopter — the rubble and reef base areas reward careful searching
- Early morning departures from Hurghada arrive at the site before the main dive traffic
- The calm bay conditions make this a comfortable site for divers who are affected by swell or choppiness
- Ask operators about current conditions — occasional upwellings from the deeper water can reduce temperature

## Safety Considerations

Fire coral is present throughout the upper reef; maintain buoyancy discipline. Lionfish in overhangs require careful attention to hand and fin placement. The usually mild conditions at Sharm el-Naga make it a manageable site, but current can develop toward the outer reef sections. Always carry and deploy an SMB. Dive within certification depth limits.

## Photography

Sharm el-Naga provides a solid reef photography environment with the calm bay conditions allowing relaxed setups. humphead wrasse and turtle portraits are reliable subjects. Wide-angle coral reef scenes with good ambient light are well-suited to this site. Macro photography in the rubble zones produces nudibranchs, shrimp, and small cryptic species. The sheltered conditions make it comfortable to take time framing shots without fighting current.
