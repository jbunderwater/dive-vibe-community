## موقع الغوص - جزيرة الأخ الصغير

موقع الغوص - جزيرة الأخ الصغير (Dive Site — Little Brother Island) refers to the wall diving around Little Brother Island (الأخ الصغير, Al-Akh al-Saghir), the smaller of the two Brothers Islands in the Egyptian Red Sea. While Little Brother lacks the famous wrecks of its larger sibling, its walls are considered by many divers to be even more spectacular for pelagic encounters and pristine coral coverage.

## Overview

Little Brother is a small, elongated reef sitting approximately 2 kilometers south of Big Brother in the open Red Sea. The island is narrower and the walls drop even more steeply, creating an intense vertical environment. Because there are no wrecks to compete for attention, divers focus entirely on the wall and the open water beside it, making pelagic marine life encounters the defining experience here. The north and south tips of the island are where currents meet and create the upwelling conditions that attract large pelagic fish, including the sharks for which the Brothers are renowned. Little Brother is exclusively a liveaboard destination requiring an Egyptian Red Sea permit.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Little Brother Island (Al-Akh al-Saghir), Brothers Islands, Red Sea, Egypt (liveaboard only)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-40 meters (80-130 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to very strong
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Little Brother is particularly celebrated for thresher shark encounters. Thresher sharks — with their distinctive elongated tail — are shy pelagic animals that are rarely seen at most Red Sea sites, but they appear regularly at Little Brother, especially during early morning dives in summer. Oceanic whitetip sharks are present throughout the season. Hammerhead sharks are encountered, particularly at the north and south tips in the stronger current zones. Grey reef sharks are present on every dive. Silvertip sharks have been reported. The walls support massive gorgonian sea fans at 20-35 meters with excellent soft coral coverage throughout. Eagle rays glide along the wall and grey reef sharks often line up in rows in the current off the north tip.

## Dive Profile

The north tip of Little Brother is the primary dive site and involves descending quickly toward 30-35 meters where shark and ray activity is concentrated in the current that sweeps around the island's tip. The "shark corner" experience at the north tip involves positioning in the current and watching sharks pass in the blue water. After the deep section, ascend along the east or west wall face to explore the coral growth at 15-25 meters. Complete the dive on the upper wall at 5-10 meters and do the safety stop on the wall face before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Liveaboard access only. Inflatable tender drop to the entry point. The island is completely exposed to the open Red Sea; conditions can be rough. Strong current at the tips requires careful entry timing and positioning. Deploy SMB before ascending and complete the safety stop at 5 meters. The liveaboard crew will be watching for diver SMBs and will collect divers after ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Pre-dawn and dawn dives at Little Brother's north tip are when thresher shark sightings are most frequent
- Stay composed during shark encounters — sharks here are habituated to divers but can be spooked by sudden movements
- The current at the north tip can be very strong; a reef hook provides stability without damaging the coral
- Be prepared for a fast drift dive — the current around the island tips can move divers quickly
- Nitrox is valuable here to extend no-decompression time in the 25-35 meter shark-watching zone

## Safety Considerations

Little Brother demands experienced open-water diving skills. Current at the island tips can be powerful and unpredictable; entry timing is critical. Never surface in the direct current line — always swim out of the strong flow before ascending. Oceanic whitetip sharks at the Brothers are more bold than at other sites; follow guide protocols regarding distance and behavior. The remote location and open-sea exposure mean conditions can deteriorate rapidly. Maintain strict buddy and group contact. Always carry multiple surface signaling devices.

## Photography

Little Brother Island offers exceptional opportunities for pelagic photography that are difficult to replicate at other Red Sea sites. Thresher sharks — with their distinctive long tail lobes — are among the most dramatic photographic subjects in the Red Sea and are reliably found here in season. Oceanic whitetips pass very close to divers. The pristine walls with large gorgonian fans provide spectacular wide-angle compositions. Sunrise dives with the early light filtering down from above and sharks visible in the blue water create images that define the Brothers Islands experience.
