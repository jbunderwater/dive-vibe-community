## Satil Wreck

Satil-class missile boat wreck at Village Beach, Eilat — a 40-meter vessel sunk as an underwater museum, sitting upright at 22-24 meters depth just 70 meters offshore.

## Overview

The Satil Wreck is a former Israeli Navy Satil-class missile boat sunk in the spring of 1994 as an underwater museum off Village Beach in Eilat. The 40-meter vessel sits in a horizontal position at 22-24 meters depth, approximately 70 meters from shore. The wreck is one of the Cherbourg boats — missile vessels famously smuggled out of a French shipping yard on Christmas Eve 1969 during a French arms embargo.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Village Beach, Eilat, Israel
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters (50-100 feet)
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, best visibility September to November

## Marine Life

The wreck hosts emperor angelfish, Arabian angelfish, pipefish, Red Sea fusiliers, sweepers, groupers, glassfish, lionfish, moray eels, and octopus. The hull is colonized by soft corals and sponges.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from Village Beach with a short surface swim. The keel sits at 22-24 meters and the deck at 18-21 meters. Entry into the bow section is possible through the former deck cannon opening. Explore the exterior and accessible bow area while monitoring air and depth.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a dive torch — the bow interior is dark
- The wreck sits elegantly upright as if still sailing, excellent for photography
- Close to shore and relatively shallow, suitable for multiple visits

## Safety Considerations

Shore entry with a short swim. Mild currents typical. Watch for lionfish around the wreck structure. Do not attempt penetration beyond the bow section without proper wreck diving training.
