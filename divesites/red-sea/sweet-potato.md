## Sweet Potato

Sweet Potato is a coral reef dive site in the southern Red Sea, near Yanbu on the Saudi Arabian coast or in the vicinity of the Sudanese or Egyptian Red Sea depending on the operating context. Its whimsical name is typical of the informal naming convention used by liveaboard guides for lesser-known offshore features that lack established Arabic designations.

## Overview

Sweet Potato is a compact reef structure — likely a coral head or small platform reef — that rises from deeper water to a shallow crest. Sites with names like this in the central-southern Red Sea are commonly encountered on liveaboard itineraries as "bonus" dives: off the beaten path, lightly visited, and often pleasantly surprising in their marine life density. The site offers a classic reef slope dive with a varied coral landscape and the characteristically excellent Red Sea visibility.

## Marine Life

Reef species typical of this latitude include anthias clouds, parrotfish, surgeonfish, and a variety of butterflyfish and angelfish. Hawkfish and dartfish occupy the reef-top rubble, while moray eels and lionfish patrol the crevices. Sea turtles are commonly seen, and Napoleon wrasse make occasional appearances. The sandy patches at depth may yield stingrays and garden eels.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top at 5-8 meters and work down the slope in a gradual descent toward the sandy base at around 18-20 meters. A slow circumnavigation of the reef structure is the recommended approach, covering all aspects of the site before returning to the reef crest for the safety stop. The site is compact enough to manage in a single dive.

## Entry and Exit

Accessed by liveaboard or day-boat, with entry via giant stride or back roll. Deploy SMB before ascending and coordinate with the dive boat for pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Treat this as an exploratory dive and cover the full reef perimeter
- Check sandy patches at the base for garden eels, rays, and flatfish
- This type of site often yields macro surprises — slow down and look carefully in the rubble
- Bring an underwater torch to peer into crevices
- An excellent site for practicing buoyancy in a relaxed environment

## Safety Considerations

Standard Red Sea reef safety applies: watch for fire coral, avoid touching the reef, and always surface with an SMB deployed. Monitor currents — even moderate current can be tiring over a full dive. Buddy up and confirm boat pickup arrangements before descending.

## Photography

A site like Sweet Potato rewards patient macro photography — the lightly visited coral rubble often yields nudibranchs, small crustaceans, and unusual small-polyp coral formations. Wide-angle reef portraits in good visibility capture the site's characteristic character.
