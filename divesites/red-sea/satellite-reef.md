## Satellite Reef

Satellite Reef is a boat dive reef site located in the Sharm el-Sheikh area of the northern Red Sea, Egypt, at coordinates placing it south of Sharm along the Sinai coastline. The reef sits in isolation from the main Sinai reef platform, making it accessible primarily by boat from Sharm el-Sheikh operators and liveaboards. "Satellite" reefs in this region typically refer to smaller reef structures that sit apart from the main barrier reef — isolated outcrops that concentrate fish and predator activity.

## Overview

Satellite Reef is a circular or elongated reef outcrop rising from the sandy seafloor, with its shallowest point at around 5 meters and a base at 15–20 meters. The reef's isolation concentrates fish life — it acts as a gathering point in otherwise open water, attracting reef fish, pelagics, and larger predators that use isolated structures as orientation landmarks. The Sharm el-Sheikh area is renowned for exceptional water clarity, regularly exceeding 25–30 meters, and Satellite Reef benefits from this visibility, making it visually dramatic in good conditions.

## Marine Life

Isolated reef outcrops like Satellite Reef tend to accumulate reef fish in numbers disproportionate to their size. Anthias pack the upper reef, and schools of fusiliers hover in mid-water. humphead wrasse are common residents at Sharm-area reefs, and turtles rest on the coral formations. The sandy base around the reef perimeter shelters rays, sand gobies, and occasional octopus. Reef sharks may be encountered on the deeper sections of the outer slope.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef crest and circle the reef at varying depths to survey the site. Begin at the shallowest section (around 5 meters) and work progressively deeper on the outer slope to 15–20 meters. The reef can typically be circumnavigated in a single dive, allowing you to explore different aspects — the current-facing sides will have denser soft coral growth and more pelagic activity. Complete the circuit and ascend to the reef top for your safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Sharm el-Sheikh operators. Entry via giant stride or back roll. A mooring buoy is often attached to the reef. Descend directly and explore. Ascend on the mooring line or deploy an SMB if no line is available. Surface with your SMB clearly visible for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Circle the entire reef to identify the most productive sections before focusing on specific features
- Early morning dives offer the calmest conditions and most fish activity
- Check for current direction on descent and plan your circuit accordingly
- A dive computer is advisable for managing time at varying depths during a circumnavigation
- The sandy base around the reef perimeter is worth exploring for rays and cryptic species

## Safety Considerations

Currents can develop around isolated reef structures, particularly on the upcurrent face. An SMB should be carried at all times for open-water ascents. Be aware that the reef's isolation means the boat may not always be within immediate visual range. Always surface with an inflated SMB.

## Photography

The isolation of the reef creates excellent photographic opportunities: wide-angle shots of the entire reef structure in clear water, reef fish schools hovering against blue water backgrounds, and close-focus wide-angle shots of coral formations. The Sharm region's exceptional clarity is a major asset. Macro subjects on the coral base reward careful searching.
