# Adantia K.

A wreck in the Fury Shoals complex south of Marsa Alam — a vessel that has been colonised by coral in the pristine southern Egyptian Red Sea environment, creating a hybrid wreck-and-reef dive in some of the clearest water accessible on Egyptian liveaboard itineraries.

## Overview

Adantia K. is a wreck site at approximately 24.21°N, 35.56°E in the Fury Shoals area south of Marsa Alam — positioned within one of Egypt's most celebrated offshore reef zones. The vessel identity is not widely documented in public sources; like several wrecks in this area, it likely refers to a smaller regional cargo or supply vessel that ran aground or sank on the reef in the mid-to-late 20th century. In the years since sinking, the hull has undergone significant coral colonisation in the clear, productive Fury Shoals water. The wreck sits at depths that suit standard Open Water certification levels — no decompression diving required for a thorough exterior exploration.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Fury Shoals, southern Egyptian Red Sea (~24.21°N, 35.56°E)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

The coral-colonised hull supports the same marine communities as the surrounding Fury Shoals reef. Glassfish schools occupy the sheltered sections of the wreck structure. Moray eels are resident in the corroded cavities throughout the hull. Schools of snapper use the wreck as shelter and reference. Groupers patrol the outer hull sections. The surrounding sand holds blue-spotted stingrays. Grey reef sharks are occasionally sighted on the outer section of the wreck site.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deepest section of the wreck at 15–20 metres. Circumnavigate the hull at depth before ascending through the coral-colonised upper structure. The transition between wreck and the surrounding reef provides a natural extension of the dive. Safety stop at 5 metres adjacent to the wreck or over the reef.

## Entry and Exit

Extended day trip from Marsa Alam (70–90 minutes) or liveaboard. Giant stride entry. Often combined with Farewell Reef or other Fury Shoals sites. SMB deployment before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Carry a torch for the hull crevices and any accessible deck sections — moray eels and glassfish are best observed with supplementary light
- The surrounding reef extends the dive naturally after completing the wreck circuit
- Do not enter enclosed hull sections without overhead-environment training

## Safety Considerations

Wreck penetration not recommended without overhead training — the hull is likely corroded. External exploration is fully satisfying. Current assessment before entry. SMB before ascending.

## Photography

The coral-colonised hull in clear Fury Shoals water — 25+ metres visibility — provides outstanding ambient wide-angle conditions for wreck portraits. Glassfish schools adjacent to the hull structure suit torch-assisted wide-angle technique.
