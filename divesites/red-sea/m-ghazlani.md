## M Ghazlani

M Ghazlani is a dive site at approximately 27.82°N, 34.27°E — coordinates that place it in the northern Red Sea near Sharm El Sheikh, in the vicinity of Ras Ghazlani within the Ras Mohammed National Park area. "Ras Ghazlani" is a known dive site at the mouth of Marsa Bareika in Ras Mohammed National Park.

## Overview

Ras Ghazlani (Ras Mohammed National Park) is described as one of the healthiest and most diverse coral sites in the Sinai region. The site was closed to divers for many years by Ras Mohammed National Park, which has preserved its delicate coral assemblages. The reef consists of a steep slope covered by coral heads dropping to 15 m, then continuing as a gradually deepening reef wall. The prevailing current makes this a drift dive best experienced in the afternoon.

## Marine Life

- Pufferfish, eagle rays, groupers, and napoleon wrasse (humphead wrasse)
- Pelagic species passing in the current
- Dense coral growth with diverse hard and soft coral communities
- Gorgonian sea fans in deeper sections

## Dive Profile

Boat dive; no mooring — drift dive with strong prevailing current. Depth: drops to 15 m initially, then deepens gradually. Suitable for experienced divers comfortable with drift conditions. Best dived in the afternoon to take advantage of current direction.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ras Mohammed area, Sharm El Sheikh, northern Red Sea
- **Entry Type**: Boat (drift dive)
- **Site Type**: Reef wall / sloping reef
- **Maximum Depth**: ~25+ metres
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate–Advanced (current management required)
