## Gota Abu Galaua

Gota Abu Galaua is an offshore pinnacle reef in the central Egyptian Red Sea, situated in the open water between Hurghada and the Brothers Islands corridor.

## Overview

"Gota" in Egyptian Arabic refers to a submerged or semi-submerged reef pinnacle, and Abu Galaua lives up to the name — a compact, rounded reef structure rising from the seafloor to within a few meters of the surface. This type of isolated offshore pinnacle creates a localised concentration of marine life, as pelagics use it as a reference point and reef fish colonise the structure densely. The pinnacle is draped in soft corals and large sea fans on its deeper flanks, while hard corals dominate the shallow crown. The surrounding blue water and clear visibility typical of this offshore corridor make the dive feel distinctly oceanic rather than coastal.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Offshore central Egyptian Red Sea, between Hurghada and the Brothers Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Offshore reef pinnacle
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Moderate; can be directional around the pinnacle
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

The isolated pinnacle acts as a magnet for pelagic species. Schools of trevally and jacks orbit the structure, while barracuda hang in the current flow above. Resident humphead wrasse and large groupers occupy the deeper overhangs below 12 meters. Sharks — particularly grey reef and whitetip reef sharks — are regularly seen circling at depth. Soft corals on the deeper flanks shelter glassy sweepers and small cardinalfish. Hawksbill turtles are a near-certain sighting on most dives. The reef top hosts the usual Red Sea reef fish community in abundance: butterflyfish, surgeonfish, and chromis in thick schools.

## Dive Profile

Descend directly to the deepest section of the pinnacle at 18–20 meters and circuit the base to observe the pelagic activity and larger residents. Work up the structure in a slow spiral, noting the change from soft-coral-dominated deeper flanks to hard coral gardens near the crown. Finish the dive on the shallow reef top for the safety stop. In current, descend on the upstream face and let the flow carry you around the structure.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Hurghada or El Gouna operators, typically as part of a two-dive day trip. Entry by giant stride. Deploy an SMB before ascending — the pinnacle is surrounded by open water and the boat may not be immediately visible after surfacing.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The pinnacle is best dived on the current — time your entry to coincide with a gentle to moderate flow
- Pelagic sightings are most common in the blue water above and around the deeper base
- A torch is useful for exploring the deeper overhangs where groupers and morays reside
- Bring a wide-angle lens for schooling fish and the pinnacle's silhouette against the blue
- This site combines well with nearby offshore reefs for a full day of diving

## Safety Considerations

The open-water environment means a missed boat pickup could result in a drifting diver — always surface with an SMB deployed and know the boat's drift plan. Currents around the pinnacle can be stronger than expected on the ebb tide. Monitor your position relative to the structure throughout the dive.

## Photography

Isolated pinnacles are ideal for wide-angle work: the structure rising from the blue provides depth and drama in composition. Schooling trevally, jacks, and barracuda circling the reef make excellent subject material. Soft-coral close-focus wide-angle shots on the deeper flanks are also productive.
