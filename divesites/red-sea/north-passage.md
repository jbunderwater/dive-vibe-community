## North Passage

North Passage is a dive site in the Gubal Strait area of the Red Sea (lat 27.78, lon 34.06), north of Gubal Island in the channel connecting the Gulf of Suez to the northern Red Sea.

## Overview

The coordinates place this site in the reef-and-channel zone north of Gubal Island in the Strait of Gubal. The name "North Passage" suggests a channel or passage cut through or between reef structures — a configuration common in this area, where tidal currents through reef gaps create nutrient-rich, current-swept dive sites with strong populations of filter feeders and small-crack-type topography.

The Strait of Gubal is well documented for its current-swept reefs, scattered wrecks (Dunraven, Thistlegorm, Ulysses), and varied topography. Channel passage sites in this area typically feature alcyonarian soft corals, large table corals, and fire corals — communities adapted to current-rich environments.
