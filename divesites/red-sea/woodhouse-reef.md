## Woodhouse Reef

Woodhouse Reef is the longest of the four reef systems in the Straits of Tiran, stretching approximately 1.2 kilometres between Jackson Reef to the north and Thomas Reef to the south. It is a classic drift wall dive, typically run from south to north, suited to experienced divers comfortable with strong and unpredictable currents.

## Overview

The reef's east face drops vertically from the surface to around 45 metres, decorated with hard and soft corals, black coral colonies at 20–25 metres, and crevices sheltering giant moray eels, groupers, and pufferfish. Halfway along the dive, a canyon cuts parallel to the reef at approximately 25 metres and opens into a coral garden threaded with sandy alleys. The northern third transitions to a sloping plateau at around 28 metres. Scanning the blue water from the wall frequently rewards divers with scalloped hammerhead sharks — most reliably July through September — along with grey reef sharks, whitetip reef sharks, leopard sharks, and eagle rays. Nudibranchs and pipefish appear around pinnacles near the wall base. Fusiliers, surgeonfish, unicornfish, and schooling snapper are abundant throughout.

## Dive Profile

- **Depth range**: 12–45 m (wall face from surface; most recreational dives to 28–30 m)
- **Dive style**: Drift dive, south to north along the east wall
- **Visibility**: Typically 20–30 m
- **Currents**: Often strong; a localised downcurrent near the saddle connecting to Jackson Reef — known locally as the "washing machine" — can be intense and unpredictable

## Marine Life

Giant moray eels, groupers, pufferfish, nudibranchs, pipefish, fusiliers, snapper, surgeonfish, unicornfish. Pelagics in the blue water: scalloped hammerheads, grey reef sharks, whitetip reef sharks, leopard sharks, eagle rays, giant barracuda, and jacks.

## Entry and Exit

Boat only. No fixed moorings — drift dive entry requires current assessment. Boats typically drop divers at the south end for a north-bound drift and collect at the north point.

## Safety

The "washing machine" downcurrent near the reef saddle can pull divers unexpectedly deep. Advanced certification and drift-dive experience are required. Always carry a surface marker buoy. Diving is restricted to suitable sea conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Straits of Tiran, northern Red Sea, Sinai Peninsula
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
