## نوميديا

نوميديا (Numidia) is one of the oldest and most historically significant wreck dives in the Red Sea, located at the north end of Big Brother Island (جزيرة الأخ الأكبر) in the Brothers Islands group. The Numidia was a British cargo steamer that struck the reef in 1901 and has been lying on the steep wall of Big Brother ever since, making it one of the most dramatic wreck experiences in Egyptian waters.

## Overview

The SS Numidia was a 130-meter British cargo ship operated by the White Star Line — the same company as the Titanic — that ran aground on Big Brother Island on July 19, 1901. The ship struck the northern tip of the island and slid down the steep wall, where it now rests in an almost vertical position against the reef face. The bow section starts at approximately 8 meters, with the wreck running steeply down to 80 meters at the stern, making it one of the deepest wrecks in the Red Sea for those who wish to explore its full extent. The Numidia's cargo included a variety of mixed goods, many of which are still partially visible among the encrusted structure. After more than 120 years on the reef, the wreck is extraordinarily well-colonized and visually spectacular.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Big Brother Island (Al-Akh al-Akbar), Brothers Islands, Red Sea, Egypt (liveaboard only)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-40 meters (80-130 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The Numidia has had 120+ years to accumulate marine life and the colonization is extraordinary. Massive gorgonian sea fans up to 2 meters in diameter grow on the hull sections at depth. Soft corals in orange, red, and purple cover large areas of the wreck. The wreck hosts large resident grouper, big-eye trevally schools, and dense glassfish swarms in the interior spaces. Oceanic whitetip sharks are a near-constant presence in the water around Big Brother. Grey reef sharks patrol the wall below and beside the wreck. humphead wrasse are resident. The cargo remnants — old engine parts, boiler sections, anchors — are individually fascinating and identifiable among the coral growth.

## Dive Profile

The recreational portion of the Numidia focuses on the upper hull from 8 to 30 meters. Enter the water at the north tip of the island and descend to the bow section visible at 8 meters. Work along the wreck following the hull downward, stopping to examine the cargo remnants and major structural features. The ship's angle creates an unusual sensation of walking along a tilted deck. Plan to spend the deeper portion of the dive in the 20-30 meter range before ascending along the hull toward the shallower sections. Do not exceed recreational depth limits; the wreck continues to over 80 meters below. Safety stop at 5 meters on the wall face.

## Entry and Exit

Liveaboard access only. The Brothers Islands require an Egyptian Red Sea liveaboard permit. Inflatable tender drop to the entry point. Conditions at Big Brother can be challenging; current and swell are common. Deploy SMB before ascending. Multi-dive days at Big Brother typically combine the Numidia with the Aida wreck and wall dives.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Take time to identify cargo remnants — experienced guides can point out original ship equipment among the coral
- The shallower bow section (8-15 meters) is remarkable for the density of fish life and can be dived on the safety stop depth
- Bring a dive torch to illuminate the interior sections and bring out the soft coral colors
- The wreck's unusual angle is disorienting at first — take a moment to orient to the ship's structure before swimming deeper
- Combine with the Aida wreck on the same liveaboard stop for a complete Brothers Islands wreck experience

## Safety Considerations

The Numidia's extreme depth (to 80+ meters) makes it critical to maintain strict depth discipline. The wreck extends well beyond recreational and even sport diver limits; do not follow it below your planned maximum depth under any circumstances. The open-sea location at Big Brother means conditions can be rough and rescue response is measured in hours. Current is present on most dives; maintain awareness of your position relative to the wall and wreck. Always carry and deploy an SMB. Never dive alone at the Brothers Islands.

## Photography

The Numidia is among the most photogenic wrecks in the Red Sea. The 120 years of coral colonization have created extraordinary visual richness — orange and red soft corals frame views of the hull, and massive gorgonian fans provide scale for wide-angle shots. The tilted angle of the wreck creates unusual compositional opportunities. Oceanic whitetip sharks are frequently in the frame when shooting toward open water. The shallow bow section provides excellent natural light for ambient shots without strobes. Bring multiple memory cards and fresh batteries.
