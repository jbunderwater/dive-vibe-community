## Wreck Jackson — Lara, Jackson Reef

The exposed wreck on Jackson Reef is the skeletal remains of the Lara, a 137.5-metre Cypriot cargo vessel that ran aground on the northern side of Jackson Reef in November 1982. Its rusting hull is visible above the waterline and is one of the most photographed shipwrecks in the Red Sea. Jackson Reef is the northernmost of the four Tiran reef systems in the Straits of Tiran.

## Overview

The Lara began life as the refrigerated cargo ship Nopal Trader, built in Kiel, Germany in 1956, measuring 137.5 metres in length with an 18-metre beam. She changed names several times — Krohn Trader, Aghios Lefteris, Crest Lion — before being sold and renamed Lara in 1980. She ran aground on Jackson Reef's northern side in November 1982. Salvage operations in the mid-1990s removed external hull plating and cut away sections that fell into deep water, leaving a skeleton of the former ship. The main debris field descends from approximately 40 metres, with the mast reaching 48 metres — technical diving territory. Shallow hull sections scattered near the reef surface are accessible to recreational divers at 10–20 metres.

The reef wall itself is equally celebrated: the southern side drops to 40 metres and features crevices sheltering sea turtles, lyretail cod, and groupers. The northern point, near the wreck, is where scalloped hammerhead sharks concentrate in the blue water — most reliably July through September, at depths between 10 and 30 metres. Grey reef sharks, whitetip reef sharks, trevally, and barracuda are also regularly seen.

## Wreck History

- **Vessel**: Lara (ex Nopal Trader, ex Krohn Trader, ex Aghios Lefteris, ex Crest Lion)
- **Built**: 1956, Kiel, Germany; 7,103 GRT refrigerated cargo vessel
- **Dimensions**: 137.5 m length, 18 m beam
- **Grounded**: November 1982, Jackson Reef northern side (28° 00′ 43″ N / 34° 28′ 26″ E)
- **Salvaged**: Mid-1990s — hull plating removed; sections cut and fell to depth
- **Current state**: Skeletal above-water hull; debris field from ~40 m into technical depth range

## Dive Profile

- **Recreational zone**: 10–20 m (scattered hull sections near reef top)
- **Technical zone**: 40–48+ m (mast, main debris field — requires tec certification)
- **Reef wall**: Down to 40 m on southern side
- **Visibility**: 20–30 m typically
- **Currents**: Strong, particularly on northern side — drift-dive experience required

## Marine Life

Scalloped hammerhead sharks (especially July–September), grey reef sharks, whitetip reef sharks, sea turtles, lyretail cod, groupers, barracuda, trevally, snapper, triggerfish, blue-spotted stingrays.

## Entry and Exit

Boat only. Strong currents at the northern point make this an advanced site. Boats typically moor on the calmer southern side for reef diving.

## Safety

Strong and unpredictable currents prevail at the northern reef tip. The wreck debris at depth is strictly for technical divers with appropriate training and equipment. Always carry a surface marker buoy.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Jackson Reef, northern Straits of Tiran, Sinai Peninsula
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 48 m (wreck mast); recreational to 30 m on reef wall
