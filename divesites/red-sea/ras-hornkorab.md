## Ras Hornkorab

Ras Hornkorab (also spelled Ras Hankorab) is a headland cape and reef dive site located approximately 70 kilometres south of the town of Marsa Alam, marking the southern boundary of the main Marsa Alam diving region. It is one of the southernmost regularly dived sites accessible from Marsa Shagra and Marsa Alam dive resorts.

## Overview

The cape projects into the Red Sea and is characterised by the fringing reef system typical of the southern Egyptian coast — close to the mainland, with excellent coral cover and clear water. The southern Red Sea in this area is noted for consistently good visibility (20–30 metres on average) and warm tropical water year-round (22–30°C). Wall and reef diving are both available around the cape, with depths reaching 40 metres on the steeper sections.

## Marine Life

The broader Marsa Alam region — in which Ras Hornkorab sits as a southern anchor point — is renowned for its exceptional marine biodiversity. Bottlenose and spinner dolphins visit the area. Sea turtles (hawksbill and green) are commonly encountered on reef dives throughout the region. Oceanic whitetip sharks, silvertip sharks, and grey reef sharks patrol offshore; tuna and barracuda are regularly present. Dugongs have been recorded in sheltered bays along this stretch of coast. Manta rays occur in summer months (May–August), and hammerhead sharks are periodically sighted (May–August). The southern Red Sea fringing reefs support dense hard and soft coral communities with groupers, moray eels, Napoleon wrasse, and abundant anthias.

## Dive Profile

Boat access typical of the Marsa Alam region; day boats and liveaboards from resorts north of the cape visit the site. Diving ranges from shallow reef tops to wall sections reaching approximately 40 metres. The site is accessible to intermediate-level divers, with deeper wall sections better suited to experienced divers.
