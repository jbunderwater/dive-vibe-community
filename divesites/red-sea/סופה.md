## סופה

סופה (Sufa, Hebrew for "Storm" or "Tempest") is a wreck dive site in the Gulf of Aqaba near Eilat, Israel. The Sufa was an Israeli Navy missile boat that was intentionally sunk as an artificial reef in 1996, and it has since become one of the most popular dives in the Eilat area.

## Overview

The INS Sufa was a Saar 4-class Israeli Navy missile boat decommissioned and purposely sunk to create an artificial reef off the coast of Eilat. The vessel sits upright on the sandy seabed at approximately 22-30 meters, in excellent condition relative to its age as an intentional sinkage. Because the wreck was prepared for diving — hatches opened, hazards neutralized — it is accessible and penetrable in several sections. The Sufa is a well-known local dive attraction and has accumulated substantial marine growth since sinking, with soft corals and encrusting life covering much of the hull.

## Marine Life

The Sufa wreck has developed into a thriving artificial reef over the three decades since sinking. The superstructure and deck are densely colonized with soft corals and sponges. Schools of glassfish and sweepers pack the interior compartments. Lionfish are resident throughout. Grouper of substantial size hold territory around the hull. Moray eels are abundant in the interior. The sandy seabed surrounding the wreck supports garden eels, flatfish, and stingrays. Triggerfish patrol the upper superstructure. The clear Gulf of Aqaba water means good visibility to the wreck from mid-water, and dolphins occasionally pass through the area.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wreck and start with the deck and superstructure at 15-22 meters before descending to the hull bottom at 30 meters. The bridge, deck fittings, and gun emplacements are interesting topside details. Several interior sections are accessible for penetration — the main deck openings lead into interior compartments where fish density is very high. Plan the deepest part of the dive early and ascend progressively. Safety stop at 5 meters can be done on the upper superstructure, which is within the stop depth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Eilat operators. The Gulf of Aqaba conditions are typically calm and mild. Standard entry by back roll or giant stride. Deploy an SMB at the safety stop. The site is regularly dived and boat crews are experienced with pickup procedures here.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Sufa is ideal for a night dive — the artificial lighting from Eilat illuminates the wreck from above and the fish activity at night is exceptional
- Bring a torch for the interior compartments where glassfish schools make it visually spectacular
- Ask the local operator for a briefing on the wreck layout — knowing the ship's history makes the dive more meaningful
- The upper superstructure is accessible to less experienced divers; save the bottom sections for those comfortable at 30 meters
- The year-round warm clear water makes Eilat and the Sufa an excellent diving destination at any time of year

## Safety Considerations

The deeper sections of the hull approach 30 meters; manage depth and no-decompression limits carefully. Penetration into interior compartments requires a torch and a clear exit plan. Do not enter spaces where buoyancy control cannot be maintained — silting in enclosed areas is a real hazard. Lionfish in the interior are a hazard for unwary divers; watch hand placement near overhangs. Always carry and deploy an SMB.

## Photography

The Sufa is one of the better-documented wreck photography subjects in the northern Red Sea. The intact superstructure and gun emplacements are compelling wide-angle subjects. Interior shots with a torch illuminating glassfish swarms produce striking images. The soft coral growth on the hull provides color for close-focus wide-angle compositions. Night diving adds cuttlefish and lobster to the subjects. The clear water allows standoff wide-angle shots showing the full vessel silhouette.
