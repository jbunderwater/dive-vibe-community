## Beacon Rock (2)

Beacon Rock (2) is a  dive site in Red Sea, Middle East.

## Overview

Beacon Rock (2) is a second OSM node recorded for the Beacon Rock dive site in the Safaga area of the northern Red Sea (coordinates 27.71°N, 34.12°E), approximately coincident with the primary "Beacon Rock" entry in this dataset. Beacon Rock is a well-known dive site in the Safaga region, often combined with the adjacent SS Dunraven wreck. The rock itself is a prominent structure rising from 24–25 m depth, with a beautiful wall face colonised by soft corals. The reef surface hosts numerous shelters — overhangs and crevices — that serve as habitat for cryptic species. Currents at this site can be tricky, requiring appropriate experience. A maximum depth of approximately 25 m recorded in dive reports is consistent with recreational diving.

## Marine Life

Blue-spotted stingrays are regularly seen digging in the sandy areas around the base of the rock. Clownfish are found in anemones on the reef surface. Free-swimming moray eels move between sections of the reef. Redtooth triggerfish, parrotfish, and surgeonfish are common. The soft-coral wall sections attract anthias clouds and small predatory fish.

## Dive Profile

Boat dive from Safaga. The site involves a descent to the reef wall/rock face at 10–25 m, with divers typically spending time at 10 m watching marine life and then exploring the wall structure. Currents can be present and variable. Often paired with the nearby Dunraven wreck (which rests at 25 m and is heavily coral-covered) for a two-site dive day. Maximum depth ~25 m.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Safaga. Shore access is also mentioned as possible via a hole in the reef from certain positions, though boat diving is the standard approach from Safaga dive centres.

## Tips and Recommendations

The combination of Beacon Rock and the Dunraven wreck makes an excellent full-day dive in the Safaga area. Spend time at 10 m on the reef wall waiting for marine life to become active. Check current direction before beginning the dive.

## Safety Considerations

Currents at Beacon Rock can be tricky and unpredictable — an experienced dive guide is recommended for first visits. Always deploy an SMB on ascent. Fire coral may be present on upper reef sections.
