## سنسبلاش هاوس ريف

سنسبلاش هاوس ريف (Sunsplash House Reef) is the dedicated house reef of the Sunsplash dive resort in the Na'ama Bay / Sharm el-Sheikh area of the northern Sinai, Egypt. As a house reef, it provides convenient and repeat-access diving directly from the resort's shoreline or jetty, allowing multiple dives per day without the need for a boat.

## Overview

House reefs at Sharm el-Sheikh resorts vary considerably in quality, but the Sunsplash reef benefits from the exceptional baseline reef health characteristic of the Na'ama Bay area. The reef drops from a shallow coral platform to approximately 25-30 meters with consistent hard coral coverage and good fish diversity. The accessible location means it is frequently dived by resort guests as first dives of the trip, checkout dives, and night dives. The combination of easy access and solid reef quality makes it a valuable site for both training dives and recreational exploration.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Na'ama Bay / Sharm el-Sheikh, Sinai Peninsula, Red Sea, Egypt
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (or shore/jetty access)
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters (65-100 feet)
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: September to May

## Marine Life

The house reef supports the typical Na'ama Bay reef community. Anthias are abundant on the upper reef formations. Moray eels are resident in the crevices. Triggerfish and parrotfish patrol the reef top. Sea turtles occasionally rest on the coral. Lionfish lurk in overhangs. Clownfish inhabit anemones in the shallower areas. Night dives on the house reef frequently produce cuttlefish, octopus, and sleeping reef fish, as well as various crustaceans that emerge after dark.

## Dive Profile

From the jetty or boat mooring, descend to the reef slope and explore at 15-30 meters before ascending progressively. The reef top at 3-8 meters is rich in shallow-water species and provides an excellent environment for the safety stop. Night dives involve a descent to the sandy rubble zones where nocturnal activity is highest before working back up to the reef top for the ascent.

## Entry and Exit

Jetty or shore access from the resort. Boat access is also available for the deeper sections. This house reef location is convenient for multiple dives in a day with short surface intervals. Night dives are easily arranged by the resort. Follow local dive briefings for any site-specific access protocols.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Take advantage of the easy access for night dives, which are consistently rewarding on Na'ama Bay house reefs
- Multiple dives per day are straightforward with the house reef — plan an early morning dive before the day-boat dives arrive
- Bring a torch even for day dives to examine the overhangs and crevices where lionfish and moray eels are found
- The shallow reef top is ideal for new divers and checkout dives
- Macro photography subjects are well-represented on house reefs due to regular survey by attentive guests

## Safety Considerations

House reef dives at a resort setting are among the safer Red Sea dive configurations due to the proximity of support and familiarity of the site. However, lionfish are present throughout and require attentive buoyancy control near overhangs. Fire coral is present in the shallows. Night dives require a dive torch for each diver plus a backup, and a surface glow stick or SMB for surface identification. Always inform the resort dive desk of your dive plan even on independent house reef dives.

## Photography

House reefs offer the luxury of repeated visits to refine photography setups and composition. The Na'ama Bay baseline reef quality provides good wide-angle coral subjects. Macro subjects — nudibranchs, cleaner shrimp, gobies with shrimp — reward patient searching. Night dives here consistently produce compelling cuttlefish and octopus images. The extended access for multiple dives makes it possible to experiment across different times of day and lighting conditions.
