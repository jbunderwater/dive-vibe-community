## ELPHINSTON

Note: This entry is a duplicate of Elphinstone Reef, the same offshore reef south of Marsa Alam. The two OSM nodes sit within metres of each other; all sourced content here applies to the same reef system.

## Overview

Elphinstone Reef is one of the world's most celebrated dive sites — a cigar-shaped offshore platform approximately 400 metres long running north to south, rising steeply from open water about 25 km north of Marsa Alam. The reef is named after the 18th-century British admiral George Keith Elphinstone. Its eastern and western walls plunge vertically to beyond 100 metres, and the northern and southern tips each feature broad plateaus between 20 and 40 metres carpeted in purple and brown dendronephthya soft corals, giant gorgonian sea fans, and sea whips. The site is among the best locations in the Red Sea to encounter oceanic whitetip sharks (Carcharhinus longimanus), which patrol the southern plateau particularly between October and December. Hammerhead sharks appear at the northern plateau and along the deeper walls. Strong currents running north–south along the walls attract large pelagic aggregations and make this a primarily drift-dive site requiring advanced experience.

## Marine Life

The reef holds exceptional diversity. On the plateaus: large schools of redtooth triggerfish, trevally (Caranx species), barracuda, and reef sharks. Along the walls: Napoleon wrasse, giant grouper, anthias clouds, masked and emperor angelfish, longnose hawkfish perched in sea fans, and moray eels. The southern tip is the best-documented location in Egypt for oceanic whitetip sharks attended by pilot fish; hammerheads are also reported. Tunas and jacks hunt in the blue water above the drop-offs. The reef's lush soft corals (dendronephthya, alcyonarian), hard corals, and sponges support unusually high density of small reef fish.

## Dive Profile

The reef offers four distinct zones: the northern plateau (20–40 m, soft-coral terrace with shark encounters), the eastern wall (near-vertical, 5 m to >100 m), the western wall (steep with ledges and caverns hosting soldierfish and snappers), and the southern plateau (20–40 m, prime oceanic whitetip territory). Currents can be strong — north–south along the walls — and the open-ocean setting means surface conditions vary. Dives are typically done as drift dives off a RIB or liveaboard. Visibility ranges from 20–35 metres. A 10-metre tunnel known as the Elphinstone Arch sits at 30–35 m on the eastern wall; it is narrow and not suited to recreational diving.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard or day-boat from Port Ghalib (approximately 30–40 minutes by RIB) or from Marsa Alam. Shore entry is not possible — the reef is approximately 12 km offshore. Advanced Open Water certification minimum; dive guide required.

## Tips and Recommendations

Dive the southern plateau early in the morning for the best oceanic whitetip activity. Stay on the plateau rather than dropping deep — the sharks and most pelagics are in the 20–40 m zone. October to December is peak season for oceanic whitetips. The northern plateau is the more reliable hammerhead location. Budget two days on-site to experience all four zones.

## Safety Considerations

Currents can be strong and unpredictable; always deploy a surface marker buoy on ascent. This is an open-ocean site with no shallow refuge — ensure adequate gas management. Oceanic whitetip sharks are curious and should be observed calmly without erratic movement. Sun exposure during surface intervals is intense; use high-SPF protection. Fire coral is present throughout the reef.

## Photography

The southern plateau offers outstanding wide-angle opportunities for oceanic whitetip sharks against blue water. The dendronephthya-covered walls reward macro and wide-angle equally. Best light on the eastern wall is mid-morning.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Offshore, ~25 km north of Marsa Alam, southern Red Sea
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (liveaboard or day boat from Port Ghalib)
- **Site Type**: Wall / plateau / drift
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 m (recreational); walls exceed 100 m
