# El Sheig Malik

A coastal reef near Quseir — "the king's elder" — with a fringing reef wall, sandy lagoon, and the calm water conditions typical of this stretch of protected Egyptian Red Sea coastline.

## Overview

El Sheig Malik sits at approximately 25.72°N, 34.55°E near Quseir (El Quseir) on the central Egyptian Red Sea coast. The name translates roughly as "the elder king" — a designation that may reflect the reef's prominence in the local coastal geography. This section of the Egyptian coast between Quseir and Safaga is characterised by fringing reefs close to the shoreline, accessible as day-trip sites from Quseir operators. The reef structure at El Sheig Malik combines a shallow inner section suitable for snorkelling with a more productive outer wall and sandy lagoon transition at diving depths.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Quseir, central Egyptian Red Sea coast (~25.72°N, 34.55°E)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters
- **Current**: Generally mild; protected coastline
- **Best Time**: Year-round; October to May optimal

## Marine Life

The fringing reef carries a diverse resident assemblage: moray eels in the wall crevices, groupers patrolling the outer slope, and anthias schools over the reef crest. Blue-spotted stingrays rest in the sandy lagoon. Cleaning stations established at 8–12 metres on the outer face attract regular fish traffic. Hawksbill turtles are documented on this stretch of coast. Barracuda patrol in open water adjacent to the outer wall. The shallow inner reef supports clownfish in anemones.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sandy lagoon at 15–18 metres and search for stingrays and macro subjects before ascending to the outer reef wall at 8–15 metres. Work along the outer wall, visiting cleaning stations and observing the resident fish communities. Transition to the shallow inner reef at 4–8 metres for the safety stop section.

## Entry and Exit

Day boat from Quseir (15–30 minutes). Giant stride entry. Often combined with Sham Fugami or other Quseir coastal sites for a two-dive day. SMB deployment before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The sandy lagoon section rewards patient searching for stingrays and flatfish
- Cleaning stations on the outer wall are productive in early morning hours when fish activity is highest
- A good accessible site for the Quseir area that complements the more exposed offshore options

## Safety Considerations

Generally mild conditions. Fire coral on the reef crest. Monitor current on the outer wall. SMB before ascending.

## Photography

Sandy lagoon macro work and outer wall cleaning station portraits are the primary subjects. Morning light on the shallow inner reef suits ambient natural-light photography.
