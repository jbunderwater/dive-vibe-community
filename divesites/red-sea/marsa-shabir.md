## Marsa Shabir

Marsa Shabir is the southern mooring lagoon within the Lagoona Reef system on the western side of Tiran Island, in the Straits of Tiran between the Sinai Peninsula and Saudi Arabia. Local fishermen know this sheltered bay as "Marsa Shabir" (meaning "small bay" in the local dialect), while dive guides commonly refer to it as South Laguna. It provides the main sheltered anchorage for liveaboards and day boats visiting the Tiran reef systems.

## Overview

A transverse hard coral wall divides the lagoon into North and South sections (Lagoona North and South), each marked by a navigation beacon. South Laguna — Marsa Shabir — is the larger of the two and provides the best mooring point in the region. The lagoon interior averages 10–12 metres in depth and is bordered by a madreporic (stony coral) formation. The outer reef wall drops further and harbours the wreck of the Zingara (also known as Kormoran), a German freighter that sank here in 1984.

## Marine Life

The lagoon's hard coral formations support anemones, gorgonians, soft corals, and table corals (Acropora species). Large colonies of table corals and fire corals populate the plateau areas. The diverse reef structure provides habitat for butterflyfish, wrasse, damsels, and other typical Red Sea reef fish. Currents sweeping through the Straits of Tiran from the Gulf of Aqaba carry nutrients that support strong coral growth across the system.

## Dive Profile

The lagoon interior at 10–12 metres offers relaxed, sheltered diving suitable for all levels. The outer reef wall with the Zingara wreck is an additional option for more experienced divers. Dive direction is strongly influenced by tidal currents; afternoon dives are generally recommended for better conditions. The site is well-suited for pre-dive check dives or safety-focused familiarisation dives.

## Entry / Exit

Boat only; typically accessed as part of a Tiran Island liveaboard or day trip from Sharm El Sheikh (approximately 45–60 minutes by boat).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Western side of Tiran Island, Straits of Tiran
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 metres (lagoon interior 10–12 m)
- **Difficulty**: Intermediate
- **Note**: Tiran Island is administered by Saudi Arabia but traditionally dived from Sharm El Sheikh, Egypt
