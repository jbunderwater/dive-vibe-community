## الجزر

الجزر (Al-Juzur, meaning "The Islands" in Arabic) is a shallow reef dive site in the northern Red Sea along the Sinai Peninsula coast near Sharm el-Sheikh, Egypt. The name reflects the site's characteristic geography of small reef formations rising near the surface in relatively protected water.

## Overview

Al-Juzur sits within the Sharm el-Sheikh dive area and is characterized by a series of shallow reef patches or coral heads that form a broken, irregular reef landscape at modest depths. The maximum depth of 16 meters makes this an accessible site for divers across a range of experience levels and is particularly suited to those building confidence on reef dives or wanting extended bottom time in the shallows. The site is sheltered enough to offer generally calm conditions and is a regular part of the day-boat rotation from Na'ama Bay operators.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sharm el-Sheikh area, Sinai Peninsula, Red Sea, Egypt
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-25 meters (65-80 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: September to May

## Marine Life

The shallow reef patches at Al-Juzur support a diverse community of reef fish and invertebrates. Clownfish in anemones are a reliable sight in the shallower sections, and the reef harbors dense populations of anthias, wrasse, and chromis. Sea turtles are occasionally observed resting on the coral. Moray eels occupy crevices throughout the reef. Triggerfish and pufferfish patrol the upper sections. Octopus are found in rubble zones, particularly on night dives. The low-depth profile means natural light penetration is excellent throughout the dive, bringing out the colors of the encrusting corals and sea fans.

## Dive Profile

The shallow, broken reef profile allows a relaxed, exploratory approach. Begin the dive at 12-16 meters in the deeper channels between reef formations, then work your way progressively shallower as the dive proceeds. The reef tops at 3-8 meters provide a rich environment for the safety stop, with good light and dense fish life. Extended bottom time is possible at this depth, making it an excellent site for photography or marine life observation.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Sharm el-Sheikh or Na'ama Bay operators. Standard back roll or giant stride entry. The shallow depth means the safety stop can be done on the reef top with good visibility to the surface and the boat. Deploy an SMB at the start of the ascent from 5 meters.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This is an excellent site for underwater photographers due to the consistent natural light at all depths
- Extended bottom times are possible — plan accordingly and bring sufficient gas
- Look carefully in the rubble zones between coral heads for octopus and nudibranchs
- The site is suitable for divers who prefer a relaxed, shallow dive over deep reef profiles
- Night dives here often produce crustacean and octopus sightings

## Safety Considerations

Fire coral is present on the upper reef — maintain buoyancy and avoid contact with the formations. Lionfish use the overhangs between coral formations; watch hand placement. Triggerfish nesting seasons can make fish in certain zones aggressive toward divers passing overhead. The shallow depth reduces risk, but always carry and deploy an SMB for boat pickup coordination.

## Photography

The excellent natural light at this shallow site makes it ideal for ambient-light photography without strobes. Wide-angle shots of the coral formations with divers for scale work well in the clear water. Clownfish-in-anemone portraits are classic subjects. Macro photography of nudibranchs, shrimp, and small gobies is productive in the rubble zones. The reef top provides excellent blue-water backgrounds for fish portraits.
