## Napoleon Reef (2)

Napoleon Reef is a coral reef dive site in the Sharm El Sheikh / Tiran Island region of the northern Red Sea. The name likely refers to the Napoleon wrasse (*Cheilinus undulatus*), a large and charismatic fish species found regularly throughout the Tiran reef system. The coordinates place this site in the vicinity of Tiran Island, where the Straits of Tiran reef systems — Jackson, Gordon, Thomas, and Woodhouse reefs — form one of Egypt's most celebrated diving areas.

## Overview

The Tiran reef systems are swept by currents flowing from the Gulf of Aqaba into the Red Sea, bringing nutrient-rich water that sustains dense coral growth and abundant fish life. Reef plateaus give way to steep walls and dramatic drop-offs. Visibility is typically 20–30 metres. Conditions range from mild to strong current depending on tidal state.

## Marine Life

Napoleon wrasse — some individuals weighing over 100 kg — are a signature species of the Tiran reefs and are regularly encountered throughout the system. Grey reef sharks, whitetip reef sharks, and occasional hammerhead sharks patrol the outer walls. Large schools of barracuda, jackfish (trevally), and fusiliers sweep through the water column. Moray eels, angelfish, and butterflyfish populate the coral structures. The rich current-fed environment supports exceptional density of anthias along the walls.

## Dive Profile

Typical of the Tiran reef system: a shallow plateau at 5–15 metres with diverse hard and soft coral cover, dropping to steep walls reaching beyond 30 metres. Drift diving along the walls is common when current is running. Boat entry, with the dive direction determined by current.

## Entry / Exit

Boat access from Sharm El Sheikh (approximately 45–60 minutes). Tiran Island day trips and liveaboards visit the site.
