## El Fanadir South

El Fanadir South is a  dive site in Red Sea, Middle East.

## Overview

El Fanadir South is the southern entry point of the Fanadir reef system, a long narrow reef lying just north of Hurghada. The Fanadir reef complex can be dived at several points — south, east, and in calm weather at the north — with each section offering a different experience. The south section presents a hard coral wall dropping at approximately 60° from the surface to a sandy ledge at 12 m; this ledge (approximately 50 m wide) narrows progressively heading north. Beyond the ledge the reef drops away to 20–25 m, then to greater depths. The site is noted by local dive operators for its high density of cryptic species and is a perennial favourite for underwater photography, particularly macro. It is accessible to all certification levels and is suitable for drift dives, deep dives, and beginner dives.

## Marine Life

El Fanadir South is especially prized for stonefish, scorpionfish, and lionfish, which are among the most concentrated of any Hurghada reef. Moray eels are abundant in crevices; octopus are commonly spotted hunting along the reef. Blue-spotted stingrays dig in sandy areas for food. Cleaner stations on the reef wall attract surgeonfish and parrotfish. Nudibranchs and trunkfish (boxfish) are recorded regularly, making this an excellent macro site. Pelagic fish — barracuda, snapper schools — patrol the drop-off edge.

## Dive Profile

Divers enter by boat and typically begin at 12–15 m on the sandy ledge, working shallower toward 3–4 m on ascent — "the shallower the better" according to local operators, as the most interesting life is concentrated on the upper reef. A drop to 20–25 m visits the deeper wall structure. Drift, deep, and beginner configurations are all possible. Night dives are popular and rewarding. Visibility 15–25 m.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Hurghada. El Fanadir is 45–60 minutes north of Hurghada by day boat. Most Hurghada operators visit this site. Also accessible by boat from El Gouna.

## Tips and Recommendations

Stay shallow (5–15 m) for the best cryptic life on the reef wall. Bring a torch even on day dives to illuminate crevices for lobsters, moray eels, and cleaning stations. Night diving here is highly recommended — lionfish and scorpionfish become active hunters. Weekdays are less crowded than weekends.

## Safety Considerations

Stonefish are present and well camouflaged on the reef — watch where you put your hands and knees. Never touch the reef. Fire coral (Millepora) is abundant. The open sandy drop-off beyond 25 m offers little of biological interest and increases decompression risk without reward.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Just north of Hurghada, northern Red Sea (45–60 min by boat)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 m (recreational)
