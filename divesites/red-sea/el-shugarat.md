## El Shugarat

El Shugarat (meaning "the trees" or "little trees" in Arabic) is a reef and canyon dive site located in the Dahab / northern Sinai area of the Gulf of Aqaba, Egypt. It is documented on PADI's dive site directory and is served by local dive operators including Circle Divers Dahab. The site is noted for its distinctive canyon structure and extensive gorgonian fan coral forest along the reef wall.

## Overview

The dive begins with a boat drop-off at the reef edge. Divers descend and swim along the reef wall until reaching a sandy bottom, where an impressive open canyon begins at around 16 metres depth. The canyon descends to approximately 30 metres and is flanked by steep walls. Exiting the canyon to the left reveals a striking forest of gorgonian fan corals scattered down the reef wall. The ascent leads back to the reef table and into a sheltered lagoon described as one of the most beautiful in Dahab, featuring giant table corals.

## Marine Life

The gorgonian fan coral forest is the site's defining feature, providing habitat for hawkfish, groupers, and moray eels. Blue-spotted stingrays (*Taeniura lymma*) rest on sandy patches within and around the canyon. Crocodilefish and nudibranchs occupy the reef flat. The lagoon section hosts giant table corals (*Acropora* species) under which diverse reef fish shelter. Long-nose hawkfish perch in gorgonians. Reef sharks and dolphins are among the larger species sometimes reported in the area.

## Dive Profile

Boat entry; multi-zone dive progressing from reef wall, into canyon, through the gorgonian forest, and finishing in the lagoon. Depth range: 5 metres (lagoon) to 30 metres (canyon floor). Safety stops are conveniently made over the shallow lagoon table corals. The site is moderately challenging due to the canyon navigation.

## Entry / Exit

Boat only; captain drops divers adjacent to the reef. Return to the boat or beach as directed by the operator.

## Photography

The gorgonian fan forest and giant table corals of the lagoon are strong wide-angle subjects. The canyon walls also offer opportunities for ambient-light canyon photography. Nudibranchs and crocodilefish provide macro subjects.
