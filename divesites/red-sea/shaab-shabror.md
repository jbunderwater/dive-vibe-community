## Shaab shabror

Shaab Shabror (also spelled Shaab Shabrur) is an offshore reef dive site near Marsa Alam, Egypt, approximately 10 minutes by boat from Marsa Alam marina.

## Overview

Shaab Shabror is a coral reef characterised by excellent visibility (typically exceeding 30 m) and low current (under 1 knot), making it accessible for divers of varied experience levels. The site is the perfect place for a drift dive around the reef, surrounded by magnificent coral gardens. A small wreck on the south side adds an additional point of interest to the dive.

Average depth is approximately 20 m, with the maximum reaching 35 m. The reef supports healthy hard and soft coral coverage, and the relatively low number of divers visiting this site compared to the major Marsa Alam sites means the reef is in good condition.

## Dive Profile

- **Average depth**: ~20 m
- **Maximum depth**: 35 m
- **Current**: Low (under 1 knot); also suitable as a drift dive
- **Visibility**: Excellent (over 30 m)
- **Entry**: Boat (under 10 minutes from Marsa Alam)
- **Suitable for**: All divers

## Marine Life

Groupers, snappers, shoals of bannerfish and jacks. Spinner dolphins occasionally visit the reef. A small wreck on the southern face provides additional habitat for wreck-associated species. Soft and hard corals line the reef wall, with scorpionfish and crocodilefish on the sandy sections.
