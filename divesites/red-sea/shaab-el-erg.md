## Shaab El Erg

Shaab El Erg is one of the best-known dolphin dive sites in the Hurghada area of the northern Egyptian Red Sea. Located approximately 27.4°N in the offshore reef belt east of Hurghada, the site is famous for the resident pod of spinner dolphins that uses the reef lagoon as a daytime resting area. Spinner dolphin interactions here are among the most reliable in the northern Red Sea, and the site is a staple of Hurghada day-boat itineraries.

## Overview

Shaab El Erg consists of a horseshoe-shaped reef with a protected inner lagoon. The lagoon is the resting ground for the spinner dolphin pod, and the outer reef walls provide conventional reef diving along sloping walls to around 20 meters. The outer reef receives more current than the protected inner lagoon and supports denser coral growth and more active fish life. The site can be crowded at peak season, with multiple boats mooring simultaneously — arriving early maximizes the quality of dolphin encounters before the mid-morning rush.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Offshore Hurghada, northern Red Sea, Egypt
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters (65-100 feet)
- **Current**: Light inside lagoon; moderate on outer reef
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

The resident spinner dolphin pod is the headline attraction. Pod size varies but typically numbers 20–50 individuals, and encounters of divers and dolphins in the lagoon are documented in almost every trip report from this site. Dolphins are the primary reason most divers visit Shaab El Erg, but the reef itself supports a full northern Red Sea reef community. humphead wrasse are resident on the outer reef edge. Sea turtles are regularly seen on the reef platform. The lagoon and outer reef host the standard anthias, parrotfish, surgeonfish, and butterflyfish assemblage. Moray eels and lionfish occupy the crevices throughout.

## Dive Profile

Dive strategy at Shaab El Erg depends on dolphin activity. For dolphin encounters, hover calmly in the lagoon and allow the pod to approach — chasing is counterproductive and stressful for the animals. For reef diving, follow the outer reef slope from the crest at 5–8 meters down to the reef base at 18–20 meters. The outer wall is the more interesting section for coral and fish diversity.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Hurghada day operators. This site is on nearly every Hurghada day-boat circuit. Entry by giant stride or back roll. Multiple boats may be present; coordinate SMB deployment carefully and be visible to boat crew before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Arrive as early as possible — pre-9 AM visits typically encounter more dolphins with less diver disturbance
- Stay calm and still in the water; approaching or chasing dolphins causes them to leave
- The outer reef offers better diving than the inner lagoon for reef fish and coral
- This site is often combined with nearby Giftun Island dives for a full day

## Safety Considerations

Multiple boats at this site create a busy surface environment. Always deploy an SMB before ascending and look up before surfacing. Dolphins are protected species — do not touch, pursue, or block their movement. The outer reef can develop moderate current at tidal changes.

## Photography

Spinner dolphins in clear shallow water are the photographic highlight. A wide or fisheye lens captures multiple dolphins in frame against the sunlit water surface. For the best results, enter the water calmly and wait — patience produces far better images than pursuit. The outer reef wall offers coral and fish photography as a secondary opportunity.
