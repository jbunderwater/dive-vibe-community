## Gota Soraya

Gota Soraya is a wall dive site in the deep south Red Sea, rated among the best wall dives in the region. Coordinates place it near the St. John's / Fury Shoals area (lat 23.41, lng 35.90), a remote liveaboard-only zone approximately south of the Wadi El Gemal National Park. Access is exclusively by liveaboard vessel.

## Overview

Gota Soraya features overhangs and cracks in the reef wall filled with marine life, typical of the Red Sea's finest offshore wall dives. The site is assessed as suitable for advanced divers due to its exposed location and potential for currents. Average visibility reported at 10–20 m. Maximum depth approximately 20 m in the primary dive zone, though the wall extends deeper.

## Marine Life

The site is documented to host large barracudas, snappers, surgeonfish schools, giant trevallies, and reef sharks. Eagle rays and occasional manta rays are also reported. The wall's overhangs shelter macro life and soft coral growth typical of southern Red Sea offshore reefs.

## Dive Profile

Wall dive accessible by boat only. Average visibility 10–20 m. Depth approximately 20 m for the main dive; the wall structure descends further. Suitable for advanced divers. Liveaboard access only given the remote location in the deep south.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Deep south Red Sea, approximately St. John's / Fury Shoals area, Egypt — liveaboard access only
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
