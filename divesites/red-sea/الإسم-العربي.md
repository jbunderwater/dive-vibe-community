## الإسم العربي ؟

الإسم العربي ؟ — meaning "The Arabic Name?" — is a reef dive site in the Ras Mohammed area at the southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, in the northern Red Sea, Egypt. The placeholder name in OpenStreetMap indicates this is a site known locally by an Arabic designation that has not yet been formally transliterated for international dive maps.

## Overview

The coordinates for this site place it within the Ras Mohammed / Sharm el-Sheikh diving corridor, one of the most intensively dived stretches of reef in the world. The site offers a standard Sinai reef profile: a sloping reef with healthy hard coral coverage transitioning to deeper sandy areas, with wall sections in certain areas. Despite its unofficial mapping status, the site is likely dived regularly by local operators who know it by its Egyptian Arabic name. The surrounding area of Ras Mohammed National Park provides exceptional baseline reef health.

## Marine Life

Within the Ras Mohammed National Park corridor, even lesser-known reef sites support the characteristic Sinai reef community. Anthias and chromis cloud the upper coral formations. Napoleon wrasse are regularly encountered. Reef sharks, including grey reef sharks, patrol the reef edges and deeper zones. Sea turtles rest on the coral platform. Triggerfish and surgeonfish are common in the shallows. Moray eels and lionfish inhabit the crevices throughout the reef depth range. Grouper of various species occupy the deeper sections.

## Dive Profile

Standard Sinai reef profile: descend to the reef slope and explore at depths between 10-30 meters. The upper reef at 5-15 meters hosts the highest fish density. The deeper sections between 20-30 meters may produce shark sightings. Ascend gradually along the reef slope to finish with a safety stop on the reef top at 3-5 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Sharm el-Sheikh operators. Standard back roll or giant stride entry from the dive deck. Deploy an SMB at the safety stop for boat pickup. Conditions in the area are variable; follow the dive briefing from the local operator who will know current state of conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Ask the local dive operator for the site's local Arabic name — this improves communication with Egyptian boat crews
- Within the Ras Mohammed area, even uncharted reef sections often produce excellent diving
- Monitor current direction during descent; Ras Mohammed currents can be strong and changeable
- Carry an SMB and signaling devices as mandatory equipment for all Ras Mohammed dives

## Safety Considerations

Ras Mohammed area currents can be strong and occasionally unpredictable. Always carry an SMB and be prepared to abort the dive if conditions deteriorate. Fire coral is abundant in the shallows. Never stand on or rest on the reef structure. Dive conservatively at depth given the proximity to the reef edge where open-water conditions can develop.

## Photography

The Sinai reef environment provides consistent photography opportunities with the characteristic mix of hard coral formations, anthias clouds, and large roving fish. Wide-angle reef shots and napoleon wrasse portraits are reliable subjects. The deeper reef sections may produce shark encounters for open-water wide-angle photography. Natural light in the Sinai's clear water is excellent for ambient-light reef photography in the morning hours.
