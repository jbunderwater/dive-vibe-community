## House Reef

House Reef is a dive site in the Hamata area of the southern Egyptian Red Sea at approximately 24.26°N, 35.41°E, placing it near the Lahami Bay resort cluster. This generic "House Reef" designation in OSM most likely refers to the fringing housereef associated with one of the dive camps or resorts in the Hamata / Lahami Bay area — a zone that includes Wadi Lahami (Red Sea Diving Safari) and Lahami Bay Beach Resort (Extra Divers).

## Overview

The Hamata coastline features a banana-shaped coral ridge stretching over 1.5 km in front of Lahami Bay Beach Resort. Outer sections of this housereef are accessible via zodiac or speedboat from the beach station and offer seven different dive spots along the outer reef structure. The fringing reef features typical Fury Shoals coastal characteristics: diverse coral formations, dramatic drop-offs, and rich fish communities.

## Marine Life

Southern Red Sea fringing reefs in this area typically support:
- Seahorses in the seagrass areas between beach and reef
- Reef fish: angelfish, parrotfish, triggerfish, groupers
- Moray eels and lionfish in reef crevices
- Sea turtles and occasional reef sharks

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from beach, with additional sections accessible by small boat (felluca or zodiac). Typical depths 5–25 m along the fringing reef. Calm conditions inshore; more exposed outer sections available by boat.
