## Daedalus Reef (2)

Daedalus Reef is one of the most celebrated offshore reefs in the Red Sea, rising from depths of around 450 metres to break the surface roughly 80 kilometres east of Marsa Alam. A staffed Egyptian lighthouse sits atop the reef's small island. Because of its remote location the site is accessible only by liveaboard, but that isolation has kept its walls in outstanding condition and the pelagic life exceptional.

## Overview

The reef is roughly 500 metres long and oval in shape. Vertical walls drop from the surface to beyond recreational limits on all sides; the main dive zone runs from the surface to around 40 metres where the wall begins to slope outward. Visibility routinely reaches 25–30 metres or more in the October-to-February peak season. Currents are variable and can be strong at the north and south tips, particularly on an incoming flow — those are also the best conditions for close encounters with schooling scalloped hammerheads.

## Marine Life

Daedalus is defined by two headline shark species. **Oceanic whitetip sharks** (*Carcharhinus longimanus*) are present from October through February and often remain with divers for an entire dive. **Scalloped hammerheads** congregate at the north and south tips on early-morning dives with an incoming current, and schooling behaviour has been repeatedly documented here — it is widely regarded as one of the best sites in the entire Red Sea for this experience. Silky sharks and occasional thresher sharks are also recorded. The walls host dense aggregations of anthias, groupers, moray eels, trevally, and tuna. Red Sea endemic species including dottybacks, bluespine unicornfish, and Red Sea anemonefish populate the shallower sections.

## Dive Profile

The eastern wall is generally recommended for morning dives; the western wall — featuring a 10-metre ledge with large porites formations, anemones, and cleaning stations — suits afternoon dives. Both walls offer drift diving when current runs along the reef. Minimum certification recommended is Advanced Open Water with at least 30 logged dives and experience in current-exposed environments.

## Entry / Exit

Liveaboard-only. No shore access. Vessels typically moor or anchor overnight, allowing up to four dives per day on the reef.

## Photography

The combination of large pelagic sharks, steep walls coloured by anthias and soft corals, and the lighthouse above water makes this one of the Red Sea's premier photography destinations. Wide-angle lenses suit shark and wall compositions; macro subjects (dottybacks, nudibranchs) are available in calmer sections.

## Tips & Safety

- Dive early-morning at the north or south tip on an incoming current for the highest chance of seeing hammerhead schools.
- A 5 mm wetsuit is appropriate for multi-dive days, especially October–February when water temperature drops to 22–24°C.
- Remain calm and composed around oceanic whitetips; avoid erratic movements. Do not ascend alone.
- This site is not suitable for divers without current experience.
