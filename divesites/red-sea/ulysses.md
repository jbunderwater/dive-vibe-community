# Ulysses

A wreck dive in the southern Hurghada offshore zone — a vessel lying at 30 metres with coral colonisation on its upper structure, resident shark activity on the sandy floor, and the deeper profile that distinguishes it from the standard Hurghada reef circuit.

## Overview

Ulysses is a 95.1-metre British iron screw steamer built in the 1880s that struck the Island of Gobal Seghir in the Straits of Gubal on 16 August 1887 after departing Suez. All 32 crew members survived and were rescued by passing ships. The wreck lay undiscovered on the sea floor until 1993, when it was located by divers from the Sharm el-Sheikh diving community. The Ulysses lies on her port side with the bow at around 4 metres and the stern at approximately 28 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Straits of Gubal, northern Egyptian Red Sea (near Island of Gobal Seghir)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; October to May optimal

## Marine Life

Whitetip reef sharks rest on the sandy floor adjacent to the wreck — they are the wreck's most reliable large-animal encounter. Schools of glassfish and snapper occupy the wreck's interior accessible sections. The upper hull structure carries soft coral and gorgonian colonisation. Moray eels are resident throughout the wreck's corroded cavities. Groupers patrol the outer hull sections. Schools of barracuda circle in the open water above the wreck.

## Dive Profile

Descend directly to the deepest section of the wreck at 28–30 metres and check the sandy floor for resting whitetip reef sharks. Work along the hull at depth for 10–12 minutes before beginning the multi-level ascent. The mid-sections of the wreck at 18–25 metres carry the best coral colonisation. The shallowest sections of the upper hull at 15–18 metres are explored last. Safety stop at 5 metres in the open water adjacent to the wreck.

## Entry and Exit

Day boat from Hurghada (40–60 minutes). Intermediate divers with some wreck experience recommended for the 30-metre profile. Giant stride entry. SMB deployment before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check the sandy floor at the bow or stern for resting whitetip reef sharks immediately on descent — they are typically in the same resting positions dive after dive
- Do not approach resting sharks — remain stationary and observe
- A torch is essential for examining the hull sections at depth — carry a primary and backup light
- Do not penetrate the interior without overhead-environment training

## Safety Considerations

The 30-metre maximum depth requires air management awareness — begin ascent at 70 bar. No-decompression limits must be respected. No hull penetration without cave/wreck speciality training. Moray eels in the interior cavities react defensively if surprised. SMB mandatory before ascending.

## Photography

Whitetip reef shark portraits on the sandy floor adjacent to the wreck are the site's signature image — approach slowly from a distance and photograph from the horizontal plane at the shark's eye level. The colonised upper hull structure in natural light at 15–20 metres suits wide-angle wreck shots. Interior accessible sections with glassfish schools reward torch-assisted wide-angle technique.
