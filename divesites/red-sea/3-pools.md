## 3 Pools

3 Pools is a coral reef dive site near Sharm el Sheikh in the northern Red Sea, Egypt.

## Overview

3 Pools is a well-known dive site in the Strait of Tiran area, northeast of Sharm el Sheikh. The site takes its name from three interconnected sandy pools or lagoons set among reef formations, creating a sheltered and characterful dive environment. The surrounding reef is rich with hard and soft corals, and the pools themselves attract a variety of reef fish seeking refuge from stronger currents in the channel. Visibility is typically outstanding, often exceeding 30 meters, and the warm clear waters of the northern Red Sea make this an appealing site year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Strait of Tiran area, Sharm el Sheikh, Red Sea, Egypt
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-40 meters
- **Current**: Moderate; can strengthen in the Tiran channel
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

The sheltered pools and surrounding reef host a healthy cross-section of Red Sea species. Expect to find bluespotted ribbontail rays resting on the sandy floors of the pools, alongside various species of grouper and snapper. The coral slopes attract anthias clouds, bannerfish, and butterflyfishes. Moray eels are common in crevices, and glassfish schools frequently appear near the reef edge. Occasional sightings of reef sharks and humphead wrasse have been reported by visiting divers.

## Dive Profile

Start in the shallower reef plateau (5–10 m) before dropping to the sandy pools at around 15–20 m. The pools provide an excellent opportunity to hover neutrally and observe benthic life without surge. End the dive on the reef top for a safety stop among the coral gardens before ascending to the surface.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by liveaboard or day-boat from Sharm el Sheikh or Na'ama Bay. Most operators moor at the reef edge and divers descend directly onto the reef. Giant stride entry is standard. Surface marker buoy deployment is recommended for the ascent, particularly when currents are running.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Dive early in the morning for the calmest conditions and best light in the pools
- The sandy pools are ideal for wide-angle photography with ambient light
- Keep an eye on the current — conditions in the Tiran Strait can change quickly
- Combine with nearby Thomas Reef or Woodhouse Reef for a full day of Tiran diving
- Maintain careful buoyancy over the sandy floors to avoid stirring sediment

## Safety Considerations

Currents in the Strait of Tiran can be strong and unpredictable. Always carry a surface marker buoy (SMB) and an audible signalling device. Follow the dive briefing closely and agree on a turnaround point and compass heading with your buddy. Dive within your certification limits.

## Photography

The sandy pools offer excellent macro opportunities — flatworms, nudibranchs, and small crustaceans are found in the sediment. The reef walls provide good wide-angle subjects with schooling fish and coral formations. Natural light is strongest and clearest in the mid-morning hours.
