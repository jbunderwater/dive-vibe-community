## شعب وودهاوس

شعب وودهاوس (Woodhouse Reef) is the second reef from the north in the Tiran Strait reef system, located between Jackson Reef and Thomas Reef in the Tiran Strait, northern Sinai, Egypt. Like its neighbors, Woodhouse is an advanced wall dive site defined by strong tidal currents and exceptional marine life.

## Overview

Woodhouse Reef forms the second in the chain of four reefs — Gordon, Thomas, Woodhouse, and Jackson — that stretch across the Tiran Strait between the Sinai Peninsula and Tiran Island. The reef is a submerged platform with vertical walls dropping steeply on its flanks, reaching depths well beyond recreational limits. The top of the reef comes very close to the surface, making it a navigational hazard for vessels — a fact attested by the number of wrecks on these reefs. The current at Woodhouse is strong and variable, and the site is best approached as a drift dive along the wall face. The reef is accessed exclusively by day boat from Sharm el-Sheikh.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Tiran Strait, near Sharm el-Sheikh, Sinai Peninsula, Red Sea, Egypt
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-35 meters (65-115 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: September to May

## Marine Life

Woodhouse Reef shares the exceptional marine life of the Tiran Strait system. Grey reef sharks patrol the wall edges, often moving in pairs or small groups in the mid-water current. Large schools of barracuda hover in the water column above the reef. Napoleon wrasse are a regular sighting. Turtles are occasionally seen resting in the calmer sections of the reef top. The walls are richly covered in soft corals and sea fans with crinoids at intermediate depths. Dense anthias populations occupy the upper reef sections, and moray eels are present throughout the wall crevices.

## Dive Profile

Typically dived as a drift along the reef wall. Entry is made upflow of the dive site, and the dive progresses with the current at 12-25 meters along the wall face. Woodhouse can be dived on its own or combined with an adjacent reef depending on current direction and boat logistics. Ascend before reaching the end of the productive wall section, deploy an SMB, and complete a safety stop at 5 meters before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Day boat from Sharm el-Sheikh operators. Entry point is briefed on the day based on current assessment. Back roll from the boat deck. Mid-water SMB deployment for pickup. The dive guide will be familiar with current patterns for this specific reef and will brief accordingly.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Woodhouse pairs well with Thomas Reef on the same day boat trip — check with operators for multi-reef dive packages
- Monitor buoyancy carefully on the wall face to avoid unintentional depth increases in the current
- A reel and SMB combination provides control during ascent in moving water
- Watch for grey reef sharks in the mid-water off the wall — they are often less visible than at Jackson but present
- Arrive at the reef positioned upgurrent and don't fight the drift

## Safety Considerations

Strong current is the primary hazard at Woodhouse Reef. Experienced drift diving skills are required. The shipping lanes in the Tiran Strait carry commercial traffic — never surface without checking for vessels. Always deploy a highly visible SMB before beginning the safety stop ascent. Carry whistle, mirror, and a surface alert device. Do not attempt this site without experience with Red Sea drift diving.

## Photography

The Tiran Strait reef system provides consistent wide-angle opportunities at all four reefs. At Woodhouse, the grey reef shark encounters in mid-water current are the primary subject. The soft coral-covered walls provide rich backgrounds. Schools of barracuda and jacks photograph well in the blue water above the reef top. The drift speed requires quick framing and fast shutter speeds; use Aperture Priority with high ISO for flexibility in changing light conditions.
