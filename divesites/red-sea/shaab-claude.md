## Shaab Claude

Shaab Claude is a coral reef in the Safaga area of the northern Egyptian Red Sea, located at approximately 26.65°N in the productive stretch of offshore reefs that define Safaga's diving landscape. Like many sites in the Safaga region, the reef takes its informal name from a diver or dive operator who regularly worked the area. The site is part of the extensive reef network that makes Safaga one of Egypt's better destinations for current-driven reef diving.

## Overview

Shaab Claude rises from deeper water to a shallow reef crest in the 5–10 meter range. The reef structure features a combination of sloping walls, coral terraces, and sandy passes between reef sections. The Safaga area is known for its strong currents, which channel between offshore reefs and shoals — these currents deliver nutrients that support unusually dense fish populations and give the soft corals on current-facing walls their characteristic fullness and color. Visibility in the Safaga region is generally strong, with averages of 20–30 meters in good conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Safaga, northern Red Sea, Egypt
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters (65-100 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

The Safaga reef system is particularly productive for pelagic encounters. Spinner dolphins frequently investigate divers in the mid-water zone around the outer reef edges. Schools of barracuda and trevally hunt the reef tips in current flow. humphead wrasse are resident in the deeper sections of the reef. The reef surface is covered in the dense anthias and chromis clouds typical of healthy Red Sea reefs. Hammerhead sharks have been reported along the deeper reef sections in the cooler winter months. Reef sharks are occasional visitors throughout the year.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef crest and assess the current direction before committing to a route. In strong current, plan a drift along the slope between 10–20 meters. In calmer conditions, the reef can be explored as a circuit covering multiple coral terraces. The sandy passes between reef sections are worth checking for rays. Ascend on the reef or in the blue with a deployed SMB for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by day boat from Safaga harbor or by liveaboard. Entry conditions depend on current strength. Giant stride entry and immediate descent if current is running. Drift dives should have a prearranged exit point confirmed with the boat crew before entry.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Visit in winter months (October to January) for the best pelagic and hammerhead sightings
- Drift dive technique makes the site more enjoyable when currents are strong
- Pair with Safaga Soraya or Shaab Hamdallah for a full Safaga reef day
- Safaga is a less crowded alternative to Hurghada — expect fewer boats at the site

## Safety Considerations

Safaga currents can be strong and require divers to be comfortable with drift conditions. Assess current strength before entry and plan the exit accordingly. SMB deployment is essential for all open-water ascents. Be aware of channel currents between reef structures that can accelerate unexpectedly. Fire coral and lionfish are present throughout.

## Photography

The combination of current-swept soft coral walls and schooling pelagics makes Shaab Claude a strong wide-angle site. Spinner dolphins in the mid-water zone are exceptional if encountered. The coral terraces at mid-depth provide structured compositions with ambient blue water backgrounds.
