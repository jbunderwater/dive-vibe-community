## Police Station

Police Station is a shore-entry reef dive site on the Red Sea coast near the Hurghada / El Gouna area, named after its position adjacent to a local police facility — a common naming convention for Red Sea dive sites that use nearby landmarks as reference points. The coordinates (27.19°N, 33.97°E) place the site in the central Red Sea coast zone between Hurghada and Safaga.

## Overview

Shore dive sites in this stretch of the Egyptian Red Sea typically feature a shallow sandy entry leading to a fringing reef running parallel to the coastline. The reef flat transitions to a coral wall or slope beginning at 5–10 metres and extending to 20–25 metres. These inshore reefs in the Hurghada region are populated by colourful hard and soft corals and support a dense community of reef fish.

## Marine Life

The central Egyptian Red Sea coast supports abundant reef fish including parrotfish, wrasse, groupers, moray eels, lionfish, crocodilefish, and schools of fusiliers. Octopus and cuttlefish are common on night dives. Blue-spotted stingrays rest on sandy patches. The hard coral formations provide shelter for anthias, damsels, and cardinalfish.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry — typical of this region — with a walk or wade across the reef flat before descending. The dive follows the reef wall or slope to a maximum of around 25 metres before ascending along the reef top. Shore diving in this area is generally uncomplicated but requires awareness of occasional boat traffic in the approach channels.
