# Tobia Soraya

Three chains of connected coral ergs forming an inverted Y-shape near Safaga — a diverse site with a swim-through cave, a gorgonian pinnacle, crocodilefish on the sandy floor, and a connection to Tobia Kebir for a two-site dive.

## Overview

Tobia Soraya lies approximately 30 minutes east of Safaga in the central Egyptian Red Sea. The reef consists of three chains of connected ergs (coral pillars) that converge in an inverted Y configuration, standing in 12–15 metres of water on a white sand seabed. Each of the three chains has a distinct character: the western chain has shallow passages at 4–6 metres; the northern formation features a mini-cove garden and a gorgonian-and-glassfish pinnacle; the eastern formation contains a small canyon and a swim-through cave. The three formations can be circumnavigated in a single 50-minute dive, providing a comprehensive experience in a modest depth range. Current can develop on the east side, and the site connects southward to the Tobia Kebir complex.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East of Safaga, central Egyptian Red Sea
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Generally mild; stronger on the east chain
- **Best Time**: Year-round; October to May optimal

## Marine Life

Crocodilefish are reliably found on the sandy seabed between the erg chains — their excellent camouflage makes them easy to miss unless specifically sought. Clownfish occupy anemones throughout the site. Puffer fish are common around the ergs. Scorpionfish occupy crevice positions on the coral walls. Cleaning stations are distributed across the site — groupers and moray eels are regularly found in cleaning positions. Blue-spotted stingrays rest on the sandy floor. Glassfish cascade from the northern pinnacle, attracting hunting jacks and groupers. Butterflyfish and anthias are abundant on the upper erg surfaces.

## Dive Profile

Begin at the northern formation to see the gorgonian pinnacle and glassfish cloud, then move to the mini-cove garden. Work along the western chain at 4–8 metres for the shallow passage experience. Finish at the eastern chain: the canyon and swim-through cave are the signature elements here. If air permits, the dive can extend south toward Tobia Kebir's southern formations, which connect at the reef base. Safety stop at 5 metres on any of the erg tops.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Safaga (30 minutes). Moorings at the site are present but not well-sheltered from wave action — conditions affect entry quality. Giant stride entry adjacent to the northern formation. The connection to Tobia Kebir is possible on a longer dive with good air management.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Examine the sandy floor between erg chains carefully for crocodilefish — slow movement and looking ahead at the sand surface is the best technique
- The swim-through cave on the eastern chain is accessible without overhead training — bring a small torch for the interior
- For the full Tobia complex experience, combine with Tobia Kebir as a second dive for a two-site day
- The cleaning stations reward stationary observation — stay still at 2–3 metres distance from the station

## Safety Considerations

Conditions are generally mild at Tobia Soraya, but the eastern chain can develop moderate current at tidal changes. Monitor position if current increases on the eastern side. The swim-through cave requires single-file movement and good buoyancy control. Scorpionfish on the erg surfaces require visual awareness. Fire coral is present on the upper erg structures. Always carry and deploy an SMB before ascending.

## Photography

The gorgonian-and-glassfish northern pinnacle is an outstanding close-focus wide-angle subject — strobes at steep angles produce the glassfish cloud in cascading silver against the gorgonian background. Crocodilefish portraits on the sand require a macro lens and an extremely patient, low-angle approach. Clownfish-in-anemone shots are consistently accessible throughout the site. The shallow eastern cave swim-through is a natural-light image from outside looking at the reef structure.
