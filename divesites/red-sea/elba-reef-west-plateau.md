# Elba Reef, West-Plateau

The sheltered western plateau of Elba Reef — the primary mooring base for liveaboard expeditions to this remote atoll, where coral coverage is exceptional and the gentle current provides a calm contrast to the exposed eastern face.

## Overview

Elba Reef West-Plateau occupies the western side of Elba Reef atoll at approximately 21.97°N, forming the more sheltered, leeward face of the reef system. The western plateau runs from the surface to approximately 20 metres before dropping into deeper water. As the primary mooring side for liveaboards visiting Elba, this section accumulates the most diver time on the atoll — though diver traffic remains a fraction of any mainstream Red Sea site. The plateau is characterised by broad terraces of hard coral in excellent condition, with sandy passages between coral ridges and a gradual outer slope. It serves as both the reference dive on Elba for groups new to the atoll and as the base for night dives when conditions permit.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Western face, Elba Reef atoll, near Egyptian-Sudanese border (~21.97°N)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–35 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; generally calmer than the eastern face
- **Best Time**: October to April

## Marine Life

The western plateau supports a comprehensive reef ecosystem: dense anthias clouds over the hard coral tops, resident humphead wrasse pairs patrolling the outer slope, and grey reef sharks visible in the mid-water during current. Large groupers occupy the coral structure at 12–18 metres. Hawksbill turtles rest on the plateau regularly — the calm conditions make close observation straightforward. Schools of goatfish and snapper concentrate on the outer slope. Cleaning stations are distributed across the plateau at 8–15 metres. Night dives on the western plateau produce Spanish dancers, octopuses, and feeding moray eels in the coral rubble.

## Dive Profile

Begin on the outer plateau edge at 15–18 metres and work the slope up toward 8–10 metres, visiting the cleaning stations and observing the humphead wrasse and sharks. Move across the plateau surface at 8–12 metres, covering the coral formations and photographing the concentrated fish life. The sandy passages between coral ridges at 12–15 metres are the most productive sections for stingrays and macro subjects. Safety stop at 5 metres over the upper plateau in excellent natural light.

## Entry and Exit

The western plateau is the standard mooring position for all Elba Reef liveaboard visits. Egyptian Navy permit required for access. Giant stride entry from the tender. Conditions are generally calmer here than on the eastern face — entry and exit are typically straightforward. SMB deployment before ascending is mandatory. Night dives are conducted from the same mooring position with buddy torches and surface marker lights.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The western plateau is the first dive on Elba for most groups — use it to calibrate buoyancy for the more demanding eastern face dives planned for subsequent days
- Cleaning stations at 8–12 metres reward patient, stationary observation — groupers and morays hold position for extended periods
- Night dives here are among the highlights of any southern Red Sea expedition — Spanish dancer encounters are reliably available
- Carry a torch on all dives to examine coral overhangs for lobsters, soldierfish, and sleeping reef fish
- The outer slope at 18–20 metres connects to the western fingers section to the north — combine for a longer dive if air permits

## Safety Considerations

The western plateau is the most benign section of Elba Reef — conditions are typically calmer and current lighter than the eastern side. Primary hazards are shared with all remote sites: equipment failure logistics, remoteness of emergency care. Fire coral on the upper plateau. Titan triggerfish may be territorial in summer months (typically outside October–April season). SMB on all ascents.

## Photography

The western plateau in good ambient light is one of the most versatile photography environments at Elba. Wide-angle reef-and-fish images in visibility exceeding 25 metres are the baseline. Humphead wrasse portraits at the outer slope are accessible with patient positioning. Night dive photography — Spanish dancers, octopuses, macro invertebrates — is a significant part of the western plateau experience and rewards dedicated housing and strobe setups.
