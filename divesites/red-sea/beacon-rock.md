## Beacon Rock

Beacon Rock is the southern tip of Sha'ab Mahmoud in the Straits of Gubal — a reef and wreck combination site where the SS Dunraven wreck lies directly below the South Cardinal Beacon. It is typically combined with the Dunraven wreck dive as a single boat trip.

## Overview

The name Beacon Rock refers to the physical beacon (navigation marker) mounted on the southern tip of Sha'ab Mahmoud. The dive site encompasses the reef structure at the southern end of the shoal as well as the Dunraven wreck, which rests on the reef face at 15–30 metres. The reef itself offers healthy coral growth on the shallower sections of the reef slope and is particularly suited to night diving. Visibility is typically 20+ metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sha'ab Mahmoud, Straits of Gubal, Egypt
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-35 meters (65-115 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate
- **Best Time**: September to May

## Marine Life

The reef supports the typical Gubal Straits fish community. Lionfish and moray eels are abundant and particularly active on night dives. The reef's coral growth hosts anthias, butterflyfish, and groupers. The Dunraven wreck attached to this reef adds the wreck community of glassy sweepers, soft corals, and resident groupers.
