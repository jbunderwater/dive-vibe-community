## L'Augustin Fresnel II

L'Augustin Fresnel II is a 53-metre Canadian lighthouse and beacon tender deliberately sunk in 2003, now resting at 33 metres after being shifted by Cyclone Omar in 2008.

## Overview

The vessel was built in Canada in the late 1940s and served the French Lighthouses and Beacons service, operating in the Mediterranean (Marseille and Corsica) before being transferred to Pointe-à-Pitre in 1990 to maintain navigation marks for the Antilles-Guyana maritime zone. It was decommissioned in 1994. Dimensions: 53 metres long, 9 metres wide, 660 gross tonnes.

After years of decommissioning, the vessel was fully cleaned of pollutants and deliberately sunk as an artificial reef on 9 July 2003 within the Cousteau Reserve area. It initially came to rest at 28–29 metres. During Cyclone Omar (October 2008), the wreck slipped deeper; it now rests flat on its keel at 33 metres at the level of the propeller.

Note: Several earlier sources — including previous versions of this database — incorrectly attributed the shift to Cyclone Dean (2007). Multiple independent sources confirm the correct cyclone was Omar (2008).

The wreck is the third and most recently added artificial reef in the Cousteau Reserve, joining the Gustavia (1991) and the Franjack (1996). It is the deepest of the three, requiring Advanced certification.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cousteau Reserve area, Bouillante, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15+ meters
- **Current**: Absent to light within the reserve

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at this site: barracuda, jacks, lobsters, moray eels, scorpionfish, nudibranchs, stingrays on the sandy bottom, sea turtles. The hull is covered in coral and sponge encrustation after more than two decades underwater.

## Dive Profile

Descend along the guide line attached to the bow. The upper superstructure elements and deck are at approximately 25–28 metres; the keel and propeller at 33 metres. Interior exploration is possible — accessible spaces include the old crew cabins, the galley, and the messdeck. Advanced divers should plan gas carefully for a 33-metre maximum depth profile. Nitrox 32% is the standard gas mix used by Bouillante operators for this dive. Minimum 20-minute boat transit from shore.

## Entry and Exit

Operated by licensed Bouillante dive operators. Permanent mooring on the wreck. Giant stride entry. Safety stop of minimum 3 minutes at 5 metres mandatory.

## Safety

At 33 metres, narcosis and no-decompression time limits require careful planning. The wreck has been in situ for over 20 years and some sections may have degraded — inspect structural condition before entering any enclosed space. Interior penetration requires proper wreck training and a primary light source; it is not an activity to attempt without appropriate certification. Never characterise penetration as fully safe.
