## L'Augustin Fresnel II

L'Augustin Fresnel II is a 53-metre Canadian lighthouse tender deliberately scuttled in 2003 within the Cousteau Reserve — the most recently added and best-preserved of the three reserve wrecks, now resting at 31–33 metres depth.

## Overview

The Augustin Fresnel II was a lighthouse service tender built in Canada in the 1940s (53 m × 9 m, 660 tonnes). It served the French Lighthouse and Beacon Service in the Mediterranean before being transferred to Guadeloupe in 1990 to maintain navigational markings for the Antilles-Guyana region. It was retired from service in 1994. After decontamination and preparation, it was deliberately scuttled on 9 July 2003 within the Cousteau Reserve as an artificial reef.

The wreck was originally placed at approximately 30 metres depth. Cyclone Dean (2007) subsequently shifted the vessel to its current position; it now rests to 31–33 metres (to the propeller) on a sandy bottom. Les Heures Saines records the depth as 30 m to the deck; Tribloo and OSM data record 33 m to the deepest point; Divingaway records 31 m — sources differ slightly on measurement point.

Interior spaces including cabins, galley, and dining areas are accessible for exploration.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cousteau Reserve, south of Malendure, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (PE40 / AOW + Deep required)
- **Maximum Depth**: 31–33 metres (sources vary slightly; deck at approximately 30 m)
- **Vessel Length**: 53 metres
- **Vessel Tonnage**: 660 tonnes
- **Scuttled**: 9 July 2003

## Marine Life

Confirmed at this wreck by Les Heures Saines: barracuda, jacks (carangues), spiny lobsters (langoustines), and moray eels. Groupers and hawksbill turtles are reported by Tribloo and other operators. No additional species are attributed without site-specific confirmation.

## Safety

Advanced certification mandatory. Penetration of interior spaces (cabins, galley) is possible — enter only with a torch and appropriate wreck training. The vessel has been in the water since 2003 and structural integrity should be assessed before penetrating deeper interior sections. Gas management at 30–33 m is critical; Nitrox is available from Bouillante operators and extends bottom time. Safety stop at 5 metres mandatory.
