## L'Augustin Fresnel II

L'Augustin Fresnel II is a 53-metre Canadian lighthouse service vessel deliberately sunk July 9, 2003 in the Cousteau Reserve — the most recently added of the reserve's three wrecks and the most accessible for exploring the architecture of a working lighthouse tender.

## Overview

The Augustin Fresnel II was built in Canada in the late 1940s (660 tons, 53 m × 9 m) and served the French Lighthouses and Beacons service, maintaining navigation markers across the Antilles and French Guiana from its assignment to Pointe-à-Pitre in 1990. Retired in 1994, the vessel was cleaned and deliberately sunk on July 9, 2003, as an artificial reef within the Cousteau Reserve.

After sinking, it was originally placed at approximately 30 metres. Cyclone Dean in 2007 shifted the hull to its current resting position, with the deepest section reaching 33 metres.

**Vessel specifications** (per Tribloo and Les Heures Saines): 53 m length, 9 m width, 660 tons. Built late 1940s, Canada. Sunk July 9, 2003.

## Marine Life

The wreck supports a growing reef community. Barracudas, jacks, spiny lobsters (crayfish), moray eels, groupers, and conger eels are confirmed residents. Sea turtles shelter against the superstructure. The hull also hosts stingrays, scorpionfish, hermit crabs, nudibranchs, angelfish, and hard coral growth. The lighthouse vessel's distinctive architecture — crane operator cabin, bow support structures, twin propellers at the stern — provides unusual structural habitats compared to the reserve's other wrecks.

## Dive Profile

Descend on the bow line to the main deck. The superstructures sit at the shallower end; the deepest keel section reaches 33 metres. Explore the distinctive lighthouse vessel features: crane cabin, hoisting equipment, bow navigation structures, and the stern with its twin screws. Interior access to the former crew spaces is possible for advanced divers. Ascend along the hull to the bow section for the safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Operated by Bouillante dive centres. Advanced (CMAS 2 / AOW + Deep Dive specialty) certification required. Boat crossing approximately 20 minutes.

## Safety

At 33 metres this is at depth for recreational diving. Gas management is important — ascend conservatively and plan a 3-minute minimum safety stop at 5 metres. Nitrox 32% recommended. The wreck was sunk 22 years ago and some sections may have reduced structural integrity — assess before penetrating enclosed spaces.
