## L'Augustin Fresnel II

L'Augustin Fresnel II is a former Canadian beacon tender deliberately sunk as an artificial reef in 2003 south of the Cousteau Reserve near Bouillante, resting at approximately 29–33 metres on a sandy bottom.

## Overview

The vessel has a thoroughly documented history. Built in Canada in the early 1940s, she measures 53 metres long and 9 metres wide with a tonnage of 660 tonnes. She served the French Lighthouses and Beacons service in the Mediterranean (Marseille, then Corsica) before being transferred to Pointe-à-Pitre in 1990 to maintain channel markers for the Antilles-Guyane zone. She was retired from service in 1994 and, after professional decontamination, was deliberately sunk as an artificial reef on 9 July 2003. She rests upright on her keel.

**Depth:** Sources give different depths — tribloo.com records the bottom at approximately 30 m; Les Heures Saines (primary operator) gives 30–33 m; the OSM water_level tag records 33 m; Archipel Plongée gives 25–28 m. The most consistent range across dedicated dive operator sources is 29–33 m. Some sources suggest Cyclone Dean (2007) shifted the wreck slightly deeper; not all sources confirm this movement.

The wreck has 15 cabins and gangways accessible to divers. It is the deepest of the three wrecks sunk in this area, requiring Advanced certification.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bouillante area, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe (south of the main Cousteau Reserve zone)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters
- **Certification Required**: PADI Advanced Open Water / CMAS 2 minimum

## Marine Life

Documented species at this site include stingrays, barracudas, moray eels, groupers, scorpionfish, trevallies, nudibranchs, and angelfish. Crayfish (spiny lobsters) have been documented in accessible interior spaces. The 15 cabins and gangways provide shelter for diverse fish communities. Note: the claim of "hawksbill turtles shelter against the superstructure" is reported by the operator Tribloo but not independently confirmed at this specific wreck; included with that caveat.

## Dive Profile

- **Depth range**: Upper deck ~29 m; keel/propeller at ~33 m
- **Entry**: Boat dive; descent via bow mooring line
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Penetration**: Interior spaces accessible (crew quarters, galley, mess deck); torch essential
- **Visibility**: Typically 15–25 m
- **Bottom time**: Limited on air at this depth — Nitrox recommended
