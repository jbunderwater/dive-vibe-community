## L'Augustin Fresnel II

L'Augustin Fresnel II is a 53-metre French lighthouse-service vessel deliberately sunk on 9 July 2003 near Bouillante, Guadeloupe, creating an artificial reef that rests on a sandy bottom at 30–33 metres. It is the third and most recently added wreck in the Cousteau Reserve diving area and the largest of the three.

## Overview

The vessel was built in Canada in the 1940s and served the French *Phares et Balises* (Lighthouses and Beacons) service — the agency responsible for maintaining maritime navigation aids. It operated for decades in the Mediterranean, serving ports around Marseille and Corsica, before being transferred to Guadeloupe in 1990 for service in the French Antilles and Guiana. Retired from service in 1994, it was held in reserve before being fully decontaminated and intentionally sunk on 9 July 2003.

The vessel measures 53 metres in length by 9 metres in width and displaces 660 tonnes, making it heavier and longer than the Franjack. It lies flat on its keel on a sandy bottom at 30–33 metres. The depth is reported as 30–31 metres by some operators and 33 metres by others; this discrepancy likely reflects different measurement points along the hull.

The wreck preserves substantial superstructure including the crane operator's cabin, hoists, forward supports, and the distinctive beacon-ship bow configuration, along with the stern with twin screws.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South of the Cousteau Reserve, Bouillante, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (PE40 / PADI Advanced Open Water with Deep Diver speciality)
- **Maximum Depth**: 30–33 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: November to May

## Dive Profile

Descend the mooring line to the wreck. The interior is accessible and includes crew cabins, the galley, and dining areas reachable by experienced divers. The crane cabin and beacon-ship bow are distinctive external features. Begin from the stern with its twin screws, work along the hull, and explore the superstructure before ascending. The shallowest accessible point is significantly below 20 metres — plan bottom time carefully.

## Marine Life

Barracuda, large jacks (trevally), groupers, lobsters, and moray eels are documented residents. The hull and superstructure support sponge and soft coral growth. Sea turtles visit the structure.

*Note: Species listed above are drawn from Les Heures Saines operator reports for this wreck. No site-specific marine life survey has been independently published.*

## Entry and Exit

Mooring line descent from licensed operator boats. Giant stride entry. Ascend on the mooring line; deploy SMB for surface pickup. Advanced certification must be verified before diving.

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification and experience are required. Interior penetration requires proper training, equipment, and a guide who knows current hull conditions. The depth limits bottom time — a conservative dive plan is essential. Never characterise interior exploration as routine; structural condition can change over time.
