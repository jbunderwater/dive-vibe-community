## L'Augustin Fresnel II

L'Augustin Fresnel II is a 53-metre Canadian buoy tender deliberately sunk in 2003 within the Cousteau Reserve near Malendure as a clean artificial reef. It is the most recently added wreck in the reserve and the second deepest, requiring PE40 / Advanced Open Water with Deep certification.

## Overview

The Augustin Fresnel II was built in Canada in the late 1940s and served as a buoy tender for the French lighthouse authority (Phares et Balises), initially operating in the Mediterranean (Marseille and Corsica). It was transferred to Pointe-à-Pitre in 1990 to service the maritime markers of the French Antilles and Guiana. Retired in 1994 and subsequently decontaminated, the vessel was deliberately sunk on 9 July 2003 at a depth of approximately 30–31 metres to create an artificial reef in the Cousteau Reserve.

The vessel measures 53 metres long by 9 metres wide and displaced 660 tonnes. It rests upright on a sandy bottom. During Cyclone Omar (2008), the wreck slipped to its current position; the propeller now sits at approximately 33 metres. (Note: one source — the `augustin-fresnel-wreck.md` curated entry — attributes the shift to Cyclone Dean (2007); multiple sources including Divessi and Les Heures Saines attribute it to Cyclone Omar (2008). The 2008 date is the more consistent source.) The forward deck, superstructures, crane operator cabin, and stern with its twin screws are all identifiable features. Interior spaces including the gallery, cabins, and engine room are accessible to divers with the appropriate training. The hull is colonised by barracudas, jacks, spiny lobsters, and moray eels in spaces coated with corals and sponges.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cousteau Reserve, south of Malendure, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters

## Safety

Deep dive requiring strict gas planning. Penetration of interior spaces requires proper training, a primary torch, and a buddy. The wreck is well-maintained and rests upright; however, always inspect structural integrity before entering any compartment. Narcosis is a consideration at 30+ metres. Nitrox strongly recommended.
