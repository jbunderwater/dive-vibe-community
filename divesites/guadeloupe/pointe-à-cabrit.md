## Pointe à Cabrit

Pointe à Cabrit is a multi-level dive site at the northern tip of Îlet à Cabrit, marking one of the entrances to the Baie des Saintes in the Les Saintes archipelago, southern Guadeloupe. The site is notable for always being sheltered, making it diveable even when conditions elsewhere in the archipelago are rough.

## Overview

The site encompasses two distinct features: La Pointe (the headland point) and La Patate Cabrit (a large coral pinnacle located between La Pointe and the anchorage). Together they offer a versatile dive with a shallow plateau at 2–5 metres for beginners and a drop-off descending to 20 metres (and beyond) for more experienced divers. The exposed point and the massive coral pinnacle both provide rich biodiversity.

The site's consistent shelter means it is offered by Les Saintes operators as a reliable back-up dive and is also frequently chosen for beginners and children's discovery dives. A boat trip of approximately 15 minutes from Terre-de-Haut is required.

## Dive Profile

- **Depth range**: 2–5 m (shallow plateau) to 20 m (drop-off); maximum records to 30–40 m
- **Entry**: Boat dive
- **Current**: Generally calm; well-sheltered location
- **Visibility**: Typically excellent — 15+ metres
- **Difficulty**: Open Water (PADI Level 1) sufficient for the plateau; more experienced divers can explore the deeper drop-off

## Marine Life

La Patate Cabrit pinnacle is described by Tribloo as "a sanctuary to a multitude of scorpionfish, rock lobsters, damselfish, Chinese trumpetfish, moray eels." Additional species documented at the site include: garden eels (heterocongers) in the sandy areas, stingrays, hawksbill turtles, groupers, lionfish, filefish, octopuses, and nudibranchs. The diverse topography — sandy bottom, coral pinnacle, and drop-off wall — supports an unusually wide range of habitats within a single dive.

## Entry and Exit

Boat entry approximately 15 minutes from Terre-de-Haut (Les Saintes main island).

## Photography

The shallow plateau depth and good light make macro photography on the pinnacle excellent. Garden eels in the sandy flats, scorpionfish, and nudibranch subjects are highlights. Wide-angle shots along the drop-off wall work well in the afternoon light.
