## Le Gustavia

Le Gustavia is a 48.32-metre cargo vessel launched in 1953 as the *Géocéanique*, deliberately sunk in 1991 as the first artificial reef within the Cousteau Reserve — now Guadeloupe's deepest and most atmospheric wreck dive.

## Overview

The *Géocéanique* was a French cargo vessel of 416 tons (48.32 m × 7.6 m), built in 1953. Renamed *Gustavia* in 1982, the ship spent its later years in cabotage trade between Caribbean islands. Hurricane Hugo struck on September 17, 1989, damaging the vessel in the port of Pointe-à-Pitre and rendering it unfit for navigation.

After a thorough cleanup and decontamination, the Gustavia was deliberately sunk in 1991 approximately one mile from the Malendure coastline at Bouillante — the first vessel sunk in the area as an artificial reef. The wreck rests upright on a sandy bottom at 40 metres.

**Vessel specifications** (per ScubaGo/Divingaway records, cross-referenced with Les Heures Saines): 48.32 m length, 7.6 m width, 416 tons. Built 1953, launched as *Géocéanique*, renamed *Gustavia* 1982.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cousteau Reserve area, Malendure, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate; can be stronger at depth
- **Best Time**: Year-round; best visibility November to May

## Marine Life

The Gustavia's 30+ years on the seabed have produced dense colonisation. The hull is blanketed in sponges and soft corals. Barracudas — which give the site a particular character — are habitually abundant around the wreck and commonly swirl around divers during ascent. French angelfish, groupers, eels, and jacks are confirmed regular inhabitants. The sandy bottom around the keel hosts garden eels. Sponge and soft coral encrustation on the superstructure makes this a colourful deep wreck.

## Dive Profile

The wreck's superstructure begins at around 35 metres; the keel at 40 metres. Descend on the mooring line to the upper deck and work along the hull. Maximum bottom time at 40 metres on air is approximately 10–15 minutes — Nitrox 32% strongly recommended to extend bottom time and reduce narcosis risk. Ascend to 35 metres to explore upper structures, then up the mooring line for the mandatory safety stop at 5 metres. Plan a 3-minute minimum stop. Total dive time from descent to surface is typically 30–35 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Only diveable with licensed Bouillante operators. Permanent mooring buoy. Giant stride or back-roll entry. Advanced (CMAS 2 / AOW + Deep Dive specialty) certification required.

## Safety

The depth of 40 metres places this at the limit of recreational diving. Narcosis at depth is possible for some divers — discuss with your buddy and agree on abort criteria. Plan your gas supply conservatively and ascend with a minimum 50 bar reserve. Nitrox strongly recommended. Always dive with an experienced buddy. Safety stop of minimum 3 minutes at 5 metres is mandatory.
