## Le Gustavia

Le Gustavia is a 48-metre cargo vessel deliberately sunk in 1991 as the first artificial reef wreck in Guadeloupe's Cousteau Reserve, now resting upright at 40 metres.

## Overview

The Gustavia was built in 1950 at PAHL SCHIFFSWERFT & MASCHINENBAU in Germany under the original name Hamburg. The vessel was subsequently renamed Drochtersen (1963) and Geocéanique (1975) before being purchased by the Guadeloupe shipping company Lacascade & Michaud and renamed Gustavia in 1982. Dimensions: 48.32 metres long, 7.6 metres wide, 416 gross tonnes.

On 17 September 1989, Hurricane Hugo struck Guadeloupe and severely damaged the Gustavia while it lay moored in Pointe-à-Pitre harbour; the vessel was declared unfit for navigation. After full decontamination and pollution clean-up, it was deliberately sunk as an artificial reef in 1991 approximately one nautical mile from the Malendure rock off Bouillante — the first wreck intentionally placed within what would become the Cousteau Reserve.

The wreck lies upright on a sandy bottom at 40 metres. The hull and superstructure are now extensively colonised by sponges and soft corals. The deep profile requires PE40/Advanced Open Water with Deep Dive specialty certification. Visibility within the reserve is typically good (15–25+ metres).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cousteau Reserve, approximately 1 mile off Malendure Rock, Bouillante, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (PE40 / PADI AOW + Deep Dive specialty required)
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate within the reserve

## Marine Life

Confirmed species on and around the wreck include: barracuda (schooling in the water column), French angelfish, groupers, moray eels, large sea turtles on the superstructure and hull, and stingrays on the sandy bottom around the keel. The hull is covered with sponges and multicoloured marine growth after more than 30 years of colonisation.

## Dive Profile

Descend the mooring line to the wreck. The upper deck and superstructure sit at approximately 36–38 metres; the keel on the sandy bottom is at 40 metres. Bottom time at this depth is limited — plan no-decompression limits carefully. Nitrox 32% is commonly used by operators to extend usable bottom time. The accessible deck sections, wheelhouse, and hold openings are the primary areas of interest. Penetration of enclosed spaces requires appropriate wreck diving training and a primary light source.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive only; operated exclusively by licensed Bouillante dive operators within the Cousteau Reserve area. Giant stride entry; mooring buoy system on the wreck. Safety stop at 5 metres is mandatory before surfacing.

## Safety

Deep dive at 40 metres — nitrogen narcosis can be a factor. Discuss with your buddy and establish agreed abort criteria. The wreck has been in situ for over 30 years and some sections may be structurally weakened; inspect before entering any enclosed space. Never characterise interior penetration as safe — it requires proper training and equipment. Always carry a primary and backup torch for any interior section.
