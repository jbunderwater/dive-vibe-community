## Le Gustavia

Le Gustavia is a 48-metre cargo vessel deliberately sunk in 1991 to form an artificial reef within the Cousteau Reserve near Malendure, Guadeloupe. It is the deepest of the three wrecks in the reserve and one of the most celebrated dive sites in the French Caribbean.

## Overview

The vessel was built in 1950 in Germany by PAHL SCHIFFSWERFT & MASCHINENBAU under the name *Hamburg*. It was lengthened in 1955, renamed *Drochtersen* in 1963 under German ownership, then *Geocéanique* in 1975 under French ownership, and finally renamed *Gustavia* in 1982 when the Guadeloupean shipping company Lacascade & Michaud acquired it. The ship was used for sand transport and water delivery between Caribbean islands.

Hurricane Hugo severely damaged the vessel in September 1989 while it was docked at Pointe-à-Pitre. After a full decontamination and cleaning process, it was deliberately sunk in 1991 approximately one mile from the Malendure beach, upright on a sandy bottom at 40 metres depth. It was the first vessel intentionally scuttled within the Cousteau Reserve as an artificial reef.

The wreck measures 48.32 metres in length by 7.6 metres in width and displaces 416 tonnes. It lies upright on a sandy bottom and has been colonised over more than three decades by sponges, soft corals, and the abundant fish life of the reserve.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cousteau Reserve, approximately 1.6 km from Malendure Beach, Bouillante, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Light to moderate within the reserve
- **Best Time**: November to May

## Dive Profile

Descend the mooring line to the sandy bottom at 40 metres where the keel rests. The classic dive profile ascends spirally from the deepest point to the highest structure, then returns to shallower depth for the ascent and safety stop. The top of the highest structure is accessible at approximately 36 metres. Total bottom time at this depth is limited — plan a conservative profile and ascend before nitrogen limits require. The vessel sits upright, so both port and starboard faces can be examined.

## Marine Life

The wreck structure hosts barracuda that school around the superstructure, along with groupers, French angelfish, and moray eels that have taken up residence in the hull. Garden eels occupy the sandy bottom around the keel. The sponge communities on the superstructure are well-developed after more than 30 years of colonisation.

*Note: No site-specific marine life surveys have been published for Le Gustavia. Species listed above are confirmed by Les Heures Saines and Les Baillantes Tortues operator reports.*

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive with mooring line descent. All diving at the reserve wrecks must be conducted through licensed operators based at Malendure. Giant stride entry; ascend on the mooring line. Deploy SMB for surface pickup. Boat traffic in the reserve channel is regular.

## Safety Considerations

This is a deep dive requiring Advanced/PE40 certification. Bottom time at 40 metres is limited — discuss the dive plan and turnaround depth with your guide before entry. The reserve is a strict no-fishing, no-collecting zone. Never attempt interior penetration of the Gustavia without appropriate certification, equipment, and a guide familiar with the wreck's interior condition.
