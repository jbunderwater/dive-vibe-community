## Le Gustavia

Le Gustavia is a deliberately sunk cargo vessel resting upright at 40 metres within the Jacques Cousteau Underwater Reserve near Malendure, Guadeloupe. It is the deepest of the three artificial reef wrecks in the reserve and requires PE40 / Advanced Open Water with Deep certification.

## Overview

The Gustavia was built in 1950 in Germany under the name "Hamburg," later lengthened in 1955 and renamed "Drochtersen," then "Geocéanique" before receiving the name Gustavia in 1982. The vessel measured 48.32 metres long by 7.6 metres wide and displaced 416 tonnes. It performed cabotage (inter-island cargo transport) across the Caribbean until Hurricane Hugo (September 17, 1989) caused severe damage while the ship was docked in Pointe-à-Pitre. After the vessel was declared unfit for navigation and fully decontaminated, it was deliberately sunk approximately 1.6 km from Malendure in 1991 to create an artificial reef. The wreck sits upright on a sandy bottom at 40 metres and is the first wreck deliberately placed in the Cousteau Reserve.

The wreck is substantially colonised with sponges and corals. French angelfish, barracudas, groupers, and moray eels patrol the hull. The accessible sections of the exterior can be circled from keel to the highest superstructure point during a single advanced dive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cousteau Reserve, Malendure, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (PE40 / AOW + Deep Dive required)
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate within the reserve

## Safety

This is a deep dive requiring careful gas planning. Bottom time on air at 40 metres is very limited — Nitrox is strongly recommended. Narcosis may be a factor. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety stop at 5 metres for a minimum of 3 minutes. Licensed Bouillante operators manage access under Cousteau Reserve rules.
