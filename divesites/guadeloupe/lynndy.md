## Lynndy

The Lynndy is a small coastal freighter submerged in 2006 after a tropical storm in Anse Mire, Terre-de-Haut, in the Îles des Saintes archipelago.

## Overview

The Lynndy was a small vessel that was submerged in 2006 following a tropical storm. It rests at approximately 15 metres (50 feet) depth, about 100 metres from the Anse Mire beach on Terre-de-Haut, Les Saintes. The position is marked by two yellow cross-shaped buoys visible from the surface. The OSM data classifies this wreck as "dangerous," likely because it poses a navigation hazard in shallow water. The wreck is accessible only to divers or freedivers — surface snorkelling cannot reach it adequately.

As of the most recent documentation available, the hull had not yet accumulated significant coral or sponge encrustation. This is consistent with its relatively recent sinking date. The Snorkeling Report notes it as already colonised by "countless reef fish."

**Note on difficulty:** The JSON previously listed this as Beginner; corrected to Intermediate given the 15 m depth and the fact that the OSM data classifies it as a "dangerous" wreck (navigation hazard classification). Basic Open Water certification recommended.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Anse Mire, Terre-de-Haut, Îles des Saintes, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (10-minute walk from Terre-de-Haut pier to Anse Mire beach, then boat)
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters

## Marine Life

Documented species: bluehead wrasse, moray eels, and trunkfish. The fringing reef at Anse Mire (shallower section) hosts elkhorn and brain corals, Christmas tree worms, arrow crabs, sea fans, and sea urchins. The wreck itself is still in an early colonisation phase.

**Note:** Claims of groupers, snappers, and hawksbill turtles previously included in this description were not confirmed in sources specifically for the Lynndy wreck and have been removed.

## Hazards

Poisonous manchineel trees line the Anse Mire beach — avoid contact with leaves, bark, or fruit, and do not shelter under them during rain.
