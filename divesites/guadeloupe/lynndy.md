## Lynndy

The Lynndy is a small passenger boat that sank in 2006 after a tropical storm in Anse Mire, Les Saintes — now a marked, shallow wreck dive at 15 metres, approximately 100 metres from the beach.

## Overview

The Lynndy went down in 2006 following a tropical storm and came to rest at 15 metres depth in Anse Mire bay, a small cove in the Baie des Saintes near Terre-de-Haut village. The wreck's position is marked by two yellow cross buoys and is visible from the surface on calm days. The vessel was a small passenger boat; no further historical record of the vessel's name origin has been found in published sources.

The OSM seamark category "dangerous" refers to the navigational hazard classification, not the dive difficulty — the site at 15 m is accessible to beginner divers under operator guidance.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Anse Mire, Baie des Saintes, Terre-de-Haut, Les Saintes, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Distance from shore**: approximately 100 metres

## Marine Life

Confirmed in published sources (Snorkeling Report, lapetitevilladessaintes.fr): "countless reef fish are already calling the Lynndy home." The wreck had minimal coral and sponge colonisation as of recent reports, with rapid colonisation in progress. No specific species have been confirmed by name at this wreck in published sources found; generic regional species lists are not attributed here.

Regional marine life applies; no site-specific species reports found beyond general reef fish.

## Notes

The wreck has become a local landmark visible from the surface and a photo opportunity for passing vessels. Freedivers can reach it easily; scuba divers access it via Les Saintes operators.
