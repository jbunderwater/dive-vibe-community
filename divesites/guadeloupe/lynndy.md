## Lynndy

The Lynndy is a small coastal freighter that sank in 2006 following tropical storm damage in Anse Mire, Les Saintes — now a shallow, accessible wreck lying 100 metres from the beach, visible from the surface and snorkel-accessible.

## Overview

The Lynndy was a small vessel that transported goods and supplies between the Caribbean islands. It ran aground and was damaged during a tropical storm in 2006 and was subsequently submerged in its present position in Anse Mire Bay, Terre-de-Haut. The wreck was not deliberately sunk as an artificial reef — it came to rest following storm damage.

The hull rests at approximately 15 metres depth (50 feet), roughly 100 metres from the Anse Mire shoreline. The wreck is marked with two yellow cross buoys. The OSM seamark record categorises it as "dangerous," likely reflecting the rusting and degraded hull structure.

The Lynndy has been on the seabed since 2006 and the hull is now gutted and heavily rusted — the salt and time have steadily consumed the structure. Reef fish are colonising the wreck, but heavy coral encrustation has not yet developed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Anse Mire Bay, Terre-de-Haut, Les Saintes, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable; dependent on sea state
- **Current**: Minimal within the bay
- **Best Time**: Year-round; morning for calmest conditions

## Marine Life

Reef fish are colonising the wreck structure. Bluehead wrasse, moray eels, and trunkfish are documented at the site. The surrounding shallow reef at Anse Mire supports additional reef species. The wreck provides artificial habitat for juvenile reef fish taking advantage of the shelter provided by the rusting hull structure.

## Dive Profile

The wreck is accessible from the surface and from scuba. Snorkelers can view the silhouette from the surface; only freedivers or scuba divers reach the full wreck at 15 metres. The dive is uncomplicated — descend to the wreck, explore the exterior (interior penetration not advised given the degraded hull), and ascend. The shallow depth allows extended bottom time.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Terre-de-Haut operators or approach from Anse Mire beach with appropriate caution regarding boat traffic in the bay. The two yellow buoys mark the wreck location. Position well clear of the buoys before descending.

## Notes

The Lynndy is a useful secondary dive for Les Saintes visitors interested in seeing a natural shallow wreck, but the more technically significant diving in Les Saintes is at sites like Pointe à Cabrit, La Baleine, and Pointe du Gouvernail. The surrounding Anse Mire reef is diveable alongside the wreck.
