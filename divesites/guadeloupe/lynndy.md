## Lynndy

The Lynndy is a small vessel deliberately immersed in 2006 in Anse Mire, Les Saintes, after sustaining damage during a tropical storm. It rests at 15 metres approximately 100 metres from the beach, marked by two yellow cross-shaped buoys, and is accessible to both scuba divers and freedivers.

## Overview

The Lynndy was sunk intentionally in 2006 following storm damage. The wreck sits in the sheltered waters of Anse Mire in the Baie des Saintes — described as one of the most beautiful bays in the Caribbean. The vessel rests at 15 metres (approximately 50 feet) on a sandy bottom and is visible from the surface in calm, clear conditions.

The wreck was relatively lightly colonised by coral and sponges at the time the available sources were published, with reef fish populations already establishing the site as a habitat. The exact vessel type and history have not been documented in available dive literature. The OSM seamark database classifies the wreck as "dangerous" — divers should exercise appropriate caution.

## Dive Profile

Locate the wreck using the two yellow marker buoys positioned above it. Descend to the wreck at 15 metres. Circumnavigate the exterior — the wreck is relatively compact. Snorkelers and freedivers can observe the structure from above in clear conditions. Return to the boat for ascent. The site is outside the designated swimming area.

## Marine Life

Reef fish had begun colonising the structure. No site-specific marine life survey has been published. The broader Baie des Saintes environment supports typical Caribbean reef species.

*No site-specific marine life documentation exists for the Lynndy wreck. The species described in previous versions of this page were regional generalisations, not site-specific reports.*

## Entry and Exit

Accessible by boat from Terre-de-Haut operators. Kayak or boat required from shore — the wreck is outside the designated swimming area. Watch for boat traffic on ascent; the wreck lies outside the protected swimming zone.

## Safety Considerations

The OSM seamark database classifies this wreck as "dangerous" — this is a navigational hazard classification rather than a dive difficulty rating, but divers should be alert to structural instability. Watch for surface boat traffic at this location. Always surface with a deployed SMB.
