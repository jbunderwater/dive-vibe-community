## Lynndy

The Lynndy is a sunken boat resting at approximately 15 metres off Anse Mire beach on Terre-de-Haut, the main island of Les Saintes archipelago, southern Guadeloupe.

## Overview

The Lynndy is a small coastal vessel that sank during a tropical storm in 2006 in the protected waters of Baie des Saintes. The wreck rests about 100 metres from the beach at Anse Mire, on a sandy bottom at approximately 15 metres depth. Its position is marked at the surface by two yellow cross-shaped buoys. The vessel is a gutted hull eaten away by salt over nearly two decades; the structure is still recognisable from above in calm conditions.

The wreck is not yet heavily colonised — coral and sponge coverage is limited compared to the older reserve wrecks at Malendure — but abundant reef fish have adopted the hull as shelter. No site-specific species list from a published dive source has been located; the broader Les Saintes reef fish community documented by local operators includes gorettes, angelfish, and snappers.

The wreck's proximity to the beach and shallow depth make it snorkel-accessible for freedivers. It is reached by boat from Terre-de-Haut village (approximately 10 minutes on foot from the pier).

Note: The vessel's original name, dimensions, and owner are not identified in available published sources. The OSM database classifies this as a "dangerous" category wreck due to structural state; divers should treat the hull as structurally uncertain.
