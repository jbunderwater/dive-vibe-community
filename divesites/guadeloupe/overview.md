## Guadeloupe

France's premier Caribbean diving destination, anchored by the Jacques Cousteau Reserve at Pigeon Island with volcanic reef formations and exceptional marine biodiversity.

## Description

Guadeloupe offers 31 documented dive sites ranging from 6 to 40 meters depth, concentrated around the volcanic west coast of Basse-Terre. The centerpiece is the Cousteau Marine Park surrounding Pigeon Island, where 13 buoyed dive sites protect thriving coral ecosystems despite heavy diving traffic. Beyond the reserve, divers find dramatic wall dives, eight documented wrecks, and the spectacular Sec Pate pinnacle — widely considered one of Guadeloupe's most impressive sites.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: 29 boat-accessible sites including the Cousteau Reserve sites at Pigeon Island, accessible in minutes from Malendure beach
- **Shore Diving**: 2 shore-accessible sites along the Basse-Terre coast
- **Wreck Diving**: 8 wreck sites including the Augustin Fresnel and other vessels along the leeward coast
- **Wall Diving**: 6 wall dive sites along Basse-Terre's steep volcanic drop-offs
- **Reef Diving**: 16 reef sites with healthy hard and soft coral formations in the protected marine park
- **Pinnacle Diving**: Sec Pate, a dramatic offshore pinnacle rising from depth

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Guadeloupe Pole Caraibes Airport (PTP) in Pointe-a-Pitre receives direct flights from Paris, Montreal, Miami, and inter-Caribbean connections. Ferries connect to Les Saintes and Marie-Galante.
- **Dive Operators**: Over 12 operators including Les Heures Saines, Tropical Sub, Les Baillantes Tortues, Archipel Plongee, and CIP Guadeloupe, most based along Malendure beach near Pigeon Island
- **Accommodation**: Range from beachfront hotels in Deshaies and Bouillante to guesthouses and vacation rentals along the Basse-Terre coast
- **Transportation**: Rental cars recommended for reaching dive sites across the island; taxis available from the airport
- **Facilities**: Full-service dive centers with equipment rental, nitrox available at several operators, cetacean encounter excursions offered seasonally

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 26-29°C year-round, visibility typically 15-30 meters, calm conditions on the leeward Basse-Terre coast
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Trumpetfish, blue tang, barracudas, sea turtles (hawksbill and green), angelfish, seahorses in the Coral Garden, moray eels, and schools of tropical reef fish. The Cousteau Reserve supports healthy coral despite heavy visitation.
- **Conservation**: The Cousteau Marine Park is part of Guadeloupe National Park. All dive sites within the reserve are buoyed to prevent anchor damage. The Grand Cul de Sac Marin is a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: December to May (dry season) for calmest seas and best visibility; diving is year-round with water temperatures rarely dropping below 26°C
- **Currency**: Euro (EUR)
- **Language**: French (primary), Creole widely spoken
- **Safety**: Recompression chamber located at CHU hospital in Pointe-a-Pitre. Conditions on the leeward coast are generally calm, but currents can be present at deeper wall sites and Sec Pate.
