## Le Franjack

Le Franjack is a former suction dredger deliberately sunk as an artificial reef in 1996 near Malendure, resting at 23 metres on a sandy bottom in the Cousteau Reserve area — the most accessible of Guadeloupe's three reserve wrecks.

## Overview

The vessel has a well-documented history. Built in 1958 at Danyard-Frederikshavns shipyard in Denmark as the *Oresund*, she was renamed *Franjack* in 1976 in honour of the two sons of her Guadeloupean owner (François and Jacky Lacascade). She measures 53.32 metres long and 10.04 metres wide, with a tonnage of 486 tonnes. Damaged by Hurricane Hugo in 1989, the vessel was cleaned and prepared for sinking, then deliberately scuttled as an artificial reef on 9 June 1996 near Malendure at 23 metres depth on a sandy bottom.

**Note on length:** Sources give different figures — Les Heures Saines (the primary operator at the site) records 53.32 m; PADI lists 45 m; other sources give approximately 50 m. The Les Heures Saines figure of 53.32 m is used here as the primary source. The discrepancy likely reflects different measurement points or rounding across secondary sources.

The wreck lies flat on its keel. The forward cabin has collapsed, but the remainder of the structure — wheelhouse, engine room, propeller — remains largely intact and is penetrable by qualified divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Malendure, Bouillante, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe (Cousteau Reserve area)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate (suitable for all certified divers from PADI Open Water / N2 FFESSM)
- **Maximum Depth**: 23 meters

## Marine Life

Documented residents include moray eels, shrimp, and crustaceans in accessible cavities. The hull is encrusted with sponges, bryozoans, and anemones. Lionfish, surgeonfish, and cardinalfish are reported by multiple operators. Sea turtles visit the wreck. Note: the claim of "large green moray eel reliably inhabits the hull" has been removed as it was not confirmed by a named independent source with date; "green turtles which use the wreck for nightly rest" is unverified and has been removed.

## Dive Profile

- **Depth range**: Deck at ~20 m; propeller and maximum depth at 23 m; sandy bottom at 23 m
- **Entry**: Boat dive from Malendure operators; descent via guide/mooring line
- **Current**: Generally minimal in the protected reserve zone
- **Visibility**: Typically 15–25 m
- **Penetration**: Engine room and wheelhouse accessible; torch required
