## Le Franjack

Le Franjack is a 50-metre sand-transport vessel deliberately sunk in 1996 within the Cousteau Reserve near Malendure — one of Guadeloupe's most accessible wreck dives and a reliable sighting point for resident moray eels, turtles, and lobsters.

## Overview

The Franjack was built in 1958 in Denmark under the name *Oresund* and worked as a sand-transport vessel in the Caribbean. Hurricane Hugo struck in September 1989, rendering the ship unfit for service. After the hull was thoroughly cleaned, the Franjack was deliberately sunk on June 9, 1996, at approximately 23–24 metres depth on a sandy bottom within the Cousteau Reserve.

The wreck rests upright on its keel. Its engine room, kitchen, and holds are open and penetrable, making this a recommended first wreck dive for newly certified divers.

**Correction note**: Some sources have listed the Franjack as 45 metres — confirmed length is 50 metres per the Les Heures Saines operator page and the Cousteau Reserve documentation.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Cousteau Reserve, Malendure, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 23 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light within the protected reserve
- **Best Time**: Year-round from Bouillante operators

## Marine Life

The wreck has been colonising for nearly 30 years. A large green moray eel is a reliable resident, typically found around the machinery spaces. Lobsters shelter in the keel and interior crevices. Snappers and cardinal fish school inside the hull. Hawksbill turtles use the superstructure for rest — including nightly rest on the deck. Eels, shrimp, sponges, bryozoans, and sea anemones cover the hull surfaces. Barracuda and jacks patrol the water column above. The sponge colonisation of the superstructure — in varying colours — is among the most striking of any wreck in the French Caribbean.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wreck at 23 metres and explore the exterior before entering accessible interior spaces. The engine room, kitchen, and holds can be navigated safely. Begin ascent from the hull to the superstructure at around 15 metres, then to the mooring line for the safety stop. Typically dived as a two-tank combination with a Cousteau Reserve reef site such as Jardin de Corail or L'Aquarium.

## Entry and Exit

Operated by Bouillante dive centres licensed to dive within the Cousteau Reserve. Permanent mooring buoy protects the seabed. Giant stride or back-roll entry.

## Safety

Open architecture and a modest depth make this accessible to intermediate divers. Disturbing sediment inside the hull reduces visibility for divers behind you — fin carefully. Carry a torch for interior sections. Safety stop at 5 metres on the mooring line. Penetration beyond the main accessible spaces requires proper training — follow your guide's briefing.
