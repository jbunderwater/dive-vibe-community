## Le Franjack

Le Franjack is a 53-metre sand dredger deliberately sunk in 1996 within the Cousteau Reserve near Malendure, Guadeloupe. Accessible to intermediate divers and resting upright at 23 metres, it is the shallowest and most dived of the reserve's three wrecks.

## Overview

The vessel was built in 1958 at Danyard shipyard in Frederikshavn, Denmark, under the name *Oresund*. It was renamed *Franjack* in 1976. From 1983 it transported sand in Guadeloupe under the ownership of a Gosier-based company. Hurricane Hugo in September 1989 caused structural damage to the vessel.

After full decontamination, the Franjack was deliberately sunk on 9 June 1996 off Malendure in Bouillante as an artificial reef — the first vessel intentionally scuttled for diving at Bouillante, predating both the Gustavia and Augustin Fresnel II. The wreck measures 53.32 metres in length by 10.04 metres in width, with a bridge height of 10.04 metres, and displaces 486 tonnes. It now lies flat on its keel on a sandy bottom at approximately 23 metres.

After nearly three decades on the seabed, the superstructure is heavily colonised by sponges, soft corals, and encrusting organisms that provide a richly coloured diving backdrop.

## Dive Profile

Descend the mooring line to the wreck. The propeller rests at approximately 23 metres and is a classic first point of interest. The wheelhouse and engine compartments offer interior access for divers comfortable with penetration; the engine room, hold, and forward sections are accessible. Circumnavigate the hull before ascending for a full impression of the vessel's size. Safety stop at 5 metres.

## Marine Life

Sponge communities on the hull and superstructure are diverse and colourful. Groupers, snappers, and cardinal fish inhabit the structure. Sea turtles are regularly encountered in and around the wreck. Moray eels occupy crevices within the hull. Shrimp and lobsters are found at the base of the hull.

*Note: Marine life listed above is drawn from Les Heures Saines and Les Baillantes Tortues operator reports for this specific wreck. The claim in previous versions that "a large green moray eel reliably inhabits the hull" has been removed — no source confirms a specific individual resident.*

## Entry and Exit

Mooring line descent from dive boats operating out of Malendure. All diving at Cousteau Reserve wrecks requires a licensed operator. Giant stride entry; ascend on the mooring line. Deploy SMB for pickup in the reserve channel.

## Safety Considerations

Interior penetration of the wreck requires appropriate training and equipment. The hold and interior spaces are accessible but require good buoyancy and a light source. Always dive with a guide familiar with the wreck's current structural condition. Never remove artifacts — the site has protected status within the reserve.
