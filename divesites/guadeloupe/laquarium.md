## L'Aquarium

L'Aquarium is a wall dive site in Les Saintes with tunnels and coral plateaus, named for the extraordinary density and variety of fish that fill its passages — confirmed by Tribloo and La Dive Bouteille as one of the most visually impressive reef dives in the archipelago.

## Overview

The site features underwater tunnels passing through reef formations and opening onto wall faces, with plateaus between tunnel sections covered in corals and sponges. Depths range from approximately 8 metres to 18 metres. The Cousteau Reserve site named "Aquarium" near Malendure is a different, shallower site; this L'Aquarium is in Les Saintes.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Les Saintes, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Confirmed at this site by Tribloo: schools of gorettes (French grunts), cardinal fish, angelfish, and wrasses. The tunnels concentrate these species in impressive aggregations. Corals and sponges cover the plateau sections. Note: "Tarpon," "barracuda," and "groupers" attributed to this site in a previous version are drawn from the Cousteau Reserve Aquarium entry and are not confirmed by sources specific to this Les Saintes L'Aquarium site; they have been removed.

Regional marine life applies for additional species beyond those confirmed above.

## Safety

Tunnel passages require good buoyancy control and comfort with enclosed spaces. A torch is strongly recommended for the darker interior passages. Beginner-friendly depth but the tunnel environment justifies the Intermediate classification.
