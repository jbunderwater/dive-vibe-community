## La Caye

La Caye is a reef dive in the Malendure area, formed from a section of Malendure rock that collapsed into the sea. It is dived in the daytime and frequently used for night dives by Bouillante operators including Les Heures Saines.

## Overview

Les Heures Saines describes La Caye as "Malendure's nursery," with depth ranging from 4 to 18 metres. The site was formed when a portion of the Malendure rock collapsed into the ocean, creating a varied underwater landscape accessible to all certified divers. It is particularly popular for night dives, when nocturnal species emerge. Visibility runs 15–25 metres in settled conditions. Water temperature averages 27°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Malendure area, Basse-Terre, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate (all certified divers)
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Generally calm; suitable for all levels
- **Best Time**: Year-round; night dives particularly popular

## Marine Life

Les Heures Saines specifically documents the following species at La Caye night dives: astrophyton (basket stars), corallimorphaires (corallimorphs), active coral polyps, and sea turtles. Night dives reveal nocturnal species not visible during day dives. Daytime species typical of shallow Cousteau Reserve-area reefs are present but no separate site-specific day dive species list has been published for La Caye specifically.

## Dive Profile

- **Depth range**: 4–18 m
- **Entry**: Boat dive from Malendure
- **Night dives**: 60-minute dives offered by Les Heures Saines with dive lamp
- **Current**: Generally calm
- **Best for**: Beginners and intermediate divers; ideal for night dive introduction

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Malendure operators. Often offered as a dedicated night dive. Giant stride or back-roll entry, SMB required on ascent.
