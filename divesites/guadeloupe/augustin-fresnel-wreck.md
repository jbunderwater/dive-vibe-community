## Overview

The Augustin Fresnel II is a 53-metre Canadian lighthouse and beacon tender deliberately sunk on 9 July 2003 as an artificial reef in the Cousteau Reserve area near Bouillante, Basse-Terre. It was shifted to its current depth of 33 metres during Cyclone Omar in October 2008 (not Cyclone Dean 2007 as stated in earlier versions of this record). The vessel was built in Canada in the late 1940s, served the French lighthouse service in the Mediterranean, and was decommissioned in 1994 before being prepared as a dive site.
