## Pointe du Gouvernail

Pointe du Gouvernail ("Rudder Point") is a wall and reef dive located off the west coast of Terre-de-Bas in Les Saintes, descending from 4 metres to 22 metres, and known for active mid-water predator action throughout the dive.

## Overview

The site begins in only 4 metres of water and drops to 22 metres. La Dive Bouteille (Les Saintes-based operator) describes it as one of the most beautiful dive sites in Les Saintes and "one of the most appreciated dives," accessible to divers of all levels though current can be strong. The promontory concentrates predatory fish in the mid-water column.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West of Terre-de-Bas, Les Saintes, Guadeloupe
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at this site by La Dive Bouteille and yacht-paradise.gp: barracuda, Spanish mackerel (thazard), large-eyed jacks (carangue gros-yeux), small jacks (gorettes), horse-eye trevally (carangue franche), large-eyed trevally, and feathered jack (carangue à plumes). Abundant predator action reported from 4 metres depth throughout the dive.

Note: "Caribbean reef sharks," "eagle rays," and "spotted eagle rays" listed in the previous version are not confirmed by site-specific sources for Pointe du Gouvernail and have been removed. Sea fans and sponge cover are present on the wall face (regional character, not site-specific confirmation).

## Dive Profile

Descend from 4 m and navigate along the promontory wall to 22 m maximum. The point concentrates current — manage this actively. Predatory fish are present throughout the water column. Ascend the wall and deploy SMB for pickup. Ferry and boat traffic in the Les Saintes channel requires care on the surface.

## Safety

Current at the promontory tip can be strong — this is typical rather than exceptional. Do not attempt to swim against a strong current. Deploy SMB early. Always dive with a buddy.
