## Ballstad Reef

Ballstad Reef is a cold-water reef and canyon dive off one of Lofoten's largest fishing villages.

## Overview

Ballstad is the base for Lofoten Diving, the primary dive operator in the archipelago, and the reef and canyon formations southwest of the village are among the most dived sites in Lofoten. The area features deep canyons with vertical rock faces covered in dead man's fingers and thousands of Dahlia anemones. Two large vertical rock faces create a passage down to the canyon floor at around 20 metres, where large kelp forests grow in the clear Arctic water. Schools of pollock hover in slight currents above the canyon while lumpfish, well-camouflaged sculpins, and large cod are found along the walls and floor. Nudibranchs — including coral nudibranchs and the purple Facelina auriculata — are common on rock surfaces and kelp holdfasts.

Water temperature ranges from approximately 4°C in winter to 12–14°C in summer. A drysuit is strongly recommended year-round; visibility is typically 15–20 metres and is best in the October–April period.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ballstad, Vestvågøy, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
