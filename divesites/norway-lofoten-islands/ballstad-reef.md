## Ballstad Reef

Ballstad is the primary dive hub of the Lofoten Islands, home to Lofoten Diving (a PADI-certified dive centre). The dive sites southwest of the village feature a mix of deep canyons, kelp-forested walls, and macro-rich rocky substrate — though the specific site name "Ballstad Reef" does not appear in operator or press sources, which use names like "The Canyon" or "Grand Canyon" for the main dives.

## Overview

Ballstad sits in the middle of the Lofoten archipelago, facing Vestfjorden to the north. The sea immediately around Ballstad features small rocky islands and submerged ridges that create a varied underwater landscape. The primary documented dive type from Ballstad is canyon/wall diving, with the site known locally as "Grand Canyon" (or simply "The Canyon") being a frequently cited spot in dive travel writing: a network of deep canyons with kelp forest canopy and dense anemone coverage on the rock faces.

Dive sites reachable from Ballstad by RIB also include the wreck of the MV Gudrun Gisladottir — a 71-metre Icelandic trawler that struck a rock in the Napp strait between Flakstadøya and Vestvågøya on 18 June 2002 and now rests at 27–40 metres depth just west of Ballstad.

The "Ballstad Reef" entry in this database may refer to shallower reef or kelp substrate dives in the immediate harbour area. No source specifically uses this name for a named dive site.

Marine life confirmed for the Ballstad dive area (X-Ray Mag, Scuba Diver Life, ScubaBoard trip report): Atlantic cod, wolffish, pollock, lumpfish, sculpin, halibut, Dahlia anemones, dead man's fingers, nudibranchs, blade shrimp, hermit crabs, sea urchins, kelp.
