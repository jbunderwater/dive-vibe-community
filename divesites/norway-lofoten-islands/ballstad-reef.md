## Ballstad Reef

Ballstad Reef is a reef and canyon dive site off one of Lofoten's largest fishing villages, characterised by anemone-carpeted walls and deep canyons.

## Overview

The dive area southwest of Ballstad is among the most-described sites in Lofoten diving literature. X-Ray Mag's dedicated Lofoten feature describes vertical rock walls covered in dead man's fingers (Alcyonium digitatum) and Dahlia anemones from close to the surface down to approximately 20 metres, with deep canyons cutting through the terrain. Lofoten Diving, the primary local operator based in Ballstad, also describes this area as featuring kelp forest, sandy patches, and small islets — a site they use for both macro diving and wall diving.

The reef site is accessible by short boat ride from Ballstad harbour. Maximum depth is approximately 18 metres. Cold-water conditions (4–12°C year-round) require drysuit equipment. Drysuit qualification and Intermediate-level cold-water experience are required.

Marine life noted in regional sources includes the wider Lofoten cold-water community: nudibranchs on kelp and rock surfaces, hermit crabs in crevices, and typical demersal fish such as cod and pollock. Wolffish and halibut are listed as characteristic of Lofoten canyon/wall habitats in regional dive literature, though not confirmed at this specific named site by a primary source.
