## Å Shore Dive

Å Shore Dive is a shallow cold-water shore dive at the southernmost village in the Lofoten Islands, at the end of European Route E10.

## Overview

Å (pronounced "Aw") is the final village on the E10 highway and one of Norway's best-preserved 18th and 19th century fishing communities. Shore diving from Å places divers at the outermost edge of the Lofoten archipelago where Atlantic Ocean waters mix with the coastal shallows around the small islands and skerries.

The dive reaches a maximum depth of approximately 10 metres. The bottom is rocky reef and kelp beds typical of exposed Lofoten coastline, with sand patches in sheltered areas. Laminaria kelp dominates the shallower zones, and the rocky substrate supports sea anemones, dead man's fingers, sea urchins, and crabs. Pollock and saithe school in the open water.

This site is rated Intermediate due to Lofoten's cold-water conditions. Despite the shallow depth, water temperature is 4–10°C depending on season, requiring drysuit competency. Shore entry on a rocky Norwegian coastline also demands cold-water experience and appropriate footwear. The shallow depth limits the diving experience compared to Lofoten's deeper wall and wreck sites, but the wild Atlantic setting and picturesque village backdrop make it a worthwhile addition for divers based at the southern end of the islands.
