## Å Shore Dive

Å Shore Dive is a shallow shore reef dive site at the southern tip of the Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

Å (pronounced "Aw") is the southernmost village in Lofoten and one of Norway's most-visited fishing communities, known for its preserved 18th and 19th century rorbuer (fishing cabins). The shore dive here is at the very end of European Route E10, one of the most scenic roads in Norway. Diving from Å places you at the outermost edge of the archipelago, where Atlantic waters mix with the coastal shallows around small islands and skerries.

The maximum depth of 10 metres makes this one of Lofoten's shallower sites, but the cold water (4–10°C year-round) places it firmly in Intermediate territory — drysuit experience and cold-water skills are required regardless of depth. The bottom consists of rocky reef, kelp beds, and sand patches in a sheltered harbour or adjacent coastal setting. Laminaria kelp forest dominates the shallow zone. No site-specific marine life source was found for Å Shore Dive; species typical of this shallow rocky kelp habitat in Lofoten (small pollock, saithe, crabs, sea urchins, anemones, nudibranchs on kelp holdfasts) are plausible but have not been confirmed at this specific location by a primary source.

The primary limitation is depth — at 10 metres, bottom time is generous but the site does not offer the dramatic wall or deeper wreck experiences found at other Lofoten sites. Its value lies in the shallow, generous bottom time, the picturesque setting, and the genuinely wild Atlantic character of the location. Water temperature is 4–10°C depending on season; drysuit is strongly recommended year-round.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Shore entry
- **Depth range**: 0–10 m
- **Bottom composition**: Kelp reef, rocky substrate, sand
- **Conditions**: Generally sheltered; mild current; wave exposure possible in bad weather
- **Water temperature**: 4°C (winter) to 10°C (summer)
- **Visibility**: 10–20 m; better in winter and spring
- **Thermal protection**: Drysuit strongly recommended

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine life list available. The shallow rocky kelp reef habitat at Å is consistent with regional Lofoten species (pollock, saithe, crabs, anemones, nudibranchs), but no primary source confirms these at this specific named site.

## Safety

Cold water and remote location require appropriate preparation. Drysuit is recommended. Entry/exit on rocky Norwegian shoreline requires appropriate footwear and care.
