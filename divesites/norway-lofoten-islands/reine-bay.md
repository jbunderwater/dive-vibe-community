## Reine Bay

Reine Bay is a reef dive site in the Moskenesøya area, Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

Reine Bay offers Arctic diving beneath the dramatic granite peaks of Moskenesøya island at the western end of the Lofoten archipelago. The bay's cold, clear waters host dense kelp forests and anemone-covered rocky walls typical of Lofoten's protected bays. The maximum depth of this site is approximately 20 metres.

Note: The MV Hadsel, a real passenger steamer that sank near Reine in 1958, rests at approximately 40–45 metres depth and is a separate, advanced wreck dive site — it is not part of this 20-metre reef site. Earlier versions of this description incorrectly attributed the Hadsel wreck to Reine Bay; that claim has been removed.

No site-specific marine life reports were found for this reef site; the cold-water species typical of Lofoten's fjords — including Atlantic cod, wolffish, pollock, lumpfish, and nudibranchs — are likely present.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Reine, Moskenesøya, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
