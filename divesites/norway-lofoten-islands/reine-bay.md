## Reine Bay

Reine Bay is a curated dive area entry representing general boat diving around Reine on Moskenesøya, the southernmost inhabited island of the main Lofoten chain.

## Overview

Reine is one of Lofoten's most photographed villages, ringed by dramatic granite peaks rising directly from the fjord. The surrounding waters offer cold, clear Arctic diving in a bay sheltered by the mountainous terrain of Moskenesøya.

The best-documented dive site in the Reine area is the wreck of the MS Hadsel, a Norwegian coastal ferry that sank in January 1958 after running onto a skerry in a strong current while en route to Bodø with passengers and mail. The Hadsel rests at approximately 37–46 metres depth on the seabed near Hamnøy island, with its hull at a 45–50 degree list. The wreck is preserved in cold water and described as largely intact, with soft corals and large fish reported on the structure. It is a deeper Advanced-level dive and is offered as a guided excursion by Lofoten Diving from Ballstad.

The shallower bay areas around Reine also offer reef diving in kelp and rocky substrate habitat at depths up to approximately 20 metres, suitable for Intermediate divers. Water temperature is sub-10°C year-round; drysuit is required. Boat access from Reine or Ballstad.
