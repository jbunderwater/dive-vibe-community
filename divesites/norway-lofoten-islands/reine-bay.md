## Reine Bay

Reine Bay is a cold-water reef dive site beneath the dramatic granite peaks of Moskenesøya in the southern Lofoten archipelago.

## Overview

Reine is widely regarded as one of the most visually striking villages in Norway, set among towering rock faces above a sheltered bay. The underwater environment mirrors the dramatic character above the waterline: the bay features rocky reef terrain, dense kelp forest, and anemone-encrusted walls accessible to Intermediate-level divers.

The site extends to approximately 20 metres maximum depth, with mixed rocky substrate and kelp forest typical of sheltered southern Lofoten bays. Water temperature ranges from approximately 4°C in winter to 12°C in summer; drysuit is required year-round.

Note: Previous versions of this description attributed the MS Hadsel wreck to this site. The Hadsel (a Hurtigruten coastal passenger liner that sank near Hamnøy in January 1958, now resting at approximately 40 m depth) is a separate, named wreck dive site accessed from the Reine area — it is not part of the Reine Bay reef dive at 20 m. The two should not be conflated.

No site-specific sources confirming the particular reef topography, entry points, or marine life at this named Reine Bay site were located during QA. The description reflects the general character of sheltered bay reef diving in the southern Lofoten area based on regional dive operator sources.
