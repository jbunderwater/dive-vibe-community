## Reine Bay

Reine Bay is a boat-accessible reef dive near the picturesque fishing village of Reine, famous for its dramatic Lofoten Wall scenery. This intermediate reef site provides access to Arctic reef ecosystems and the vicinity of the MV Hadsel wreck.

## Overview

Reine Bay is a reef dive in the scenic Reine bay area, featuring rocky reef structures and sandy bottom at depths from 6-20 meters. Visibility typically ranges 10-20 meters, with water temperatures between 10-15°C in summer and 0-3°C in winter. The bay offers intermediate-level diving with moderate currents. The area is known for its proximity to the dramatic coastal landscape and the deeper MV Hadsel wreck (45 meters) which can be visited by advanced divers from this location.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Reine Bay, Moskenes, Lofoten Islands, Arctic Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Depth Range**: 6-20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 0-15°C (seasonal)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Arctic reef species including starfish, sea urchins, crabs, anemones, and cold-water fish inhabit the bay bottom. The rocky reef structure supports dense invertebrate communities. Macro opportunities abound. Cod and halibut are common species in the area. Seals occasionally visit the bay, and pelagic species may pass through deeper water.

## Dive Profile

Enter via boat and descend to reef structures at 6-10 meters. Explore reef formations, rocky outcrops, and sandy patches extending to 20 meters. The moderate depth allows for extended bottom time. Topography is generally gentle with natural features to navigate. Ascend slowly, conducting appropriate safety stops based on bottom time and depth exposure.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by boat from Reine or nearby Lofoten bases. Entry is via giant stride. Follow operator briefing for reef descent procedures. Deploy surface marker buoy (SMB) during safety stop. Exit via boat pickup as directed by crew.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a dive torch to illuminate reef details and colors
- Bring macro lens for photographing small organisms
- Wear thick thermal protection; water temperatures are consistently cold
- Visit during summer (May-August) for relatively warmer conditions
- Advanced divers may book trips to the MV Hadsel wreck (45 meters) from this location
- Check weather forecasts; Arctic conditions change rapidly
- Book with local Reine-based operators for best knowledge of bay conditions

## Safety Considerations

Cold water requires proper thermal protection and hypothermia awareness. Monitor air consumption carefully. Currents can be present in the bay. Always dive with a buddy and use a safety sausage. Respect the marine ecosystem. Plan conservative dive profiles appropriate to cold-water conditions.

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
