## Reine Bay

Reine Bay is a cold-water reef dive site near the village of Reine, Moskenesøya, Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

Reine Bay offers diving beneath the dramatic granite peaks of Moskenesøya island. X-Ray Magazine notes a kelp forest dive site accessible by short boat ride from Reine, with clear Arctic water and typical Lofoten marine life. The bay's cold waters support kelp forests and anemone-covered rock structures.

Note on nearby wrecks: The MS Hadsel, a Norwegian passenger liner that sank in January 1958 during bad weather near Reine, rests on a flat sandy seabed in the vicinity. Multiple sources give the wreck depth as 37–46 metres (top of wreck at approximately 37m, bottom at approximately 46m) — this is a separate, deeper boat-accessed wreck dive, not the same site as Reine Bay reef at 20 metres. The previous version of this page incorrectly described the MS Hadsel as part of the Reine Bay reef site. No passengers died when Hadsel sank; all 20 passengers and 26 crew evacuated by lifeboat.

Marine life confirmed for the broader Reine/Moskenesøya area by multiple Lofoten diving sources includes cod, halibut, wolffish, pollock, and sea anemones. No sources were found confirming species at the reef portion of Reine Bay specifically by name.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Reine, Moskenesøya, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
