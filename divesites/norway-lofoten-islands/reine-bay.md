## Reine Bay

Reine Bay is a dive area in the southern Lofoten Islands, best known as the access point for the MV Hadsel wreck — a confirmed, well-preserved Norwegian coastal passenger ferry that sank in January 1958 and rests at approximately 40 metres depth near Hamnøy island, adjacent to Reine.

## Overview

Reine is one of the most photographed villages in Norway, set beneath dramatic granite peaks on the island of Moskenesøya in the southern Lofoten chain. The surrounding waters are dived by operators from both the village and from Ballstad (approximately 25 km north), and the main draw is the MV Hadsel wreck.

**Note on this database entry:** The database records "Reine Bay" as a reef site at 20 metres depth. Research indicates the primary documented dive near Reine is the MV Hadsel wreck (approximately 40m deep, confirmed across multiple independent sources), not a shallower reef site. Shallow reef and kelp diving consistent with the regional Lofoten pattern likely exists in the bay, but has not been independently confirmed at this specific named location at 20m depth.

### The MV Hadsel Wreck (nearby, not this entry)

The MV Hadsel was a Norwegian coastal passenger ferry that foundered and sank in January 1958 during severe winter weather and strong currents near Hamnøy island, just outside Reine. No passengers were lost. The vessel rests nearly upright on a flat sandy bottom with a 45-degree list to port, with the top of the wreck at approximately 37 metres and the hull base at approximately 46 metres. This wreck is in a separate dive category from this "Reine Bay" entry — Advanced difficulty due to depth (37–46m).

The wreck is described as "beautifully preserved" by cold Arctic conditions, with the foremast still standing upright and the superstructure intact, allowing limited penetration by experienced divers. X-Ray Mag (2015) reported a length of approximately 220 feet (~67 metres).

## Marine Life

Norway's fjords and coastal waters host cold-water species typical of the North Atlantic. No site-specific marine life reports were found for the "Reine Bay" reef dive specifically. The Lofoten regional fauna includes Atlantic cod, wolffish, halibut, pollock, Dahlia anemones, dead man's fingers, nudibranchs, and sea urchins; these are likely present in the bay area but have not been confirmed in published sources at this specific location.
