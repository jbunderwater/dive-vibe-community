## Stamsund Kelp Forest

Stamsund Kelp Forest is a cold-water shore dive through dense Laminaria kelp in a Lofoten fishing village setting.

## Overview

The kelp forest off Stamsund grows in dense stands of Laminaria hyperborea along the rocky shoreline, with the towering kelp canopy extending close to the surface and creating a shaded, cathedral-like passage below. Stamsund is a working fishing village served by the Hurtigruten coastal ferry, giving the site an accessible, lived-in character.

The dive extends to approximately 14 metres maximum depth, with a mix of kelp-covered rocky reef and sand patches. Cold-water species typical of Lofoten inhabit the kelp ecosystem — the site is not visited specifically for unusual biodiversity, but for the experience of diving through a productive, temperate-Arctic kelp forest environment.

Despite the shallow depth, water temperature of 4–12°C year-round means drysuit and cold-water experience are required. This is an Intermediate-rated cold-water destination site; the low depth does not reduce the thermal demands or equipment requirements.

Regional cold-water species (cod, saithe, crabs, nudibranchs on kelp holdfasts) are characteristic of this environment but have not been confirmed at this specific named site by a primary source.
