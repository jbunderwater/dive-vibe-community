## Stamsund Kelp Forest

Stamsund Kelp Forest is a cold-water kelp-reef dive near Stamsund, a Lofoten fishing village served by the Hurtigruten coastal ferry. Kelp forest diving is a signature experience of the Lofoten Islands, though no published dive source specifically documents a named site at Stamsund.

## Overview

Stamsund is located on the north coast of Vestvågøya, the second-largest Lofoten island. The village is a working fishing port and a stop on the Hurtigruten coastal route. While the broader Lofoten Islands are well-documented for kelp diving — particularly around Ballstad, approximately 20 kilometres to the south — Stamsund is not mentioned as a specific dive destination by any operator or dive travel source found during research.

Kelp forests in the Lofoten Islands are described by dive journalists as "magnificent" and "cathedral-like," with Laminaria hyperborea (tangle kelp) on stalks of 1–2 metres creating a canopy that filters Arctic light. The habitat beneath the canopy supports the full cold-water community of Lofoten: cod, saithe, lumpfish, nudibranchs, hermit crabs, sea urchins, and dense invertebrate life on rocky substrate.

The maximum depth of 14 metres and shore entry are consistent with a kelp-reef dive, though these specifications have not been confirmed from a site-specific source.

**Difficulty note:** Classified Intermediate (upgraded from Beginner). Cold water (4–12°C year-round depending on season) requires drysuit competency. No cold-water Lofoten dive should be rated Beginner for visiting divers unfamiliar with Arctic conditions.

## Marine Life

Norway's fjords and coastal waters host cold-water species typical of the North Atlantic. No site-specific marine life reports were found for Stamsund specifically. Confirmed for Lofoten kelp sites in general (X-Ray Mag, Scuba Diver Life): Atlantic cod, wolffish, pollock, lumpfish, sea urchins, starfish, hermit crabs, nudibranchs, blade shrimp; Laminaria kelp forest canopy throughout.
