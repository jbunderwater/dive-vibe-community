## Stamsund Kelp Forest

Stamsund Kelp Forest is a kelp reef shore dive site near Stamsund fishing village, Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

The kelp forest off Stamsund grows in dense stands along the rocky shoreline of this Lofoten fishing village on the south coast of Vestvågøya island. The Laminaria kelp canopy reaches close to the surface and creates a shaded, cathedral-like environment below. The maximum depth is approximately 14 metres.

Cold-water conditions in Lofoten (4–10°C year-round) require drysuit diving and a minimum of Intermediate certification; despite the shallow depth this is not a beginner site for divers without drysuit experience.

No site-specific marine life reports were found; the cold-water species typical of Lofoten's kelp forests — including Atlantic cod, saithe, lumpfish, edible crab, sea urchins, and nudibranchs on kelp holdfasts — are likely present based on regional diving conditions.
