## Stamsund Kelp Forest

Stamsund Kelp Forest is a cold-water kelp reef dive site in the Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

Stamsund is a fishing village on Vestvågøy island served by the Hurtigruten coastal ferry route. The kelp forest dive here is a shore entry accessible from the village shoreline.

No site-specific sources confirming diving conditions at Stamsund specifically were located during research. The description below reflects the well-documented character of Lofoten kelp forest diving, particularly from accounts of similar sites in the Ballstad–Stamsund area of central Lofoten.

Giant Laminaria hyperborea kelp forms dense stands along the rocky shoreline, with the canopy creating a shaded environment below. This habitat type is well-documented throughout central Lofoten. Species confirmed in Lofoten kelp forest diving include Atlantic cod, pollock, and wolffish; sea anemones, dead man's fingers (Alcyonium digitatum), nudibranchs, hermit crabs, and sea urchins on rock surfaces beneath the canopy. These species are confirmed for Lofoten kelp diving in general but have not been confirmed at Stamsund specifically.

Despite the shallow maximum depth of 14 metres, this is cold Arctic water year-round; drysuit is strongly recommended. This site is not appropriate for beginner divers without cold-water experience.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Stamsund, Vestvågøy, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 meters
