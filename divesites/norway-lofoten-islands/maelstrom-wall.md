## Maelstrom Wall

Maelstrom Wall is an advanced wall dive named for the famous Saltstraumen maelstrom currents in the region. This dramatic vertical wall offers deep water diving with significant Arctic currents and exceptional wall structure.

## Overview

Maelstrom Wall is a steep underwater drop starting at approximately 8-10 meters and descending to 32 meters. The wall is exposed to open water conditions with strong currents characteristic of the Saltstraumen area. Visibility typically ranges 10-20 meters, with water temperatures between 0-15°C depending on season. This is a challenging advanced site requiring experience with wall diving, depth management, strong currents, and cold-water skills. The dramatic vertical structure and deep exposure make this suitable only for highly qualified divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Saltstraumen area, Lofoten Islands, Arctic Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 32 meters
- **Depth Range**: 8-32 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 0-15°C (seasonal)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong (Saltstraumen effect)
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The wall supports Arctic marine communities with anemones, sea urchins, crabs, and cold-adapted fish species. The deep sections host different organisms than shallow areas. The exposed position may attract pelagic species into the blue water beyond the wall. The current-swept conditions support filter-feeding organisms.

## Dive Profile

Entry begins with descent to the wall top at approximately 8-10 meters. Descend along the vertical wall face maintaining excellent buoyancy and awareness of current direction. Monitor depth carefully—the wall can lead to unplanned deep descents. Work deeper gradually, exploring features and organisms at 15-32 meters. The strong currents require excellent positioning and streamlined movement. Plan appropriate safety stops with significant deep exposure. Begin ascent well before air reaches critical minimums.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by boat from operators experienced with Saltstraumen conditions. Entry is via giant stride. Follow operator briefing for wall descent and current management. Deploy surface marker buoy (SMB) during safety stop. Exit via boat pickup as directed by crew.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced divers only—requires excellent buoyancy, depth management, and current awareness
- This is a challenging current dive; verify current forecasts before diving
- Monitor air consumption closely; breathing increases with depth and current stress
- Bring a dive torch for colors and wall details
- Wear thermal protection or dry suit; Arctic water is consistently cold
- Book only with operators specialized in Saltstraumen current diving
- Plan conservative dive profiles with significant safety margins
- Visit during summer (May-August) for slightly better conditions
- The Saltstraumen maelstrom currents affect diving conditions; plan dives during slack water periods

## Safety Considerations

This is a challenging dive requiring advanced skills and strong current experience. Cold water demands proper thermal protection and hypothermia awareness. The strong currents of Saltstraumen require excellent positioning and awareness. Monitor air consumption closely at depth. Always dive with a buddy experienced in current diving. Use a safety sausage. Dive within your advanced certification limits and current experience. Respect the power of this site.
