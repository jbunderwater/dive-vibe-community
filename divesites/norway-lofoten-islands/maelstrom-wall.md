## Maelstrom Wall

Maelstrom Wall is a current-driven wall dive site near the Moskstraumen in the Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

This site sits near the Moskstraumen — one of the world's strongest tidal current systems, located between the island of Moskenesøya and the small island of Mosken. The Moskstraumen is approximately 4–5 kilometres wide and has been measured at maximum current speeds of 27.8 km/h. These tidal currents create nutrient-rich, highly productive waters.

No site-specific dive accounts for a named "Maelstrom Wall" dive site were located during research. The site's classification as a wall dive in strong current waters is consistent with the Moskstraumen's known geography and comparable to documented diving at Saltstraumen, Norway's other major maelstrom dive site. Saltstraumen dive accounts describe cold-water, current-driven walls with anemone and sponge colonisation as typical features — conditions consistent with what divers would encounter near Moskstraumen as well.

This is an Advanced dive requiring expert timing at slack water. Conditions revert rapidly around the tidal turn; this dive is only appropriate for experienced divers with strong current experience and a knowledgeable local guide.
