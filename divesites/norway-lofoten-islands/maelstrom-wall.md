## Maelstrom Wall

Maelstrom Wall is a dive site in the vicinity of the Moskstraumen — one of the strongest tidal current systems in the world — between Moskenesøya and the small island of Mosken at the southwestern tip of the Lofoten chain.

## Overview

The Moskstraumen (the Lofoten Maelstrom) is a system of powerful tidal eddies and whirlpools approximately 4–5 kilometres wide between Moskenesøya and Mosken island. Wikipedia records current speeds up to 27.8 km/h and notes the seafloor in the Moskstraumen area rises to a ridge approximately 20 metres deep — consistent with the maximum depth recorded for this dive site entry.

Zentacle.com lists a dive site at "Moskstraumen" with a maximum depth of approximately 20 metres, boat access, and Intermediate difficulty. The site name "Maelstrom Wall" used in this database is a descriptive label — no operator or dive-travel source uses this exact name, and the dive may not be classified as a wall dive. The main characteristic of diving here is the extreme tidal current; diving is only possible at precisely planned slack water, with conditions reverting rapidly once the tide begins to run. Local knowledge and an experienced guide are essential.

The site demands Advanced classification in this database due to: cold water (4–10°C), remote boat access near an open-ocean current system, and the narrow operational window requiring expert timing.
