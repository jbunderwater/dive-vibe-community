## Nusfjord Wall

Nusfjord Wall — known locally as "Shit Wall" — is a vertical anemone-covered wall dive directly opposite Nusfjord harbour, rated by multiple dive journalists as one of the most colourful cold-water dives in Europe.

## Overview

The site takes its informal name from the cormorants nesting above on the harbour's granite cliff face: their guano enriches the water with nutrients, feeding thousands of Dahlia anemones that carpet the wall from just below the surface to approximately 20–22 metres depth. The result is a dense, vivid display that X-Ray Mag describes as rivalling any tropical coral reef in colour intensity.

The wall begins at roughly half a metre below the surface and descends steeply to its base at around 20–22 metres. The upper section near the harbour is the most densely populated, with anemones covering virtually every centimetre of rock face in shades of orange, purple, and white. The surrounding cold water carries excellent visibility — typically 10–20 metres — and the dive takes place in the shadow of one of Lofoten's best-preserved 18th-century fishing village clusters, with traditional red and yellow rorbu (fishing cabin) buildings visible from the surface.

Nusfjord is accessed by boat from Ballstad (approximately 30 minutes by RIB) through Lofoten Diving or independently. The dive is rated Intermediate due to cold water requiring drysuit competency; the wall itself is navigationally straightforward with no significant current at most states of tide.

## Marine Life

Confirmed at this site (X-Ray Mag, Zentacle):
- Thousands of Dahlia anemones (Urticina felina) covering the full wall face
- Fish in open water above and alongside the wall (species typical of Lofoten: saithe, pollock, cod)

Norway's fjords and coastal waters host cold-water species typical of the North Atlantic. Species confirmed at the Nusfjord Wall specifically are the Dahlia anemones described above; individual fish species reported at the site in general diving sources include saithe and pollock in the water column.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Nusfjord, Flakstadøya, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 metres (wall base; anemones dense from surface to ~20m)
