## Nusfjord Wall

Nusfjord Wall is a cold-water wall dive site in the Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

A steep rock face in Nusfjord harbour — locally described in dive accounts as providing some of the most colourful shallow wall diving in Lofoten — drops from just below the surface to approximately 20 metres. The wall is colonized by thousands of Dahlia anemones forming a dense carpet from around half a metre depth down to the wall's base, creating what X-Ray Magazine described as a "kaleidoscope of colors" rivalling tropical coral reef diving. Dead man's fingers (Alcyonium digitatum) and encrusting coralline algae fill every available surface, fed by nutrients delivered by the tidal currents that flow through the Nusfjord harbour entrance.

Nusfjord itself is one of Lofoten's best-preserved historic fishing villages. The dive site is accessible by boat from Ballstad (operated by Lofoten Diving). The site is classified Advanced due to cold-water conditions, the commitment of open-water boat diving, and the requirement for drysuit competency.

Marine life confirmed at Nusfjord by local dive accounts includes Dahlia anemones and dead man's fingers as dominant colonisers. No site-specific fish or nudibranch species lists were located for this exact wall; general Lofoten species (wolffish, pollock, cod) are likely present but have not been independently confirmed at this specific site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Nusfjord, Flakstad, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
