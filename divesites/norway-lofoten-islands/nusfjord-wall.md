## Nusfjord Wall

Nusfjord Wall is a cold-water wall dive site near one of Lofoten's best-preserved historic fishing villages.

## Overview

Nusfjord is a small, historically significant fishing village on the south coast of Flakstadøya. The dive site known locally as the "Shit Wall" — referenced in this database as Nusfjord Wall — is a steep rock face situated just across from the village harbour. According to X-Ray Mag's account of Lofoten diving, this wall is considered one of the most beautiful dive sites in the Lofoten area, with the rock face descending from shallow depth and blanketed in Dahlia anemones and dead man's fingers (Alcyonium digitatum).

The wall drops to approximately 20 metres and is accessible by boat from local operators based at Ballstad. Cold-water conditions (4–12°C year-round) require drysuit diving. Visibility in the sheltered Nusfjord area is typically 10–20 metres.

Note: An earlier version of this description described Nusfjord as "UNESCO-listed." Nusfjord is historically significant and protected but does not appear on the UNESCO World Heritage List. That claim has been removed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Nusfjord, Flakstadøya, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
