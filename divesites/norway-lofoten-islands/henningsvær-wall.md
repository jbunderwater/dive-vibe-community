## Henningsvær Wall

Henningsvær Wall is a dramatic advanced wall dive located near the picturesque artist village of Henningsvær. This stunning vertical underwater structure drops away into the blue, offering challenging wall diving with Arctic marine ecosystems.

## Overview

The Henningsvær Wall is a steep underwater drop starting at approximately 8-10 meters and descending to 28 meters. The wall is exposed to open water conditions with variable currents. Visibility typically ranges 10-20 meters, with water temperatures between 0-15°C depending on season. This is an advanced site requiring experience with wall diving, depth management, and cold-water skills. The dramatic vertical topography and deep sections make this suitable only for qualified divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Henningsvær, Vågan, Lofoten Islands, Arctic Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Depth Range**: 8-28 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 0-15°C (seasonal)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The wall supports Arctic marine communities with anemones, sea urchins, crabs, and cold-adapted fish species. The deep sections may support different organisms than shallow areas. The wall's exposure to blue water can attract pelagic visitors. Seals are occasionally seen in the area.

## Dive Profile

Entry begins with descent to the wall top at approximately 8-10 meters. Descend along the vertical wall face maintaining proper buoyancy. Monitor depth carefully—it's easy to descend too quickly on steep walls. Work deeper gradually, exploring features and organisms at 15-28 meters. Plan appropriate safety stops with deep exposure. Begin ascent well before air reaches critical minimums, conducting safety stops at 5 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by dive boat from Henningsvær operators. Entry is via giant stride. Follow operator briefing for wall descent procedures. Deploy surface marker buoy (SMB) during safety stop. Exit via boat pickup as directed by crew.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced divers only—requires excellent buoyancy and depth management skills
- Watch depth carefully; the wall can lead to unplanned deep descents
- Monitor air consumption closely; breathing increases at depth and in cold
- Bring a dive torch to see wall colors and details
- Wear thermal protection or dry suit; Arctic water is consistently cold
- Book only with experienced Arctic wall-diving specialists
- Visit during summer (May-August) for slightly warmer conditions
- Plan conservative dive profiles with appropriate safety margins

## Safety Considerations

This is a challenging dive requiring advanced skills. Cold water demands proper thermal protection and hypothermia awareness. Monitor air consumption closely at depth. Currents may be significant; always dive with a buddy. Use a safety sausage. Dive within your advanced certification limits. Respect the depth and exposure of this site.

## Photography

Wall dives offer stunning wide-angle opportunities with dramatic depth perspectives. The interplay of light and shadow along the wall creates compelling compositions. Look for macro subjects in wall crevices and overhangs.
