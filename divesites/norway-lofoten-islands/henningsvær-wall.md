## Henningsvær Wall

Henningsvær Wall is a cold-water wall dive near one of Lofoten's most scenic fishing villages, operated by Lofoten Opplevelser from Henningsvær.

## Overview

Henningsvær, built across a cluster of small islands connected by bridges on the southern coast of Austvågøya, sits approximately 185 kilometres north of the Arctic Circle. The underwater terrain mirrors the dramatic, vertical character of the Lofoten landscape above water: walls of exposed rock drop from kelp-covered shallows to a maximum of approximately 28 metres, with surfaces carpeted in dead man's fingers (Alcyonium digitatum), Dahlia anemones, and encrusting coralline algae.

Wolffish are encountered in crevices along the wall; pollock school in the water column; and large Atlantic cod patrol the wall base. Lumpfish and sculpin rest on rocky substrate. The kelp canopy above the wall top shelters nudibranchs and hermit crabs.

Water temperature ranges from 4°C in winter to 12–15°C in summer. Drysuit is strongly recommended year-round. The dive is classified Advanced due to sub-10°C water temperatures requiring drysuit competency, exposed conditions with potential tidal current, and the buoyancy control required on vertical wall terrain. Lofoten Opplevelser operates local guided dives; Lofoten Diving (Ballstad) also serves the Henningsvær area by RIB.
