## Henningsvær Wall

Henningsvær Wall is a cold-water wall dive site in the Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

Henningsvær is one of the most picturesque fishing villages in Lofoten, built across several small islands connected by bridges approximately 185 kilometres north of the Arctic Circle. Lofoten Diving and Lofoten Opplevelser both operate guided diving from the Henningsvær area.

No site-specific sources uniquely describing a dive wall at Henningsvær were located during research; the X-Ray Magazine account of Lofoten diving describes wall sites generally in the Ballstad–Nusfjord range. The wall and canyon topography around Henningsvær is consistent with the broader Lofoten underwater landscape. The site is classified Advanced due to cold-water conditions, open-water boat access, and the requirement for drysuit competency.

General Lofoten wall diving characteristics that likely apply: dead man's fingers (Alcyonium digitatum) and Dahlia anemones colonising the wall face; kelp forest above; cold-water fish including wolffish, pollock, and cod. These species are confirmed for Lofoten wall diving in general but have not been confirmed at this specific site by name.

Water temperature ranges from approximately 4°C in winter to 12–15°C in summer. Drysuit is strongly recommended year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Henningsvær, Vestvågøy, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 metres
