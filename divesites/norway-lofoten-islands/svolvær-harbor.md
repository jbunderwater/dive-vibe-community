## Svolvær Harbor

Svolvær is the largest town in the Lofoten Islands and a wreck-diving hub, with several accessible wrecks in or near the harbour dived by operators including NDL Diving Lofoten. The "Svolvær Harbor" database entry likely refers to recreational shore diving in the harbour area, distinct from the named wreck dives.

## Overview

Svolvær sits on Austvågøya beneath the dramatic Svolværgeita peaks. The harbour is known in Norwegian diving circles primarily as a wreck-diving destination: X-Ray Mag and Scuba Diver Life confirm that named wrecks in the Svolvær area include the SS Ramø, SS Hamburg, MRS-25 (German minesweeper mothership), MV Karlshorst, and MV Siw-Aina. MV Siw-Aina is described as the shallowest and easiest of the Svolvær wrecks and is accessible by the same RIB that services the Ballstad dive sites. NDL Diving Lofoten operates wreck diving excursions directly from Svolvær.

For shore-based recreational diving in the harbour area (as this entry describes), the bottom is a mix of rocky reef, kelp, and sand in the sheltered inner harbour. Visibility benefits from Lofoten's clean, nutrient-rich coastal waters.

**Difficulty note:** This site is classified Intermediate (upgraded from Beginner). Cold water (4–15°C year-round depending on season) requires drysuit competency. No cold-water Lofoten dive should be rated Beginner for visiting divers unfamiliar with Arctic conditions.

## Marine Life

Norway's fjords and coastal waters host cold-water species typical of the North Atlantic, but no site-specific marine life reports were found for shore diving specifically in Svolvær Harbour. Regional harbour-area species in Lofoten include pollock, saithe, shore crabs, sea anemones, and nudibranchs on rocky substrate.
