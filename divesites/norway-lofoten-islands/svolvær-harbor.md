## Svolvær Harbor

Svolvær Harbor is a cold-water shore dive site in the Lofoten Islands, Norway.

## Overview

Svolvær is the largest town and administrative centre of the Lofoten Islands, situated on Austvågøya island. The harbour area offers a sheltered shore dive with a maximum depth of 15 metres.

No site-specific sources confirming diving conditions within Svolvær harbour specifically were located during research. The description below reflects general Lofoten harbour and coastal reef characteristics.

Lofoten Diving (based in Ballstad) operates wreck dive excursions from Svolvær to nearby wrecks including the SS Hamburg and MV Siw-Aina — the MV Siw-Aina in particular is noted as the shallowest and easiest wreck in the Svolvær area. Those boat-based wreck dives are a different category from this shore reef entry.

For the shore dive itself: the harbour bottom is rocky reef with kelp and sand. Species typical of Lofoten harbour environments include pollock, saithe, Atlantic cod, shore crabs, sea urchins, and anemones. Despite the shallow depth and sheltered conditions, Svolvær water is cold Arctic water year-round; this site is not appropriate for beginner divers without cold-water experience. Drysuit is strongly recommended.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Svolvær, Austvågøya, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
