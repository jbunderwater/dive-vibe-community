## Svolvær Harbor

Svolvær Harbor is a shallow harbor shore dive located in Svolvær, the primary gateway town to Lofoten diving. This sheltered site provides easy access to Arctic reef ecosystems and is home to the MV Siw-Aina wreck in its shallowest form.

## Overview

Svolvær Harbor is an accessible shore dive in the commercial harbor of Svolvær. The site features a gradual descent to approximately 15 meters with mixed rocky and sandy bottom. Visibility typically ranges 10-20 meters, with water temperatures between 10-15°C in summer and 0-3°C in winter. The protected harbor conditions and accessible shore entry make this suitable for divers building cold-water experience. The area includes access to shallow wreck remains and macro diving areas.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Svolvær Harbor, Vågan, Lofoten Islands, Arctic Norway
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Depth Range**: 0-15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 0-15°C (seasonal)
- **Current**: Light to very light
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Harbor depths support Arctic marine life including crabs, anemones, sea urchins, starfish, and small fish species. The harbor bottom is transitional between rocky reef and sandy substrate, supporting diverse small organisms. Macro photography subjects abound. Occasionally seals visit the harbor in search of fish.

## Dive Profile

Enter from harbor shore and descend gradually into shallow water. Navigate through harbor bottom, observing reef structure and marine life. Extend to maximum depth of 15 meters if desired. The site offers flexibility for building bottom-time and navigation practice. Excellent for newer divers practicing in cold water. Ascend slowly, monitoring buddy positioning.

## Entry and Exit

Enter from rocky harbor shore in central Svolvær. Check conditions and visibility before entry. Carefully approach water and descend. Exit at same location, monitoring for surge near shore. Be mindful of commercial harbor activity; check diving windows with local operators.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent for practicing cold-water entry and navigation skills
- Bring macro lens for photographing small marine organisms
- Bring a dive torch to see colors and details
- Wear thermal protection; harbor water is consistently cold
- Visit during summer (May-August) for slightly warmer water
- Check harbor traffic schedules; coordinate with dive operators
- This site is ideal for practicing skills before deeper, more exposed dives
- Visit during slack water for best conditions

## Safety Considerations

Cold water requires proper thermal protection. Harbor entry requires awareness of boat traffic; coordinate timing with local operators. Exit carefully to avoid surge zones. Always dive with a buddy. Monitor air consumption—cold water increases consumption. Respect the working harbor environment.

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
