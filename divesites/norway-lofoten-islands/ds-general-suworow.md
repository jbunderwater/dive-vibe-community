## D/S General Suworow

D/S General Suworow is a historic steamship wreck dive in the Lofoten Islands area, Norway.

## Overview

The D/S General Suworow is a steamship that sank on 5 August 1919 (confirmed by OSM wreck data). The wreck is tagged in OpenStreetMap at coordinates 68.14°N, 13.53°E in the Vestfjorden area south of the Lofoten archipelago, with a recorded depth of 18 metres. A YouTube dive video confirms the wreck exists as a diving destination in the Lofoten region.

No site-specific depth, condition, or history details beyond the sinking date could be verified from independent published sources. The description of this wreck resting "at around 40 meters depth north of Bodø" that appeared in an earlier version of this page was not confirmed by any verifiable source and has been removed. The OSM-recorded depth of 18 metres is used until a site-specific source is found.

No site-specific marine life reports were found; the cold-water species typical of Lofoten's fjords — including Atlantic cod, wolffish, pollock, lumpfish, and nudibranchs — are likely present on the wreck structure.
