## Kabelvåg Shore

Kabelvåg Shore is a shallow cold-water shore dive near the historic town of Kabelvåg on Austvågøya island, one of the oldest settlements in northern Norway and a base for the local operator LOFOTDYKK.

## Overview

Kabelvåg is situated on the southwestern coast of Austvågøya, Lofoten's largest island. The town is one of the oldest in northern Norway and was the historic administrative centre of Lofoten. Shore diving at Kabelvåg is offered by the local dive operator LOFOTDYKK, whose centre is located in or near the town.

The site's maximum depth of approximately 12 metres places it firmly in shallow reef territory. The bottom is a mix of rocky substrate and kelp forest, with sand patches in the shallower sections. Laminaria kelp (often called tangle kelp locally) dominates the upper zone from near the surface down to the light limit, and the rocky walls and boulders below support communities of sea anemones and coralline algae. The shallowness allows extended bottom time despite thermal drain from the cold water.

Water temperature ranges from approximately 4°C in winter to 10–13°C in summer. A drysuit is strongly recommended; semi-dry acceptable only in peak summer.

**Difficulty note:** Classified Intermediate (not Beginner). Arctic cold water requires drysuit competency and appropriate training regardless of depth. Lofoten shore dives are not beginner-appropriate for divers unfamiliar with cold-water conditions.
