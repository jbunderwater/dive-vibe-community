## Kabelvåg Shore

Kabelvåg Shore is a cold-water shore dive near one of Lofoten's oldest towns, on the southwestern coast of Austvågøya.

## Overview

Kabelvåg, formerly the trading centre of Lofoten and one of the oldest towns in northern Norway, sits on a sheltered coastal section of Austvågøya island. The shore dive here reaches a maximum depth of approximately 12 metres in a rocky reef and kelp forest habitat. The sheltered setting limits current, making this one of the more manageable entry points for divers new to Norwegian cold-water conditions.

The bottom is rocky substrate with dense Laminaria hyperborea kelp covering the shallower zones, transitioning to open rock with sea anemones, dead man's fingers (Alcyonium digitatum), and coralline algae at depth. Typical fish species for the area — saithe, pollock, Atlantic cod, and sculpin — move through the kelp canopy and along the rocky bottom. Nudibranchs are found on kelp holdfasts.

Water temperature ranges from approximately 4°C in winter to 10–12°C in summer. A drysuit is strongly recommended year-round for comfort and thermal safety in sub-10°C conditions. This is an Intermediate site due to cold-water requirements and rocky shore entry, not technical depth.

Lofoten Opplevelser operates guided dives from Henningsvær to the east; Lofoten Diving serves the broader Austvågøya area from Ballstad. Shore divers should inform someone of their plan before entering.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kabelvåg, Austvågøya, Lofoten Islands, Nordland, Norway
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
