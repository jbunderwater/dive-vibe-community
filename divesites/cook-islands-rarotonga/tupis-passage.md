# Tupis Passage

A reef site on Rarotonga's northeast corner, a short distance south of Arbuthnotts. Ridges and gullies run WNW from flat sand at 12 meters to a broken reef edge at 20 meters dropping to 30+ meters. Whitetip reef sharks, guineafowl moray eels, and giant clams are the characteristic species. Eight minutes from harbour.

## Overview

Tupi's Passage (also written Tupis Passage) sits on the northeast corner of Rarotonga, a few hundred metres south of Arbuthnotts, near a small creek on the adjacent shoreline. The site is approximately eight minutes by boat from Avatiu Harbour.

The terrain features a series of ridges and gullies running WNW from a flat sandy area at approximately 12 meters. The ridges slope to a broken reef edge at 20 meters, which then drops to 30 meters and beyond with small overhangs and isolated formations along the edge. Coral cover is approximately 10% — slightly higher than Arbuthnotts to the north, but still characteristically sparse for Rarotonga's northeast coast. No sponges or gorgonians are present.

Despite the modest coral, the broken reef edge with its overhangs concentrates the characteristic northeast-coast species: whitetip reef sharks patrol and rest in the overhangs, guineafowl moray eels shelter in the crevices, and giant clams occupy the gullies. The site shares a very similar character to Arbuthnotts and the two are close enough to be explored consecutively when air supply allows.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northeast corner, Rarotonga, Cook Islands (south of Arbuthnotts, near a small creek)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 40+ meters
- **Water Temperature**: 24-27°C
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Whitetip reef sharks are seen along the broken reef edge and resting under overhangs. Guineafowl moray eels shelter in various sizes throughout the volcanic rock crevices. Giant clams sit in the gullies. Surgeonfish, trevally, triggerfish, threadfin and longnose butterflyfish, and Moorish idols are present throughout the depth range. Black sea cucumbers are abundant.

## Dive Profile

Drop to the flat sand at approximately 12 meters and follow the ridges WNW as they slope toward the broken reef edge at 20 meters. Investigate the small overhangs along the broken edge — these are the best spots for resting whitetip reef sharks and moray eels. The edge drops to 30 meters with deeper sections beyond — monitor depth carefully. The proximity to Arbuthnotts means the two sites can be combined in a single longer dive if air supply permits.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Avatiu Harbour, approximately eight minutes. Entry by giant stride.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check the broken reef edge overhangs carefully for whitetip reef sharks resting underneath
- The guineafowl morays at various sizes make good macro subjects
- Can be combined with Arbuthnotts as a longer single dive if air permits — the two sites have nearly identical character

## Safety Considerations

The reef edge drops into deeper water — monitor depth carefully near the broken edge. Currents are generally mild but can strengthen near the reef edge. The 30-meter depth requires careful gas management.
