# Matavera School Reef

A reef on Rarotonga's northeast corner, off Matavera School, with a sandy anchor at 10 meters sloping to coral bommies and wall overhangs at 30 meters before plunging severely to 100+ meters. The lowest coral cover on the island (~5%), but resident turtles and firefish (lionfish) in the overhangs. Twelve minutes from harbour.

## Overview

Matavera School Reef lies on the northeast corner of Rarotonga, offshore from Matavera School, approximately twelve minutes from Avatiu Harbour. The anchor drops on a sandy bottom at approximately 10 meters. The reef then slopes outward through coral bommies separated by sand — not a continuous reef wall — to a 30-meter intermittent wall with overhangs that drops severely into water exceeding 100 meters.

Coral cover at this site is the lowest observed around Rarotonga at approximately 5%. The volcanic rock substrate dominates, with scattered coral colonies and no sponges or gorgonians. Despite the sparse coral, the dramatic topography and resident marine life make the site rewarding. Two turtles are regularly seen, and large firefish (lionfish) inhabit the overhangs along the wall.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northeast corner, off Matavera School, Rarotonga, Cook Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 40+ meters
- **Water Temperature**: 24-27°C
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Two turtles are regularly encountered at this site. Large firefish (lionfish) shelter under the wall overhangs at 30 meters. Surgeonfish, trevally, and a range of butterflyfish are present. The low coral cover (~5%) means fewer small reef fish than the west-side sites, but the wall and bommie structure concentrates what fauna is present.

## Dive Profile

Drop to the anchor at 10 meters and navigate through the coral bommies and sandy sections as the reef slopes outward. The bommies provide natural waypoints as the depth increases to 20 meters and then to the intermittent wall overhangs at 30 meters. The overhangs are where the firefish and turtles are most often encountered. Do not follow the wall beyond 30 meters — the drop into very deep water is severe and well beyond sport diving limits. The excellent visibility means the deep abyss is clearly visible from the wall edge; it is dramatic but requires discipline to avoid. Ascend along the sandy bommie slope back to the anchor.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Avatiu Harbour, approximately twelve minutes. Entry by giant stride.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check the 30-meter overhangs carefully for firefish tucked underneath — they blend well into the volcanic rock
- The two resident turtles are accustomed to divers; maintain a respectful distance
- Despite the low coral cover, the topography with its severe dropoff and the resident wildlife make this a worthwhile northeast-coast dive

## Safety Considerations

The severe dropoff beyond 30 meters demands strict depth monitoring. The crystal-clear visibility can make the abyss inviting — do not descend beyond 30 meters. Currents are generally mild but can increase near the wall edge.
