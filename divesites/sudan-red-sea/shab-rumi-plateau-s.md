## Shab Rumi, Plateau S

Sha'ab Rumi's South Plateau sits at the exposed southern tip of the atoll, roughly 25 miles northeast of Port Sudan, with a shallow plateau at around 9 metres giving way to a steep drop-off.

## Overview

The South Plateau is one of Sha'ab Rumi's best-regarded shark dives. Grey reef sharks and blacktip reef sharks are a constant presence, with hammerhead sharks — Sha'ab Rumi's headline species — most reliably encountered here during the spring season. The reef's southwest tip is a particular hotspot, where a school of scalloped hammerhead sharks regularly patrols the open water. Snappers, sweetlips, and occasional dolphins round out the resident community.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern tip of Sha'ab Rumi, northern Sudan Red Sea
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters

## Marine Life

Grey reef sharks and blacktip reef sharks are resident; scalloped hammerhead sharks school at the southwest tip, especially in spring. Snappers and sweetlips are common, with dolphins seen on occasion. A previously listed "barracuda tornado" specific to this plateau could not be independently corroborated and has been removed — that phenomenon is commonly associated with other Red Sea sites (e.g., Elphinstone, Daedalus in Egypt) and should not be assumed here without a direct source.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the plateau at around 9 metres and move toward the southwest tip, where hammerhead and shark activity concentrates. The plateau's edge drops steeply — maintain depth discipline rather than following sharks into deeper water.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by liveaboard anchored at Sha'ab Rumi. This dive pairs naturally with the Precontinent II (Conshelf II) site in the same atoll's lagoon.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Spring is the reported best season for hammerhead encounters
- Hold a stable position at the southwest tip rather than chasing sharks along the wall
- Combine with the Precontinent II lagoon dive for a full Sha'ab Rumi day

## Safety Considerations

The plateau edge is an abrupt drop-off — divers following sharks below the plateau can descend rapidly without noticing; monitor your depth gauge continuously. Maintain a firm depth limit before entering the water and carry a high-visibility SMB.
