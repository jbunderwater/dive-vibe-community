## Merlo Reef

Merlo Reef is a pinnacle reef just over a mile southwest of Angarosh, rising from a seafloor roughly 450 metres deep.

## Overview

Merlo Reef's eastern and western walls are marked by cracks and caverns, with the eastern wall's vertical crevices running down to about 15 metres. A small plateau at the reef's north end carries a soft coral bommie, and a small wreck lies on the reef's northwest side. The reef is regularly visited by liveaboards travelling the Sanganeb-Angarosh corridor and is regarded as a consistently productive site, if less famous than its larger neighbours.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern Sudan Red Sea, ~1 mile southwest of Angarosh
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters

## Marine Life

Hammerhead shark schools, whitetip reef sharks, grey reef sharks and large tuna are reported here, with mantas seen occasionally. Soft corals cover the reef's walls and the north-end bommie.

## Dive Profile

Explore the eastern and western walls' cracks and caverns, and the small wreck on the northwest side, before finishing on the north-end plateau and its soft coral bommie. Current exposure varies by wall face — confirm direction with your guide before descending.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by liveaboard, typically paired with Angarosh on the same itinerary day.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check current direction before descent — Merlo's paired walls can have different exposure
- The small wreck on the northwest side is worth a dedicated pass with a dive light
- Wide-angle setup suits both the wall topography and any shark encounters

## Safety Considerations

Strong and variable currents are the primary hazard at the reef's exposed points. The wall drops well past recreational limits — monitor depth continuously. Carry a high-visibility SMB for the offshore pickup.
