## Merlo Reef

Merlo Reef is an offshore reef in Sudan's northern Red Sea, positioned close to Angarosh and visited on the same liveaboard itineraries. This dataset floors difficulty at Advanced as a destination-wide policy for this remote liveaboard region.

## Overview

Multiple independent sources place Merlo Reef roughly 1.7 nautical miles (about 2-3 km) southwest of Angarosh reef, with surrounding water depth of around 450 metres — this corrects an earlier, vaguer description in this dataset that placed it "~70 km north of Port Sudan in the same neighbourhood as Angarosh," which was imprecise and not well supported. dive-the-world.com describes it as an elongated reef with eastern and western walls featuring many cracks and caverns, plus a small plateau at the north end.

Two independent sources (dive-the-world.com and divescover.com) confirm that Merlo Reef holds the wreckage of the liveaboard dive boat "Freedom," which ran aground on the reef in 1999 after what is described as a navigation error; due to subsequent salvage work, mainly the hull and a sawn-off bow section remain. Two further independent sources (zentacle.com and wannadive.net) independently confirm a wreck on the northwest side of the reef, though neither names it — consistent with, but not an independent confirmation of, the "Freedom" identification.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern Sudan Red Sea, approximately 2-3 km (roughly 1.7 nautical miles) southwest of Angarosh reef
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall/reef dive with an incidental wreck (the "Freedom" liveaboard remains) on the northwest side
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (destination-wide floor; wannadive.net separately rates it Intermediate/CMAS**-AOW level in general recreational terms)
- **Maximum Depth**: Sources vary — wannadive.net cites an average depth of 21 m and maximum of 50 m; zentacle.com cites a shallower ~21 m (68.9 ft) maximum. This spread likely reflects the difference between the shallow plateau/crest and the surrounding wall, so treat 20-50 m as the range depending on where on the reef a dive is conducted.
- **Current**: wannadive.net describes current here as generally low (under 1 knot), which is milder than earlier drafts of this page implied
- **Best Time**: October to May (general Sudan liveaboard season)

## Marine Life

Giant moray eels are confirmed at this site by dive-the-world.com. The same source lists anemonefish, goatfish, snappers, unicornfish, whitetip reef sharks, Napoleon wrasse, and butterflyfish for Merlo Reef specifically. Zentacle.com and wannadive.net both independently note sharks and occasional manta ray sightings at this reef. Beyond these confirmed mentions, no further site-specific species claims are made here.

## Dive Profile

Descend onto the reef crest/small plateau at the north end and explore the eastern or western wall faces, which feature cracks and caverns per dive-the-world.com. The wreck of the Freedom lies on the northwest side of the reef and can be incorporated into the dive plan. Current here is generally reported as mild, but always confirm direction with the crew before descending. Finish with a safety stop on the shallower plateau.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by liveaboard dive boat, consistent with other sites in this itinerary. Entry is by giant stride or back roll, with pickup coordinated by the boat following an SMB-signalled safety stop.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The wreck of the liveaboard "Freedom" (aground 1999) lies on the reef's northwest side — ask your guide to include it in the dive plan if of interest
- Merlo is commonly paired with Angarosh on the same liveaboard day given the short distance between them
- Confirm current conditions with the crew before descent, even though current here is generally reported as mild

## Safety Considerations

Treat any exploration of the Freedom wreckage as requiring care — avoid penetration without proper wreck training, since salvage work has left sharp, cut sections (the bow was reportedly sawn off during salvage). Standard depth and buoyancy discipline applies on the wall sections given the depth range spread across sources (20-50 m).

## Photography

The eastern and western walls' cracks and caverns offer strong wide-angle reef subjects. The Freedom wreckage provides a wreck-photography subject distinct from the reef's natural features.
