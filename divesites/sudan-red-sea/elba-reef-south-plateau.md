## Elba Reef, South Plateau

The South Plateau is the shallow, accessible face of Elba Reef, a coral-block platform running roughly 5-14 metres and a popular night-diving location.

## Overview

Sources describe Elba Reef's south side hosting coral-block formations rising 1-2 metres from the sandy base, in shallow water well suited to extended bottom times and night diving. Liveaboard route descriptions place the south plateau within a broader 20-45 metre range for the reef overall, but this specific listing's shallow 5-14 metre profile is consistent with the reef-crest area closest to the surface. The plateau sits near the wreck of the SS Isola di Levanzo, which lies at the edge of Elba Reef's south side (see the separate Levanzo Wreck listing) — qualified wreck divers sometimes combine both on an extended profile.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Elba Reef southern face, Egypt-Sudan border (Halaib Triangle)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 meters

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine-life report beyond general reef fish and coral communities was found for the south plateau specifically. Given Elba Reef's very low diving pressure, expect typical Red Sea reef fish (wrasse, butterflyfish, parrotfish) in good numbers, but treat this as a general characterisation rather than a confirmed site-specific list.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the plateau at 5-10 metres and explore the coral-block formations at a relaxed pace; the shallow depth supports long bottom times. Divers wanting to extend toward the nearby Levanzo Wreck should plan that as a distinct, deeper profile beginning around 22 metres, in coordination with the dive guide.

## Entry and Exit

Reached by liveaboard; the sheltered southern aspect of the reef generally offers calmer entry and exit conditions than the more current-exposed north and west faces.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Good candidate for a night dive if your operator offers one at Elba Reef
- Use this as an introductory dive to Elba Reef before attempting the deeper north or west plateaus
- Discuss any combined Levanzo Wreck extension with your guide before entering the water

## Safety Considerations

This is the most forgiving profile at Elba Reef — shallow depth and generally moderate current. Fire coral is present on the coral-block formations; maintain good buoyancy. Standard buddy-diving and SMB practices apply.
