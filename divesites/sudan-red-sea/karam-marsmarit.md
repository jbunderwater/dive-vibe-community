## Karam Marsmarit

Karam Marsmarit (documented elsewhere as "Karam Masamirit") is an advanced wall dive in Sudan's remote southern Red Sea, roughly 2 nautical miles southeast of Masamirit Island in the Suakin Islands. The southeastern side features a deep drop-off wall where sharks and napoleon wrasse congregate.

## Overview

Karam Marsmarit is visited as part of specialized deep-south liveaboard itineraries alongside nearby sites such as Masamirit Island, Barra Musa Kebir, and the reefs of Keary, Pender, Logan, and Protector, Shaab Ambar, and the Umbria wreck. Multiple independent sources (a dive-site directory, a dive-site review aggregator, and a liveaboard operator) describe it as a drop-off/wall dive that circles the reef, with the southeastern face — the "Karam" side proper — dropping deepest and attracting the most pelagic activity.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Suakin Islands, ~2 nautical miles southeast of Masamirit Island, deep-south Sudan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters (sources describe the wall continuing well beyond recreational depths)

## Marine Life

The southeastern wall is reported as a shark aggregation area: grey reef sharks, silvertip sharks, and hammerheads are mentioned by multiple sources, along with occasional tiger sharks per one operator account. Large napoleon wrasse ("napoleons") are specifically called out sheltering along the deeper wall sections. Carangids (jacks/trevally) and tuna are also reported patrolling the drop-off. Claims of silky sharks, oceanic whitetips, or manta rays specific to this site were not found in the sources reviewed and have been removed — regional deep-south fauna may include these species, but no site-specific report was found.

## Dive Profile

The site is a drop-off dive around the island, with the southeastern ("Karam") side dropping deepest — sources describe an average depth around 40-50 metres on this face, well beyond recreational no-decompression limits, so divers should plan a conservative profile in the 20-40 metre range and treat the deeper wall as a below-the-plan zone to observe rather than descend into. A strong surface current is commonly reported at this site; drift-diving technique and close coordination with the boat crew are advisable.

## Entry and Exit

Access is only via specialized deep-south expedition liveaboards. Entry and exit are coordinated with the liveaboard crew given the strong currents reported at the site. A high-visibility surface marker buoy (SMB) is recommended for safety-stop signaling and boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Underwater camera recommended for sharks and napoleon wrasse encounters
- Strong surface currents are commonly reported — confirm conditions with your dive guide before descending
- Maintain depth discipline; the wall face descends well beyond recreational limits
- Book only with operators running Sudan's deep-south/extreme-south itineraries

## Safety Considerations

Strong currents and the depth of the wall face are the primary hazards at this site. The remote location means emergency response is significantly delayed compared to Sudan's northern dive sites. Maintain strict depth discipline, monitor air consumption, and always dive with an experienced buddy.
