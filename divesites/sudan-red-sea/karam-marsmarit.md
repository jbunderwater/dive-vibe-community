## Karam Marsmarit

Karam Marsmarit (also spelled Karam Masamirit) is a drop-off dive around a small island roughly 2 nautical miles southeast of Massamirit Island in the Suakin Islands, part of Sudan's southern liveaboard routes.

## Overview

The site is a full drop-off dive around the island, with strong surface current a regular feature. Zentacle lists a maximum depth of around 25 metres (82ft) and Intermediate difficulty; DiverProfile notes the site's southeast side has deeper walls where sharks and napoleon wrasse are found. Only two independent sources were located for this site during the 2026-07-02 QA pass, so it is not marked fully validated pending a third source.

**Note on validation:** Zentacle rates this site Intermediate at ~25m, but Sudan's diving is remote and liveaboard-only throughout, so this destination applies a minimum Advanced difficulty regardless of a given site's underwater profile.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~2nm southeast of Massamirit Island, Suakin Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters (sources note the southeast side has additional deeper wall sections)

## Marine Life

Sharks and napoleon wrasse are reported on the southeast wall's deeper sections. No further site-specific species list was confirmed.

## Dive Profile

The dive follows the drop-off around the island; the southeast side offers the deepest wall sections and the best chance of shark and napoleon encounters. Strong surface current should be expected and briefed before entry.

## Entry and Exit

Access is via liveaboards running Sudan's southern routes, from Shaab Jibna south to Daharat Abida.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Expect strong surface current — confirm entry/exit procedure with your dive guide
- The southeast wall is the priority section for shark and napoleon sightings

## Safety Considerations

Strong surface current is the primary hazard reported at this site. As with all of Sudan's southern route sites, this is remote diving with limited emergency support — dive within your demonstrated current-diving experience.
