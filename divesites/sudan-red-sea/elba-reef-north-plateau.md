## Elba Reef, North Plateau

The North Plateau is the deeper of Elba Reef's two main plateaus, beginning around 20 metres and dropping toward 45 metres at its outer edge, with substantial coral fan growth lining the rim.

## Overview

Elba Reef sits in the remote Halaib Triangle on the Egypt-Sudan border. Its north plateau is described by liveaboard operators as running roughly 20-45 metres, edged with large coral fan formations. The 33-metre depth recorded for this specific listing sits within that broader range and likely represents a recreational-limit cutoff point on a plateau that continues deeper. Because Elba Reef sees very little diving traffic, marine life here is reported as comparatively undisturbed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Elba Reef, Egypt-Sudan border (Halaib Triangle)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters

## Marine Life

Grey reef sharks are reported patrolling the plateau edge, with hammerheads appearing seasonally at depth. No source gives a fuller site-specific species list for the north plateau in particular; earlier claims of pygmy seahorses and other detailed macro life could not be corroborated and have been removed.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the plateau surface around 20 metres and work toward the outer rim, where the coral fan growth is most developed. Following the wall deeper toward 33-45 metres is an option for experienced divers only, with strict depth and gas planning given the drop continues well beyond recreational limits.

## Entry and Exit

Reached by liveaboard from the Elba Reef anchorage; entry and exit logistics depend on current conditions, which can be significant on this exposed plateau.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Set a firm maximum depth before entering the water — the plateau edge invites descent past comfortable limits
- Dive the shallower South Plateau first if this is your introduction to Elba Reef, to gauge conditions
- Bring a wide-angle setup for the coral fan formations along the rim

## Safety Considerations

The depth profile makes this an advanced dive; nitrogen narcosis is a real consideration below 30 metres. Currents at the plateau edge can be strong. The remote location limits emergency response — dive conservatively and within your demonstrated experience.
