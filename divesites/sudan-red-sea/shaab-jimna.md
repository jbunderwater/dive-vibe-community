## Shaab Jimna

Shaab Jimna (also spelled Shaab Jibna or Shaab Jumna) is a small offshore reef roughly 50km (about 27 nautical miles) from Port Sudan, known for some of the largest scalloped hammerhead schools in Sudan.

## Overview

Shaab Jimna is a compact reef marked by a beacon, offering separate east- and west-side dives depending on current. The reef features vertical walls with caves and crevices covered in soft corals, and it is regarded by Red Sea liveaboard operators as one of the last reliable havens for large hammerhead shark schools in the region. The diving season runs roughly February to May.

## Site Information

- **Location**: ~50km (27nm) offshore from Port Sudan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters

## Marine Life

Scalloped hammerhead sharks are the signature encounter, schooling in large numbers close to the reef. Reef fish shelter in the caves and crevices along the walls; other pelagic and schooling species pass through the current-exposed sides, though no specific additional species list beyond hammerheads was independently confirmed.

## Dive Profile

Dive plans typically split between the east and west sides depending on current direction. Walls, caves and crevices offer exploration between the surface and 28 metres; hammerhead sightings are most likely along the current-exposed wall faces.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by liveaboard, as part of central/southern Sudan itineraries. Entry and exit are coordinated with the boat crew given the offshore, current-exposed position.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Plan around current direction — the east/west side choice is made based on conditions at the time
- February-May is the reported season for the best hammerhead encounters
- Cave and crevice exploration should stay within your comfort zone without cave-specific training

## Safety Considerations

Strong offshore currents require careful buoyancy and current management. The cave and crevice features add entanglement and navigation risk — do not penetrate deeply without appropriate training. Always dive with a buddy and carry a high-visibility SMB for pickup.
