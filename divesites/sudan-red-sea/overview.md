## Sudan Red Sea

Pristine, lightly-dived Red Sea reefs, walls, and shark encounters, plus two celebrated wrecks — the WWII-era SS Umbria and the historic Cousteau habitat at Sha'ab Rumi — reached exclusively by liveaboard out of Port Sudan.

## Description

Sudan's Red Sea coast is one of the least-dived stretches of the Red Sea, accessible only by multi-day liveaboard safaris departing Port Sudan — there is no shore-based or day-boat diving infrastructure comparable to Egypt's resort towns. Water temperatures run roughly 24-30°C depending on season, and operators consistently report excellent visibility. The destination is built around a core of well-known named reefs and pinnacles (Sanganeb, Sha'ab Rumi, Angarosh, Merlo Reef), the WWII wreck of the SS Umbria near Port Sudan, and — for the small number of liveaboards that run "deep south" routes toward the Eritrean border — remote sites like Dahrat Abid and the Karam Masamirit/Suakin Islands area.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat/Liveaboard Diving**: All diving in Sudan is liveaboard-based; there is no day-trip or shore-diving infrastructure
- **Wreck Diving**: The SS Umbria (scuttled 1940) and the historic Cousteau habitat remains at Sha'ab Rumi are the destination's signature wreck/historic dives; the Toyota Wreck (Blue Belt) and SS Isola di Levanzo add more technical deep-wreck options, though current accessibility of some of these is disputed between sources — see individual site listings
- **Wall and Plateau Diving**: Sudan's offshore reefs typically rise from very deep water (400m+) to shallow plateaus, producing dramatic wall profiles at sites like Angarosh, Sanganeb, and Elba Reef
- **Shark Diving**: Grey reef, whitetip, hammerhead, and silvertip sharks are reported at multiple sites, particularly the exposed plateaus and pinnacles in the north

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Flights typically route through Port Sudan; check current airline routings before booking, as regional access can change.
- **Dive Operators**: Diving is organized exclusively through liveaboard operators running multi-day safaris; there is no independent resort-based diving.
- **Accommodation**: Diving accommodation is aboard the liveaboard vessel itself for the duration of the safari.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Visibility is consistently reported as excellent by operators; currents range from mild to strong depending on the site's exposure.
- **Marine Life**: Sharks are a defining feature of Sudan diving — grey reef, whitetip reef, hammerhead, and silvertip sharks are reported across multiple sites (see individual listings for site-specific confirmation). Manta rays are reported seasonally at specific sites such as the Mesharifa lagoon. Species claims below the destination level (e.g., whale sharks, oceanic whitetips, specific reef-fish lists) should be verified against individual site pages rather than assumed to apply destination-wide.
- **Conservation**: Sanganeb Marine National Park and the Dungonab Bay-Mukkawar Island Marine National Park were jointly inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2016.

## Additional Information

- **Currency**: Sudanese Pound (SDG)
- **Language**: Arabic, English
- **Safety**: This is remote, liveaboard-only diving with limited emergency support; confirm your operator's emergency and evacuation plan before booking. Be aware of strong currents at exposed sites, fire coral, and lionfish. All sites in this destination are rated Advanced or above, reflecting the remote, boat-only access even where underwater conditions are otherwise moderate.
