## Elba Reef East (Southplateau)

This is the shallow eastern extension of Elba Reef's south plateau, running roughly 5-16 metres as it transitions toward the more current-exposed eastern wall.

## Overview

No source reviewed names this exact eastern sub-section separately from the general Elba Reef south plateau description. Based on the broader south-plateau characterisation from liveaboard route operators — coral-block formations in shallow, sheltered water — this site is treated as a shallower, transitional extension of that same plateau rather than a distinct named feature. Its depth profile (5-16m) is consistent with the shallow shelf reported for the south side of Elba Reef.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Elba Reef southeast, Egypt-Sudan border (Halaib Triangle)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 meters

## Marine Life

No site-specific marine-life report was found for this exact location. General Elba Reef sourcing describes healthy coral growth and typical Red Sea reef fish in the shallow south-plateau area; treat that as the applicable regional character rather than a confirmed site-specific list.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef surface at 5-10 metres and work east along the coral field as depth gradually increases toward 16 metres near the transition to the eastern wall. The shallow depth supports long, relaxed bottom times.

## Entry and Exit

Reached by liveaboard; the sheltered southeastern aspect of the reef generally offers straightforward entry and exit.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Pair with the main South Plateau dive for a fuller shallow-Elba-Reef profile
- Good candidate for a slower macro-focused dive given the shallow, sheltered conditions

## Safety Considerations

This is one of Elba Reef's more forgiving profiles — shallow depth, moderate current. Fire coral is present on the reef surface; standard buddy-diving and SMB practices apply.
