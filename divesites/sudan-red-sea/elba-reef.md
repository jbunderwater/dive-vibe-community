## Elba Reef

Elba Reef is an advanced-diving triangle-shaped reef straddling the Egypt-Sudan border in the disputed Halaib Triangle, known for a north-south plateau structure, deep walls, and the SS Isola di Levanzo wreck. Despite the border ambiguity, it is overwhelmingly marketed and dived as part of Egypt "Deep South" liveaboard itineraries departing from Port Ghalib rather than as a Sudan-based Port Sudan itinerary.

## Overview

Elba Reef is a triangle-shaped reef with two well-documented plateau systems — north and south — each beginning around 20 meters and sloping to roughly 45 meters. Multiple independent liveaboard and dive-site sources place the reef geographically at the Egypt-Sudan border in the Halaib Triangle, but describe the country association inconsistently: some (Royal Evolution) call it Sudanese territory at the border with Egypt, while others (Sadko Safari Fleet, Divebooker) describe it as a remote southern Egyptian site extending into Sudanese waters. In practice, Elba Reef is consistently sold and dived as part of Egypt-based "Deep South" liveaboard routes departing from Port Ghalib (e.g., MS Royal Evolution, Sadko Safari Fleet boats), alongside Egyptian sites like St. John's, Elphinstone, and Rocky Island — not as part of Port Sudan-based Sudan liveaboard itineraries, which typically focus on more northerly Sudanese sites (Sanganeb, Sha'ab Rumi/Conshelf, Angarosh, the Umbria wreck). **This is flagged as a likely geographic/destination misclassification: this reef may fit better under an Egypt "Deep South" destination than under "Sudan Red Sea."** Due to its remoteness, the reef is reported by multiple sources as seeing minimal diver traffic, with pristine coral and grey reef shark and hammerhead sightings.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Egypt-Sudan border (Halaib Triangle). Country association is genuinely disputed in sources, but the reef is dived almost exclusively as part of Egypt-based liveaboard itineraries out of Port Ghalib.
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters (sources report the two plateaus ranging approximately 20-45 meters)

## The Two Documented Plateaus

Multiple independent sources (Royal Evolution, Seawolf Safari, Sadko Safari Fleet) consistently describe **two** named plateaus at Elba Reef — **North** and **South** — each starting around 20 meters and sloping to about 45 meters. No independent source was found documenting a separate "West Plateau" or "East (Southplateau)" as distinct named dive sites; those subdivisions in this destination's other pages appear to be informal directional/positional descriptions rather than independently documented sites (see the individual plateau pages for details).

**North Plateau**: 20-45 meters, with large coral fan formations lining the plateau edge.

**South Plateau**: 20-45 meters, with large pinnacles; the SS Isola di Levanzo wreck lies at the edge of this plateau's drop-off.

## Marine Life

Grey reef sharks and hammerheads are reported by multiple independent sources (Royal Evolution, Sadko Safari Fleet, Seawolf Safari) as regular sightings at Elba Reef, attributed to its remoteness and minimal diving pressure. Large schools of fusiliers, surgeonfish, and jacks are also reported. Sources describe abundant coral growth and generally low diving impact. Claims of manta rays, eagle rays, and other pelagic species beyond sharks and schooling fish were not independently corroborated and have been removed.
