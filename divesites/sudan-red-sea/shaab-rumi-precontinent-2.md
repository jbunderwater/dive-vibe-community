## Shaab Rumi, Precontinent 2

Precontinent II is the site of Jacques Cousteau's 1963 Conshelf Two experiment — the remains of a purpose-built underwater habitat in the lagoon of Sha'ab Rumi. Because this is an artificial structure rather than a natural reef feature, the site is classified here as a wreck/historic-structure dive rather than a wall.

## Overview

In 1963, Cousteau's team installed a starfish-shaped main habitat ("Starfish House") in the lagoon at Sha'ab Rumi, where oceanauts lived at roughly 10 metres depth for 30 days (Wikipedia gives the crew as six; some operator sources state five — not resolved to a single figure here). A separate "Deep Cabin" held two oceanauts at approximately 30 metres for about a week, testing longer-duration deep saturation stays. The project also included a submarine hangar that housed a two-person submersible nicknamed the "diving saucer," with two support ships on the surface supplying compressed air and logistics. The experiment was documented in Cousteau's 1964 film *World Without Sun*, which won the Academy Award for Best Documentary Feature. After the experiment ended, some structures were dismantled and removed, but others — including the submarine hangar and shark cages — remain in place today.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sha'ab Rumi lagoon, northern Sudan Red Sea
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters

## Marine Life

No source reviewed reports species specific to the Precontinent II structures themselves. The surrounding Sha'ab Rumi lagoon and south plateau (see the separate Shab Rumi, Plateau S listing) are known for shark activity, but that is a distinct dive site — treat this as a historical/structural dive rather than a wildlife-focused one.

## Dive Profile

The dive centres on exploring the remaining structures — the submarine hangar and shark cages are the features most consistently mentioned by sources — at a shallow, forgiving depth of around 10 metres. Reaching the separate "Deep Cabin" location requires a descent to around 30 metres, a materially different and more demanding profile than the shallow habitat area.

## Entry and Exit

Reached by liveaboard anchored in the Sha'ab Rumi lagoon; often paired on the same day with a dive on the South Plateau nearby.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Ask your dive guide to point out the submarine hangar and shark-cage remains — they are easy to miss without local knowledge
- Treat this as a historical/photography dive rather than a big-marine-life dive
- Combine with the South Plateau dive for a fuller Sha'ab Rumi day

## Safety Considerations

The shallow main habitat depth (~10m) is not technically demanding, but the site includes metal structures that have been underwater for over six decades — avoid contact with degraded or sharp material, and do not attempt to enter any enclosed structure without appropriate training.
