## Shaab Rumi, Precontinent 2

Shaab Rumi's Precontinent 2 site is one of the most historically significant dive sites in the world — the location of Jacques-Yves Cousteau's 1963 experimental underwater habitat, Conshelf II (Précontinent II). Remnants of that pioneering project still rest on the sandy bottom inside Sha'ab Rumi's lagoon, making this a pilgrimage site for divers interested in the history of underwater exploration. Note: this is treated as an Advanced-minimum site in this dataset because Sudan's remote liveaboard-only Red Sea destinations are floored at Advanced difficulty; the lagoon itself is a relatively gentle, sheltered dive that some operators would otherwise class as Intermediate.

## Overview

In 1963, Jacques-Yves Cousteau's team lived and worked underwater at Sha'ab Rumi, roughly 30-48 km from Port Sudan (sources vary on exact distance), as part of the Conshelf II programme. Sources consistently describe a team living at 10 metres in a starfish-shaped main house ("Starfish House") for about a month; source counts of the team size vary (five, six, and eight-member figures all appear across accounts, so the exact number of oceanauts is not firmly established here). A separate "Deep Cabin" at roughly 27-30 metres housed two divers for about a week. The complex also included a submarine hangar (nicknamed "Sea Urchin" by some divers) that stored a two-person "diving saucer" submersible, plus a tool shed and shark/fish cages.

According to a ScubaBoard diver discussion on the site's remains, most of the Starfish House and Deep Cabin equipment — living quarters, labs, and related gear — was salvaged and removed after the experiment ended, which is why divers today do not find the iconic starfish-shaped structure itself underwater. What remains on the seabed is the submarine hangar (still substantially intact and enterable with care), along with the tool shed and shark/fish cages, all now heavily encrusted with coral and sponge growth. Divers should be aware before visiting that the famous Starfish House is not intact on the bottom — only its anchor points and surrounding remains.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sha'ab Rumi lagoon, northern Sudan Red Sea, roughly 30-48 km from Port Sudan (sources differ on precise distance)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (destination-wide floor; the lagoon itself is often rated Intermediate in general dive literature)
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters (the Deep Cabin remains lie somewhat deeper, around 27-30 meters per historical accounts)
- **Current**: Moderate
- **Best Time**: October to May

## The Conshelf II Remains

The submarine hangar is the best-preserved and most recognizable structure, still resting on the seafloor and reportedly enterable by divers with appropriate training — treat any wreck-style penetration as requiring proper training and equipment, not something to attempt casually. Nearby, encrusted remains of the tool shed and the shark/fish cages are visible. The main "Starfish House" living quarters are not present as an intact structure on the seabed; accounts indicate this and other valuable equipment were removed after the 1963 experiment concluded, rather than left in place.

## Marine Life

Two independent sources (dive-the-world.com and bluewaterdivetravel.com) confirm grey reef sharks, silky sharks, and silvertip sharks around Sha'ab Rumi, with hammerhead sharks reported November through April. Both sources also mention barracuda schools. Beyond these, no site-specific confirmation was found distinguishing marine life at the Precontinent II lagoon structures themselves (as opposed to the broader Sha'ab Rumi reef) — divers should expect the general reef fauna typical of a sheltered Sudanese lagoon, but no site-specific reports for fish assemblages inside the hangar or on the habitat remains were located.

## Dive Profile

Enter the lagoon and descend toward the sandy bottom to locate the submarine hangar and surrounding tool shed/cage remains, at roughly 9-20 metres depending on structure. Divers with appropriate training and planning may continue to the deeper Deep Cabin remains around 27-30 metres. Finish with a circuit of the lagoon before ascending to a safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by liveaboard dive boat anchored at Sha'ab Rumi. Entry is by giant stride or back roll into the lagoon. The lagoon's relatively protected waters make entry and exit straightforward in most conditions. Deploy an SMB on ascent for liveaboard pickup coordination.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Starfish House itself is gone — don't expect to find the iconic five-armed structure; the hangar, tool shed, and cages are what remain
- Ask your dive guide to help identify remaining Conshelf II components — local knowledge is valuable given how encrusted the structures now are
- Respect the historical structures — nothing should be touched or removed
- Combine this dive with the South Plateau for a fuller Sha'ab Rumi itinerary

## Safety Considerations

Avoid contact with encrusted metal structures — fire coral and sharp edges are present. Do not enter confined areas within the hangar without proper wreck/overhead training. If descending toward the Deep Cabin remains at 27-30 metres, plan conservatively and monitor depth and gas closely.

## Photography

The submarine hangar is the centerpiece photographic subject, heavily encrusted with coral and sponge growth after six decades underwater. Wide-angle shots work well for the structure itself.
