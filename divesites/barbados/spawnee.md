# Spawnee

A popular shallow reef on Barbados's northwest coast, favoured as a second dive for its relaxed conditions and the opportunity to observe species — including Caribbean reef squid — not commonly seen on deeper sites.

## Overview

Spawnee is a shallow reef site on Barbados's northwest coast near Weston and Gibbes Beach. Dive Hightide lists it as a "second dive option allowing inexperienced divers to see deep-water species" — a deliberately accessible site suited to all experience levels including beginners. It sits within the same offshore barrier reef system as Tropicana and Whitegates to the south. The reef ranges from approximately 12 to 21 metres. Light currents are typical. Visibility is 15–30 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Northwest coast, offshore from Weston/Gibbes Beach
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 metres
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest December to April

## Marine Life

Spawnee is particularly noted for its resident population of Caribbean reef squid, which hover in small groups above the reef and are approachable for patient divers. Trumpetfish are a common sight, often hanging vertically among gorgonians. Schools of tropical fish populate the reef. Moray eels occupy reef crevices. No further site-specific marine life reports were found; the general reef species of Barbados's north coast are expected throughout.

## Dive Profile

The reef ranges from about 12 to 21 metres — ideal for a comfortable second dive after a deeper morning site. The gentle profile allows extended bottom time. Follow the reef contours and observe marine life at a relaxed pace.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from local operators. Grouped with nearby sites like Tropicana and Whitegates for two-tank dive trips from Speightstown or Holetown.

## Tips and Recommendations

Approach the reef squid slowly — they are curious but will retreat if startled. Often scheduled as the second dive on a two-tank trip with Maycocks Bay or Tropicana. Excellent choice for newly certified divers.

## Safety Considerations

Gentle conditions make this a low-stress dive. Light currents typical. Standard boat dive safety applies — carry an SMB and monitor air supply.
