# Whitegates

A relaxed barrier reef dive south of Tropicana, noted for large hawksbill turtles grazing the reef and dense schools of blue tangs, sergeant majors, and creole wrasse filling the coral interior.

## Overview

Whitegates sits on the same offshore barrier reef system as Tropicana, just to its south along Barbados's west coast. Dive Hightide characterises it as an offshore barrier reef offering a "relaxed dive." Active Caribbean Travel describes it as an "aquarium-like" experience — a reference to the density of schooling tropical fish that fill the reef interior. The site depth profile is 15–24 metres, similar to Tropicana. Hawksbill turtles are regularly noted by operators as a reliable encounter at this site. Visibility averages 20–30 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C.

## Site Information
- **Location**: West coast, offshore barrier reef south of Tropicana
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: December to April (calmest seas)

## Marine Life

Whitegates is particularly noted for its hawksbill turtles, which are reliably encountered grazing on the reef. Dense schools of tropicals — blue tangs, sergeant majors, creole wrasse — create the "aquarium" atmosphere described by operators. Barracuda and blackjacks patrol the reef edge. Parrotfish, angelfish, and butterflyfish are common throughout. No site-specific marine life reports beyond these sources were found.

## Dive Profile

A comfortable reef dive from 15 to 24 metres. Work along the reef edge to spot larger pelagic species, then explore the interior coral gardens for the schooling fish and turtle encounters. The gentle profile allows generous bottom time.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from west coast operators. Frequently paired with Tropicana or Spawnee for two-tank trips.

## Tips and Recommendations

Hawksbill turtles here are accustomed to divers — maintain the Barbados Marine Trust's 10-foot buffer but the turtles often remain close. Great site for wide-angle photography of schooling fish. Similar depth to Tropicana, so plan air management accordingly for two-tank trips.

## Safety Considerations

Standard barrier reef dive safety. Moderate currents possible. Maintain awareness of depth at the reef edge. Carry an SMB for boat pickup.
