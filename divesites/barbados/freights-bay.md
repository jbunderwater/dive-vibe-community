## Freights Bay

Freights Bay is a scenic bay on the south coast of Barbados, known primarily for its surfing conditions but also offering opportunities for shore diving along its reef formations. The site features a sandy bottom with scattered coral patches and reef structures that support diverse marine life typical of Barbados' south coast.

## Overview

Located on the south coast of Barbados, Freights Bay offers a relatively protected diving environment with easy shore access. While the bay is more famous for its consistent surf breaks, the underwater terrain features a combination of sandy bottom areas and coral reef formations that create habitat for various Caribbean marine species. The site is suitable for beginner divers due to its shallow depths and generally calm conditions, though surfers in the area should be considered when planning entry and exit.

The reef structure extends from the shore, with depths ranging from shallow areas near the beach to approximately 15 meters further out. The site's proximity to the south coast's main diving areas makes it accessible, though it may be less frequently dived than more established sites in Carlisle Bay or along the west coast.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South coast of Barbados, near Oistins
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry from the beach
- **Site Type**: Fringing reef with sandy patches
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 meters (varies with conditions)
- **Current**: Light to moderate, can be affected by surf conditions
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with calmest conditions typically during dry season (December to May)

## Marine Life

Divers at Freights Bay can expect to encounter typical Caribbean reef fish including parrotfish, angelfish, damselfish, and various species of wrasses. The sandy areas may reveal garden eels, flounders, and various bottom-dwelling species. The coral formations provide habitat for small reef inhabitants including cleaner shrimp, arrow crabs, and various species of gobies and blennies. Occasional sightings of sea turtles, rays, and barracuda are possible, particularly in deeper areas or when venturing further from shore.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive at Freights Bay begins with a shore entry, swimming out to reach the main reef areas. Depths are generally shallow, ranging from 3-15 meters, making this an excellent site for extended bottom times and relaxed exploration. Divers typically follow the reef line, exploring coral formations and sandy areas. Due to the site's popularity with surfers, it's advisable to plan dives during times when surf activity is minimal, typically early morning or late afternoon.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from the sandy beach area. Divers should be aware of surf conditions and time their entry between wave sets. The sandy bottom makes for relatively easy entry, though care should be taken with surfboards and other watercraft in the area. Exit follows the same route, with attention to surf conditions and other water users. Surface conditions can vary depending on weather and surf activity.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This site is ideal for beginner divers and those looking for relaxed, shallow diving

## Safety Considerations

The primary safety consideration at Freights Bay is the presence of surfers and potential boat traffic. Divers should maintain proper surface signaling and be aware of their surroundings when entering and exiting. Currents can vary with weather conditions, and surf can create challenging entry/exit conditions. Always dive with a buddy and follow standard safety protocols. Be mindful of the reef and maintain proper buoyancy to avoid damaging coral formations.

## Photography

The site offers good opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The combination of sandy areas and coral formations provides varied compositions. Visibility is typically good, though can be affected by surf activity. The shallow depths allow for extended photography sessions with good natural light.
