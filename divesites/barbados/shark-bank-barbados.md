## Shark Bank Barbados

Shark Bank is an advanced deep coral reef located offshore from Barbados, reaching depths of 43 meters and offering excellent opportunities for deep diving, pelagic encounters, and dramatic coral formations.

## Overview

Shark Bank is an advanced deep reef dive situated further offshore from the main island of Barbados. The site is characterized by deep coral formations reaching maximum depths of 43 meters (135-145 feet), making it suitable only for experienced advanced divers. The reef's depth and offshore location create more challenging diving conditions with stronger currents. The reward for experienced divers is dramatic deep coral structures, opportunities to encounter larger pelagic species including sharks, and an exciting deep diving experience. Visibility is typically excellent, but divers must plan carefully for nitrogen narcosis and depth-related factors.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Offshore southeast of Barbados
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 43 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-35 meters (80-115 feet) - excellent at depth
- **Current**: Moderate to strong, offshore conditions
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season) - calmest sea state

## Marine Life

Shark Bank's depth and offshore location attract larger pelagic species. Sea turtles, particularly green and hawksbill turtles, are regularly sighted. Rays including southern stingrays, eagle rays, and spotted eagle rays are common. Sharks—both reef sharks and larger pelagic species—are frequently encountered, giving the site its name. Barracuda schools patrol the deep reef. Groupers, snappers, and various reef fish species inhabit the coral structures. Moray eels and lobsters shelter in the deep coral formations. The dramatic deep coral structures support a specialized deep-reef ecosystem distinct from shallower sites.

## Dive Profile

This is a deep dive suitable only for advanced divers with significant experience. Most dives focus on the reef structure and pelagic encounters between 30-43 meters. Divers typically descend quickly to the deep reef, spend 10-15 minutes at maximum depth due to no-decompression limits, observe the dramatic coral formations and pelagic life, then ascend gradually with a required safety stop. The strong current may require drift diving techniques. Nitrogen narcosis becomes noticeable at this depth; divers must monitor themselves and dive partners carefully. Most recreational divers will benefit from using enriched air (nitrox) or consider Technical Diving certification for extended bottom times.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Safety Considerations

This is an advanced deep dive requiring Deep Diver or Advanced Open Water certification with significant experience. Nitrogen narcosis becomes significant at 40+ meters—monitor yourself and buddy continuously. The offshore location and strong currents present navigation challenges; stay close to your dive buddy and maintain excellent buoyancy control. Rapid ascent from depth is dangerous; descend and ascend slowly on the moored reference line. A mandatory safety stop at 5 meters is essential. Carry an alternate air source and a redundant depth gauge. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during ascent. The boat captain will track your dive closely—coordinate all procedures before descent. This site is not recommended for divers anxious about deep diving or strong currents. Book only with dive operators experienced with this specific site.
