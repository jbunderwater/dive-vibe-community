# Maycocks Bay

The northernmost dive site on Barbados's west coast, featuring a "jigsaw puzzle" pattern of large coral formations separated by corridors of white sand, with moderate to strong currents making it suitable for experienced divers.

## Overview

Maycocks Bay is located off the island's northwest coast near Speightstown and is among the most northerly regularly dived sites on Barbados's west coast. Dive Hightide describes the site as featuring "large coral reefs separated by corridors of white sand" — the distinctively segmented reef topography that makes Maycocks visually striking. Scuba Diving Earth describes a similar "jigsaw puzzle" pattern. The reef slopes from around 15 metres at the top to approximately 38–40 metres on the deeper edges. Strong currents are characteristic of this exposed northern position, making it more demanding than the central west coast sites. Most of the marine life activity occurs between 15–25 metres where the reef structure is densest.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Northwest coast, off Maycocks Bay near Speightstown
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 38 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 metres
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: December to April (calmest seas)

## Marine Life

The distinctive finger reef formations attract varied marine life. Dive Hightide notes large schools of Bermuda chub patrolling the sand corridors, with barracuda along the reef edges. Southern stingrays and eagle rays glide over the sandy channels between reef fingers. Parrot fish are present. Scuba Diving Earth cites blue-headed wrasse, creole wrasse, hogfish, rays, and turtles at the reef system. Hawksbill turtles are commonly reported by operators.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top at approximately 15 metres and explore the finger formations, working through the sand corridors between them. Most activity occurs between 15–25 metres where the reef is densest. The deeper edges slope to 38 metres. Plan ascent carefully given the depth and current.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Speightstown-based operators including Reefers & Wreckers. Longer boat ride from operators based further south. Always carry an SMB — essential at this exposed northern site.

## Tips and Recommendations

Best dived on calm days when currents are manageable. The sand corridors are the unique feature — work them slowly for ray sightings. Often paired with Spawnee or Brightledge as a two-tank dive. Not recommended for inexperienced divers due to current exposure.

## Safety Considerations

Currents can be strong and unpredictable at this exposed northern site. Depth reaches recreational limits on the deeper sections — monitor air supply carefully. Always carry an SMB for drift scenarios.
