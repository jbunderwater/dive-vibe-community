# Stavronikita Wreck

Barbados's most celebrated wreck dive — a 111-metre Greek freighter deliberately sunk in 1978 as the Caribbean's first purpose-built artificial reef wreck, now heavily encrusted and home to one of the island's most impressive fish communities.

## Overview

The Stavronikita ("Stav") was a 111-metre Greek cargo ship that was deliberately sunk on 22 November 1978 off Barbados's southwest coast in one of the Caribbean's earliest and most successful artificial reef programmes. The vessel was stripped and prepared for sinking and now lies at a maximum depth of 40 metres on the port side, with the shallowest accessible sections of the superstructure at around 10–12 metres. The Stav has been underwater for nearly five decades and the encrustation reflects this — the hull is carpeted with black coral, orange and purple sponges, and dense gorgonian sea fans. The site is regarded as Barbados's finest wreck dive and one of the premier intermediate-to-advanced wreck dives in the eastern Caribbean. The clear west coast water delivers visibility of 20–30 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C.

## Marine Life

The Stav supports a rich community accumulated over nearly fifty years of colonisation. The superstructure and hull surfaces are dense with black coral (including significant stands on the deeper sections), orange cup corals, and encrusting sponges. Large schools of glassy sweepers and soldierfish pack the interior spaces. Caribbean reef sharks patrol the open water around the wreck. Green morays and spotted morays occupy hull crevices throughout. Hawksbill turtles graze the sponge-covered hull. Large groupers have established territory in the engine room and deeper sections.

## Dive Profile

Descend the mooring line to the upper superstructure at 10–12 metres. Explore the mast and upper deck sections first before descending the hull face to mid-depth at 20–25 metres. A second descent to the deeper 30–40 metre sections can be undertaken for experienced divers with sufficient bottom time available. NDL management is critical at the deeper sections. Ascend via the mooring line.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from west coast Barbados operators — the majority of Bridgetown-area dive centres include the Stavronikita on their regular program. Entry by backward roll or giant stride. Permanent moorings at the wreck. DSMB required.

## Tips and Recommendations

The Stav rewards multiple dives — the scale of the 111-metre vessel means each dive can focus on a different section. Plan the deepest section first and ascend progressively. The upper mast in 10–12 metres is the most photogenic section and can be covered in good natural light. Carry a torch for the interior spaces and the darker hull sections.

## Safety Considerations

Maximum depth of 40 metres requires advanced dive experience and careful NDL management. Do not penetrate enclosed interior spaces without proper wreck training. A torch, reel, and silt-consciousness are required for interior exploration. DSMB required for the ascent. This is an advanced dive — minimum 50 logged dives recommended.

## Photography

The Stavronikita is Barbados's premier underwater photography subject. The upper mast structure in natural light at 12 metres is the classic composition — wide-angle shots of the mast silhouetted against the blue water. The dense encrustation on the hull at 15–25 metres with schools of fish creates compelling close-focus wide-angle images. A strobe is essential for the deeper and interior sections.
