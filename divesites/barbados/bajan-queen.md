# Bajan Queen

A former Barbadian tugboat converted into a party cruiser, sunk on 19 May 2002 in Carlisle Bay Marine Park — lying at 11 metres with the deck, washrooms, hold, and captain's bridge all accessible.

## Overview

The Bajan Queen began her working life as the Pelican — Barbados's first tugboat, serving the harbour from the 1960s. She was later converted to a party cruise vessel operating out of Bridgetown. On 19 May 2002 she was deliberately sunk in Carlisle Bay Marine Park to add to the bay's growing artificial reef wreck cluster. The 120-foot vessel rests on the sandy bottom at 11 metres (35 feet), with her deck accessible at 4–6 metres. She is one of the larger wrecks in Carlisle Bay and features intact interior spaces including the captain's bridge, hold, and washrooms. Carlisle Bay's sheltered west coast conditions give consistent visibility of 15–20 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C year-round.

## Marine Life

The Bajan Queen's structure hosts a productive community. Schools of Atlantic spadefish congregate above the wreck. Large barrel sponges have established on the hull. Hawksbill turtles are reliably present in Carlisle Bay and visit this wreck. Schools of French grunts and sergeant majors occupy the deck and superstructure. Caribbean reef octopus are found in hull exterior crevices. Southern stingrays rest on the surrounding sandy bottom.

## Dive Profile

Boat descent to the wreck top at 4–6 metres and circumnavigation at deck level before dropping to the sandy base at 11 metres. The captain's bridge, hold, and accessible interior spaces reward careful exploration. The full vessel can be explored in a single dive. Combine with adjacent bay wrecks on a multi-wreck boat tour for the best Carlisle Bay experience.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Carlisle Bay operators. Giant stride or backward roll entry. DSMB recommended.

## Tips and Recommendations

The Bajan Queen is best experienced as part of a multi-wreck Carlisle Bay tour — the bay's cluster (Berwyn, Ce-Trek, Eilon, and the Stavronikita further offshore) together offer the full range of Barbados wreck diving. The intact interior spaces (hold, bridge, washrooms) reward exploration with a torch. Good site for night diving — the resident fish community is active after dark.

## Safety Considerations

Straightforward at 11 metres in calm bay conditions. Fire coral is present. DSMB required. Be alert for boat traffic in the active Carlisle Bay area. Do not enter fully enclosed spaces without wreck diving training.

## Photography

The accessible depth and calm bay conditions make the Bajan Queen suitable for natural-light photography. The intact superstructure, barrel sponges, spadefish schools, and turtle encounters are the main subjects.
