## Cala Sidoti

Cala Sidoti is a reef dive site in Ustica, Europe.

## Overview

Cala Sidoti is a sheltered black-sand cove on Ustica's northwestern shore, sitting inside Zone A of Italy's oldest Marine Protected Area where fishing has been banned since 1986. The shallow reef barely exceeds 10 metres, making it one of the island's best shore entries for snorkelers and newer divers, with crystal-clear water and permanent populations of brown grouper, wrasse, and damselfish that have lost all fear of divers. It also serves as an excellent night dive, where octopuses, cuttlefish, and sea hares emerge to hunt across the volcanic rubble in calm, protected conditions.

## Marine Life

Divers at this site can expect to encounter groupers, moray eels, octopus, barracuda, sea bream, amberjack, nudibranchs, seahorses. Additional species commonly sighted include posidonia seagrass, red coral, sea fans, sponges.
