## Grotta dei Gamberi

Grotta dei Gamberi (Shrimps Cave) is Ustica's most famous and beautiful cave dive, named for its abundant shrimp colonies at significant depths.

## Overview

Grotta dei Gamberi (Shrimps Cave) is one of the most famous and beautiful dives in Ustica, featuring a spectacular colony of shrimps at significant depths. The dive features exceptional deep cave penetration reaching approximately 37-42 meters, making it one of Ustica's most challenging and rewarding technical dives. Hundreds of small shrimp with antennae move to escape the light from divers' torches, creating a unique spectacle rarely seen in other Mediterranean caves.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ustica, Mediterranean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 37 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Good - usually 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The cave is named for its abundant shrimp population. Hundreds of small shrimps move to escape diver lights at around 37 meters depth. Beyond shrimp, divers witness cuttlefish, octopus, moray eels, and significant numbers of red shrimps and scorpionfish in the cave environment.

## Dive Profile

This is a serious deep cave dive. The descent reaches 37-42 meters depth to reach the shrimp colony. Only very experienced divers with Deep Diver certification should attempt this dive. Careful air consumption management is essential due to significant depth. The spectacular shrimp encounter occurs in the deepest sections where hundreds of small creatures react to dive light. Allow adequate air reserves for the ascent and safety decompression.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. This is an advanced deep cave dive requiring strict adherence to dive plan and careful depth management. Descent to the shrimp colony at 37+ meters requires experience and proper training. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Only for very experienced divers with Deep Diver certification
- Thorough briefing from dive operator absolutely essential
- Bring quality dive light to illuminate the shrimp colony and cave interior
- Macro camera recommended for shrimp photography
- Expect nitrogen narcosis effects at this depth
- Perfect buoyancy control essential in confined cave passages
- Excellent opportunity to observe unique deep Mediterranean cave fauna

## Safety Considerations

This is an advanced technical cave dive that requires strict adherence to dive planning and depth limits. Only attempt if you hold Deep Diver certification and have significant deep diving experience. Be aware of nitrogen narcosis effects at 37+ meter depths. The confined cave passages and significant depth make this a serious undertaking. Always dive with a knowledgeable buddy and monitor air consumption carefully. Carry a safety sausage (SMB) and maintain constant communication with your dive partner.

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
