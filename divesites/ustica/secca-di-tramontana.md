## Secca di Tramontana

Secca di Tramontana is a deep technical pinnacle approximately 2 miles northwest of Ustica in the Tyrrhenian Sea.

## Overview

Secca di Tramontana is an extreme deep-water pinnacle requiring technical diving skills. Unlike the recreational sites around Ustica itself, this offshore shoal has its shallowest cap at approximately 60 metres depth — well below recreational diving limits — with the northwest face plummeting to over 80 metres and the outer ledge reaching 110 metres with a DPV (diver propulsion vehicle). Multiple sources classify it as "very challenging" and among the most demanding sites in the Ustica area.

This site is not suitable for recreational open water diving. Technical certification (minimum Tec 40 or equivalent), redundant equipment, and dive planning at depth are required.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ustica, Europe (~2 miles northwest of the island)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 80 meters
