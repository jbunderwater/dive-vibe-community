## Ustica

Volcanic Mediterranean island 60 km north of Palermo, home to Italy's first marine reserve since 1986, with dramatic lava formations, underwater caves, archaeological sites, and over 30 years of protected biodiversity.

## Description

Ustica's database documents 13 dive sites around the island, with depths ranging from 10 to 38 meters. As the emerging summit of a submarine volcano, Ustica offers an unusual variety of underwater terrain for the Mediterranean: caves (4 sites), walls (3 sites), pinnacles (2 sites), reefs (3 sites), and a seamount. The island's Marine Protected Area -- Italy's oldest -- has produced thriving marine ecosystems after decades of protection, and boat rides to all dive sites take under 15 minutes from the port.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: 11 sites accessed by boat from the island's small harbour, with short transit times around the 3.5 km-wide island
- **Shore Diving**: 2 shore-accessible sites for independent exploration
- **Cave Diving**: 4 cave sites including Grotta dei Gamberi (Shrimp Cave) with colonies of Plesionika narval shrimp, and Grotta della Pastizza, considered one of the best night dive locations on the island
- **Wall Diving**: 3 wall dives along volcanic drop-offs, including sites at Punta dell'Arpa with its red gorgonian forest
- **Pinnacle Diving**: 2 pinnacle sites including Secca della Colombara, where clouds of barracuda and pelagic fish gather around the offshore reef
- **Archaeological Diving**: Two marked underwater archaeological itineraries at Punta Omo Morto and Spalmatore, with ancient anchors, pottery, and artifacts from antiquity

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Fly to Palermo Falcone-Borsellino Airport (PMO). From there, take the Prestia e Comande bus (approximately 45 minutes) or taxi (approximately 30 minutes, around 50 EUR) to the Port of Palermo at Banchina Crispi. Hydrofoils (Liberty Lines) take approximately 1 hour 15 minutes; car ferries (Siremar) take approximately 2 hours 45 minutes. Multiple daily departures in summer; reduced schedule in winter
- **Dive Operators**: Several centres operate on the island including Orca Diving Ustica (PADI), Ustica Diving / Mare Nostrum, Blue Diving Ustica, Evasioni Blu Diving, and Lustrica Diving. All operate from or near the main harbour
- **Accommodation**: Over 46 hotels and guesthouses available on the island, concentrated around the main village. Book well in advance for July-August high season. Shoulder season (May-June, September-October) offers lower prices and excellent diving conditions
- **Transportation**: The island is small enough to explore on foot or by scooter rental. No car rental is necessary
- **Facilities**: Dive centres offer full equipment rental, guided dives, PADI courses, and Nitrox at select operators

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperatures range from 15 to 26 degrees C, with summer highs of 24-26 degrees C (June-September) and winter lows around 15 degrees C. Visibility is generally excellent, often exceeding 30 meters in summer. Mild currents at most sites, though some exposed points can have moderate flow
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Over 30 years of marine reserve protection have produced thriving populations. Large groupers are abundant, particularly in cave entrances. Mediterranean barracuda form large schools at offshore sites like Secca della Colombara. Other residents include moray eels, dentex, amberjack, sea bream, octopus, nudibranchs, red gorgonians, and yellow sponge colonies on the volcanic walls. The volcanic substrate creates unique habitat niches not found on typical limestone Mediterranean coastlines
- **Conservation**: The Marine Protected Area of Ustica, established in 1986, covers approximately 15 km of coastline divided into three zones: Zone A (60 hectares, no-take), Zone B (approximately 8,000 hectares, general reserve), and Zone C (approximately 8,000 hectares, partial reserve). Diving is permitted in Zones B and C with licensed operators. Anchoring on the seabed is prohibited at dive sites

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: May to October, with peak conditions June through September when water temperatures reach 24-26 degrees C and visibility is at its best. The diving season effectively runs mid-April to late October, as winter ferry schedules are unreliable due to weather
- **Currency**: Euro (EUR)
- **Language**: Italian. Some English spoken at dive centres and tourist establishments
- **Safety**: Mild currents at exposed sites require attention to briefings. Some cave dives require appropriate certification. The volcanic terrain includes sharp rock formations -- gloves are useful. Hydrofoil crossings can be cancelled in rough weather, particularly outside summer months, so allow schedule flexibility
