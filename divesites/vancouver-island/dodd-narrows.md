## Dodd Narrows

Dodd Narrows is an advanced tidal drift dive site between Cedar and Mudge Islands near Nanaimo. The narrow passage is known for extreme tidal currents that can exceed 9 knots, making this a challenging current dive requiring experienced guides and precise timing.

## Overview

Dodd Narrows is a tidal drift site where most of the water flow from two inlets must pass through a narrow channel. Currents typically run between 5-9 knots through the narrows. Most divers either live boat drop (current drift) or time dives to enter at slack tide in the small bay and ride the current through the narrows, exiting when the current changes direction. This is an advanced-only site requiring experienced guidance.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Between Cedar and Mudge Islands near Nanaimo, southern Vancouver Island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-15 meters
- **Current**: Extreme tidal current 5-9 knots average
- **Best Time**: Summer and early fall

## Marine Life

The strong currents create habitat diversity. Divers encounter rockfish, lingcod, starfish, anemones, nudibranchs, and other invertebrates. The narrow passage and current mix create interesting ecosystems along the narrows.

## Dive Profile

Two diving approaches exist: (1) Live boat drop into the current and drift through the narrows, exploring the channel sides; or (2) Anchor in a small bay at the south entrance (on the Cedar side) and time entry during slack tide, riding the current through the narrows, then returning when the current reverses. The dive typically lasts 20-30 minutes depending on approach. Depth ranges from surface to 21 meters.

## Entry and Exit

For live drops: Enter via giant stride directly into current and descend rapidly to avoid turbulent surface flows. For slack-tide approaches: Enter from anchored boat at the narrows entrance. Deploy SMB during ascent for boat pickup in current approaches. Timing is critical — exit before the current exceeds navigable speed or before the dive team loses visual contact.

## Tips and Recommendations

- MUST use experienced guide/charter with Dodd Narrows experience
- Confirm current timing and direction before dive
- Excellent navigation and current awareness essential
- Bring high-capacity air supplies (expect heavier consumption)
- Carry redundant lights and signaling devices
- Conservative bottom time approach to ensure air reserves
- Cold water (8-12°C) requires proper thermal protection
- Plan dives during daylight only

## Safety Considerations

This is an advanced current dive. Requirements:
- Advanced Open Water certification minimum
- Extensive current diving experience
- Demonstrated excellent buoyancy and finning technique
- Depth control discipline (stay above 21m)
- Cold-water diving experience essential (8-12°C)
- Proper thermal protection (7mm+ wetsuit or drysuit)
- Redundant signaling systems
- Always dive with experienced current-diver buddy
- Abort if current exceeds safe navigable speed
- Use SMB for all ascents

Strong currents (5-9 knots) make this unsafe for less-experienced divers. Always use current forecasts and experienced guides.

## Photography

Current makes detailed photography challenging. Wide-angle shots during brief slack periods and macro subjects on narrows walls are best. Bring stabilized equipment.
