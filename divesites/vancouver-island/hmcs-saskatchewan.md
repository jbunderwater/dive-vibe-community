## HMCS Saskatchewan

The HMCS Saskatchewan is a Mackenzie-class destroyer that was intentionally sunk in 1997 to create an artificial reef near Nanaimo. At 366 feet in length, she is one of the world's premier wreck dives and has been specifically prepared for safe wreck penetration.

## Overview

HMCS Saskatchewan rests near Snake Island in Nanaimo with her deck at approximately 24-30 meters and surrounding structures creating a world-class artificial reef. The wreck has been extensively colonized by marine life and features numerous entry and exit points for trained wreck divers. The ship's substantial size and strategic placement make it a destination dive site for advanced divers in British Columbia.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Snake Island, Nanaimo, British Columbia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5-15 meters
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: Summer and early fall

## Marine Life

The wreck is heavily colonized with marine life. Plumose anemones form dense clusters that serve as nurseries for juvenile fish. Other residents include cabezon, lingcod, rockfish, cloud sponges, and grunt sculpins. The entire wreck structure is covered in invertebrate life, creating both a thriving ecosystem and excellent photography opportunities.

## Dive Profile

Dives typically begin with a descent to the top of the wreck at 24-30 meters. Most dives focus on exterior exploration and accessible penetration points, with 25-30 minutes of bottom time recommended. The wreck sits upright on the seafloor, providing multiple access points. Plan your dive to allow at least 10 minutes for your safety stop at 5 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by dive boat from local Nanaimo operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll depending on sea conditions. Descent is controlled to the wreck. Exit procedures follow dive briefing protocols with SMB deployment during ascent for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring high-intensity dive torches for wreck interior exploration
- Maintain excellent buoyancy to avoid silt disturbance inside the wreck
- Do not attempt internal penetration without advanced wreck diving certification
- Penetration points are clearly marked and designed for safe access
- Book with Nanaimo-based dive operators familiar with the site
- Expect cold water and limited visibility; come prepared
- Macro photography of encrusted surfaces is exceptional

## Safety Considerations

This is an advanced-only site. Requirements:
- Advanced Open Water certification minimum
- Cold-water diving experience essential (8-12°C)
- Wreck penetration requires specialized training and equipment
- Excellent buoyancy control required to protect silt layers
- Maximum air consumption discipline; watch consumption carefully
- Proper thermal protection (7mm+ wetsuit or drysuit)
- Always dive with experienced buddy
- Use SMB/safety sausage for controlled ascents

## Photography

The wreck provides dramatic wide-angle subjects with natural light through openings and portholes. Torches illuminate interior details and bring out the colors of anemones and other encrusting organisms. Macro opportunities abound on the heavily colonized surfaces.
