## Porteau Cove

Porteau Cove Provincial Park is a popular diving destination featuring multiple artificial reefs and two shipwrecks. Located 38 kilometers north of Vancouver in Howe Sound, the site offers beginner-friendly depths with several wrecks and man-made reef structures creating a diverse diving experience suitable for all skill levels (with advanced sections for deeper exploration).

## Overview

Porteau Cove is one of the most accessible and beginner-friendly dive sites in the Vancouver area. The site features multiple wrecks and carefully placed artificial reef structures. Depths range from 6 to 18 meters in the main diving area, with deeper sections available for advanced divers. Multiple marker buoys identify dive sites. The site is operated as a provincial park with facilities including parking, showers, and day-use areas.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Howe Sound, 38km north of Vancouver on Highway 99
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-15 meters (seasonal dependent)
- **Current**: Generally manageable
- **Best Time**: Spring through fall (March-November)

## Marine Life

The artificial reef environment hosts diverse marine life including rockfish, lingcod, sculpin, crab, starfish, anemones, and nudibranchs. The site's location in nutrient-rich Howe Sound creates productive ecosystems on the various reef structures and wrecks.

## Dive Profile

Beginner dives typically occur on the shallow wrecks and artificial reef structures at 6-18 meters. Primary sites include the Granthall (sunken barge), Centennial (sailboat hull), and Leaning Tower (concrete pillars). Each structure is marked by buoys. Dives typically last 40-50 minutes allowing thorough exploration. More advanced divers can explore the Nakaya minesweeper in 70-100 feet (21-30m), a decaying wreck suitable only for advanced divers.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from the shore via steps leading to the water from the main parking area. Water entry is straightforward and protected. Return to the same location for exit. The park facilities make this ideal for training and recreational dives.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent beginner site with calm, protected conditions
- Bring dive torches to explore wreck interiors and look in crevices
- Marker buoys clearly identify dive sites
- The site is excellent for certification training
- Shallow depths allow longer bottom times (air conservation)
- Outdoor shower and bathrooms available (March-November)
- Best underwater viewing during slack tides
- Avoid the Nakaya (minesweeper) unless trained for deep wreck diving

## Safety Considerations

This is a beginner-friendly site with important considerations:
- Beginner areas (shallow structures 6-18m) suitable for newly certified divers
- The Nakaya minesweeper (21-30m depth) is advanced only — hull is decaying and unsafe for penetration
- Cold water (8-12°C) requires proper thermal protection
- Always dive with a buddy
- Beginner-suitable depths minimize air consumption issues
- Use SMB for ascents (practice safety procedures)
- Check provincial park conditions and closures

## Photography

Shallow depths and good visibility make this excellent for beginner photography. The artificial structures and wrecks provide diverse subjects. Wide-angle opportunities with the structures and macro subjects on encrusted surfaces.
