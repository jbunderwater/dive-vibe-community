## CJ's Drop Off

CJ's Drop Off is one of Utila's most dramatic north side wall dives, with an impressively steep sheer drop that one diver compared to "swimming in the Grand Canyon."

## Overview

CJ's Drop Off marks the western end of Turtle Harbour on the north side of Utila. The reef top ranges from 20 to 45 ft (6–14 m), covered in sand channels and healthy coral, before the wall falls away to beyond 140 ft (43 m) and opens into deep blue water. The combination of vertical scale, vibrant reef, and strong pelagic action makes this an Advanced-rated site. Morning dives here catch the best light and calmer conditions; north side currents can accelerate along the wall at certain tidal states. The site is accessed by boat and typically run by operators as a morning dive.

## Marine Life

The wall and reef top support a strong mix of Caribbean species. Spotted eagle rays, barracuda, and sea turtles are commonly reported along the drop-off face. Moray eels inhabit crevices in the shallower reef section. Large barrel sponges appear in the deeper sections below 130 ft, and divers visiting in April have reported observing them spawning. Smaller crevice dwellers — wrasses, damsels, and juvenile fish — populate the shallow coral garden above the wall lip.

## Dive Profile

- **Reef top**: 20–45 ft (6–14 m), sand channels with corals
- **Wall starts**: ~45 ft (14 m)
- **Wall depth**: Drops past 140 ft (43 m)
- **Current**: Variable — can be strong; enhances pelagic encounters
- **Visibility**: Typically excellent on the north side

## Entry / Exit

Boat dive only. Morning trips from Utila town are standard; afternoon conditions can deteriorate with north side swells and wind.

## Tips

- Rated Advanced due to wall depth and current potential — good buoyancy control required.
- Plan a safety stop on the shallow reef top where ample life is found at 20–30 ft.
- An SMB is recommended for drift exits.

## Safety

Monitor depth closely on the wall — the dramatic vertical environment can lead to inadvertent depth gain. Turn the dive well before no-decompression limits allow.
