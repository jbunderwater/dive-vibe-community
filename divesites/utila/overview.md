## Utila

The Caribbean's whale shark capital and a world-renowned diving hub. Utila offers 60+ dive sites ranging from vibrant shallow reefs to dramatic wall dives, with year-round whale shark encounters and affordable, welcoming dive operations.

## Description

Utila is a premier dive destination in the Caribbean, surrounded by continuous fringing reefs with 60+ documented dive sites at varying depths. Water temperatures average 26-29°C (79-84°F), with visibility typically reaching 20-40 meters (65-130 feet). Year-round diving is possible, with the best conditions during December to April (dry season). The island is particularly famous as the "whale shark capital of the Caribbean," with recorded sightings for all 12 months of the year—peak season is March-May and August-October.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: Boat-accessible sites reached through local dive operators
- **Reef Diving**: Healthy coral reef systems supporting diverse marine ecosystems
- **Night Diving**: After-dark diving reveals nocturnal marine species and different reef behaviors

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Utila is accessible via international and regional flights to nearby airports. Check with airlines for current routes and connections.
- **Dive Operators**: Professional dive operators offer equipment rental, guided dives, certification courses, and boat trips to offshore sites.
- **Accommodation**: Options range from dedicated dive resorts to budget-friendly guesthouses, with many properties located near popular dive sites.
- **Transportation**: Local transportation and dive operator transfers are the primary means of reaching dive sites.
- **Facilities**: Dive sites vary in available amenities; operator-run sites typically provide comprehensive facilities while remote sites may have limited infrastructure.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperatures range from 26-29°C (79-84°F) with visibility of 20-40 meters (65-130 feet). Currents are generally light to moderate.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: The waters support diverse marine ecosystems including whale sharks, dolphins, sea turtles, grouper, moray eels, seahorses.
- **Conservation**: Marine protected areas help preserve the reef ecosystems and regulate diving activities.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: December to April (dry season). Diving is possible year-round.
- **Currency**: Honduran Lempira (HNL)
- **Language**: Spanish
- **Safety**: Always dive within certification limits. Be aware of boat traffic, fire coral, sea urchins. Verify the location of the nearest hyperbaric chamber before diving.
