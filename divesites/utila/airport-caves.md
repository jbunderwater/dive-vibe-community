## Airport Caves

A beginner-friendly cavern and swim-through site featuring beautiful corals, hundreds of glassy sweepers, and excellent conditions for new divers.

## Overview

Airport Caves is located on the east side of Utila in front of the old airstrip, offering beautiful caverns and swim-throughs. The site features hundreds of glassy sweepers hiding inside caverns and a beautiful coral wall. The top of the reef starts at 10 feet and drops to 50+ feet where a sandy slope continues to 100+ feet. A second reef appears at depth. Generally, there is no current, making this excellent for new divers. Located in the Caribbean region, this site offers 10-30 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-29°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Utila, Caribbean (East side, 10-15 minutes from main dock)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-30 meters (33-100 feet)
- **Current**: Generally none—excellent for new divers
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

Hundreds of glassy sweepers hide inside the caverns, and the site features beautiful coral formations. The beautiful coral wall and surrounding reef host turtles and eaglerays that often pass around. In the sand, look for garden eels, stingrays, and pipefish. On the reef, expect a variety of fish like barracuda, black durgeon, angelfishes, and on the second deeper reef, you may find the rare reef butterfly fish. The cave environment shelters species adapted to low-light conditions, including crustaceans and small fish that seek protection in the cavern overhangs.

## Dive Profile

Begin on the reef top at 10 feet and explore the beautiful coral wall and cavern formations. With no current, you can take your time navigating through the caverns and swim-throughs. The most abundant marine life is typically found between 5-20 meters. You can then descend to 50+ feet into the sandy slope area looking for garden eels, stingrays, and other sand-dwelling creatures. A second reef appears at depth (30+ meters) for more advanced divers. Plan your dive within your certification limits and allow adequate air for a safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Excellent site for newer divers—no current makes navigation easy
- Perfect for practicing cavern and swim-through skills without advanced cave training
- Bring a dive torch to illuminate cavern interiors and the glassy sweepers
- Close, safe location from the main dock (10-15 minutes)
- Suitable for both beginners and skilled divers—various depth options available
- Book with reputable local dive operators who know the site conditions
- Bring an underwater camera—this site offers excellent cavern and wide-angle photography opportunities
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of boat traffic, fire coral, sea urchins in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. This site is recommended for experienced divers only. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
