## Black Hills

An underwater seamount a mile south of Utila offering spectacular biodiversity and abundant fish life in colorful coral gardens.

## Overview

Black Hills is a sea mountain (seamount) located about a mile offshore from Utila, starting at 12 meters and descending to over 45 meters. The seamount's peak is covered in vibrant corals and offers probably the greatest diversity of fish life around the island. A true intermediate-level dive featuring schools of barracudas, jacks, turtles, and impressive coral formations. Located in the Caribbean region, this site offers 20-40 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-29°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Utila, Caribbean (1 mile south of island)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters (65-130 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season) when conditions allow

## Marine Life

Black Hills boasts the greatest diversity of fish life around Utila. Divers frequently spot schools of great barracudas, jacks, turtles, green morays, lobsters, groupers, and scorpionfish. Sea turtles (green, hawksbill), southern stingrays, eagle rays, nurse sharks, reef sharks, barracuda, parrotfish, angelfish, blue tangs, and trumpetfish populate the reef structure. The seamount configuration creates varied ecosystems with pelagic visitors in the blue water.

## Dive Profile

Begin your descent to the seamount's peak at 12 meters. The most colorful coral gardens and vibrant fish life are found on and around the peak. You can then work your way around the sides of the seamount, descending to deeper sections if you wish to explore to 45 meters. Careful depth management is essential. Most of the interesting features and marine life are found between 10-30 meters. Maintain good buoyancy control and monitor your depth gauge regularly.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Watch your depth carefully—seamount diving can lead to unintentional deep descents
- Arrive with good weather and sea conditions—this is a must-dive when conditions allow
- Circle the seamount to maximize the diversity of marine encounters
- Look in crevices and overhangs for hidden marine life
- Book with reputable local dive operators who know the site conditions
- Bring an underwater camera—this site offers excellent wide-angle photography opportunities
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of boat traffic, fire coral, sea urchins in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. This site is recommended for experienced divers only. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

Wall dives offer stunning wide-angle opportunities with dramatic depth perspectives. The interplay of light and shadow along the wall creates compelling compositions. Look for macro subjects in wall crevices and overhangs.
