# Jack Neil Point

*A gently sloping reef on Utila's south side, transitioning from a shallow coral garden to a deeper reef slope, suitable for all levels.*

## Overview

Jack Neil Point is a broad reef site on the southern side of Utila, featuring a shallow coral garden beginning at 5 meters and a gradual slope descending to 30 meters. While the maximum depth reaches the advanced range, the site is dived primarily in the 8–18 meter zone where coral coverage and fish life are most productive. The gently sloping topography makes this an accessible site for beginners who can stay in the shallower upper sections, while the deeper slope provides more for experienced divers. Visibility averages 20–25 meters. The site is named after an early diving pioneer associated with Utila's dive community.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South coast of Utila, Bay Islands, Honduras
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–25 meters (65–80 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate; the south-facing exposure can have current
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmer conditions April to September on the south side

## Marine Life

Jack Neil Point has particularly good coral coverage, with extensive hard coral formations — star corals, brain corals, and mountainous star coral — providing habitat for a dense reef fish community. Hawksbill turtles are regularly encountered feeding on sponges. Nurse sharks rest in the deeper sections of the slope. Large southern stingrays glide over the sandy sections at the base of the slope. Groups of spotted eagle rays pass through seasonally. The shallow coral garden section (5–10 meters) has outstanding parrotfish activity, schools of blue tang, and visible cleaning stations. Moray eels inhabit the crevices throughout.

## Dive Profile

Beginners descend to the shallow garden at 5–8 meters and explore this section for the full dive, enjoying excellent fish diversity in comfortable, bright water. Intermediate divers descend the slope to 15–20 meters for the main dive, then ascend to the garden for the safety stop. Advanced divers can extend to the 25–30 meter slope to observe the deeper reef community. All levels complete the final portion of the dive in the shallow garden section.

## Entry and Exit

Short boat ride from Utila Town. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Exit via boat ladder.

## Tips

- Jack Neil Point works well as a second dive after CJ's Drop Off — the shallower profile is appropriate for the second dive of the day
- The shallow coral garden at 5–8 meters is excellent for beginners and student divers practicing skills in real reef conditions
- Snorkeling is productive in the shallowest sections close to the boat
- Whale sharks are occasionally reported passing through this section of Utila's south coast on their way to the eastern end of the island
- This site is particularly good for a slow, naturalist-style dive — take a slate for recording fish species

## Safety

Manageable for beginners in the shallow sections. Instructor supervision of student dives in this section is straightforward. For experienced divers venturing deeper, monitor depth and air carefully as the slope provides no clear depth boundary. Current can pick up on the south-facing reef. Always carry an SMB.

## Photography

Excellent natural-light photography in the shallow garden section. Wide-angle reef scenes with good coral coverage and natural light at 5–10 meters look spectacular. Fish portraiture in good visibility with bright ambient light. Turtle encounters in the shallows are particularly well-lit subjects.
