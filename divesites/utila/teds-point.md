## Ted's Point

A south-side wall dive featuring huge coral heads, healthy coral formations, and an interesting shipwreck on the sand floor.

## Overview

Ted's Point is a south-side dive site featuring sandy bottom at about 18 meters with an east-sloping wall reaching depths over 30 meters. The site is known for huge coral heads in the middle of the sand and lots of large coral heads to explore with healthy coral formations. Interestingly, southwest of the mooring lies a ferro-cement sailboat wreck in 20-23 meters with a hull in good shape. Located in the Caribbean region, this site offers 20-40 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-29°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Utila, Caribbean (South side)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters (65-130 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate, some surge reported
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

Divers explore huge coral heads and healthy coral formations populated by sea turtles (green, hawksbill), southern stingrays, eagle rays, nurse sharks, reef sharks, barracuda, parrotfish, angelfish, blue tangs, and trumpetfish. The wall structure creates a vertical ecosystem with different species at varying depths. Moray eels, lobsters, and crustaceans hide in reef crevices. Pelagic visitors such as large jacks are common in the blue water beyond the wall.

## Dive Profile

Begin your descent to the sandy bottom at approximately 18 meters where huge coral heads dominate the landscape. Explore the large coral formations and then work your way up the east-sloping wall. The wall ascends to significant depths, so careful depth management is essential. Most of the interesting features and marine life are found between 10-30 meters. Don't miss the ferro-cement sailboat wreck southwest of the mooring, sitting in 20-23 meters. Maintain good buoyancy control and monitor your depth gauge regularly.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Watch your depth carefully—wall dives can lead to unintentional deep descents
- Look in crevices and overhangs for hidden marine life including moray eels and lobsters
- Don't miss the ferro-cement sailboat wreck—ask your dive guide to point it out
- The huge coral heads are great wide-angle subjects for photography
- Be prepared for some surge but still excellent diving conditions
- Book with reputable local dive operators who know the site conditions and the wreck location
- Bring an underwater camera—this site offers excellent wide-angle photography opportunities
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of boat traffic, fire coral, sea urchins in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

Wall dives offer stunning wide-angle opportunities with dramatic depth perspectives. The interplay of light and shadow along the wall creates compelling compositions. Look for macro subjects in wall crevices and overhangs.
