## Alaska

Extreme cold-water diving in pristine wilderness waters, featuring kelp forests, giant Pacific octopus, king crab, and sea otters across the vast coastline from the Inside Passage to the Aleutian Islands.

## Description

Alaska offers 31 documented dive sites with depths ranging from 12 to 30 meters, including 22 reef sites and 9 wreck sites. All sites are rated Advanced difficulty due to the remote locations, cold water temperatures, and challenging conditions. Dive sites span from the Inside Passage of Southeast Alaska (Juneau, Sitka, Ketchikan) to Prince William Sound and the remote Aleutian Islands, covering a coastline that stretches over 54,000 kilometers. The waters support rich cold-water ecosystems with kelp forests, colorful invertebrate walls, and encounters with marine mammals found nowhere else in such abundance.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: All 31 sites require boat access, reflecting Alaska's remote and rugged coastline
- **Reef Diving**: 22 reef sites including Bligh Reef (site of the 1989 Exxon Valdez grounding) in Prince William Sound, Vanderbilt Reef near Juneau, and numerous reefs in the Haida Gwaii-adjacent waters of Southeast Alaska
- **Wreck Diving**: 9 wreck sites including the MV Vashon (sunk 1986), MV Leschi (historic steam ferry sunk 1978 in Prince William Sound), USS Coldbrook, M/V Oduna (1965), and the Boat Graveyard near Sitka

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Juneau International Airport (JNU), Ketchikan International Airport (KTN), and Sitka Rocky Gutierrez Airport (SIT) serve Southeast Alaska. Ted Stevens Anchorage International Airport (ANC) is the gateway for Prince William Sound and southcentral sites. Many remote sites require floatplane or extended boat travel
- **Dive Operators**: Wind and Water Charters in Ketchikan (husband-wife team, both instructors), Deep Six Dive Center in Ketchikan, The SCUBA Tank in Juneau, and Dive Alaska. Selkie Snorkels in Sitka offers guided snorkel/dive experiences in Sitka Sound
- **Accommodation**: Hotels and lodges in Juneau, Ketchikan, and Sitka. Remote sites may require liveaboard vessels or wilderness lodges
- **Transportation**: Alaska Marine Highway ferry system connects Southeast Alaska communities. Floatplanes serve remote locations. A car is useful in larger towns but many sites are boat-access only
- **Facilities**: Limited dive shops compared to lower 48 states. Bringing your own drysuit and cold-water gear is strongly recommended. Fills available in major towns

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperatures 4-15C (40-60F), with winter temps around 4C (40F) and summer reaching 12-15C (53-60F) in sheltered areas like Sitka Sound. Winter offers the best visibility with dramatic underwater clarity. Tidal currents can be extreme in narrow channels
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Giant Pacific octopus, wolf eels, king crab, Dungeness crab, rockfish, kelp greenling, sculpins, lingcod, sea scallops, nudibranchs, plumose anemones, tubeworms, sea stars, and abalone. Marine mammals include sea otters (keystone predators maintaining kelp forest ecosystems), Steller sea lions, harbor seals, humpback whales, and orcas
- **Conservation**: Alaska's waters are among the least impacted by human activity in North America. Tongass National Forest protects much of the Southeast Alaska coastline. Various state and federal marine protections apply

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: May through September for the mildest conditions, longest daylight (up to 18+ hours in June), and warmest water. Winter diving offers exceptional visibility but extreme cold and limited daylight
- **Currency**: USD
- **Language**: English
- **Safety**: Drysuit absolutely required year-round — water is dangerously cold. Cold-water rated regulators mandatory. Remote locations mean limited emergency medical access; DAN insurance strongly recommended. Strong tidal currents in many channels require advanced dive planning. Hypothermia risk is significant even in summer. Many sites involve long boat transits in exposed waters where sea conditions can change rapidly
