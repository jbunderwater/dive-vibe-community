# Anchor Point South

A reef slope on Statia's southern leeward coast scattered with 18th-century iron anchors and chain — physical evidence of the hundreds of merchant ships that once crowded the Golden Rock roadstead.

## Overview

Anchor Point South sits on the leeward western side of Sint Eustatius, in waters that were once among the busiest anchorages in the Atlantic world. During Statia's commercial peak in the 1770s — when the island processed more cargo per capita than anywhere in the Western Hemisphere — hundreds of vessels anchored in these waters simultaneously. The seafloor retains the evidence: large iron anchors, chain segments, and ballast stones are distributed across the reef slope between 8 and 18 meters. STENAPA (Sint Eustatius National Parks Foundation) manages the marine protected area and all artifacts are strictly protected under Dutch law. The reef itself features volcanic boulder structures, hard coral formations including brain and star corals, and sandy clearings with good invertebrate diversity. Visibility averages 20–30 meters.

## Marine Life

The iron anchors and chain provide hard substrate for coral and sponge recruitment, creating micro-habitats for moray eels, groupers, and chromis clouds. Caribbean spiny lobster shelter beneath anchor flukes. Yellowtail snapper, blue tang, and French angelfish are common throughout. Sandy areas between reef structures host southern stingrays and garden eels. Hawksbill turtles are regular visitors. Statia's diving receives relatively little traffic compared to most Caribbean islands, and marine life is notably less accustomed to divers than at busier destinations.

## Dive Profile

Descent via mooring to the reef top at 8 meters, then navigate south along the reef slope following the guide to the anchor concentrations at various depths between 10 and 18 meters. Each anchor grouping merits close inspection. A typical dive covers 150–200 meters of reef before returning to the mooring. Maximum depth of 18 meters allows 50–60 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive operated by Golden Rock Dive Center or Scubaqua, Statia's main operators. Permanent mooring maintained by STENAPA. Entry by giant stride or backward roll. DSMB required for ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

Carry a torch to examine the undersides of anchor flukes and chain links, where cleaning shrimp, nudibranchs, and crustaceans concentrate in the shade. Statia's low diver traffic means the reef here feels unhurried — take time to explore the full depth range of the site rather than simply collecting anchor sightings.

## Safety Considerations

All artifacts are protected — touching, moving, or collecting is illegal. Fire coral grows on some anchor metal — maintain appropriate distance. Current on the outer reef increases on incoming tides. Always carry a DSMB.

## Photography

Iron anchors against coral backdrop provide strong compositional elements. Wide-angle shots with an anchor as foreground and a diver in the midground are a signature image of Statia diving. Macro subjects around anchor encrustation are rich. The volcanic rock texture adds visual interest distinctly different from typical Caribbean reef photography.
