# Blue Bead Hole

A shallow shore dive famous for the cobalt-blue Dutch glass trade beads that centuries of current and wave action have deposited in sandy pockets along the seafloor — a living treasure hunt in Statia's ancient roadstead.

## Overview

Blue Bead Hole is one of Sint Eustatius's most singular diving experiences — a shallow site renowned not for dramatic topography but for the extraordinary historical artifacts scattered across the sandy bottom. During Statia's commercial peak in the 17th and 18th centuries, when the island was a hub of the Atlantic slave trade and among the world's busiest trading entrepôts, cobalt-blue glass trade beads made in Amsterdam passed through in enormous quantities. These pentagonal beads were used as currency in the slave trade — transported to Africa to purchase enslaved people. After emancipation, accounts suggest many freed people gathered at the coastal cliffs and threw their beads into the sea. Centuries of currents and wave action have concentrated many of these beads in sandy depressions along the leeward coast. STENAPA strictly protects the site — beads must not be collected, but divers can observe and photograph them in situ. The site is suitable for snorkelling as well as scuba, and the adjacent reef provides a pleasant shallow dive alongside the bead hunt.

## Marine Life

The shallow reef adjacent to the bead holes features brain and star coral formations. Blue tang, parrotfish, and sergeant majors are abundant in the shallows. Sandy depressions between reef patches host garden eels and occasional flounder. Green sea turtles are common in the shallow grass beds around the site.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from the beach near Oranjestad, with divers following the sandy depressions northward in the 3–10 meter range to look for the blue bead concentrations. Local guides know the current bead-finding areas, which shift over time as sand moves. A guided 30–40 minute dive covers the main areas. Snorkellers can access the shallowest bead pockets without scuba equipment.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry via the beach below the fort at Oranjestad. Entry over the rocky shoreline requires care — booties or water shoes recommended. The slope is gradual. Exit at the same beach. No boat required.

## Tips and Recommendations

Blue Bead Hole is best experienced with a local guide who knows the current bead concentration areas — these shift seasonally and a guide dramatically improves the probability of finding beads. The beads are small, pentagonal, and dark cobalt-blue — typically found after storms that stir up the sand and expose them. Photograph rather than collect: removing any artifact is illegal under STENAPA regulations. An excellent pairing with Crooks Castle or Double Wreck for a full day of historical Statia diving.

## Safety Considerations

All artifacts including blue beads are protected by STENAPA and Dutch law — collecting is illegal. Shore entry over rock requires careful footing. Visibility can reduce after rain events. Current is light but can develop in the afternoon. Always dive with a buddy and inform someone ashore.

## Photography

The dark-blue pentagonal beads on white sand make striking macro subjects. A close-up lens at 1:2 or 1:1 ratio captures the rich blue colour and any encrustation on older beads. Shallow ambient light at 3–8 meters is excellent throughout the day for natural-light photography of the reef and sand areas.
