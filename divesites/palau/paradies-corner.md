# Paradies Corner

A reef corner in the central Rock Islands known for its lettuce coral formations, Napoleon wrasse, and hawksbill turtles in a more sheltered setting than Palau's outer wall sites

## Overview
Paradies Corner is a reef dive located among the central Rock Islands. The site is noted for its distinctive lettuce coral formations and serves as a reliable stop for Napoleon wrasse sightings at the reef edge. Conditions are more moderate than at Palau's exposed outer barrier reef sites, making this suitable for intermediate divers and those seeking a relaxed dive between more demanding sites. The reef slopes from shallow coral gardens to about 25 meters, with the corner providing the most interesting fish aggregation.

## Marine Life
Napoleon wrasse are documented at the reef edge at this site. Hawksbill turtles are reported as regular visitors. The lettuce coral formations are the site's signature visual feature. Typical central Rock Islands reef fish — fusiliers, surgeonfish, parrotfish, and anemonefish — populate the reef. Beyond these documented species, specific marine life claims for this site require further on-the-ground validation.

## Dive Profile
Descend to the reef top and navigate to the corner where fish life is concentrated. The reef slopes to approximately 25 meters, with the most interesting terrain between 8–20 meters. Explore the coral formations along the slope. The corner can be circled if current is light, or drifted if current is moderate. Safety stop at 5 meters on the reef top. Total dive time typically 50–60 minutes.

## Entry and Exit
Boat dive from Koror (approximately 25–35 minutes). Entry by giant stride with descent to the reef. Depending on conditions, this is a return-to-boat or gentle drift dive. The sheltered Rock Islands location provides calm surface conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Focus time around the corner where fish life is most concentrated
- This is an excellent relaxed dive to balance a day of intense current diving
- The shallow reef top is ideal for extended safety stops with reef fish to observe
- Ask your guide about current direction before descending — approach angle matters at corner sites

## Safety Considerations
Paradies Corner is one of Palau's more forgiving dive sites, with moderate conditions suitable for confident intermediate divers. Standard reef diving precautions apply — maintain buoyancy, stay within certification limits, and carry an SMB. No extreme depths or overhead environments.

## Photography
Close-focus wide-angle works well for reef scenes with coral formations and schooling fish. The lettuce coral fields are visually distinctive subjects. Macro shooters will find anemonefish and encrusting invertebrates throughout. Calm conditions allow careful composition without fighting current.
