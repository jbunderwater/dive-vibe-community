# Paradies Corner

A healthy reef corner in the central Rock Islands offering reliable fish life and coral diversity in a more sheltered setting than Palau's outer wall sites

## Overview
Paradies Corner is a reef dive located among the central Rock Islands, between Koror and the southern dive sites. The site features a reef promontory where currents converge mildly, concentrating fish life around a corner adorned with hard and soft corals. While it lacks the dramatic walls and heavy shark populations of Blue Corner or Peliliu Corner, Paradies Corner delivers a solid Palau reef experience with good fish density, healthy coral formations, and more moderate conditions suitable for intermediate divers. The site is often included as a second or third dive on day-boat itineraries returning from the southern sites, taking advantage of its central location and reliable conditions. The reef slopes from shallow coral gardens to about 25 meters, with the corner itself providing the most interesting topography and marine life aggregation.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Rock Islands, Palau, Micronesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 meters (50-80 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; sheltered central location

## Marine Life
The reef corner attracts schools of fusiliers, snappers, and surgeonfish that aggregate in the mild current. Reef sharks -- both grey reef and whitetip -- patrol the corner area, though in smaller numbers than at the more exposed sites. Napoleon wrasse are regularly sighted. Hawksbill turtles rest on the reef and feed on sponges. Anemonefish are abundant in the shallows. Schools of sweetlips and groupers occupy the reef structure. The coral formations host nudibranchs, flatworms, and cleaning stations with cleaner wrasses and shrimp servicing larger fish. Barracuda schools occasionally pass through. The overall fish density is good, reflecting the healthy reef ecosystem of the Rock Islands.

## Dive Profile
Descend to the reef top at 5-8 meters and navigate to the corner where fish life is concentrated. The reef slopes to approximately 25 meters, with the most interesting terrain between 8-20 meters. Explore the coral formations along the slope, checking overhangs and cleaning stations for marine life. The corner can be circled if current is light, or drifted if current is moderate. Safety stop at 5 meters on the reef top. Total dive time typically 50-60 minutes with relaxed air consumption.

## Entry and Exit
Boat dive from Koror (approximately 25-35 minutes). Entry by giant stride with descent to the reef. Depending on conditions, this is a return-to-boat or gentle drift dive. The sheltered Rock Islands location provides calm surface conditions for entry and exit.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Focus your time around the corner where current concentrates the fish action
- Look for cleaning stations on prominent coral heads -- these are hotspots for close marine encounters
- This is an excellent relaxed dive to balance a day of intense current diving at Blue Corner or Ulong Channel
- The shallow reef top is ideal for extended safety stops with plenty of reef fish to observe
- Ask your guide about the best approach direction based on current -- arriving at the corner correctly matters

## Safety Considerations
Paradies Corner is one of Palau's more forgiving dive sites, with moderate conditions suitable for confident intermediate divers. Currents are generally mild but can strengthen. Standard reef diving precautions apply -- maintain buoyancy to protect corals, stay within certification limits, and carry an SMB. The central Rock Islands location keeps surface conditions calm. No significant overhead environments or extreme depths.

## Photography
A versatile photography site. Close-focus wide-angle works well for reef scenes featuring coral formations and schooling fish. The corner concentrates fish life into a photogenic area where patient positioning yields images of schools against the reef backdrop. Macro shooters will find nudibranchs, cleaning stations, and anemonefish throughout. Natural light is good in the shallows for ambient-light reef photography. This is a relaxed site where you can take time to compose carefully without fighting strong current.
