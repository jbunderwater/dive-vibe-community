# Peliliu Corner

Remote southern Palau's most challenging wall dive, where the Philippine Sea and Pacific Ocean currents converge at the tip of Peleliu reef producing some of the strongest currents in Palau and dense pelagic aggregations

## Overview
Peliliu Corner is a wall dive at the southern tip of Palau's reef system, near the historic WWII island of Peleliu. Two reef walls meet at a corner that drops to approximately 150 meters, with ocean floor extending far below. The convergence of the Philippine Sea and Pacific Ocean currents at this point produces some of the strongest and most unpredictable currents in all of Palau — operators explicitly warn of extreme down-currents. The site's remoteness — roughly 90–120 minutes by boat from Koror — means it is primarily accessible by liveaboard or long-range day boat. The combination of strong current, remote location, and exposure to open ocean makes this one of Palau's most demanding dives. Pelagic life is abundant: grey reef sharks, silvertip sharks, eagle rays, large schools of barracuda, jacks, rainbow runners, tuna, and snappers. Occasional unusual sightings — hammerhead sharks, bull sharks, and on rare occasions sperm whales, orcas, and sailfish — have been documented at this southern reef extremity.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Southern tip of Peleliu reef, Peleliu, Southern Palau, Micronesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–50 meters (65–165 feet)
- **Current**: Very strong; extreme down-currents are a documented hazard
- **Best Time**: Year-round; best conditions and visibility November to May

## Marine Life
Grey reef sharks and silvertip sharks (distinguishable by their white-tipped fins) patrol the corner area and are among the most consistently documented species here. Eagle rays pass in small groups. Large schools of barracuda, bigeye jacks, rainbow runners, dogtooth tuna, and sweetlips move through on the current. Yellow soft corals, cable coral strands, and black coral bushes cover the wall. Hammerhead sharks, bull sharks, tiger sharks, and pelagic megafauna such as sperm whales and orcas have been reported here on rare occasions, according to local operators. The wall's remote status means marine life encounters are relatively undisturbed by diver traffic.

## Dive Profile
Descend to the reef top at 5–8 meters and work toward the corner where the current is strongest. Hook in on dead coral and observe the shark and pelagic aggregation during incoming current. After the hook phase, drift along the wall between 10–25 meters where coral and marine life is most concentrated. A deeper plateau extends to approximately 45 meters and attracts additional pelagic species. The current can be very strong and change direction mid-dive — stay alert and maintain close contact with your guide. Safety stop at 5 meters; deploy SMB for pickup.

## Entry and Exit
Primarily accessible by liveaboard or long-range day boat from Koror (approximately 90–120 minutes). Entry by giant stride into current. The boat captain assesses current direction and strength before determining the drop point. Exit is a drift pickup — SMB deployment is mandatory. Surface conditions in this exposed southern location can be rougher than at northern sites.

## Tips and Recommendations
- This site is best experienced from a liveaboard that can time the dive to optimal current conditions
- Bring a reef hook and be prepared for the strongest currents in Palau
- The pelagic encounters here can be among the most intense in Palau — stay calm and stay with your guide
- Carry a dSMB and audible signaling device — the remote location demands reliable surface visibility
- Nitrox extends usable bottom time if accessing the deeper plateau

## Safety Considerations
Peliliu Corner is classified by local operators as an extremely advanced dive. Extreme down-currents along the wall are documented and can rapidly push divers well below planned depth. The remote location means that emergency medical assistance is significantly delayed compared to northern sites. The distance from Koror requires particularly careful pre-dive planning and conservative gas management. Dive only with operators experienced in southern Palau conditions. This site demands advanced certification, extensive current-diving experience, and excellent physical fitness. It is not appropriate for divers without strong current experience, regardless of certification level.

## Photography
Wide-angle is the only sensible lens at Peliliu Corner. Shark aggregations are best captured when divers are hooked in and the sharks approach on the current — shoot into the blue with sharks overhead for dramatic compositions. The pristine wall provides excellent backdrop with yellow soft corals and black coral. The exceptional visibility on good days allows ambient-light shooting at distance. The remoteness and undisturbed conditions yield images with a quality that distinguishes this site from Palau's more visited locations.
