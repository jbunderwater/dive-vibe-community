## Siaes Corner

Siaes Corner is a dynamic current-swept wall dive renowned for large shark schools and sea turtle encounters.

## Overview

Siaes Corner is a vertical drop-off positioned at a channel junction where tidal currents create ideal conditions for marine life aggregations. During incoming tide, strong currents sweep food particles past the wall, attracting large gray reef shark schools. The corner features a clean vertical wall dropping from shallow depths to significant depths, with multiple dive profile options. Reef hooks are highly recommended for stationary observation of the shark activity.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ulong Islands, central Palau
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 meters
- **Current**: Strong (especially incoming tide)
- **Best Time**: April to November; dive on incoming tide

## Marine Life

Siaes Corner is famous for large shark populations. Schools of gray reef sharks patrol the wall edge, with 20+ sharks commonly seen on a single dive. Hawksbill and green sea turtles are frequent visitors and easily approachable. The site also hosts schooling fusiliers, snappers, groupers, Napoleon wrasse, barracuda, and occasional trevally. Hard corals and sea fans decorate the wall structure.

## Dive Profile

Descend the anchor line and position yourself near the wall using a reef hook. Use the current-driven food delivery system to observe shark behavior. Gray reef sharks typically maintain their distance but will investigate divers. Most action occurs between 10-20 meters. The vertical wall allows flexibility in depth management. Maintain a stationary position to fully appreciate the shark activity without disturbing their feeding patterns.

## Entry and Exit

Boat entry via back roll or giant stride. Descend the anchor line to your desired depth. Position on wall with reef hook for stationary diving. Maintain buddy contact throughout. Ascend slowly and perform safety stop before SMB deployment and boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Reef hook practice is essential—bring equipment and be proficient
- Dive on incoming tide for strongest shark activity
- Bring a GoPro—shark encounters are memorable
- Remain calm; sharks at Palau are typically not aggressive toward humans
- Watch for turtle interactions and photo opportunities
- This corner dive is a gateway to Palau's best shark dives
- Combine with Siaes Tunnel for a full day of Ulong Island diving

## Safety Considerations

Advanced Open Water certification required with current experience. Strong currents possible—drift protocol may apply. Watch shark behavior and maintain appropriate distance. Carry signaling equipment (SMB, whistle). Reef hook proficiency required. Never touch marine life. Abort the dive if conditions exceed your experience level. Always dive with a buddy.

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
