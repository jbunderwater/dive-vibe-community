# Siaes Corner

A current-swept wall on Palau's western barrier reef that delivers Blue Corner-caliber shark action with a fraction of the diver traffic

## Overview
Siaes Corner is a wall dive on the western side of Palau's barrier reef, north of the more famous Blue Corner and Big Drop-Off sites. The site features a pronounced reef corner where ocean currents converge, attracting large numbers of grey reef sharks, eagle rays, and other pelagics. The wall drops steeply from a coral-topped reef at 5-8 meters to well beyond recreational limits. What makes Siaes Corner special is its relative remoteness -- fewer boats visit this stretch of reef, so encounters with sharks and rays feel more intimate and less crowded than at Blue Corner. The coral along the wall is excellent, with sea fans, soft corals, and hard coral formations in pristine condition. Siaes Corner is a favorite of repeat Palau visitors and liveaboard operators who want to offer their guests a less-trafficked alternative to the southern sites.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Rock Islands, Palau, Micronesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters (65-130 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: Year-round; best pelagic action with incoming current

## Marine Life
Grey reef sharks are the main attraction, often seen in groups of five to twenty patrolling the corner. Spotted eagle rays pass by regularly. Whitetip reef sharks rest in crevices along the wall. Schools of barracuda and bigeye jacks form in the blue water off the wall edge. Napoleon wrasse cruise the reef top. Hawksbill turtles graze on sponges. The wall is covered in sea fans, soft corals, and barrel sponges. Fusiliers and surgeonfish school along the wall face. Macro life includes nudibranchs and small crustaceans in the crevices.

## Dive Profile
Descend to the reef top at 5-8 meters and make your way to the corner where the current is strongest. Hook in if conditions require it and watch the pelagic show. Then drift along the wall, staying between 10-21 meters where the coral and fish life is most concentrated. The wall drops away to depth, so watch your gauges. Ascend to the reef top for a safety stop in the coral garden. Total dive time typically 45-55 minutes depending on current and air consumption.

## Entry and Exit
Boat dive from Koror (approximately 50 minutes) or from liveaboards. Entry by giant stride with descent to the reef top. The dive is usually a drift with SMB pickup at the end. Current direction determines the drop and drift direction. Deploy your SMB at 5 meters.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Bring a reef hook -- the current on the corner can be as strong as Blue Corner
- Visit on a day when boats are not heading to Blue Corner for a more exclusive experience
- The corner itself is where the action is -- spend your time here rather than rushing to drift the wall
- Watch the blue water off the wall edge for passing eagle rays and shark schools
- Pair with Siaes Tunnel on the same dive trip for a full Siaes experience

## Safety Considerations
Strong and unpredictable currents are the main hazard, similar to Blue Corner but with less boat traffic for rescue support. Down-currents along the wall can occur. Stay close to your guide and group. Carry signaling devices and an SMB. Reef hook use should be on dead coral or rock only. Advanced certification and current-diving experience are required.

## Photography
Wide-angle is essential for capturing sharks on the corner and the wall's sea fan gardens. The lower diver traffic means marine life is often less skittish, allowing closer approaches for photography. Shoot the sharks against the blue water background for clean compositions. The coral wall provides excellent backdrop for wide-angle reef shots. Light conditions are typically good on the western-facing wall in the afternoon.
