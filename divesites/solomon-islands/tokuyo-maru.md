# Tokuyo Maru

A Japanese fishing trawler approximately 40 metres long, sunk on 24 December 1987 near Ferguson Passage after striking a reef when locals moved a channel marker. Not a war wreck -- this is a modern sinking. The vessel lies on her port side at 38 metres with her name still visible on the stern.

## Overview

The Tokuyo Maru was a Japanese fishing trawler approximately 40 metres in length. On 24 December 1987, while navigating near Ferguson Passage, the vessel ran over a reef after locals moved a channel navigation marker. The trawler sank and came to rest on her port side at 38 metres depth.

Unlike the many WWII wrecks in the Solomon Islands, this is a modern vessel with a known, mundane cause of sinking. The ship's name remains visible on the stern -- an unusual feature that confirms the wreck's identity. The 38-metre depth and the vessel's 40-metre length make for a focused deep dive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Ferguson Passage, Western Province, Solomon Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive from Gizo
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive (modern fishing trawler)
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 38 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 27-28°C
- **Current**: Variable near Ferguson Passage

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wreck lying on her port side at 38 metres. The vessel's 40-metre length allows a complete circuit of the hull in a single dive, though bottom time at this depth is limited. The stern with the visible name "Tokuyo Maru" is a key feature and photographic subject. Work along the hull, examining the trawler's deck equipment, superstructure, and fishing gear. The port-side orientation means the starboard deck fittings are uppermost and most accessible.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The name visible on the stern is a rare feature for wreck identification -- photograph it
- At 38 metres, plan for approximately 15-20 minutes of bottom time on air
- Nitrox extends bottom time but the depth approaches EANx32 limits
- The modern construction means the wreck is more intact than many older WWII wrecks in the area
- The story of the moved channel marker is a uniquely Solomon Islands tale -- ask your dive operator about it

## Safety Considerations

The 38-metre depth requires advanced certification and careful gas management. Bottom time on air is limited; plan ascent and safety stop carefully. Current near Ferguson Passage can be unpredictable -- carry an SMB. The wreck structure may have sharp edges from fishing equipment and deck hardware. Maintain good buoyancy to avoid contact with the hull.
