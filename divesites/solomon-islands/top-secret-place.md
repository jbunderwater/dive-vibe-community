# Top Secret Place

A wall dive south of Nusa Aghana Island in the southeast of Ferguson Passage, dropping more than 60 metres into the deep. Known for manta ray encounters, large rays, grey reef sharks, and sea snakes, with visibility that can exceed 40 metres at depth. Approximately 20 minutes by boat from Gizo.

## Overview

Top Secret Place is located south of Nusa Aghana Island, in the southeast portion of Ferguson Passage. The site features a wall that drops away more than 60 metres into deep water. At depth, visibility can exceed 40 metres -- some of the clearest water in the Solomon Islands.

The site is known for encounters with manta rays and other large ray species. Grey reef sharks patrol the wall, and sea snakes are regularly observed. The combination of extreme visibility, dramatic wall topography, and large marine life makes this one of the premier dives in the Gizo area, though the depth and exposed location place it in the advanced category.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South of Nusa Aghana Island, SE Ferguson Passage -- ~20 min from Gizo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 40+ metres at depth
- **Water Temperature**: 27-28°C
- **Current**: Variable; can be strong in the passage

## Marine Life

Manta rays are the signature encounter at this site. Large rays of other species are also seen. Grey reef sharks patrol along the wall edge. Sea snakes are regularly observed on the reef and wall face. The nutrient-rich waters of Ferguson Passage support the large marine life that gives this site its reputation.

## Dive Profile

Descend along the wall, which drops away steeply to more than 60 metres. The exceptional visibility at depth gives a dramatic sense of the wall's scale. Position yourself at a comfortable depth along the wall face and scan the blue water for mantas and sharks. Grey reef sharks tend to patrol the wall edge, while manta rays may appear from the deeper water. Watch for sea snakes on the wall surface and along the reef. Work shallower as the dive progresses. The wall structure provides reference points for depth management throughout the dive.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The exceptional visibility (40+ metres) at depth is a major draw but can also lead to inadvertent deep diving -- monitor your depth constantly
- Manta encounters are not guaranteed but the site has a strong reputation for them
- Grey reef sharks respond best to calm, patient observation -- do not chase or approach aggressively
- Sea snakes are generally docile but should not be handled or cornered
- The 20-minute boat ride from Gizo means conditions should be assessed carefully before committing to the trip

## Safety Considerations

The wall drops more than 60 metres, and the exceptional visibility can create a "blue water" sensation that draws divers deeper than planned. Set a maximum depth before the dive and stick to it. Current in Ferguson Passage can be strong and unpredictable. Carry an SMB for mid-water ascents. Sea snakes are venomous but rarely aggressive -- give them space. This is an exposed oceanic site and conditions can change rapidly.
