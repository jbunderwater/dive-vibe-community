# Tambea Caves

A labyrinth of roughly half a dozen interconnected caves and tunnels within a small reef near Tambea village, approximately 40 kilometres west of Honiara. Divers are led through the system by a divemaster, threading through passages typically 1-2 metres wide with at least one tight squeeze along the route.

## Overview

The Tambea Caves system is located within a small reef near the village of Tambea on Guadalcanal's western coast, about 40 kilometres from Honiara. The site consists of approximately half a dozen interconnected caves and tunnels that thread through the reef structure. Passages are typically 1 to 2 metres wide, with at least one section requiring a tight squeeze to pass through.

This is a guided dive -- a divemaster leads divers through the labyrinth, navigating the interconnected passages and ensuring the group stays on route. Visibility in the area is approximately 24 metres, though it can be reduced inside the caves if silt is disturbed. The maximum depth is shallow at around 15 metres, making this an accessible cave experience for intermediate-level divers who are comfortable in confined spaces.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Tambea village, Guadalcanal -- approximately 40 km west of Honiara
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: ~24 metres (outside caves)
- **Water Temperature**: 27-28°C
- **Current**: Generally mild at this site

## Dive Profile

The divemaster leads the group into the cave system, navigating through the interconnected tunnels. The passages are generally 1-2 metres wide -- wide enough for single-file passage but requiring good buoyancy control. At least one section involves a tight squeeze that requires careful maneuvering. The shallow maximum depth of approximately 15 metres means air consumption is not typically a limiting factor, allowing time to appreciate the cave formations and the interplay of light and shadow through the tunnels.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This dive is always done with a local divemaster who knows the cave system -- do not attempt to navigate it independently
- Carry a primary torch and a backup; the caves are dark and disorienting without light
- Streamline your equipment to avoid snagging in the narrow passages and tight squeezes
- The tight squeeze section may not be suitable for larger divers -- discuss this with your operator beforehand
- Excellent buoyancy control is essential; silting out a narrow cave passage affects everyone behind you

## Safety Considerations

While this is a relatively shallow cave dive, it involves genuine overhead environments where direct ascent to the surface is not possible. Maintain close contact with the divemaster at all times. Do not attempt to explore side passages independently. The tight squeeze section requires calm, controlled movement -- if you feel uncomfortable, communicate with your guide before entering. Carry redundant lighting. Fin carefully to avoid disturbing silt in the narrow passages.
