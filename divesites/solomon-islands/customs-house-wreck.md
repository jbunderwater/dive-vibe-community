## Custom's House Wreck

The Custom's House Wreck is a small, shallow wreck lying close to shore near Honiara's waterfront, offering an accessible and rewarding dive for beginners and those looking for a relaxed afternoon dive close to the capital. Its depth, calm conditions, and excellent coral growth make it a versatile site as both a beginner wreck experience and a productive macro dive.

## Overview

This small vessel — likely a harbour utility craft or patrol boat — lies at a maximum depth of 15 meters in protected waters near the customs house area of Honiara Harbour. The wreck is heavily colonised by coral and sponge growth, having had decades to accumulate reef life on its steel frame. While it lacks the dramatic scale of the Bonegi wrecks, it more than compensates with intimate encounters, accessibility, and the density of invertebrate life covering its surfaces. The site is a favourite for photographers with macro interests and for divers who want an easy, exploratory dive without committing to deeper water.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Honiara Harbour area, Guadalcanal, Solomon Islands
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: Year-round; sheltered conditions

## Marine Life

The wreck's encrusted surfaces host an impressive variety of invertebrates well-suited to macro photography. Nudibranchs in multiple species are reliably found on the hull, along with flatworms, tiny crabs, and shrimp hiding in crevices. Lionfish and scorpionfish take up residence under overhangs, and frogfish are occasionally spotted on the more encrusted sections. Smaller resident reef fish — damselfish, cardinalfish, and anthias — occupy the structure in large numbers. Anemones with clownfish are distributed across the wreck. In the sand surrounding the wreck, garden eels emerge from their burrows and flatfish rest camouflaged against the bottom. The calm, sheltered nature of the site means marine life is undisturbed and easy to approach.

## Dive Profile

A relaxed, non-linear dive. After entering from shore and navigating to the wreck in shallow water, divers can conduct a leisurely circumnavigation of the hull, pausing to inspect encrusted surfaces. There is no single prescribed route — the wreck is small enough to explore fully in one dive and interesting enough to merit multiple visits at different parts of the structure. The maximum depth of 15 meters allows extended bottom time under recreational certification limits. A safety stop in the shallows before exit is straightforward and often productive for additional marine life observation.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry near the Honiara waterfront area. The entry point is easy to locate with local knowledge or directions from Honiara-based dive operators. The water is typically calm, making entry and exit straightforward. The short surface swim to the wreck can be done on the surface or as a shallow descent from the point of entry. Booties are recommended for the shore entry.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a macro lens and a torch — the encrusted hull is the best feature here
- Move slowly along the hull and check every overhang and crevice for hidden life
- An ideal site for a night dive — many of the invertebrate species are more active after dark and the nudibranch diversity increases significantly
- Excellent introductory wreck dive before progressing to the Bonegi sites
- The calm, sheltered conditions make this a good option in poor surface weather when exposed sites are difficult

## Safety Considerations

This is one of the most benign dive sites near Honiara. Minimal current, shallow maximum depth, and calm conditions mean the primary safety considerations are standard beginner protocols — dive with a buddy, monitor air consumption, and complete a safety stop. Harbour water visibility can reduce to 10 meters or less after heavy rain. Night divers should carry a primary and backup torch and leave a surface marker visible from the shore.

## Photography

The Custom's House Wreck rewards a dedicated macro setup — a 60mm or 100mm lens with dual strobes is ideal for the nudibranchs, flatworms, and crustaceans covering the hull. Frogfish and scorpionfish provide larger macro subjects. For photographers who want to practice wide-angle wreck composition before tackling the larger Bonegi wrecks, the smaller scale here makes framing and lighting more manageable. Night photography at this site can produce exceptional nudibranch and invertebrate images.
