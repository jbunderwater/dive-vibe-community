# Buceo House Reef

A shore dive directly off Buceo Anilao Dive Resort with a sandy slope to 30 meters and beyond, packed with critters -- three anglerfish on a single dive is not unusual, along with nudibranchs, sea moths, squat lobsters, and mantis shrimp.

## Overview

Buceo House Reef is the house reef of Buceo Anilao Dive Resort, accessible directly from shore. The site features a sandy slope descending to 30 meters and beyond, with featherstars, sea whips, gorgonias, and anemones scattered across the bottom. Despite being a house reef -- often the least exciting dive at a resort -- Buceo's house reef delivers exceptional critter diving. The density of macro subjects is remarkable: three anglerfish have been found on a single dive, and the site hosts an impressive variety of nudibranchs, sea moths, squat lobsters, mantis shrimp, and numerous shrimp species.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Buceo Anilao Dive Resort, Anilao, Batangas, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Shore dive (directly off resort)
- **Site Type**: Muck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 27-29°C
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: November to June (dry season)

## Marine Life

The critter diversity at Buceo House Reef is outstanding for a shore dive. Anglerfish are reliably present -- multiple specimens have been found on a single dive. Nudibranchs of numerous species are abundant throughout the site. Sea moths -- unusual, bottom-dwelling fish with wing-like pectoral fins -- are a highlight. Squat lobsters hide in sponges and crevices. Mantis shrimp occupy burrows in the sandy areas. The featherstars, sea whips, gorgonias, and anemones host numerous shrimp species, including commensal shrimps on featherstars, whip coral shrimps, and anemone shrimps. The sheer variety and density of critters makes every dive here a productive critter hunt.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore at the resort and descend onto the sandy slope. The site is straightforward to navigate -- head out from the resort steps and work along the slope. Scan the bottom methodically for anglerfish, sea moths, and mantis shrimp burrows. Check featherstars for commensal shrimps, sea whips for associated critters, and gorgonias for invertebrates. The productive range extends from the shallows to 30 meters. Being a house reef, you can dive at your own pace and schedule without waiting for a boat.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry directly from Buceo Anilao Dive Resort. Walk in from the resort's beach access point, put on fins in shallow water, and descend onto the slope. Exit at the same point. The convenience of a shore dive means you can dive this site at any time, including dawn, dusk, and night.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Night dives on the house reef are exceptional -- different critters emerge after dark, and the site is easily accessible
- The convenience of shore access means you can do unlimited dives here between boat dives
- Ask the resort dive guides where the current anglerfish residents are -- they track their locations
- Move slowly and scan carefully; the critter density rewards patience over distance covered
- An excellent site for newer underwater photographers to practice without the pressure of a boat dive schedule

## Safety Considerations

Buceo House Reef is a straightforward shore dive with generally mild conditions, making it suitable for beginners. The sandy slope descends gradually, making depth control easy. The maximum depth of 30 meters is at the deeper end, and most productive diving is shallower. Night dives should be done with a good primary light and a backup, and it is advisable to carry a marker light visible from shore.

## Photography

Buceo House Reef is a macro photographer's playground. The density and variety of subjects -- anglerfish, sea moths, nudibranchs, squat lobsters, mantis shrimp, and numerous shrimp species -- means you can fill dive after dive with different subjects. A 60mm or 100mm macro lens covers most critters. The unlimited shore access allows you to return with different lens setups and try different techniques. Sea moths are unusual and photogenic subjects. The various commensal shrimps on featherstars and sea whips are excellent supermacro targets. Night dives with a snoot or focus light add creative possibilities.
