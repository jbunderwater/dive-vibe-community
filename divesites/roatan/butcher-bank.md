# Butcher Bank

*An offshore seamount and reef bank southeast of Roatán, renowned for large pelagic visitors and excellent shark encounters in open water.*

## Overview

Butcher Bank is an offshore reef structure located southeast of Roatán, rising from deeper water to a shallow pinnacle plateau. Unlike the fringing reefs and walls directly adjacent to the island, Butcher Bank is an open-water site requiring a 15–20 minute boat crossing. The seamount top sits at approximately 12–15 meters while the sides drop to 37 meters and beyond. The offshore location and associated upwellings attract larger pelagic species than the inshore reefs. Visibility is typically excellent at 25–35 meters. This is a site for divers who want to experience the pelagic zone that sits just beyond Roatán's reef system.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Offshore southeast of Roatán, Bay Islands, Honduras (open water crossing required)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 37 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–35 meters (80–115 feet)
- **Current**: Variable; can be moderate to strong at the bank edges
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmer seas needed for the crossing; April to September generally best

## Marine Life

Butcher Bank is noted for its shark encounters. Caribbean reef sharks are regularly seen circling the bank. Occasional whale shark sightings have been reported, as the offshore location is on the edge of whale shark habitat. Eagle rays are more reliably encountered here than at inshore sites. Schools of horse-eye jacks and greater amberjack patrol the water column. Large cubera snappers and yellowfin groupers hold station near the bank's deeper sections. Hammerhead sharks have been reported but are uncommon. The bank top has excellent coral coverage with parrotfish, surgeonfish, and triggerfish.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the bank top at 12–15 meters and begin an exploration of the plateau, keeping the drop-off within sight. Use the bank top as a reference point while making short excursions to the deeper slopes at 20–30 meters. Spend time hovering at the bank edge watching the open water — pelagic species often approach from the blue. Return to the top before ascending, and perform the safety stop in the shallower section of the bank. Deploy an SMB early for the drift pickup boat.

## Entry and Exit

Requires an offshore boat crossing — conditions must be suitable. Boat entry via giant stride; surface pickup by the chase boat after the safety stop. This is a drift dive format when current is running.

## Tips

- Check sea conditions carefully before committing to this offshore crossing — the site is exposed and unsuitable in rough weather
- Keep alert to the open water beyond the bank edge — large animals approach from outside your peripheral vision
- This is one of the best sites from Roatán for a genuine pelagic dive experience
- Carry a signaling device (audible and visual) in addition to an SMB given the offshore location
- Schedule Butcher Bank as the first dive of the day when conditions and visibility are typically at their best

## Safety

Offshore dive requiring excellent conditions and experienced boat crew. The crossing can be rough in trade wind weather. Confirm the dive only in suitable sea state. Current on the bank edges requires active management. The depth drops quickly off the plateau — monitor depth continuously. Always carry and deploy an SMB for the open-water ascent.

## Photography

Butcher Bank is Roatán's best site for pelagic photography. Wide-angle shots with reef sharks in the blue water create dramatic compositions. Schools of horse-eye jacks in spiral formations around the bank top are stunning subjects. The offshore visibility is typically the clearest of all Roatán sites, giving a pure blue-water backdrop that makes wildlife images pop.
