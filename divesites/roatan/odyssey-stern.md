## Odyssey Stern

*The stern section of the Odyssey — a 91-meter cargo freighter scuttled on November 15, 2002 off Roatán's south coast — offering the deepest penetration diving on the wreck with multi-level cargo holds and stairwells.*

## Overview

Odyssey Stern refers specifically to the stern portion of the Odyssey, Roatán's largest diveable wreck. The Odyssey is a 91-meter (300-foot) cargo freighter donated by Hybur Limited (the Hyde family) to Anthony's Key Resort after an engine-room fire destroyed the vessel's commercial viability; it was scuttled on November 15, 2002. The stern section, which contains the main cargo holds and engine spaces, rests at the deepest part of the wreck's profile at 30–33 meters. The bow and upper deck structures rise to approximately 18–20 meters. The stern is the preferred access point for divers seeking to explore the interior — cargo holds and accessible passageways can be entered with appropriate wreck training and redundant lighting. After more than two decades submerged, the hull is heavily encrusted with sponges, black coral, and wire corals. Visibility is consistently 25–35 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South coast of Roatán, Bay Islands, Honduras
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–35 meters (80–115 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate; can be stronger on the south wall
- **Best Time**: Year-round; best conditions December to April

## Marine Life

The Odyssey wreck complex supports groupers, cubera snappers, moray eels, schools of horse-eye jacks, and black durgon. Encrusting sponges cover the hull in orange, red, and purple patches. The stern section's enclosed spaces concentrate fish species that shelter in low-light environments, including soldierfish. Roatán's general reef community inhabits the surrounding sandy shelf. No site-specific marine life census for the Odyssey stern section has been published; species listed reflect multiple dive operator reports for the wreck as a whole.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the upper hull at 18–20 meters and navigate aft toward the stern section. Drop to the main hull at 25–30 meters, where cargo holds are accessible from the exterior. The deepest penetrable spaces reach 30–33 meters — manage bottom time carefully at this depth. Interior penetration requires proper wreck diver certification and redundant lighting; the spaces are dark and silt easily. Ascend the mooring line or make a free ascent with SMB to a safety stop at 5 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Served by West End and West Bay dive operators. Permanent mooring on the wreck. Entry via giant stride or back roll.

## Tips

- Wreck diver certification (or equivalent penetration training) is strongly advised before entering the cargo holds
- Nitrox 32% significantly extends bottom time at 30 meters
- Carry a redundant light source — the hold interiors are completely dark
- Complete all deep sections first, then ascend to the shallower upper deck structures

## Safety

Advanced dive requiring solid buoyancy and gas management. The 30–33 meter depth limits bottom time on air. Do not penetrate enclosed spaces without proper wreck training, a torch, and a dive buddy who remains at the entrance. The hull has sharp edges and protruding metal. Always make a 3–5 minute safety stop at 5 meters.
