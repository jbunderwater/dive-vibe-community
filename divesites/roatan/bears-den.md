# Bear's Den

*A remarkable north-coast cavern accessible through a vertical chimney chute — a favourite among experienced divers for its circular sandy-floored chamber and resident critters.*

## Overview

Bear's Den is a cavern dive on the north side of Roatán, near Half Moon Bay and the West End area. The site's defining feature is a vertical chimney passage — a cylindrical swimthrough descending through the reef — that leads into a large, circular cavern chamber. The chimney chute enters at approximately 15 meters (50 feet); the cavern itself opens into an "enormous and beautiful" room with a sandy floor at around 9 meters (30 feet). Sunlight filters in through the chimney and additional reef openings, creating shafts of blue light across the sandy floor. The site is compared by local operators to a natural cathedral and is one of the more memorable reef structures on the north side of the island.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North coast of Roatán, near Half Moon Bay, Bay Islands, Honduras
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–28 meters
- **Current**: Generally light on the north side
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Bear's Den's sheltered cavern environment supports species that favour low-light, protected habitats. Spotted drums — consistently reported by operators — shelter in the darker areas of the cavern. Glassy sweepers pack into tighter recesses. Spiny lobsters, crabs, and nudibranchs inhabit the cavern walls and floor. Seahorses have been reported at this site. Large groupers and barracuda are sometimes seen near the chimney entrance on the exterior reef. The exterior reef surrounding the site has the typical north-coast Roatán community of parrotfish, angelfish, and wrasse.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the mooring and locate the chimney entrance at 15 meters. Drop through the vertical chute — it requires single-file passage — emerging into the cavern chamber at approximately 9 meters. Take time to explore the room and allow eyes to adjust to the lower light. Exit the cavern the same way you entered, ascending through the chimney, or locate an alternative shallow exit if your guide leads one. Complete your safety stop on the shallow exterior reef at 5 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Served by West End, Sandy Bay, and Half Moon Bay dive operators. Mooring at the site. Entry via giant stride from the boat. Bring a dive torch — the chimney interior is dark enough to benefit from artificial light.

## Tips

- Bring a dive torch — the chimney is dark and highlights the cavern details
- Descend the chimney single-file and wait at the bottom for your buddy before exploring
- The cavern floor is sandy — maintain excellent buoyancy to avoid silting the chamber
- Combine Bear's Den with nearby Spooky Channel for a full north-side cave/cavern dive day
- Night dives at Bear's Den are exceptional — the cavern critters are fully active after dark

## Safety

Cavern dive with good light references — natural light enters through the chimney and reef openings, so true cave conditions do not apply. However, buoyancy discipline inside the chimney is critical: fin contact with the walls disturbs sediment and can disorient other divers. Descend the chimney slowly. Always know your exit route. Dive with a buddy and carry a torch.

## Photography

The chimney chute is one of the most architecturally striking features on Roatán's north side. Shoot upward through the chimney toward the daylight for a classic beam-of-light composition. Wide-angle shots of the cavern chamber with a diver for scale convey the size. Spotted drum portraits under coral ledges are a reliable close-up subject.
