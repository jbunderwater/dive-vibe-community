## Roatan

Honduras' premier dive island, sitting directly on the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef with extensive documented dive sites, dramatic wall dives starting at 6 meters, and two on-island recompression chambers.

## Description

Roatan is the largest of Honduras' Bay Islands, located 50 km off the northern coast in the Caribbean Sea. It is one of the most dive-dense destinations in the Caribbean. Sites span a wide range of depths and types, including walls, reefs, caves, wrecks, drift dives, and pinnacles. The island sits on the southern edge of the Mesoamerican Barrier Reef — the second-largest barrier reef system in the world — giving divers direct access to steep walls, swim-throughs, and healthy coral formations along nearly the entire coastline.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Wall Diving**: Wall dive sites line the island's reef, many starting at just 6-10 meters before dropping into deep blue. Destinations like Mary's Place offer dramatic vertical fissures and swim-throughs.
- **Boat Diving**: Boat-accessible sites spread across the island's north and south shores, with most dive operators running short 5-15 minute boat rides to sites.
- **Shore Diving**: Shore-accessible sites, including several along West Bay and Half Moon Bay.
- **Wreck Diving**: Wreck sites including the El Aguila (a 63-meter cargo ship intentionally sunk in 1997), the Odyssey Wreck, and the Shang Ying.
- **Cave Diving**: Cave and cavern sites offering swim-throughs and tunnels within the reef structure.
- **Reef Diving**: Reef sites with healthy hard and soft coral coverage along the barrier reef.
- **Night Diving**: Widely available from operators across the island, with bioluminescent displays and active nocturnal marine life.

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Juan Manuel Galvez International Airport (RTB) receives direct flights from Houston, Miami, Dallas, and Atlanta, plus regional connections from San Pedro Sula and La Ceiba on the Honduran mainland. Ferries operate from La Ceiba.
- **Dive Operators**: Dozens of operators island-wide, including Sun Divers (eco-focused, small groups), Reef Gliders in West End, and Naboo Dive Center in West Bay. Roatan is known for competitive dive pricing in the Caribbean.
- **Accommodation**: Options range from budget hostels in West End to luxury resorts in West Bay and French Harbour. Many resorts offer dive-and-stay packages.
- **Transportation**: Taxis, rental cars, and water taxis connect the main dive areas of West End, West Bay, and French Harbour. The island is about 77 km long.
- **Facilities**: Two recompression chambers on the island provide emergency hyperbaric treatment.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature ranges from 26-29°C (78-84°F) year-round, with summer shallows reaching 31°C. Visibility typically 24-30 meters (80-100 ft), with spring and summer offering the clearest conditions.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Hawksbill and green sea turtles, spotted eagle rays, southern stingrays, nurse sharks, Caribbean reef sharks, moray eels, barracuda, parrotfish, and dense schools of blue tangs and creole wrasse. Whale sharks occasionally pass through, though they are more reliably seen at nearby Utila.
- **Conservation**: The Roatan Marine Park manages the island's reef system, enforcing mooring buoy use, no-touch policies, and mandatory reef fees that fund conservation efforts.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: Year-round diving, with the dry season (March-September) offering the best visibility and calmest seas. Whale shark season peaks March-May and August-October (more common at Utila).
- **Currency**: Honduran Lempira (HNL), though US dollars are widely accepted.
- **Language**: Spanish is the official language; English is widely spoken in tourist and dive areas.
- **Safety**: Two recompression chambers on the island (one at Cornerstone Medical Center). DAN insurance recommended. Be aware of sun exposure and hydration at tropical latitudes.
