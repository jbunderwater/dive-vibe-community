## Wrasse Hole

Wrasse Hole is a drift dive site in Roatán, Caribbean.

## Overview

Wrasse Hole is a drift dive site along Roatan's south coast barrier reef. As the name suggests, this site is known for concentrations of wrasse species riding the current along the reef wall. The drift carries divers past healthy coral formations adorned with barrel sponges and gorgonians, with opportunities to spot eagle rays, groupers, and moray eels tucked into wall crevices. Visibility typically ranges 20-40 meters with warm Caribbean water temperatures of 26-29°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Roatán, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters

## Dive Profile

Enter the water up-current and allow the flow to carry you along the reef. Stay close to the wall or reef structure and use it for reference. Your dive guide will manage the drift direction and boat pickup location. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop.

## Photography

Drift dives offer unique opportunities to photograph marine life in motion. Wide-angle shots of schooling fish and passing pelagics work well. Shoot into the current to capture marine life facing you.
