## Lighthouse Reef

Lighthouse Reef is a reef dive site in Roatán, Caribbean.

## Overview

Lighthouse Reef is located just outside Half Moon Bay and is easily accessible just a minute boat ride from West End. This convenient, accessible site features amazing walls, schools of fish, and cool macro critters. The shallows are between 6-12 meters (20-40 feet), while the adjoining wall drops off to 39 meters (130 feet). Large sandy patches make it ideal for coursework, and it is also a great location for night dives. Water temperatures average 26-29°C.

## Marine Life

Divers at this site can expect to encounter sea turtles (green, hawksbill), southern stingrays, eagle rays, nurse sharks, reef sharks, barracuda, parrotfish, angelfish. Additional species commonly sighted include blue tangs, trumpetfish, moray eels, lobsters.

## Dive Profile

The shallows are between 6-12 meters (20-40 feet), while the adjoining wall drops off to 39 meters (130 feet). Large sandy patches throughout the site provide easy navigation and are perfect for training dives and navigation practice. Schools of fish and interesting macro critters are found throughout. Most features of interest are found between 6-18 meters, making it suitable for divers of various experience levels.

## Entry and Exit

Enter from the shore following established entry points. Check conditions before entering and be mindful of waves, surge, and underwater obstacles. Navigate to the dive site using natural landmarks or compass bearings. Exit at the same location, approaching the shore carefully to avoid surge zones.

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of boat traffic, fire coral, sea urchins in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
