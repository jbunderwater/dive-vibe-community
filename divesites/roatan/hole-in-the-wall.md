## Hole In The Wall

*A distinctive wall dive near Sandy Bay on Roatán's north coast, centered on a sand-chute tunnel that passes through the reef from the back side to the open wall face.*

## Overview

Hole In The Wall is a wall and swim-through site on Roatán's north coast near Sandy Bay. The site is named for a tunnel/opening in the reef through which divers pass from the shallow back-reef area to the open wall face. The passage — a sand-chute tunnel — begins at approximately 40 feet (12 meters) on the inner side and exits onto the wall at around 30 meters, creating the characteristic "hole in the wall" transition. The surrounding reef structure has an irregular, heavily textured character with additional swim-throughs and recesses. Eagle rays and sea turtles are reported visitors. The site is classified as a wall dive with significant swim-through elements rather than a true cave system — natural light is maintained throughout the passage.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Sandy Bay, north coast of Roatán, Bay Islands, Honduras
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters

## Dive Profile

Approach the cave entrance carefully and assess conditions before entering. Maintain excellent buoyancy to avoid disturbing sediment. Follow the cave walls and always keep the entrance in sight unless properly trained for overhead environments. Exit the same way you entered.

## Photography

The interplay of light and shadow at cave entrances creates dramatic wide-angle shots. Use a torch to illuminate interior details and bring out colors. Silhouette shots of divers in the entrance are particularly striking.
