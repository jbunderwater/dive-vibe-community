## Aksona

Aksona Bay is an advanced wall and drop-off dive site in the Bodrum area of Turkey's Aegean Sea, famous for its dramatic underwater topography and challenging diving conditions.

## Overview

Aksona Bay is one of Bodrum's premier advanced dive sites, named after the legendary sponge diver "Aksona Mehmet" of Bodrum. Located approximately 50 minutes by boat from Bodrum harbor, this site is renowned among experienced divers for its dramatic wall structures, steep drop-offs, and challenging conditions. The site features a complex underwater topography with multiple drop-off zones descending from just 3 meters to depths exceeding 45 meters, making it suitable only for Advanced-certified divers with good buoyancy control and depth management skills.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bodrum area, Aegean Sea, Turkey (between Pınar Wreck and Poyraz Bay)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters
- **Typical Depth Range**: 3-35 meters (recreational limit), extends to 45+ meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 meters
- **Current**: Variable to strong (can be challenging/dive-aborting)
- **Best Time**: May to October (calmer seas, better visibility)
- **Water Temperature**: 16-28°C (varies seasonally)
- **Boat Time from Bodrum**: 45-50 minutes
- **Certification Requirement**: Advanced Open Water minimum; Intermediate not recommended

## Dive Profile

**Shallow Descent Zone** (3-10m): Begin at the shallow water zone near the starting point. Monitor conditions and drift patterns immediately.

**Upper Wall Section** (10-25m): The first wall section descends gradually, offering excellent marine life observation zone and lower-stress diving for orientation.

**First Major Drop** (17m to depth): Initial descent reaches 17 meters before the major wall drop-off begins. This is a good turnaround point for conservative divers.

**Deep Wall Section** (25-45m+): The dramatic wall face provides visual interest for experienced wall divers. Due to depth and potential narcosis, bottom time here should be carefully limited (8-12 minutes maximum at 35m+).

**Ascent**: Controlled ascent with mandatory safety stop at 5 meters. Total dive time typically 45-60 minutes including decompression.

## Marine Life & Features

Aksona's dramatic topography supports diverse marine ecosystems including:
- Schools of groupers (friendly, acclimated individuals)
- Barracuda and amberjack in the water column
- Moray eels and conger eels in crevices
- Octopus and cuttlefish
- Sponge gardens covering rock formations
- Soft coral communities
- Fish species: sea bream, damselfish, wrasses
- Crabs, lobsters, and other crustaceans in sheltered areas

The rocky formations are heavily encrusted with Mediterranean flora and fauna, creating complex habitat structures.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by boat from Bodrum dive operators. The boat journey is approximately 45-50 minutes. Entry is via giant stride or back roll from the dive boat. Divers typically follow the anchor line or natural descent to the wall structure. An SMB (surface marker buoy) is deployed during the safety stop for boat pickup. Exit is standard back-roll or ladder re-entry.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Advanced certification required**: This site demands good depth management and buoyancy control
- Check weather and sea conditions thoroughly before committing—strong winds create challenging current conditions
- Dive with experienced local operators familiar with Aksona's variable conditions
- Plan your dive profile carefully considering the depth and current exposure
- Use a dive computer capable of handling the depth range
- Monitor air consumption closely—the distance to the wall and current can increase consumption
- Bring a torch to illuminate deep wall crevices and structures
- Expect variable conditions—this site can be extremely challenging on windy days
- Best dived in calmer morning conditions when current is predictable
- Late May through September generally provides most stable conditions
- The site gets less crowded than other Bodrum sites due to its advanced difficulty level

## Safety Considerations

**Current**: The primary hazard at this site. Currents are variable and can be strong, sometimes making the site undiveable ("dive-aborting" conditions noted in dive operator reports). Always verify current conditions with your dive master before descent.

**Depth**: The wall drops to depths where nitrogen narcosis becomes a significant factor. Remain aware of narcosis effects and dive conservatively.

**Buoyancy**: The site's topography and current demands excellent buoyancy control. Any loss of buoyancy control results in rapid uncontrolled descent or ascent.

**Visibility**: While often 15-25 meters, visibility can be affected by current. Poor visibility combined with current makes the site exponentially more challenging.

**Decompression**: Mandatory safety stops are required. Plan bottom time accordingly and monitor your dive computer.

**Skill Requirements**: This is not a site for building skills. Only dive here if you have advanced experience with wall dives and deep diving.

## Certification Recommendations

- Advanced Open Water (minimum)
- Deep Diving Specialty (strongly recommended)
- Nitrox Certification (recommended to extend safe bottom time)

## Photography

Wall photography requires careful attention to buoyancy control while maintaining lighting. The wall face provides dramatic wide-angle subjects. Powerful torches help illuminate the deep wall sections and bring out colors lost at depth. The sponge-covered topography offers interesting textural subjects. Macro photography is possible on the upper sections but difficult at depth.

## Seasonal Conditions

**May-June**: Water warming, currents often manageable, visibility improving (18-25m)
**July-August**: Warmest water, can be more stable, early morning best for current
**September-October**: Still warm, variable conditions, visibility often excellent (20-25m)
**November-April**: Not recommended—cold water, stronger winter storms
