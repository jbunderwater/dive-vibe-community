## Shark Sculpture

Shark Sculpture is a beginner-friendly reef and art dive site located at Pigeon Island near Kaş on Turkey's Turquoise Mediterranean coast.

## Overview

This unique dive site combines marine art with natural reef exploration. The famous white marble shark sculpture sits in shallow water at approximately 5-10 meters, making it accessible to beginners and snorkelers. Beyond the sculpture, the broader Pigeon Island site features a healthy reef system with scattered wreck remains including an old wooden wreck at 22 meters and a sheet metal artificial reef at 28 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Pigeon Island, Kaş, Antalya, Turkey
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters (depending on season)
- **Current**: Minimal to light
- **Best Time**: May to October (water temperature peaks at 28°C in August)
- **Water Temperature**: 16-28°C depending on season

## Marine Life & Features

The sculpture area attracts schools of striped groupers, particularly friendly and acclimated to divers. The deeper reef sections host groupers, moray eels, octopus, barracuda, nudibranchs, and seahorses. Scattered around the site are broken amphorae, ancient pottery fragments that add historical interest. Sponges and soft corals colonize the wreck structures.

## Dive Profile

**Shallow Section** (0-10m): Begin at the shark sculpture in the shallows. This section is ideal for photography and observation. Visibility is typically excellent in the shallow zone.

**Reef Section** (10-20m): Descend to explore the natural reef structures with their healthy coral communities and fish populations. Plan bottom time for 30-40 minutes depending on your certification.

**Deeper Features** (20-28m): The old wooden wreck and sheet metal artificial reef provide interesting penetration opportunities for experienced divers, but exploration of these features should be done carefully.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Kaş dive operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll from the dive boat. Coordinate with your operator for descent to either the shallow sculpture area or deeper reef sections. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This is an excellent site for underwater photography—bring a camera to capture the sculpture and marine life
- Go early in the dive to see the shark sculpture when light conditions are best
- The site is suitable for certification dives and training due to calm conditions and reasonable depths
- Visit during May to October for best water clarity and warmth
- The site can become crowded with tour groups, so diving with a smaller guided group or early morning is recommended

## Safety Considerations

Currents are generally minimal but check conditions with local operators. Visibility can vary seasonally. Respect the sculpture and marine life. Dive within your certification limits and always use a buddy system.

## Photography

Excellent site for both wide-angle (capturing the sculpture in context) and macro photography (nudibranchs, small crustaceans). The shallow depths allow natural light to illuminate subjects. The marble sculpture provides striking contrast with the blue water background.
