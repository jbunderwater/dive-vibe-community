## Dominican Republic

Silver Bank humpback whale encounters, Catalina Island wall dives, Bayahibe reef systems, and historic wrecks in La Caleta Underwater National Park.

## Description

The Dominican Republic offers diverse dive sites ranging from 9 to 44 meters depth, spread across Bayahibe, Santo Domingo, and the south coast. Bayahibe serves as the country's primary dive hub with over 20 official sites, including access to Catalina Island and Saona Island. The La Caleta Underwater National Park near Santo Domingo preserves deliberately sunk wrecks and cave systems, while Silver Bank off the north coast provides seasonal humpback whale encounters that draw divers worldwide.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: Boat-accessible sites including Catalina Island walls, Bayahibe reefs, and offshore wrecks
- **Shore Diving**: Shore-accessible sites along the south coast
- **Wreck Diving**: Wreck sites including the Atlantic Princess (11m, encrusted with sponges and coral), the St. George (deep artificial reef), and Guaraguao (sunk in 1999 with displayed underwater artifacts including muskets and cannon balls)
- **Cave Diving**: Cave sites including La Sirena Cave near La Caleta
- **Wall Diving**: Wall sites at Catalina Island and along the south coast drop-offs
- **Reef Diving**: Reef sites including Dos Cocos, La Tortuga, and Aquarium Profundo at Bayahibe

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Punta Cana International Airport (PUJ) is closest to Bayahibe (1.5 hours drive). Las Americas International Airport (SDQ) in Santo Domingo serves La Caleta sites. Bayahibe is the main departure point for boat dives.
- **Dive Operators**: Multiple operators in Bayahibe including Reef Oasis Dive Club, ScubaFun, Dressel Divers, Coral Point Diving, and Pro Dive International. Several resort-based operators serve Punta Cana.
- **Accommodation**: Bayahibe village offers small hotels and guesthouses; nearby Dominicus has all-inclusive resorts. Punta Cana and Boca Chica provide additional resort options.
- **Transportation**: Taxis and private transfers from airports to Bayahibe; local operators provide boat transport to dive sites
- **Facilities**: Full-service dive centers with PADI certification, equipment rental, and multi-dive packages. Several operators offer nitrox.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 26-29°C year-round, visibility 20-40 meters in Bayahibe area, generally calm Caribbean conditions on the south coast
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Angelfish, squirrelfish, barracudas, moray eels, lobsters, nurse sharks, spotted eagle rays, sea turtles, and diverse reef fish. Silver Bank (seasonal, January-March) hosts North Atlantic humpback whale breeding grounds.
- **Conservation**: La Caleta Underwater National Park protects wreck sites and cave systems near Santo Domingo. Catalina Island is within a protected marine area. Coral reef restoration projects active in Bayahibe.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: December to April for driest weather and calmest seas. Whale watching at Silver Bank runs January to March. Hurricane season is June to November.
- **Currency**: Dominican Peso (DOP); US dollars widely accepted at tourist areas
- **Language**: Spanish
- **Safety**: Recompression chamber available in Santo Domingo. Currents can be strong at Catalina Island wall sites. Cave diving at La Sirena requires proper training and certification.
