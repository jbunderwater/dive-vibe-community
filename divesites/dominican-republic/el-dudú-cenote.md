# El Dudú Cenote

*A large freshwater sinkhole near Cabrera on the Dominican Republic's north coast, with a main tunnel extending over 100 metres, a pronounced halocline, and a breathable air dome 50 metres in.*

## Overview

El Dudú (also written Dudu) is a freshwater sinkhole and cave system located approximately 15 minutes east of Cabrera on the north coast of the Dominican Republic. Entry is via concrete stairs leading directly to the water's edge. The main cave tunnel extends over 100 metres and features a halocline (a mixing zone of fresh and salt water) near the entrance and mainline. The mainline sits right at the halocline level. A large air dome — featuring a breathable air pocket with no surface exit — is located approximately 50 metres into the passage. The main chamber contains columns exceeding 15 metres in height. Maximum depth in the main tunnel reaches 20 metres.

The cave connects to a second entrance, Cueva de Lily, accessible after approximately 45 minutes of swimming for advanced cave divers. A third entrance, Pozo de los Caballos, is accessible from the cavern zone. The cavern zone at the main entrance features unusual water colours and shellfish-encrusted stalactites.

El Dudú is a tourist attraction in addition to a dive site: ziplines and cliff jumping operate above the sinkhole, so diving should be coordinated to avoid surface conflicts.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Cabrera (approximately 15 minutes east), north coast Dominican Republic
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry (concrete stairs)
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern (freshwater)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate (cavern zone); cave certification required for tunnel sections beyond the cavern zone
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: Exceeds 50 metres in undisturbed conditions
- **Current**: Minimal; halocline creates an optical mixing effect
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

El Dudú is a freshwater cave system. No marine fauna are present. The halocline near the entrance creates a visually striking optical effect — a shimmering, mirror-like layer where fresh and salt water meet — that is the primary sensory experience of the dive alongside the geological formations. The cave system supports bats, cave insects (whip scorpions, tarantulas), and snakes in the aerial portions of the cave above the waterline, none of which are underwater.

## Dive Profile

Descend the concrete steps to the water's edge and enter the main pool. The cavern zone is immediately visible with good natural light. The main passage extends toward the halocline and then beyond to the air dome at 50 metres. Maximum depth is 20 metres in the main tunnel. The air dome at 50 metres in provides a breathable air pocket — a distinctive feature reported by the DR-SS cave database — but note this dome has no outside exit. Certified cave divers continue beyond toward the Cueva de Lily connection (approximately 45 minutes of swimming).

## Entry and Exit

El Dudú is easily accessible by road from Cabrera or from the Samaná/Las Terrenas area. Concrete stairs make entry straightforward. Dive operators based on the north coast offer guided cave diving packages here; this site requires advance booking and appropriate cave certification for sections beyond the cavern zone.

## Tips

- The halocline creates a disorienting visual shimmer where fresh and salt water mix — prepare for this optical effect, which can briefly affect depth perception
- The air dome 50 metres into the tunnel is one of the most distinctive features of El Dudú — experienced cave divers report it as a highlight
- Coordinate your dive with the zipline and tourist operators above to avoid surface conflicts
- Bring a primary and backup torch — the cave becomes dark quickly beyond the cavern zone

## Safety

The cavern zone can be experienced by supervised divers with Open Water certification, but all sections of the cave beyond the light zone require cave diving certification, redundant gas, and proper line work. The halocline can cause brief spatial disorientation. Do not penetrate the tunnel without appropriate qualifications. The sinkhole is also used for non-diving tourist activities — confirm there is no conflict at the surface before diving.
