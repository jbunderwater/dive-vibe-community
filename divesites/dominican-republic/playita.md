# Playita

*A protected shallow reef near Las Galeras on the Samaná Peninsula, ideal for beginners and used for training dives by local operators.*

## Overview

Playita — meaning "little beach" — is a shallow coral reef site located near Las Galeras at the eastern tip of the Samaná Peninsula. The reef runs along a protected bay where depths stay between 3 and 9 metres, making it one of the most accessible dive sites in the Dominican Republic for new divers and those completing certification dives. The sheltered position reduces current and wave action, providing calm, clear conditions most of the year. Visibility averages 15–20 metres. Water temperatures hold at 26–28°C year-round.

Las Galeras Divers — the local dive centre based in the same village — and other Samaná Peninsula operators use Playita as a beginner and training dive site. The site is also snorkeable from the beach at Playita itself.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Las Galeras, Samaná Peninsula, Dominican Republic
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 9 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 metres (50–65 feet)
- **Current**: Minimal; well-protected bay
- **Best Time**: Year-round; December to April for best visibility

## Marine Life

The Samaná Peninsula reef system hosts hawksbill and green sea turtles, spotted eagle rays passing through sandy areas, parrotfish, French and queen angelfish, blue tang, sergeant majors, and peacock flounders on sandy patches. No site-specific species reports were found for Playita as a distinct site from other Las Galeras dive sites. Juvenile fish sheltering in coral heads is noted as a feature of these shallow protected reefs. Regional reef fauna for the Samaná Peninsula applies. Humpback whale song is audible on dives here between January and March.

## Dive Profile

The reef top sits at 3–5 metres with gradual slopes to a maximum of 9 metres over sandy patches. Begin on the shallowest section and work the perimeter at your own pace. Extended bottom times are possible at this depth. No safety stop is required given the maximum depth, but hovering at 5 metres for 3 minutes is good practice.

## Entry and Exit

Short boat ride from Las Galeras. Entry via ladder or giant stride depending on the boat. The shallow, calm conditions make exit easy even for new divers. Snorkellers can also access Playita directly from the beach.

## Tips

- An excellent site for night diving — the reef comes alive after dark with octopus, Spanish lobsters, and sleeping parrotfish
- Morning dives have the best light for photography
- Combine with a deeper site at nearby Las Galeras for a two-tank day
- Snorkelers can join from the beach — confirm boat operator details in Las Galeras village

## Safety

Minimal hazards at this site. Watch for fire coral on the reef top. Avoid touching anything. Boat traffic in the bay is generally light. Carry a surface marker buoy as a precaution even at this depth.

## Photography

Excellent conditions for wide-angle reef photography in shallow, well-lit water. Hawksbill turtle encounters offer portrait opportunities. Macro on sea fans may reveal small gobies and flamingo tongues. The colorful reef fish population is accessible to photographers in calm conditions.
