# Aquarium

*Bayahibe's most popular beginner site, named for the exceptional density and variety of Caribbean fish life in calm, clear shallow water.*

## Overview

Aquarium is a shallow fringing reef site operated from Bayahibe, named for the sheer density and variety of Caribbean reef fish that populate it. At depths of 5–12 metres over a mixed reef-and-sand bottom, the site is accessible, calm, and consistently impressive for first-time divers and photographers. It pairs naturally with The Wall for a classic Bayahibe two-tank morning — The Wall done first at greater depth, followed by Aquarium as the shallower second dive. Visibility is typically 20–28 metres. Water stays at 27–29°C most of the year.

Seahorse sightings are specifically confirmed at Aquarium by multiple sources — operators brief divers that guides typically spot seahorses here. This is one of the key reasons this site earns its reputation among Bayahibe divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Offshore Bayahibe, southeastern Dominican Republic
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–28 metres (65–90 feet)
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: Year-round; one of the most consistently accessible sites in the country

## Marine Life

Sources confirm the following at Aquarium specifically: seahorses (noted as a reliable sighting here with guide assistance), yellowtail snappers (schools), French grunts, French angelfish, queen angelfish, parrotfish, cleaning stations with shrimp and wrasse, hawksbill turtles feeding on the reef, green moray eels, spotted drums (near larger coral heads), and garden eels at the sandy reef border. The site is known for fish density — over 30 species visible simultaneously on a typical dive, according to operator descriptions. Nurse sharks are reported as occasional visitors under ledges.

## Dive Profile

An exploratory shallow dive. Descend to 5–6 metres over the reef crest and move slowly over the reef, pausing at coral heads and overhangs. Drop to 10–12 metres where the reef transitions to sand. Average dive time is 50–65 minutes. Beginners can focus entirely on enjoying the marine life without concern about depth management.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Bayahibe, typically paired as the second dive of a morning two-tank trip. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Exit via boat ladder in calm conditions.

## Tips

- Guides know where to find seahorses — follow their lead and move slowly near sea fans and coral rubble on the sandy-reef border
- Move slowly and avoid sudden movements near fish — they are accustomed to divers but still more active when approached gently
- Cleaning stations on large coral heads reward patient observation — fish queue to be cleaned by shrimp
- Combine with The Wall (done first, deeper) for a classic Bayahibe two-tank morning

## Safety

One of the safest dive environments in the Dominican Republic. Fire coral — the white, branching variety — is present on the reef and causes a burning sting on contact. Maintain buoyancy and avoid touching the reef. Sea urchins occupy crevices. Follow dive guide instructions regarding approach to marine life.

## Photography

Aquarium is among the best sites in the Dominican Republic for reef fish photography. The fish density and calm behavior allow close approach. Wide-angle lenses capture sweeping reef scenes. The macro photographer will find seahorses (with guide help), spotted drums, and cleaning shrimp as subjects. Natural light photography produces excellent results in these shallow, clear conditions.
