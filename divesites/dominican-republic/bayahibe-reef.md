# Bayahibe Reef

*A shallow Caribbean reef directly offshore from Bayahibe village, an ideal warm-up or certification dive on the southeast coast.*

## Overview

Bayahibe Reef lies just offshore from the small fishing and dive village of Bayahibe on the southeastern Dominican Republic coast. The reef system runs parallel to the coast at depths of 5–14 metres, featuring brain corals, star corals, and encrusting formations over a sandy bottom. Bayahibe is the primary dive hub for the La Romana area and one of the most active dive communities in the country, with operators including Coral Point Diving, Dressel Divers, Pro Dive International, and ScubaCaribe all running boats from the village. Day boats to the local reef sites depart frequently throughout the day. Visibility averages 20–25 metres. Water temperatures sit at 27–29°C for most of the year.

The site serves both first-time divers completing Discover Scuba experiences and certified divers wanting a relaxed shallow dive before a deeper afternoon trip to sites like the St. George Wreck or Catalina Wall.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bayahibe village, southeast Dominican Republic (La Romana province)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–25 metres (65–80 feet)
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: Year-round; consistently calm on this leeward coast

## Marine Life

The broader Bayahibe reef system is confirmed to host queen angelfish, French angelfish, parrotfish (multiple species), yellowtail snappers, groupers, nurse sharks under ledges, sea turtles (green and hawksbill), moray eels, lobsters, rays, barracuda, and cleaning stations on coral heads. Site-specific reports for Bayahibe Reef (as distinct from other Bayahibe sites) reference parrotfish, grunts, and sea turtle encounters as the reliable highlights. The sandy areas around the reef edge shelter southern stingrays and peacock flounders.

## Dive Profile

A gentle dive over the reef structure. Descend to the reef top at 5–8 metres and follow the contour as the bottom gradually deepens to 12–14 metres over sandy patches. Most guided dives run 50–60 minutes. No safety stop is required at this depth. Beginners will find the low depth and calm conditions good for practicing skills.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats depart from Bayahibe's small waterfront. Short 10-minute crossing. Entry via giant stride. Multiple dive operators are based in the village with boats running throughout the day.

## Tips

- This site is frequently used for Open Water certification dives
- Night dives on Bayahibe Reef are productive — lobster and octopus are active after dark
- Combine with Aquarium (another nearby shallow site) for a two-tank morning
- Bayahibe village has good seafood restaurants for a post-dive meal

## Safety

Minimal hazards. Fire coral is present on the reef structure — avoid contact. Stay neutrally buoyant to avoid breaking fragile coral. Boat traffic near Bayahibe can be active on weekends. Always surface near the dive boat or with a deployed SMB.

## Photography

Excellent ambient-light photography given the shallow, clear conditions. Wide-angle shots of the reef structure with natural light look particularly compelling. Sea turtle encounters are the photographic highlight. Macro subjects on the coral surface include Christmas tree worms and cleaner shrimp.
