# Padre Nuestro

*A freshwater cave system in Parque Nacional del Este near Bayahibe, requiring a 45–60 minute jungle trail approach and offering highly decorated passages and connection to the Chicho II cave system.*

## Overview

Padre Nuestro is a freshwater cave dive located inside Parque Nacional del Este, accessible via a 45–60 minute trail through dry karst tropical forest from the Bayahibe trailhead. The cave entrance opens into a highly decorated chamber with stalactites throughout. The main passage descends to approximately 10 metres. The downstream section features dark, tannic-stained rock and silty conditions. A connection to the Chicho II cave system exists, making this part of a larger cave network of significance to technical cave divers.

The site was first explored in the downstream section by Dave Pratt and Cristian Pittaro in February 2008, and surveyed in 2010 as part of the Manantial La Oculta project. Divers must register with the local Medio Ambiente office in Bayahibe and pay the park fee before entering.

The cave contains Taíno petroglyphs in the surrounding dry cave sections, which are of archaeological significance. This is purely a freshwater system — no marine life is present.

**Note on difficulty:** The prior entry listed this as Beginner. Given the cave environment (silt floors, tannic staining that reduces visibility, requirement for cave gear), Intermediate is more appropriate for guided cavern access; full cave sections require cave certification.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Parque Nacional del Este, near Bayahibe, southeastern Dominican Republic
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry (45–60 minute trail approach required)
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern (freshwater)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate (cavern zone); cave certification required for deeper sections
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 metres (main passage)
- **Typical Visibility**: Excellent in undisturbed conditions; silt reduces visibility rapidly if the bottom is disturbed
- **Current**: Mild flow possible, dependent on rainfall
- **Best Time**: Year-round; access limited by trail conditions in heavy rain

## Marine Life

Padre Nuestro is a freshwater cave system. No marine species are present. The cave waters are crystal clear when undisturbed. The surrounding dry cave areas support Taíno petroglyphs and historical significance; the underwater sections themselves have no fauna documented in sources consulted.

## Dive Profile

After the trail approach, enter the cave via the water pool. Descend to the main passage at around 10 metres. The highly decorated initial chamber with stalactites provides the main visual experience. Movement must be slow and deliberate to avoid silting the passage. Stay on the established line and do not exceed the cavern zone without cave certification. The downstream section leads toward the Chicho II connection for certified cave divers.

## Entry and Exit

Register at the Medio Ambiente office in Bayahibe and pay the park entry fee before starting the trail. The trail is approximately 45–60 minutes through dry forest terrain. Cave-certified dive operators in Bayahibe (including those offering cave diving packages) provide guided access. This is not a self-guided dive site.

## Tips

- Cave certification is required to go beyond the cavern zone — beginners can observe the initial chamber with a certified guide under appropriate supervision
- Silt on the cave floor is very fine; even gentle fin movements can reduce visibility to zero — practice excellent buoyancy and use appropriate cave dive techniques
- The trail is strenuous in humid conditions; carry water and wear appropriate footwear
- Combine with the nearby Chicho II cave on the same day for those with cave certification

## Safety

Cave diving requires specific training beyond recreational Open Water certification. The cavern zone near the entrance can be visited by suitably supervised divers, but the full cave system requires at minimum a Cave Diver certification, redundant gas, and a reel and line. Silt conditions inside can create immediate zero-visibility if disturbed. Never dive beyond the light zone without proper training and equipment.
