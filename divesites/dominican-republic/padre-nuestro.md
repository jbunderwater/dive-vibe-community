## Padre Nuestro

Padre Nuestro is a cave dive site in Dominican Republic, Caribbean.

## Overview

Padre Nuestro is a freshwater cenote and cave system in Parque Nacional del Este near Bayahibe, reached via a 45-minute trail through dry karst jungle that was once home to the Taino people whose petroglyphs still mark the surrounding landscape. The cave entrance opens into a highly decorated first chamber of stalactites and stalagmites, with crystal-clear 25°C water at a shallow depth of 10 metres, and a connecting passage leading to the large hall of nearby Chicho II — one of the most visually striking cave dives in the Caribbean.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dominican Republic, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters

## Marine Life

As a freshwater cenote, Padre Nuestro's marine life differs completely from coastal dive sites — the crystal-clear cave waters host freshwater fish, blind cave shrimp adapted to the dark passages, and endemic freshwater invertebrates rather than typical reef species. Stalactites and stalagmites are the primary visual draw, with the pristine 25°C water offering perfect visibility to appreciate the cave's extraordinary geological formations.
