# Sosua Bay

*The primary diving hub on the Dominican Republic's north coast, with a protected horseshoe bay offering over 15 named sites from shallow coral pinnacles to wall dives and the 35-metre Zingara wreck.*

## Overview

Sosua Bay is the most developed scuba diving destination on the Dominican Republic's north coast. The bay's horseshoe shape — with offshore winds and minimal wave action — creates consistently clear water and low current for the majority of its sites. Operators including Superior Dive Sosua, Aqua Adventures (Scuba Dive Sosua), Sosua Diving Center, and Northern Coast Diving base their boats here.

More than 15 named sites exist within and around the bay, ranging in depth from 3 metres (Three Rocks) to 40 metres (Ray Point). The most commonly dived sites for beginner and intermediate divers include Three Rocks / Coral Gardens (a shallow coral pinnacle and nursery restoration area), the Canyon (a sandy-bottom reef with a large rock pinnacle and a historically encrusted anchor), the West Wall / La Puntilla (reef with boulder passages and confirmed seahorse sightings), the Airport Wall (a substantial wall with tunnels and swim-throughs, considered one of the best walls in the DR), and the Zingara wreck (a 35-metre advanced dive — see the dedicated Zingara entry). For this entry's purposes, the data represents the bay's accessible shallow reef sites accessible to beginner divers, with a maximum depth of 20 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sosua Bay, Puerto Plata province, north coast Dominican Republic
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 metres (best in summer months)
- **Current**: Generally minimal in the sheltered bay; variable at the bay mouth
- **Best Time**: May to October for calmest Atlantic conditions; year-round diving possible

## Marine Life

The following marine life is confirmed by multiple Sosua Bay operator sources: sea turtles (multiple species), spotted eagle rays, seahorses (reported at West Wall / La Puntilla and confirmed as a draw for the area), stingrays (including yellow stingrays), moray eels, barracuda, French grunts, French angelfish, blue tang, sergeant majors, snappers, bluehead wrasse, lobsters, porcupine fish, frogfish, and reef squid. Nurse sharks are occasional visitors. The Canyon site contains a historically encrusted anchor reported by multiple operators. The Airport Wall has tunnels and swim-throughs.

## Dive Profile

For the shallow bay sites (beginner level): enter from the beach at Sosua, or by short boat ride. Three Rocks starts at 3 metres with sandy bottom sloping to 9 metres; Canyon reaches 9–18 metres. Descend to the reef or pinnacle base and explore at a slow pace. Shore access from Sosua Beach is possible for the nearest sites (notably Canyon via its rock channel entry) according to operator briefings.

## Entry and Exit

Multiple operators run boats from Sosua Beach. The Canyon and Three Rocks are accessible in a short boat ride from the beach or by shore for experienced navigators. Superior Dive Sosua, Aqua Adventures, and Sosua Diving Center all offer guided boat dives to the full site menu.

## Tips

- Seahorse sightings are a specific attraction at West Wall / La Puntilla — ask your operator about current chances
- The Coral Gardens site features an active coral restoration nursery project
- The Canyon's encrusted anchor is a historical curiosity that operators include on guided tours
- Humpback whales pass through Dominican Republic north coast waters between January and March — surface sightings (and occasionally hearing whales underwater) are possible during this season

## Safety

The bay is generally well-protected, but the Airport Wall and Ray Point (40+ metres) are Advanced sites with current. For beginners, stay with operators on guided boat dives and confirm the site level before booking. Lionfish (invasive species) are present in north coast waters.

## Photography

Seahorse encounters at West Wall are the standout macro subject. Canyon's encrusted anchor makes an unusual wide-angle subject in shallow, well-lit water. The Coral Gardens restoration nursery frames nicely with ambient light and the reef background.
