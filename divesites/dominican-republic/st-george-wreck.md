# St. George Wreck

*A deep wreck off the southern coast near Santo Domingo, at the limit of recreational diving limits and reserved for advanced and technical divers.*

## Overview

The St. George is a wreck located in the southern waters of the Dominican Republic off the coast near Santo Domingo. Resting at approximately 35–44 meters, the wreck sits at the bottom of recreational dive limits and requires advanced certification and solid dive planning. The vessel has been on the bottom long enough to develop substantial encrustation. Visibility in these southern waters ranges from 15–25 meters. Water temperatures average 26–28°C. The deep depth makes this a dive requiring nitrox or careful air management, and the site is generally reserved for advanced and technical divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern coast, Dominican Republic, near Santo Domingo
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 44 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters (50–80 feet)
- **Current**: Variable; can be moderate
- **Best Time**: May to November for generally calmer southern coast conditions

## Marine Life

At depth, the wreck supports large groupers and snapper schools typical of older Caribbean wrecks. Lion fish have become established on the structure. Moray eels and spiny lobsters inhabit the deeper crevices of the hull. Wire corals and black coral bushes grow on the superstructure at depth. Glassy sweepers and soldierfish crowd around any overhangs. Barracuda and jacks patrol the water column above. The deeper sections of the wreck are less frequently dived, meaning marine life here is less disturbed.

## Dive Profile

This is a deep wreck requiring careful dive planning. Descent should be direct to the wreck top. Spend the first half of your planned bottom time exploring the deeper sections, then work your way shallower along the wreck as air depletes. Given the depth, bottom time is limited — typically 15–20 minutes at maximum depth on air. An extended safety stop at 5 meters (minimum 3–5 minutes) is essential. Nitrox enriched air (32%) is strongly recommended to maximize bottom time and reduce decompression loading.

## Entry and Exit

Dive operators from Santo Domingo and La Caleta serve this site. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Deploy an SMB from depth before ascending for open-water safety stops. Coordinate pickup carefully with the boat crew given the deep descent.

## Tips

- Plan this dive conservatively and dive with an experienced buddy who knows the site
- Nitrox 32% or 36% extends bottom time and reduces post-dive fatigue at this depth
- A dive computer with deep stop function is advisable
- Visit La Caleta Underwater Park (the shallower Hickory wreck) the same day as a shallower second dive
- Do not attempt wreck penetration at this depth without advanced wreck training and redundant lighting

## Safety

The 44-meter maximum depth puts this site at the absolute limit of recreational scuba. Narcosis at depth can impair judgment — know your personal narcosis susceptibility before diving here. Ascend immediately if you feel impaired. Maintain a gas reserve of at least 100 bar before beginning ascent. A five-minute safety stop at 5 meters is mandatory. Always dive with a buddy.

## Photography

Deep wreck photography requires a capable strobe system to bring out colors, as natural light at 35+ meters is dim. Wide-angle shots of the hull silhouette with a diver for scale are dramatic. Macro subjects — flamingo tongues, nudibranchs, tunicates — are visible on the hull surfaces if you look carefully with a torch.
