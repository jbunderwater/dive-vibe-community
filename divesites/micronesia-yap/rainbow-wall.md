# Rainbow Wall

A deep, color-saturated wall dive where vibrant soft corals in every hue justify the name, with grey reef sharks and eagle rays in the blue

## Overview
Rainbow Wall is one of Yap's deepest recreational wall dives, reaching 35 meters along a sheer face blanketed in soft corals of extraordinary color variety. The wall earns its name from the rainbow of hues on display -- reds, oranges, yellows, purples, and pinks all represented in the dense soft coral growth. At depth, where these colors are invisible to the naked eye, a torch or strobe reveals the full spectrum in stunning fashion. Grey reef sharks are ever-present along the wall edge, eagle rays glide past in the blue water, and the dramatic topography includes overhangs, crevices, and buttress formations. Strong currents are common, making this a site for experienced divers.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Yap, Federated States of Micronesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-45 meters (80-150 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life
Grey reef sharks are the dominant predators, patrolling the wall edge in groups. Spotted eagle rays pass through regularly, sometimes in small squadrons. Dogtooth tuna and yellowfin tuna hunt along the wall. The soft coral gardens host an astonishing variety of color and form, with dendronephthya soft corals in brilliant reds and oranges dominating the deeper sections. Large gorgonian sea fans anchor the wall, and black coral trees reach outward into the current. Longnose hawkfish perch on gorgonians. Whitetip reef sharks rest in wall crevices. The reef top above the wall supports healthy hard coral with schools of surgeonfish, unicornfish, and fusiliers.

## Dive Profile
This is a deep wall dive requiring careful planning. Descend to the wall edge and work down to your maximum planned depth of 30-35 meters in the first minutes of the dive. The most spectacular soft coral coverage is between 25-35 meters. Begin a slow, steady ascent along the wall face, spending progressively more time at each depth level. The wall shallows to a reef top at 5-8 meters for your safety stop. Total bottom time at maximum depth should be limited to 10-15 minutes to stay within no-decompression limits. Nitrox is strongly recommended.

## Entry and Exit
Boat access from Colonia. Negative entry is often necessary due to current. The boat follows divers along the wall. SMB deployment well before surfacing to alert the boat captain.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Bring a powerful dive torch to reveal the true colors of the soft corals, which appear grey-blue at depth without artificial light
- Plan a conservative profile and use Nitrox to extend your no-decompression time at the deeper sections
- Spend the first third of the dive at maximum depth, then ascend gradually -- do not yo-yo between depths
- Look into the blue water off the wall for eagle rays and sharks, which are often just at the edge of visibility
- This dive pairs well with a shallow reef or manta dive afterward, as it will be your deepest dive of the day

## Safety Considerations
The 35-meter depth demands respect. Nitrogen narcosis can affect judgment at this depth, and no-decompression limits are short. Plan your dive conservatively and stick to your plan. Strong currents can push divers deeper or sweep them off the wall. Downcurrents along the wall face are possible. Carry a dive computer with depth and time alerts. A safety stop at 5 meters for a minimum of 3 minutes is mandatory. This dive should only be attempted by divers with deep and wall diving experience.

## Photography
Rainbow Wall is a color photographer's dream -- but only with artificial light. At depth, the human eye sees only blues and greys, so strobes are essential to bring out the vivid soft coral colors. Wide-angle with dual strobes positioned for even coverage is the standard setup. Shoot close to your subjects to minimize backscatter and maximize color saturation. Upward angles along the wall with a shark silhouette create dramatic compositions. For the deepest sections, boost strobe power and shoot at shorter distances. The excellent visibility allows creative negative-space compositions with the deep blue water.
