## Dibba Rock Reef

Dibba Rock Reef is a beginner-friendly reef dive near Dibba town on the Musandam Peninsula, within one of the region's oldest no-fishing marine protected areas.

## Overview

Dibba Rock sits approximately 10–15 minutes by boat from Dibba town, near the Oman/UAE border on the Musandam Peninsula's south coast. The reef descends from 7 to 16 metres, combining hard and soft corals with caves and drop-offs on the rocky slopes. The site has been a no-fishing zone for over 15 years, which has allowed marine life to recover to good density.

Confirmed marine life includes turtles (common on the ocean-facing side), clownfish, parrotfish, butterflyfish, moray eels, rays, cuttlefish, squid, and occasional blacktip reef sharks. Visibility typically reaches 15–30 metres in October–April. The site is accessible from dive operators on both the UAE (Fujairah) and Oman (Musandam) sides of the bay.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Dibba town, Musandam Peninsula / Oman–UAE border area
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 meters
- **Note**: The site sits on the geographical boundary between Oman's Musandam governorate and UAE's Fujairah emirate; the OSM coordinates are within the Oman-administered area.

## Marine Life

Confirmed: turtles (hawksbill, green), clownfish, parrotfish, butterflyfish, moray eels, rays, cuttlefish, squid, blacktip reef sharks. Whale sharks are occasionally reported in the wider Dibba area but not confirmed as site-specific to Dibba Rock. Visibility 15–30m in cooler months.
