## Eagle Bay

Eagle Bay is a sheltered coral bay near Mirbat in southern Oman, named for the eagles that nest in the rock face alongside the bay.

## Overview

Eagle Bay sits about 70 kilometres from Salalah along the Mirbat coastline of Oman's Dhofar region, accessible by 4x4. The bay features a large sandy patch in the centre, a long sloping coral bank on the south side, and numerous rocky outcrops on the north side at depths of 5 to 15 metres. Entry is directly from shore — no boat is required.

The site is one of the most reliable for turtle and eagle ray encounters along the Mirbat coast. Clownfish, large batfish, boxfish, and tropical reef species typical of the Indo-Pacific character of Mirbat diving are common throughout the coral gardens. The sheltered geometry of the bay makes it suitable for all certification levels and a popular first dive of the day for operators running trips during the October to May season.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mirbat coast, Dhofar, southern Oman
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Season**: October to May (khareef monsoon closes the coast June–September)

## Marine Life

Confirmed sightings at Eagle Bay include turtles, eagle rays, large batfish, boxfish, clownfish among anemones, moray eels, nudibranchs, stonefish, and tropical reef fish. The Indo-Pacific character of the marine community here is unusual for the Arabian Peninsula.

## Dive Profile

Enter directly from the beach and follow the sloping coral bank on the south side of the bay. The sandy central patch is good for spotting rays and turtles cruising along the bottom. Rocky outcrops on the north side harbour moray eels and resting fish. Maximum depth 15 metres.
