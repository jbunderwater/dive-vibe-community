## Snoopy Island Reef

> **Location Note:** Snoopy Island is located in the Emirate of Fujairah, United Arab Emirates — not Oman. This entry exists in the Oman dataset due to a coordinate/assignment error in the source OSM data. The site is confirmed as UAE territory by PADI, Wikipedia, and multiple UAE tourism sources.

Snoopy Island Reef is a reef dive and snorkel site off Al Aqah beach in Fujairah, UAE.

## Overview

Snoopy Island is a small rocky island approximately 200 metres offshore from Al Aqah beach in Fujairah, UAE, named for its resemblance to the cartoon beagle lying on his back when viewed from the shore. The island sits within a protected marine area where fishing is prohibited. PADI lists it as a United Arab Emirates dive site in the UAE dive guide, and Wikipedia confirms its location in the Emirate of Fujairah. The reef surrounding the island is shallow, with a maximum depth of approximately 7 metres, making it suitable for snorkellers and beginner divers with shore access directly from Al Aqah beach. Confirmed marine life at the site includes trumpetfish, sergeant major fish, redtoothed triggerfish, parrotfish, turtles, moray eels, and cuttlefish (Sandy Beach Dive Academy). The nearby Snoopy Deep Reef, approximately 500 metres east and at 24 metres depth, is a separate artificial reef site requiring Advanced Open Water certification.
