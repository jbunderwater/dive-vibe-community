## Snoopy Island Reef

Snoopy Island Reef surrounds a small rocky island off Al Aqah beach in Fujairah, United Arab Emirates — note that this site is located in the UAE, not in Oman, and appears in this dataset due to an OSM coordinate boundary issue.

## Overview

Snoopy Island (also called Al Aqah Island) is a 0.7-hectare rock formation off the coastal town of Al Aqah in Fujairah emirate, named for its resemblance to the cartoon beagle lying on his back. The island is 200 metres from Al Aqah's Sandy Beach and is a predominantly shore dive, with a maximum depth of approximately 7 to 8 metres around the island itself. A short five-minute boat ride is an alternative access option. The site sits within a protected marine area where fishing is prohibited, supporting healthy reef fish populations. Common species include trumpetfish, sergeant major, redtoothed triggerfish, parrotfish, angelfish, clownfish, butterflyfish, and green and hawksbill turtles. Moray eels and cuttlefish occupy the rocky crevices. The calm, clear, warm waters make it an excellent beginner dive and snorkelling site accessible year-round.
