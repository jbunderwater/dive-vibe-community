## China Wreck (direction 150deg)

China Wreck is a historic wreck dive site near Mirbat in Oman's Dhofar region.

## Overview

The China Wreck is a popular dive and snorkel site near Mirbat in southern Oman, accessible directly from the beach using a compass bearing of approximately 150 degrees from shore. The PADI Pros blog lists it as a beach diving location suitable for entry-level PADI courses. Beautiful Salalah describes it as a wreck that has become an artificial reef "with a wealth of species," including nudibranchs, flatworms, shrimp, crustaceans, clownfish, octopuses, moray eels, stonefish, rays, turtles, and barracudas. The wreck is noted as a very shallow structure, making it accessible to snorkelers as well as divers.

**Vessel identity and history:** Sources conflict on the origin of this wreck. One source (lelas-world.com) describes it as a Portuguese steamship that wrecked approximately 120 years ago. Another (pelakita.id) notes it carried porcelain and artifacts associated with ancient trade routes, which may be the basis for the "China" name, but states vessel identity is unknown. The name "China Wreck" likely refers to ceramic cargo recovered from the site rather than a Chinese vessel. No independent sources confirm vessel name, build date, tonnage, or length — these details are omitted per anti-hallucination policy.

The direction "150 degrees" in the site name is the compass bearing used to navigate to the wreck from the shore entry point.
