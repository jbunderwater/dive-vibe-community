## China Wreck (direction 150deg)

The China Wreck is a shallow, unidentified steam-era wreck near Mirbat in southern Oman, one of the most accessible wreck dives in the Arabian Peninsula.

## Overview

The China Wreck lies approximately 100 metres from shore near Mirbat in Oman's Dhofar region, reached by swimming a compass bearing of 150 degrees from the entry point. The wreck is a steam-powered vessel of unknown origin and age — no vessel name or confirmed history has been documented — resting at depths of around 5 to 12 metres. The structure has broken down considerably, but the remains attract dense marine life and function as an active artificial reef.

ABT Divers operates trips to this site from Salalah, and the shallow depth and shore access make it accessible for beginners. The site is available October to May before the annual khareef monsoon closes the Dhofar coast.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mirbat coast, Dhofar, southern Oman
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Season**: October to May (khareef monsoon closes the coast June–September)

## Marine Life

Multiple sources confirm fish life at the wreck but specific species per source are limited. The wreck functions as an artificial reef attracting schooling fish, moray eels, and reef species typical of the Mirbat area. Turtles and rays are reported in the surrounding waters.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry; swim bearing 150 degrees for approximately 100 metres to locate the wreck. Depths of 5–12 metres allow extended bottom time. The scattered remains include structural components that shelter macro life.
