## China Wreck (direction 150deg)

China Wreck is a shallow shore-accessible wreck dive near Mirbat in Oman's Dhofar region, named for reasons that remain unclear — the vessel's identity and origin are unknown to local divers and operators.

## Overview

The China Wreck lies approximately 100 metres from the beach at Mirbat, scattered across a roughly 80-metre stretch along a north-south line in water between 3.5 and 8 metres deep. The wreck is entered from shore using a compass bearing of 150 degrees, which is reflected in its OSM name. The vessel was a steam-powered ship and the remains include anchor, boilers, propeller shaft, and hull fragments heavily colonised by marine growth. Its shallow depth allows long bottom times and makes it accessible to all certification levels. No historical records have been found to establish the vessel's name, build date, dimensions, or sinking cause — it is consistently described by local operators as an unidentified wreck of unknown origin. Sources disagree on whether it carried porcelain cargo; this claim appears in regional maritime heritage literature but is not confirmed by dive-specific accounts of the site. Rays, cuttlefish, snappers, moray eels, and turtles are reported in the area around the wreck. The site is available from October to May.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mirbat, Dhofar, Oman
- **Entry Type**: Shore dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters
