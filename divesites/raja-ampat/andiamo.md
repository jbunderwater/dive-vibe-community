## Andiamo

A large seamount and two small islets northwest of Daram Island in the Misool Saghof-Daram archipelago. Two rocky islets protrude above the surface with a shallow channel between them; a separate seamount lies to the north, connected to the rocks by a sandy plateau at around 30 metres. The seamount top reaches approximately 5 metres below the surface.

Soft corals, sponges, and boulder formations blanket the seamount at every depth. Schools of blackfin barracuda and golden-line snapper regularly aggregate around the structure, joined by giant trevally, Napoleon wrasse, and large groupers. Whitetip reef sharks rest on the sandy plateau between the seamount and the islets; blacktip reef sharks appear occasionally in open water. Macro subjects include Denise pygmy seahorse, orangutan crabs, bubble coral shrimp, and Ocellated Tozeuma shrimp.

A manta ray cleaning station sits on the far side of the ridge and receives seasonal visits during manta migrations; sightings are unreliable and not guaranteed. Visibility typically ranges 15–30 metres. Currents run medium to strong and are best timed on a rising tide; Advanced diver rating is appropriate.

Named after the liveaboard vessel used by early Raja Ampat explorer Burt Jones during initial surveys of the Misool area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Daram), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
