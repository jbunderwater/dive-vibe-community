## Myoskon

Myoskon is an alternate transliteration of **Mioskon** (also spelled Mios Kon, Mioskin, Mioskun) — the reef system surrounding Mioskon Island in the Dampier Strait, Raja Ampat. The PADI-listed coordinates for this site (-0.49742, 130.72699) are a near-exact match to this entry's coordinates, confirming both refer to the same location.

**Duplicate note**: The entry "Mioskun" in this database (lat -0.4970983, lon 130.719783) is approximately 700 metres west of this entry and represents a different GPS pin dropped on the same reef system, which wraps around the island in a V-shape. These are not two separate dive sites — they are two recorded access points to the same Mioskon reef. See also: mioskun.md.

## Overview

The Mioskon reef surrounds Mioskon Island in a V-shape, with the most productive section running as a long ridge to the southeast. The reef slopes gently from the surface to a sandy bottom at 25 metres — this is a sloping coral reef system, not a pinnacle. The site suits all experience levels and is often described as one of the most accessible productive reefs in northern Raja Ampat. The eastern side allows for a lazy drift dive.

The reef is characterised by numerous bommies (coral heads) densely covered in soft corals, including staghorn, leather corals, orange sea fans, and sponges. The best coral and marine life density is reported between 7–12 metres depth. Water temperature is 27–29°C and visibility is typically 15–25 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mioskon Island, Dampier Strait, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 metres
- **Current**: Mild; eastern side suitable for drift diving
- **Best Time**: October to April

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at Mioskon reef:

- Wobbegong sharks (rare, endemic tasselled wobbegong; found under ledges and on shallow bommies — the highlight species)
- Napoleon (humphead) wrasse
- Groupers
- Yellowtail snappers and blue-lined snappers (schools)
- Jacks, fusiliers, sweetlips, butterflyfish, batfish, parrotfish
- Moray eels
- Pontohi pygmy seahorses and pygmy seahorses (on gorgonian fans)
- Peacock mantis shrimp and harlequin shrimp
- Blue-ring octopus (rare; confirmed sightings)
- Sponge crabs
- Giant clams (at approximately 9–12 m alongside tiny seahorses)
- Nudibranchs

Regional fauna applies for additional species; all species listed above are confirmed at this site from multiple sources.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef bommies and explore the V-shaped reef system around the island. The best coral density and marine life is between 7–12 metres. The eastern side allows a relaxed drift along the ridge. Wobbegong sharks rest under ledges and on bommies in the shallow section — move slowly and look carefully. The sandy patches between bommies are productive for mantis shrimp and macro subjects.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. All skill levels welcome in calm conditions. The sheltered nature of the reef makes this a good site in variable weather.
