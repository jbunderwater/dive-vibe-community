## Four Kings

Four submerged pinnacles east of Pele Island in the Pele area of Misool — one of the most celebrated dive sites in the entire Indo-Pacific. The pinnacles rise from a base at 40 metres, with summit depths varying across the formation: the shallowest (northeast) pinnacle reaches approximately 5–7 metres, a second at around 16 metres, a third at about 20 metres, and the deepest requiring descent to around 22 metres to reach the top. A dramatic deep canyon separates the last two pinnacles. The site is extensive enough that most divers need two separate dives to fully explore it.

Every surface of all four pinnacles is blanketed in soft corals, gorgonian sea fans, and tunicates in dense, colourful growth. Schools of hundreds of barracuda, trevally, and bigeye jacks orbit the structures. Bumphead parrotfish, Napoleon wrasse, and sea turtles are regularly encountered. Macro subjects include pygmy seahorses, nudibranchs, flatworms, longnose hawkfish, and pipefish. Pelagic manta rays visit the shallow plateau tops as a cleaning station, best October through March, though sightings are seasonal and not guaranteed.

Currents are moderate to strong and can reverse mid-dive; drift-diving capability is essential. Advanced rating is appropriate for all four pinnacles.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Pele), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
