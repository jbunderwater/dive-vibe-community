## Boo Point

The eastern face and point of Boo Island in the central Misool area, sometimes referred to as Boo East. The topography transitions from a gentle slope to a steep wall festooned with soft corals, gorgonian sea fans, and sea squirts. Overhangs and ledges break up the wall face and provide shelter for resting marine life.

Wobbegong sharks and reef sharks are present within the broader Boo Island complex; green sea turtles and Napoleon wrasse are regularly sighted along the wall. The eastern tip connects to a rubble-and-sand channel at around 25 metres that wraps toward the southern sloping reef. Reef manta rays visit the Boo area seasonally.

Currents at the eastern face can be moderate to strong, making this an Intermediate dive with Advanced conditions possible when tidal flow is running. Maximum depth 30 metres; the wall base extends to 35–40 metres in some sections. Frequently dived as a complement to the adjacent Boo Windows site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Boo Island, eastern face), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
