## Boo Point

Boo Point is a wall dive on the face of Boo Island in the Misool area, confirmed by sources as a sloping and steep slope contour dive to 30 meters — not a reef flat. It is adjacent to Boo Windows and typically dived together as part of the same Boo Island site visit.

## Overview

The site features a slope transitioning to steep wall diving, draped in soft corals and gorgonian fans. Overhangs along the wall provide shelter for wobbegong sharks and epaulette sharks.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Current**: Variable; assess before entry

## Marine Life

Wobbegong sharks and epaulette sharks rest on ledges. The wall is draped in soft corals. Regional Raja Ampat Misool marine life applies; no additional site-specific species reports were available beyond what sources confirm above.
