## Whale Rock

Two small islands in the Fiabacet chain of central Misool, linked underwater by a reef and a pinnacle. From the right angle at the surface, the combined formation resembles a whale's profile, giving the site its name. Divers cross a shallow channel between the two islands to reach the submerged reef and pinnacle connecting them.

The walls and the pinnacle are densely covered with gorgonian sea fans and soft corals. Pygmy seahorses are abundant — Denise and Bargibanti species are specifically confirmed at this site, distributed across the fan corals throughout the dive. Schools of reef fish and baitfish fill the water column, with giant trevally and reef sharks hunting in the open water. Wobbegong sharks have been reported in the vicinity of the Fiabacet sites.

The site is sometimes cited in connection with hammerhead sharks in the deeper adjacent waters, but this is a single-source report and hammerheads are not consistently documented here. The site's reliable highlights are the gorgonian coverage and pygmy seahorse density.

A diver's first-hand account records a maximum depth of 23 metres with 15 metres visibility, broadly consistent with the 30 metre max depth listing. Advanced diver rating applies due to currents in the Fiabacet area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Fiabacet), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
