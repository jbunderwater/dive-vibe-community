## Kabui Pass

Kabui Pass is a drift dive through the passage between Gam and Waigeo islands in Raja Ampat's Kabui Bay area. Tidal currents carry divers through a channel reaching 15 metres, with mangrove-lined walls and soft coral growth.

**Note on site context:** Kabui Pass (this curated entry, at -0.431117, 130.556967) is positioned between the two OSM nodes for this waterway: Selat Kabui (-0.4321515, 130.5515835) and The Passage (-0.4286672, 130.5610216). All three entries appear to represent sections of the same continuous passage — the narrow strait between Gam and Waigeo known as Selat Kabui or "The Passage" to dive operators. See also: [Selat Kabui](selat-kabui.md) and [The Passage](the-passage.md).

## Overview

The Kabui Pass is part of the narrow channel connecting Kabui Bay to the Halmahera Sea, characterised by limestone karst walls, mangrove-fringed margins, and a tidal drift that carries divers through one of Raja Ampat's most atmospheric diving environments. The passage is approximately 25 metres wide, with the primary dive depth in the 5–15 metre range along the passage walls and root systems.

Multiple sources confirm the passage carries soft corals, sea fans, sponges, and a mangrove-edge habitat that supports unusual shallow-water species not found on open reefs. Visibility is typically lower than open-water Raja Ampat sites due to tidal nutrient mixing and mangrove tannins.
