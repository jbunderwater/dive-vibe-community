## Melissa's Garden

Melissa's Garden is among the most celebrated hard coral gardens in Raja Ampat — an oval-shaped shallow reef flanked by two limestone pinnacles in the Penemu chain, between three small islands near Pulau Keruwo. The site is named after Melissa Ammer, daughter of Max Ammer, the Dutch founder of Papua Diving Resorts who pioneered diving in Raja Ampat.

## Overview

Situated in the Penemu area west of the Dampier Strait, approximately 1.5 hours by boat from Papua Diving Resorts on Kri Island, Melissa's Garden features a flat-topped reef plateau beginning at 8–12 meters, with sloping edges descending to a white sandy bottom at 25 meters. The shallow reef top (from 5 meters) is snorkeler-friendly. The coral structure is described by multiple sources as among the oldest and most diverse in Raja Ampat, with hard corals of multiple morphologies — staghorn, plate, cabbage, and massive Porites — covering nearly the entire seafloor of the plateau. Visibility reaches up to 25 meters; water temperature holds at around 29°C year-round. Currents are typically mild to moderate, though conditions are variable and can strengthen on tidal exchange.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Penemu chain, near Pulau Keruwo, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef

- **Typical Visibility**: Up to 25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; variable

## Marine Life

The following species have been documented at this site by multiple independent sources:

**Sharks:** Wobbegong shark, blacktip reef shark, grey reef shark, zebra shark. Raja Ampat epaulette shark (walking shark) has been reported by two local operator sources; widespread in the broader Fam/Penemu area.

**Macro fauna:** Pygmy seahorses (on gorgonian sea fans), ghost pipefish, mushroom coral pipefish, nudibranchs, crocodilefish, ribbon eels, anemonefish.

**Reef fish:** Anthias, fusiliers, damsels, pinjalo snapper, barracuda, angelfish, lionfish, surgeonfish, parrotfish, rabbitfish.

**Other:** Giant tridacna clams. Manta rays pass through occasionally, particularly during plankton-rich tidal periods.

Species removed from prior version as unconfirmed at this specific site: humphead/bumphead parrotfish in groups (single operator source only), hawksbill turtles specifically at this site (single source), blue-spotted lagoon rays (no site-specific source found).

## Dive Profile

The recommended pattern is to begin on the outer slope and work up to the plateau between the two pinnacles. The main garden lies at 8–12 meters and extends horizontally; the outer slopes descend to 25 meters where the reef meets sand. This is a relaxed dive — the site's beauty lies in slowly drifting across the plateau and inspecting the coral structure at close range. Safety stop in the 5-meter shallows is straightforward.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard tender or day-trip boat. The Penemu area is approximately 1.5 hours by boat from resorts on Kri Island and is a standard stop on central Raja Ampat liveaboard routes. Entry via giant stride or back roll. The reef top is shallow, so entry positioning is important to avoid landing on coral.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Do not touch or stand on the coral — the hard acropora structures are irreplaceable
- Perfect buoyancy is essential; a fin strike into staghorn can destroy years of growth
- Morning dives offer the best light angles in the shallows
- Scan gorgonian sea fans carefully for pygmy seahorses
- The site is suitable for snorkeling; snorkelers and divers can work the same area safely
