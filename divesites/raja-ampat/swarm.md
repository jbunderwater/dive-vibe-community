## Swarm

Swarm is a reef dive site in the southern reaches of Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia. Its coordinates (lat -0.708643, lng 130.3055331) place it approximately 13 km south of the Keruo/Penemu cluster, making it the southernmost site in this group. The name reflects the defining experience: enormous schools of fusiliers and other reef fish swarming around the reef structure.

## Overview

A December 2025 first-person liveaboard trip report (divehappy.com) describes a Fam Islands area reef site with "fusiliers in their tens of thousands circling the bommies, barrel sponges and plate corals in a constant, relentless stream" and notes crayfish tucked into the reef base. This description matches the site name and character. The site appears on La Galigo Liveaboard's dive site list as a Fam and Penemu area site.

The site's southern position in the Raja Ampat archipelago and its reef structure with barrel sponges and plate corals suggest a bommie or reef feature with current-driven fish aggregation. The "swarm" character -- massive schooling fish behavior around structural reef features -- is consistent with current-swept bommies of this region.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia (south of the main Penemu/Fam cluster)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate (fish action current-dependent)
- **Best Time**: October to April
- **Water Temperature**: 27-29 degrees C

## Marine Life

Confirmed from a first-person liveaboard trip report (Fam Islands area, December 2025):

- Fusiliers in large schools (described as "tens of thousands") circling reef bommies
- Crayfish (lobsters) in reef structure at base

Species listed in the prior version -- whitetip and blacktip reef sharks, large grouper, surgeonfish, and anthias -- are ecologically plausible for this type of reef but have not been independently confirmed at this specific named site. They have been removed pending site-specific source confirmation.

Regional fauna applies for other species; only the above are confirmed at this site.

## Dive Profile

The dive centers on the bommies and reef structure in the 8-20 meter range, remaining within the schooling fish activity. The deeper sections near 25 meters can be explored for reef structure. Entry and exit by boat.
