## Mioskun

Mioskun is an alternate transliteration of **Mioskon** (also spelled Mios Kon, Mioskin, Myoskon) — the reef system surrounding Mioskon Island in the Dampier Strait, Raja Ampat.

**Duplicate note**: This entry (lat -0.4970983, lon 130.719783) is approximately 700 metres west of the "Myoskon" entry (lat -0.497365, lon 130.726819) in this database. Both entries refer to the same Mioskon reef system, which wraps around the island in a V-shape. The ~700 m separation reflects different GPS pins dropped on different sections of the same reef, not two separate dive sites. No dive database, liveaboard itinerary, or dive operator treats Myoskon and Mioskun as distinct sites. The canonical name is **Mioskon**. See also: myoskon.md.

## Overview

The Mioskon reef surrounds Mioskon Island in a V-shape. The reef slopes gently from the surface to a sandy bottom at 25 metres — this is a sloping coral reef system, not a pinnacle. The site suits all experience levels. The eastern side allows for a lazy drift dive. Numerous bommies densely covered in soft corals — staghorn, leather corals, orange sea fans, and sponges — characterise the structure. The best coral and marine life density is between 7–12 metres depth.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mioskon Island, Dampier Strait, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 metres
- **Current**: Mild; eastern side suitable for drift diving
- **Best Time**: October to April

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at Mioskon reef:

- Wobbegong sharks (rare, endemic tasselled wobbegong; found under ledges and on shallow bommies — the highlight species)
- Napoleon (humphead) wrasse
- Groupers
- Yellowtail snappers and blue-lined snappers
- Jacks, fusiliers, sweetlips, butterflyfish, batfish, parrotfish
- Moray eels
- Pontohi pygmy seahorses and pygmy seahorses (on gorgonian fans)
- Peacock mantis shrimp and harlequin shrimp
- Blue-ring octopus (rare; confirmed sightings)
- Sponge crabs
- Giant clams
- Nudibranchs

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef bommies and explore the V-shaped reef system. The best coral density and marine life is between 7–12 metres. Wobbegong sharks rest under ledges — move slowly. The eastern side allows a relaxed drift along the ridge.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. All skill levels welcome in calm conditions.
