## Cave Lagoon

Cave Lagoon is a cave-entrance dive site in Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia, recorded in OpenStreetMap with the tag `natural=cave_entrance`. The site is located in the eastern Dampier Strait / Gam island area, south of the Kabui Bay passage.

## Overview

Raja Ampat's karst limestone coastline is extensively honeycombed with caves, caverns, tunnels, and enclosed lagoons. The Gam Island area is specifically noted in travel sources for a hidden lagoon accessible by passing through a cave at low tide. The OSM `natural=cave_entrance` tag confirms a real physical cave entrance exists at these coordinates.

Cave lagoon formations — where a cave passage opens into a sheltered interior lagoon — are a well-documented feature of the Raja Ampat archipelago, found across Gam, Misool, and the Fam islands. The sheltered lagoon character, if present at this site, would create conditions distinct from open ocean reefs: calmer water, nursery habitat for juvenile fish, and cave walls supporting low-light encrusting organisms.
