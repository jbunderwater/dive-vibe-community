## Friwen Laut

Friwen Laut is a sloping reef site adjacent to Friwen Island in northern Raja Ampat — distinct from Friwen Bonda (the wall) and Friwen Garden. Sources confirm a shallow start at 3 meters sloping to 25 meters, with none to mild current making this a Beginner-accessible site.

## Overview

Friwen Laut ("sea Friwen") is the sloping reef site of the Friwen Island group, distinguished from the wall (Friwen Bonda) and the garden (Friwen Garden). The reef starts shallow at 3 meters and descends to 25 meters. Current is none to mild, confirmed by rajaampatbiodiversity.com, lagaligoliveaboard.com, and meridianadventuredive.com. Water temperature averages 27–29°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Shallow Start**: 3 meters
- **Current**: None to mild
- **Best Time**: October to April

## Marine Life

Sources confirmed at this specific site (rajaampatbiodiversity.com, lagaligoliveaboard.com, meridianadventuredive.com): endemic Raja Ampat garden eels, ribbon eels, blue-spotted stingrays, and dense nudibranch populations. The shallow accessible start makes this suitable for snorkeling as well.
