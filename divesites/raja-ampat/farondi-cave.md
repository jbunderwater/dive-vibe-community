## Farondi Cave

Farondi Island lies approximately 20 km east of Misool Island and is host to one of the most dramatic topographical dives in southern Raja Ampat. The main dive (known as Goa Besar) begins in a shallow area at around 5 metres where the seafloor opens into a large descending tunnel — roughly 20 metres long and 10 metres wide — with a sandy bottom lined with sea fans and barrel sponges. Stalactites and unusual limestone formations cover the arching ceiling. The tunnel descends steeply, exiting on the island's outer wall at 26–30 metres, where divers continue a drift along a steep reef covered in hard corals and gorgonians.

A second feature at the southwestern corner — Verena's Garden — is a cavern accessible at 5–12 metres depth, approximately 30 metres wide and penetrating 20 metres into the island, with an air pocket inside and a sandy bottom. Soft coral arches mark the approach to Goa Besar.

Confirmed marine life at Farondi includes Bargibanti and Denise pygmy seahorses on gorgonian fans, ghost pipefish, bumphead parrotfish, fusilier schools, Napoleon wrasse, giant trevally, and bamboo sharks resting in shadowed corners. Mobula rays hunt in the blue outside the cave entrance.

Advanced diver rating is appropriate given the cave topography and potential for strong drift on the exit wall.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Farondi Island), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
