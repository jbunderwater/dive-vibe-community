## Nudi Rock

A small rocky island off the shores of Pulau Batbitieru in the Fiabacet chain of central Misool. The island's name comes from its above-water shape, which resembles a nudibranch complete with two small trees mimicking the creature's horn-like rhinophores.

The north face of the island is a steep vertical wall blanketed in soft corals, dropping to a maximum depth of 28 metres. On the south side, a shallow plateau at 7–8 metres attracts large concentrations of fish when current is running; the east side has a sandy bottom at around 25 metres. This mixed topography — wall, sloping reef, and small surrounding pinnacles — makes the site accessible from multiple angles.

Flamboyant nudibranchs are abundant among the soft corals and give the site much of its character. Gorgonian sea fans host pygmy seahorses and cowries. Schooling barracuda, hunting giant trevally, and grey reef sharks are confirmed pelagic visitors; occasional oceanic manta rays use the site as a cleaning station. Napoleon wrasse, groupers, snappers, and porcelain crabs round out the species list.

Currents run medium to strong; a reef hook is useful during peak flow. Advanced diver rating is appropriate despite shallow sections.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Fiabacet), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
