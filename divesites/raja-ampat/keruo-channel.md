## Keruo Channel

Keruo Channel is a channel drift dive on the east side of Keruo Island in the Penemu area of Raja Ampat. Also referred to as Fam Wall and Dinding Warna Banyak, the channel runs between Keruo and Keruwo islands for approximately 300 meters. Its walls are covered in gorgonian sea fans, soft corals, and sponges, and the tidal current that funnels through makes it one of central Raja Ampat's most reliable drift dives.

## Overview

The channel runs north-south between two islands; dive direction is determined by the tide — on a falling tide divers enter from the north end, on a rising tide from the south. The western wall face receives more sunlight during the day and is generally favored. The wall is interrupted by steep slope sections but is largely a continuous reef structure.

A shallow zone at either end of the channel (approximately 5 meters) is suitable for photography and safety stops. Gorgonian sea fans are the defining structural feature and are notably large in this channel. Three pygmy seahorse species have been confirmed at this site. Visibility is typically around 15 meters; current is medium to strong. Recommended for intermediate to experienced divers. Water temperature averages 27–30°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East side of Keruo Island, channel between Keruo and Keruwo islands, Penemu area, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: ~15 meters
- **Current**: Medium to strong; direction reverses with tide
- **Best Time**: October to April
- **Water Temperature**: 27–30°C

## Marine Life

The following species are documented at this site from operator sources (La Galigo Liveaboard, based on their dedicated site profile):

Pygmy seahorse (Bargibanti, Denise, and Pantohe species confirmed), barracuda, blacktip reef sharks, midnight snapper, angelfish, spadefish, hawkfish, giant trevally, soldierfish, manta rays, octopus, Phyllidia carlsonhoffi nudibranch, green turtles, moray eels (giant and white-eyed), yellowtail coris, Jansen's wrasse, sweetlips, Spanish mackerel, round batfish, mantis shrimp, scorpionfish, sea snakes, black corals (deeper sections).

Species removed from prior version as unconfirmed at this specific site: grey reef sharks (no site-specific source — blacktip sharks are confirmed), rainbow runners (not listed in site-specific sources), grouper and snapper "in current eddies" (not confirmed at this site). Manta rays are confirmed as occasional, not a regular feature.

## Dive Profile

Enter upstream and drift through the channel at 10–25 meters. The boat follows the bubble trail and picks up divers at the channel exit. Keep the wall on one side to maintain depth control and position in eddies when needed. The shallow ends (~5 meters) at each terminus offer calmer water for safety stops.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive with drift pickup. Entry by giant stride or back roll upstream. Exit at the downstream channel terminus; surface with SMB for boat visibility.
