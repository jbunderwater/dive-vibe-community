## Anita's Garden

Anita's Garden is a hard coral garden dive site in the Penemu/Fam area of Raja Ampat, named by Max Ammer — founder of Papua Diving Resorts and the pioneer of diving in Raja Ampat — after his daughter Anita. It is a companion site to Melissa's Garden, also named after one of Ammer's daughters.

## Overview

Anita's Garden is centred on a hard coral plateau at around 8–10 meters depth, covered in diverse hard coral formations including tables, staghorn, and massive Porites. An overhang just below the surface provides shelter and creates dramatic light angles. Soft corals and gorgonians grow up to the reef top. On the eastern side of the site, the bottom reaches a sandy area at around 25 meters where schools of barracuda are commonly observed. Conditions are generally suitable for intermediate divers. Water temperature averages 27–29°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Penemu/Fam area, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to April

## Marine Life

The following species have been documented at or in direct association with this site by operator sources:

Groupers, snappers, barracuda (schools on sandy eastern side at depth), trevallies, napoleon wrasse, manta rays, eagle rays, whitetip and grey reef sharks, angelfish, ghost pipefish, mushroom coral pipefish, wobbegong sharks.

Note: The prior description listed seahorses, nudibranchs, clownfish, and flatworms as highlights — while these are plausible in a Fam/Penemu coral garden habitat, no source specifically confirms them at Anita's Garden as distinct from the broader regional fauna. The description has been revised to reflect confirmed site-specific data only.

## Dive Profile

Begin at the shallow overhang or reef top (5–8 meters) and explore the coral plateau at 8–10 meters, which is the site's defining zone. Move gradually to the eastern side to reach the sandy bottom area at around 25 meters where barracuda schools are commonly found. The hard coral diversity is best appreciated at slow pace in the 8–15 meter range.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat. Entry via giant stride or back roll near the reef top. Mild current conditions are typical. Deploy SMB for boat pickup. The calm conditions make this a good afternoon or third-dive option.
