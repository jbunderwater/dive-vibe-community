## Candy Store

A wall and pinnacle site in the Daram Islands of eastern Misool. The site descends close to sharp black rocks breaking the surface, dropping into an underwater landscape where each section of reef is lined with sea fans, soft corals, and hard corals closer to the top. Two pinnacles with a sheer-walled valley between them form the site's central structure. A swimthrough at approximately 2 metres depth provides an additional feature at the shallow end.

The site name comes from the explosion of colour that greeted early explorers — vivid reds and greens in the soft coral, ribbons of glassfish constantly shifting formation, and massive elephant ear sponges and gorgonian fans on the western end of the reef. Clusters of fusiliers, anchovies, giant trevally, and Spanish mackerel concentrate on the north-facing pinnacles. Snappers, breams, and sweetlips hang in the current in the valley. Pygmy seahorses and nudibranchs inhabit the gorgonian fans. Batfish congregate near the swimthrough.

A first-hand trip report from December 2025 (DiveHappy) recorded the site at 21 metres depth with 10 metres visibility and described it as suitable for a check dive. Maximum depth 30 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Daram Islands), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
