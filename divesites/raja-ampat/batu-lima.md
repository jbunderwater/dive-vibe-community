## Batu Lima

Batu Lima — "Five Rocks" in Indonesian — is a reef and wall dive on the eastern side of Gam Island, situated along the channel that leads into Kabui Bay. The five rocky outcrops create a complex, multi-habitat dive known for both macro life and reef fish aggregations.

## Overview

Multiple sources confirm Batu Lima is located on the eastern side of Gam Island in the northeastern part of the Dampier Strait, along the pass into Kabui Bay. The five rock formations give the site its name and structure — their presence in a tidal channel creates eddies and current effects that concentrate marine life. The site is noted specifically for night diving by La Galigo Liveaboard and diveconcepts.com, with unusual nocturnal species behaviour including epaulette shark movement.

Visibility averages approximately 20 metres. The site can be snorkelled in addition to scuba dived. Water temperature averages 27–29°C. Maximum depth is approximately 24–27 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Eastern side of Gam Island, Dampier Strait, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: ~20 metres
- **Current**: Mild to medium typical; can be strong
- **Best Time**: Year-round accessible; October to April preferred

## Marine Life

The following species are confirmed at Batu Lima by multiple independent sources:

- **Epaulette (walking) sharks**: Confirmed by La Galigo Liveaboard and diveconcepts.com; particularly active on night dives
- **Blacktip reef sharks**: Confirmed by multiple sources
- **Whitetip reef sharks**: Confirmed
- **Fusiliers**: Large schools confirmed
- **Triggerfish**: Confirmed
- **Unicorn fish**: Confirmed
- **Giant trevally**: Confirmed hunting at the site
- **Barracuda (pickhandle)**: Confirmed by diveconcepts.com
- **Hawksbill turtles**: Confirmed
- **Blue-spotted stingrays**: Confirmed by diveconcepts.com
- **Crocodile fish**: Confirmed by diveconcepts.com
- **Nudibranchs**: Confirmed
- **Octopus**: Confirmed
- **Parrotfish, wrasse, damselfish, pufferfish, rabbitfish**: Confirmed
- **Large purple coral fans and soft corals**: Confirmed
- **Red snappers**: Confirmed

Manta rays have been reported as occasional visitors, not a regular sighting.

## Dive Profile

The site suits a circumnavigation of the five rock structures. Depths range from 3–5 metres on the rock tops to 24–27 metres at the deeper bases. Night dives in the shallow 3–5 metre zone are particularly recommended for epaulette shark encounters.

## Safety Considerations

Currents are variable — mild to medium typical but can strengthen. Carry an SMB. The rock structures provide orientation references but flow reversals in the eddies between rocks can be disorienting.
