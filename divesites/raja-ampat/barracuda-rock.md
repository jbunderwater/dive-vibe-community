## Barracuda Rock

A small mushroom-shaped islet in the Wayilbatan area of western Misool, its base undercut by wave erosion to give the limestone formation a distinctive mushroom profile. The dive descends over white sand where garden eels emerge cautiously, then moves onto a series of limestone steps, coral heads, and sandy channels leading to a sloping wall with an overhanging curving section.

Schooling barracuda are the site's signature attraction — both blackfin and mixed-species schools are regularly reported. Giant trevally and reef sharks patrol the blue water off the wall. The macro-filled undercut holds Bargibanti pygmy seahorse, Denise pygmy seahorse, and a Santa Claus colour variant of pygmy seahorse; black coral bushes grow along the limestone steps. Currents at this site are variable and can start gently, accelerate, stall, and reverse within a single dive. Nurse sharks rest under rock ledges in calmer sections.

Advanced rating reflects the unpredictable currents and depth. Maximum depth 35 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Wayilbatan), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
