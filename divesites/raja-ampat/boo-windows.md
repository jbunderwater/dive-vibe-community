## Boo Windows

Boo Windows is one of Misool's most iconic dive sites — a steep southwest-facing wall dropping to approximately 35 meters, with iconic swim-through windows carved into the rock formation. Every source including Misool Eco Resort's official site describes this as a wall dive, not a reef flat.

## Overview

The site's defining features are the swim-through "windows" in the limestone rock, which are carpeted with vibrant soft corals and gorgonian fans. The wall face drops steeply from the surface to around 35 meters, making this a true wall dive. Visibility is typically excellent in the Misool area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Current**: Variable; assess before entry

## Marine Life

Wobbegong sharks, epaulette sharks, and pygmy seahorses inhabit the overhangs and windows. The wall and swim-through structures are encrusted with soft corals and sponges. Regional Raja Ampat Misool marine life applies; the sources above confirm the species listed.
