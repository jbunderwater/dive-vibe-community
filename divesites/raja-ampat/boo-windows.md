## Boo Windows

One of the most photographed dive sites in Raja Ampat, located at the western end of Boo Island in central Misool. The site's signature feature is two large rounded openings — "windows" — that pierce the main rock formation from the surface down to approximately 5 metres, large enough for several divers to pass through simultaneously. Sun rays streaming through the windows create distinctive light effects that draw underwater photographers from around the world.

The reef starts at 5–10 metres and drops as a steep wall to a sandy bottom at around 30 metres. Wobbegong sharks are confirmed residents, often resting on ledges beneath plate corals. Epaulette (walking) sharks are another highlight, confirmed by multiple diver accounts at this specific site. Raja Ampat pygmy seahorse and Bargibanti pygmy seahorse have been documented on the gorgonian fans; a Santa Claus colour variant of Denise's pygmy seahorse has also been recorded here. Hairy squat lobsters inhabit barrel sponges. Schools of batfish, fusiliers, and red-tooth triggerfish sweep through the site, and whitetip and grey reef sharks patrol the wall.

An offshore pinnacle to the south sits at roughly 15 metres and attracts pelagic species when current is running. Manta rays visit occasionally. Currents can be strong, running east to west across the site; the sheltered windows area is accessible to Intermediate divers while the exposed wall and pinnacle suit Advanced conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Misool area (Boo Island), Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
