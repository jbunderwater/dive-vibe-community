## The Cathedral

The Cathedral is a cave and cavern dive site located at the entrance to the channel in the middle of Kabui Bay, Raja Ampat. Multiple sources describe it as one of the most celebrated dives in the area, featuring small caverns with openings at the top that allow shafts of sunlight to illuminate the interior — the light effects that give the site its name.

## Overview

The Cathedral is positioned in the Kabui Bay area near the entrance to the passage between Gam and Waigeo islands, making it a natural companion dive to The Passage and Selat Kabui. The site combines two diving environments: shallow protected bays on the exterior with current-driven fish activity, and a cavern/cave zone with characteristic karst limestone formations and natural light shafts.

Moving to the island slope leads to small caverns with openings at the top through which sunlight penetrates, creating the illuminated interior that gives this site its name. The cave/cavern classification in the OSM data is consistent with the site description. The dive is described by rajaampatbiodiversity.com as "the Raja Ampat version of muck diving" — combining protected-bay macro hunting with the cavern section, rather than being primarily a large open cave dive.

Water temperature is approximately 27–29°C throughout the Kabui Bay area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kabui Bay entrance channel, Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable; protected bay conditions
- **Current**: Protected bay; channel currents on exterior section
- **Best Time**: October to April

## Marine Life

The following species are confirmed at The Cathedral by source documentation:

- **Ghost pipefish**: Confirmed by stayrajaampat.com and rajaampatbiodiversity.com as a key species here
- **Dwarf cuttlefish**: Confirmed by stayrajaampat.com and rajaampatbiodiversity.com
- **Stonefish**: Confirmed by stayrajaampat.com and rajaampatbiodiversity.com
- **Exotic sea slugs (nudibranchs)**: Confirmed
- **Partner gobies and blind shrimp**: Confirmed (commensal pair typical of sandy/rubble patches)
- **Bumphead parrotfish**: Confirmed in current zones near the site
- **Sea turtles**: Confirmed
- **Barracuda**: Confirmed in current zones
- **Sharks**: Confirmed (species not specified in sources)

The site's protected bay character and muck-style substrate make it an excellent macro site. The cave/cavern interior supports low-light species: encrusting sponges, tunicates, and crinoids on shaded cave walls are expected given the karst environment, though individual cave-interior species beyond the confirmed list above are not documented in available sources.

## Dive Profile

The dive begins in the protected bay exterior where divers can encounter current-loving species. Moving to the island slope brings divers to the cavern entrances. Inside, small openings at the top allow sunlight to enter on appropriate days and conditions. The depth in the cavern zone is within the Advanced but not technical range. Always maintain visual reference to natural light when inside the cavern.

## Safety Considerations

Advanced rating reflects the overhead environment. Divers without cavern experience should remain near the entrance and not proceed beyond visible daylight. Buoyancy control is critical to avoid disturbing silt. A torch is recommended. The Kabui Bay area is accessible by boat from Waisai, Sorong, and local homestays on Gam and Waigeo.
