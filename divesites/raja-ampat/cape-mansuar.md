## Cape Mansuar

Cape Mansuar is a wall and sloping reef dive off the tip of Mansuar Island in Raja Ampat, West Papua, Indonesia, positioned near the heart of the Dampier Strait's most productive diving. The cape's exposure to open-water currents channels large schools of fish around the point and draws reef sharks and occasional mobula rays from the open ocean.

## Overview

The dive begins at the cape tip, where large fish schools aggregate around the point, then follows a steep wall that transitions to a gentler reef slope further along. PADI's site profile describes "big seafans and many species of fish schooling on the point." Papua Diving classifies it as an Intermediate level site offering both wide-angle and macro opportunities, with mobula rays visiting from the open ocean. Soul Scuba Divers' site listing confirms barracuda, triggerfish, trevally, bumphead parrotfish, and reef sharks; a separate listing (Best Raja Ampat) notes scorpionfish, spiny lobsters, frogfish, and fan corals.

PADI records the site's maximum depth at 40 metres; the typical recreational range is 5–25 metres. Currents run around the cape and can be moderate to strong on peak tidal flow, sweeping divers along in a drift. Visibility averages 15–30 metres. Water temperature holds between 27–30°C. The site is approximately 5 minutes by boat from Yenbuba.

## Marine Life

Confirmed by site-specific sources:

- Large schools of fish aggregating at the cape tip
- Reef sharks (multiple sources; specific species not detailed)
- Barracuda, triggerfish, trevally, and bumphead parrotfish
- Scorpionfish, spiny lobsters, and frogfish in macro habitat
- Large sea fans on the wall face
- Mobula rays (occasional, from open ocean)

Wobbegong sharks, batfish, cleaning stations, and napoleon wrasse are not confirmed by sources specifically reviewed for Cape Mansuar. Wobbegong sharks are documented at nearby Mansuar-area jetties (Sawandarek, Yenbuba) but not at the cape itself by any source found. Batfish and napoleon wrasse are probable given the regional context but not sourced for this exact site.

## Dive Profile

The dive begins at the cape tip with the schooling fish and drifts with the current along the steep wall, transitioning to a sloping reef. Depths of 10–25 metres cover the most productive zone, with the deeper wall accessible to 35–40 metres. The ascent along the shallower reef suits the safety stop. Entry and exit are by boat.

## Practical Information

- **Depth range**: 5–25 m (wall drops to 40 m+)
- **Visibility**: 15–30 m
- **Current**: Moderate to strong (tidal drift)
- **Best time**: October–April
- **Water temperature**: 27–30°C
- **Entry**: Boat (approximately 5 minutes from Yenbuba)
