## L'Abym

L'Abym — meaning "The Abyss" — is a legendary wall in the Soufriere Scotts Head Marine Reserve where the vertical cliff face drops 1,500 feet (460 metres) into the deep, making it one of the most dramatic wall profiles in the Caribbean.

## Overview

Despite the intimidating scale, L'Abym is rated suitable for all skill levels by the SSMR, Dive Dominica, and Divers Alert Network — the dive starts on a sandy and rocky shelf at 25 feet (8 metres), and there is rarely any current in this protected section of the bay. Divers approach the wall edge and can float 30 feet off the face, simultaneously looking up to where the surface cliff enters the sea and down into the bottomless blue — an experience described as uniquely impressive even by experienced divers. The wall itself is colonised by black coral trees, whip corals, gorgonian clusters, and large orange elephant-ear sponges on the deeper sections, with encrusting sponges throughout the shallower reaches. A difficulty rating of Intermediate reflects the wall environment rather than challenging conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dominica, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters

## Marine Life

Seahorses, scorpionfish, and turtles are the most consistently reported species at L'Abym, with solitary barracuda commonly seen hovering in the blue water off the wall face. The DAN article documents black coral trees, whip corals, gorgonian clusters, and large orange elephant-ear sponges providing habitat for diverse invertebrates. Sargassum triggerfish have also been noted at this site. No source confirms hammerhead sharks or manta rays as regular or typical sightings here.
