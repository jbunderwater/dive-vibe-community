## Scotts Head Pinnacle

Scotts Head Pinnacle is one of Dominica's most celebrated dive sites, combining a signature swim-through, a soldierfish-packed cave, and a dramatic volcanic crater wall that plunges beyond 120 feet.

## Overview

Located where the Caribbean Sea meets the Atlantic at the tip of Scotts Head within the Soufriere-Scotts Head Marine Reserve, the dive begins at Swiss Cheese — a large rock formation riddled with holes and passages that is home to the famous Soldierfish Cave, a swim-through typically packed with blackbar soldierfish and French grunts that briefly part to let divers pass through. From Swiss Cheese, the route crosses to the main pinnacle where a picturesque swim-through at 35 feet bisects the formation and deposits divers on the inner face of the submerged volcanic crater, where the wall falls steeply to beyond 120 feet. The crater wall is draped in gorgonians, deepwater sea fans, and barrel sponges. Currents can be variable at this exposed site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dominica, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters

## Marine Life

The Soldierfish Cave holds dense schools of blackbar soldierfish and French grunts. The pinnacle walls are covered in gorgonians, deepwater sea fans, and barrel sponges. Hawksbill turtles and longsnout seahorses have been documented at this site, along with spiny lobsters in the crevices and peacock flounders on the sandy areas. Larger pelagics, including barracuda, pass through on stronger current days.
