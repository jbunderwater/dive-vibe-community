## Dominica

The "Nature Isle" of the Caribbean, where volcanic underwater hot springs create the unique Champagne Reef bubble dive and resident sperm whale pods can be encountered year-round.

## Description

Dominica is a rugged, volcanic island in the eastern Caribbean known for pristine, uncrowded dive sites along its sheltered west coast. Our database documents 12 sites ranging from 10 to 40 meters depth, including 6 reef sites, 4 wall dives, and 2 pinnacles. The island's volcanic geology produces unique underwater features found nowhere else in the Caribbean — geothermal vents that create curtains of warm bubbles at Champagne Reef, dramatic pinnacles rising from deep water, and nutrient-rich conditions that support dense marine life. Dominica is also the only place in the world with a year-round resident population of sperm whales, though whale encounters are snorkel-only by regulation.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Shore Diving**: 3 shore-accessible sites, including the famous Champagne Reef where volcanic gases bubble through fissures in the seafloor, creating a sensation of diving through champagne.
- **Boat Diving**: 9 boat-accessible sites along the west coast, with most operators running from Roseau and Soufriere. Boat rides to sites are typically short.
- **Wall Diving**: 4 wall dive sites with volcanic drop-offs along the leeward coast, featuring sponge-covered faces and overhangs.
- **Pinnacle Diving**: 2 pinnacle sites rising from deep water, attracting schooling fish and pelagics.
- **Reef Diving**: 6 reef sites over volcanic substrate, with healthy coral growth supported by nutrient-rich volcanic water.

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Douglas-Charles Airport (DOM) is the primary airport, receiving regional flights from Antigua, Barbados, Guadeloupe, Martinique, and Puerto Rico via carriers like LIAT, interCaribbean, and Air Antilles. There is no direct service from North America or Europe — connections through Antigua or Barbados are the most common routing. Canefield Airport near Roseau handles smaller aircraft.
- **Dive Operators**: Dive Dominica (the island's longest-running operation), Fort Young Hotel Dive Centre, and Nature Island Dive are the primary operators, all PADI-certified. Small group sizes are the norm.
- **Accommodation**: Roseau and the surrounding west coast offer guesthouses, eco-lodges, and the Fort Young Hotel which has an integrated dive centre. Dominica is not a mass-tourism destination — expect a quieter, more nature-focused experience.
- **Transportation**: Rental cars and taxis available. Roads are winding mountain roads. Dive operators typically provide hotel pickup.
- **Facilities**: Limited compared to larger Caribbean islands. Basic medical facilities in Roseau.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 26-29°C (79-84°F) year-round, with slightly cooler water (24-26°C) from January to March. Visibility typically 15-30 meters (50-100 ft). The west coast is sheltered from trade winds, creating generally calm dive conditions.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Hawksbill and green sea turtles, reef sharks, spotted eagle rays, seahorses, frogfish, jawfish, trumpetfish, moray eels, barracuda, and dense schools of creole wrasse. Sperm whale pods are resident year-round (encounters are snorkel-only, not scuba, and require a licensed operator with a government permit). Best whale season is November through March.
- **Conservation**: Dominica established the world's first sperm whale reserve in 2023, protecting the waters off the west coast. Dive operators follow strict environmental guidelines. The Soufriere-Scotts Head Marine Reserve protects key dive sites in the south.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: Year-round diving. November through May offers the driest weather. Sperm whale encounters are most reliable November through March. Hurricane season runs June through November.
- **Currency**: Eastern Caribbean Dollar (XCD); US dollars accepted at most dive operations.
- **Language**: English (official); Dominican Creole French widely spoken locally.
- **Safety**: Nearest recompression chamber is in Martinique or Guadeloupe. DAN insurance strongly recommended. The volcanic hot springs at Champagne Reef are warm but not dangerously hot. Always dive with a licensed operator for whale encounters.
