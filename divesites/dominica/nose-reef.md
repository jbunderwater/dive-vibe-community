## Nose Reef

Nose Reef is a distinctive site on Dominica's central west coast where three nose-shaped rocky protrusions extend from the Grand Savanne shelf edge into the open blue, creating a rich fringing reef loaded with sponges and critters on the structure and pelagic action in the water column beyond.

## Overview

Located off Grand Savanne on Dominica's central coast — a stretch that sees fewer visitors than the island's more famous northern and southern dive areas — Nose Reef sits on the south drop-off of the Grand Savanne Flats. The ridged reef starts at about 55 feet (17 metres) and cascades down to 130 feet (40 metres) and beyond, with the best diving on top of the deeper ledge at around 80 feet (24 metres). The three proboscis-like formations that give the site its name channel current along the structure, encouraging the growth of sponges of every colour and size and concentrating marine life at the reef's edges. The adjacent Whale Shark Reef takes its name from a historical encounter where a juvenile whale shark reportedly followed divers for the duration of their stay; it is not a site where whale sharks are regularly or predictably seen today.

## Marine Life

Sources document small schools of silvery bogas drifting along the reef top, Spanish mackerel and schooling jacks along the shelf edge, and the occasional yellowfin tuna in the blue water beyond the drop. Arrow crabs, cleaner shrimp, spotted flamingo tongue snails, and sea turtles are noted as regular sightings on the reef structure. Giant barrel sponges and yellow tube sponges are prominent. No source documents reliable whale shark encounters at Nose Reef itself.
