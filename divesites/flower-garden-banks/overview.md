## Flower Garden Banks

The northernmost coral reefs in the continental United States, rising from salt domes 100-115 miles offshore Texas in the Gulf of Mexico, with massive star coral heads, schooling hammerheads, and seasonal whale sharks.

## Description

Flower Garden Banks National Marine Sanctuary encompasses documented dive sites across 17 reefs and banks, with depths ranging from 7.6 to 43.9 meters. The sanctuary includes reef sites and wall sites, with all sites accessed exclusively by boat — typically multi-day liveaboard trips departing from the Texas coast. The coral cap of the East and West Flower Garden Banks sits atop salt domes and supports over 50% living coral cover, making these among the healthiest reefs in the Western Atlantic.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: All sites are boat-access only, reached via liveaboard vessels on 2-3 day expeditions. Typical dive profiles are 21-40 meters (70-130 ft) on the main banks
- **Reef Diving**: Reef sites including East Flower Garden Bank, West Flower Garden Bank, and deeper banks like McGrail Bank (43.9m), Bright Bank (34.1m), Geyer Bank, and Rankin Bank
- **Wall Diving**: Wall sites — Stetson Bank features dramatic pinnacles and ledges, while East Flower Garden Stairway offers a shallower wall profile starting at 7.6 meters

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Liveaboard vessels depart from Galveston, Freeport, Sabine Pass, and Surfside, Texas. Houston's George Bush Intercontinental Airport (IAH) or William P. Hobby Airport (HOU) are the nearest major airports, approximately 1-2 hours from departure ports
- **Dive Operators**: The 100-foot liveaboard vessel Fling is the primary operator, running two- and three-day trips from February through October. Gigglin' Marlin Dive Charters and Texas Caribbean Charters also serve the sanctuary. Trips cost approximately $600-700 per person
- **Accommodation**: Liveaboard vessels provide accommodation during dive trips. Shore-based lodging available in Galveston and Freeport for pre/post trip stays
- **Transportation**: A car or shuttle is needed to reach departure ports from Houston airports
- **Facilities**: Dive operators provide tanks and weights. Divers should bring their own equipment. Nitrox typically available on liveaboards

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperatures range 20-29C (68-84F) seasonally, with best conditions May through September. Visibility typically 18-30 meters (60-100 ft). Currents can be unpredictable this far offshore in the Gulf. Thermoclines are common on deeper banks
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Massive star coral (Orbicella) heads up to 3 meters across, spotted eagle rays schooling in early spring, scalloped hammerhead sharks (large schools of males in winter/spring), whale sharks (summer migrants, typically June-August), manta rays, loggerhead sea turtles, creole fish, and reef sharks. Over 300 species of fish documented in the sanctuary
- **Conservation**: Flower Garden Banks National Marine Sanctuary is managed by NOAA. Mooring buoys are required at all dive sites — anchoring is prohibited. Collecting anything is forbidden. The sanctuary was expanded in 2021 to include 14 additional reefs and banks

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: May through September for warmest water and calmest seas. February-April for hammerhead shark encounters. June-August for whale shark sightings. Weather windows matter — Gulf seas must be calm enough for the 10-12 hour transit
- **Currency**: USD
- **Language**: English
- **Safety**: All sites rated Advanced difficulty due to depth (most dives 21-40m), distance from shore (100+ miles, 10-12 hour boat transit), and unpredictable Gulf currents. Advanced Open Water certification required by most operators. Nitrox certification recommended for the repetitive deep profiles. Surface intervals spent on the liveaboard — no shore access during trips
