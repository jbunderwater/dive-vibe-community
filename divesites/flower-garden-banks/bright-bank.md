## Bright Bank

Bright Bank is a damaged but recovering deep reef within the Flower Garden Banks National Marine Sanctuary, notable for its lion's-paw shape and a history of severe destruction from 1980s treasure salvage operations.

## Overview

Bright Bank lies 124 miles (200 km) from shore, east of East Flower Garden Bank, and was added to the sanctuary in the 2021 expansion. Named after Tom Bright, a Texas A&M University marine biologist who studied the region in the 1970s–1980s and is credited as "the father of the Flower Garden Banks," the bank's crest sits at approximately 34 meters (112 feet) — within the 130-foot recreational dive limit. Since the 1980s, treasure hunters claiming the existence of a Spanish Galleon on the bank caused what NOAA describes as "tremendous destruction to the coral boulders," leaving excavation pits 3–4 meters (10–13 feet) deep. Only a small amount of coral reef habitat remains on the crest. The bank is shaped like a lion's paw and is connected to Rankin and 28 Fathom Banks via patch reefs, forming part of a larger Habitat Area of Particular Concern (HAPC). Current sanctuary regulations prohibit excavation and anchoring.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gulf of Mexico, 124 miles offshore (27.88°N, 93.30°W)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 34.1 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters (50–100 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 20–21°C (68–70°F) in winter; 29–30°C (84–86°F) in summer
- **Current**: Moderate to strong; variable offshore conditions
- **Best Time**: May–October for warmest water and best visibility

## Marine Life

NOAA documents boulder-like reef structures and coral patches in the shallower areas of Bright Bank, with coralline algae, hard corals, and extensive *Codium* algae fields in deeper zones. NOAA also notes crinoids and feathery crinoids among the documented fauna. The broader sanctuary fish community — butterflyfish, wrasses, groupers, snappers, jacks — is present, though the crest has reduced coral habitat due to historical salvage damage. Recovery is ongoing under sanctuary protection.

*No site-specific marine life census for Bright Bank has been published in the sources reviewed; regional sanctuary fauna applies.*

## Dive Profile

The reef crest sits at approximately 34 meters (112 feet), right at the recreational dive limit. Given the deep starting point, bottom time is extremely limited for recreational divers — a brief excursion to the crest followed by an ascent along the reef slope is the standard profile. The bank connects to adjacent Rankin and 28 Fathom Banks via patch reefs in shallower areas. Mooring buoys are in place (NOAA confirms Bright Bank has buoy access). Plan for extended safety stops; air management is critical.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by charter vessel; anchoring is prohibited throughout the sanctuary. Bright Bank is 124 miles from shore, farther offshore than the main Flower Garden Banks. Texas Caribbean Charters (texascaribbean.com) is the primary operator serving the sanctuary. Giant stride entry with full gear is standard. Deploy an SMB for ascent given offshore conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

Bright Bank is primarily a research destination and a conservation landmark — evidence of what unchecked treasure salvage can do to a coral reef. Dive the crest briefly and explore the connecting patch reefs at intermediate depths for a longer dive. Document the recovering coral structures if possible; sanctuary staff welcome observational reports. The site is less frequently visited than East/West Banks and Stetson, so expect a quieter experience.

## Safety Considerations

Bright Bank presents significant deep-diving challenges at 34 meters, combined with being 124 miles offshore. Air management is critical given the deep starting depth and the need for extended safety stops. Currents are variable. Emergency evacuation from this distance is a multi-hour operation. Advanced Open Water or equivalent minimum; deep diving experience strongly recommended. Follow all NOAA HAPC regulations: no anchoring, no take, no contact with reef.

## Photography

The recovering coral formations and connecting patch reef structures offer documentary photography opportunities. Wider-angle compositions of coral boulders amid excavation damage tell an important conservation story. Limited light at 34 meters requires supplemental lighting for macro work.
