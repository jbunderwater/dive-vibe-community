## Rankin Bank

Rankin Bank is a deep offshore bank within Flower Garden Banks National Marine Sanctuary, located approximately 9.3 miles east of East Flower Garden Bank, with a minimum depth of 164 feet (50 m) — beyond the 130-foot recreational dive limit.

## Overview

Rankin Bank lies just north of 28 Fathom Bank, separated from it by a 3,280-foot (1,000 m) wide trough reaching 571 feet (174 m) depth. The combined area of Rankin and 28 Fathom Banks is 5.6 square miles. Both were added to the sanctuary in 2021 as part of an expansion that tripled its protected area. The bank is named after John L. Rankin (1919–2011), the inaugural Gulf Region Director for the Minerals Management Service (now the Bureau of Ocean Energy Management), who oversaw oil and gas leasing in the Gulf for over 25 years. 28 Fathom Bank takes its name directly from its water depth.

Rankin Bank is part of a Habitat Area of Particular Concern (HAPC) that also encompasses Bright Bank and the extensive patch reefs connecting them. NOAA does not list Rankin Bank among the seven FGBNMS banks with reef habitat within the 130-foot recreational dive limit (that list includes Stetson, West Flower Garden, East Flower Garden, Bright, Geyer, McGrail, and Sonnier Banks). No mooring buoys are currently installed at Rankin Bank. The bank is documented as a research and conservation site rather than a recreational dive destination.

**Note for divers:** Rankin Bank's minimum depth is 164 feet (50 m), placing it beyond the recreational no-decompression limit. Diving here requires technical diving qualifications (extended-range or decompression diving certification) and appropriate equipment. Do not plan a recreational dive to Rankin Bank.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Flower Garden Banks, Gulf of Mexico (approximately 124 miles from land)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 50.0 meters

## Marine Life

NOAA documents black corals, octocorals, algae, sponges, stony corals, and a variety of invertebrates forming the reef community at Rankin and 28 Fathom Banks. Photographic surveys show fields of corkscrew-shaped whip corals on muddy bottoms, with grouper activity documented. Schooling fish are present. No recreational diver reports of specific species at Rankin Bank were found during research; the site is primarily accessed by NOAA research submersibles and technical divers.

## Dive Profile

The bank drops from a minimum of 164 feet (50 m) to 571 feet (174 m). Exploration of the shallowest areas requires decompression-capable equipment and gas planning. No mooring buoys exist at this site; vessels must maintain position. The bank connects to MacNeil Bank via a ridge system extending toward East Flower Garden Bank.

## Entry and Exit

No commercial dive charters regularly visit Rankin Bank. Research access is primarily by NOAA vessel and submersible. Technical divers wishing to visit would need to arrange private charter access from Freeport, Texas, and plan for technical decompression profiles.

## Safety Considerations

Rankin Bank is a technical dive destination at a minimum depth of 164 feet (50 m) in open Gulf water, 124 miles from land. Emergency evacuation is a multi-hour operation. Technical diving training and equipment are required. This site is not appropriate for recreational divers regardless of certification level.
