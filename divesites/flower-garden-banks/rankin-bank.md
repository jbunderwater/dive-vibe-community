## Rankin Bank

Rankin Bank is a deep reef within the Flower Garden Banks National Marine Sanctuary, beyond standard recreational diving limits.

## Overview

Rankin Bank is named after John L. Rankin (1919–2011), the first Director of the Gulf Region for the Minerals Management Service (now the Bureau of Ocean Energy Management). Located approximately 9.3 miles east of East Flower Garden Bank and 124 miles (200 km) from shore, it was added to the sanctuary in the 2021 expansion. NOAA documents the combined Rankin and 28 Fathom Banks area at 5.6 square miles (14.5 sq km), with a bottom depth range of 164–571 feet (50–174 m) — the shallowest point at 164 feet (50 m) places Rankin's reef beyond the 130-foot (39.6 m) recreational diving limit.

**This site is not accessible to recreational divers under standard certification limits.** NOAA's official list of seven sanctuary banks with reef habitat within the 130-foot recreational limit does not include Rankin Bank. The original data entry of 39.6 m (130 ft) for this site appears to have been in error; NOAA confirms the minimum depth is 50 m (164 ft).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gulf of Mexico, 124 miles (200 km) offshore (27.68°N, 93.58°W)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (technical diving only)
- **Site Type**: Coral reef (deep)
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (technical diving required; beyond recreational limits)
- **Minimum Depth to Reef**: 50 meters (164 feet) — beyond recreational no-decompression limits
- **Full Depth Range**: 50–174 meters (164–571 feet)

## Marine Life

NOAA documents the Rankin/28 Fathom Bank complex as hosting "black corals, octocorals, algae, sponges, stony corals, and a variety of invertebrates." This is a deep mesophotic reef ecosystem. No published species-specific dive reports exist for recreational visitors to Rankin Bank, as the depth precludes standard recreational access. The banks are designated as a Habitat Area of Particular Concern (HAPC), reflecting conservation importance rather than dive accessibility.

## Dive Profile

Rankin Bank is not a recreational dive site. The nearest reef structure begins at 164 feet (50 m), requiring decompression-capable technical diving equipment and training (minimum Advanced Nitrox/Decompression Procedures). The 124-mile offshore location adds extreme remoteness. The bank is separated from 28 Fathom Bank by a 3,280-foot (1,000 m) wide trough reaching 571 feet (174 m) depth.

## Entry and Exit

Access to the Rankin Bank area is by research or technical diving charter only. No commercial recreational dive operators are known to visit this site as a dive destination. The M/V Fling (Texas Caribbean Charters) and other Flower Garden Banks operators focus on the seven banks within recreational depth limits.

## Safety Considerations

Rankin Bank lies beyond standard recreational diving limits at a minimum reef depth of 50 m (164 ft), in open Gulf waters 124 miles from shore. Any diving here requires full technical diving certification, appropriate gas planning, decompression equipment, and an experienced operator familiar with deep offshore Gulf diving. This site is unsuitable for recreational scuba divers.
