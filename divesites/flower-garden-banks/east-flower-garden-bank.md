## East Flower Garden Bank

East Flower Garden Bank is one of the two original coral reef units of Flower Garden Banks National Marine Sanctuary, 100 nautical miles south of the Texas/Louisiana border in the open Gulf of Mexico.

## Overview

East Flower Garden Bank sits atop a salt dome rising from the Gulf floor, supporting the northernmost coral reef on the North American continental shelf. Over 52% of the reef cap — as much as five times the coral cover of comparable Caribbean reefs — is dominated by massive boulder-shaped brain and star corals (*Orbicella* species), creating a dense carpet of coral heads separated by sand channels. The reef cap tops out at approximately 55–65 feet (17–20 m); typical recreational dives run 65–95 feet. The sanctuary was designated in January 1992 as the 10th U.S. National Marine Sanctuary.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gulf of Mexico, ~100 nautical miles south of the Texas/Louisiana border
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (liveaboard overnight trips only)
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 21.3 meters (typical dive depth 20–29 m)

## Marine Life

NOAA sanctuary data and DAN Alert Diver document the following species confirmed at this site. Oceanic manta rays (*Mobula birostris*): over 100 individuals catalogued by unique belly spot patterns; research published in 2018 confirmed the area as juvenile manta ray nursery habitat, with the great majority of observed individuals below reproductive size. Loggerhead sea turtles and nurse sharks are regularly encountered. Scalloped hammerhead sharks school at the bank from January through early April. Whale sharks visit from July through September. Spotted eagle rays are reported in winter months. Fish life includes blue creole wrasses, yellowmouth grouper, marbled grouper, great barracuda, horse-eye jacks, squirrelfish, spotted moray eels, yellowheaded jawfish, and queen and French angelfish. Invertebrates include Christmas tree worms and queen conchs. Dominant corals are mountainous star coral (*Orbicella faveolata*), lobed star coral (*Orbicella annularis*), and boulder star coral (*Orbicella franksi*).

## Dive Profile

Seven mooring buoys are in place at East Bank, set at depths of 61–70 feet (19–21 m); vessels over 100 feet are not permitted on the buoys. The dense coral boulders rise 15–20 feet above the surrounding sand in some areas, creating complex topography. Sand channels between coral heads allow navigation. Visibility ranges from 75 to 150 feet, best in summer. Water temperature ranges from around the mid-70s°F in winter to the mid-80s°F in summer. The annual mass coral spawning event occurs 7–10 days after the August full moon, beginning around 8:30–9 p.m., when seven coral species including mountainous star coral, boulder star coral, symmetrical brain coral, and boulder brain coral simultaneously release gamete bundles — divers describe the ascending clouds of light-pink spheres as an underwater snowstorm.

## Entry and Exit

East Flower Garden Bank is approximately 100 nautical miles south of the Texas/Louisiana border and is not accessible by day boat from the Texas coast. The primary dive operator is Texas Caribbean Charters aboard the M/V *Fling*, a 100-foot liveaboard vessel departing from Freeport, Texas (726 West Brazos Street). Two- and three-day trips are offered from roughly February through October; most diving occurs at depths of 65–90 feet. Reservations book well in advance, particularly for August coral spawning trips.

## Tips and Recommendations

Schedule around the August full moon to witness coral spawning. Hammerhead schools are best in January through early April; whale sharks in July through September. Manta rays are present year-round and most reliably seen on slow drift passes along the reef edge — do not chase them. Night dives at East Bank are exceptional, with abundant bioluminescence. The NOAA sanctuary requires gear disinfection to prevent spread of stony coral tissue loss disease — follow Texas Caribbean Charters protocols exactly.

## Safety Considerations

East Flower Garden Bank is not suitable for novice divers. The site is approximately 100 nautical miles from shore in open Gulf water; weather windows can close with little warning, particularly during late-summer hurricane season. Currents are variable and can shift mid-dive — use the mooring line on descent. Carry a surface marker buoy at all times; separation from the vessel at this distance from shore is a serious emergency. Emergency medical evacuation is a multi-hour operation. The reef cap is within recreational depths, but remoteness and currents raise the risk profile.

## Photography

Wide-angle subjects abound: the dense coral boulder carpet, manta ray passes, and during August, the coral spawning gamete cloud rising from the reef. Hammerhead silhouettes (winter) and whale shark encounters (summer) are bucket-list shots. A fisheye or ultra-wide lens (8–15mm) suits most of the best images here; the natural light at 65–80 feet is good in summer. For spawning photography, position just above the reef and shoot upward into the ascending gamete bundles.
