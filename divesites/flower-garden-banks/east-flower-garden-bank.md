## East Flower Garden Bank

East Flower Garden Bank is the northernmost coral reef on the North American continental shelf, sitting atop a salt dome 119 miles south of the Texas–Louisiana border in the Gulf of Mexico.

## Overview

East Flower Garden Bank is one of the healthiest coral reef systems in the Western Hemisphere. Over 52% of the reef cap consists of living brain and star corals — approximately five times the coral cover found on most Caribbean reefs. The dominant species are three threatened Orbicella corals (mountainous star coral, boulder star coral, and lobed star coral), forming massive boulders that pile on one another and rise 15–20 feet above the surrounding sand. Seven mooring buoys provide access to the reef cap, which starts at 61–70 feet (19–21 m) depth. The bank is also confirmed as the world's first known manta ray nursery, with 95% of sighted individuals being juveniles — a finding published in 2018 by researchers from Scripps Institution of Oceanography and NOAA.

## Marine Life

NOAA and DAN Alert Diver document the following species confirmed at East Flower Garden Bank: oceanic manta rays (over 80 individuals identified by unique belly markings; nursery confirmed 2018 with ~95% juveniles), spotted eagle rays (school in early spring), loggerhead sea turtles, and nurse sharks year-round. Scalloped hammerhead sharks school from roughly January through March. Whale sharks visit especially during July, August, and September. Fish life confirmed at this site includes blue creole wrasses, queen and French angelfish, yellowmouth grouper, marbled grouper, rock beauty angelfish, Spanish hogfish, squirrelfish, scrawled cowfish, great barracuda, horse-eye jacks, yellowheaded jawfish, pufferfish, and moray eels. Invertebrates include red night shrimp, Christmas tree worms, and queen conchs. Orange and yellow sponges grow on the coral boulders. Silky sharks are documented at reef edges. Roper's inshore squid aggregate in August and September, sometimes coinciding with coral spawning.

## Dive Profile

Seven mooring buoys service East Flower Garden Bank, with attachment depths ranging from 61–70 feet (18.6–21.3 m) — buoy 4 is the shallowest at 61 feet (18.6 m). Typical dives run 65–95 feet over the brain and star coral boulders, which cover over 52% of the reef cap. The reef drops away beyond recreational range into mesophotic habitat. Sand channels between coral heads allow navigation, and nurse sharks or sea turtles are sometimes encountered resting in the open sand areas. Visibility ranges from 75 to 150 feet (23–46 m), best in summer. Water temperature ranges from the high 60s°F (about 20°C) in winter to the mid-80s°F (about 29°C) in summer. The mass coral spawning event occurs approximately 7–10 days after the August full moon, between 9 p.m. and midnight, when seven coral species simultaneously release gametes in what divers describe as an upside-down underwater snowstorm.

## Entry and Exit

East Flower Garden Bank is a liveaboard-only destination, approximately 100 nautical miles southeast of Galveston, Texas, in the open Gulf of Mexico. The primary operators are the M/V *Fling* (a 100-foot vessel, maximum 31 divers) and the M/V *Spree*, both departing from Freeport, Texas. Two- and three-day trips run from February through October. There is no day-boat access — the overnight crossing and 36-hour round trip require liveaboard scheduling. Reservations typically book months in advance; the coral spawning trips in August are especially popular.

## Tips and Recommendations

Schedule around the August full moon to witness coral spawning — one of the most spectacular natural events in Gulf diving. Arrive in January–March for scalloped hammerhead schools, and July–September for whale sharks. Manta rays are present year-round but most reliably seen on drift passes along the reef edge. Night dives at East Bank are exceptional; DAN describes the darkness as resembling outer space, with abundant bioluminescence. The NOAA sanctuary requires gear disinfection to prevent spread of stony coral tissue loss disease — follow charter protocols.

## Safety Considerations

East Flower Garden Bank is not suitable for novice divers. The site is 100 miles from shore in open Gulf water; weather windows are critical and can close with little warning. Currents are highly variable and can change mid-dive — strong surface currents may require hand-over-hand descent on the mooring line. Carry a surface marker buoy; separation from the vessel is a serious contingency at this distance from shore. Emergency evacuation to the nearest hospital would take hours. Dive within your certification limits; the reef cap is well within recreational depths, but currents and remoteness elevate the risk profile.

## Photography

East Flower Garden Bank is among the finest wide-angle diving subjects in the Gulf of Mexico. Manta ray portraits using an 8–15mm fisheye lens are a highlight; approach slowly and hover at mid-water to let mantas pass overhead. During coral spawning, wide-angle captures the gamete blizzard rising from the reef. Scalloped hammerhead silhouettes in the blue water column (winter) and whale shark encounters (summer) are bucket-list shots. The dense coral boulder carpet with abundant fish life rewards both macro and wide-angle approaches.
