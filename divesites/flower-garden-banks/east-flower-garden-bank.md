## East Flower Garden Bank

East Flower Garden Bank is a reef dive site in Flower Garden Banks, North America.

## Overview

East Flower Garden Bank sits atop a salt dome 100 nautical miles off Galveston, Texas, supporting the northernmost coral reef on the North American continental shelf. Coral covers more than 50 percent of the reef cap down to about 30 meters, dominated by massive brain and star coral formations, making it one of the healthiest reef systems in the Western Hemisphere. Resident manta rays, loggerhead sea turtles, and nurse sharks patrol the sand channels, while scalloped hammerhead sharks school here in winter and whale sharks pass through during summer months.

## Marine Life

NOAA and DAN Alert Diver document a dense Atlantic and Gulf assemblage at East Flower Garden Bank. Over 100 individual oceanic manta rays have been identified by their unique ventral markings; research published in 2018 confirmed the sanctuary as a manta nursery, with approximately 95% of individuals smaller than mature adults. Spotted eagle rays, loggerhead sea turtles, and nurse sharks are present year-round. Scalloped hammerhead sharks school here from roughly January through March. Whale sharks visit especially during July, August, and September. Fish life documented at the bank includes schools of blue creole wrasses, queen and French angelfish, yellowmouth grouper, marbled grouper, great barracuda, horse-eye jacks, squirrelfish, spotted moray eels, yellowheaded jawfish, and pufferfish. Invertebrates include red night shrimp, Christmas tree worms, and queen conchs. Barrel sponges are present among the coral boulders. About 20 species of sharks and rays have been documented across the sanctuary.

## Dive Profile

The reef cap of East Flower Garden Bank tops out at approximately 55–65 feet (17–20 m), with typical dives running to 65–95 feet over brain and star coral boulders that cover more than 52% of the reef cap. The reef drops away to mesophotic depths beyond recreational range. Sand channels between coral heads allow navigation. Seven mooring buoys are in place; vessels over 100 feet are not permitted on the buoys. Visibility ranges from 75 to 150 feet, best in summer. Water temperature ranges from the high 70s°F in winter to the mid-80s°F in summer. The mass coral spawning event occurs approximately 7–10 days after the August full moon, between 9 p.m. and midnight, when brain and star coral species simultaneously release gametes in what divers describe as a slow-motion underwater snowstorm.

## Entry and Exit

East Flower Garden Bank is a liveaboard-only destination, approximately 119 miles southeast of Galveston, Texas, in the open Gulf of Mexico. The primary operator is Texas Caribbean Charters, running the M/V *Fling* (a 100-foot vessel, maximum 31 divers) from Freeport, Texas. Two- and three-day trips run from February through October; NOAA's charter page lists Texas Caribbean Charters as the current operator. There is no practical day-boat access — the overnight crossing and 36-hour round trip require liveaboard scheduling. Reservations typically book months in advance; the coral spawning trips in August are especially popular.

## Tips and Recommendations

Schedule around the August full moon to witness coral spawning — one of the most spectacular natural events in Gulf diving. Arrive in January–March for scalloped hammerhead schools, and July–September for whale sharks. Manta rays are present year-round but most reliably seen on drift passes along the reef edge. Night dives at East Bank are exceptional; DAN describes the darkness as resembling outer space, with abundant bioluminescence. The NOAA sanctuary requires gear disinfection to prevent spread of stony coral tissue loss disease — follow charter protocols.

## Safety Considerations

East Flower Garden Bank is not suitable for novice divers. The site is 100 miles from shore in open Gulf water; weather windows are critical and can close with little warning. Currents are highly variable and can change mid-dive — strong surface currents may require hand-over-hand descent on the mooring line. Carry a surface marker buoy; separation from the vessel is a serious contingency at this distance from shore. Emergency evacuation to the nearest hospital would take hours. Dive within your certification limits; the reef cap is well within recreational depths, but currents and remoteness elevate the risk profile.

## Photography

East Flower Garden Bank is among the finest wide-angle diving subjects in the Gulf of Mexico. Manta ray portraits using an 8–15mm fisheye lens are a highlight; approach slowly and hover at mid-water to let mantas pass overhead. During coral spawning, wide-angle captures the gamete blizzard rising from the reef. Scalloped hammerhead silhouettes in the blue water column (winter) and whale shark encounters (summer) are bucket-list shots. The dense coral boulder carpet with abundant fish life rewards both macro and wide-angle approaches.
