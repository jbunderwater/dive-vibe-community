## McGrail Bank

McGrail Bank is a deep coral reef within Flower Garden Banks National Marine Sanctuary, accessible only to technical divers; its reef cap begins at 144 feet (44 m) — right at the edge of the recreational limit.

## Overview

McGrail Bank (formerly known as 18 Fathom Bank) is one of the seven sanctuary banks confirmed to have reef habitat within the 130-foot (39.6 m) recreational dive limit — though just barely. The reef cap peaks at 144 feet (44 m) and covers 4.7 square miles in two separate sanctuary sections, located approximately 109 miles from shore. Its scientific distinction is unique: the reef is dominated by blushing star coral (*Stephanocoenia intersepta*), and according to NOAA, no other known coral reef in the world is dominated by this species. Coral coverage reaches approximately 28% in the shallowest areas. The bank is designated a Coral Habitat Area of Particular Concern (HAPC).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gulf of Mexico, ~109 miles from shore, east of Geyer Bank
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (specialist charter only; no confirmed recreational mooring buoys)
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (technical diving depth — 44 m)
- **Maximum Depth**: 44.0 meters (reef cap; bank continues to 512 feet/156 m)

## Marine Life

NOAA's McGrail Bank page documents creolefish, groupers, snappers, reef butterflyfish, blue chromis, creole wrasses, squirrelfish, and horse-eye jacks in the water column above the reef cap. The shallow reef areas are dominated by blushing star coral colonies — this is the defining ecological feature of the bank. Deeper mesophotic zones (below approximately 60 m, accessible only by submersible or technical diving equipment) host black corals, octocorals, sponges, and various invertebrates. Sharks are documented in deeper zones. No site-specific marine life source was found for species not already confirmed by NOAA's McGrail Bank page; regional species present at East/West Banks may also occur here but cannot be attributed to this site specifically.

## Dive Profile

McGrail Bank's reef cap starts at 144 feet (44 m) — at or just beyond the no-decompression limit for recreational diving. The bank descends to 512 feet (156 m). Coral coverage reaches about 28% on the crest; deeper areas transition to coralline algae and mesophotic habitat. The site covers 4.7 square miles in two sections. No mooring buoys are confirmed at McGrail (NOAA mooring buoy records list buoys only at Stetson, East FGB, West FGB, Bright, Geyer, and Rezak Banks). Research and technical dive expeditions access the site directly.

## Entry and Exit

McGrail Bank is approximately 109 miles from shore — further than the three primary banks. Access requires a liveaboard-capable vessel; the primary operator for the region is Texas Caribbean Charters on the M/V *Fling* from Freeport, Texas, but McGrail is not a standard trip destination — confirm whether the bank is on the itinerary before booking. Most visitors are research or technical divers. NOAA lists McGrail among the seven banks with reef within recreational limits, but the 44 m reef cap means standard recreational divers are at or beyond their no-decompression limits immediately on arrival.

## Tips and Recommendations

McGrail requires careful gas planning for 44 m with a conservative bottom time and decompression stop capability. The unique blushing star coral formation is the primary scientific and visual draw. Carry a computer configured for your gas mix. This is not a site suited to improvised planning or casual recreational diving. The HAPC designation means no anchoring and strict no-contact reef rules apply.

## Safety Considerations

At 44 m, the reef top is beyond safe recreational no-decompression limits without careful gas management. Technical diving training (Advanced Nitrox/Decompression Procedures or equivalent) is appropriate. Narcosis risk at depth, unpredictable Gulf currents, and distance from shore (109 miles) all elevate the risk profile. Emergency medical evacuation is a multi-hour operation. The HAPC designation prohibits anchoring and bottom-tending gear, even outside sanctuary boundaries.
