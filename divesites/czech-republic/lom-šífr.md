## Lom Šífr

A long, narrow slate quarry near Svobodné Heřmanice with depths to 36 meters, a flooded diving caisson, and dramatic wall profiles.

## Overview

Lom Šífr is a flooded slate quarry located near the small village of Svobodné Heřmanice, approximately 140 km from Žilina near Opava in the Moravskoslezský region. The quarry is approximately 500 meters long and 30-70 meters wide, reaching depths of up to 36 meters. It sits at an altitude of 450 meters above sea level. The narrow, elongated shape of the quarry creates dramatic wall diving along the slate cliffs.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Svobodné Heřmanice, near Opava, Moravskoslezský kraj
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 36 meters
- **Current**: None
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Underwater Features

The main attractions include a fascinating flooded diving caisson, a well structure, and a telephone booth — quirky artifacts placed for diver interest. The slate walls create clean, angular underwater cliff faces that extend the full depth of the quarry. The narrow profile means wall diving is available from virtually any entry point.

## Marine Life

Limited freshwater life typical of high-altitude quarries. Some small fish and aquatic invertebrates.

## Dive Profile

The narrow quarry allows for wall diving along the entire length. Descend along the slate walls, which drop steeply to the 36-meter maximum. The caisson and well provide intermediate-depth points of interest. Due to the depth and cold water at altitude, this site requires advanced-level skills and proper gas management.

## Safety Considerations

The 36-meter depth combined with the 450-meter altitude requires careful dive planning. Altitude diving adjustments to decompression tables or computers are necessary. Cold water temperatures demand a dry suit and adequate thermal protection. The narrow quarry can feel confined at depth.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Adjust your dive computer for altitude diving — the quarry sits at 450 meters elevation
- The caisson is the highlight — plan your dive to include adequate bottom time at its depth
- Bring lights for exploring the wall features and submerged structures
