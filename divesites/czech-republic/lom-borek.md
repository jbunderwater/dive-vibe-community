## Lom Borek

A flooded serpentinite quarry with a multi-level profile, a sunken articulated bus, and clear water — one of the Czech Republic's most beautiful dive sites.

## Overview

Lom Borek is a flooded serpentinite (magnesium-rich rock) quarry located between Chotěboř and Golčův Jeníkov in the Havlíčkův Brod district, Kraj Vysočina. Serpentinite was mined here from World War II until 1992, and the quarry now covers approximately 2.5 hectares. A natural spring slowly fills the quarry, with the maximum depth increasing by a few centimeters each year — as of 2014 it measured 34.4 meters. The quarry serves as both a dive training and recreation center with on-site facilities.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Between Chotěboř and Golčův Jeníkov, Havlíčkův Brod district, Kraj Vysočina
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 34 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Clear, well-transparent water (approximately 5 meters average)
- **Current**: None
- **Best Time**: Year-round; visibility up to 25 meters under winter ice

## Underwater Features

The quarry has a distinctive stepped profile: an "upper platform" behind a flooded gate at approximately 6 meters, walls descending to a "lower platform" and corridor at approximately 21 meters running around the entire quarry, then another wall to the "bottom" at approximately 31 meters, with a "hole" at the former pump location reaching the maximum depth of 34.4 meters.

Notable sunken attractions include an Ikarus articulated bus (placed in 2003, intact except glass and engine), a stripped Wartburg car wreck, and a "submarine" named Stejškalník (actually a former tank/cistern). A mermaid statue adds a whimsical touch.

## Marine Life

Rich freshwater life: perch, bream, carp, pike, catfish, zander, and crayfish. Fish congregate around the bus, car, and other submerged structures. Freshwater jellyfish (Craspedacusta sowerbii) have been documented in Czech quarry sites generally but sightings at this specific quarry have not been independently confirmed — visit in late summer if this is a goal.

## Dive Profile

Multiple depth levels offer dives for various skill levels. Start at the upper platform (6 meters) for orientation, then descend along the wall to the lower corridor (21 meters) for a scenic circuit. Advanced divers can continue to the bottom at 31 meters or explore the deepest point at 34.4 meters. The stepped profile naturally supports multi-level dive planning.

## Access and Fees

From Golčův Jeníkov toward Chotěboř, take a right turn before the village of Borek. You can drive right to the water's edge. Diving requires signing a daily dive form; fees are 100 CZK per dive or 150 CZK per day. Facilities include a filling room, rental room, common room, changing areas, and sauna.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Winter ice diving offers visibility up to 25 meters — the best conditions of the year
- The multi-level profile is perfect for practicing depth management and multi-stop ascents
- On-site facilities make this a convenient base for a full day of diving
