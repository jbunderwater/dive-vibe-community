## Jezero Barbora

A former brown coal mining pit transformed into one of the Czech Republic's premier dive training centers, managed by the Czech Diving Association.

## Overview

Jezero Barbora (Lake Barbora) sits in the Mostecká basin near Oldřichov, approximately 6 km west of Teplice in the Ústecký region. Surface mining of brown coal began here and the residual pit filled with water in the 1970s. The lake spans approximately 55 hectares with a total depth of around 60 meters. Thanks to the depth and the lake's strong self-purification capacity, cyanobacteria is not a significant issue and the water quality is frequently compared to sea water. The site serves as a popular dive training center used by Czech diving clubs.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Oldřichov, near Teplice, Ústecký kraj
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Beach dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable — 1-2 m on shallows to 8 m, improving to 7-9 m below the thermocline at depth
- **Current**: None
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Underwater Features

The lake contains numerous training and attraction features, all marked with buoys: training platforms at 5, 12, and 22 meters depth, a caisson (keson) at 10 meters, a sunken Wartburg van at 7 meters, a Jesus statue, a submarine model, an airplane model, a kayak, a diver figurine, and a treasure chest. Submerged trees from the pre-flooding era are also present.

## Marine Life

Freshwater species typical of Czech standing water bodies. Expect to encounter carp, perch, pike, and various smaller freshwater fish among the submerged structures and trees.

## Dive Profile

Multiple depth levels make this site suitable for various experience levels. Shallow platforms at 5 meters are ideal for training exercises, while deeper platforms at 12 and 22 meters offer progression for more experienced divers. The deepest areas reach 40 meters or more. The numerous sunken artifacts provide navigation landmarks and points of interest throughout the dive.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from designated access points. The lake is well-organized as a training facility with marked entry/exit points.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A dry suit is strongly recommended year-round due to cold water temperatures
- Bring a dive light for exploring deeper features and the submerged artifacts
- Follow buoy markers to locate the various training platforms and attractions

## Safety Considerations

Cold water temperatures require appropriate thermal protection. As a managed dive training center, the site has established safety protocols. Dive within your certification limits and be aware of depth-related hazards at the deeper areas.
