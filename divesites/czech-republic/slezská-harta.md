## Slezská Harta

The Czech Republic's youngest major reservoir, offering deep technical diving to a submerged bridge at 40-50 meters — boat access only.

## Overview

Slezská Harta is a large reservoir constructed between 1987 and 1997 on the Moravice River in the Nízký Jeseník mountain range near Bruntál, Moravskoslezský region. Covering 8.7 km², it is the 9th largest reservoir in the Czech Republic and the youngest major dam in the country. The most notable diving feature is the original bridge across the Moravice River, now submerged with the bridge top at approximately 40 meters and maximum depth around 50 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Bruntál, Moravskoslezský kraj
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 50 meters
- **Current**: None to light
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Underwater Features

The star attraction is the original pre-dam bridge across the Moravice River, now fully submerged. The bridge top sits at approximately 40 meters, with the maximum depth around 50 meters at the riverbed. Remnants of the flooded valley — roads, foundations, and vegetation — line the bottom.

## Restrictions

The reservoir serves as a pre-reservoir before the Kružberk water supply dam, so access is strictly regulated. Motor vessels are restricted to dam management only. Diving is prohibited in the dam safety zone (approximately 1 km from the dam to the yellow buoy line) and in the upper sections of the Dlouhá Stráň and Pod Černým Mostem bays (protected fish spawning areas).

## Marine Life

Freshwater species typical of Czech reservoirs. The deep, cold water limits biological activity at diving depths.

## Dive Profile

Access is by non-motorized boat only. The bridge dive is a deep technical dive requiring proper training and equipment. Plan gas management carefully for depths of 40-50 meters in cold freshwater. Ascent rates and decompression stops must be strictly observed.

## Safety Considerations

This is a technical diving site. Cold water at depth, boat-only access, and the 40-50 meter depth range require advanced or technical certification, appropriate gas planning, and redundant equipment. Familiarize yourself with the reservoir's access restrictions before diving.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Technical divers gathered here in November 1999 for a notable dive to the submerged bridge
- Coordinate access in advance due to strict reservoir regulations
- A dry suit and appropriate thermal undergarments are essential
