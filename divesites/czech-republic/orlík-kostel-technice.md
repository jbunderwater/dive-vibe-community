## Orlík - Kostel Technice

The submerged ruins of a 14th-century Gothic church lying 45-60 meters below the surface of Orlík Reservoir — one of the Czech Republic's most iconic dives.

## Overview

When the Orlík Dam on the Vltava River was completed and the reservoir began filling in 1961, the village of Těchnice and its Gothic church were submerged. The reservoir became the Czech Republic's largest-volume reservoir (716 million m³). The church was cleared and burned before flooding, but the structural remains are remarkably well preserved. Divers can explore the Gothic gable, ribbed vaulted ceiling, Gothic window openings, and cast-iron bannisters at depths of 45-60 meters below the reservoir surface. Note: the village name is "Těchnice" (the site is sometimes anglicised as "Technice").

## Site Information

- **Location**: Orlík Reservoir, South/Central Bohemia (Středočeský / Jihočeský kraj)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 60 meters
- **Reservoir Maximum Depth**: 74 meters
- **Current**: None to light
- **Best Time**: Late summer/autumn when reservoir levels are lower

## Underwater Features

The church ruins retain substantial architectural detail despite decades of submersion. The Gothic gable, ribbed vaulting, and window openings are identifiable. Cast-iron bannisters mark the former entrance. The surrounding area includes foundations and remnants of the flooded village of Technice.

## Marine Life

Freshwater reservoir species. Visibility and biological activity vary significantly with season and depth.

## Dive Profile

This is a deep dive requiring careful planning. Access is by boat only. The church depth varies with reservoir water level but typically sits between 45-60 meters. Decompression diving is likely required. Cold water at depth demands proper thermal protection and gas management.

## Safety Considerations

This is an advanced to expert dive due to depth, cold water, and limited visibility at depth. Proper training in deep diving and potentially decompression procedures is required. Dive with a qualified guide who knows the current depth and position of the ruins. Reservoir water levels change seasonally, affecting the dive profile.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Contact local dive operators for current water levels and church depth
- This dive may require technical certification depending on current depth
- Bring powerful lights — visibility at 45-60 meters in a reservoir can be very limited
- The Orlík area also offers shallower dive sites along the reservoir banks
