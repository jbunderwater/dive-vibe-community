## Černá Voda - Lom Rampa

A dramatic flooded granite quarry in the Žulovská pahorkatina, famous for its 16.5-meter perpendicular rock wall rising above the water.

## Overview

Lom Rampa is one of the most famous flooded quarries of the Žulovská pahorkatina (Žulová Hills) near the village of Černá Voda in the Jeseníky area, Olomoucký region. Before World War I, the quarry was operated by the H. Kulka company, and a 2,832-meter railway spur was built in 1918-1919 to transport stone. It is one of the "Rychlebské zatopené lomy" (Rychleby flooded quarries). The quarry's defining feature is a high perpendicular rock wall rising 16.5 meters above the waterline, also popular with cliff jumpers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Černá Voda, Jeseníky area, Olomoucký kraj
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 1-2 meters near the surface, improving below 5 meters
- **Current**: None

## Underwater Features

The granite walls continue below the waterline, creating an underwater wall dive experience. The quarry floor holds remnants from the stone-cutting era. The granite formations and underwater cliff faces provide the main visual interest.

## Marine Life

Limited freshwater life typical of flooded quarries. Small fish and aquatic plants may be present.

## Dive Profile

A relatively shallow dive suitable for intermediate divers. The underwater walls mirror the dramatic above-water cliffs. Visibility improves with depth — expect murky conditions in the first few meters. Explore along the quarry walls for the best experience.

## Entry and Exit

From Žulová, head toward Černá Voda. At the beginning of the village past a children's home, turn right between houses, continue approximately 500 meters past scattered dwellings, then straight 300 meters to the quarry.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a light for exploring the wall features at depth where visibility improves
- The site is also popular for cliff jumping, so watch for jumpers when surfacing
- A dry suit is recommended due to cold water temperatures
