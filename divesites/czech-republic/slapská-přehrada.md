## Slapská Přehrada

A large reservoir on the Vltava River south of Prague with deep wall profiles, submerged wrecks, and remnants from pre-dam villages.

## Overview

Slapská Přehrada (Slapy Dam) is a major reservoir built in the 1950s on the Vltava River, located approximately 22 km south of Prague in the Central Bohemian Region. The reservoir covers 11.63 km² and reaches depths of up to 56 meters. When the river was dammed and the reservoir completed in 1961, several villages and structures were submerged, including remnants divers can still explore today. Multiple named dive entry points exist around the reservoir, including a beach entry that leads to a dramatically steep underwater profile.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Slapy Dam, Central Bohemian Region (Středočeský kraj)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 56 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 3-12°C (varies by season and depth)
- **Current**: None to light
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Underwater Features

The bottom descends gradually from the beach entry to about 9 meters, then drops steeply to 40+ meters. Training platforms are positioned at 7 meters (30 meters from shore) and 25 meters (depth varies with water level). Notable submerged features include a pontoon at 43 meters, a boat wreck at 25 meters, numerous tree stumps and wood remains from the flooded valley, several barrels, and rocky steps from the pre-dam landscape.

## Marine Life

Freshwater species typical of large Central Bohemian reservoirs. Fish congregate around the submerged structures and wood remains. No site-specific aquatic life reports confirmed for this entry point. Typical central European freshwater fauna (perch, bream, carp) may be present.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the Měřín beach and descend gradually across a sandy-muddy bottom to the 9-meter break point. Beyond this, the profile drops steeply. The training platform at 7 meters makes a good reference point. Advanced divers can explore the boat wreck at 25 meters or continue to the pontoon at 43 meters. Plan gas management carefully for the deeper features.

## Safety Considerations

A dive buoy (SMB) is strongly recommended — the reservoir sees significant boat traffic, especially in summer. Watch for boats when surfacing. The deep profiles and cold bottom temperatures make this an advanced-level site. Ensure adequate thermal protection and gas reserves.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Deploy an SMB before ascending — boat traffic is a real hazard
- Deeper features are subject to water level changes; depths given are approximate
- Bring lights for the deeper areas and wreck exploration
