## Písník Březhrad

A shallow, clean-water sand pit between Hradec Králové and Pardubice, ideal for training dives and relaxed freshwater exploration.

## Overview

Písník Březhrad (also known as Opatovický písník or "Opaťák") is a flooded sand pit located between the villages of Březhrad and Opatovice nad Labem, on the road connecting Hradec Králové and Pardubice in the Pardubický region. Sand and gravel mining ended in 2001, leaving a clean-water lake of approximately 30 hectares. The older, less-visited section of the pit offers cleaner water due to fewer swimmers, and the site doubles as a fishing reserve (mimoprstruhový rybářský revír).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Between Březhrad and Opatovice nad Labem, Pardubický kraj
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Beach dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Good (clean water in the older section)
- **Current**: None
- **Best Time**: Year-round; cleanest water outside swimming season

## Underwater Features

Mining equipment, cranes, tires, and iron objects remain on the sandy bottom. Several small boat wrecks provide additional points of interest. The sandy substrate and scattered industrial remnants create a shallow exploration playground.

## Marine Life

As an active fishing reserve, the site hosts a variety of freshwater fish. Expect to encounter carp, perch, and other common Czech freshwater species among the submerged structures.

## Dive Profile

A simple, shallow dive with a maximum depth of 7 meters. Enter from shore and navigate between the scattered industrial remnants and boat wrecks. The flat, sandy bottom and calm conditions make this an excellent site for training dives, equipment testing, and photography practice.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The older section of the sand pit offers the cleanest water — avoid the swimming areas
- A dry suit is recommended, especially outside summer months
- Good site for new divers to build confidence and log easy dives
