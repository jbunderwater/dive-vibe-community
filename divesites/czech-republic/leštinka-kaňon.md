## Leštinka - Kaňon

A small, atmospheric granite quarry tucked into a forest canyon near Skuteč, part of the broader Leštinka diving area.

## Overview

Leštinka - Kaňon is a small flooded granite quarry accessed from the village of Leštinka near Skuteč in the Pardubický region. The quarry lies at the end of a 400-meter path through forest and meadow, past a transformer station and two cottages, then through a narrow canyon. Despite its modest depth, the quarry's romantic surroundings and proximity to the larger Divepark Leštinka complex make it a popular stop. The area is busy with campers in summer but quiet the rest of the year.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Leštinka, near Skuteč, Pardubický kraj
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Beach dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Current**: None
- **Best Time**: Spring and autumn for fewer crowds

## Underwater Features

The quarry bottom holds remnants of granite mining equipment and pipes, an old training rig, a caisson, and a sunken museum exhibit. The canyon walls continue underwater, creating an enclosed, atmospheric diving environment.

## Marine Life

Limited freshwater life typical of small quarries. Some small fish and aquatic invertebrates.

## Dive Profile

A straightforward shallow dive. Enter from shore and explore the quarry floor, following the walls for orientation. The maximum depth of 10 meters makes this suitable for training dives and checkout sessions. The confined space provides a good environment for practicing buoyancy skills.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Several dive centers in the broader Leštinka / Divepark area provide bottle filling, equipment rental, food, and accommodation
- This is a distinct quarry from the main Lom Leštinka — follow signs toward "Kaňon"
- A dry suit is recommended due to cold water temperatures
