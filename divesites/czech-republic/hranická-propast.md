## Hranická Propast

The deepest flooded pit cave on Earth — a 519-meter karst abyss in eastern Moravia whose true bottom remains unknown.

## Overview

Hranická Propast (Hranice Abyss) is located within the Hůrka u Hranic National Nature Reserve on the right bank of the Bečva River, near the town of Hranice in the Olomoucký region. A 69.5-meter dry shaft leads down to the water surface, below which the cave extends at least 450 meters underwater — confirmed by an autonomous underwater vehicle from INESC TEC in 2022. The total confirmed depth is 519.5 meters, making it the deepest flooded pit cave in the world. The true bottom remains unknown and may exceed 1 kilometer.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Hůrka u Hranic Nature Reserve, near Hranice, Olomoucký kraj
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 450 meters
- **Current**: None
- **Water Temperature**: Thermal mineral water, warmer than surrounding quarries

## Notable Explorations

Polish explorer Krzysztof Starnawski reached 265 meters on a closed-circuit rebreather in 2015, setting exploration records. In 2022, a Portuguese-built AUV descended to 450 meters without reaching the bottom. The cave's geological origin is linked to deep hydrothermal processes in the Earth's crust, with mineral-rich water rising from below.

## Safety Considerations

This is not a recreational dive site. Access is restricted within the national nature reserve and requires special permits. Only professional cave divers with extreme-depth experience and specialized equipment should attempt any diving here. The site has been the focus of scientific expeditions, not recreational visits.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This site is included for completeness as a world-record natural feature in the Czech Republic
- Recreational divers can visit the nature reserve above ground and view the dry shaft entrance
- The nearby Lom Opatovice u Hranic offers accessible quarry diving in the same region
