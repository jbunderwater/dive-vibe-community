## F2

A WWII-era German escort vessel lying on her port side in Gutter Sound, the F2 is typically dived alongside the adjacent YC-21 salvage barge — giving divers two wrecks in just 15 metres of water, making it one of the best second dives in Scapa Flow.

## Overview

The F2 was built by Germaniawerft at Kiel in 1936. Though she resembles a destroyer, she was actually a *Geleiteboot* — a light escort vessel — fitted experimentally with high-pressure boilers. She was later lengthened by four metres and converted to a torpedo recovery vessel at Wilhelmshaven in 1938–1939. After Germany's defeat, she was handed to Britain as war reparations and sunk at her present location in Gutter Sound in 1946 during a gale. In 1967, Metrec Engineering purchased her for salvage; the mid-to-aft sections were heavily blasted during the salvage work, though the bow section and bridge area survive well.

The wooden salvage barge YC-21, which was actively working on the F2, sank in a subsequent storm on 15 November 1968 and now lies alongside — connected to the F2 by a 30-metre shot line. Notably, the F2's own 20mm gun was on the barge when it sank and can still be found on the YC-21's wreckage.

## Marine Life

The F2 and YC-21 are in Gutter Sound, sheltered from the strongest tidal races that affect Burra Sound, so marine life is plentiful and diverse. Shoals of small fish hover around the superstructure. Cod, ling, saithe, wrasse, and conger eels inhabit the wreck. Lobsters and edible crabs tuck into the hull plating. Plumose anemones and dead man's fingers colonise the exposed metalwork.

## Dive Profile

The F2 lies on her port side in a maximum depth of around 15 metres, with the hull rising to approximately 8 metres at the keel. Descend to the bow section first — the 4.1-inch open-backed gun turret pointing forward is immediately impressive, and the anchor chain runs visibly from the hawse pipe. Swim along the keel side of the wreck keeping the hull to your left, taking in the bridge section before the heavily blasted mid-section opens into a tangle of cables, bollards, and stern post fittings. A 30-metre shot line then leads to the YC-21 barge. Operators typically start divers on the YC-21 as it is easier to find the connecting line from there.

## Entry and Exit

Day charter boats operate from Stromness and St Margaret's Hope. Gutter Sound does not carry the extreme tidal flows of Burra Sound, so this site has wider tidal windows than the blockship dives. Entry by giant stride or back roll; ascend on the shot line and deploy an SMB at the 5-metre safety stop.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A drysuit is essential — water temperature 6–12°C year-round
- Budget for a full dive on both the F2 and YC-21 — the connecting shot line makes it simple to visit both in one dive
- This is an ideal second dive of the day after a morning on the deeper German battleships
- Bring a torch to illuminate the blasted mid-section and look for conger eels in the darker recesses
- Best months: May to September

## Safety Considerations

Water temperature in Scapa Flow is 6–12°C year-round; a drysuit and adequate undersuit are essential. While Gutter Sound does not have the extreme tidal flow of Burra Sound, always confirm conditions with your skipper. Dive within your certification limits. Deploy an SMB on ascent.
