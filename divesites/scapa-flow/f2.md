## F2

A shallow Scapa Flow wreck dive that serves as an excellent introduction to the Flow's cold-water marine life and historical atmosphere.

## Overview

The F2 is a smaller, shallower wreck in Scapa Flow that functions as an excellent orientation dive for newcomers to the Flow's cold-water environment before tackling the deeper German High Seas Fleet wrecks. Resting in just 15 meters, the F2 allows divers to acclimatize to Scapa Flow conditions — cold water, often limited visibility, and dry suit diving requirements — in a more forgiving depth profile than the battleships and cruisers. The wreck has become well-colonized by the cold-water marine life characteristic of Scapa Flow: plumose anemones, dead man's fingers, encrusting sponges, and the resident fish that shelter in the structure. The F2 is listed as a dive site paired with the YC-21 Salvage Barge in Burra Sound, providing additional underwater interest in the same area.

## Marine Life

Scapa Flow's cold, nutrient-rich water supports dense cold-water invertebrate communities on all wreck surfaces. The F2 carries plumose anemones, dead man's fingers, encrusting sponges, and various sea star species. Conger eels, crabs, and lobsters occupy sheltered spaces in the wreck structure. Schools of saithe (coalfish) and pollock are common in the water column above the wreck. Velvet swimming crabs are abundant throughout the Flow's wreck systems.

## Dive Profile

The 15-meter depth makes this a comfortable Intermediate dive with extended no-decompression time. Descend to the wreck structure and explore the outer surfaces systematically before examining any accessible interior sections. The marine life on the encrusted surfaces rewards slow, careful observation. Conduct a 3-minute safety stop before surfacing and deploy an SMB.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Stromness-based operators. Most Scapa Flow liveaboards and day charter operations include the F2 as part of their rotation, particularly as a first or last dive of the day. Dry suit essential. Descend directly on a shot line.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Use the F2 as a practice dive to verify dry suit buoyancy management before tackling the deeper fleet wrecks
- Camera settings, strobe placement, and buoyancy should all be tested here before the German fleet dives
- Cold water and dry suit buoyancy management take adjustment — do not rush this acclimatization
- The wreck's marine life is rich enough to be worthwhile on its own merits, not just as a training dive
- Orkney's summer evenings offer extended daylight that makes late afternoon dives possible

## Safety Considerations

Tidal current in Burra Sound is significant — slack tide timing is important. Dry suit proficiency is essential in Scapa Flow waters where temperatures reach 6°C. Visibility in the Flow can be poor in certain seasons — brief on navigation and buddy protocols before descending. Hypothermia risk is real in these waters without proper thermal protection.

## Photography

The F2's shallowness and rich invertebrate coverage make it an excellent photography site for testing setups before the main fleet dives. Plumose anemone clusters and dead man's fingers corals photograph well with artificial light. Conger eel portraits in the wreck crevices are compelling subjects. The shallow depth allows more natural light exposure in summer than the deeper battleships.
