## Arta Beach Reef

Arta Beach Reef is a shore-entry dive site on the southern shore of the Gulf of Tadjoura, east of Djibouti City, noted for its accessibility and for the seasonal concentration of whale sharks just offshore.

## Overview

Arta Beach sits at the confluence of Red Sea and Indian Ocean ecosystems along the Gulf of Tadjoura. The dive site features underwater cliffs, coral formations accessible to novice divers, and schools of barracuda. Green turtles are regularly reported at this site by Scuba News and Scuba Diver Life. From November to February, concentrations of plankton offshore draw juvenile whale sharks to the surface, where they feed in the first five metres of the water column — making encounters accessible to snorkellers as well as divers.

Multiple diving sources (Dive The World, Scuba Diver Life, The Scuba News) describe Arta Beach as one of Djibouti's most accessible sites for whale shark encounters. The site has been described as a favourite of Jacques Cousteau; this connection is repeated across several travel sources but has not been verified against primary historical records — it should be treated as a popular claim rather than a confirmed fact.

The nearby headland Ras Eiro (east of Arta) offers more varied diving with drop-offs to 40–45 metres and reported sightings of groupers, morays, and anemone fish, though conditions there are better suited to intermediate divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern shore of Gulf of Tadjoura, near Arta, Djibouti
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
