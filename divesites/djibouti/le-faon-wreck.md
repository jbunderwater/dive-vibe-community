## Le Faon Wreck

Le Faon is a large cargo ship wreck lying on its port side near Moucha Island in the Gulf of Tadjoura, one of Djibouti's most popular dive sites.

## Overview

The wreck known locally as Le Faon was built in 1959 at the Howaldtswerke shipyard in Kiel, Germany, and operated under several names during its working life: MV Priamos (Hamburg banana carrier), MV Santa Trinidad, MV Phaon, and finally MV Orchard Reefer (registered in Singapore from 1974). On 22 November 1974, while sailing from Jeddah to Singapore, a fire broke out in the engine room after passing Aden. The 27-man crew — including a Norwegian master, an Indonesian radio operator, and 25 Filipino crew members — safely abandoned ship and were rescued by the motor tanker Dora. The vessel was towed to Djibouti, where it capsized and ran aground near Moucha Island.

The wreck lies on its port side in 10–28 metres of water approximately 9 km from Djibouti City, making it easily accessible as a day dive from the capital. Note: sources disagree on the ship's length, reporting figures between 120 metres and 135 metres; the most detailed account (Lukas Spieker's Underway Blog, using shipyard records) gives 126 metres. The hull is now heavily colonised by hard and soft corals. There is a prominent crack in the hull behind the bridge where divers can pass from what was the deck side to the keel side — wreck penetration requires appropriate training and equipment. Schools of batfish, glassfish, and snappers congregate throughout the structure, and moray eels are frequently seen in the coral-encrusted superstructure. Atlas & Boots (2018 firsthand dive account) report glassfish and snappers on this wreck.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Moucha Island, Gulf of Tadjoura, approximately 9 km from Djibouti City
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
