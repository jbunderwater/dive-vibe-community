## Îles Moucha الجزر موشا

The Moucha Islands archipelago sits in the Gulf of Tadjoura about 11 km north-west of Djibouti City, forming part of Djibouti's first designated marine protected area.

## Overview

The Moucha Islands group — encompassing Moucha and Maskali islands plus smaller islets — offers the most accessible diving in Djibouti and is the primary day-trip destination from Djibouti City. The protected area status limits industrial activity, and the reefs show healthy coral growth across multiple dive sites. Dive The World identifies three main diving areas around the archipelago: The Aquarium (sheltered bay with calm conditions and colourful reef fish), Dolphin Reef (where spinner dolphins are frequently encountered), and Coral Gardens (shallow hard coral formations). Visibility ranges from 15 to 30 metres across sites.

Atlas & Boots, diving with local operator Dolphin Excursions, report batfish, glassfish, snappers, lionfish, and rays. Sea turtles and dolphins are noted by Dive The World as regular visitors to the archipelago. No site-specific marine life inventories were found; these species represent what operators and divers have reported across the island group.

The wreck of Le Faon (MV Priamos / Orchard Reefer) lies adjacent to the islands at 10–28 metres and is a separate entry; most day trips from Djibouti City combine reef dives at the Moucha Islands with a wreck dive. Dolphin Excursions, the main PADI-certified operator, runs regular trips to the archipelago and can be contacted through divedjibouti.com.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gulf of Tadjoura, Djibouti, approximately 11 km from Djibouti City
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
