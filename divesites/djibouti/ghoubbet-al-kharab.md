## Ghoubbet Al-Kharab

Ghoubbet Al-Kharab — Arabic for "Gulf of the Demons" — is a deep volcanic bay at the junction of the African and Arabian tectonic plates, connected to the Gulf of Tadjoura by a narrow, current-charged channel.

## Overview

The bay is rimmed by mountains and cliffs rising to 600 metres and sits directly on the Asal Rift, where the African plate is actively pulling apart from the Arabian plate. The signature dive feature is La Faille (also called the Djibouti Crack), a submerged tectonic fissure that divers can swim through at approximately 8 metres depth — a shallow, accessible dive, though the surrounding channel currents can exceed 2 knots and require careful timing. The bay itself plunges to nearly 200 metres, hosting a mix of shallow coral reef patches and stark volcanic geological formations.

Ghoubbet Al-Kharab functions as a whale shark nursery: juvenile whale sharks gather between November and January to feed on dense plankton blooms driven by the powerful tidal exchange through the channel. The area supports approximately 200 species of coral along with sharks, rays, dolphins, and large schools of reef fish. The strong and unpredictable currents, depth extremes, and remote access mean this site is recommended for experienced divers only, despite the Crack itself being shallow.

**Note on site type:** Earlier records classified this as a wall dive at 25 metres. Research indicates no primary wall diving feature has been documented here; the site is characterised by tectonic reef features and the shallow Crack (8 m), making reef the more accurate classification. The 25-metre depth figure appeared unsupported — the Crack dive is at 8 m, and the channel drops to 200 m; no intermediate wall diving profile at 25 m was confirmed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gulf of Tadjoura / Bay of Ghoubbet, western Djibouti
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth (Crack dive)**: 8 metres (bay plunges to approximately 200 metres)
