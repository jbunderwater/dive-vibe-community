## Iles des Sept Frères

The Îles des Sept Frères (Seven Brothers Islands) are a remote volcanic archipelago in the Bab-el-Mandeb Strait offering Djibouti's most demanding and spectacular diving, accessible only by liveaboard.

## Overview

The archipelago — known also as the Sawabi Islands — consists of six volcanic islands strung in an east-west line across the southern entrance to the Red Sea, within the Obock sub-prefecture of Djibouti. Strong Indian Ocean currents sweeping through the strait support extraordinary concentrations of pelagic life. Dive-the-world.com describes the sites as featuring "colourful reef fish, schools of jacks and barracuda" on steep walls and drop-offs. Scuba Diver Life and Divezone confirm the islands offer some of the most biodiverse diving in the Red Sea, with manta rays, whale sharks, pilot whales, and reef sharks recorded.

A notable dive site in this area is the wreck of the Italian submarine Evangelista Torricelli, a Brin-class vessel (72 metres long, approximately 1,000 long tons surfaced displacement) that was scuttled in the Red Sea on 23 June 1940 after an artillery engagement with British destroyers HMS Kandahar, Kingston, and the sloop Shoreham. U.S. Navy casualty records and the navypedia.org database place the sinking at coordinates 12°34'N, 43°16'E — within the Bab-el-Mandeb area near the Seven Brothers Islands. The submarine sits upright at approximately 25 metres and is surrounded by white gorgonians, according to ZuBlu and Divezone diving sources. **Note on length:** Some diving sources describe this wreck as "120 metres long," which conflicts with the Brin-class design specification of 72.47 metres. The larger figure may reflect an error or confusion with another vessel; 72 metres is supported by naval historical records.

A separate wreck, La Dame Blanche, is located north-east of Ras Siyan near the Seven Brothers area. It is an unidentified cargo vessel (approximately 130 metres) lying at 25–30 metres and densely covered with white gorgonians — which gives it its name "White Lady." Sources (Divingaway, Scuba Diver Life) note it remains unidentified, so no sinking date or vessel history is included here.

Best diving season is reported as February through May when currents are more predictable.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bab-el-Mandeb Strait, northern Djibouti, Obock sub-prefecture
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
