## Iles des Sept Frères

Iles des Sept Frères is a wall dive site in Djibouti, Africa.

## Overview

The Îles des Sept Frères (Seven Brothers Islands) is an archipelago of seven volcanic islands in the Bab-el-Mandeb Strait at the mouth of the Red Sea, accessible only by liveaboard and now a designated Marine Protected Area. The strong Indian Ocean currents sweeping the islands draw extraordinary concentrations of marine life — pilot whales, beaked whales, manta rays, dolphin pods, nurse and blacktip sharks, and massive schools of jacks and stingrays — while the relatively untouched hard and soft coral reefs and dramatic drop-offs make this the most spectacular diving in Djibouti. A WWII-era wreck draped in spectacular white gorgonians lies at 30–35 metres between the islands, and the best diving season runs February through May.
