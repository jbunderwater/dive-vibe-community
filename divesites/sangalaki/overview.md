## Sangalaki (Derawan Islands)

Indonesia's manta ray capital off East Kalimantan, where resident mantas patrol Sangalaki's reefs, stingless jellyfish fill Kakaban's ancient lake, and Maratua atoll delivers big fish drift dives.

## Description

The Derawan Archipelago comprises 31 islands off East Kalimantan (Indonesian Borneo), with Sangalaki, Kakaban, Maratua, and Derawan as the primary dive islands. The database documents 13 dive sites with depths from 15 to 65 meters. The archipelago ranks second only to Raja Ampat for coral diversity in Indonesia, with over 460 coral species and 870 fish species documented. Sangalaki island is the focal point for year-round manta ray encounters and is one of the largest green sea turtle nesting sites in the Celebes Sea.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Reef Diving**: 7 sites including Manta Run, Manta Avenue, Manta Parade, Coral Gardens, Turtle Town, Turtle Traffic, and North Sangalou, many featuring manta ray cleaning stations and turtle encounters
- **Wall Diving**: 3 sites (Barracuda Point, Gorgonzola, Kelapa Dua) with dramatic drop-offs, gorgonian sea fans, and strong currents attracting large pelagics
- **Drift Diving**: 1 site (Big Fish Country) off Maratua atoll with grey reef sharks, eagle rays, and schooling barracuda in strong currents
- **Cave Diving**: 1 site (Blue Light Cave) at 65 meters depth off Kakaban, for experienced technical divers only
- **Shore Diving**: 1 site (Kakaban Jellyfish Lake) for the unique experience of swimming among thousands of stingless jellyfish in a landlocked saltwater lake

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Fly to Kalimarau Airport (BEJ) in Berau from Jakarta or Balikpapan (BPN). From Berau, travel by road to Tanjung Batu, then a 2-hour speedboat ride reaches Derawan Island. Sangalaki and other islands are accessed by boat from Derawan. Some liveaboard itineraries include the Derawan archipelago.
- **Dive Operators**: Scuba Junkie Sangalaki operates from Sangalaki island and is the primary dedicated dive operator. Green Nirvana Resort and Maratua Seaview Resort offer diving from their respective islands. Liveaboard vessels including White Manta, Katharina, and Arenui visit on seasonal itineraries.
- **Accommodation**: Scuba Junkie Sangalaki offers on-island accommodation at Sangalaki. Derawan Island has several guesthouses and small resorts. Maratua has a handful of resorts including Maratua Seaview Resort. Accommodation options are limited and should be booked well in advance.
- **Transportation**: Speedboats connect the islands, with typical transit times of 30-60 minutes between major dive areas. Resort operators arrange boat transfers.
- **Facilities**: Limited infrastructure across all islands. Berau on the mainland is the nearest town with hospital facilities. No hyperbaric chamber in the archipelago; the nearest is in Balikpapan or Makassar. Dive insurance with evacuation coverage is strongly recommended.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature ranges from 27-29C year-round, with occasional cooler upwellings to 23C in channels. Visibility varies by site, with 15-30 meters typical. Currents range from gentle at manta cleaning stations to strong at wall and drift sites like Big Fish Country and Barracuda Point.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Manta rays are the headline species, with resident populations found year-round at Sangalaki's reef sites (best December-March during plankton-rich rainy season). Over 870 fish species documented. Green and hawksbill sea turtles nest on Sangalaki and are encountered on virtually every dive. Kakaban's landlocked jellyfish lake contains four species of stingless jellyfish that evolved over millennia without predators. Wall and drift sites produce encounters with grey reef sharks, leopard sharks, occasional hammerheads, eagle rays, schooling barracuda, giant trevally, and pygmy seahorses.
- **Conservation**: Sangalaki is a designated marine protected area and critical turtle nesting habitat. Kakaban's jellyfish lake is a protected natural monument. The Berau Marine Protected Area covers significant portions of the archipelago. Turtle egg collection has been restricted to support population recovery.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: March to October for the calmest seas. December to March brings plankton-rich waters that attract the highest concentrations of manta rays. Year-round diving is possible, though November to February can bring rougher seas and reduced visibility.
- **Currency**: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
- **Language**: Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia) and Berau Malay locally
- **Safety**: Manta sites are generally suitable for all levels, but wall and drift sites like Barracuda Point and Big Fish Country require advanced skills due to strong currents and depth. Blue Light Cave at 65 meters is strictly for technical divers. The remote island location means long evacuation times; the nearest hyperbaric chamber requires a boat-and-flight journey to Balikpapan.
