## транспортный рефриджератор "Петрозаводск"

The Petrozavodsk refrigerator transport is a modern shipwreck dive site at Bear Island (Bjørnøya), Svalbard.

## Overview

The MS Petrozavodsk was a Russian refrigerated cargo vessel (reefer ship) that wrecked on the southern tip of Bear Island (Bjørnøya), Norway, on 11 May 2009. She was built in 1978 by Skala Skipasmidja in the Faroe Islands under the name Nordlandia, and later renamed Zapolyarye (2003) and Petrozavodsk (2004). The vessel measured 67 metres in length with a beam of 12 metres and a gross tonnage of 1,130 GT. She carried a crew of 12, all of whom were successfully rescued after the ship ran aground on rocks on the southern tip of Bear Island while operating in the Barents Sea. The vessel sustained deep gashes in her port side from the rocks. The captain was subsequently charged with being intoxicated while on duty.

The wreck remained on site at Bear Island because salvage was deemed too dangerous due to rockfalls from surrounding cliffs. By 2011, the vessel had split into two parts. Environmental concerns persisted given the ship's cargo of diesel fuel, lubrication oil, and hydraulic oil adjacent to Bear Island's internationally significant seabird sanctuary. The wreck has gradually disintegrated in the Arctic environment.

The coordinates place this wreck near Bear Island (Bjørnøya), which lies between Svalbard and mainland Norway at approximately 74°N. Diving here is extreme: Arctic water temperatures, potential sea ice, remote location, and deep decompression obligations make this accessible only to highly experienced technical or polar dive expeditions. The site has not been documented as a recreational dive destination by established polar dive operators.

## Wreck History

- **Built**: 1978 by Skala Skipasmidja, Faroe Islands; original name Nordlandia
- **Name history**: Nordlandia → Zapolyarye (2003) → Petrozavodsk (2004)
- **Type**: Refrigerated cargo vessel (reefer ship)
- **Dimensions**: 67 m length, 12 m beam, 1,130 GT gross tonnage
- **Wrecked**: 11 May 2009, southern tip of Bear Island (Bjørnøya)
- **Cause**: Ran aground on rocks; crew rescued, all 12 survived
- **Current state**: Split into two sections; disintegrating in situ

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat (extremely remote; Bear Island has no permanent dive operations)
- **Maximum depth**: ~25 m
- **Water temperature**: Below 0°C to ~4°C year-round
- **Conditions**: Arctic; sea ice possible; extreme remoteness; rescue response near-impossible
- **Required experience**: Technical/polar diving experience only

## Safety

This site is in one of the world's most hostile diving environments. Bear Island has no permanent human infrastructure for diving support. Sea ice, sub-zero water temperatures, extreme remoteness, and the physical condition of the disintegrating wreck make this site unsuitable for recreational diving. Any dive operation here would require full expedition logistics including surface support vessel, decompression chamber, and polar diving experience. This entry is documented for completeness; the site is not recommended for recreational divers.
