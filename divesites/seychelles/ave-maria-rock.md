## Ave Maria Rock

Ave Maria Rock is a tiny rocky island in the channel between Praslin and the Sister Islands, ringed by granite boulders forming canyons and crevices that support abundant reef life and calm conditions suitable for beginner divers.

## Overview

Ave Maria sits in the channel between Praslin Island and the Sister Islands (Île aux Récifs area), accessible by short boat ride. Wannadive records the site at coordinates 4° 19' S, 55° 49' E with a maximum depth of 20 metres and average depth of 18 metres. The site is classified as Open Water / CMAS 1 difficulty, confirming its suitability for less experienced divers.

The underwater terrain consists of massive granite boulders creating canyons, crevices, and overhangs blanketed in soft coral. Species confirmed by multiple sources include reef sharks, stingrays, batfish, lobsters, octopus, barracuda, and sea turtles. Fusiliers and snappers school through the passages between the boulders. The current at Ave Maria is generally mild, making it a reliable all-conditions site and a popular pick for guided beginner dives from Praslin. Wannadive describes the site as "easy to find" by boat, with good visibility of 10–30 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Channel between Praslin and Sister Islands, Seychelles
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef / granite boulder
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
