## Marianne Island

South Marianne is a challenging dive off the southern tip of Marianne Island, reached by a 30-minute boat journey from Praslin or La Digue, known for strong currents, granite pinnacles, and large aggregations of pelagic fish.

## Overview

The southern tip of Marianne Island — accessible from Praslin or La Digue — is considered one of the finest dive sites in the Seychelles Inner Islands. The topography features granite rock formations, caves, boulders, and pinnacles descending to around 25 metres. Currents running past the island are described as occasionally strong at up to 2 knots, which brings in schooling jacks, yellowfin tuna, eagle rays, and grey reef sharks hunting in the current. Barracuda, giant moray eels, and hawksbill turtles are regular sightings, and the diversity and quantity of fish life is frequently highlighted by divers who visit the site. Seyvillas describes the conditions as "challenging," with potentially rough seas on the boat ride out. This site is best suited to experienced divers comfortable with current and exposed open-water conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South tip of Marianne Island, Seychelles Inner Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
