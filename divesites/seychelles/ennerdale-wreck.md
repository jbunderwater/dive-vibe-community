## Ennerdale Wreck

The Ennerdale is the largest shipwreck in the Seychelles, a Royal Fleet Auxiliary tanker that struck an uncharted granite pinnacle off Mahé in June 1970 and now rests in around 30 metres of water northeast of Victoria.

## Overview

RFA Ennerdale (A213) was a Dale-class mobile reserve tanker originally named *Naess Scotsman*, acquired by the British government in 1968. On 1 June 1970, while departing Mahé after a crew rest period, she struck an uncharted granite pinnacle off the northeast coast of the island and began taking on water rapidly, sinking within approximately 30 minutes. Her full load of oil created an environmental emergency; the subsequent clean-up operation involved placing torpedo warheads on the hull by helicopter to rupture the oil tanks, allowing the South-East Monsoon to disperse the spill away from Mahé. Six of the ten warheads were successfully detonated on 22 July 1970.

The vessel's exact length is cited differently across sources — the Dale-class specification of 710 feet equates to approximately 216–220 metres depending on rounding, and she is consistently described as well over 200 metres long, making her by far the largest wreck in the Seychelles. The destruction by explosives scattered much of the hull, and the wreck now lies in sections at 20–30 metres depth. The stern section remains the most intact and impressive part of the site, and the propeller and prop shaft are recognisable. Interior penetration is not recommended without appropriate wreck training and equipment.

Marine life at the site includes giant groupers, moray eels, and dense schools of fusiliers and barracuda. Eagle rays and large pelagics pass regularly over the scattered hull plates. Best diving conditions are October to May.
