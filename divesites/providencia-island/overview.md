## Providencia Island

Colombia's remote Caribbean gem surrounded by the world's third-largest barrier reef, offering pristine walls, exceptional 25-30 meter visibility, and abundant reef sharks in a UNESCO Seaflower Biosphere Reserve.

## Description

Providencia Island is a volcanic island in Colombia's San Andres archipelago, widely regarded as the top dive destination in Colombia. The island is protected by a 32-kilometer (20-mile) barrier reef -- the third-largest in the world -- within the UNESCO Seaflower Biosphere Reserve. There are documented dive sites ranging from 12 to 52 meters depth, all within a 15-minute boat ride from shore. The diving features dramatic wall drops, pristine coral reefs, a wreck site, and a cave with spiral tunnel, with consistently warm water and visibility between 25 and 30 meters (80-100 feet).

### Diving Opportunities

- **Wall Diving**: Wall sites including the deep Stairway to Heaven (52m), Nick's Place (40m), The Pinnacle (35m), and Felipe's Place (24m) — Providencia's walls are its signature attraction
- **Reef Diving**: Reef sites including Manta's City (20m), Convento Reef (15m), Crab Cay Reef (12m, beginner-friendly), and Tete's Place (9m, Providencia's "fishiest" site) with patch reefs, pillar coral, and large fish schools
- **Wreck Diving**: Blue Diamond wreck at approximately 14 metres, accessible to Open Water divers
- **Cave Diving**: Table Rock features interconnected caves at 2–8 metres with nurse sharks and silversides; suitable for snorkelers and all diver levels
- **Boat Diving**: All sites are boat-access, with short rides from the island's dive centers

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Providencia is reached by a 25-minute flight from San Andres Island on the regional airline Satena. San Andres receives flights from Bogota (2 hours) via Avianca, and some international connections. There is no direct international service to Providencia
- **Dive Operators**: Felipe Diving Center (coordinates accommodations with El Encanto Cabins), Sonny Dive Shop, Sirius Dive Center, and Anda Di Wata dive school on Santa Catalina islet
- **Accommodation**: Small guesthouses, posadas (local homestays), and boutique hotels. The island has limited large-scale tourism, preserving its quiet character. Felipe Diving Center partners with El Encanto Cabins for dive-and-stay packages
- **Transportation**: The island is small enough to explore by scooter, golf cart, or on foot. Taxis and moto-taxis are available. A pedestrian bridge connects Providencia to tiny Santa Catalina island
- **Facilities**: Small shops, restaurants serving Caribbean and Colombian cuisine, a few ATMs (bring backup cash), and a basic medical clinic

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 27-29°C (81-84°F) year-round. Visibility consistently 25-30 meters (80-100 feet). Currents are generally mild, though deeper wall sites can have moderate currents
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Caribbean reef sharks are abundant and a signature sighting. Also hawksbill and green sea turtles, spotted eagle rays, southern stingrays, nurse sharks, large grouper, barracuda, schools of grunts, squirrelfish, and snappers. Healthy hard and soft corals, barrel sponges, and pillar coral formations
- **Conservation**: Part of the UNESCO Seaflower Biosphere Reserve, the largest marine protected area in the Caribbean. Old Providence McBean Lagoon National Natural Park protects nearshore mangroves and coral ecosystems on the island's east coast

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: Year-round diving, with the driest months from January to April and a secondary dry spell in July. Hurricane season (June-November) rarely affects Providencia directly but can reduce visibility
- **Currency**: Colombian Peso (COP). US Dollars occasionally accepted but not reliable; bring pesos
- **Language**: English Creole is the native language of the Raizal islanders; Spanish is the official language. Basic English is understood in tourist areas
- **Safety**: Most sites are suitable for intermediate divers and above. The deep walls at Nick's Place and Stairway to Heaven require Advanced certification. The island is remote with limited medical facilities; the nearest hyperbaric chamber is in San Andres
