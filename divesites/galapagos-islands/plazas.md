# Plazas

A wall dive off South Plaza Island where a resident sea lion colony dives in to play and eagle rays cruise along the drop-off, just a short boat ride from Santa Cruz.

## Overview

Plazas is a wall dive site off the southern shore of South Plaza Island, one of the small islets east of Santa Cruz. A wall drops from a shallow reef top to depths of 15-18 metres, with an eel garden on the sandy bottom and good pelagic species in the channel. The nearby South Plaza sea lion colony sends its members into the water constantly, creating reliable and often extended interactions with divers. Its relative proximity to Puerto Ayora on Santa Cruz makes it one of the more accessible dive sites in the Galapagos, though currents require proper experience.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off South Plaza Island, east of Santa Cruz, Galapagos Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-18 meters (30-60 feet)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate (generally calmer than exposed Galapagos sites)
- **Best Time**: Year-round; best visibility June to November

## Marine Life

Galapagos sea lions from the South Plaza colony are the highlight, launching off the rocky shore and descending to interact with divers along the wall. Spotted eagle rays are regularly seen cruising along the wall edge, sometimes in groups. Garden eels populate the sandy bottom. Bigger pelagic species including rays, sharks, and green turtles are present in the channel. Schools of reef fish -- king angelfish, surgeonfish, parrotfish, and damselfish -- populate the wall face. Whitetip reef sharks rest in crevices. Moray eels peer from holes.

## Dive Profile

The dive begins with a descent along the wall face from the shallow reef top at 3-5 metres down to the sandy bottom at 15-18 metres. Divers drift along the wall with the mild current. The best sea lion encounters usually happen during the ascent as divers return to the shallower reef top. Safety stop is performed in generally calm conditions on the reef top.

## Entry and Exit

Day boat from Puerto Ayora, Santa Cruz, approximately 30-45 minutes transit. Giant stride entry near the wall edge. The dive follows the wall, with the boat repositioning to meet divers at the exit point. Exit via ladder onto the day boat, or panga pickup if current has carried divers further than expected. SMB deployment is recommended regardless.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Spend time on the reef top during your safety stop -- sea lions are most playful in the shallows
- Look along the wall edge at mid-depth for eagle rays, which tend to cruise at 10-15 metres
- Morning departures from Santa Cruz offer the best conditions and light on the south-facing wall
- Check wall crevices carefully for resting whitetip sharks
- This is a good site for divers who want Galapagos marine life encounters without the extreme currents of Gordon Rocks or Wolf

## Safety Considerations

Conditions are generally more forgiving than at the Galapagos' more exposed sites, but currents can still be present and may strengthen without warning. The wall drops beyond recreational limits, so depth awareness is important. Sea lions can be playful to the point of bumping equipment or pulling on hoses -- secure loose gear. Standard Galapagos thermal protection (5-7mm wetsuit) is required. Carry an SMB and signaling devices.

## Photography

Sea lions playing along the wall provide outstanding wide-angle subjects -- shoot upward to silhouette them against the surface light. Eagle rays along the wall edge are best captured with a wide-angle lens from below. Sea turtles resting on ledges are patient portrait subjects. The relatively mild current makes this a more comfortable site for photography than many alternatives. Morning light on the south-facing wall provides good illumination.
