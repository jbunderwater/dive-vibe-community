## Gordon Rocks

Gordon Rocks is an extinct tuff cone roughly one hour east of Puerto Ayora, Santa Cruz. Three rocks break the surface above a submerged volcanic crater — the site has earned the nickname "The Washing Machine" for the powerful upwellings and swirling currents generated by its unusual topography.

## Overview

The outer walls of the tuff cone drop approximately 60 meters. Most diving takes place inside the crater, where three pinnacles rise from a sandy bottom at 30 meters. The shallowest pinnacle sits 5–6 meters below the surface; the central pinnacle descends to around 21 meters. Strong currents funnel through the gaps between pinnacles, concentrating plankton and drawing large pelagics from surrounding waters. Gordon Rocks is consistently ranked among the world's best sites for schooling scalloped hammerhead sharks.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Santa Cruz, Galápagos Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–30 meters; can drop lower during plankton blooms
- **Water Temperature**: 16–20°C (June–December); 20–26°C (January–May)
- **Current**: Strong to very strong; strong upwellings and downwellings on the northeast tip
- **Best Time**: Year-round for hammerheads; June–November for manta rays and mola mola

## Marine Life

Gordon Rocks is best known for large resident schools of scalloped hammerhead sharks that use the pinnacles as cleaning stations, attended by butterflyfish and bannerfish. Galápagos sharks and whitetip reef sharks are also common. During the cold season (June–November), manta rays and the endemic Mola alexandrini (southern sunfish) make regular appearances in the blue water beyond the pinnacles. Green sea turtles rest on the sandy bottom. Eagle rays, spotted eagle rays, and mobula rays pass through. Additional residents include Galápagos sea lions, barracuda, moray eels, and octopus.

## Dive Profile

Entry is by giant stride from a pangas (inflatable dinghy). Descend quickly to the pinnacles; conditions on the surface often differ markedly from below. Divers typically hold position on the pinnacle tops or in the saddle between pinnacles to observe hammerheads drifting along on the current. Down-currents can occur on the northeast tip — be prepared to abort and ascend if conditions deteriorate. The best hammerhead encounters are usually at 18–25 meters. Always carry and deploy an SMB for pickup.

## Safety Considerations

Gordon Rocks demands solid buoyancy control and current-diving experience. Currents shift unpredictably; a reef hook is recommended. Down-currents on the northeast tip can be sudden. Visibility of less than 10 meters is possible in plankton-rich water. A 7 mm wetsuit or semi-dry is appropriate year-round; temperatures can drop below 18°C inside thermoclines. This site is not suitable for divers who are not comfortable with surge and disorientation in open water.

## Photography

Gordon Rocks is a classic wide-angle site. Frame hammerheads against the blue water or the silhouette of the pinnacle. Schools of hundreds of hammerheads are possible in the cold season. The sandy crater floor offers macro opportunities — octopus, moray eels, and sea turtle resting shots when currents permit.
