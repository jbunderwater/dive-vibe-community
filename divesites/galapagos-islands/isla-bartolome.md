# Isla Bartolome

A sandy and rocky reef dive near Santiago Island, featuring a large platform at 10 meters, a drift circuit around a volcanic pinnacle, and regular sea lion interactions.

## Overview

Isla Bartolome is located near Santiago Island and is famous above the surface for its iconic Pinnacle Rock. Below the surface, the dive site centres on a large sandy and rocky platform at approximately 10 meters, with terraced steps descending to around 27 meters and cliff edges that attract pelagic species. The dive is typically conducted as a circuit around the Tower — a volcanic pinnacle rising close to the surface — in the path of the current. Multiple sources describe the site as accessible to Open Water certified divers with moderate currents and sheltered conditions. Galapagos penguins nest at the base of Pinnacle Rock and are observed from the boat after diving, not typically encountered during the underwater dive itself.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bartolome Island, near Santiago Island, Galapagos Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-18 meters (30-60 feet)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Whitetip reef sharks are the most reliably seen large species, resting on sandy ledges and in rock crevices. Green sea turtles are common throughout the dive. Spotted eagle rays, golden rays, mobula rays, stingrays, and marble rays have all been confirmed at this site. Schools of barracuda hang above the reef. Pacific seahorses inhabit the black coral steps along the terraced walls. Galapagos sharks patrol the deeper cliff edges. Manta rays appear seasonally. Sea lions from the island colony are playful and frequently join divers. Moray eels and octopus occupy the rocky formations. Stone scorpionfish, grunts, snappers, groupers, hogfish, jacks, and parrotfish complete the community. Galapagos penguins nest at Pinnacle Rock and are sometimes seen swimming from the surface, though encounters during the underwater dive are incidental.

## Dive Profile

The main dive takes place on a broad sandy and rocky platform at approximately 10 meters depth, with terraced steps descending toward 27 meters. The dive typically circuits the Tower — a volcanic pinnacle that rises near the surface — in the direction of the current. Cliff edges beginning around 15 meters provide observation points for pelagic species. Most of the interesting marine life concentrates on the shallower platform zone at 9-15 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Panga from liveaboard or day boat. Giant stride entry into sheltered water. The drift circuit follows the current around the Tower. Exit via panga pickup. Conditions are generally calm in the lee of the island, making this one of the more accessible Galapagos sites.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The combination with Cousins Rock is the standard day-trip pairing from Santa Cruz — both sites in one day is typical
- Seahorses are found on the black coral terraced steps — ask your guide where the current residents are
- After the dive, the boat approaches Pinnacle Rock for a surface look at Galapagos penguins in their nesting area
- Look carefully under rocky ledges at the base of the Tower for resting whitetip sharks

## Safety Considerations

One of the calmer Galapagos dive sites. Currents can increase during tidal changes. The volcanic rock formations have sharp edges — maintain buoyancy control. Standard Galapagos thermal protection (5-7mm wetsuit, water 18-23°C) and safety equipment apply.

## Photography

The platform at 10 meters offers good natural light for wide-angle reef photography. Seahorses on black coral steps reward macro shooters who search carefully. Sea lions make willing wide-angle subjects. The terraced steps create layered compositional opportunities. Bring both a macro and wide-angle setup if possible.
