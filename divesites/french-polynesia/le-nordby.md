## Le Nordby

Le Nordby is the most historically significant wreck dive in the Society Islands, a three-masted Danish sailing schooner approximately 60 metres long and 8 metres wide that ran aground on the Raiatea barrier reef on 22 August 1900 and settled on the lagoon floor at 20–29 metres depth.

## Wreck History

The *Nordby* was purchased in Le Havre in 1890 for 90,000 crowns by Danish shipowner P.N. Winther, who renamed it after his home village of Nordby on the island of Fanø, Denmark. The vessel departed Hamburg on 31 August 1899 for the South Seas and New Zealand. In May 1900 it completed its New Zealand voyage and was making for Liverpool via Tahiti and Raiatea to load copra. Captain Christiansen requested a pilot to navigate the pass, but a strong Mara'amu wind caused the anchor to slip from too-short scope, and the *Nordby* ran aground on the rocky drop-off opposite Teavapiti Pass. The ship sank to around 20 metres depth. The wreck's identity remained unknown until December 1996, when a local journalist discovered its name in the archives of the Raiatea notary.

## Overview

The *Nordby* lies on its side in the Raiatea lagoon. The wooden deck planking has completely disappeared over the course of 125 years, allowing divers to enter the open hull and explore the interior holds directly. Two of the original three masts remain, creating the distinctive silhouette visible from the surface in calm conditions. The hull and rigging are encrusted with hard and soft corals, sponges, and tunicates. Lagoon visibility typically reaches 25–35 metres in the calm water above the wreck, though proximity to a nearby river mouth can reduce clarity during and after heavy rain. Water temperatures average 27–28°C. The site is accessible to Level 1 (Open Water equivalent) certified divers.

## What to Expect

Surgeon fish, jacks, angelfish, and nudibranchs are the most consistently documented species at this site. Schools of jacks circle the mast stumps. The exposed interior of the holds — opened by the collapse of the wooden deck — is easily entered through multiple openings. Ghost pipefish and frogfish have been observed among the encrusting growth, and moray eels occupy crevices in the hull plating. The site is operated by Hemisphere Sub in Raiatea, which lists it as a Level 1 dive (depth 0–29 m). Night diving at the *Nordby* is available through local operators for enhanced invertebrate activity.

## Conditions

- **Best season**: April–November; year-round, but rain can reduce visibility
- **Visibility**: 25–35 m typical; may drop after heavy rainfall due to river runoff nearby
- **Current**: Generally mild in lagoon setting
- **Water temperature**: 27–28°C
- **Entry**: Boat dive from Raiatea operators (Hemisphere Sub and others)

## Tips

A torch is useful for the interior holds and to illuminate the colour of encrusting sponges. Wide-angle photography of the mast profile is best achieved from 5–8 metres off the hull. Night diving reveals nocturnal invertebrate activity and is offered by local operators. Bring a wetsuit — the lagoon can feel cool at depth on extended dives.
