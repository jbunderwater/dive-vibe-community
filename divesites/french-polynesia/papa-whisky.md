## Papa Whisky

Papa Whisky takes its name from the Papeete West (PW) aviation beacon — NATO phonetic "Papa Whisky" — located on an artificial islet in the sea opposite Faa'a airport. The underwater topography is an amphitheater formed by the partial collapse of an underwater plateau, creating two distinct exploration circuits (north and south) and a depth range from approximately 9 metres to 45 metres.

## Overview

The amphitheater shape — formed by the collapse of a formerly continuous plateau — creates a dramatic bowl of vertical walls and ledges accessible as two separate dives, one from the north and one from the south. The north side features a dense population of gorgonian sea fans interspersed with brightly coloured violet coral. The south side is a fault face densely covered in brilliant yellow gorgonian coral formations, most impressive around 40 metres depth. The site is set away from the main passes, producing calmer conditions than pass dives while still receiving regular pelagic visitors. The deeper sections (35–45 m) are advanced territory requiring appropriate certification and gas management.

## What to Expect

The defining feature is the gorgonian coral coverage — yellow and purple specimens of impressive size are documented on both the north and south circuits, with the best concentrations between 35 and 45 metres. Grey reef sharks and lemon sharks are present; eagle rays and sea turtles have been confirmed at the site. Butterflyfish and groupers are consistently observed at shallower depths. The amphitheater walls at 15–30 m hold diverse reef fish communities. Schools of tuna pass through unpredictably on some dives. Advanced divers willing to push to 40 m will find some of the most spectacular gorgonian sea fans accessible in the Tahiti area.

## Conditions

- **Best season**: April–November
- **Visibility**: 20–35 m typical
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; sheltered from main pass flows
- **Water temperature**: 27–28°C upper sections, slightly cooler at 40–45 m
- **Entry**: Boat dive from Papeete and Punaauia operators

## Tips

This site requires Advanced Open Water certification as a minimum due to its depth. Plan two separate dives to cover the north and south amphitheater circuits fully. Keep gas reserves conservative for ascent from the deeper gorgonian sections (35–45 m). A dive computer with a depth alarm is strongly advised. Despite its proximity to the airport, the underwater environment is surprisingly pristine.
