## Tao-tao

Tao-tao is a reef dive site in the northern Taha'a lagoon, French Polynesia. The name is Tahitian and likely refers to a local coral or geographic feature of the reef at this location.

## Overview

Located on the north coast of Taha'a, Tao-tao is notable for exceptional clarity — its distance from the main passes means the water reaches 40–50 metres visibility, among the clearest in the island group. The site sits within a still-wild reef environment with relatively little fishing pressure, producing a density and tameness of fish life that distinguishes it from more frequently dived sites. The reef is described by Hemisphere Sub as particularly beautiful coral. The depth range is 0–50 metres, offering profiles for all levels. Water temperatures are a comfortable 27–28°C year-round.

## What to Expect

Napoleon wrasses and lemon sharks are among the signature species documented at this site. Large groupers (perches) are also confirmed, along with schools of barracudas. The unusually clear water and wild character of the reef contribute to abundant, less-wary fish behaviour than at more commonly dived sites. The peaceful, unhurried quality of Taha'a diving — fewer boats, smaller groups, and a genuine sense of remoteness — makes Tao-tao a particularly enjoyable site for divers wanting to explore at their own pace.

## Conditions

- **Best season**: April–November; calm year-round in the lagoon
- **Visibility**: 25–30 m typical
- **Current**: Mild
- **Water temperature**: 27–28°C
- **Entry**: Boat dive from Taha'a operators or from liveaboards

## Tips

Taha'a's limited dive infrastructure means smaller groups and more personalised dives. The island is best reached by boat from Raiatea or via liveaboard. Combining Tao-tao as a morning reef dive with an afternoon pass dive at Passe de Céran makes for an excellent contrast of dive styles in one day.
