## Mairie II

Mairie II is a shallow wreck dive site in the Tuamotu Archipelago, French Polynesia. The vessel — an inter-island supply ship approximately 50 metres wide at its resting position — sank in November 1989 and was then towed to its current location on the atoll floor, where it rests at 17 metres depth as a non-hazardous wreck and established artificial reef.

## Wreck History

The *Mairie II* sank at its dock during a strong westerly wind. Arriving at the quay on a day of severe west wind around 3pm, the vessel was violently pushed against the dock before unloading had barely begun. A hull breach caused the sinking, and the ship was subsequently towed to its current position on the sandy atoll floor several days later. The wreck has a 3-metre clearance at its shallowest point above the seafloor and maximum depth of 17 metres. The sinking date of November 1989 and these structural details are documented in the OpenStreetMap record derived from a local dive assessment. ## Overview

The *Mairie II* lies in the lagoon of a remote Tuamotu atoll, accessible primarily by liveaboard or inter-island flight. The sheltered atoll setting produces excellent visibility of 25–35 metres and calm conditions, with water temperatures averaging 27–28°C. The wreck is rated non-dangerous (seamark:wreck:category = non-dangerous) and is not visible at either high or low tide. Over 35+ years of submersion, the hull and superstructure have developed substantial coral and sponge growth. The remote Tuamotu environment means less diving pressure than equivalent wrecks near populated islands.

## What to Expect

The wreck's hull and deck structures are colonised by hard corals, tunicates, and encrusting sponges. The shallow 17-metre maximum depth provides generous bottom time for open-water certified divers. The remote setting — characteristic of Tuamotu atoll diving — offers an undisturbed marine environment. No site-specific marine life documentation beyond what could reasonably be inferred from the atoll setting was found in published sources.

## Conditions

- **Best season**: Year-round; April–November optimal for access
- **Visibility**: 25–35 m typical in atoll lagoon
- **Current**: Mild; atoll lagoon setting
- **Water temperature**: 27–28°C
- **Entry**: Boat dive; remote atoll — accessible by liveaboard or inter-island flight

## Tips

The Tuamotu Archipelago is most efficiently explored by liveaboard — land-based accommodation on most atolls is limited to basic guesthouses. A torch is useful for any interior sections. The remote setting means self-sufficiency in dive equipment is important; spare parts and fills are limited outside Rangiroa and Fakarava.
