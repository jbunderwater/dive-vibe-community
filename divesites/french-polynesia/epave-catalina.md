## Epave Catalina

Epave Catalina is a wreck dive site in the Tahiti lagoon, French Polynesia, featuring the remains of a Consolidated PBY Catalina flying boat — a WWII-era maritime patrol and amphibious aircraft resting on the lagoon floor near the end of Faa'a airport's runway. The site is part of a grouping locally known as "Les 3 Épaves" (The Three Wrecks), which also includes a 40-metre schooner and a sunken boat.

## Overview

The Catalina rests on the lagoon floor at approximately 20–25 metres depth, near the end of one of the Papeete airport runways inside the marked channel. The aircraft's fuselage, tail section, and wings are substantially intact, though the engines have separated from the airframe and lie on the seafloor beside the wreck. Machine guns and ammunition are reported to remain in place within the fuselage. The wreck can be entered via a hatch near the tail section, and divers can navigate through to the cockpit. Visibility in the lagoon here is generally good, and water temperatures average 27–28°C year-round.

Note: The circumstances of how this particular aircraft came to rest in the lagoon are not confirmed in published sources. The wreck exists and is well-documented as a dive site; its specific service history and sinking cause have not been independently verified and are therefore not stated here.

## What to Expect

The Catalina's fuselage interior is penetrable from the tail hatch, leading forward to the cockpit — a highlight for aviation-history-minded divers. The exterior is covered in soft corals, sponges, and tunicates. Parrotfish, blue-striped snappers, lionfish, and stonefish inhabit the structure and its immediate surroundings. Nudibranchs are documented at the site. The adjacent schooner wreck (8–22 metres) and a third sunken vessel offer additional structure on the same dive or as a second dive. Turtles and whitetip reef sharks are present in the broader lagoon area.

## Conditions

- **Best season**: Year-round; April–November for best visibility
- **Visibility**: 15–25 m typical in the lagoon
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Water temperature**: 27–28°C
- **Entry**: Boat dive, near Faa'a airport

## Tips

A torch is recommended for the fuselage and cockpit interior. The site is beginner-accessible at the shallower aircraft sections; divers venturing to the deeper third wreck (30 metres) should have an advanced certification. Operators in the Papeete area offer combination dives covering all three wrecks on a single trip.
