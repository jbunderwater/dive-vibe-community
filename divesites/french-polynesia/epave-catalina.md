## Epave Catalina

Epave Catalina is a wreck dive site in the Faa'a lagoon near Papeete airport, Tahiti, French Polynesia, featuring the submerged remains of a Consolidated PBY Catalina flying boat deliberately sunk in 1962 to create an artificial dive site.

## Overview

The PBY Catalina at Tahiti was a twin-engine amphibious patrol aircraft that operated between Tahiti and Bora Bora during WWII (when Bora Bora had only a single airstrip). The aircraft was deliberately scuttled in 1962 — not sunk accidentally — to create a dive attraction. The wreck rests at approximately 13–21 metres depth on a sandy slope in the Faa'a lagoon, marked by a buoy near Papeete Airport. The fuselage, wings, and tail section remain recognisable. Lagoon visibility averages 20–25 metres, and water temperature is 27–28°C year-round. The wreck is often dived as part of a combination trip with a nearby cargo schooner wreck a few hundred metres away.

## What to Expect

The fuselage and wings provide structure for corals and encrusting growth accumulated over six decades. The interior is accessible through hatches near the tail section and through the cockpit. Parrotfish, blue-striped snappers, lionfish, and stonefish inhabit the site. Whitetip reef sharks and turtles are occasionally encountered. The starboard wing extends to the deeper end of the site at around 21 m, while the main fuselage sits shallower. Buoyancy control is important in the confined fuselage sections.

## Conditions

- **Best season**: Year-round; May–October for best visibility
- **Visibility**: 20–25 m typical
- **Current**: Very mild in lagoon setting
- **Water temperature**: 27–28°C
- **Entry**: Boat dive; site marked by buoy

## Tips

A torch is useful for exploring the cockpit and fuselage interior. Operators in Papeete and Punaauia run this site as part of combination wreck dives including the adjacent cargo schooner. The site is accessible to Open Water-certified divers. Note: wreck penetration of the interior compartments requires appropriate training and equipment — confined spaces with overhead environment should not be entered without proper preparation.
