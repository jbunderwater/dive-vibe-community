## Plateau aux tortues

Plateau aux tortues — "Turtle Plateau" — is a reef dive site in the Tahiti lagoon, French Polynesia, named for the large green turtles that regularly rest and feed on its shallow coral plateau.

## Overview

The site is centred on a coral plateau at about 13 metres depth — its top rises to around 13 m from the surface — adjacent to Fishermen's Bay (Baie des Pêcheurs). The plateau is dotted with coral heads and sandy patches, with a drop-off descending to 50 metres at the edge, where whitetip reef sharks sometimes rest on the sandy bottom. The coral has suffered some sediment damage from a nearby river during the rainy season, but the site remains active with marine life. Water temperatures are 27–29°C year-round.

## What to Expect

Turtles are the headline attraction — the "fantastique tortues" described by TOPDIVE as typically 3–5 very friendly individuals are a reliable encounter. The drop-off edge at the plateau's perimeter descends to 50 metres and is patrolled by whitetip reef sharks, blacktip sharks, grey sharks, and — on occasion — leopard rays. Titan triggerfish and diverse tropical fish inhabit the plateau top. Lionfish and other reef fish find refuge in the coral structure. The shallower plateau top (13 m) is accessible to beginner divers and snorkellers; the drop-off to 50 m requires intermediate-to-advanced experience.

## Conditions

- **Best season**: Year-round; April–November for best visibility
- **Visibility**: 25–30 m typical
- **Current**: Generally very mild on the plateau
- **Water temperature**: 27–29°C
- **Entry**: Boat dive

## Tips

Approach resting turtles slowly and from the side — never from above or directly in front. Turtles regularly surface to breathe; hovering neutrally buoyant and watching them rise and descend is one of the memorable experiences at this site. Morning dives catch turtles actively feeding before they rest.
