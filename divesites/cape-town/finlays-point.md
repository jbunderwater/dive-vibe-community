## Finlay's Point

Finlay's Point (also known as Jenga Reef) is a popular shore-entry dive near Simon's Town on the False Bay coast, where a shallow rocky reef descends through kelp forest to around 15 metres, featuring Finlay's Pinnacle rising from 15m to 6m and reliable pyjama catshark sightings.

## Overview

Finlay's Point is a popular shore entry dive on the False Bay side of the Cape Peninsula, near Simon's Town. The site features kelp forest, swim-throughs, and a rocky reef descending gradually to about 15 metres, with the notable Finlay's Pinnacle rising from the sand at 15 metres up to 6 metres. The site is also known as Jenga Reef. Its sheltered position relative to open Atlantic sites makes it diveable on more days of the year, and a reliable option when the western seaboard is too rough.

Bryozoans, sponges, colonial ascidians, and anemones encrust the reef surfaces. Pyjama catsharks are a reliable sighting at this site. Reef fish species typical of False Bay inhabit the rocky margins.

*Note: broadnose sevengill sharks and "large seven-gill cow sharks" were listed in a previous version. Per Dive Team Cape Town, sevengill sharks have been elusive in the False Bay area since 2016. Sevengill sharks have been removed as a reliable sighting at Finlay's Point.*

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Simon's Town, False Bay, Cape Town
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 10–18°C — 5mm wetsuit minimum
- **Conditions**: Sheltered; diveable in more conditions than Atlantic sites

## Marine Life

Pyjama catsharks, bryozoans, sponges, colonial ascidians, anemones, and reef fish are documented at this site. No additional specific species are confirmed by reviewed sources.
