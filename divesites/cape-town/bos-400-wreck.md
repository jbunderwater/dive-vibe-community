## Bos 400 Wreck

Bos 400 Wreck is a shore-accessible wreck at Sandy Bay near Hout Bay — the remains of a massive French crane barge that ran aground off Duiker Point on 26 June 1994 while under tow, now at an advanced stage of deterioration with collapsed steel hazardous to divers.

## Overview

The BOS 400 was a French derrick/lay barge built in 1984, measuring 100 metres in length and 12,000 long tons displacement, and capable of lifting 1,200 tons with its crane — one of the most powerful crane barges in the world at the time. On 26 June 1994, the Russian tugboat Tigr was towing the BOS 400 from Pointe-Noire in the Republic of Congo to Cape Town when the tow cable broke in a force-9 northwesterly gale. All 14 crew aboard were airlifted to safety, but the barge drove aground off Duiker Point near Sandy Bay on the Cape Peninsula.

The BOS 400 ran aground at the same location as an earlier wreck, the SS Oakburn. Despite salvage attempts, the barge was considered a total loss. The wreck remains partially above the waterline — the crane base and sections of steel superstructure are visible from the beach at Sandy Bay. The submerged sections lie in 8–20 metres of cold, turbulent Atlantic water. Cape Town Maritime Safety Authority has issued warnings about the site: the wreck is in an advanced state of deterioration, with rusted steel threatening to collapse and fallen steel dispersed across the surrounding seabed, making the wreck and surrounding water unsafe. Divers are advised to maintain a safe distance from the structure rather than entering it.

Shore access is via Sandy Bay beach. The site is exposed to Atlantic swell and should only be visited on genuinely calm days.

*Note: a previous version of this description claimed the wreck overlies the SS Oakburn and that divers could find "railway lines and antique glassware" on the Oakburn remains. No independent source was found confirming the Oakburn's specific cargo. That claim has been removed pending source confirmation.*

## Site Information

- **Location**: Duiker Point, Sandy Bay, Cape Peninsula, Cape Town
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry (Sandy Bay beach)
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive (partially submerged barge)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Depth Range**: 8–20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 8–14°C cold Atlantic — thick wetsuit or drysuit required
- **Conditions**: Exposed to Atlantic swell; strong surge in shallows; calm days only
- **Warning**: Unstable structure; do not penetrate or approach collapsing sections

## Marine Life

Regional marine life includes cape rock lobster, octopus, and cold-water reef fish typical of the Atlantic seaboard kelp zone; no site-specific species reports were found for this wreck beyond general encrusting invertebrate communities.

## Safety

The NSRI has issued safety alerts at this site following diving injuries (August 2021). The collapsed steel and debris field are the primary hazard. Treat this as an external inspection dive only — do not attempt to penetrate unstable wreck structure.
