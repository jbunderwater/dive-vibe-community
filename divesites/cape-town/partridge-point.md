## Partridge Point

Partridge Point is a boulder-reef dive near Miller's Point on the False Bay coast, widely regarded as second only to Castle Rocks among Cape Peninsula sites for fish variety and abundance, with resident Cape fur seals and a diverse granite reef structure extending to 26 metres.

## Overview

Partridge Point encompasses several distinct reef features — Big Rock, Seal Rock, Deep Partridge, Peter's Pinnacles, and the Gorgonian Forest — all within the southern Castle Rocks restricted zone of the Table Mountain National Park Marine Protected Area. The reef is a chaotic jumble of large granite boulders and outcrops, some breaking the surface, others stacking into gullies, overhangs, and swim-throughs. This is not a wall dive; the topography is multi-level boulder reef from the surface down to sand at around 26 metres, with productive zones concentrated between 5–15 metres.

Fish diversity is exceptional for a cold-water temperate site: large shoals of Hottentot sea bream and Fransmadam fill the water column, while Roman, Two-tone Fingerfin, Galjoen, John Brown, and klipfish work the reef margins. The Gorgonian Forest section is named for its dense coverage of large gorgonian sea fans that persist in the colder, nutrient-rich water. Cape fur seals from the colony at Seal Rock are frequent and enthusiastic companions — peeling off to dart through the kelp alongside divers. The site is almost exclusively accessed by boat from Miller's Point and is best dived after northwest wind when conditions settle on the False Bay shore.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Miller's Point, False Bay, Cape Town, South Africa
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (from Miller's Point)
- **Site Type**: Boulder reef (previously miscategorised as wall — corrected)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters (east of Seal Rock on sand); ~17m at Big Rock; ~22m at swim-through feature
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–10 meters (up to 20m on exceptional days)
- **Water Temperature**: 8–16°C — 5–7mm wetsuit or drysuit required
- **Best Time**: April–September (winter, after NW wind)

## Marine Life

Cape fur seals are the signature attraction — reliably encountered from the haul-out at Seal Rock. Fish diversity is among the highest on the Cape Peninsula: Hottentot sea bream, Fransmadam, Roman, Two-tone Fingerfin, Galjoen, klipfish, John Brown. Invertebrates include gorgonian sea fans (the Gorgonian Forest zone), nudibranchs, sea cucumbers, brittle stars, anemones, and octopus. Cape rock lobster inhabit the deeper crevices.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sandy base east of Seal Rock at 22–26m, then work up through the boulder structure. The shallowest sections of Big Rock are at 5–6m. Most productive marine life is in the 5–15m transition zone. The swim-through southeast of Big Rock at ~22m is a notable feature. Peter's Pinnacles provide a shallower option at ~13m for divers who want to start shallow.

## Entry and Exit

Boat drop-off; anchor or live-boat depending on operator. Coordinate return time with skipper. Carry an SMB for ascent.
