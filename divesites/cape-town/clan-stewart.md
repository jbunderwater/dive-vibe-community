## Clan Stewart

Clan Stewart is a shallow shore-entry wreck dive in Mackerel Bay, Simon's Town — the remains of the SS Clan Stuart, a 3,594-ton British turret steamer that grounded in a SE gale on 21 November 1914, lying at 7–9 metres with its engine block still projecting above the waterline.

## Overview

The SS Clan Stuart (the dive site is widely known as "Clan Stewart") was a 3,594-ton British turret steamer built in 1900 by W. Doxford and Son in Sunderland, England. On 21 November 1914, she was caught by a severe southeasterly gale while carrying coal; her anchor dragged and she was blown ashore near Glencairn. Crew were rescued via lifelines. The salvage tug Ludwig Wiener pulled her off the rocks, but she was refused entry into Simon's Town dry dock. Her captain deliberately ran her aground on the sandy bottom of nearby Mackerel Bay, running cables to shore and using two anchors to keep her upright. Despite four months of salvage attempts, she could not be refloated. The steam engine block still projects above the waterline today.

This shallow shore dive sits at 7–9 metres in the Benguela's cold water, within the Table Mountain National Park Marine Protected Area (protected since 2004). The hull is encrusted with red bait (ascidians), coralline algae, bryozoans, and nudibranchs. Entry is from Mackerel Beach approximately 100 metres from the wreck. A TMNP permit is required.

*Note: a previous version of this description mentioned great white shark sightings at this specific site. Great whites have been absent from False Bay since approximately 2017–2018 (peer-reviewed research, 2025); this claim has been removed.*

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mackerel Bay, Simon's Town, False Bay, Cape Town (~100m offshore from Mackerel Beach)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry (Mackerel Beach)
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive (partially exposed hull)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 9 meters
- **Depth Range**: 7–9 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 10–18°C (False Bay) — 5mm wetsuit minimum
- **Permit**: TMNP permit required (Table Mountain National Park MPA)

## Marine Life

The wreck is colonised by red bait ascidians, coralline algae, bryozoans, and nudibranchs — cold-water invertebrates typical of False Bay. No additional site-specific species are confirmed at this location by reviewed sources. Regional marine life in this area of False Bay includes reef fish and occasional Cape fur seal visits.
