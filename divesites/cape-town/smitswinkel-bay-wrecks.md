## Smitswinkel Bay Wrecks

Smitswinkel Bay Wrecks is a collection of five deliberately scuttled vessels in False Bay, forming one of southern Africa's most celebrated artificial reef dive sites, with wrecks spanning depths of 22–38 metres across 400 metres of sandy bay floor.

## Overview

Between 1972 and 1983, the South African Navy scuttled five vessels in Smitswinkel Bay to create an artificial reef — the most ambitious such project in South African diving history. The five wrecks are: the diamond dredger MV Rockeater (scuttled 15 December 1972, ~30m), the Loch-class frigates SAS Good Hope (18 June 1978, ~36m) and SAS Transvaal (3 August 1978, 34m), and the fishing trawlers MFV Princess Elizabeth (27 June 1983, 22m) and MFV Orotava (August 1983, 24m).

All five are densely colonised by soft corals, sea fans, sponges, and anemones, with rich fish life sheltering in the fractured superstructures. The wrecks are spread across roughly 400 metres of flat sandy bottom, and experienced navigators attempt the "Smits Swim" — visiting all five in a single no-decompression dive on exceptional visibility days. Visibility in the bay often surpasses inshore sites, reaching 10–20 metres on good days. The site is best dived in winter when southeast winds ease and the bay calms.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Smitswinkel Bay, False Bay, near Miller's Point, Cape Town
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 38 meters
- **Depth Range**: 22–38 meters across all five wrecks
- **Water Temperature**: 10–18°C — drysuit or thick wetsuit recommended
- **Best Time**: Winter (April–September) when SE winds relent

## Marine Life

The wrecks are heavily colonised by soft corals, sea fans, sponges, nudibranchs, starfish, and anemones. Dense fish populations shelter in the superstructures and between deck plates. Bring a torch to illuminate the colours properly — ambient light at depth in the bay does not reveal the full range of the invertebrate growth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. Operators depart from Miller's Point slipway (approximately 4 km) or Kalk Bay harbour. Divers descend on the anchor or mooring line. An SMB is essential for ascent due to boat traffic in the bay.

## Tips

- Nitrox is strongly recommended for extended bottom time at 30–38m
- Plan dive order by starting at the deepest wreck (Good Hope or Transvaal) and working shallower
- The "Smits Swim" (all five in one dive) requires strong navigation and careful air management
- Always bring a torch — colours are dramatic but require light at depth
