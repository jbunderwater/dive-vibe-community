## Het Huis te Kraaienstjin

Het Huis te Kraaienstjin (also spelled Het Huis te Kraaiestein) is South Africa's oldest known shipwreck — a Dutch East India Company vessel that struck the rocks at Oudekraal in thick mist on 27 May 1698 on her maiden outward voyage, leaving scattered iron cannon and anchors on a cold Atlantic reef.

## Overview

The Het Huis te Kraaienstein was a 1,154-ton Dutch pinas (square-rigged galleon) built in 1697 at the Zeeland Yard for the Zeeland Chamber of the Dutch East India Company (VOC), commanded by Jan van de Vijver. On her maiden outward voyage from the Netherlands, she struck the rocks at Oudekraal in thick mist while attempting to locate Table Bay's entrance, on 27 May 1698. The vessel carried 19 chests of pieces-of-eight (approximately 57,000 pieces); three of those chests were unaccounted for after the wreck — their whereabouts remain unknown. The name "Geldkis" (money-chest) appears on historical maps of the area.

The wreck has broken up completely. What divers find today are scattered iron cannon, anchors, and occasional pieces of wood, all heavily encrusted with invertebrate growth and kelp and recognisable mainly by the unusually straight lines of the cannon barrels. Two iron cannon approximately 3 metres long lie in a V-formation at the southern end of a boulder gap; two more cannon of the same type are partly or completely buried in the sand nearby. A large iron anchor is documented near the site. Another anchor attributed to this wreck sits among the rock outcrops to the north near Justin's Caves.

The site lies within the Table Mountain National Park Marine Protected Area (designated 2004) and requires a TMNP permit to dive. Depth to sand bottom is approximately 10m.

*Note: previous versions of this description referred to "bronze cannon" — this is incorrect. Wikivoyage and other authoritative sources consistently describe them as iron cannon.*

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Oudekraal, Atlantic seaboard, Cape Peninsula (Table Mountain National Park MPA)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Water Temperature**: Cold (8–14°C Atlantic) — 5–7mm wetsuit or drysuit required
- **Permit**: TMNP permit required

## Marine Life

The site is surrounded by Cape kelp forest and cold-water reef community typical of the Atlantic seaboard. No site-specific species reports were found for this site beyond general kelp forest and encrusting invertebrates. Regional marine life in this area includes cape rock lobster, various reef fish, nudibranchs, and sea urchins.

## Tips

- The cannon and anchor can be difficult to spot — look for unusually straight lines among the otherwise irregular boulders
- Best dived in summer after a south-east wind when Atlantic conditions calm and visibility improves
- Combine with Justin's Caves nearby for a fuller Oudekraal dive trip
