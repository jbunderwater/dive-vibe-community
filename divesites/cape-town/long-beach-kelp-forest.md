## Long Beach Kelp Forest

Long Beach Kelp Forest is a sheltered, sandy-bottomed shore dive in Simon's Town — one of the most protected dive sites on the Cape Peninsula — with kelp beds, rocky outcrops, and eelgrass patches to 8–10 metres, plus scattered historical wreck remains within swimming distance of shore.

## Overview

Long Beach in Simon's Town is one of the most sheltered dive sites on the Cape Peninsula, protected from southwest swell and largely shielded from southeasterly wind waves. The bottom is a sandy bay floor interspersed with kelp beds, rocky outcrops, and patches of eelgrass, descending gradually to about 8–10 metres. This makes it one of Cape Town's most reliable all-conditions dive sites and a popular training venue.

The site is famous in the international diving community as a location to experience the "Great African Seaforest" — the shallow-water kelp ecosystem featured in various wildlife documentaries. Several shallow historical wrecks lie within easy swimming distance of shore, including scattered remains from World War II-era vessels. The nearby SAS Pietermaritzburg — an A-Class minesweeper and D-Day veteran — rests at a maximum depth of 20 metres and is accessible to experienced divers from this entry point.

False Bay water temperatures here are 12–18°C, warmer than the Atlantic seaboard sites, with the warmer summer months attracting a broader range of species.

*Note: previous versions listed pyjama sharks, gully sharks, and shysharks as marine life at this site. These species are documented in the broader Long Beach / Simon's Town area but not confirmed as regular sightings at this specific kelp forest site by the reviewed sources. Southern Right whales have been reported in False Bay but not confirmed at this dive site specifically.*

## Site Information

- **Location**: Long Beach, Simon's Town, False Bay, Cape Town
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 12–18°C (False Bay, warmer than Atlantic side)
- **Conditions**: Most protected site on Cape Peninsula; minimal swell; good for training

## Marine Life

Octopus, biscuit skates, blue-fin gurnard, pineapple fish, stingrays, and Cape fur seals are documented in the area. Kelp forest invertebrates typical of False Bay include sea urchins, nudibranchs, and encrusting sponges. The SAS Pietermaritzburg wreck accessible from this point is colonised by invertebrates and fish at 20m depth.
