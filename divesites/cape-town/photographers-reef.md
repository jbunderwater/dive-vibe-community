## Photographer's Reef

Photographer's Reef — also known as JJM Reef — is a compact granite corestone reef near Seaforth in Simon's Town, sitting about 4 km from Miller's Point on the False Bay coast and rising from 13 metres on the sandy base to a flat top at 3 metres.

## Overview

Photographer's Reef (alternate name JJM Reef, from the initials of three divers who established it as a dive site in the 1980s) is a drowned granite tor: a compact cluster of high-profile outcrops and boulders with deep gaps, crevices, deep overhangs, and a few swim-throughs. It is usually considered a boat dive from Miller's Point (approximately 4 km), though it can also be accessed as a shore entry from North Windmill Beach or a gully south of the Penguin Point parking area.

The topography is densely varied for the reef's modest size: crevices, bowls, overhangs, caves, and swim-throughs on all faces, all encrusted with cold-water invertebrate communities — sponges, sea fans, anemones — fed by the Benguela Current. The site requires a TMNP permit. Visibility correlates with wind direction: northwesterly winds tend to produce better visibility than SE, and winter typically offers clearer water than summer.

*Difficulty upgraded to Intermediate: cold Atlantic water conditions apply at this site despite its modest depth; open water boat dive with exposure to False Bay swell.*

## Site Information

- **Location**: Seaforth area, Simon's Town, False Bay, Cape Town (4 km from Miller's Point)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 13 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 10–18°C — 5mm wetsuit minimum
- **Permit**: TMNP permit required

## Marine Life

Regional marine life includes cold-water reef fish, nudibranchs, sponges, sea fans, and the encrusting invertebrate communities typical of False Bay granite reefs. No site-specific species reports were found for this site beyond the general cold-water invertebrate community described in Wikivoyage.
