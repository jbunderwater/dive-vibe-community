## Justin's Caves

Justin's Caves is a cluster of large granite corestone boulders at Oudekraal on Cape Town's Atlantic seaboard, riddled with swim-throughs, narrow gaps, overhangs, and a prize cavern decorated with pink and purple noble corals — one of the most visually striking dive sites on the Cape Peninsula.

## Overview

Justin's Caves lies in the north Oudekraal area of the Atlantic seaboard, approximately 150 metres offshore from the parking area. The site consists of a group of large granite outcrops and boulders with several swim-throughs, caves, overhangs, deep narrow gaps, and a celebrated cavern lined with pink and purple noble corals — the highlight of the dive.

The centre of the site is relatively shallow at about 5 metres, while the seaward edge drops to approximately 17 metres, with the sand bottom generally between 8–13 metres. On good summer visibility days after a southeasterly wind, the Atlantic here can clear to over 20 metres, with water temperatures dropping below 10°C. A large iron anchor attributed to the 1698 VOC wreck Het Huis te Kraaienstein lies among the outcrops to the north of the caves.

The site is technically a rocky reef with prominent cave and cavern features; the "cave" classification reflects the celebrated cavern system that is the primary attraction. All passages described at this site are swim-throughs and caverns with ambient light — no full cave diving equipment is required, though the narrow gaps demand good buoyancy control.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Oudekraal, Atlantic seaboard, Cape Peninsula, Cape Town
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Depth**: 5–13 meters across most of site
- **Water Temperature**: Cold (8–12°C) — 7mm wetsuit or drysuit required
- **Visibility**: Up to 20m+ on good summer days after SE wind
- **Conditions**: Strong swell in southwesterly winds; currents at passages; avoid in poor conditions

## Marine Life

The reef hosts Cape sea urchins, dark sea cucumbers, spiny starfish, cape rock lobster (in crevices), sponges, soft corals, noble corals (pink and purple in the prize cavern), sea fans, and ascidians under overhangs and in swim-throughs.

## Tips

- The noble coral cavern is the centrepiece — ask local operators for the current entry route as it requires navigating through narrow gaps
- Combine with the nearby Het Huis te Kraaienstein cannon site for a full Oudekraal dive day
- Entry from the roadside parking requires a brief surface swim — check conditions before committing
