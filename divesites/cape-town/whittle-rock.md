## Whittle Rock

Whittle Rock is an extensive offshore rocky reef pinnacle in False Bay, rising from 40 metres to a shallowest point of approximately 5 metres, covering roughly 1 km² of granite corestone topography with pinnacles, gullies, walls, and occasional swim-throughs.

## Overview

Whittle Rock is a large offshore rocky reef on the central west side of False Bay, approximately 9 km from Miller's Point. The main pinnacle rises to about 5 metres below sea level at its shallowest, with surrounding sand bottom at 30–42 metres. The reef extends approximately 1,300 metres east to west and 950 metres north to south, giving it the most substantial footprint of any Cape Peninsula dive site. Multiple named features provide varied diving across ability levels: the main 5m pinnacle, the West Pinnacle (7m), Table Top (flat-topped at 14.5–15m), Grant's Spike (18m), and the Shards cluster.

The granite corestone topography includes pinnacles, gullies, walls, overhangs, and occasional swim-throughs, all colonised by the cold-water invertebrate communities fed by Benguela upwelling. A Cardinal East marker buoy moored by chain at approximately 21m depth marks the main navigational hazard. Visibility ranges from 3m to over 20m depending on conditions. The site is 8 km offshore from Miller's Point, requiring a significant boat journey from Simon's Town (14.5 km) and good sea conditions. Gully sharks inhabit the swim-throughs, and large short-tailed stingrays are documented at the site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Central west False Bay, approximately 9km from Miller's Point
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Shallow Area**: Large area shallower than 18m near main pinnacle (suitable for entry-level divers with appropriate supervision)
- **Typical Visibility**: 3–20+ meters
- **Navigation Buoy**: Cardinal East marker at ~21m, ~50m south of main pinnacle

## Marine Life

Gully sharks are documented in the swim-throughs. Large short-tailed stingrays are sighted at the site. Basket stars, feather stars, sea fans, and sponges colonise vertical surfaces. Seasonal pelagic species include shoals of yellowtail. Seals occasionally visit, particularly during decompression stops.
