## Tafelberg Reef

Tafelberg Reef is a rugged offshore granite reef on the Atlantic seaboard near Hout Bay, featuring cold-water invertebrate communities in dense Benguela Current upwelling, accessible only by boat approximately 4.5 km from Hout Bay Harbour.

## Overview

Tafelberg Reef is located on the Atlantic seaboard of the Cape Peninsula, approximately 4.5 km from Hout Bay Harbour — not in Table Bay as might be inferred from the name. The reef consists of rugged granite corestone outcrops with high relief, deep crevices, and gullies, with the highest pinnacles reaching approximately 8 metres and the reef dropping to a maximum of around 30 metres. The formation encompasses three main recorded dive sites: Cowrie Caves, Tafelberg Reef North, and Tafelberg Reef West.

The site is within the Table Mountain National Park Marine Protected Area and requires a TMNP diving permit. Boats may anchor but live-boat diving is often more appropriate given swell conditions. The site is cold, exposed to south-westerly swell surge, and a drysuit is recommended.

Invertebrate colonisation is the primary attraction: red and brown algae cover shallow surfaces, while sponges, soft corals, hard corals, and ascidians encrust vertical faces in the colder, deeper zones. Colonial ascidians, dreadlocks hydroids, zoanthids, nudibranchs, and basket stars are documented at the site. Visibility is typically 10m, reaching over 20m on optimal days following strong south-easterly winds that cause upwelling of clear water (though subsequent plankton blooms can reduce it again).

*Note: a previous version of this description mentioned pyjama sharks as a reliable sighting at Tafelberg Reef — no reviewed source confirms this species at this specific site. That claim has been removed.*

## Site Information

- **Location**: Atlantic seaboard, approximately 4.5 km from Hout Bay Harbour, Cape Peninsula
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10m (up to 20m+ with upwelling)
- **Water Temperature**: 8–14°C cold Atlantic — drysuit strongly recommended
- **Permit**: TMNP diving permit required

## Marine Life

Colonial ascidians, dreadlocks hydroids, zoanthids, nudibranchs, basket stars, brittle stars, and various sponges and soft corals colonise the reef surfaces. Occasional visiting seals have been reported, particularly at decompression stops.
