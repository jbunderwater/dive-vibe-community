## Batu Kapal

Batu Kapal ("Ship Rock") is a multi-pinnacle dive site north of Pulau Pisang in the Banda Islands. The main rock breaks the surface in a shape resembling a vessel hull, and a series of submerged pinnacles extends below — dropping from 8 m down to beyond 50 m — making this one of the deeper and more current-exposed sites in the Banda archipelago.

## Overview

The pinnacles are encrusted with enormous orange gorgonian fans and barrel sponges, and the channels between them are swept by strong, shifting currents that concentrate pelagic fish. Midnight snappers, bigeye bream, and moray eels inhabit the rocky structure, while grey reef sharks circle in the open water above the pinnacle tops. Rainbow runners and schooling jacks sweep through on current pushes.

Visibility here is exceptional by Banda Sea standards — sources report 40–50 m on good days, particularly between February and May. The shallower sections (8–15 m) have leather corals, sea kraits in crevices, and octopus on the rocky walls. The site's strong and unpredictable currents demand advanced diving skills.

## Dive Profile

Pinnacles begin at 8 m and drop beyond 50 m. The 10–40 m range has the densest life. Currents change direction unpredictably — timing dives around slack water is recommended. Best visibility: February–May (40–50 m). Typical visibility: 20–30 m. Water temperature: 27–29°C.

## Marine Life

- Grey reef sharks in open water above pinnacles
- Moray eels (giant and spotted varieties) in crevices
- Midnight snappers, bigeye bream
- Rainbow runners and schooling jacks
- Enormous gorgonian fans (orange) and barrel sponges
- Red-tooth triggerfish on the pinnacle tops
- Octopus and sea kraits in shallower crevices

## Entry / Exit

Boat dive from liveaboard or Banda Neira day-trip vessel. The changing currents mean entry and exit timing must be planned with the guide. Negative entries may be required on some dives.

## Safety

Strong and unpredictable currents — this site is rated Advanced. A surface marker buoy (SMB) is essential. Dive with an experienced local guide who knows the current patterns. Diving may be impossible at some tidal states.

## Photography

The orange gorgonians against blue water are a classic Banda Sea wide-angle subject. Grey reef sharks in the water column above the pinnacles make for compelling open-water shots. High visibility periods (Feb–May) are ideal for wide-angle work.
