## Pohon Miring

Pohon Miring ("Crooked Tree") is a drift dive site on the northeastern tip of Banda Besar. The site is named for a distinctive leaning tree on the shore above the dive site. Divers come here for an impressive swim-through tunnel and for the convergence of north and south currents that aggregates large schools of pelagic fish.

## Overview

The defining feature of Pohon Miring is a 15-metre-long almond-shaped swim-through tunnel at 26–30 m depth. The passage walls are lined with orange dendronephthya soft corals, barrel sponges, and sea fans — making it one of the more visually striking structures in the Banda Islands. Moray eels, scorpionfish, leaffish, and ghost pipefish inhabit the tunnel and its surrounds.

At the current-swept plateau above and beyond the tunnel, north and south current systems meet, creating an upwelling that concentrates bumphead parrotfish, Napoleon wrasse, dogtooth tuna, and emperor angelfish in large numbers. This is a drift dive: divers descend, transit the tunnel, and are swept along the reef in the combined current flow. Sweetlips and black snappers are found on the outer reef edge.

## Dive Profile

Drift dive on the northeastern tip of Banda Besar. The swim-through is at 26–30 m; the current-swept plateau above is at 15–20 m. Currents can be strong where north and south flows converge. Plan a drift exit and coordinate pickup with the liveaboard tender. Visibility: 20–30 m. Water temperature: 27–29°C.

## Marine Life

- Bumphead parrotfish (schools, on current-swept plateau)
- Napoleon wrasse, dogtooth tuna
- Emperor angelfish, sweetlips, black snappers
- Moray eels, scorpionfish, leaffish
- Ghost pipefish in the tunnel interior
- Orange dendronephthya soft corals, barrel sponges, sea fans

## Entry / Exit

Boat dive with drift exit. Entry at the uptide end of the site; pickup at the end of the drift. Strong current makes this an intermediate-to-advanced drift dive despite the 30 m maximum depth.

## Tips

- The swim-through is the centrepiece — follow the guide through in single file
- Station yourself on the upcurrent side of the tunnel exit to catch bumphead parrotfish returning to the plateau
- Best current for fish action: moderate incoming push from either direction

## Photography

The orange dendronephthya in the swim-through makes a striking frame for wide-angle shots with a diver or school of fish visible at the exit. The plateau's bumphead parrotfish schools are a classic wide-angle subject.
