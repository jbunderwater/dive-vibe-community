## Pohon Miring

Pohon Miring ("Leaning Tree" or "Crooked Tree") is a drift dive on the northeastern tip of Banda Besar, named for a distinctive leaning tree visible on the shore above the site. The defining features are a colourful swim-through at around 26 m depth and a current-swept plateau where north and south current systems converge.

## Overview

A large crack in the reef at approximately 26 m depth forms a swim-through lined with orange dendronephthya soft corals, sea fans, and large barrel sponges — one of the more visually striking structures in the Banda Islands. Moray eels, scorpionfish, and ghost pipefish inhabit the tunnel and the surrounding reef structure.

Above and beyond the swim-through, the north and south current systems meet, creating an upwelling that concentrates bumphead parrotfish, napoleon wrasse, dogtooth tuna, and sweetlips in large numbers. This is a drift dive: divers descend, transit the swim-through, and are carried along the reef in the combined current flow. Black snappers and sweetlips aggregate on the outer reef edge.

Strong currents at the convergence zone can be significant. The combination of 26 m swim-through and open-water drift exit elevates this site to Advanced despite the 30 m maximum depth.

## Dive Profile

Drift dive on the northeastern tip of Banda Besar. The swim-through is at approximately 26 m; the current-swept plateau above is at 15–20 m. Currents can be strong where north and south flows converge. Plan a drift exit and coordinate pickup with the liveaboard tender. Visibility: 20–30 m. Water temperature: 27–29°C. Best season: September to April.

## Marine Life

- Bumphead parrotfish (schools on current-swept plateau)
- Napoleon wrasse, dogtooth tuna
- Sweetlips, black snappers
- Moray eels, scorpionfish
- Ghost pipefish in and around the swim-through
- Orange dendronephthya soft corals, sea fans, barrel sponges lining the swim-through

## Entry / Exit

Boat dive with drift exit. Entry at the uptide end of the site; pickup at the end of the drift. Strong current and the swim-through depth make this an Advanced dive despite the 30 m maximum depth.

## Tips

- The swim-through is the centrepiece — follow the guide through in single file
- Station yourself on the upcurrent side of the tunnel exit to watch bumphead parrotfish on the plateau
- Best current for fish action: moderate incoming push from either direction

## Photography

The dendronephthya-lined swim-through makes a striking wide-angle frame with a diver or school of fish visible at the exit. The bumphead parrotfish on the plateau are a classic wide-angle subject.
