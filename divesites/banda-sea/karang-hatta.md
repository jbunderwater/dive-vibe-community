## Karang Hatta

Karang Hatta (also called Sekaru — meaning "shallow area" in local usage) is a submerged reef system approximately 500 metres wide, located a few kilometres off the south coast of Pulau Hatta. It sits closest to the Banda Trench (approximately 6,000 m deep) of any commonly dived site in the archipelago, which concentrates an exceptional range of pelagic species over its sloping reefs.

## Overview

The reef slopes gradually from around 5 m down to beyond 40 m. Multiple sources place the productive action zone at around 25 m, where dense neon fusilier schools — yellow and blue, running so thick at times that they block the view — sweep down the reef slope. Large dogtooth tuna, eagle rays, giant barracuda, and schooling trevally follow the fusiliers. Blacktip sharks and red snappers circle in the depths. Hammerhead sharks are reported at this site, most often when upwelling currents are active; their presence is unpredictable rather than routine.

The deeper wall sections feature some of the region's largest sea fans and sponges. X-Ray Mag specifically describes the site for its "immense elephant ear sponges, huge sea fans" and the fusilier clouds.

## Dive Profile

Gently sloping reef from 5 m to 25–30 m sweet spot, then dropping more steeply. Typical dive depth 15–35 m. Current variable — upwelling pushes concentrate fish on the reef edge. Advanced divers can follow the deeper wall sections.

## Marine Life

- Schooling fusiliers (yellow and blue; extremely dense aggregations)
- Dogtooth tuna, giant barracuda, schooling trevally
- Eagle rays, green turtles
- Blacktip sharks (in the depths); hammerhead sharks (current-dependent, unpredictable)
- Triggerfish, groupers, napoleon wrasse
- Large elephant ear sponges and sea fans on deeper sections

## Entry / Exit

Boat dive from liveaboard. Hatta Island is approximately 25 km from Banda Neira. Calmer conditions are typical but current can pick up along the outer reef.

## Tips

- Best combined with Goa Hatta for a full day on Hatta Island
- Best season for pelagics: September–November and March–April when currents are stronger
- Check current direction with your guide before entry

## Photography

Outstanding wide-angle destination. The fusilier schools combined with the deep blue background make for dramatic open-water compositions. Bring a large dome port for reef-and-blue shots.
