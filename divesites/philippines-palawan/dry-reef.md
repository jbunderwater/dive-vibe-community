## Dry Reef

Dry Reef is a submerged reef formation in the central Palawan sea, located in the inner passage between Palawan island and the island clusters to the east, near the approach to the Puerto Princesa area.

## Overview

The name refers to the reef's tendency to break the surface or approach it closely during low tide, a common characteristic of the shallow offshore reefs throughout Palawan waters. Underwater, the site offers a compact reef structure with hard coral formations at 5–15 meters transitioning to sand and rubble margins at 20 meters. The inner passage location provides somewhat more sheltered conditions than the outer southern banks, and the moderate diving depth makes it accessible for a range of certification levels. Coral health is generally good, reflecting the reduced fishing pressure in offshore reef areas.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Central Palawan inner passage, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate; tidal influence
- **Best Time**: October to May

## Marine Life

The reef hosts a typical Palawan inner-passage marine community. Resident fish include parrotfish, wrasse, grouper, and butterflyfish. Sea turtles are commonly encountered on the shallower sections. Batfish hover near coral overhangs, and schools of snapper occupy the mid-water around the reef structure. The sandy margins around the reef base are worth checking for flounder, stingrays, and burrowing eels.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top and begin a counterclockwise circuit at 10–12 meters to establish orientation. Descend to the sandy perimeter at 18–20 meters for the second half of the dive, then return up the slope for the safety stop. The compact nature of the reef allows a thorough exploration without covering excessive ground.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Puerto Princesa or other central Palawan ports. Day trips may be feasible depending on the exact location, or the site can form part of an island-hopping dive itinerary. Entry by giant stride; return to boat via SMB deployment at the safety stop.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check the sandy margins carefully — stingrays and flatfish are easily overlooked here
- Visibility can be reduced after strong winds; check sea conditions before the trip
- Morning slack tide dives are generally most productive
- Bring a torch for exploring any overhangs or reef crevices
- The moderate depth and generally mild current makes this a relaxed dive

## Safety Considerations

Standard boat diving precautions apply. The reef's shallow maximum depth reduces decompression risk, but a safety stop remains good practice. Carry an SMB for the ascent. Be aware of any boat traffic in the inner passage.

## Photography

Good natural light on the shallow reef enables clear macro photography throughout the day. Reef fish portraits and wide-angle coral garden shots are the primary photographic opportunities. Check sandy patches for camouflaged subjects like flatfish and scorpionfish.
