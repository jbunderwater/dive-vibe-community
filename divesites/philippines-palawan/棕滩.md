## 棕滩

棕滩 is a reef site in the southern Palawan sea bearing a Chinese name meaning roughly "Brown Beach" or "Brown Bank," consistent with the brownish appearance of coral-dominated shallows when viewed from the surface.

## Overview

Like several other features in the southern Palawan sea corridor, 棕滩 carries a Chinese-language name that appears on historical Chinese navigation charts of these waters. The descriptive name refers to the brownish colour of the shallow reef when sunlight illuminates it from above — a common characteristic of coral-dominated reef flats throughout the region. The site forms a reef structure with a coral platform at approximately 8–10 meters sloping to sandy and rubble substrate at 22 meters. The surrounding deep blue water of the open sea creates a classic offshore reef diving environment with excellent visibility and minimal human impact on the ecosystem.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Palawan offshore, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters
- **Current**: Variable; moderate open-ocean influence
- **Best Time**: November to May

## Marine Life

The site's undisturbed status makes for memorable marine encounters. Sea turtles are reliably present, often found resting on the coral platform in the 8–12 meter range. Schooling fish including fusiliers, snapper, and barracuda are common. Reef sharks patrol the outer edges. The sandy perimeter at depth yields stingrays, flatfish, and sand-dwelling species not often seen at more frequently dived sites.

## Dive Profile

Begin on the shallow coral platform and work outward toward the reef edges before descending to the 18–22 meter sandy perimeter. Search the sandy zones for depth-dwelling species before ascending the slope and finishing on the bright, shallow reef top. The extended shallows allow good photography time before the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard from southern Palawan. Entry by giant stride; SMB for ascent. Open-ocean surface conditions require attention, especially when current is running.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The sandy perimeter at depth is worth exploring for rays and flatfish
- Sea turtles on the shallow platform are a consistent highlight
- Morning dives in good conditions can offer visibility exceeding 25 meters
- The reef's compact size allows thorough exploration on a single dive
- Part of the southern Palawan bank circuit — combine with other sites for a diverse itinerary

## Safety Considerations

Remote open-ocean site. Standard liveaboard dive safety protocols apply. Carry SMB and emergency equipment. Air management is important given the extended bottom time possible at this shallow-to-moderate depth range.

## Photography

The combination of clear water, shallow reef, and abundant sea turtles makes this a rewarding photography destination. Natural light is excellent on the 8–12 meter platform. Sandy margins offer behavioural photography of bottom-dwelling species.
