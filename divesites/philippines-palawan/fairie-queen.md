## Fairie Queen

Fairie Queen is a shallow shoal dive site in southern Palawan, one of the evocatively named underwater features in the remote passage waters between the Palawan archipelago and Borneo.

## Overview

Located in the open southern Palawan sea, Fairie Queen is a submerged shoal where the seafloor rises to form a productive shallow reef habitat. The site's accessibility is determined by sea conditions — this is open-ocean diving where swells can make the surface uncomfortable even when visibility below is excellent. The reef structure features a mosaic of hard corals on the shallowest sections, with the surrounding deeper margins offering sandy flats and scattered coral boulders. The poetic name reflects the tradition among early chart-makers of giving imaginative names to navigational hazards in these waters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Palawan open sea, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Shoal / coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 meters
- **Current**: Variable; open-ocean influence
- **Best Time**: November to May (calmer seas)

## Marine Life

The shoal's isolated position in open water makes it a magnet for pelagic species. Schooling barracuda and trevally are common overhead. The reef structure itself supports typical Palawan reef life: parrotfish, grouper, wrasse, and resident sea turtles. Reef sharks are occasionally sighted on the deeper perimeter, and manta rays have been reported in the broader area during the appropriate season.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the shallowest section of the shoal and conduct an orientation before working toward the edges. The most interesting life is typically around the shoal's perimeter, where the reef meets the sandy margins. Keep an eye on the blue water above and to the side for pelagic passers-by. Ascent with SMB.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard or long-range chartered boat from southern Palawan. Surface conditions can be choppy depending on wind direction and swell. Entry by giant stride; return to boat requires SMB deployment as surface drift may affect pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check weather forecasts carefully — this site is exposed to open-ocean swell
- Look into the blue water periodically for pelagics that may pass through
- The shoal's remoteness means marine life is relatively undisturbed
- Plan dive timing around slack tide for calmest conditions and clearest water
- Bring both wide-angle and macro gear to cover all photographic opportunities

## Safety Considerations

Open-ocean conditions demand additional precautions. Surface conditions may deteriorate quickly. Carry an SMB, whistle, and if available a mirror or light for signaling. Ensure the boat crew is briefed on your expected dive time and has a clear visual on the area.

## Photography

The open water setting creates good conditions for blue-water wide-angle work. Schooling pelagics against a light background make striking subjects. The shallow reef offers coral and reef fish portrait opportunities in excellent natural light.
