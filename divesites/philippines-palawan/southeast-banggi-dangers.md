## Southeast Banggi Dangers

Southeast Banggi Dangers is a shallow reef formation on the southeastern flank of the Banggi Island complex in the Balabac Strait, one of several "Dangers" features in the area whose chart-name hazard designations now serve as dive site identifiers.

## Overview

The southeast position of this reef exposes it to the combined influence of tidal flows from the Balabac Strait and Sulu Sea, creating variable current conditions that keep the reef well-nourished. The "Dangers" classification reflects the site's historical significance as a shallow hazard for vessels navigating the strait. Underwater, the site presents a compact reef structure rising to near the surface in its shallowest sections, with a maximum diving depth of 18 meters. Hard coral formations dominate the reef structure, interspersed with sandy channels. The site's low profile and clear surrounding water make it navigable and enjoyable for intermediate-level divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southeast Banggi, Balabac Strait, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Current**: Variable tidal; southeast exposure
- **Best Time**: November to May

## Marine Life

The shallow reef supports dense reef fish populations including parrotfish, surgeonfish, butterflyfish, and chromis. Sea turtles regularly cross the reef. Lionfish and scorpionfish occupy coral crevices. The sandy channels between coral formations are productive for finding garden eels, gobies, and sand-dwelling species. Occasional barracuda schools pass through the open water above the reef.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deepest accessible section and work around the reef structure, using the sandy channels as navigational reference points. Explore coral heads carefully with a torch to find sheltering species. The shallow maximum depth enables a comprehensive, unhurried dive covering the full extent of the structure.

## Entry and Exit

Access by boat from the Banggi/Balabac area or liveaboard. Entry by giant stride; SMB for ascent. Surface conditions can be unpredictable at this exposed position in the strait.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Sandy channels between reef sections are excellent for spotting burrowing species
- The southeast exposure means conditions are typically better during the southwest monsoon season
- Fish populations here are extremely undisturbed — approach slowly for maximum observation
- A torch is essential for exploring coral crevices where lionfish and moray eels hide
- The shallow site makes it ideal for a final dive when air and no-decompression time are limited

## Safety Considerations

Monitor current direction carefully at this southeast-exposed site. Currents can shift direction with tidal changes. Carry an SMB and be prepared for surface conditions that may be choppier than at more sheltered sites in the same reef group.

## Photography

Good natural light in the shallows makes this an excellent photography site throughout the day. Sandy channel species including garden eels and gobies provide unique behavioural photography subjects. Reef fish portraits benefit from the clear, bright water.
