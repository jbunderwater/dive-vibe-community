## 雄南礁

雄南礁 is a reef feature in the waters west of Palawan bearing a Chinese name meaning approximately "Southern Hero Reef," one of the Chinese-designated reefs in the southern Palawan sea that sits in the approach waters north of the Balabac Strait.

## Overview

Located in the western Palawan sea north of the Balabac Strait approaches, 雄南礁 occupies a position at the northern end of the cluster of Chinese-named features in this area. The reef rises from deeper water to form a coral structure accessible to recreational divers, with a maximum depth of 22 meters. The site sits in open ocean exposed to the seasonal monsoon patterns that shape diving conditions throughout the year — northeast monsoon from November to April and southwest monsoon from May to October. Like the other remote Palawan offshore reefs, it benefits enormously from limited human pressure on the marine ecosystem.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Western Palawan sea, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters
- **Current**: Variable monsoon-driven current
- **Best Time**: November to May (northeast monsoon, generally cleaner conditions)

## Marine Life

The reef supports the characteristic marine community of an undisturbed Palawan offshore reef. Sea turtles visit the shallow sections, and reef sharks patrol the deeper edges. Schooling pelagics including barracuda and trevally pass through regularly. Napoleon wrasse and grouper occupy permanent territories within the reef structure. The reef fish community is diverse and largely unconditioned to human presence.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top and orient before exploring the perimeter. The 15–22 meter range on the outer slopes is the primary target for larger animal sightings. Work back up the slope for an extended exploration of the shallower reef before the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Liveaboard access from Puerto Princesa or other Palawan ports. The western sea location requires careful weather monitoring. Entry by giant stride; SMB for ascent with drift pickup if current is running.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The open western sea location may create different conditions from the southern Palawan passage sites
- Westerly swell can affect surface conditions; choose calm-weather windows for this dive
- The deeper maximum depth allows exploration of more varied reef habitats than the shallower shoals
- Bring both macro and wide-angle gear — the reef offers both close-up and expansive photography subjects
- This is the most northerly of the Chinese-named reef sites — a geographically distinct diving experience

## Safety Considerations

Open western sea exposure with variable conditions. Be cautious of westerly swells which can make entry and exit challenging. Carry comprehensive emergency equipment. Nearest hyperbaric facility is Puerto Princesa.

## Photography

The western sea exposure may deliver different lighting conditions than eastern sites — late afternoon light from the west can be particularly atmospheric. Wide-angle reef scenes and pelagic encounters are the primary photography subjects.
