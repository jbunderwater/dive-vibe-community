## 莪兰暗沙

莪兰暗沙 is a submerged shoal in the southern Palawan sea carrying a Chinese name, part of the collection of Chinese-designated features scattered across the contested waters southwest of Palawan proper.

## Overview

The name 莪兰暗沙 appears in Chinese navigational records of the southern Philippine sea, where generations of fishermen and maritime traders charted these waters before modern political boundaries were established. Underwater, the site presents a compact reef formation with coral growth across its structure, rising to accessible depths with a maximum of 20 meters at the outer edges. The platform at 7–10 meters supports a productive hard coral community fed by the currents crossing the open Palawan sea. Clear visibility is typical in this open-ocean setting, particularly from November through May when the northeast monsoon brings settled conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Palawan open sea, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral shoal / reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 meters
- **Current**: Variable open-ocean
- **Best Time**: November to May

## Marine Life

The reef attracts sea turtles, which are regularly seen feeding and resting on the platform. Schooling fish species including fusiliers, snapper, and jacks are present. Reef sharks occasionally patrol the deeper edges. Hard coral diversity supports a range of resident reef species including parrotfish, surgeonfish, and wrasse. The site's isolation from regular fishing and diving means marine life is plentiful and relatively undisturbed.

## Dive Profile

Begin on the shallowest section and work around the reef, descending to the 15–20 meter range on the outer edges. The circuit of the reef at moderate depth is typically the most productive, combining reef edge activity with the shelter of the platform on the return. Safety stop on the shallow platform.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard from southern Palawan. Open-ocean entry and exit; SMB essential. Boat crew should maintain visual contact throughout the dive.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The isolation of this site is its greatest asset — treat the ecosystem with care
- Dive conservatively on air given the remote location
- Morning conditions typically offer the best visibility
- Sea turtle encounters on the shallow platform are a highlight
- Combine with nearby Chinese-named reefs for a fascinating historical-geographic diving day

## Safety Considerations

Remote open-ocean location. Comprehensive safety equipment required. Conservative dive profiles are essential given the distance from emergency services. Puerto Princesa is the nearest hyperbaric facility.

## Photography

Clear water and undisturbed marine life provide exceptional photography opportunities. Wide-angle reef panoramas and sea turtle portraits are the key subjects. Natural light in the shallows is excellent in clear conditions.
