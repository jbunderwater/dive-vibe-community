## Leslie Bank

Leslie Bank is an offshore coral bank in the southern Palawan sea corridor, part of the cluster of submerged banks that make this remote stretch of Philippine waters a destination for dedicated liveaboard divers seeking pristine, seldom-visited reefs.

## Overview

Sitting in open water between Palawan and Borneo, Leslie Bank is named after historical nautical chart designations for this area. The bank rises from the seabed to form a coral-covered plateau at 10–12 meters, with sloping sides descending to 26 meters. The deeper edges of the bank attract the best large animal activity, as currents sweeping around the bank concentrate nutrients and draw pelagic species. Hard coral coverage is generally intact, with sea fans appearing on the deeper slopes alongside soft corals and whip corals. The bank's isolation ensures it receives virtually no recreational dive traffic, leaving the ecosystem in a largely natural state.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Palawan open sea, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral bank / reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters
- **Current**: Variable open-ocean; can be strong on bank edges
- **Best Time**: November to May

## Marine Life

Leslie Bank delivers the pelagic encounters typical of isolated Philippine offshore banks. Schools of barracuda and trevally are reliable sightings overhead and along the edges. Reef sharks patrol the deeper perimeter. Napoleon wrasse and large grouper inhabit the bank's interior. Sea turtles visit regularly. The deep, clear water surrounding the bank occasionally yields encounters with eagle rays and other open-ocean species passing by.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the bank's deeper edge at 20–26 meters first, where the best shark and pelagic activity typically occurs. Work your way progressively shallower, spending time on the sea fan zone at 15–20 meters before exploring the coral plateau. Finish on the shallow bank top and conduct your safety stop there. Air management is important given the deeper initial descent.

## Entry and Exit

Leslie Bank requires liveaboard or long-range chartered vessel access. The bank is too remote for day trips from any port. Entry by giant stride; SMB deployment for ascent is essential. Agree drift pickup procedures with the boat crew before descending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The deeper starting depth means careful air monitoring is required
- Dive the bank's edges rather than just the plateau — the edges are where the action is
- Bring a reef hook; you may want to rest and observe if a good current is running
- This bank is best dived as part of a multi-bank circuit in the southern Palawan sea
- Water temperature is warm year-round, but thermoclines may be encountered below 20 meters

## Safety Considerations

Open-ocean diving at the deeper limit for recreational diving. Be strict with depth and bottom time management. Carry a dive computer and SMB. Strong currents on the bank edges require situational awareness. Nearest hyperbaric facility is in Puerto Princesa, many hours distant.

## Photography

The combination of clear open water, sea fans, and pelagic activity makes Leslie Bank a wide-angle photography site. Shark portraits at depth against the bank structure are memorable images. Sea fan details and coral plateau scenes round out the photographic possibilities.
