## 彬礁

彬礁 is a reef feature in the southern Palawan sea bearing a Chinese-language name, reflecting the overlapping historical maritime claims and navigation records that various nations maintained for these disputed but ecologically rich southern Philippine waters.

## Overview

The Chinese name on this reef echoes the contested history of the southern Palawan sea, where Chinese fishing and navigation maps predating modern territorial boundaries recorded and named features across the Spratly Island approaches and adjacent waters. The reef itself rises from deeper water to form a coral-covered structure with a platform in the range of 8–12 meters, sloping to 21 meters on the outer edges. Regardless of its naming history, the reef supports a healthy Coral Triangle ecosystem with good hard coral coverage and rich fish life. Remote location ensures the ecosystem remains largely undisturbed by recreational diving.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Palawan sea, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 meters
- **Current**: Variable open-ocean current
- **Best Time**: November to May

## Marine Life

Typical of the remote southern Palawan reefs, fish populations here are dense and unguarded. Schooling barracuda and trevally patrol above the reef. Sea turtles visit the coral platform. Reef sharks are present on the deeper perimeter. Napoleon wrasse and grouper occupy reef territories. The relatively undisturbed state of the reef means behavioural observations here are more natural than at frequently dived sites.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef platform and work methodically around the structure, descending the outer slopes to the 21-meter maximum. The reef perimeter is the most active zone for larger fish. Return to the shallower platform for a relaxed finish and safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard from southern Palawan. Open-ocean conditions apply. Entry by giant stride; SMB essential for drift ascent if any current is running. Coordinate with boat crew for pickup location.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The remote, rarely dived nature of this reef makes it special — take time to appreciate the intact ecosystem
- Current conditions vary; dive on slack tide for the most comfortable conditions
- Both wide-angle and macro photography are productive here
- Sea turtles on the reef platform are often very relaxed around divers
- Carry a dive computer to monitor depth precisely during the deeper perimeter sections

## Safety Considerations

Open-ocean conditions with significant distance from emergency services. Standard remote-diving precautions apply: comprehensive SMB, emergency signaling equipment, air management, and conservative depth and time limits. Nearest hyperbaric facility is Puerto Princesa.

## Photography

Clear open-water conditions with undisturbed marine life provide excellent photography. Sea turtle encounters on the reef platform are photogenic. Wide-angle reef panoramas in good natural light are the primary opportunity.
