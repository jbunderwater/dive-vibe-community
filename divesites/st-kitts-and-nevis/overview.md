## St. Kitts and Nevis

The MV River Taw wreck split in two by Hurricane Hugo, volcanic reef formations with giant barrel sponges, and uncrowded diving in the Lesser Antilles.

## Description

St. Kitts and Nevis offers diverse dive sites ranging from 8 to 40 meters depth, with most diving concentrated on St. Kitts' leeward Caribbean coast. The MV River Taw — a 44-meter freighter that sank in 1981 and was split into two sections by Hurricane Hugo in 1989 — is the signature dive, now heavily encrusted in coral and home to octopus, turtles, and stingrays. The volcanic origins of both islands create unique underwater topography with lava formations colonized by barrel sponges and healthy coral gardens, and dive operators report access to 27 sites across the federation.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: All sites are boat-accessible, with short rides from Frigate Bay and Basseterre on St. Kitts
- **Wreck Diving**: Wreck sites including the MV River Taw (12-15m depth, split into two coral-encrusted sections off Frigate Bay) and the Brassball Wreck
- **Reef Diving**: Reef sites with volcanic rock formations supporting diverse coral growth and giant barrel sponges, including Coconut Tree Reef and Booby Shoals
- **Wall Diving**: Wall site along the Caribbean shelf edge
- **Cave Diving**: Cave site with volcanic swim-throughs

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Robert L. Bradshaw International Airport (SKB) on St. Kitts receives flights from Miami, New York, Charlotte, London, and inter-Caribbean connections. Vance W. Amory International Airport (NEV) on Nevis has regional flights. A regular ferry connects Basseterre to Charlestown, Nevis (45 minutes).
- **Dive Operators**: Pro Divers St. Kitts (family-owned, over 35 years experience, PADI center), Dive Nevis (PADI 5-Star center based in Nevis, also picks up from St. Kitts), and two additional operators covering both islands
- **Accommodation**: St. Kitts offers resorts at Frigate Bay (closest to major dive sites), hotels in Basseterre, and boutique properties. Nevis has plantation-style inns and eco-lodges.
- **Transportation**: Taxis and rental cars on both islands. Inter-island ferry between Basseterre and Charlestown. Dive operators provide hotel pickup and boat transport.
- **Facilities**: PADI-certified dive centers with full equipment rental, certification courses from beginner to advanced. Pro Divers has trained over 2,500 students. Both islands have small but well-equipped operations.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 26-28°C year-round, visibility typically 15-25 meters, calm conditions on the leeward Caribbean coast
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Octopus, southern stingrays, sea turtles (hawksbill and green), lobsters, nurse sharks, barracudas, moray eels, angelfish, and abundant reef fish. The MV River Taw wreck shelters large populations in its broken structure. Giant barrel sponges are a signature feature of the volcanic reef formations.
- **Conservation**: St. Kitts and Nevis have established marine protected areas with mooring buoys at popular sites. The volcanic reef structures create naturally resilient habitats.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: Year-round diving; December to May offers the driest weather and calmest seas. Hurricane season runs June to November.
- **Currency**: Eastern Caribbean Dollar (XCD); US dollars widely accepted
- **Language**: English
- **Safety**: No recompression chamber on St. Kitts or Nevis; nearest chambers on Sint Maarten or Saba. DAN insurance recommended. Most sites are suitable for Open Water certified divers, with deeper wall sites appropriate for advanced divers.
