## MV River Taw Wreck

MV River Taw Wreck is a historic wreck dive in St. Kitts and Nevis, Caribbean.

## Overview

The MV River Taw is St. Kitts' most celebrated wreck dive, a 44-meter freighter that sank in 1981 off Frigate Bay and was split into two sections by Hurricane Hugo in 1989 — the bow and stern now lie about 15 meters apart on a sandy bottom at 12 meters. The wreck is encrusted in coral and teeming with life: yellowtail snappers school around the hull, green turtles rest on the structure during the day, and night dives reveal octopus, eels, and a famously bold resident moray. A nearby sunken bulldozer and vehicle chassis round out one of the Caribbean's most character-filled shallow wrecks.
