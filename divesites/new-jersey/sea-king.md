## Sea King

Sea King is the remains of a 1963 fishing vessel grounding near Barnegat Light — now a landlocked historical site in the dunes of Long Beach Island, not an active dive site.

## Overview

**SITE STATUS NOTE:** The Sea King is not a submerged dive site. The vessel ran aground on Barnegat Shoals on February 12, 1963, and was never recovered. Subsequent decades of coastal sand accumulation — accelerated by jetty extension in the late 1980s — have gradually buried and moved the wreck inland. What was once grounded in shallow water approximately 100 yards offshore is now entombed in the dunes approximately 100 yards inland from the current shoreline on Long Beach Island near 11th Street, Barnegat Light. The vessel's mast — a 30-foot metal spike — still rises visibly above the sand. The coordinates recorded for this entry (39.753°N, 74.102°W) are in the Barnegat Light area and likely reflect the current dune location rather than a submerged site.

This entry should be reviewed for removal from the dive site dataset or reclassified as a historical/beachcombing site. There is no documented submerged diving on the Sea King.

The Sea King's story is well documented by the NJ Maritime Museum and local historical sources: she was a 95-foot commercial scalloper from Atlantic City that ran aground during a nor'easter on February 12, 1963, while towing a decommissioned Navy minesweeper (the 136-foot USS Prescott / PCS-1423) from the Brooklyn Navy Yard to Atlantic City for conversion. The towline fouled in the Sea King's propeller during the storm, disabling the vessel. The Sea King grounded in approximately 10 feet of water off 11th Street. A refloating attempt on February 21 failed when the vessel sank again alongside another boat. She was not recovered and has since been buried by coastal sand dynamics.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The 30-foot mast spike is visible above the dunes and can be found by walking the sand paths from the 11th Street area in Barnegat Light
- The Barnegat Light Museum / NJ Maritime Museum has documented the Sea King's history and holds artifacts from related wrecks in the area
- The nearby Barnegat Inlet dive sites (listed separately) offer legitimate shallow boat and shore diving opportunities in the area
