# Tolten

A 280-foot Chilean cargo freighter torpedoed by a German U-boat in 1942, now resting at 95 feet off Barnegat with intact bow and stern sections draped in anemones and productive lobster grounds.

## Overview
The SS Tolten was originally built in 1938 in Denmark as the *Lotta*, a steel-hulled cargo vessel measuring 280 x 43 feet and 1,858 gross tons. Sailing under the Chilean flag, she was torpedoed by the German submarine U-404 on March 13, 1942, while en route from Baltimore to New York — one of hundreds of Allied vessels sunk off the US East Coast during WWII's "Battle of the Atlantic." She sank with all hands in her then-neutral Chilean crew. Today the Tolten rests in 95 feet of water off Barnegat, New Jersey, with the broken bow rising 20 feet off the bottom and the intact stern offering several penetrable enclosed spaces. The wreck is extensively colonized by hydroids, anemones, and other encrusting marine life, making it visually rewarding despite the cold NJ waters. Offshore conditions at Tolten are often excellent with visibility reaching 30–50 feet.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Offshore Barnegat, New Jersey (Barnegat chart area)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 29 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 30–50 feet on good days; clearer than inshore sites
- **Current**: Moderate; conditions offshore are generally good
- **Best Time**: Late spring through fall

## Marine Life
The Tolten is a consistent lobster producer. The south side of the wreck in and around fallen masts and hull plates is especially productive. The entire wreck is covered in a dense layer of hydroids and anemones. Schools of sea bass, blackfish, and porgies inhabit the structure. The boiler and shaft alley sections attract larger fish.

## Dive Profile
Begin the dive on the prominent bow, which rises clearly off the bottom and is easily navigated. The boiler and machinery section amidships provides interesting exploration. The stern area offers multiple enclosed spaces suitable for experienced wreck divers. The port anchor remains secured in its hawsepipe — a noteworthy detail. Work up the wreck from bow to stern using the hull contours as a guide, finishing your dive on the shallower sections.

## Entry and Exit
Charter boats depart from Barnegat Light Inlet and other NJ inlets for this offshore site. The Tolten is far enough offshore that sea conditions should be checked carefully before departure. Ascend on the anchor line and perform a safety stop at 15 feet.

## Tips and Recommendations
- The south edge along fallen masts and hull plates produces the best lobster sightings
- The port anchor in the hawsepipe is a specific detail worth locating
- Explore the stern enclosed spaces carefully — good photo opportunities inside
- Check visibility at depth — conditions offshore here can be exceptional, making this a rewarding photography site
- A dive light is useful for exploring the stern interior spaces

## Safety Considerations
Advanced divers only. The 95-foot depth requires proper gas planning and a planned decompression or safety stop. The offshore location means longer transit times and variable sea conditions. Always carry a DSMB. Cold water requires a dry suit or 7mm wetsuit.

## Photography
Excellent visibility at this offshore site makes it one of the better NJ photography wrecks. Wide-angle shots of the bow rising from the sand are dramatic. The anemone-covered hull surfaces provide beautiful close-up compositions. Marine life portraits are well-rewarded given the fish population density.
