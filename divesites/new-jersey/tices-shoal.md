# Tices Shoal

A shallow natural shoal in Barnegat Bay near Island Beach State Park, popular for snorkeling, wade-in exploration, and beginner underwater photography — not a traditional scuba dive site.

## Overview
Tices Shoal is a natural underwater shoal located in Barnegat Bay, just off the western shore of Island Beach State Park in Lacey Township, Ocean County. Known primarily as a summer recreational boating and swimming destination, the shoal sits in very shallow water — typically 1–3 meters deep — and can be accessed by small boats that anchor nearby. The name "Tices Shoal" is also used informally to describe the anchoring area and surrounding waters slightly to the east of the shoal itself. The site is a popular weekend gathering spot for local boaters who wade, snorkel, and explore the shallow sandy bottom. Marine life includes small reef fish, crabs, and invertebrates typical of Barnegat Bay's estuarine environment. This is not a traditional scuba diving destination; full scuba gear is not practical at these depths, and the site lacks the wreck structure or reef complexity found at NJ's offshore sites.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Barnegat Bay, near Island Beach State Park, Lacey Township, Ocean County, NJ
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 3 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Variable; often 5–10 feet in Barnegat Bay conditions
- **Current**: Tidal flow; can be moderate in bay channels
- **Best Time**: Summer, weekdays to avoid heavy boat traffic

## Marine Life
Barnegat Bay supports typical Mid-Atlantic estuarine species: blue crabs, spider crabs, small reef fish, flounder, weakfish, and seasonal jellyfish. Seagrass beds near the shoal provide habitat for juvenile fish and invertebrates.

## Dive Profile
Not suited to traditional scuba diving given the 1–3 meter depth. Best explored by snorkeling or freediving. Wading from an anchored boat is the typical mode of exploration.

## Entry and Exit
Access is by small boat from marinas along Barnegat Bay or through Island Beach State Park. No direct shore access to the shoal itself — the park's beach faces the ocean side. Anchor in the designated shoal area and wade or snorkel.

## Tips and Recommendations
- This is a summer recreational site and can be very crowded on weekends with power boats and jet skis
- Use a dive flag even for snorkeling — boat traffic is heavy
- Bring water shoes; the sandy bottom can have shell debris
- For actual scuba diving in the Barnegat area, book a charter to offshore wrecks like Great Isaac or Tolten

## Safety Considerations
Boat traffic is the primary hazard. Always use a dive/snorkel flag and stay visible to boaters. Tidal currents can move through bay channels. Sun protection is important in summer.

## Photography
Shallow water macro photography of crabs, small fish, and invertebrates is possible. The shallow, bright environment suits beginner underwater photographers with entry-level cameras.
