## USS San Diego (ACR-6)

USS San Diego (ACR-6) is a historic wreck dive in New Jersey Shore, North America.

## Overview

The USS San Diego was a 504-foot armored cruiser that sank in July 1918 after striking a mine (or possibly a torpedo from U-156) about 10 miles south of Fire Island — the only major US Navy warship lost in the Atlantic during WWI, going down in just 28 minutes with the loss of 6 crew. She lies upside down at 33 meters, her hull plating and gun turrets visible above the sandy bottom, a distinctive inverted profile that means divers navigate along the keel and between propeller shafts rather than over a conventional deck. Decades of encrustation have transformed the inverted hull into a dense reef supporting tautog, sea bass, and in summer, sand tiger sharks that cruise the length of the wreck.
