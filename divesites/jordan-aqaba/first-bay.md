## First Bay

First Bay is one of Aqaba's most popular and accessible dive sites, located adjacent to the Aqaba Marine Reserve. The site spans two distinct sections — North and South — offering a range of conditions from protected shallow reef to a dramatic sloping wall, making it suitable for divers from beginner to advanced level.

## Overview

First Bay South (at these coordinates, ~29.44°N) features an undulating, kaleidoscopic reef that descends from around 6 meters to 30 meters. The topography varies from sandy patches with coral heads near shore to seamounts covered in black corals and cabbage coral gardens at depth. A permanent navigation line assists divers in the deeper sections. First Bay is one of Aqaba's most dived sites and an important training location, yet it retains excellent biodiversity throughout.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Jordan - Aqaba, adjacent to Aqaba Marine Reserve
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; strong downward currents possible near sandy valley edges (called "Death Valley" by technical divers)
- **Best Time**: Year-round; September–October for best visibility

## Marine Life

Schools of damsels, parrotfish, butterflyfish, fusiliers, and surgeonfish populate the reef. Seamounts covered in black corals attract larger fish seeking feeding grounds. Pelagics cruise the outer reef. Fire coral is present near the entry point — gloves are recommended. Eagle rays and occasional reef sharks have been reported in deeper sections. The site is popular for night diving, when octopi and other invertebrates emerge.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore and descend over sandy bottom with scattered coral heads before reaching the main reef structure between 5–12 meters. The reef slopes to around 30 meters, with the richest coral growth and marine diversity found between 12–18 meters. The deeper section offers black coral gardens and dramatic topography. Technical divers access depths beyond 40 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry directly from the beach. Entry is straightforward, though fire coral near the entry zone requires care. A permanent mooring buoy assists navigation at depth. Strong downward currents near sandy channels should be avoided — stay on the reef structure.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Explore the black coral seamount between 18–25 meters for large predatory fish
- Avoid the sandy valley edges where strong downward currents can develop rapidly
- Excellent for night diving
- Popular training site — expect other divers in the water

## Safety Considerations

Strong downward currents can develop suddenly near sandy channels in the deeper sections. Night divers should carry a primary and backup torch. Always carry a surface marker buoy (SMB).

## Photography

Excellent for reef fish schooling shots and macro work on coral heads. The black coral seamount offers dramatic wide-angle subjects. Night dive photography of invertebrates is a highlight.
