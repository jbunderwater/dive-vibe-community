## Japanese Gardens Coral Reefs

Japanese Gardens Coral Reefs is a reef dive site in Jordan - Aqaba, Middle East.

## Overview

Japanese Gardens takes its name from the large coral heads that resemble ancient Japanese pagodas, rising from a reef that begins in just 1–2 metres of water and extends to a drop into Black Coral Valley at 26–30 metres, where impressive black coral specimens line the wall. Staghorn, brain, and massive table corals dominate the shallower sections alongside resident sea turtles, Red Sea clownfish in anemones, moray eels, and schools of fusiliers, while eagle rays are regularly spotted cruising above the reef. In 2013 corals were relocated here from a threatened area near the Saudi border, and they are now flourishing and integrated into one of Aqaba's most iconic reef systems.

## Marine Life

Divers at this site can expect to encounter whale sharks, manta rays, hammerhead sharks, oceanic whitetip sharks, sea turtles, dolphins, napoleon wrasse, giant moray eels. Additional species commonly sighted include lionfish, clownfish, butterflyfish, anthias.

The most abundant marine life is typically found between 5-15 meters. Plan your dive within your certification limits and allow adequate air for a safety stop.
