## Plane wreck

Plane wreck is a historic wreck dive in Jordan - Aqaba, Middle East.

## Overview

The C-130 Hercules is a four-engine military transport aircraft donated by the Royal Jordanian Air Force and deliberately sunk in November 2017 as part of King Abdullah II's initiative to build Aqaba's artificial reef programme. The plane sits upright at a maximum depth of 17 metres with its cargo doors removed, offering easy access to a cavernous interior roughly 12 metres long, while a skeletal captain figure still occupies the cockpit; a storm in 2020 broke the fuselage into sections but the tail remains intact and soft corals have begun colonising the wings. The site sits just metres from the M42 Duster tank, allowing both to be covered in a single dive in the clear Red Sea waters of the Aqaba Marine Park.
