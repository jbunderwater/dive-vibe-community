## Tank

The Tank is an M42 Duster anti-aircraft gun scuttled in 1999 as part of Aqaba's innovative underwater military museum. It sits in just 5-7 meters of water, making it one of the best beginner wreck dives in the world.

## Overview

The M42 Duster is an American-made self-propelled anti-aircraft gun that served the Royal Jordanian Army before being deliberately sunk in September 1999 by the Royal Marine Conservation Society of Jordan. This artificial reef now hosts soft corals, sponges, and colorful fish. The extreme shallowness (5-7 meters) and easy conditions make it perfect for divers earning their first wreck certification or exploring an unusual military artifact.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Jordan - Aqaba, Red Sea

- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 6 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Current**: Minimal to gentle
- **Best Time**: September to May

## Marine Life

Over 25 years, the tank's metal structure has become colonized with soft corals and sponges. Schools of colorful reef fish—emperorfish, anthias, and fusiliers—swarm around the gun. Small crustaceans including shrimps, crabs, and starfish inhabit the nooks and crannies. Larger species like groupers and occasional lionfish hunt the schooling fish.

## Dive Profile

Descend directly to the tank on the sandy bottom. The extreme shallowness allows for an extended bottom time suitable for photography and detailed exploration. Circumnavigate the gun, examining the coral growth, marine life, and the fascinating mechanical details. The minimal depth allows you to spend the entire dive at this depth with only a brief safety stop needed. This site is ideal for training new open water divers.

## Entry and Exit

Board the dive boat and enter via giant stride or back roll. The short descent to 5-7 meters puts you directly on the tank. After exploration, ascend slowly with a brief safety stop. Deploy your surface marker buoy (SMB) and coordinate with the boat crew for pickup.

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of strong currents, fire coral, lionfish stings in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Strong currents are possible — carry appropriate signaling devices and be prepared to abort the dive if conditions deteriorate. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
