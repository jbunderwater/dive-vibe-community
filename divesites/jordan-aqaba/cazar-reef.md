## Cazar Reef

Cazar Reef is one of Aqaba's classic dive sites, located adjacent to the Aqaba Marine Reserve and in front of the Seastar Watersports beach club. It is one of the most frequently visited night dive sites in Aqaba, combining an approachable profile for Open Water Divers with deeper sections suitable for experienced and technical divers.

## Overview

The site features a wall formation starting in very shallow water (6 meters) and dropping to substantial depths. Beginners follow the top of the wall through cabbage coral fields, while experienced divers descend past black coral trees to garden eels at 30 meters and continue along the reef to large cabbage coral formations near the second mooring buoy. The shallow sandy area in front of the wall is widely used for training, with a marked 30-meter navigation line and a buoyancy training hoop. Night diving at Cazar Reef is particularly popular and well-established.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Jordan - Aqaba, adjacent to Marine Reserve / Seastar Watersports
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Mild; turtles sometimes appear in stronger currents near northern mooring buoy
- **Best Time**: Year-round; popular for night dives

## Marine Life

Cazar Reef supports an exceptionally diverse community. Shallow cabbage coral hosts lunar tail groupers and fusiliers; the wall surface features table corals, black corals, and grey morays. Sandy areas are productive for flounders, Moses soles, and dragonets. Near the deep section: garden eels at 30 meters. The safety stop zone (5 meters) regularly reveals scorpionfish, lionfish, and frogfish among the fringing reef. Aggressive clownfish guard their anemones near the navigation route. Barracuda patrol the outer reef. Hawksbill turtles are occasionally seen near the second mooring buoy.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore and cross the shallow sandy training area (navigation line and buoyancy hoop visible). Reach the wall and choose your depth — the top of the wall at 6–10 meters for beginners through the cabbage coral fields; mid-wall to 18–20 meters for intermediate divers passing black coral trees; deeper to 30 meters for garden eel colonies. Return along the wall at your planned depth before ascending to the 5-meter safety stop zone.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry directly from the Seastar Watersports beach area. The shallow sandy entry flat doubles as a skills training area. Good access for all experience levels.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Night diving here is excellent — one of Aqaba's most popular after-dark sites
- The 5-meter safety stop zone is rich with scorpionfish and lionfish — explore slowly
- Ideal training site with dedicated navigation and buoyancy aids in the sandy shallows
- Look for flounders and dragonets on the sandy bottom during the entry/exit swim

## Safety Considerations

Mild currents. Lionfish and scorpionfish are present near the reef — maintain buoyancy control to avoid accidental contact. Always carry a surface marker buoy (SMB).

## Photography

Night photography is the highlight — scorpionfish, frogfish, and sleeping reef fish. During the day, black coral trees at depth offer dramatic wide-angle frames. Macro subjects include nudibranchs, shrimps, and the giant clams on the return route.
