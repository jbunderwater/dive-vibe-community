## Gorgon 1

Gorgon 1 is named for two large gorgonian sea fans that wave over a trio of coral pinnacles — one of Aqaba's most reliable turtle-spotting sites.

## Overview

The site takes its name from two large gorgonian fan corals located to the west of the three pinnacles (sources note two fans, not one). The three pinnacles rise from around 18 metres up to the surface, each supporting its own community of marine life and cleaning stations. The central pinnacle has a particularly active cleaning station. Beyond the third pinnacle, the reef flattens to a shelf with a house-sized cabbage coral sheltering numerous reef fish including a resident large yellowmouth moray.

Documented marine life at this specific site includes hawksbill turtles (described as Aqaba's best chance for a reliable turtle encounter), fusiliers, schooling banner fish, big-eye emperors, sergeant majors, glassfish, lionfish, stonefish, and moray eels. The site is accessible primarily by boat, though it can also be reached as a long drift from Japanese Gardens. Its shallow depth and calm conditions make it suitable for beginners.
