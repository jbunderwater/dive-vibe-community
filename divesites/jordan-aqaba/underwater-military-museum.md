## Underwater Military Museum

The Underwater Military Museum is the world's first dedicated underwater military museum, opened in July 2019 off South Beach in Aqaba.

## Overview

Inaugurated on 25 July 2019 by the Aqaba Special Economic Zone Authority (ASEZA) in cooperation with the Jordanian Armed Forces, the museum features decommissioned Royal Jordanian Army vehicles arranged in a tactical battle formation on the sandy seabed. Sources report 19 to 21 total pieces; the full inventory includes FV701 Ferret armoured reconnaissance cars, FV101 Scorpion light tanks, Chieftain (Khalid) main battle tanks, a Chieftain armoured recovery vehicle, a FV103 Spartan APC, a FV104 Samaritan armoured ambulance, a Ratel-20 infantry carrier, M1 155mm field guns, Willis MB jeeps, an M42 Duster anti-aircraft gun, and two Bell AH-1F Cobra attack helicopters. The helicopters form the centrepiece of the formation at the deepest section of the site.

Depths range from approximately 5–7 metres for the shallowest pieces (jeeps) up to 25–28 metres for the helicopters and field guns. The site was specifically located on a sandy seabed area with no pre-existing coral to avoid reef damage, and the vehicles were fully cleaned of hazardous materials before deployment. Garden eels, moray eels, scorpionfish, bannerfish, and reef fish have been documented colonising the structures. The site is accessible by boat and is suitable for beginner divers at shallower sections; the deeper helicopter section is best for certified divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Jordan - Aqaba, South Beach
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters (helicopter section; OSM records 20m)
