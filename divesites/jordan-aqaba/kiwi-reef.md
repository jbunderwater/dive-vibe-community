## Kiwi Reef

Kiwi Reef is a popular dive site in southern Aqaba, named after a New Zealand visitor who first explored and documented it. The site's distinctive landscape of over a dozen coral pinnacles rising from dense seagrass beds makes it one of the most varied and rewarding reef dives in the area.

## Overview

The site features extensive seagrass beds along a gentle slope, with a garden of coral pinnacles distributed across the reef between 10 and 20 meters depth. Each pinnacle supports its own distinct ecosystem, creating an unusually high diversity of habitats within a single dive. The reef acts as a nursery for juvenile fish, and moray eel populations here are particularly dense. A mooring buoy marks the area between the pinnacles and the main reef. An advanced feature called the Kiwi Arch exists at technical diving depths beyond 70 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Jordan - Aqaba, southern coastal reef zone
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; popular for night dives

## Marine Life

Kiwi Reef is one of Aqaba's best sites for varied marine life encounters. Regular sightings include moray eels (exceptionally abundant here), lionfish, stingrays, thorny seahorses (in the seagrass), triggerfish, emperor fish, fusiliers, damselfish, and banner fish. Nudibranchs and octopus are found during night dives. Each coral pinnacle hosts its own community of resident fish and invertebrates. Juvenile fish nursery conditions mean unusually high fish densities for the pinnacle size.

## Dive Profile

Enter from shore and descend through seagrass beds, watching for seahorses among the seagrass fronds. The first coral pinnacles appear around 10 meters, with the main cluster between 12–20 meters. Explore each pinnacle individually — they reward thorough investigation. A navigation line and mooring buoy assist orientation. Night diving is particularly rewarding as large populations of lionfish emerge from the pinnacles after dark.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry. The current access point via a dive center platform may be restricted by a gated area — check with local dive operators for the current best access route. Snorkeling is available in calmer conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Allow time to inspect each pinnacle individually — they differ significantly
- Look in the seagrass beds for thorny seahorses during descent
- Night dives are exceptional here — lionfish are particularly active
- This is an excellent training and photography site at shallow to moderate depths

## Safety Considerations

Mild to moderate currents typical. Mooring buoy assists navigation. Always carry an SMB. Night divers need primary and backup torches.

## Photography

Superb macro photography: seahorses, nudibranchs, frogfish, and resting lionfish. Each pinnacle offers close-up reef portrait opportunities. Wide-angle shots of fish schooling around the pinnacles work well in clear conditions.
