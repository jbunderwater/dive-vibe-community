## US Virgin Islands

Three islands spanning beginner-friendly national monument reefs on St. Croix, historic wrecks off St. Thomas, and protected coral gardens around St. John — all with no passport required for US citizens.

## Description

The US Virgin Islands — St. Thomas, St. Croix, and St. John — offer diverse Caribbean diving across three distinct islands. Our database documents 14 sites ranging from 5 to 40 meters depth, including reef sites, wrecks, wall dives, and 1 pinnacle. St. Croix is home to the famous Cane Bay Wall, which starts at just 8 meters and drops over 3,000 meters into the Virgin Islands Trough, as well as Buck Island Reef National Monument with its underwater snorkeling trail through elkhorn coral. St. Thomas offers wreck diving on vessels like the Cartanser Sr and WIT Shoal II, while St. John's Virgin Islands National Park protects pristine reef systems.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Reef Diving**: Reef sites across all three islands. Buck Island Reef National Monument on St. Croix features an underwater trail through elkhorn coral barrier reef, established in 1961 as one of the first protected marine areas in the Caribbean.
- **Wall Diving**: Wall dive sites, headlined by Cane Bay Wall on St. Croix — a shore-entry wall dive starting at 25 feet that drops thousands of feet, with dramatic overhangs and barrel sponges.
- **Wreck Diving**: Wreck sites including the Cartanser Sr (St. Thomas, 11m depth), WIT Shoal II (St. Thomas), Grain Wreck/Suffolk Maid, and the RMS Rhone (a famous wreck shared with the British Virgin Islands).
- **Boat Diving**: Boat-accessible sites across the three islands.
- **Shore Diving**: Shore-accessible sites, including the famous Cane Bay Wall on St. Croix — one of the Caribbean's few shore-entry wall dives.
- **Night Diving**: Available through operators on all three islands, with Frederiksted Pier on St. Croix being a renowned night dive for seahorses and macro life.

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Cyril E. King Airport (STT) on St. Thomas receives direct flights from major US cities including Miami, New York, Atlanta, and Charlotte. Henry E. Rohlsen Airport (STX) on St. Croix also has direct US flights. St. John is reached by ferry from St. Thomas (20 minutes from Red Hook). No passport required for US citizens.
- **Dive Operators**: St. Thomas: Red Hook Dive Center (PADI 5-Star), Aqua Action, Admiralty Dive Center, St. Thomas Dive Center. St. Croix: Cane Bay Dive Shop (PADI 5-Star IDC), St. Croix Ultimate Bluewater Adventures, Sweet Bottom Dive Center.
- **Accommodation**: St. Thomas has the widest range from budget to luxury, concentrated around Charlotte Amalie and Red Hook. St. Croix offers quieter options near Cane Bay and Frederiksted. St. John has eco-resorts and vacation rentals.
- **Transportation**: Taxis, rental cars, and ferries between islands. A car is helpful on St. Croix for accessing Cane Bay and Frederiksted. St. Thomas taxi rates are government-regulated.
- **Facilities**: Full tourist infrastructure across all three islands, with hospitals, dive shops, and equipment rental readily available.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 26-29°C (78-84°F) year-round, warmer in summer. Visibility typically 18-30 meters (60-100 ft), with the best clarity December through May. Mild currents at most sites.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Hawksbill and green sea turtles, nurse sharks, Caribbean reef sharks, spotted eagle rays, southern stingrays, barracuda, tarpon, seahorses (especially at Frederiksted Pier), parrotfish, angelfish, and dense hard coral including elkhorn and staghorn coral at Buck Island. Humpback whales pass through in winter months.
- **Conservation**: Buck Island Reef National Monument (managed by the National Park Service) and Virgin Islands National Park (St. John) protect extensive reef systems. Virgin Islands Coral Reef National Monument adds further protection. Moorings are required at many sites to prevent anchor damage.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: Year-round diving. December through May offers the driest weather, lightest winds, and best visibility. Summer is warmer with fewer crowds. Hurricane season runs August through October.
- **Currency**: US Dollar (USD).
- **Language**: English.
- **Safety**: Recompression chamber available on St. Thomas at the Roy L. Schneider Hospital. DAN insurance recommended. Cane Bay Wall depths exceed recreational limits quickly — monitor depth carefully. Buck Island requires a licensed guide for access.
