## Rhone

The RMS Rhone is one of the top recreational wreck dives in the Caribbean, a historic 1867 shipwreck in British Virgin Islands waters accessible from USVI operators.

## Overview

The RMS Rhone met her fate off Salt Island in the British Virgin Islands during a hurricane in October 1867. Now a protected national park wreck since 1980, the Rhone is a magnificent dive site with both historical significance and abundant marine life. The wreck is large enough to explore extensively without feeling crowded, and while the highlights can be experienced in two dives, one could easily spend a full day exploring this remarkable vessel. The stern sits in 11 meters and the bow in 24 meters, providing excellent diving across multiple depth ranges.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Salt Island, British Virgin Islands, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters (65-130 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

The wreck provides excellent habitat for marine life including groupers, snappers, and other reef fish. Coral and sponge encrustation on the hull creates color and texture. Sea turtles may rest on the deck, while morays and octopuses shelter in wreck crevices.

## Dive Profile

Begin your descent to the wreck top and explore the accessible sections. The stern sits in shallower water, making it suitable for longer bottom times, while the bow extends to deeper depths requiring careful depth management. Plan your dive with adequate reserves for a safety stop. The wreck's size allows for extended exploration, making multiple dives advisable.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from BVI operators or selected USVI operators with BVI diving permits. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- This is one of the most popular scuba diving spots in the BVI—book in advance during peak season
- Bring a dive torch to illuminate wreck interiors and enhance color photography
- Plan multiple dives to fully appreciate this massive wreck
- Allow adequate air supply—bottom time can exceed expectations on large wrecks
- Bring an underwater camera—this site offers excellent wide-angle and historical photography opportunities
- Book with reputable operators experienced with BVI diving

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of boat traffic and moderate currents in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. This site is recommended for experienced divers with wreck-diving experience. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

The wreck structure provides dramatic wide-angle subjects with natural light filtering through openings and gaps in the hull. A torch is essential for illuminating interior details and bringing out colors in the darker sections. Historical and archaeological subjects abound, making this site attractive to photographers interested in wreck documentation.
