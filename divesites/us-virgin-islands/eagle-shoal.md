## Eagle Shoal

Eagle Shoal is a distinctive reef site off St. John featuring a famous open-water cave system and excellent pelagic viewing opportunities.

## Overview

Eagle Shoal is located off the east end of St. John island, between Leduck Island and Ram Head. The heart of the shoal features "The Cathedral," a sun-dappled open cave chamber at 12 meters where divers can kneel on sand and admire glowing fresco-like walls of orange cup corals, fiery sponges, and soft emerald algae. The cathedral takes on an ethereal appearance as sunlight dramatically streams through multiple entryways. The caves are filled with diverse marine life including copper sweepers, jacks, queen triggerfish, and a kaleidoscope of sponges clinging to the walls.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East End, St. John, US Virgin Islands, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters (65-130 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

The site is known for diverse reef fish, including jacks chasing prey through the water column, queen triggerfish, copper sweepers schooling in the caves, and diverse sponge ecosystems. Because Eagle Shoal is exposed to the open ocean on all sides, pelagic visitors are not uncommon—sharks and manta rays gliding by is a thrilling possibility.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the cave features at 12 meters and explore the Cathedral's multiple entryways and interior passages. The site allows for extended exploration of the reef structure and marine life habitats. Plan your dive to manage bottom time and ensure adequate air for a safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Plan your dive to maximize time at The Cathedral's depths
- Bring an underwater camera—the cave formations and lighting create ethereal photographic opportunities
- Watch for pelagic visitors—the open-ocean exposure means shark and ray encounters are possible
- Maintain proper buoyancy to avoid stirring silt inside cave passages
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of boat traffic and currents at this exposed location. The cave features are open chambers suitable for intermediate divers—avoid penetrating deeper into narrow passages without cave-diving training. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

The cave formations with dramatic sunlight streaming through entryways create stunning wide-angle subjects. The colorful sponge walls and coral formations offer excellent macro opportunities. The blue water and light effects make Eagle Shoal particularly photogenic.
