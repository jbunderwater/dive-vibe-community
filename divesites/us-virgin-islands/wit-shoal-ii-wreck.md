## WIT Shoal II Wreck

WIT Shoal II Wreck is a historic wreck dive in US Virgin Islands, Caribbean.

## Overview

The WIT Shoal II is a 210-foot hopper dredge intentionally sunk in 1990 off St. Thomas, sitting upright on the sandy bottom at 21 meters with her superstructure and machinery still largely intact and accessible for penetration diving. The wreck is thoroughly encrusted with orange tube sponges, purple sea fans, and sheet corals, and its large holds and open decks support an exceptionally dense fish population including schools of porkfish, snapper, and French grunts alongside resident moray eels and resting nurse sharks. The WIT Shoal II is a popular second dive site on St. Thomas half-day charters due to the moderate depth and reliably good conditions.
