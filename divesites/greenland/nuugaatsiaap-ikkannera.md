## Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera

Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera is an Arctic rocky reef in the waters west of Disko Bay, Greenland.

## Overview

Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera (alternate name: Nûgâtsiaup íkánera, recorded by Kleinschmidt) is a rocky reef formation in west Greenland at approximately 68°N, in the coastal waters near the Disko Bay region. It descends to around 22 metres over a typical west Greenland rocky substrate.

No site-specific dive reports or operator descriptions have been found for this location. The site name derives from the Greenlandic geographic name for the reef feature — it should not be confused with Nuugaatsiaq, an entirely separate settlement located approximately 350 km to the north in Karrat Fjord (which was evacuated following a landslide-triggered tsunami in June 2017 and has no known recreational dive activity). That tsunami event has no connection to this site.

Diving in this region of Greenland requires a full drysuit, advanced certification, and significant cold-water experience. Water temperatures are typically 2–5°C in summer. Boat access is required.
