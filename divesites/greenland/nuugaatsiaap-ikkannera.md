## Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera

Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera is a rocky reef in northwest Greenland, near the settlement of Nuugaatsiaq in the Uummannaq Fjord area.

## Overview

Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera is tagged in OpenStreetMap as a rocky reef (natural=reef, reef=rock) with an alternative historical name "Nûgâtsiaup íkánera" from Kleinschmidt's records. The charted depth of approximately 22 metres is from OSM data and has not been confirmed by independent dive records.

Nuugaatsiaq was evacuated following a major landslide-triggered tsunami on 17 June 2017, which generated a wave reaching over 90 metres up the adjacent hillside and resulted in four deaths. The settlement has been largely abandoned since that event. No dive surveys of the underwater topography changes caused by the 2017 landslide have been found in any source, and no claims about post-tsunami underwater conditions are made here.

At depths consistent with northwest Greenland rocky reef sites, the substrate would support cold-water kelp forests, sea anemones, sea urchins, and typical Arctic cold-water invertebrates. No diver accounts or operator records mentioning this specific site have been found. Access to this area is remote; the nearest towns are Uummannaq and Ilulissat. Full expedition planning and drysuit equipment are required.
