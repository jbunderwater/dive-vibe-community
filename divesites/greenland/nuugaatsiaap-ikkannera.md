## Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera

Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera is a rocky reef recorded near the former settlement of Nuugaatsiaq in northwestern Greenland, in the Uummannaq Fjord basin.

## Overview

Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera is an OSM-recorded reef feature near Nuugaatsiaq, a settlement that was evacuated and abandoned following a June 17, 2017 landslide-triggered megatsunami in Karrat Fjord. The tsunami killed four residents and destroyed eleven buildings; the settlement remains abandoned as of 2023 due to ongoing landslide risk. Access to this area now requires expedition logistics with no local support infrastructure.

The reef reaches depths to approximately 22 metres. No dive operator or published source has been identified that describes diving at this specific named site, before or after the 2017 event. The claim in previous versions of this page that "underwater topography reflects the dramatic geology" of the tsunami has been removed — no dive survey of the area post-2017 has been documented in available sources.
