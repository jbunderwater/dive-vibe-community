## Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera

Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera is a submerged rocky reef near the former settlement of Nuugaatsiaq in northwestern Greenland.

## Overview

Nuugaatsiaap Ikkannera is a rock reef formation in the Uummannaq Fjord basin of northwestern Greenland, in waters near the former settlement of Nuugaatsiaq. The rocky seabed drops to approximately 22 metres in cold, clear Arctic water.

Nuugaatsiaq itself was evacuated and permanently closed after a catastrophic landslide on 17 June 2017 sent a megatsunami into Karrat Fjord: the landslide mobilised an estimated 35–58 million cubic metres of rock that fell approximately 1,000 metres into the fjord, generating a wave exceeding 90 metres near the source and reaching Nuugaatsiaq at a run-up height of around 9 metres, killing four people and destroying approximately half the settlement's buildings. As of 2023 both Nuugaatsiaq and the adjacent settlement of Illorsuit remain abandoned due to continuing landslide risk. This geological context — a region of steep, recently destabilised terrain — shapes the dramatic underwater topography of the surrounding fjord system.

Cold-water rocky reefs across this part of Greenland are characterised by kelp-covered substrates with sea urchins, crabs, and Arctic fish species. No dive-specific documentation has been found for this specific site.
