## Ikkanneq

Ikkanneq is an Arctic rocky reef dive site in the Disko Bay region of western Greenland.

## Overview

Ikkanneq (alternate name: Íkáneq, recorded by Kleinschmidt) is a rocky reef formation in the waters of Disko Bay, positioned north of Ilulissat at approximately 68.8°N. The site reaches around 18 metres depth over a typical west Greenland rocky substrate. Ikkanneq means "reef" or "shallow underwater ledge" in Greenlandic — the name is a general geographic descriptor rather than a reference to a specific named dive site.

No site-specific dive reports, operator descriptions, or forum accounts have been found for this location. Regional Greenland diving sources document kelp forests and cold-water invertebrate communities as the characteristic fauna of west Greenland rocky reefs, but specific marine life cannot be attributed to this site without source confirmation.

Full drysuit, advanced certification, and significant cold-water experience are required. The Ilulissat area is the most accessible part of Disko Bay for organised diving; the operator Sirius Greenland runs liveaboard dive trips in this region during summer (July–September).
