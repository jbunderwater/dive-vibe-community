## T33 57-763 wreck

The T33 57-763 is a USAAF military aircraft (T-33 Shooting Star trainer jet) that crashed in December 1968 near Sondre Strømfjord in Central West Greenland. This historically significant memorial wreck lies on the seafloor at approximately 45 meters depth.

## Overview

The T33 57-763 is an advanced Arctic wreck dive suitable only for experienced divers with cold-water and technical certifications. The aircraft wreck is remarkably preserved on the sandy seafloor of Sondre Strømfjord. This memorial dive honors the crew and represents one of Greenland's notable aircraft wreck sites. The site requires full Arctic diving gear including a thick dry suit, undersuits, and advanced planning for extreme conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Sondre Strømfjord, Central West Greenland (67°N, 50°W)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8-15 meters (26-50 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 0 to 3°C (32-37°F) — **DRY SUIT REQUIRED**
- **Current**: Variable, tidal influence in fjord
- **Best Time**: June to September (brief Arctic summer only)

## Marine Life

Arctic marine species include ringed seals, bearded seals, kelp forests, polar cod, Arctic char, sea anemones, and occasional narwhals in summer months. The wreck may host bioluminescent organisms and cold-adapted invertebrates. **Surface hazard alert**: Polar bears are a significant risk in remote Greenland fjords.

## Dive Profile

Descend carefully to the wreck at approximately 35-45 meters. This is a deep dive requiring conservative bottom time planning and mandatory decompression obligations. Allow significant air reserve for the ascent with safety stops at 9 and 3 meters. The seafloor is sandy and stable. Strong currents can be present depending on tidal conditions. Total dive time should be carefully planned with technical diving principles.

## Entry and Exit

Boat entry from local operators in Sondre Strømfjord. Standard mooring at wreck if available, otherwise careful anchor placement. Ascent should follow a planned decompression schedule with safety stops. Exit at boat. Surface conditions must be monitored constantly for wildlife and weather changes.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Arrange with local Greenlandic dive operators well in advance (very few exist)
- Ensure redundant air supplies (sidemount or twinset configuration recommended)
- Check extended weather forecast — conditions deteriorate rapidly in Arctic
- Mandatory polar bear awareness briefing before entering water
- Carry surface signaling equipment (dye marker, whistle)
- Hydrospace/thick dry suit with quality undersuits essential
- Plan for short seasonal window (late June to early September only)

## Safety Considerations

**EXTREME HAZARDS**: Frigid water temperature (0-3°C), severe hypothermia risk, nitrogen narcosis and hypoxic conditions at 45m depth, mandatory decompression with serious DCS risk, extreme isolation with very limited rescue resources, potential ice conditions near fjord, variable strong currents, and **polar bear presence on surface**. This is an expert-only dive requiring Advanced cold-water certification, technical wreck training, and experienced Arctic guide. Never attempt without proper certifications and professional guidance. Treat as remote expedition diving.
