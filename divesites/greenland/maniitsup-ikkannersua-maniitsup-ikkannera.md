## Maniitsup Ikkannersua, Maniitsup Ikkannera

Maniitsup Ikkannersua (also referred to as Maniitsup Ikkannera) is a shallow underwater bank in west Greenland waters near Maniitsoq, known in Danish as Sukkertoppen Banke.

## Overview

The OSM record tags this feature as a natural reef bank (natural=reef, source:type=BANK), Danish name Sukkertoppen Banke, in waters off Maniitsoq on Greenland's west coast. Maniitsoq (formerly Sukkertoppen) is a town known for its intricate network of natural channels among the surrounding islands. The bank's recorded depth of approximately 18 metres is from OSM data and has not been confirmed by independent dive records.

Shallow banks in this part of west Greenland typically feature rocky substrates with kelp beds, sea urchins, starfish, crabs, and cold-water invertebrates. Atlantic wolffish and lumpsucker are documented coastal species in this region. No site-specific dive operator reports or diver accounts mentioning this bank by name have been found.

Diving here requires drysuit equipment and cold-water regulators. Tidal currents can be strong around the island channels near Maniitsoq. Water temperatures are typically 0–6°C during the June–September season. No shore access is documented; boat is standard.
