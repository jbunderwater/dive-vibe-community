## Maniitsup Ikkannersua, Maniitsup Ikkannera

Maniitsup Ikkannersua and Maniitsup Ikkannera are submerged rocky bank formations in the waters surrounding Maniitsoq on Greenland's west coast.

## Overview

These two features — sometimes referred to together as the Sukkertoppen Banke in Danish — are submerged rocky banks in the waters around Maniitsoq (historically Sukkertoppen), a town on Greenland's west coast known for its network of natural channels among small islands. The banks reach a maximum depth of approximately 18 metres and sit in nutrient-rich cold water typical of this stretch of west Greenland coast.

No dive-specific documentation has been found for these sites. The rocky underwater terrain of the broader Maniitsoq area supports kelp-draped boulders with cold-water invertebrates, consistent with west Greenland cold-water environments generally. Humpback and minke whales are known to frequent the coastal waters of this region during summer; these animals are observed at the surface.

**No site-specific marine life observations have been confirmed for these sites through independent sources. Regional cold-water fauna (kelp, crabs, sea urchins, starfish, amphipods) applies across this area.**
