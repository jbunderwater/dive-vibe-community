## Aasiaat Nugguat

Aasiaat Nugguat is a rocky reef diving site in Disko Bay, West Greenland. The site features jagged Arctic rock formations and is one of the accessible Disko Bay sites for advanced Arctic diving. Located at 68.68°N, this site epitomizes high-Arctic fjord diving with extreme environmental conditions.

## Overview

Aasiaat Nugguat offers advanced Arctic diving among Greenland's remote Disko Bay islands. The site features underwater rock formations characteristic of fjord diving environments. Divers encounter pristine Arctic waters with dense kelp forests, cold-adapted fish species, and occasionally marine mammals including seals and narwhals. This is expedition-style diving requiring specialized training, equipment, and local knowledge.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Disko Bay, Aasiaat area, West Greenland (68.68°N, 52.89°W)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-18 meters (33-60 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 0 to 4°C (32-39°F) — **DRY SUIT REQUIRED**
- **Current**: Variable, tidal and fjord currents
- **Best Time**: June to September (Arctic summer only)

## Marine Life

Disko Bay is renowned for Arctic wildlife. Divers encounter ringed seals, bearded seals, narwhals (in summer), beluga whales, and occasionally walruses. Beneath the surface: dense kelp forests, polar cod, Arctic char, sea anemones, sea urchins, starfish, and cold-adapted crustaceans. The ecosystem thrives in nutrient-rich Arctic waters.

## Dive Profile

Descend through the kelp forest zone (0-8m) which can be dense and complex. Navigate carefully through rocky formations to 15-20 meters. Most marine life concentrates around rock crevices and kelp bases at 5-15 meters. Conservative dive planning is essential in Arctic conditions. Allow adequate air for safety stop at 5 meters. Be prepared for variable visibility and surge from tidal flow.

## Entry and Exit

Boat-based entry from Aasiaat settlement (small Greenlandic town). Typical boat drops divers at rock formation, with surface interval between dives. Descent along rock formation or kelp line. Ascent conservative with safety stop. Exit via boat. Surface conditions require constant monitoring for wind, ice, and wildlife.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Arrange with Arctic dive operators in Aasiaat (very limited options)
- Double-check dry suit integrity before trip
- Heavy thermal undersuit system mandatory
- Bring redundant gauges and underwater lights
- Plan for only one or two dives per day due to extreme conditions
- Study Disko Bay fjord hazards and currents before diving
- Polar bear awareness training required before entering water

## Safety Considerations

**EXTREME CONDITIONS**: Ice floe hazards, severe cold (0-4°C), hypothermia risk, strong tidal currents, kelp entanglement risk, isolated location with minimal rescue resources, and **polar bear surface presence**. This is Advanced-only Arctic diving requiring cold-water technical training, expedition experience, and professional guide. Dry suit failure = rapid incapacitation. Never dive without redundant safety systems and expert local guidance.
