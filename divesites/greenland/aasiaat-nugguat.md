## Aasiaat Nugguat

Aasiaat Nugguat is a rocky reef site in the waters near Aasiaat, a town situated on an island in the southern part of Disko Bay, west Greenland.

## Overview

Aasiaat Nugguat lies in the waters around Aasiaat at a charted depth of approximately 20 metres (OSM data; unconfirmed by independent dive sources). As a rocky Arctic reef, the substrate is consistent with the kelp forest terrain documented at comparable sites throughout west Greenland: large granite boulders draped in kelp, with cold-water invertebrates including sea urchins, starfish, crabs, sea cucumbers, and anemones occupying the rock surfaces. Atlantic wolffish (locally called "catfish") and lumpsucker are among the fish species recorded in west Greenland's rocky reef habitats.

Aasiaat is a known departure point for whale-watching boat trips in southern Disko Bay, where humpback and fin whales feed during summer. While cetacean encounters cannot be guaranteed at this specific reef, the broader area is active feeding habitat during the June–September season. Visibility in Disko Bay can reach 10–20 metres in calm conditions, though glacial meltwater can reduce it significantly.

Diving here requires a drysuit, cold-water regulators, and guide support. No shore dive access is documented; boat access is standard for reef sites in this area.
