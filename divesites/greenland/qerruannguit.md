## Qerruannguit

Qerruannguit is a shallow cold-water rocky reef in the Disko Bay region of western Greenland.

## Overview

Qerruannguit is a submerged rock formation in western Greenland's Disko Bay, tagged in OpenStreetMap as a natural rocky reef. The site reaches approximately 16 metres depth in near-freezing water. Rocky substrates at this depth in Disko Bay are typically draped in kelp, with cold-water invertebrates such as sea anemones, sea urchins, amphipods, and nudibranchs inhabiting the algae-covered boulders — conditions consistent with documented diving in the broader Disko Bay and Sisimiut area.

The Ilulissat Icefjord, a UNESCO World Heritage site, lies within the same broader region. Icebergs calving from glaciers are part of the surface environment throughout the Disko Bay area, though the 16-metre maximum depth at this site limits the iceberg-diving experience.
