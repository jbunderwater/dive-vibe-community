## Little Africa Reef

Little Africa Reef is a shallow coral reef off the north side of Loggerhead Key, named because its coral formations, seen from above, trace the outline of the African continent.

## Overview

Off the north side of Loggerhead Key rests one of the Dry Tortugas' most accessible reef dives. The coral formations at Little Africa are shallow — around 20 feet at their deepest — and the site's sheltered position makes it consistently calm, suitable for both snorkelers and beginner divers. The park's NPS page and the Loggerhead Key guide both identify this as one of the finest reef experiences in the park, valued for its diversity of coral heads, soft corals, and abundant tropical fish in gin-clear water. The Africa-shaped outline of the reef structure is best appreciated by hovering above and looking down before descending.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North side of Loggerhead Key, Dry Tortugas National Park (~3 miles west of Garden Key)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 6 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 18–37 meters (60–120 feet)
- **Current**: Generally light and sheltered on the north shore
- **Best Time**: May to September
- **Water Temperature**: 26–29°C (79–84°F) summer

## Marine Life

The NPS identifies the following marine life at Little Africa specifically: juvenile barracuda, spiny lobster, various coral heads (stony and gorgonian), soft corals, and tropical fish. The site's protected national park status means fish are notably approachable. No site-specific reports of nurse sharks, tarpon, or goliath grouper at this location were found in reviewed sources; those species are documented in the park generally but not confirmed as resident at Little Africa itself.

## Dive Profile

Little Africa is a shallow patch reef in approximately 20 feet of water. The coral formations, when viewed from above, approximate the shape of the African continent — this is the origin of the site's name. Descend to the coral structures and explore the heads at a relaxed pace. The shallow depth allows extended bottom time and excellent natural lighting for photography. No safety stop is required at these depths. The site's calm, sheltered north-side location makes it one of the most comfortable dives in the park.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by boat only — the park is 70 miles west of Key West. Little Africa is off Loggerhead Key, approximately 3 miles west of Garden Key (Fort Jefferson). Visitors arriving on the Yankee Freedom III ferry (which docks at Garden Key) would need a separate vessel to reach Loggerhead Key. Private boaters and dive charters can anchor on sandy bottom clear of coral. No air fills are available in the park; arrive with full tanks.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Hover above the reef first to appreciate the Africa-shaped coral layout before descending
- The shallow depth and calm conditions make this an excellent first or final dive of the day
- Snorkelers can explore the same formations as divers
- Arrive with a full tank — no facilities in the park
- Loggerhead Key's lighthouse (1858) is visible from the water and worth the boat ride alone

## Safety Considerations

While relatively sheltered, the Dry Tortugas are 70 miles from Key West with no onsite emergency support. Use the buddy system, display a dive flag, and carry a surface marker buoy. Watch for fire coral and jellyfish. The park has no cell service; carry a marine VHF radio. The nearest hyperbaric chamber is in Key West.
