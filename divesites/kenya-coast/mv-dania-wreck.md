## MV Dania Wreck

MV Dania Wreck is a historic wreck dive in Kenya Coast, Africa.

## Overview

The MV Dania is Kenya's best wreck dive — a 77-meter Norwegian-built cargo vessel that worked East African trade routes before being deliberately scuttled in 2002 inside Mombasa Marine Park, resting upright on a sandy bottom at 30 meters with the shallowest structures starting at 12 meters. A decade of coral and sponge colonization has transformed the hull into a thriving artificial reef, with resident groupers, lionfish, and barracuda patrolling the upper deck and bridge, while morays and lobsters claim the darker recesses. The dive begins along an ascent line to the bow and works toward the intact engine room, offering exploration options for both recreational and deeper-certified divers.
