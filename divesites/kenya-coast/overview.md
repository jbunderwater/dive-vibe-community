## Kenya Coast

East African coral gardens within protected marine parks, where whale sharks, manta rays, and sea turtles share warm Indian Ocean waters with over 360 species of reef fish.

## Description

The Kenya Coast database documents dive sites across the country's marine parks and reef systems, with depths ranging from 15 to 30 meters. Diving is concentrated around five marine parks -- Kisite-Mpunguti, Watamu, Mombasa, Malindi, and Kiunga -- stretching along Kenya's Indian Ocean coastline. The majority of sites are boat-access dives (9 of 10) featuring reef formations, wall dives, caves, and one notable wreck.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: Sites accessed by boat, typical for Kenya's reef systems which lie offshore beyond the fringing reef
- **Shore Diving**: Shore-accessible site
- **Reef Diving**: Coral reef sites featuring hard and soft coral gardens with over 45 coral species documented in Kisite-Mpunguti alone
- **Wall Diving**: Wall dive with reef drop-offs characteristic of the deeper sections along the coast
- **Cave Diving**: Cave or swim-through sites within the reef structures
- **Wreck Diving**: Wreck dive

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Moi International Airport (MBA) in Mombasa is the main gateway, with direct flights from Nairobi (approximately 1 hour) and seasonal international charters. Malindi Airport (MYD) serves the northern dive areas around Watamu and Malindi. Diani Beach is approximately 30 km south of Mombasa
- **Dive Operators**: Diving the Crab operates multiple bases along Diani Beach and is the largest operator in the region. Ocean Tribe in Diani is an SSI centre and Disabled Divers International training facility. Baracuda Diving Kenya has operated since 1978. Peponi Divers covers Mombasa-area sites
- **Accommodation**: Diani Beach, Watamu, and Malindi all have extensive resort and hotel options ranging from budget guesthouses to luxury beach resorts. Many dive operators are based at or affiliated with beachfront hotels
- **Transportation**: Matatus (minibuses) and taxis connect Mombasa to Diani Beach (approximately 45 minutes). Watamu is about 2 hours north of Mombasa by road. Private transfers can be arranged through dive operators
- **Facilities**: Dive centres offer full equipment rental, PADI and SSI courses from beginner to instructor level, and Nitrox at select locations

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperatures range from 25 to 29 degrees C year-round, warmest in March-April and coolest in July-August. Visibility varies from 10 to 30 meters depending on season, with best clarity from October to March during the northeast monsoon (Kaskazi) season
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Kisite-Mpunguti Marine Park alone hosts 360 documented fish species and 45 coral types. Key encounters include whale sharks (November-February), manta rays, hawksbill and green sea turtles, whitetip reef sharks, moray eels, seahorses, sailfish, and large schools of pelagic fish. Humpback whales pass through from July to October during their annual migration
- **Conservation**: Kenya's marine parks system protects key reef areas with regulated access. Kisite-Mpunguti Marine National Park (15 sq km) and Watamu Marine National Park are the primary protected diving areas. Park entry fees support conservation efforts. Turtle nesting programs operate along the Watamu coastline

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: October to March offers the calmest seas and best visibility during the northeast monsoon. Whale sharks and manta rays are most frequent from November to February. July to October brings humpback whale sightings but rougher seas during the southeast monsoon (Kusi). April-June can bring reduced visibility due to seasonal rains
- **Currency**: Kenyan Shilling (KES)
- **Language**: English and Swahili are both widely spoken
- **Safety**: Currents can be strong at some sites, particularly during tidal changes and monsoon transitions. Stonefish are present on sandy bottoms -- reef-safe footwear recommended for shore entries. Marine park regulations require diving with a licensed operator. Malaria prophylaxis is recommended for the coastal region
