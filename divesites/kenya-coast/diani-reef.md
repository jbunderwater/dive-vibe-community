## Diani Reef

Diani Reef is the primary fringing reef off Diani Beach, Kenya's most developed south coast resort area, with over 30 named dive sites ranging from beginner coral gardens to an advanced wreck and wall sites, making it Kenya's most accessible base for variety diving.

## Overview

Diani Beach stretches approximately 17 kilometres along Kenya's south coast, and the fringing reef running parallel to the shoreline sits 300–600 metres offshore. Over 30 named dive sites operate in the Diani area. The reef crest begins at 3–5 metres and drops to around 16 metres over a broad, sloping profile with diverse hard coral formations. Multiple PADI dive centres operate from the beach, and the short boat transit (5–15 minutes) makes this a very accessible base.

Named sites documented in the area include: Papa Mashilingi (average 12m, gentle drift dive named for the Swahili word for whale shark, abundant corals and schooling fish), Galu Reef (macro site — ornate ghost pipefish, seahorses, frogfish, leaf scorpionfish confirmed), Kinondo Reef (10–20m, large pelagics including barracuda and dolphins), and the MV Alpha Funguo Wreck (48-metre fishing trawler, the first purpose-sunk wreck on Kenya's coast, scuttled in 2002 by Diani Marine, sitting at 17–28 metres, Advanced divers).

## Site Information

- **Location**: Diani Beach, South Coast, Kenya
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 12–20 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to March (northeast monsoon)

## Marine Life

Confirmed at Diani's reef sites: sea turtles (green and hawksbill, regular on the reef), ornate ghost pipefish, seahorses, frogfish, and leaf scorpionfish at Galu Reef specifically. Large pelagics including barracuda and dolphins at Kinondo Reef. Whale sharks reported October to March (the Papa Mashilingi site is named for them). Moray eels, lionfish, and reef fish throughout the reef. The "secret turtle garden" outside the reef is a documented turtle congregation area according to operators.

*Marine life above derives from operator site descriptions for named individual sites where possible, and from regional dive operator sources for the reef generally.*

## Dive Profile

The main fringing reef follows a gentle slope from 3–5 metres at the crest to 14–16 metres at the base. Currents on the Diani reef are generally light to moderate; Papa Mashilingi offers a gentle drift experience. The broad slope profile allows unhurried navigation.

## Entry and Exit

Multiple PADI dive centres operate along Diani Beach, including Diani Marine, Ocean Tribe, and Diani Scuba Duka. Short boat transits of 5–15 minutes. Most are walking distance from major resort hotels. Morning dives typically offer better visibility than afternoon.

## Safety Considerations

Low-risk beginner diving on the main fringing reef. SMB required on ascent due to boat traffic in peak resort season. Fire coral and sea urchin contact are the main physical hazards. Night diving is popular and accessible from this beach.
