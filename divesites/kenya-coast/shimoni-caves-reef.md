## Shimoni Caves Reef

Shimoni Caves Reef is a cave and cavern dive near Shimoni in Kisite-Mpunguti Marine National Park, descending to a sandy bottom at around 20 metres with a chimney exit that brings divers up to 10 metres, and resident groupers and snappers in the cave interior.

## Overview

The Shimoni area provides access to an underwater cave system within Kisite-Mpunguti Marine National Park, where fishing is prohibited and reef protection is enforced. The dive site is distinct from the famous Shimoni Caves on land — those are terrestrial coral limestone caverns with slave trade history, approximately 7 kilometres long, now a show cave open for walking tours. The underwater dive site involves descending to a sandy bottom at roughly 20 metres, exploring the reef along the cave system, and exiting through a chimney that ascends to about 10 metres on the reef top.

The cave interior shelters large groupers and snappers that are visible toward the back of the cave. The open water around the cave entrance, outside the protected park, is where dolphins are occasionally sighted, along with barracuda and eels.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Shimoni, Kisite-Mpunguti Marine National Park, Kenya
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Depth Range**: 10–20 meters (chimney exit at 10m)
- **Best Time**: November to March (northeast monsoon)

## Marine Life

Confirmed species in the cave: large groupers and snappers inhabit the cave interior. Moray eels are reported in the surrounding reef structure. The open water near the cave entrance may yield sightings of dolphins, barracuda, and reef sharks. The broader Kisite-Mpunguti park supports over 360 fish species and 45 coral species. Species not specifically confirmed at this site have been omitted from this description to avoid attributing regional fauna to the specific cave location.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sandy bottom at approximately 20 metres and navigate the reef toward the cave entrance. Explore the cave openings — the interiors are cavern-style with natural light penetration rather than true technical cave diving. The chimney ascent from the cave to the reef top at 10 metres is the navigational highlight. Complete the dive on the reef top before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Shimoni jetty. Kenya Coastal Divers is a PADI-certified dive centre based in Shimoni offering guided dives here. KWS marine park fees apply.

## Safety Considerations

The chimney navigation requires controlled buoyancy and should not be attempted by divers without cave or cavern orientation. True cave penetration in enclosed overhead environments requires specialist training. The Kisite-Mpunguti waters can have mild to moderate currents depending on conditions. Dive with a local guide familiar with the cave layout.
