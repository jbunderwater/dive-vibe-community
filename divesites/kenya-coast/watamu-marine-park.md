## Watamu Marine Park

Watamu Marine Park is a reef dive site in Kenya Coast, Africa.

## Overview

Watamu Marine National Park is Africa's first marine park, established in 1968 and forming part of a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve on Kenya's northern coast, protecting some of the healthiest and most diverse coral reef systems in East Africa with over 600 fish species and 110 coral species within its 10 square kilometers. The park's 20 dive sites cover depths from 5 to 30 meters and include coral gardens, canyon drop-offs, overhangs, and the shallow Tewa Caves, with sea turtles and Napoleon wrasse encountered on nearly every dive. Whale sharks visit seasonally during the northeast monsoon, and all sites have been on permanent mooring buoys for years, keeping the reefs in exceptional condition.
