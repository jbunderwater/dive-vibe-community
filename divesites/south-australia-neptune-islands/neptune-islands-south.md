## Neptune Islands South

Deep submerged cage diving site with great white sharks along the kelp seabed. Requires PADI Open Water certification minimum.

## Overview

Neptune Islands South is an advanced submerged cage diving site requiring PADI Open Water certification or equivalent. The site features deep cage diving to approximately 18–20 meters along the kelp-covered seabed in the southern Neptune Islands group (sources report depths between 18 and 21 meters). This site offers intimate deep encounters with great white sharks in their natural hunting habitat above seal breeding areas.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South Neptune Islands, 40 nautical miles south of Port Lincoln, South Australia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Depth**: 18–20 meters where cage rests above or on the kelp-covered seabed (sources report depths between 18 and 21 meters)
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-20 meters (50-65 feet)
- **Current**: Generally mild in sheltered bay areas
- **Water Temperature**: 14-22°C (57-72°F) — coldest June-August, warmest December-May
- **Best Time**: May-October for peak great white activity; submerged cages year-round viable

## Marine Life

Great white sharks (males year-round, large females arriving approximately April–August), Australian sea lions, long-nosed fur seals. At ocean-floor cage depth, divers also encounter southern eagle rays, giant blue wrasse, and trevally passing through, with sea urchins and kelp-forest invertebrates visible on the seabed. These species are specifically confirmed by Rodney Fox Shark Expeditions as regularly encountered from the ocean-floor cage. Note: cuttlefish are not confirmed at Neptune Islands — their well-known SA aggregation occurs at Whyalla in Spencer Gulf.

## Dive Profile

Certified divers wear 5-7mm wetsuits and descend via cage to approximately 18–20 meters where the cage rests on or hovers above the kelp-covered seabed. Typical dive time 30-45 minutes per rotation with 3-4 cage cycles. Divers breathe from scuba air supplies while remaining within the secure cage structure. Shark activity and seabed ecosystem visibility provide primary focus for this deep cage dive experience.

## Entry and Exit

Divers must be certified PADI Open Water (or CMAS 1 Star equivalent) minimum. Entry via cage platform descent into water; controlled descent in cage to depth. Pre-dive briefing covers equalization techniques, air management, and emergency procedures. Exit via controlled ascent in cage with safety stop protocols. Professional divemasters monitor all divers throughout the experience.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Valid PADI Open Water or equivalent certification required (bring card)
- Book with Rodney Fox Shark Expeditions for this deep cage diving specialty
- Wear a 5-7mm semi-dry or full suit; water is 14-22°C year-round
- Experienced deep divers will maximize bottom time and seabed observation
- Peak activity May-October; winter months (June-August) see large females
- Plan 2-3 cage rotations for optimal experience; budget 3-5 hours total operation
- Bring motion sickness medications if sensitive; sea state can affect comfort

## Safety Considerations

Cold water requires proper thermal protection and buddy awareness. Submerged cage diving at 18–20 meters demands standard deep diving protocols: equalization awareness, nitrogen narcosis management, air consumption monitoring, and safety stop compliance. Operators provide professional supervision and emergency protocols. Always verify operator licensing, equipment maintenance records, and insurance before booking. Maintain current certifications and log cold-water dive experience.

## Photography

Submerged cage diving offers dramatic encounters with great white sharks against the seabed backdrop. Wide-angle photography captures shark behavior; macro subjects include kelp forest invertebrates and cold-water fish. Lighting challenges require camera settings optimized for low-light, high-contrast shark silhouettes against ambient seabed light.
