## Great White Shark Cage Dive Site

Beginner-friendly surface cage diving site perfect for non-divers and uncertified visitors.

## Overview

Great White Shark Cage Dive Site is the most accessible Neptune Islands diving experience, offering surface cage encounters with great white sharks in approximately 2 meters of water. No diving certification or prior water experience required. All participants breathe surface-supplied air or snorkel within the secure floating cage. Sheltered Main or Action Bay locations provide calm conditions ideal for non-divers and families.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Main Bay or Action Bay, Neptune Islands, 40 nautical miles south of Port Lincoln, South Australia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Depth**: 2 meters (cage floats at surface)
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-20 meters (50-65 feet)
- **Current**: Negligible in sheltered bay
- **Water Temperature**: 14-22°C (57-72°F) — coldest June-August, warmest December-May
- **Best Time**: May-October for peak shark activity; year-round viable

## Marine Life

Great white sharks (males year-round, occasional large females April-August), Australian sea lions, long-nosed fur seals, cold-water fish species, seabirds.

## Dive Profile

Non-divers and divers alike board a floating surface cage secured to the dive boat. Breathing air is provided via snorkel or surface-supplied regulator inside the cage. The cage remains at approximately 2 meters depth with natural light and clear shark visibility. Cage dive duration typically 60-90 minutes with rotating groups accessing different cage sections. No special skills, equalization, or decompression procedures required.

## Entry and Exit

Open to all ages and swimming abilities. No diving certification needed. Entry via secure boat platform onto cage. Breathing apparatus (snorkel or supplied air) provided. Exit is simple return to boat via same platform. Family-friendly with professional staff assistance available. Typical operation time 4-6 hours including safety briefing and multiple cage rotations.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Perfect for non-divers, families, and visitors without certification
- Book 3-7 days in advance with licensed operators
- Wear 5mm wetsuit minimum; 7mm semi-dry recommended for all-day comfort in 14-22°C water
- Best shark activity typically early morning or during bait deployment
- May-October offers peak encounters; year-round options available
- Bring waterproof camera for iconic great white photographs
- Check weather forecasts; rough seas occasionally restrict cage operations
- No prior swimming experience required; minimum swimming ability helpful

## Safety Considerations

Surface cage diving prioritizes safety with secure cage construction, professional guide supervision, and no decompression or equalization requirements. Great whites are naturally cautious apex predators; cage encounters are conducted safely. Hypothermia risk in cold 14-22°C water — proper thermal protection essential. Seasickness risk on boat; bring anti-nausea medication if sensitive. Always verify operator licensing, safety records, and insurance before booking. Follow all operator briefing instructions.
