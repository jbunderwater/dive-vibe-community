## Biatabang

Biatabang is a wall dive site off Ternate island, near the village of Ternate Selatan (southern Ternate), North Maluku, Indonesia.

## Overview

Biatabang is a boat-access wall dive on the flanks of Ternate — an active volcanic island sitting just off the northwest coast of Halmahera. The underwater terrain features a wall and overhangs covered in soft corals and sponges, supporting a notable mix of large pelagic species and macro life. The site is characterised by strong currents and cold thermoclines at depth, making it appropriate for experienced divers. It is listed on PADI's dive site database and featured on liveaboard itineraries that combine Ternate, Tidore, and western Halmahera sites.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off southern Ternate (Ternate Selatan), Ternate Island, North Maluku, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 metres

## Marine Life

Large pelagic species documented at the site include Napoleon wrasse (*Cheilinus undulatus*), bumphead parrotfish (*Bolbometopon muricatum*), and dogtooth tuna. Macro subjects include ghost pipefish, leaf scorpionfish (*Taenianotus triacanthus*), nudibranchs, and crabs. Standard reef fish communities including scorpionfish, boxfish, surgeonfish, angelfish, parrotfish, grouper, and snappers are present throughout the wall.

## Dive Profile

The wall begins from relatively shallow water and drops to 30 metres with overhangs and soft coral-encrusted sections. Cold thermoclines are documented at depth, requiring an appropriate wetsuit (5mm recommended for the deeper zones). Currents can be strong and variable. The structure of the wall allows divers to shelter in the overhangs when current is heavy. This site is suited to divers certified to the Advanced Open Water level with experience in current and wall diving.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Ternate's waterfront. Local Ternate dive operators (including Nukila Dive Center) and liveaboard operators running Ternate / Halmahera itineraries cover this site. Sultan Babullah Airport (TTE) in Ternate is the main regional access point, with daily connections from Jakarta and Manado.

## Tips and Recommendations

A 5mm wetsuit is recommended for the deeper sections where thermoclines can drop water temperature significantly. Begin on the shallower wall to locate macro subjects (ghost pipefish, leaf scorpionfish) before descending to the deeper wall where the large pelagics are found. The site is best dived on a slack or moderate tide rather than peak current. Recommended PADI specialties for this site include Deep Diver and Drift Diver.

## Safety Considerations

Strong and variable currents make this an advanced-only site. Downcurrents are possible on the wall face. Carry an SMB and ascend off the wall rather than fighting current. Cold thermoclines at 30 metres can cause rapid heat loss — a thick wetsuit is important. Despite being near Ternate City, the nearest recompression chamber is in Manado; emergency services in Ternate are limited.
