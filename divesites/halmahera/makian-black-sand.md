## Makian Black Sand

Makian Black Sand is a muck dive site off the volcanic island of Makian, in the Molucca Sea southwest of the Halmahera mainland, Indonesia.

## Overview

Makian is a stratovolcano island, and its eruptions have blanketed the surrounding seabed with the dark volcanic sediment that creates ideal muck diving conditions — analogous to the world-famous black sand sites of Lembeh Strait in North Sulawesi. The northern areas of Makian feature colourful coral growing against the black sand backdrop, providing both macro habitat and photogenic contrast. The site is included on South Halmahera liveaboard itineraries and is widely described as an excellent macro dive site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Makian Island, South Halmahera, North Maluku, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Muck dive (volcanic black sand)
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 metres
- **Typical Depth Range**: 5–20 metres

## Marine Life

The black sand substrate supports classic Lembeh-style critters. Documented species at Makian-type black sand sites in the region include frogfish, nudibranchs (multiple species), bobbit worms, and the endemic Halmahera epaulette shark (*Hemiscyllium halmahera*) — a species discovered in 2008 that is found only in the shallow reefs and rocky substrates around Halmahera and neighbouring islands. Colourful hard and soft corals in the northern areas also support reef fish.

## Dive Profile

Dives are conducted on a gently sloping volcanic black sand bottom, typically at depths from the surface down to around 20 metres. Currents are generally minimal, making this a relaxed dive focused on slow, careful searching for camouflaged critters. This calm profile makes it suitable for beginner divers with basic open-water certification.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by boat as part of a South Halmahera liveaboard itinerary (operators include Sea Safari Cruises and Coralia Liveaboard). There are no permanent dive shops on Makian Island itself. Ternate is the main transport hub for the wider region.

## Tips and Recommendations

Muck diving rewards patience and a slow pace. Move carefully to avoid disturbing the sediment and reducing visibility. A macro lens or wet dioptre is strongly recommended. Night dives on black sand sites in this region are highly productive, as many critters are more active after dark. Bring a torch even for day dives to illuminate crevices.

## Safety Considerations

Makian is an active volcano with periodic eruption alerts — confirm current volcanic status before visiting. The island is remote, with limited medical infrastructure. The nearest recompression chamber is in Manado. Water temperature is 27–30°C year-round; a 3mm wetsuit is comfortable for most divers.

## Photography

Black sand muck photography is a speciality discipline. A macro lens or 60mm/100mm equivalent with dual strobes is the standard setup for nudibranchs and frogfish. The dark substrate creates natural contrast, making even simple compositions effective. The northern coral-and-black-sand interface offers opportunities for mixed-style shots combining reef and muck subjects.
