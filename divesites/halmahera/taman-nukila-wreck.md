## Taman Nukila Wreck

Taman Nukila Wreck is a shore-entry dive site in Taman Nukila park, central Ternate City, on the island of Ternate adjacent to Halmahera, North Maluku, Indonesia.

## Overview

Taman Nukila is an urban waterfront park in the centre of Ternate City, and the dive site beneath it has been deliberately developed as an artificial reef and recreational dive destination. The primary attraction is the KM Pari, a 36-metre former cargo vessel deliberately sunk at 15–23 metres depth to serve as a marine habitat. A second vessel — an illegal fishing boat intentionally sunk by the Indonesian Navy as part of conservation enforcement — rests at 19 metres. Additional objects including motorcycles (sunk since 2017, now covered in encrusting coral) and two city buses with intact seating have been added to create a varied and accessible underwater environment. The site is reachable within 5 minutes of central Ternate hotels, making it one of the most convenient dive sites in the North Maluku region.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Taman Nukila, Ternate City, Ternate Island, North Maluku, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 23 meters

## Marine Life

The artificial reef structures support a diverse community typical of established wreck dive sites. Documented species include snappers, fusiliers, sweetlips, butterflyfish, cardinalfish, angelfish, grouper, and morays eels sheltering in the wreck structure. Macro life includes nudibranchs, flatworms, gobies, and frogfish on the encrusting coral growth. Clownfish in anemones have been documented on the artificial reef components.

## Dive Profile

The main KM Pari wreck lies at 15–23 metres, with the artificial reef objects (motorcycles, buses) distributed at various depths from around 10–19 metres. This range of depths and the calm, sheltered urban setting make Taman Nukila suitable for beginner and open-water certified divers. Currents are minimal in this sheltered bay. Visibility is typically adequate, though urban runoff can occasionally reduce it.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Taman Nukila park in central Ternate. The site is approximately 5 minutes from major hotels in Ternate City and is accessible for a quick day or evening dive without extensive logistics. No liveaboard is required; local dive operators in Ternate (including Nukila Dive Center, an SSI-affiliated operator based in Ternate) service this site.

## Tips and Recommendations

This is an excellent first-dive site when arriving in Ternate before continuing to Halmahera by ferry. The motorcycles and buses are photogenic subjects and accessible in the shallower range. Municipal clean-up efforts help maintain site quality, but a morning or early-afternoon dive before peak boat traffic is recommended for best visibility.

## Safety Considerations

This is a sheltered, urban, beginner-friendly site with minimal hazards beyond standard open-water precautions. Urban boat traffic near the shore requires divers to surface carefully and use a dive flag. Occasional debris from urban runoff is present. The nearest medical facilities are in Ternate City itself, making this one of the better-served dive sites in the North Maluku region for emergency response.

## Photography

The artificial reef components offer excellent macro and close-focus wide-angle subjects. The motorcycles overgrown with coral are popular photographic subjects, as are clownfish in anemones and frogfish on the encrusting surfaces. The wrecks themselves provide architectural subjects for wide-angle ambient-light shooting.
