## Halmahera

North Maluku's unexplored diving frontier with WWII wrecks, endemic walking sharks, pristine soft coral walls, and volcanic black sand muck diving across the largest island in the Moluccas.

## Description

Halmahera is the largest island in the Maluku archipelago, straddling the equator between Raja Ampat and North Sulawesi. Dive sites range in depth with depths from 15 to 50 meters, spread across a vast area encompassing Ternate, Weda Bay, the Goraici Islands, and Morotai in the far north. With an estimated 686 fish species and 450 coral species, Halmahera offers biodiversity rivaling its more famous neighbors but with a fraction of the diver traffic. Sites range from WWII wrecks and plane debris to pristine pinnacles and black sand muck.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Wreck Diving**: Sites including WWII relics USS PT-371 and Tosei Maru (sunk 1941), Foya Wreck, and the Bristol Plane Wreck at 50 meters depth
- **Wall Diving**: Sites (Biatabang, Batu Mandi) with strong currents attracting large pelagics in deep channels
- **Pinnacle Diving**: Sites (Terumbu Gora, Shark Mountain, Pulau Pisang, Womow Reef) where seamounts and submerged rock formations concentrate marine life
- **Reef Diving**: Sites (Pulau Tifore, Reenie's Rock, Batu Jabu) with healthy hard and soft coral communities
- **Muck Diving**: Site (Makian Black Sand) on volcanic black sand with critter hunting
- **Drift Diving**: Site (Proco Channel) with strong tidal currents and big fish encounters
- **Shore Diving**: Site (Taman Nukila Wreck) accessible from shore near Ternate

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Fly to Babullah Airport (TTE) on Ternate Island, with connections from Manado (MDC), Jakarta, or Makassar. From Ternate, ferry or speedboat transfers reach Sofifi on Halmahera's west coast. Weda Bay on the east coast is reached by road from Sofifi or by liveaboard. Morotai in the far north requires a separate flight or extended liveaboard itinerary.
- **Dive Operators**: Weda Resort in Weda Bay and Sali Bay Dive Resort in South Halmahera are the primary land-based operations. Liveaboard vessels including Malaillo and Mermaid operate North Halmahera and Morotai expedition itineraries.
- **Accommodation**: Weda Resort and Sali Bay Resort offer dedicated dive accommodation. Ternate city has hotels ranging from budget to mid-range. Liveaboards provide the only practical access to remote northern sites.
- **Transportation**: Boat transfers from resorts to dive sites. The geographic spread of sites across Halmahera means liveaboards can access far more sites than land-based operations.
- **Facilities**: Ternate has basic hospital facilities but no hyperbaric chamber. The nearest recompression chamber is in Manado. Dive insurance with evacuation coverage is essential.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature ranges from 27-30C year-round. Visibility is generally good, with clear conditions at the Goraici Islands and outer reef sites. Currents are usually gentle to moderate across most sites, though certain straits and channels like Patinti can experience strong tidal surges.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Halmahera hosts an estimated 686 fish species and 450 coral species. Notable encounters include the endemic Halmahera walking shark, hammerhead sharks in deep channels, reef sharks, manta rays, massive schools of barracuda and fusiliers at Tifore, Napoleon wrasse, pygmy seahorses, nudibranchs, and diverse macro life on black sand slopes. WWII wrecks are colonized by soft corals and host resident marine life.
- **Conservation**: The region's low visitor numbers and remoteness provide natural protection. Weda Bay's reefs show minimal bleaching damage. No formal marine park designation covers the main dive areas, though the biodiversity warrants protection.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: March to November, with October to May often cited as the optimal window. The transition seasons (March-April and October-November) can bring plankton blooms that attract whale sharks and manta rays.
- **Currency**: Indonesian Rupiah (IDR)
- **Language**: Indonesian (Bahasa Indonesia) and local North Maluku languages
- **Safety**: Most sites are suitable for intermediate divers, though strong channel currents at sites like Biatabang and Proco Channel require advanced drift diving skills. The Bristol Plane Wreck at 50 meters is a deep dive requiring appropriate certification and experience. Remote location means long evacuation times to the nearest hyperbaric chamber in Manado.
