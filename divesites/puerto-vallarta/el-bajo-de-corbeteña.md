## El Bajo de Corbeteña

El Bajo de Corbeteña (also known as La Corbeteña or La Piedra) is one of Banderas Bay's most remote and celebrated pelagic dive sites, located approximately 36 nautical miles northwest of Puerto Vallarta in open Pacific water. The site is reserved for experienced divers and accessed by a one-hour (or more) boat journey, offering some of the best pelagic encounters in Mexico's Pacific coast diving.

## Overview

Corbeteña is a deepwater seamount / pinnacle rising from the ocean floor, with walls, caves, and low arches encrusted with barnacles and filter feeders that attract an extraordinary concentration of larger pelagic species. The site is known for crystal-clear water with visibility often exceeding 30 metres (100 feet), particularly in summer. Giant manta rays are a primary draw during summer and fall, when their activity increases dramatically. The remote location and challenging currents make this a site for experienced divers — most operators require at minimum 25 logged dives and Advanced certification, and the trip requires a minimum group of 4-8 divers to confirm departure.

## Marine Life

Giant manta rays are the headline attraction, with peak activity during summer and fall. Nurse sharks rest in the caves and crevices of the wall structure. Reef sharks patrol the open water around the seamount. Large schools of jacks and trevally circulate the structure. Sea turtles, sailfish, marlin, wahoo, and dolphins are reported on the surface and at depth. The walls support barnacle and filter feeder communities with microscopic life concentrations that draw the larger pelagics.

## Dive Profile

Depth ranges from approximately 20 to 37 metres (65–120 feet) on the main structure, with the south face dropping rapidly to a sandbar at around 37 metres (120 feet). The dive involves navigating caves, low arches, and steep walls on the seamount structure. Strong currents are possible and conditions can deteriorate rapidly. Visibility is best during summer months and can reach 30+ metres on good days.

## Safety Considerations

Advanced certification and a minimum of 25 logged dives are required by most operators. Strong and unpredictable currents necessitate drift diving experience. The remote location (approximately 36 nautical miles offshore, roughly a 2-hour boat crossing each way) means rescue response times are extended — dive conservatively. An SMB is mandatory. Currents can vary significantly within a single dive.

## Best Season

Summer and fall for manta ray encounters and peak visibility. Conditions are variable and trips may be cancelled due to weather or sea state.
