# Las Gemelas

Twin submerged rock formations near Los Arcos — cup corals, sea fans, moray eels, and schooling fish on two closely spaced pinnacles in clear Banderas Bay water

## Overview

Las Gemelas (The Twins) is a dive site consisting of two closely spaced submerged rock formations located near Los Arcos, just south of Mismaloya. The site takes its name from the twin pinnacles that rise from the seafloor as a matched pair, separated by a sandy channel that divers can cross between dives on the two formations.

The pinnacles are densely covered with encrusting life — cup corals, sea fans, and gorgonians cover the rock surfaces where current flows across the formations. The twin structure creates two distinct dive experiences within a single site: each formation has its own profile, orientation, and resident species, meaning a single dive can cover notably varied terrain by crossing between the two. The proximity to Los Arcos means similar species composition but with the added structure of the twin rock formations providing different angles and habitat types.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Los Arcos, south of Mismaloya; 10-15 minutes from Los Arcos
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-28°C (72-82°F)
- **Current**: Usually mild; can strengthen between the formations
- **Best Time**: May to November

## Marine Life

Cup corals and orange cup corals encrust large sections of the pinnacle walls, providing brilliant color at depth. Sea fans and gorgonians grow on the more current-exposed faces of the formations. Moray eels are resident throughout the rocky crevices of both pinnacles. Schools of snapper and parrotfish circle the formations, while jacks occasionally school in the open water above. Nudibranchs are found on the cup coral sections and in sheltered pockets on the rock faces. Sea turtles visit the site and are often seen resting on the sandy channel between the twin formations. Eagle rays pass by the base of the pinnacles occasionally.

## Dive Profile

Most dives begin on one of the two formations, descending to the base at around 18-20 meters before working up and across the rock faces toward shallower depths. Crossing the sandy channel between the twins to explore the second formation is straightforward and adds variety to the dive. Both formations can be explored within a single air supply if the sandy crossing is kept brief. The shallower tops of the pinnacles at 8-12 meters provide productive safety stop depth with continued marine life.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat. Las Gemelas is typically included on trips that also visit Los Arcos, as the two sites are close together. Entry is giant stride; ascend with SMB from the pinnacle tops at depth before surfacing.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Carry a torch — cup corals and the creatures living among them are far more vivid with artificial light
- Cross the sandy channel between the two formations midway through the dive to see both pinnacles
- The gap between the twins can channel current; be aware of flow direction when crossing
- Las Gemelas pairs well with Los Arcos on a half-day trip from Puerto Vallarta or Mismaloya
