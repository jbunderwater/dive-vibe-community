# Quimixto

Rocky granite reef near a remote fishing village south of Puerto Vallarta — lobsters, octopus, moray eels, and schooling fish on boulder-strewn bottom

## Overview

Quimixto is a rocky reef dive site located near the small fishing village of the same name on the southern shore of Banderas Bay, about 45 minutes by boat from Puerto Vallarta. Like Majahuitas nearby, the site has no road access, which limits tourist traffic and keeps the marine life relatively undisturbed.

The underwater landscape is defined by large granite boulders and rock formations scattered across a sloping reef bottom. These boulders create an intricate network of ledges, overhangs, and narrow passages that shelter an abundance of invertebrate life. The topography is similar to other southern bay sites but with a slightly more rugged, irregular character — the granite boulder formations feel more stacked and jumbled than the smoother rock faces at Los Arcos. Depths range to about 24 meters across the dive area, with the most productive diving occurring on the mid-depth slopes between 10-20 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South shore of Banderas Bay, near Quimixto village; 45 minutes from PV
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-28°C (72-82°F)
- **Current**: Usually mild; can be moderate
- **Best Time**: May to November

## Marine Life

Lobsters are a consistent highlight at Quimixto, sheltering in the crevices and overhangs between granite boulders throughout the site. Octopus are frequently encountered on open rock faces, often hunting during the day or tucked into holes with only their arms visible. Moray eels occupy ledges at virtually every depth. The boulder formations attract schooling fish: snapper, jack, and parrotfish move through in numbers. Angel fish and butterflyfish work the reef surface. The rocky bottom provides habitat for nudibranchs, urchins, and cleaning shrimp that reward close-focus searching.

## Dive Profile

Most dives at Quimixto begin in shallower water and work down the boulder slope to around 20-24 meters before ascending to finish the dive on the richer shallower sections. The irregular boulder topography allows for a meandering route rather than a strict direction — there is enough variety in the rock formations that exploratory navigation stays interesting throughout the dive. The typical calm conditions in the cove make depth management straightforward.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Puerto Vallarta, approximately 45 minutes each way. Entry is giant stride from the boat. The protected cove location generally keeps entry and exit conditions calm.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Look carefully under every boulder overhang and ledge — lobsters and octopus are there but require deliberate searching
- Night dives at Quimixto are productive: lobsters roam openly and octopus are actively hunting
- The fishing village of Quimixto is accessible by water taxi for a surface interval break with lunch at local restaurants
- Pair with Majahuitas on a full-day southern bay trip to see both boulder-reef environments
