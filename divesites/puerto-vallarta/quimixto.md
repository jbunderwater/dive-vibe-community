# Quimixto

Rocky reef near a remote fishing village south of Puerto Vallarta — tropical fish, rays, and moray eels among jumbled granite boulder formations, accessible only by boat

## Overview

Quimixto is a rocky reef dive site located near the small fishing village of the same name on the southern shore of Banderas Bay, about 45 minutes by boat from Puerto Vallarta. The village has no road access, which limits tourist traffic to the site and keeps the marine life relatively undisturbed compared to closer-in destinations.

The underwater terrain is defined by large granite boulders and rock formations scattered across a sloping reef bottom, reaching to about 24 meters across the dive area. These boulders create an intricate maze of ledges, overhangs, and narrow passages that shelter an abundance of lobster, octopus, and eels. The sloping profile gives divers a gradual gradient to work with rather than a sudden wall drop, which makes depth management comfortable and allows extended time on the productive mid-depth sections between 10 and 20 meters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern Banderas Bay, near Quimixto village; ~45 min from Puerto Vallarta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-28°C (72-82°F)
- **Current**: Usually mild; can be moderate
- **Best Time**: May to November

## Marine Life

Rays are a consistent highlight at Quimixto — stingrays rest on the sandy patches between boulder formations, and electric rays are encountered on the gentler sloped sections. Moray eels occupy ledges at virtually every depth level across the site. Tropical reef fish move through the formations in numbers: snapper, parrotfish, angelfish, and butterflyfish are the dominant species. Lobsters shelter in the crevices and overhangs beneath the boulders throughout the site. Octopus are regularly seen on open rock faces and are active during daylight hours. The sandy channels between boulder groups attract additional invertebrate species.

## Dive Profile

Most dives at Quimixto begin in shallower water and work down the boulder slope to 20-24 meters before ascending to finish on the shallower sections. The irregular boulder topography supports exploratory navigation — there is enough variety in the rock formations and channels that a meandering route stays interesting throughout. Typical calm conditions in the protected cove make depth management straightforward. Most productive marine life concentrates in the 10-20 meter range where boulder density is highest.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Puerto Vallarta, approximately 45 minutes each way. Giant stride entry from the boat. The protected cove location keeps entry and exit conditions calm in most weather.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Inspect the underside of every boulder overhang and ledge — lobsters and moray eels are there but require deliberate searching
- Night dives at Quimixto are productive: lobsters emerge from their shelters and octopus are actively hunting on open rock
- The fishing village of Quimixto is accessible by water taxi for a surface interval break with lunch at local restaurants
- Often combined with Majahuitas on a full-day southern bay trip — the two sites are similar in character but offer different underwater topography
- Rays on the sandy sections tend to hold still if approached slowly and at a low angle from the side
