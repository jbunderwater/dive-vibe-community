# Los Arcos Marine Park

Iconic protected granite islets south of Puerto Vallarta — shallow arches, natural tunnels, and some of the bay's densest reef fish populations including angelfish, pufferfish, seahorses, and sea turtles

## Overview

Los Arcos Marine Park is a cluster of granite islets and exposed rock formations located about 12 km south of Puerto Vallarta near Mismaloya, protected as a national marine park since 1984. Fishing and removal of marine life are prohibited throughout the park, which has produced fish populations noticeably denser and bolder than at unprotected sites in Banderas Bay.

The shallow zone covered here — sometimes called El Acuario (The Aquarium) — stays within 13 meters and offers the most accessible diving at Los Arcos. Natural arches and short swim-through passages connect the islets underwater, and the protected character of this section means calm conditions even when other parts of the bay have chop. The shallower depth and predictable conditions make this the standard area for introductory dives, first open-water certification dives, and visiting divers who want a relaxed, high-yield experience close to PV.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Mismaloya, 12 km south of Puerto Vallarta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 13 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-28°C (72-82°F)
- **Current**: Mild; well-protected central zone
- **Best Time**: Year-round; May to November for warmest water

## Marine Life

The national park protection means the fish here behave differently than at open-access sites — angelfish, pufferfish, butterflyfish, and trumpetfish approach divers without alarm. Seahorses cling to algae patches in the quieter sections and require patient searching to locate. Sea turtles rest on the substrate or cruise through the arches regularly. Moray eels occupy virtually every crevice in the rock formations. Pufferfish are abundant in the shallows and tend to be tolerant of close approach. Schools of snapper move through the tunnels. Octopus appear at dusk and are excellent night-dive subjects. The arches themselves are colonized by colorful encrusting invertebrates that reward divers with a torch.

## Dive Profile

Most dives in the marine park's shallow zone run 5-13 meters, staying in the central section around the arches and tunnels. The route typically weaves between the rock formations, through short swim-throughs, and over the rocky reef bottom. Navigation is straightforward — the islets provide clear orientation at all times. Extended bottom times are possible given the shallow depths, making this good for photographers who want unhurried time with macro subjects. The calm, sheltered conditions make buoyancy practice easy for beginners.

## Entry and Exit

All diving at Los Arcos is by boat. The site is a 10-15 minute ride from Mismaloya Beach or approximately 30 minutes from the Puerto Vallarta marina. Entry is giant stride from the boat; operators moor on the protected side. Deploy an SMB before ascending — boat traffic around the islets is heavy during midday snorkeling hours.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Dive early morning for best visibility and to avoid snorkeling tour crowds that arrive midday
- Bring a dive torch — the arches and overhangs reveal far more detail with artificial light
- Night dives are excellent here: octopus, lobster, and sleeping turtles are approachable and easy to photograph
- The park protection means you can take your time around the fish — they won't scatter
- Combine with El Bajo del Cristo on a two-tank trip to see both the shallow and mid-depth zones of Los Arcos
