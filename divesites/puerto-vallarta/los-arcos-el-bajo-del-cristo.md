# Los Arcos - El Bajo del Cristo

Submerged volcanic peak within Los Arcos Marine Park — colorful nudibranchs, parrotfish, moray eels, and snapper along rocky walls and ledges from 9 to 26 meters

## Overview

El Bajo del Cristo is a submerged volcanic peak within Los Arcos Marine Park, sitting a short swim from the main granite islets. While the shallow El Acuario zone draws beginners, El Bajo del Cristo occupies the mid-depth range (9-26 meters) where the rock structure becomes more complex and the marine life changes character. The formation rises from deeper water, with the peak accessible to intermediate divers and the flanks descending toward the lower end of recreational depth.

The volcanic rock surface here is different in texture and character from the smoother granite of the main islets — rougher, with more pronounced ledges, overhangs, and crevices that harbor an abundance of invertebrate life. This is the site within Los Arcos best suited for nudibranch hunting; the combination of volcanic substrate, moderate depth, and park protection creates conditions where these colorful sea slugs appear in unusual variety. The site falls within the national marine park's no-take zone, so fish are relaxed and reef communities are healthy throughout.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Los Arcos Marine Park, 12 km south of Puerto Vallarta
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 22-28°C (72-82°F)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; can increase on exposed faces
- **Best Time**: May to November

## Marine Life

Nudibranchs are the signature find at El Bajo del Cristo, appearing in the crevices and on rocky surfaces throughout the dive. Colorful parrotfish work the reef surface in numbers. Moray eels occupy ledges at multiple depth levels and are frequently visible with heads extended from their holes. Schools of snapper aggregate around the peak, along with jacks that patrol the edges of the formation. Angelfish and butterflyfish are common on the shallower sections. Octopus are present in the rock formations and are active during the day. The deeper flanks of the pinnacle may yield sea turtles passing through or resting on ledges.

## Dive Profile

Dives on El Bajo del Cristo typically begin at the peak (around 9 meters) and work progressively deeper along the flanks to 20-26 meters before ascending. The rocky surface provides clear visual navigation — follow the structure down and back up rather than navigating by compass. The most productive sections for nudibranchs and macro life are the mid-depth ledges between 12-20 meters. Allow time at the peak on the ascent; fish concentrations are highest here. Plan a standard safety stop at 5 meters, using the mooring line or the formation's upper section for reference.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from the Puerto Vallarta marina (about 30 minutes) or from Mismaloya Beach (10-15 minutes). Giant stride entry; giant stride or ladder exit to the boat. The site is usually dived as part of a two-tank trip combining El Bajo del Cristo with either the shallow El Acuario zone or El Cañón for more advanced divers.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Search the rocky surfaces slowly for nudibranchs — they blend well and reward careful observation
- Moray eels here are accustomed to divers and rarely retreat far into their crevices
- The formation's peak at 9 meters is excellent for extended time with snapper and parrotfish on safety-stop depth
- Bring a macro lens: the volcanic surface hosts small crustaceans and invertebrates that photograph well with a diopter
- Often paired with the shallow El Acuario zone on a two-tank trip — do El Bajo del Cristo first while air is fresh
