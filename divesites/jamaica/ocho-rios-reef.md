# Ocho Rios Reef

A lively north-coast reef near Jamaica's cruise port town, where the Cayman Trench approaches close to shore and the coral is fed by cold-water upwelling.

## Overview

Ocho Rios is Jamaica's second most popular dive destination after Negril, offering close to two dozen dive sites within easy reach. The Cayman Trench approaches the shoreline in this area, and the resulting nutrient-rich upwelling feeds the reef communities, producing good coral coverage and abundant fish life. The main reef system at Ocho Rios consists of spur-and-groove formations with sandy channels, interspersed with small caverns and overhangs. The SS Kathryn — a WWII minesweeper intentionally sunk in the 1950s — lies in the shallows as a highlight wreck dive nearby. The reef itself features warm water (26–29°C), moderate visibility (20–25 m), and calm, sheltered conditions most of the year.

## Marine Life

The upwelling-fed reef supports schools of blue chromis, four-eyed butterflyfish, parrotfish, and trumpetfish. Nurse sharks rest beneath coral ledges on the deeper sections. Hawksbill turtles are common visitors. Spotted eagle rays occasionally cruise through the sandy channels. Barracuda hold position above the reef in the mid-water. Moray eels — green and spotted — occupy crevices. Goatfish forage on the sandy bottom between coral heads. Lionfish have been observed at some sites in the area. Octopus are active at night on the reef.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef at 8–12 metres and follow the spur-and-groove formations. Sandy channels between the coral ridges provide natural navigation. The deeper sections of the reef reach 18–20 metres at the outer edge. Work inward from the reef edge for the ascent and safety stop at 5 metres. Many operators in Ocho Rios also offer dives on the nearby SS Kathryn wreck, which is worth combining on a two-tank trip.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Ocho Rios operators, including ScubaCaribe Ocho Rios and other local operators based near the cruise port. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Deploy SMB on ascent for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Combine this site with the SS Kathryn wreck for a diverse two-tank dive day
- The reef is close to Ocho Rios town — dive trips are easily organized from most resorts
- Morning dives provide the best light and least boat traffic
- Ask about night dives — octopus activity on the reef at night is excellent

## Safety Considerations

Boat traffic near Ocho Rios can be heavy due to cruise ship activity in the bay. Always deploy SMB before surfacing. Fire coral is present on some structures. Stay within your certification depth limits.

## Photography

The clear water and active fish life make Ocho Rios reef a versatile photography site. Schools of chromis and butterflyfish provide motion subjects. Turtle encounters are frequent enough to plan a dedicated photo session. The cavern sections at depth can be lit with strobes for dramatic interior shots.
