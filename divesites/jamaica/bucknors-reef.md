# Bucknors Reef

A reef site along Jamaica's northwest coast between Montego Bay and Negril, with classic Caribbean spur-and-groove formations and reliable turtle sightings.

## Overview

Bucknors Reef occupies a stretch of Jamaica's northwest coast in the transition zone between the Montego Bay and Negril dive regions. The reef features the characteristic spur-and-groove formations of Jamaican shallow-water reefs, with coral ridges at 8–12 metres separated by sandy channels that extend down to 16 metres. The site is relatively sheltered and calm, with light current and good visibility of 20–25 metres. The northwest coast receives consistent boat traffic from operators based in both Montego Bay and Negril, particularly during peak season. Water temperature averages 27–29°C year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest Jamaica coast
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–25 meters (65–80 feet)
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

Hawksbill turtles are a consistent feature of this reef section. Parrotfish, French angelfish, blue tangs, and schooling grunts are abundant. Nurse sharks rest under overhangs in the deeper sections. Spotted moray eels and spiny lobsters are found in reef crevices. Southern stingrays rest on the sandy channels between coral ridges. Trumpetfish are common in the sea fans. Barracuda patrol the mid-water column above the reef.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef plateau at 8–10 metres and explore the spur-and-groove formations. Follow the reef crest to the deeper sandy areas at 14–16 metres where stingrays are most active. Ascend from the outer reef back to the shallower section for a safety stop at 5 metres. Bottom time is generous at these depths.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Montego Bay or Negril operators. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Follow the divemaster's briefing for current direction and navigation at this site. Deploy SMB before surfacing for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Good site to combine with nearby Montego Bay Marine Park sites on a multi-tank day
- The spur-and-groove terrain is easy to navigate — a good site for independent navigation practice
- Check for cleaning stations between the coral ridges — parrotfish and wrasse are active throughout the day
- Morning light creates beautiful visibility in the clear shallow water

## Safety Considerations

Fire coral is present on some reef structures. Maintain buoyancy above the coral. Deploy SMB before surfacing and be aware of boat traffic on this busy coastal corridor.

## Photography

Classic spur-and-groove reef photography — wide-angle from within the sand channel looking up the coral ridges creates strong compositions. Turtle and nurse shark subjects are consistently available at this site.
