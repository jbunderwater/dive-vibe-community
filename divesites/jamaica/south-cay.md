# South Cay

A small cay marking the southern extent of the Portland Bight cay cluster, with a productive fringing reef and peaceful, protected diving conditions.

## Overview

South Cay lies in the southern portion of the Portland Bight Protected Area cay group, marking the southernmost reach of this cluster of offshore islets. The cay's fringing reef extends to 15 metres and features the typical Caribbean spur-and-groove formations with sandy channels and reef overhangs. The protected area designation and minimal fishing pressure in this region contribute to healthy reef conditions. The cay's position in the open coastal waters gives it slightly more exposure than the more sheltered inner cays, and some current can develop on the seaward side. Visibility is typically 15–20 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Portland Bight Protected Area, south Jamaica
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters (50–65 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate (seaward side)
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

The reef supports a typical south-coast Caribbean community. Hawksbill turtles graze sponges across the reef. Schoolmaster snapper and French grunts school under overhangs. Parrotfish and blue tangs are abundant in the shallower sections. Nurse sharks rest at the reef base on the seaward side. Spiny lobsters and spotted moray eels inhabit crevices. Southern stingrays rest on sandy patches around the cay. The outer exposure of this cay sometimes attracts bar jacks and horse-eye jacks in the mid-water column.

## Dive Profile

Moor on the protected lee side and begin the dive on the shallow inner reef at 5–8 metres. Work around to the seaward outer reef at 12–15 metres, spending time on the deeper section before returning. Ascend to the lee side for the safety stop in calmer water.

## Entry and Exit

Access by local charter from the south coast. Limited commercial dive infrastructure. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Moor on the sheltered side of the cay.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The exposed position of this cay means it is best dived in calm conditions (dry season preferred)
- Combine with Maiden Cay and Rackhmans Cay for a productive south-coast cay diving day
- Check current conditions before the dive — the seaward side may have noticeable flow

## Safety Considerations

Slightly more exposed than the inner cays. Current on the seaward side may require adjustment to the dive plan. Carry full safety equipment and deploy SMB clearly before surfacing.

## Photography

The combination of clear water, turtles, and nurse sharks provides strong subjects. The cay reef environment is conducive to wide-angle reef landscape shots.
