# Fish Pond Edge (Dive Site)

The outer drop-off extension of Fish Pond reef, where the coral formations give way to a deeper edge with sponge walls and open-water fish action.

## Overview

Fish Pond Edge is the outer margin of the Fish Pond reef complex within the Montego Bay Marine Park, situated where the reef slope transitions toward deeper water. While Fish Pond itself focuses on the caverns and overhangs at 15–18 metres, Fish Pond Edge extends the dive along the reef crest and outer slope where larger sponge formations dominate and pelagic fish activity increases. Azure vase sponges, large barrel sponges, and rope sponges appear in profusion along this section of the reef. The site offers slightly more current exposure than the inner Fish Pond area, which draws better fish activity. Visibility is excellent, typically 20–28 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Montego Bay Marine Park, Jamaica
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–28 meters (65–90 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

Large azure vase sponges and barrel sponges characterize the outer reef edge at 15–18 metres. Hawksbill turtles are frequent at this site, feeding on sponges along the reef edge. Horse-eye jacks and creole wrasse school in the mid-water column above the reef. Barracuda are common overhead. Spotted eagle rays glide past along the reef edge. French angelfish and rock beauties are abundant. At depth, large black grouper and tiger grouper hold position near sponge formations. Spotted moray eels and green moray eels occupy the crevices.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top at 10–12 metres. Navigate outward to the reef edge at 15–18 metres, where the sponge formations become largest. Follow the edge, exploring the slope below the main reef. Maintain depth discipline at this site as the outer edge begins to slope more steeply. Ascend back along the reef to the mooring area and complete your safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Montego Bay operators within the Marine Park. Boats use mooring buoys. Entry via giant stride or back roll. This site is typically combined with Fish Pond or Stingray City. Deploy SMB before surfacing for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Fish Pond Edge is a good complement to the inner Fish Pond site — the reef edge extends the terrain
- Watch mid-water regularly — the edge draws schooling jacks and passing eagle rays
- The sponge formations are the visual highlight here; position yourself among the large barrel sponges for landscape shots
- Slightly more current than the inner Fish Pond can be expected — check conditions before descending

## Safety Considerations

The outer reef edge begins to slope more steeply — monitor depth carefully. Current can be stronger here than at the sheltered inner Fish Pond site. Maintain a reserve for the ascent and safety stop. Deploy SMB clearly before surfacing.

## Photography

The large sponge formations — barrel sponges, azure vase sponges, rope sponges — are the dominant photo subjects at the reef edge. Wide-angle compositions with a diver dwarfed by a giant barrel sponge work well. Schooling jacks in the mid-water above the edge are excellent silhouette subjects shot upward toward the surface light.
