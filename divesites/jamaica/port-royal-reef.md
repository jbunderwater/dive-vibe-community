# Port Royal Reef

A reef adjacent to one of the Caribbean's most historically significant sites — the partially submerged pirate city of Port Royal, destroyed by the 1692 earthquake — offering quiet, off-the-beaten-path diving near Kingston.

## Overview

Port Royal Reef sits near the southeastern tip of Jamaica, in the historically important waters off Port Royal. On June 7, 1692, a major earthquake (estimated magnitude 7.5) struck Jamaica, causing a large portion of the then-prosperous Port Royal to submerge beneath the sea. Approximately 33 acres of the city sank, and the ruins now lie under up to 40 feet of water. The submerged ruins of Port Royal are a UNESCO tentative World Heritage site and access to the archaeological zone requires government authorization — recreational diving in the ruins area is restricted. The reef entry here refers to natural reef diving in the Port Royal area adjacent to (not within) the restricted archaeological zone. The reef features slopes of coral heads and sandy patches at 8–14 metres. Visibility is moderate (15–20 m) and can be affected by harbor current and runoff from Kingston.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Port Royal, Kingston Harbour, Jamaica
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters (50–65 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate; variable with harbor tides
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

The reef supports a Caribbean reef fish community including parrotfish, blue tangs, French grunts, and snapper. Southern stingrays are found on the sandy sections. Spiny lobsters inhabit coral crevices. Hawksbill turtles are seen occasionally. The area near Port Royal cays also has some wreck diving — the ScubaBoard-documented Cayman Trader sits at approximately 40 feet in the area, overgrown with coral. No site-specific marine life reports were found for Port Royal Reef beyond these regional observations.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef at 5–10 metres and navigate along the coral slope. The reef gradient is gentler than north-coast sites. Navigation is straightforward along the reef crest. Ascend from the outer reef for a safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Port Royal or Kingston operators. This is one of Jamaica's less-visited dive areas with minimal resort dive infrastructure. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Coordinate exit with the boat at the mooring or anchor point.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The restricted Port Royal archaeological ruins require government authorization — confirm access with operators before expecting to dive the historical site itself
- This reef site offers standard Caribbean reef diving with the added historical context of the Port Royal setting
- Combine with a surface tour of the Port Royal historical area for full context
- Morning dives after calm weather offer the best visibility

## Safety Considerations

Harbor current can be stronger and more variable than at Jamaica's north coast reef sites. Visibility is lower than at Negril or Montego Bay. Stay close to the reef for navigation reference. Deploy SMB before surfacing.

## Photography

Wide-angle shots of the reef landscape provide standard Caribbean documentation. The limited fish density compared to north-coast sites means patience is needed for animal behavior shots.
