# Port Royal Reef

A reef adjacent to one of the Caribbean's most historically significant dive sites — the sunken pirate city of Port Royal, lost in the 1692 earthquake.

## Overview

Port Royal Reef sits near the southeastern tip of Jamaica, in the historically charged waters off Port Royal — once the wealthiest and most notorious pirate port in the Americas before the catastrophic earthquake of 1692 submerged a large portion of the city beneath Kingston Harbour. The reef itself features slopes of coral heads and sandy patches at 8–14 metres, with the added fascination of lying adjacent to the submerged historical ruins of old Port Royal. The diving conditions here differ from Jamaica's north coast — less built-up tourism infrastructure, quieter dive boats, and a more off-the-beaten-path experience. Visibility is moderate (15–20 m), and conditions can be affected by harbor current and runoff from Kingston.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Port Royal, Kingston Harbour, Jamaica
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 meters (50–65 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate; variable with harbor tides
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

The reef supports the typical complement of Caribbean reef fish including French angelfish, parrotfish, blue tangs, and snapper. Southern stingrays are common on the sandy sections. Spiny lobsters inhabit the coral crevices. Green turtles and hawksbill turtles are seen occasionally. Nurse sharks rest under deep overhangs. On the sand near the Port Royal ruins, goatfish and spotted moray eels are active foragers.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef at 5–10 metres and navigate along the coral slope. The reef here is gentler in gradient than the north coast sites, with a gradual slope to 14 metres. Navigation is straightforward along the reef crest. The historical context of diving near the submerged city adds a unique dimension — local dive guides can point out the transition between natural reef and the remnants of old Port Royal structure on the seafloor. Ascend from the outer reef for a safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat from Port Royal. This is one of Jamaica's less-visited dive areas — operators are smaller and less resort-oriented than in Montego Bay or Negril. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Coordinate exit with the boat captain at the mooring or anchor point.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The primary appeal of this site is the historical context — the sunken ruins of old Port Royal are nearby and diveable
- Combine with a tour of the Port Royal historical area above water for full context
- Visibility can vary with current and harbor runoff — morning dives after calm weather offer the best conditions
- This is one of Jamaica's least-visited dive areas and offers a quiet, uncrowded experience

## Safety Considerations

Harbor current can be stronger and more variable than at Jamaica's north coast reef sites — check conditions carefully before diving. Visibility is lower than Negril or Montego Bay. Stay close to the reef for navigation reference. Deploy SMB clearly before surfacing.

## Photography

The historical context adds interest to underwater photography here — wide-angle shots incorporating any visible Port Royal structure fragments provide unique images. The fish life, though less dense than the north coast, includes photogenic angelfish and snapper subjects.
