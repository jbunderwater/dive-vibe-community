# Airport Reef

A sheltered Montego Bay reef within the Marine Park, home to abundant coral formations and a resident plane wreck on the sandy bottom.

## Overview

Airport Reef sits within the boundaries of the Montego Bay Marine Park, Jamaica's first declared Marine Protected Area established in 1991. The park's no-anchor and no-fishing rules have allowed the reef to recover and thrive. The site features spur-and-groove coral formations rising from sandy channels and includes a small single-engine plane wreck resting at around 18 metres on the sandy bottom — a popular secondary target for divers. The reef plateau itself tops out at 3–5 metres, making the site suitable for beginners and second dives. Visibility is typically 20–30 metres, and the protected inshore location keeps currents minimal. Water temperatures stay between 27–29°C year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Montego Bay, Jamaica
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters (65–100 feet)
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

The reef supports healthy populations of French angelfish, queen angelfish, parrotfish, and blue tangs grazing across coral heads. Hawksbill turtles are frequently seen resting on the reef or cruising through the spur-and-groove channels. Nurse sharks shelter under coral ledges and large overhangs. Spotted moray eels and spiny lobsters occupy the reef crevices. The plane wreck attracts black durgon triggerfish, glassy sweepers, and occasional barracuda hovering above the fuselage. Spadefish are commonly sighted in the mid-water column above the site.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef plateau at 5–10 metres and follow the spur-and-groove formations. The sandy channels lead toward the plane wreck at around 10–12 metres. Spend the bulk of bottom time exploring the reef before visiting the wreck, then ascend back up the reef for your safety stop in the shallows at 3–5 metres. Tides and currents are minimal inside the Marine Park.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Montego Bay operators. Entry via giant stride or back roll from the dive boat. Boats use mooring buoys inside the Marine Park — do not anchor. Deploy your SMB before ascending to signal the boat. The site is a short boat ride from the Montego Bay waterfront.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Morning dives offer the best natural light on the coral
- The plane wreck makes a good second stop after exploring the main reef
- Night diving on Airport Reef reveals octopus, hunting lobster, and sleeping parrotfish with vivid colors
- The Marine Park boundary is well-marked — stay within the protected area

## Safety Considerations

Boat traffic from the Montego Bay harbor area can be present. Always ascend close to the mooring line and deploy your SMB before surfacing. Fire coral is present on some reef sections — wear a full exposure suit. Maintain buoyancy above the reef to avoid damaging coral.

## Photography

Wide-angle shots of the spur-and-groove reef structures work well in the clear water. The plane wreck is a strong subject with natural light filtering down from above. Bring a macro lens for blennies on coral heads and cleaning shrimp at stations. Mornings offer the best visibility and light angles.
