## Turtle Reef

Turtle Reef is a crescent-shaped reef near Anse Chastanet, named for its shape more than a guarantee of turtle sightings — though hawksbill and green turtles are still regularly seen here.

## Overview

Turtle Reef is a crescent-shaped reef adjacent to turtle grass beds near Anse Chastanet. According to the Soufrière Marine Management Area's own description, the site is "named more for its crescent shape than for its attraction of turtles," though divers are still likely to spot a few hawksbill and green turtles. The reef begins around 12-15 metres (40-50 feet) on a plateau and slopes away into deeper water, with some sources describing drop-offs well beyond recreational depths — this is a more substantial dive than the shallow plateau alone suggests, which is why it is rated Intermediate rather than Beginner. Pillar corals and barrel sponges cover the plateau, and the site is reached by a short boat ride from the Anse Chastanet dive operation.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. Lucia, Caribbean
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters

## Marine Life

Hawksbill and green turtles are regularly, though not guaranteed, sightings here. Confirmed sightings at this site also include flying gurnards, peacock flounders, sharptail eels, goldspotted snake eels, burgundy seahorses, blackbar soldierfish, and smallmouth grunts. Moray eels and spiny lobsters are found in the reef's deeper sections. Earlier drafts described turtles here as "near-guaranteed" and "accustomed to divers, allowing very close approach" — neither claim is supported by the SMMA's own site description or by other sources, and both have been removed. Blue chromis and damselfish, previously listed, could not be confirmed for this specific site and have also been removed.
