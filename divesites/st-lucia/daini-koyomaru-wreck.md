## Daini Koyomaru Wreck

The Daini Koyomaru is an advanced wreck dive in St. Lucia — a Japanese dredger purchased by the St. Lucia government in the early 1990s to maintain the dredged shipping channel into the Port of Vieux Fort, then deliberately scuttled in September 1996 to create an artificial reef.

## Overview

The Daini Koyomaru was a Japanese dredger bought by the St. Lucia government in the early 1990s specifically to keep the access channel to the Port of Vieux Fort clear for banana boats. It was deliberately sunk in September 1996 as an artificial reef. The vessel is most commonly reported at around 244 feet (roughly 74 metres) long; a figure of 75 metres appears in some sources and is likely just a rounding difference rather than a genuine conflict. Accounts differ on how it came to rest on its side: some sources describe it sitting upright after sinking until Hurricane Lenny toppled it in 1999, while at least one other account describes it landing on its side at the moment it was sunk in 1996. Neither version is confirmed strongly enough to state as fact, so both are noted here rather than picking one. Sources also report varying depths for the wreck today — figures from roughly 20 metres (to the shallowest point of the now-sideways hull) up to 33-34 metres (to the deepest point) appear across different sources, reflecting a large vessel resting on its side rather than a single flat depth. This is a technically demanding Advanced dive requiring solid buoyancy and navigation skills, strong currents are commonly reported in the area, and it should be dived with a local operator experienced with the site.

**Note on location**: at least one dive-site database gives GPS coordinates for a wreck of this name near Canaries on St. Lucia's west coast (south end of Anse Cochon), which would put it well south of the "north coast" position recorded here. This discrepancy has not been resolved and is flagged for a future review rather than silently relocating the site — divers should confirm exact coordinates with a local operator before planning a dive.

The hull supports French angelfish, jacks, barracuda, pufferfish, and green moray eels as regularly reported inhabitants. A tonnage figure of roughly 16,000 tons appears in one source but is not independently corroborated, so it is reported here only as a single-source figure. Turtle sightings have been claimed for this site but could not be confirmed against a specific, citable source, so that claim has been removed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. Lucia (recorded position: north coast; see location note above regarding a conflicting west-coast/Canaries reference)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters

## Marine Life

French angelfish, jacks, and barracuda are regularly reported around the hull, along with pufferfish and green moray eels sheltering in the wreck's structure. Turtle sightings have been claimed by some sources but could not be independently confirmed for this specific site, so they are not listed as a confirmed sighting here.
