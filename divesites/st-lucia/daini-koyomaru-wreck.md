## Daini Koyomaru Wreck

The Daini Koyomaru is an advanced wreck dive off St. Lucia's west coast, near Canaries — a former Japanese dredger scuttled in September 1996 as an artificial reef.

## Overview

The Daini Koyomaru began life as a Japanese dredger, one of two purchased by the St. Lucia government in the early 1990s to maintain the shipping channel at the Vieux Fort banana port. Once that project concluded, it was scuttled in September 1996 as an artificial reef, coming to rest off Canaries on St. Lucia's west coast — about a mile offshore from the Lesleen M wreck at Anse Cochon (not on the north coast, as earlier versions of this page stated). The vessel's length is reported as 244 feet, which sources round to either roughly 74 metres (DeeperBlue) or 75 metres (divingaway.com, thescubadirectory.com).

**Sources disagree on how the wreck came to lie on its side.** DeeperBlue reports that a trapped air pocket caused the vessel to shift and turn over during its 1996 sinking, so it settled inverted from the start. Other sources (divingaway.com) report that it sank upright and was later toppled onto its starboard side by Hurricane Lenny in 1999, with much of the interior deliberately left intact for divers to explore. This has not been resolved between sources — divers should be aware of that uncertainty rather than take either version as settled fact.

Reported depths cluster in the 10-34 metre range: moorings.com cites 10m-33m, thescubadirectory.com cites approximately 34m/110ft, and DeeperBlue cites a maximum depth of around 30m/100ft. This makes it a complex Advanced dive requiring solid navigation skills and a local operator familiar with the site. The hull supports pufferfish, French angelfish, barracuda, moray eels, and turtles as regular inhabitants.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast, near Canaries, St. Lucia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters
