## Coral Gardens Soufrière

Coral Gardens Soufrière is a reef dive site in the Soufrière Marine Management Area (SMMA) on St. Lucia's west coast, situated at the base of Gros Piton — the UNESCO World Heritage volcanic spire that rises 770 metres above the water. The site is operated by Scuba St. Lucia and reached by a short boat ride from their dive centre at Anse Chastanet Resort.

## Overview

Coral Gardens lies within St. Lucia's protected marine reserve, giving it some of the healthiest coral formations on the island's west coast. The site spans from very shallow depths (around 5 metres) down to roughly 18 metres — some trip reports describe depths closer to 17-21 metres, so treat depth as a range rather than a fixed number — making it accessible to beginners while rewarding experienced divers with dense sea plume forests along coral and sponge formations. The backdrop of Gros Piton rising directly from the water creates one of the Caribbean's most dramatic above-water settings. **Correction**: earlier versions of this page described a shore-entry option here. Every source found for this site — the operating dive shop and two independent diver trip reports — describes Coral Gardens as boat-accessed only, with no shore-entry option. This has been corrected below.

## Marine Life

Dense forests of sea plumes in the shallows serve as nursery habitat for juvenile reef fish in high numbers. Sargassum triggerfish and barracuda are reported in the deeper sections of the site, alongside a wide variety of tropical reef fish moving through the coral structures.

## Dive Profile

Dive depth progresses from a shallow coral garden around 5 metres into deeper reef structure in the 17-21 metre range. Some dives here are conducted as a light drift when conditions allow, with the boat relocating to collect divers at the end. The site is suitable for beginners.

## Entry and Exit

Boat entry only — no shore-entry option is documented for this site. Reached by a short boat ride from the Scuba St. Lucia dive centre at Anse Chastanet; the previously stated "~20 minutes" transit time could not be confirmed by any source and has been removed.
