## Anse La Raye Wall

Anse La Raye Wall is an advanced wall dive site featuring dramatic vertical drops and excellent opportunities to see crustaceans and deep-water marine life.

## Overview

Situated at the base of the underwater continuation of St. Lucia's volcanic structure, Anse La Raye Wall features a dramatic wall that drops from the surface to many hundreds of feet below. This advanced wall dive is covered with Sea Whips, gorgonians, delicate soft corals and lots of Feather Duster Worms. The wall structure creates numerous crevices and overhangs that harbor crustaceans and critters at varying depths. Strong currents are common at this exposed site, making it suitable only for advanced divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Anse La Raye, St. Lucia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters (65-130 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: December to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

The wall structure provides a great opportunity to see varieties of crustaceans and critters hidden in crevices and overhangs. Divers encounter sea turtles, southern stingrays, eagle rays, nurse sharks, and reef sharks. Schools of barracuda, parrotfish, and angelfish are common. The soft coral and sponge growth on the wall supports abundant macro subjects. Sea fans and feather duster worms create a delicate landscape.

## Dive Profile

Begin along the reef top at shallower depths before descending along the wall. The wall descends to significant depths, so careful depth management is essential. Most of the interesting features and marine life are found between 10-40 meters. Maintain good buoyancy control and monitor your depth gauge regularly. Look closely at wall crevices and overhangs for crustaceans and small creatures. The strong currents mean you may be swept along the wall, so proper current awareness is essential.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Watch your depth carefully — wall dives can lead to unintentional deep descents
- Look in crevices and overhangs for hidden marine life and crustaceans
- Book with reputable local dive operators who know the site conditions
- Bring an underwater camera — this site offers excellent macro photography opportunities
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of boat traffic, fire coral, sea urchins in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. This site is recommended for experienced divers only due to depth and currents. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

Wall dives offer stunning wide-angle opportunities with dramatic depth perspectives. The interplay of light and shadow along the wall creates compelling compositions. Look for macro subjects in wall crevices and overhangs among soft corals and feather dusters.
