## Maria Island Reef

Maria Island Reef lies in the waters off the Maria Islands Nature Reserve near Vieux Fort in southern St. Lucia. The reef's existence is confirmed by OSM data (natural=reef), and the Maria Islands themselves are a confirmed nature reserve managed by the St. Lucia National Trust.

## Overview

The Maria Islands (also spelled Marie Islands) form a nature reserve off the south coast of St. Lucia near Vieux Fort. OSM data confirms a reef in this area, and general tourism sources describe the reserve's waters as usable for "snorkeling or diving." However, a repeat search on 2026-07-01 across dive-shop listings, ScubaBoard, and dive databases again found no scuba-specific source treating this as an established dive site — it is documented almost exclusively as a snorkeling and nature-tour destination, reached via Saint Lucia National Trust boat tours from Vieux Fort, rather than as a site on local dive operators' scuba circuits. Specific details about scuba depth, marine life, and dive conditions cannot be confirmed without primary dive sources. Divers interested in this site should inquire with local St. Lucia operators based in Vieux Fort for current access and conditions.
