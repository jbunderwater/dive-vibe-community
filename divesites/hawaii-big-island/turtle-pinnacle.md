## Turtle Pinnacle

A submerged volcanic seamount on the North Kona coast that functions as one of the island's most consistent Hawaiian green sea turtle cleaning stations, Turtle Pinnacle rises to within approximately 7 metres of the surface and offers exceptional visibility in light, typical Kona currents.

## Overview

Turtle Pinnacle is located off the west coast of the Big Island, north of Kailua-Kona, and features in operator write-ups by Kona Honu Divers and Bluewater Dive Travel as a standout site for turtle encounters. The submerged pinnacle (a subaqueous volcanic feature) rises from the surrounding reef to within about 7 metres (25 feet) of the surface, reaching a maximum depth of approximately 18 metres. The volcanic substrate supports coral growth and provides structure for the cleaning station.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Kona coast, west of the Big Island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: Reported at 45–60 metres by multiple sources — exceptional even by Kona standards
- **Water Temperature**: 26–28°C
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## The Cleaning Station

Turtle Pinnacle functions as a natural cleaning station for Hawaiian green sea turtles (Chelonia mydas). Multiple sources confirm that surgeonfish/tang (family Acanthuridae) work as cleaner fish here, removing algae and parasites from the turtles' shells as the turtles remain stationary and allow the cleaners to work. Multiple turtles are typically present simultaneously at the cleaning station, making this site particularly productive for turtle observation.

Bluewater Dive Travel notes that Turtle Pinnacle is "one of the best dive sites in all of the Hawaiian Islands for its turtle cleaning station." Divers can observe individual turtles holding position at the pinnacle top while tangs work over their shells — a markedly different behaviour from the turtles simply resting on the seafloor seen at many other Kona sites.

## Site Features

The volcanic substrate of the pinnacle supports coral growth. Kona Honu Divers describes the site as featuring "massive swim-throughs (lava tubes and caverns) where beams of light cut through the dark" — the pinnacle is surrounded by the lava formations characteristic of this part of the coast, including Golden Arches nearby.

## Marine Life

Hawaiian green sea turtles (Chelonia mydas): multiple individuals confirmed at the cleaning station.
Surgeonfish / tang (family Acanthuridae): confirmed cleaner fish at this site.
Tropical reef fish: typical for Kona reef environments.

No other specific marine life has been confirmed at this particular site through current independent sources. The Hawaii State Parks blog additionally lists octopus, eels, and tropical fish as present, consistent with general Kona reef conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Observe turtles from a respectful distance (minimum 1.5 metres per Hawaii regulations)
- Do not touch or pursue turtles
- The exceptional visibility (45–60 m) makes this site excellent for wide-angle photography
- Light currents make this a relaxed dive suitable for all experience levels within the intermediate category

## Safety Considerations

- Boat access only; coordinate descent and ascent times with the captain
- Maintain buoyancy to avoid contact with the pinnacle or surrounding reef
- Use an SMB on ascent
