## Wild Beast Point

Wild Beast Point is an advanced wall dive offering a dramatic vertical structure and challenging conditions. The site's name suggests dynamic and unpredictable conditions—it's a serious Poor Knights dive suitable for experienced divers comfortable with current, depth, and demanding conditions. The wall offers excellent marine life viewing and photography for divers with the skills to manage the dive.

## Overview

Wild Beast Point is an aptly-named advanced wall dive site. The dramatic vertical wall presents significant depth potential and often encounters notable current. This is a site for experienced divers seeking challenging Poor Knights diving with substantial depth and dynamic water conditions. The name reflects the wild nature of the site—divers should approach with respect for conditions and be prepared for a demanding dive profile.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Poor Knights Islands, Northland, New Zealand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 14-17°C (winter/May-Sept), 19-25°C (summer/Nov-april)
- **Current**: Often strong; a defining characteristic of the site
- **Best Time**: May to September for best visibility; year-round diving possible

## Marine Life

The wall structure and current support excellent marine life:
- Large kingfish, snapper, and giant trevally hunting in the blue
- Schooling blue maomao and pink maomao
- Short-tailed stingrays
- Hard and soft corals covering the wall
- Sponges and anemones
- Moray eels and nudibranchs in crevices
- Larger pelagic visitors

## Dive Profile

This is a deep wall dive requiring careful management. Descend to the wall structure and monitor your depth closely. The wall has significant depth potential—resist the urge to exceed your planned maximum depth. Current, when present, will influence your dive direction and requires excellent positioning and buddy contact. Allow significant time at shallower depths (10-15m) before descending deeper. Plan conservative bottom times and substantial safety stop time. This is not a leisurely reef dive—it requires focus and discipline.

## Entry and Exit

Boat entry via giant stride with full briefing on current direction and depth hazards. Your guide will emphasize depth management and current awareness. Exit via ascent with safety stops and boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Advanced training required**: Deep dive certification and substantial Poor Knights experience essential
- **Current respect**: The current is part of the site character—work with it, not against it
- **Depth discipline**: Set your depth limit BEFORE the dive and stick to it
- **Experienced guide essential**: Use guides experienced with Wild Beast Point conditions
- **Conservative profile**: Plan shorter bottom times than intermediate sites
- **Cold water preparation**: Appropriate thermal protection is mandatory
- **Check weather**: Conditions can change—verify with operator before diving

## Safety Considerations

**Requirements:**
- Advanced open water certification with deep dive specialty
- Significant Poor Knights experience
- Cold-water diving experience and comfort with challenging conditions
- Excellent buoyancy control and depth awareness
- Strong current management skills
- Understanding of nitrogen narcosis below 30m
- Conservative air consumption and dive planning
- Never dive this site on your first Poor Knights visit
- Current can increase without warning—abort if uncomfortable
- Strong buddy contact and communication essential
- This site demands respect and discipline

## Photography

Advanced photographers will find excellent opportunities despite challenging conditions:
- **Wide-angle**: The wall structure with pelagic fish in the blue
- **Fish behaviour**: Large predator fish positioning relative to current
- **Macro**: Wall-encrusting organisms in crevices
- Positioning in current requires practice and skill
- Neutral buoyancy is critical for stable shooting
- Consider shooting during the first part of the dive when air and attention are freshest
