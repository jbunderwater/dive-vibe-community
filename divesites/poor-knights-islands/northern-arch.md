## Northern Arch

Located at the northern end of the Poor Knights Islands, Northern Arch is regarded as one of the finest dives at the Poor Knights and requires respect and experience. The arch is described as a strictly parallel cleft through a rocky promontory, approximately 6-8 metres wide and 40 metres tall, with a base at 36-40 metres. Unpredictable currents and recorded fatalities place this firmly in the Advanced category.

## Overview

Northern Arch is a dramatic geological feature: a nearly vertical parallel cleft cut through a rocky promontory. The base of the arch sits at 36-40 metres depth, with the top rising toward the surface. The location ensures consistent, sometimes strong currents — which create nutrient-rich conditions supporting exceptional marine life but also requiring careful dive planning. A memorial plaque for diver John Catton is located at the site. Dense schools of pink maomao and blue maomao swarm the arch when conditions allow.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern end of Puketuaho Island, Poor Knights Islands, Northland, New Zealand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters (arch base at 36-40m; surrounding bay deeper)
- **Minimum Depth**: 15 meters (arch entrance)
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 14-17°C (winter/May-Sept), 19-25°C (summer/Nov-April)
- **Current**: Often strong and consistent—factor this into dive planning
- **Best Time**: May to September when visibility is best

## Marine Life

Northern Arch is famous for its fish life in the current-swept archway:
- Massive schools of pink maomao and blue maomao
- Large kingfish and snapper hunting in the blue water
- Short-tailed stingrays, which use this area for mating in late February to March
- Colourful sponges, anemones, and encrusting life covering the arch walls
- Bronze whaler sharks in summer months

## Dive Profile

This is a deep arch dive requiring careful depth management. Descend to the arch entrance (15-38m) and navigate the archway, keeping your depth monitored closely. The arch itself is short but very deep. The bouldery bottom on either side of the arch drops steeply beyond 50 meters, so stay focused on the structure. Allow time in shallower water (15-25m) to observe fish life without pushing depth limits. Current can be strong, so maintain excellent buoyancy and buddy contact. Plan conservative bottom times and adequate safety stops.

## Entry and Exit

Boat entry via giant stride. Your guide will brief on current direction, depth hazards, and safe descent procedures. The arch passage is wide enough for comfortable navigation but depth requires focus. Exit via ascent with safety stops and boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Deep dive experience required**: This is a 40m+ site—verify your qualifications
- **Expect current**: The current that makes this site special also requires excellent buoyancy and positioning
- **Cold water certification**: 5mm+ wetsuit or drysuit essential
- **Conservative depth management**: Plan dives to 40m maximum with good safety stop time
- **Buddy contact essential**: Current and depth increase the importance of buddy awareness
- **Best fish viewing at 20-35 meters**: This is the depth range where schools concentrate
- **Book with experienced guides**: Ask operators about recent conditions and current strength

## Safety Considerations

**Requirements:**
- Advanced open water certification with deep dive specialty
- Cold-water experience and appropriate thermal protection
- Excellent buoyancy control and depth awareness
- Awareness of nitrogen narcosis potential below 30m
- Conservative air consumption planning
- Strong current awareness and positioning skills
- Never dive alone at this site
- Monitor your air pressure continuously
- Understand your personal depth limits and respect them
- Current can increase difficulty unexpectedly—abort if conditions worsen

## Photography

Wide-angle shots of the arch structure with fish schools are striking. The interplay of current and fish creates dynamic compositions. Macro photography of wall-encrusting organisms is possible. Position yourself carefully in the current to maintain stable shooting position. Best photography occurs at 25-35m where fish life is dense but light is still strong.
