## San Miguel Island

San Miguel Island is a reef dive site off the small island of the same name in Sorsogon Bay near Donsol, offering coral reef diving in the same productive waters that attract the whale sharks and manta rays for which the region is internationally known.

## Overview

San Miguel Island is a small island in Sorsogon Bay, and its surrounding reef provides diving that complements the whale shark snorkeling experience that draws most visitors to Donsol. The island's reef extends around its perimeter, with the shallower sections suitable for snorkeling and the deeper slopes to 20 meters accessible to certified divers. The reef benefits from the productive upwelling waters of Sorsogon Bay, supporting a healthy coral ecosystem with typical Philippine reef fish diversity. The island's position in the bay offers some shelter from open-ocean conditions, making it accessible in a wider range of weather than more exposed sites.

## Site Information

- **Location**: San Miguel Island, Sorsogon Bay, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8-20 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate; bay location provides some shelter
- **Best Time**: November to June

## Marine Life

The island reef supports the productive marine community of Sorsogon Bay. Reef fish including parrotfish, surgeonfish, grouper, and wrasse are abundant. Sea turtles visit the reef regularly. The bay waters occasionally bring whale shark sightings to divers in the area — this is incidental rather than guaranteed, but memorable when it occurs. Schools of snapper and fusiliers are present throughout the reef. Manta ray sightings are possible seasonally.

## Dive Profile

Begin at the island's downstream current side and work around the reef perimeter, exploring the coral slope from 5 to 20 meters. The island reef allows a complete circumnavigation on a single dive depending on the island's size and swim pace. Finish on the shallower reef section before your safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by boat from Donsol operators. The island is a short boat ride from the town. Entry by giant stride; return to the boat by ladder. The sheltered bay location makes entry and exit generally easy.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Combine with a morning whale shark snorkeling interaction for the full Donsol experience
- The reef is suitable for snorkelers as well as divers — the shallow sections are equally productive above water
- Ask operators about recent whale shark sightings near the island during your stay
- Water clarity varies with plankton blooms in whale shark season; both reduced visibility and mantas are signs of productive water
- Bring a wide-angle setup for reef photography and be prepared for any large animal encounter

## Safety Considerations

Standard conditions in the sheltered bay. Currents are generally mild but can increase around the island's tips. Deploy SMB before surfacing. Be aware that whale shark boats may be operating in the area.

## Photography

The reef provides good wide-angle reef photography and fish portrait opportunities. Any whale shark encounter while diving is a rare and special photographic moment — have the camera ready. Natural light conditions in the bay are generally good from mid-morning.
