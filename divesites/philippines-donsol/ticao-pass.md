## Ticao Pass

Ticao Pass is one of the Philippines' most celebrated advanced drift dives, a powerful tidal channel between Ticao Island and the Sorsogon coast where manta ray aggregations and schooling pelagics make this a bucket-list dive site.

## Overview

The Ticao Pass cuts between Ticao Island and the Sorsogon mainland, channelling the tidal exchange between the Sibuyan Sea and the San Bernardino Strait. The resulting currents can be substantial, creating a nutrient-rich corridor that attracts large aggregations of marine life. Manta rays are the headline attraction — both reef mantas and occasional oceanic mantas feed here during plankton blooms, and concentrations of multiple animals performing barrel rolls are a documented highlight. The pass walls and reef structures are swept clean by constant current, supporting dense sea fan growth at depth. At 25 meters maximum accessible depth, the site covers the productive reef and water column zone where the most impressive encounters occur.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ticao Pass, Sorsogon, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (drift)
- **Site Type**: Drift dive / channel
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters (variable with plankton)
- **Current**: Strong tidal; direction reverses with tide
- **Best Time**: December to May (manta and whale shark season)

## Marine Life

Manta rays are the primary attraction at Ticao Pass, with multi-animal feeding aggregations possible during peak season. Whale sharks occasionally pass through the channel during their Sorsogon Bay feeding visits. Thresher sharks have been reported at depth in the pass on early morning dives. Large schools of barracuda and trevally are consistent inhabitants. Hammerhead sharks appear seasonally in the deeper water. The channel walls host excellent sea fan growth with associated fish communities.

## Dive Profile

Ticao Pass requires careful timing — the best diving is on the outgoing tide when current flows in a predictable direction. Enter upstream and drift through the pass at your target depth. The manta rays feed in the water column during active plankton periods; position yourself in the current flow at 10–20 meters to observe them. Deploy SMB for the drift ascent and exit the channel with the current.

## Entry and Exit

Boat-supported drift dive requiring an experienced skipper who knows the pass well. The boat must track the divers' SMB continuously throughout the drift. Entry by negative descent from upstream; exit downstream after completing the planned drift distance.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Only dive Ticao Pass with operators who specialise in this site — current knowledge and timing are critical
- The manta encounter protocol is strict: no pursuing, no touching, no bubble blowing in their faces; maintain 3-meter distance minimum
- Plankton-reduced visibility is a sign of productive water — don't be discouraged by reduced visibility if mantas are present
- Wide-angle photography setup before entry is essential — encounters can be brief
- This is one of the Philippines' top dive sites — consider planning your Donsol trip around the December to May manta peak season

## Safety Considerations

Ticao Pass drift diving is genuinely demanding. Strong current, potential for surge at the channel constriction, and open-water conditions require experienced divers with strong buoyancy control and drift diving skills. The current cannot be fought — if conditions deteriorate, abort. Always carry SMB and deploy it early. Emergency procedures must be clearly agreed before entry.

## Photography

Ticao Pass manta ray photography is among the most spectacular in the world. Wide-angle lenses (16–24mm equivalent) capture multiple mantas in formation against the blue water or channel reef background. Shooting from below with mantas silhouetted against surface light creates the most iconic images. Current requires fast shutter speeds and quick repositioning — be ready to work with what the current gives you.
