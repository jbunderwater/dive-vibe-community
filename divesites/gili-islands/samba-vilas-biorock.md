## Samba Vilas Biorock

A Biorock coral restoration structure on Gili Trawangan's east coast, part of the world's second-largest Biorock reef restoration project.

## Overview

Samba Vilas Biorock is one of the cluster of Biorock structures positioned along the east coast of Gili Trawangan, where the sheltered conditions and calm water allow for consistent electrical connection and uninterrupted coral growth stimulation. The Gili Islands Biorock program began in 2004–2005 under the Gili Eco Trust, and each structure in the cluster has developed its own character as different species of coral and fish have established over time. The Biorock project has been instrumental in increasing the biodiversity of the Gili Islands' marine environment and protecting the islands' coastlines from erosion.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East coast Gili Trawangan, Gili Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Artificial
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–20 m
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Reef fish communities established on the coral-covered frame. Lionfish, sweetlips, pufferfish, and batfish shelter within the structure. Turtles cruise past the east coast Biorock cluster. Nudibranchs and cleaner shrimps inhabit the coral crevices. The surrounding sandy bottom has attracted garden eels near the base of the structure.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the structure and explore its coral coverage. Examine the frame systematically for residents. Extend the dive to include other nearby Biorock structures in the east coast cluster before ascending for the safety stop. Multiple structures can be visited in a single dive.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Gili Trawangan east coast. Calm, sheltered conditions. Entry via back roll or giant stride. Straightforward return to mooring.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The east coast Biorock cluster has multiple structures that can all be visited in a single dive
- Good site for beginner divers to explore a structured and calm environment
- Night dives on the Biorocks reveal octopus, crabs, and other nocturnal species
- The conservation story is worth understanding — talk to your operator about the Gili Eco Trust
- Works well as a second dive after a deeper natural reef dive when air budget is lower

## Safety Considerations

Standard precautions for artificial structure dives — no touching the frame, maintain buoyancy, watch for lionfish. The low-voltage current is safe and harmless. Keep fins well clear of the structure to prevent damaging growing coral colonies.

## Photography

The Biorock frames covered in growing coral make visually interesting abstract subjects. Multiple structures photographed together show the scale of the restoration project. Fish portraits against the coral-encrusted steel frame create distinctive backgrounds uncommon in natural reef photography.
