## Embudu Channel / Embudu Express

Embudu Channel (Embudu Express) is one of the Maldives' best-known current dives in South Male Atoll — a protected marine area channel approximately 15 minutes by boat from Embudu Village where incoming tides can generate currents up to 5–6 knots.

## Overview

The "Express" name refers specifically to the high-speed current conditions when the incoming tide pushes through the channel. Divers descend to around 30 metres at the deep channel edge and are swept along large overhangs packed with colourful hard corals, soft corals, and sea fans. The Diverland Embudu dive centre — the resident operator — documents confirmed species at this site as eagle rays (sometimes 20 or more in formation), large stingrays, schools of tuna and barracuda, grouper, and sharks. The site is a protected marine area partly due to its resident grey reef and whitetip reef shark population. Visibility is good (10–30 m) and averages 18 m. The site is recommended for experienced divers only, and Nitrox is advised by the resident operator for extended bottom time at 30 m.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South Male Atoll, Maldives (15 minutes by boat from Embudu Village)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive (Protected Marine Area)
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30–32 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–30 metres
- **Current**: Up to 5–6 knots on strong incoming tide

## Marine Life

Eagle rays (formations of 20+ confirmed), large stingrays, tuna schools, barracuda, grouper, grey reef sharks, whitetip reef sharks. Overhangs lined with hard corals, soft corals, and sea fans. Diverland records this as one of their most productive shark dives.

## Tips for Divers

Nitrox is recommended for extended bottom time at 30 m. Time the dive with the incoming tide — the divemaster positions the boat at the channel mouth. Stay close to the wall; currents can push you into blue water. An SMB is mandatory. Not suitable for first-time drift divers.
