## Bolifushi

**Location:** North Malé Atoll, Maldives
**Depth:** 5–25 m
**Difficulty:** Intermediate
**Entry:** Boat

### Overview

Bolifushi is a wreck site in North Malé Atoll, where a sunken vessel has over time been colonised by corals and become an artificial reef. Wreck diving in the Maldives combines the fascination of structural exploration with the rich marine life that takes advantage of the hard substrate. The Bolifushi wreck has been in place long enough for substantial coral growth to soften the original hull lines and create a flourishing habitat.

### Diving

The wreck sits in water shallow enough to allow multiple dive profiles. The upper sections can be explored in the 5–15 m range, while the seabed around the wreck is at around 25 m. The hull and superstructure are encrusted with hard and soft corals, sea fans, and sponges. Schools of glassfish congregate around the wreck structure, drawing hunting lionfish and trevally.

Moray eels occupy gaps and recesses in the metal structure, and scorpionfish use their camouflage to blend with the encrusted surfaces. Surgeonfish and parrotfish graze the coral growth on the hull. For divers interested in wreck penetration, the accessible interior spaces offer further exploration opportunities.

### Marine Life

- Moray eels and scorpionfish
- Glassfish schools and hunting lionfish
- Trevally and tuna (occasional)
- Grouper and sweetlips
- Corals and sponges encrusting the hull

### Practical Notes

A well-established wreck site suitable for intermediate divers. Penetration of interior spaces requires proper wreck diving training and equipment. Visibility typically 15–20 m. Boat access from North Malé Atoll resorts.
