## ? thila

This unnamed thila (submerged pinnacle) in South Ari Atoll is a classic Maldivian pinnacle dive — a coral-encrusted seamount rising from a sandy bottom at 30 metres to within 8–10 metres of the surface, completely surrounded by open water. The site's unresolved name in dive records suggests it is infrequently visited, giving it an exploratory character.

### Site Conditions

- **Depth range**: 8–30 m
- **Typical visibility**: 15–30 m
- **Currents**: Variable; can be strong around the exposed pinnacle
- **Best season**: November–April for northeast monsoon clarity

### What to Expect

Thilas in the Maldives concentrate marine life in a way few other reef structures can match. This pinnacle's flanks are encrusted with sea fans, soft corals, and encrusting sponges, with schooling fusiliers and glassfish creating shimmering clouds around the summit. Reef whitetip sharks rest along the base of the pinnacle, while grey reef sharks occasionally circle in the blue water beyond. Napoleon wrasse, large grouper, and hawksbill turtles are consistent residents. The sandy bottom surrounding the structure hosts blue-spotted rays and small gobies tending their burrows.

### Tips for Divers

Pinnacle dives require confident buoyancy control — the open-water setting means there is no easy shallowing option if depth discipline lapses. Circumnavigating the pinnacle on a single dive is achievable with careful gas management. A reef hook kept ready allows divers to hold position on the current-exposed side without exertion, maximising shark observation time. Wide-angle photography capturing the full pinnacle silhouette against the blue works best around midday when light penetration is optimal.
