## Rannamaari Wreck

The Rannamaari Wreck lies in North Male Atoll, a steel-hulled vessel resting on a sloping sandy bottom that drops to around 42 metres at the deepest extent of the hull. The wreck is relatively intact and has developed into a mature artificial reef over the years since sinking, offering a rewarding penetration dive for experienced divers.

### Site Conditions

- **Depth range**: 25–42 m
- **Typical visibility**: 15–30 m
- **Currents**: Variable; check conditions before penetrating the hull
- **Best season**: December–April for clearest visibility

### What to Expect

The superstructure and hull plating are densely colonised with encrusting soft corals, hydroids, and sponges. Lion fish hover in the shadows of the cargo holds, and large grouper have claimed resident positions in the deeper sections of the wreck. Glassfish schools fill the mid-water around the hull, attracting predatory trevally that stage swift ambush runs. Nurse sharks rest on the sandy bottom alongside the lower hull, and moray eels emerge from every available crevice at night. The upper sections of the wreck sit within recreational limits and receive good ambient light.

### Tips for Divers

Advanced certification and wreck diving experience are recommended before attempting penetration of the deeper hull sections. Penetration requires a torch and wreck buoyancy skills; silt disturbance reduces visibility rapidly inside the vessel. A nitrox fill extends bottom time on the shallower sections of the wreck without incurring decompression obligations.
