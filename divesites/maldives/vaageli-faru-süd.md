## Vaageli Faru Süd

Vaageli Faru Süd ("South Reef of Vaageli") is the southern section of the Vaageli reef formation in South Male Atoll, sitting adjacent to Vaageli Faru Nord and providing a complementary dive along the south-facing reef wall. The south exposure gives different current characteristics and a distinct character to the marine life distribution compared to the northern section.

### Site Conditions

- **Depth range**: 5–25 m
- **Typical visibility**: 15–25 m
- **Currents**: Light to moderate; often calmer than the north wall
- **Best season**: November–April for best conditions

### What to Expect

The south-facing wall is more sheltered during the northeast monsoon, resulting in a gentler dive profile with excellent coral coverage and rich invertebrate diversity on the reef face. Soft corals and sea fans are more prevalent here than on the exposed north wall, and the calmer conditions attract schooling surgeonfish and rabbitfish that graze the algae on the upper reef. Nurse sharks and white-tip reef sharks are particularly abundant resting on sandy patches at depth, often accessible for close-range photography. Large grouper inhabit the deeper overhangs, and hawksbill turtles are a consistent presence throughout the dive.

### Tips for Divers

The southern exposure makes this an excellent choice when the northeast monsoon picks up — the sheltered wall produces calmer entry and exit conditions. The site pairs naturally with Vaageli Faru Nord and Vaageli Faru Tila for a complete survey of the Vaageli reef system in a single half-day trip. Depth profiles are forgiving for air management, making this a relaxed, photogenic site.
