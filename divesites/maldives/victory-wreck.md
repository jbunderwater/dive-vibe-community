## Victory Wreck

Victory Wreck is an advanced wreck dive site located in South Male Atoll, Maldives.

## Overview

Victory Wreck is a challenging deep wreck dive for experienced divers. The wreck lies at significant depth with the shallowest point around 20 meters and the bottom at 35-38 meters. The site features an intact wreck structure with excellent visibility. This is a technical wreck dive requiring advanced training, excellent buoyancy control, and careful air and decompression management.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South Male Atoll, Maldives
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 38 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-40 meters (80-130 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: October to April (dry season)

## Marine Life

Divers at this site can expect to encounter batfish, lionfish, sweetlips, coral growth, soft corals, manta rays, whale sharks, reef sharks. Additional species commonly sighted include sea turtles, barracuda, trevally. The wreck structure provides shelter and habitat for a thriving marine ecosystem, attracting both resident and transient species.

## Dive Profile

Victory Wreck is a deep wreck dive requiring careful planning. Descent is typically made to the wreck at 20-25 meters, with exploration continuing deeper to 35-38 meters. The intact wreck structure provides multiple exploration opportunities, but penetration should only be attempted by technical divers. Bottom time is limited to 15-20 minutes at depth before mandatory decompression. Multiple safety stops at 12, 9, and 6 meters are required.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced wreck diver training (such as PADI Deep or Wreck Diver) strongly recommended
- Carry a dive computer capable of calculating decompression
- Double the gas supplies recommended
- Use a descent line and maintain visual reference at all times
- A torch is essential even with good visibility
- Plan your bottom time strictly — calculate nitrogen loading carefully
- Consider hiring a guide familiar with this wreck
- This is best treated as a technical dive

## Safety Considerations

This is a technical dive and should be approached with serious planning. Nitrogen narcosis becomes a factor at these depths — be aware of cognitive changes and take extra precautions. Decompression obligation is mandatory and non-negotiable. Strong currents are possible. Do not attempt this dive without advanced training and proper equipment. Always dive with an experienced buddy. Ascend on a line if available and maintain positive buoyancy during decompression. An SMB deployment during ascent is mandatory.

## Photography

The wreck structure provides dramatic wide-angle subjects with natural light filtering through openings. A torch is essential for illuminating interior details and bringing out colors. Macro opportunities abound on the encrusted surfaces.
