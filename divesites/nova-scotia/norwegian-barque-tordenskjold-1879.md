## Norwegian barque Tordenskjold (1879)

The Tordenskjold was a Norwegian sailing barque lost at the entrance to Halifax Harbour near Maugher's Beach on McNabs Island. Historical accounts record that the vessel lost steering control while tacking into the harbour when the lighthouse took the wind from her sails, driving her onto the shoals near the beach. The crew scrambled safely ashore with no reported casualties.

**Date discrepancy:** The site is dated 1879 in our OSM data, but historical accounts accessed via the Nova Scotia Museum's shipwreck database reference an 1872 or 1879 entry depending on the source. The Maritime Museum of the Atlantic's "On the Rocks" database had a record for this vessel (ID 2971), but direct database access is no longer available online. The quarter name board "Tordem Skjold" was reportedly displayed at the Maugher Beach boathouse for years afterward. Neither 1872 nor 1879 can be definitively confirmed without consulting the museum's records. Both years appear in independent sources.

The previous description's claim of "excellent preservation of wooden structures" at 29 metres is unsupported — no dive report source was found confirming the wreck's current condition, and after 140+ years in Halifax Harbour waters, wooden hulls typically have limited remaining structure.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Maugher's Beach, McNabs Island, Halifax Harbour entrance, Nova Scotia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 29 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 4-15°C (winter-summer)

## Entry and Exit

Access by boat to McNabs Island area. Contact East Coast Scuba & Watersports (Bedford) or Torpedo Rays (Dartmouth) for local guidance on this site. Halifax Harbour diving requires following Nova Scotia Underwater Council protocols due to vessel traffic.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Cold Atlantic water (4–15°C) requires a drysuit or very thick wetsuit
- Halifax Harbour has significant vessel traffic — use a surface marker buoy (SMB)
- Wooden wreck may have limited remaining structure after 140+ years
