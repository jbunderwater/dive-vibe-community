## British tanker Kars (1942)

**Location note:** The motor tanker Kars was torpedoed approximately 25 miles south-southeast of Halifax (44°15'N, 63°25'W) on February 22, 1942 — not in the Shubenacadie Channel as previously described. The vessel broke in two; the forward section sank offshore. The aft section was towed to Halifax and beached on February 27, 1942, and became a total loss. The OSM coordinate for this site (near McNabs Island, Halifax Harbour) likely corresponds to remnants of the beached aft section, not the primary offshore wreck location. No dive operator lists this as a confirmed accessible dive site.

**Previous versions of this description incorrectly placed the wreck "in the Shubenacadie Channel." The torpedoing occurred ~25 miles offshore; only beached aft section remnants are near Halifax.**

## Wreck History

The Kars was an 8,888 GRT British motor tanker built in 1939 by Blythswood Shipbuilding Co Ltd, Glasgow. She was carrying 12,700 tons of aviation spirit and fuel oil from Trinidad to Belfast via Halifax when she was torpedoed on February 22, 1942 at 22:57 hours by U-96 (Kapitänleutnant Heinrich Lehmann-Willenbrock). The vessel caught fire and broke in two three days later. Of the 47 persons aboard (master, 39 crew, 5 gunners, 2 others), 46 perished — one of the worst single-vessel loss-of-life incidents in Nova Scotia maritime history. The forward section sank at the attack position; the aft section was towed to Halifax and beached February 27, declared a total loss. Sources: two independent (uboat.net, Virtlo location data).
