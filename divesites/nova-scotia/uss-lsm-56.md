## USS LSM-56

**Location note:** This wreck is located in the **Musquash Estuary, New Brunswick** (not Nova Scotia or Passamaquoddy Bay, despite the Nova Scotia dataset listing). The OSM coordinates (45.1931°N, 66.2717°W) place this site in the Saint John area of New Brunswick, near the Musquash Estuary.

USS LSM-56 was a US Navy Landing Ship Medium (LSM) — 203 ft 6 in (62 m) long, displacing 520 tons light — decommissioned in 1946 and sold as surplus in 1947 to the St. John Tugboat Company of New Brunswick. The vessel was put out of service around 1960 (the exact abandonment date is unknown per the UNB Maritime Heritage Project), with scrapping abandoned mid-process, leaving the hull at the Musquash Estuary where it remains. The UNB database describes "two beached hulks" and "one submerged craft" at this location; which of these is LSM-56 specifically is not confirmed in sources consulted. A companion unidentified LSM and the USS LSM-46 are located nearby.

## Overview

USS LSM-56 is a wreck site in the Musquash Estuary near Saint John, New Brunswick. The vessel rests in the intertidal zone and is visible above water at low tide. Diving depth is shallow (maximum approximately 18 m to surrounding seabed). The site is accessible but the Bay of Fundy tides dominate conditions — the world's highest tides produce strong currents in this estuary, requiring tidal planning for any diving.

## Wreck History

USS LSM-56 was laid down in 1944, commissioned 5 October 1944, and deployed to the Pacific theater. She was decommissioned 18 March 1946 and sold on 23 October 1947 to the St. John Tugboat Company of East Saint John, New Brunswick. Her commercial life ended around 1960 when she was sold for scrapping; however, scrapping was never completed and the hull was abandoned at the Musquash Estuary. The vessel measures 203 ft 6 in (62.03 m) in length, with a beam of 34 ft 6 in (10.52 m) and light displacement of 520 long tons. A companion vessel (USS LSM-46) and another unidentified LSM-class vessel are located nearby in the same area.

## Marine Life

Regional marine life in the Bay of Fundy / Musquash Estuary includes Atlantic herring, Atlantic cod, lobster, rock crab, green urchin, and cold-water anemones. The Musquash Estuary is a Marine Protected Area supporting significant biodiversity. No site-specific diver species reports were found for the LSM-56 wreck itself.

## Safety Considerations

Bay of Fundy tidal flows are extreme in this area — timing all dives to slack water is essential. Cold water year-round (4–12°C) requires a drysuit. The Musquash Estuary is a federal Marine Protected Area (DFO); consult relevant regulations before diving.
