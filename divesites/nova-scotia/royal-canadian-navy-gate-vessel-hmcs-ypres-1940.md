## Royal Canadian Navy gate vessel HMCS Ypres (1940)

HMCS Ypres was a Royal Canadian Navy Battle-class trawler that served as Gate Vessel 1 at Halifax Harbour, controlling the anti-submarine boom net across the harbour entrance. On May 12, 1940, it was rammed by the departing British battleship HMS Revenge when the boom gate was not fully open in time. Dragged roughly 500 yards attached to the boom, the vessel sank at approximately 21:25 with no loss of life — 13 crew were rescued by HMS Revenge and 5 more by the sister gate vessel HMCS Festubert (Gate Vessel 2). The wreck rests in Halifax Harbour at approximately 27 meters. This dive site was not listed by East Coast Scuba or other Nova Scotia dive operators in sources available as of 2026; contact local operators for current access information.

**Note on vessel classification:** HMCS Ypres was originally built as a Battle-class naval trawler (1917), later held a minesweeper administrative designation (1922–1938), but was serving as a gate vessel — not a minesweeper — at the time of sinking in 1940.

## Wreck History

- **Built:** Polson Iron Works, Toronto; launched June 16, 1917; commissioned November 13, 1917
- **Displacement:** 320 long tons
- **Length:** 130 ft (40 m); beam 23 ft 5 in; draft 13 ft 5 in
- **Sunk:** May 12, 1940, Halifax Harbour — rammed by HMS Revenge; no lives lost
- **Cause:** Collision during harbour departure maneuver; boom gate inadequately opened before Revenge approached
