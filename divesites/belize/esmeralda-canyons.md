## Esmeralda Canyons

Esmeralda Canyons is a canyon and reef dive site on the Belize Barrier Reef, approximately five minutes by boat from San Pedro on Ambergris Caye.

## Overview

The Esmeralda Canyons site consists of multiple parallel slot-canyon formations cut into the barrier reef. Frenchie's Diving Services describes the experience as "swimming up and down rows of bookshelves in a coral library" — the canyon walls rise close on both sides for much of the dive route. Swim-throughs connect some of the canyon sections.

PADI's dive site entry records GPS coordinates at approximately 17.910°N, 87.952°W and a maximum depth of 24 metres (78 feet). Frenchie's Diving Services, which regularly runs trips to this site, provides a maximum depth of 21 metres (70 feet) and confirms nurse sharks and Caribbean reef sharks as consistent presences in the canyon formations. Groupers, lobsters, and eels are also documented at this specific site by Frenchie's trip descriptions.

Esmeralda is typically paired with the nearby Tackle Box site (canyons, caverns, and tunnels immediately off Ambergris Caye) for a two-tank day trip. Visibility at Esmeralda averages around 21 metres (70 feet). The site is appropriate for Intermediate-level Open Water certified divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Belize Barrier Reef, approximately 5 minutes by boat from San Pedro, Ambergris Caye
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
