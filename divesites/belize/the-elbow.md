## The Elbow

The Elbow is an Advanced drift dive site at the southernmost tip of Turneffe Atoll, approximately 32 kilometres (20 miles) east of Belize City.

## Overview

The Elbow occupies the point where the Turneffe Atoll's southern reef changes direction, creating a convergence of ocean currents. This current convergence generates nutrient-rich upwellings that support high densities of both reef and pelagic species. The site is widely regarded by operators as the most spectacular dive on Turneffe Atoll and one of the premier drift dives in Belize.

The reef crest lies at approximately 24–27 metres (80–90 feet); drift dives typically end in the 36–42 metre (120–140 feet) range before being brought back to shallower depths. Currents run at approximately 2 knots from the north, and their strength makes this site unsuitable for beginners. Advanced Open Water certification is required or strongly recommended by all operators who run trips here.

Belize Dive Haven Resort describes large formations of gorgonian fan corals as a defining feature of the wall here. Confirmed sightings by multiple operators (Belize Dive Haven, Turneffe Atoll Marine Reserve, Turneffe Flats Lodge) include schools of eagle rays — often seen in groups — horse-eye jacks, cubera snappers, large groupers, reef sharks, barracudas, nurse sharks, hawksbill and loggerhead sea turtles, and moray eels. The Turneffe Atoll Marine Reserve website characterizes The Elbow as offering "phenomenal biodiversity" attributable to the current-driven feeding conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern tip of Turneffe Atoll, approximately 32 km east of Belize City
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
