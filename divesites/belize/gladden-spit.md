## Gladden Spit

Gladden Spit is a seasonal drift dive site at the easternmost point of the Belize Barrier Reef, within the Gladden Spit and Silk Cayes Marine Reserve, located approximately 36 kilometres (22 miles) off the coast of Placencia.

## Overview

Gladden Spit marks where the southern Belize Barrier Reef makes an almost ninety-degree turn eastward. The underwater promontory features a short sloping shelf that drops steeply from about 40 metres to 2,000 metres depth within 10 kilometres of the reef edge. Diving is conducted mid-water at depths no greater than 24 metres (80 feet), typically in the 15–20 metre range, without reef or bottom reference — this is a blue-water dive requiring strong buoyancy control.

The site is globally significant as a spawning aggregation zone. Multiple fish species — including cubera snapper, mutton snapper, and dog snapper — aggregate here to spawn between March and June, timed to the full moon through last-quarter lunar phase. Whale sharks gather at the surface and mid-water column to feed on the broadcast spawn. This spawning-driven aggregation is the primary reason divers visit Gladden Spit; outside of spawning season, the site still offers wall diving with Caribbean reef sharks and other large pelagics, but operator trips are infrequent.

Group sizes are strictly limited (typically a maximum of 12 divers per session) to reduce disturbance to the sharks. Advanced Open Water certification and recent dive experience are required by all permitted operators. The Gladden Spit and Silk Cayes Marine Reserve was established in 2003 and is managed by the Southern Environmental Association.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gladden Spit and Silk Cayes Marine Reserve, 36 km off Placencia, Stann Creek District
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Season**: March–June (spawning aggregation and whale sharks); wall diving possible year-round
