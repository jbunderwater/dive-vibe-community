## Laughing Bird Caye

Laughing Bird Caye is a reef dive area surrounding a small National Park island approximately 19 kilometres (12 miles) east of Placencia in the Stann Creek District, Belize.

## Overview

Laughing Bird Caye sits atop a geological formation called a faro — an angular atoll structure on a continental shelf — which creates unusually diverse coral habitats: fringing reef, spur-and-groove formations, patch reefs, and occasional drop-offs, all within a compact area. The caye's 0.73-hectare (1.8-acre) island was declared a protected area in 1981 and a National Park in 1991, and became part of the UNESCO World Heritage Belize Barrier Reef Reserve System in 1996.

Multiple named dive sites exist within the park. Laughing Bird Drop Off begins at 5–8 metres (15–25 feet) on a sandy bottom suitable for training dives, with a gradual coral wall sloping to 24 metres (80 feet). Coral Garden, located about 0.8 kilometres (0.5 miles) southeast, is noted for high coral diversity and density. Laughing Bird North is specifically noted for queen conch and spiny lobsters. Laughing Bird South features a canyon formation.

Operator descriptions (Splash Dive Center, Go Sea Belize) list spotted drums, large spiny lobsters, cleaner shrimp, arrow crabs, groupers, snapper, barracuda, and southern stingrays as recurring sightings at the park's dive sites. These are site-specific operator observations. Conditions are generally calm, making this a practical destination for Open Water-certified divers.

The boat ride from Placencia takes 30–40 minutes.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Laughing Bird Caye National Park, 19 km east of Placencia, Stann Creek District
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
