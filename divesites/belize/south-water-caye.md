## South Water Caye

South Water Caye is a reef and wall dive area within the South Water Caye Marine Reserve — at approximately 47,673 hectares (117,878 acres), the largest marine reserve in Belize — located off the coast of Stann Creek District.

## Overview

South Water Caye sits directly atop the Belize Barrier Reef, placing it at the boundary between shallow inner lagoon habitats and deep outer wall diving. The reserve stretches from the barrier reef east to the open sea, encompassing both patch reefs within the lagoon and vertical wall formations along the outer reef edge.

Multiple named sites exist within the reserve. Rosalie's Garden features a wall starting at about 14 metres (45 feet) with dives typically conducted around 18 metres (60 feet); small crevices in the wall face provide shelter for spiny lobsters, spider crabs, and spotted eels. The Abyss begins at about 12 metres (40 feet) on healthy coral formations that slope toward the wall edge before dropping into deep water. Trick Ridge, located east of Carrie Bow Caye, presents a sand channel flanked by hard and soft corals; the site is locally nicknamed "Eagle Ray City" for the frequency of spotted eagle ray sightings. Southern stingrays are also confirmed at multiple South Water Caye sites by multiple operator sources (Seahorse Dive Shop, Go Sea Belize).

Visibility at these sites regularly exceeds 30 metres (100 feet). Depths across the named sites range from about 12–18 metres operating depth, with wall faces dropping well beyond recreational limits. The site suits Intermediate-level certified divers. The boat ride from Dangriga takes approximately 35 minutes; from Placencia approximately 60–75 minutes.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South Water Caye Marine Reserve, Stann Creek District, Belize
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
