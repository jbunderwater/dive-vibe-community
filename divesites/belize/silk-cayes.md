## Silk Cayes

Silk Cayes (also called Queen Cayes) is a reef dive area around three small uninhabited islands on the outer fringe of the Belize Barrier Reef, approximately 20 miles east of Placencia, within the Gladden Spit and Silk Cayes Marine Reserve.

## Overview

The Silk Cayes sit at the junction of the outer reef crest and the open Caribbean. The dive area offers multiple distinct experiences along the same stretch of reef. One common dive profile starts at 24 metres (80 feet) on the outer wall face, then ascends to about 12 metres (40 feet) on the reef crest — a multi-level dive through the canyons and spur-and-groove formations. Another local dive route stays in the 15–18 metre (50–60 foot) range entirely within canyon formations. A third dive profile begins on a sandy bottom at about 10 metres (30 feet).

Operator trip reports (Splash Dive Center, Captain Jak's) document hawksbill turtles, spotted eagle rays, large black groupers, horse-eye jacks, schoolmasters, green moray eels, spotted moray eels, and nurse sharks as regular sightings at this specific site. Snorkeling is also offered at Silk Cayes for shallower sections.

The Silk Cayes are accessible by a 45–60 minute boat ride from Placencia. The dive site is suitable for Open Water-certified divers; the outer wall portions and deeper canyons are more appropriate for Intermediate-level divers. All divers require a licensed guide within the marine reserve.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gladden Spit and Silk Cayes Marine Reserve, 20 miles east of Placencia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
