## Sayonara Wreck

Sayonara Wreck is a deliberately sunk artificial reef at Turneffe Atoll, located in the southwestern area of the atoll.

## Overview

The Sayonara was the transport boat for the Turneffe Island Lodge. It was deliberately sunk by Dave Bennett in 1985 to create an artificial reef diving destination. The wreck now rests at approximately 15–18 metres (50–60 feet) depth, southwest of the Turneffe Islands.

No independent records of the vessel's build date, country of registry, dimensions, or tonnage were located. All available sources describe the vessel only as a transport boat. The 1985 intentional sinking and the vessel's prior role as the Turneffe Island Lodge transport are confirmed by Wannadive.net's dive logs and Dive-the-World's Belize reference.

In the decades since sinking, the hull has become colonized by soft corals, sponges, and encrusting organisms. Wannadive.net dive log entries document a large green moray eel, spotted sea cucumber, grey angelfish, lobsters, flamingo tongue shells, and hard coral formations as specific observations at this site. Dive-the-World describes the wreck as supporting schools of French grunts, parrotfish, and angelfish. Currents at the site are typically minimal. The site is described as suitable for Open Water-certified divers.

**Note on vessel history:** Build date, vessel dimensions, tonnage, and flag state were not found in any source consulted. The 1985 sinking and transport-boat role are the only vessel facts confirmed across multiple independent sources.
