## Shark Hole

Shark Hole is a cavern and sinkhole dive site located inside the main Belize Barrier Reef, four miles northwest of Tobacco Caye, approximately 58 kilometres (36 miles) from Placencia.

## Overview

Shark Hole is the remnant of a formerly enclosed domed cave. The roof partially collapsed, creating a roughly 45-metre-wide (150-foot-wide) opening. Entering the site at approximately 9 metres (30 feet) depth, the cavern drops progressively — the domed ceiling with stalactites is encountered at around 18 metres (60 feet), a central sandhill rises from the floor at approximately 30 metres (100 feet), and the deepest point at the chamber edge reaches about 40 metres (130 feet). A shaft of natural light illuminates the central sandhill through the surface opening; a dive light is required once divers move into the upper cavern areas away from the opening.

The site's name derives from the multiple shark species regularly observed circling the central sandhill. Seahorse Dive Shop's trip documentation lists bull sharks, nurse sharks, lemon sharks, and Caribbean reef sharks as site-specific observations. Schools of permit and tarpon are also documented at the entrance. Operator dive briefings classify this as a cavern dive — not a full cave dive — and no penetration beyond the natural cavern opening is involved.

All operators (Seahorse Dive Shop, Splash Dive Center) require Open Water certification with recent dive experience, and classify the site as Intermediate to Advanced due to depth and cavern environment. Snorkeling is not offered at this site. Seahorse Dive Shop typically pairs this site with two additional dives at South Water Caye Marine Reserve in the same day trip.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Inside the Belize Barrier Reef, 4 miles northwest of Tobacco Caye, ~58 km from Placencia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
