## Shark Airport

One of Tubbataha's most famous dive sites, named for the sight of multiple resting whitetip sharks lined up on the shallow plateau like aircraft at an airport.

## Overview

Shark Airport is one of the signature Tubbataha sites, known for reliable shark encounters and the distinctive naming reference. The site features a wide plateau at shallow depth that transitions to a deeper shelf, creating an ideal setup for experiencing Tubbataha's abundant shark populations. The site's name comes from the common sight of whitetip sharks resting on the plateau, their arrangement resembling aircraft lined up near airport hangars.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Atoll, Philippines Tubbataha Reefs
- **Entry Type**: liveaboard
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 32 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 30-45 meters
- **Current**: Variable, can be strong
- **Best Time**: March 16 - June 15

## Marine Life

Shark Airport is famous for large populations of whitetip sharks and blacktip reef sharks. The wider plateau and drop-off structure also attracts grey reef sharks, silky sharks, and occasional whale sharks or guitar sharks. Other species include trevally, snappers, jacks, and various schooling reef fish. The plateau provides habitat for nudibranchs, lionfish, and other benthic species.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically begins on the plateau at 15 meters, where most of the resting sharks are concentrated. Spend time here photographing and observing behavior before gradually exploring deeper sections of the plateau. The shelf drops to about 25 meters, offering good viewing of sharks in transition. The wall continues deeper to 30+ meters, but most activity and shark encounters happen in the shallower sections. Plan for a gradual exploration of depth rather than deep descents.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by liveaboard tender. Entry is via giant stride or back roll as directed. Most dives start with a descent to the plateau at 15 meters using a natural guide or shot line. Plan your ascent gradually from the deeper sections, completing a safety stop at 5 meters. Signal your boat with an SMB for pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Position yourself on the plateau early to observe resting sharks before they scatter
- Move slowly and deliberately to avoid startling the sharks
- Look along the drop-off edges for large pelagic species
- Bring a wide-angle lens for shark photography - you'll want to capture their formation
- This is an excellent site for documenting natural shark behavior

## Safety Considerations

Sharks here are not aggressive toward divers and are typically indifferent to human presence. Maintain calm, slow movements and avoid sudden gestures. This is fundamentally a safe site for intermediate divers. Variable currents may occur - be prepared to adjust position if conditions change. Never attempt to touch or chase any sharks.

## Photography

The arrangement of resting sharks on the plateau creates compelling compositions. Shoot wide-angle from 12-18 meters to capture multiple sharks and their positioning. Dawn dives often provide the best light and highest shark density on the plateau.
