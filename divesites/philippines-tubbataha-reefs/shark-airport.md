## Shark Airport

Shark Airport is one of Tubbataha's most evocatively named dive sites, where the frequency of reef shark arrivals and departures has earned it a name that captures the almost traffic-like nature of shark activity at this productive section of the North Atoll wall.

## Overview

Located on North Atoll in the Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park, Shark Airport sits on the atoll's reef structure where sharks use the wall as a reference for their movements, arriving and departing in numbers that can seem constant during productive dive periods. The name is apt — rather than a single shark encounter, divers at Shark Airport experience a continuous procession of whitetip and grey reef sharks that have turned this section of reef into a regular patrol route. The wall drops from near-surface coral gardens to 32 meters at the accessible limit, with the shark activity concentrated in the 15–30 meter zone. Sea fans and soft corals carpet the wall below 15 meters, creating a colourful backdrop for the shark activity above them.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Atoll, Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park, Sulu Sea, Philippines
- **Entry Type**: liveaboard
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 32 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Current**: Moderate to strong along the wall
- **Best Time**: March to June

## Marine Life

Reef sharks — primarily whitetip and grey reef sharks — are the defining feature of Shark Airport and are present in impressive numbers throughout most dives. The continuous nature of shark activity here is remarkable even by Tubbataha standards. Napoleon wrasse and large grouper rest in coral overhangs and cleaning stations on the wall. Schools of jacks and barracuda exploit the current-fed upwellings. Eagle rays are occasional visitors to the deeper sections. Sea turtles cross the wall regularly. Manta rays visit during their seasonal appearances in the park.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the prime shark observation zone at 20–25 meters and position yourself on or just off the wall. The sharks here are accustomed to diver presence and will continue their patrols without fleeing. Remain still or move slowly along the wall, observing without pursuing. The deeper sections to 32 meters offer less coral cover but more shark activity in the open water. Ascend via the colourful upper wall and safety stop on the shallow reef plateau.

## Entry and Exit

Liveaboard access only. Dinghy entry by giant stride; SMB required for return to dinghy. Park ranger protocols apply to all Tubbataha diving. The site may be on a drift plan depending on current conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The shark activity at this site is continuous during good conditions — spend the maximum permitted time at your target depth
- Stay calm and still; the sharks at Shark Airport are habituated to divers and remain close when not disturbed
- Do not chase or approach sharks quickly — they will circle away and the encounter ends
- Early morning dives see the highest shark numbers; sharks seem to patrol the wall most actively before midday
- This is a compelling site for underwater video as well as stills; the sustained shark activity lends itself to sequences and behaviour documentation

## Safety Considerations

Depths to 32 meters require attention to no-decompression limits, particularly on multi-dive days in Tubbataha where exciting encounters can tempt divers to stay deeper longer than planned. Monitor depth carefully during shark encounters. Current along the wall requires awareness. Emergency evacuation from Tubbataha is a multi-hour operation.

## Photography

Shark Airport is one of the world's premier shark photography sites. Multiple sharks in a single wide-angle frame, shark portraits against the sea fan wall, and behaviour documentation of patrol patterns are all achievable. The exceptional visibility and calm sharks make this more accessible to photographers than most shark diving destinations. Bring a reliable strobe system for the deeper sections.
