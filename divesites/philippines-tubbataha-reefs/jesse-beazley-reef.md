## Jesse Beazley Reef

A compact but stunning isolated reef roughly 20 km northwest of North Atoll, featuring a mushroom-shaped structure with an undercut reef top. Home to turtles, whale sharks, hammerheads, and diverse colorful fish.

## Overview

Jesse Beazley Reef is an isolated atoll located approximately 20 km northwest of Tubbataha's North Atoll. This small but spectacular reef delivers outsized diving experiences. The reef features a distinctive mushroom shape created by undercut terrain, where the reef top overhangs the deeper structure. This unique topography combined with isolation from the main Tubbataha complex creates distinctive dive conditions and marine life assemblages.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Isolated reef, 20 km northwest of North Atoll, Philippines Tubbataha Reefs
- **Entry Type**: liveaboard
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 30-45 meters

## Marine Life

Jesse Beazley Reef hosts an impressive array of marine life including sea turtles, whale sharks, hammerhead sharks, and diverse colorful reef fish species. The isolation from the main atoll complex creates unique species assemblages and potentially more pristine reef conditions. Pelagic encounters are common given the open ocean location. Schooling fish, groupers, snappers, and other reef residents thrive on this isolated reef.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef top at 8-12 meters where you encounter the distinctive undercut mushroom structure. Explore the ledge and overhanging sections at moderate depths (12-20 meters). The reef structure continues deeper to 25-30 meters for those interested in deeper exploration. The isolation and unique topography make this a special dive site. Plan for thorough exploration of the undercut features and surrounding reef, with a generous safety stop given the location's remoteness.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by liveaboard tender - Jesse Beazley Reef cannot be reached by shore diving due to its isolation 20 km from main Tubbataha. Entry is via giant stride or back roll as directed by your dive guide. Descend to the reef top using natural features or a descent line. Explore the distinctive undercut structure and surrounding reef. Ascend gradually to allow exploration of shallower reef sections. Complete your safety stop at 5 meters and signal the boat with an SMB for pickup - this is a remote location requiring reliable surface communication.

## Safety Considerations

The isolation requires reliable surface communication. Always maintain excellent SMB technique and surface signaling. Be aware that strong currents are possible - be prepared to move shallower or abort if conditions deteriorate. The undercut structure is safe for exploration but maintain visual contact with open water - do not attempt penetration beyond comfortable swim-throughs. Maintain excellent buoyancy control to protect both the reef ecosystem and avoid disorientation from the distinctive topography.

## Photography

The mushroom-shaped reef structure creates unique photographic opportunities. Position yourself below the overhang to capture the distinctive undercut silhouette. Wide-angle compositions of the reef shape work well from 15-20 meters. Macro opportunities exist on the colorful reef inhabitants. Pelagic encounters offer occasional opportunities for dramatic large-animal photography.
