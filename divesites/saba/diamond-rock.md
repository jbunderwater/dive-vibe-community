# Diamond Rock

An exposed volcanic pinnacle on Saba's northwest coast, swept by Atlantic currents and colonised by dense black coral, sea fans, and large tube sponges from base to crest.

## Overview

Diamond Rock is an isolated volcanic outcrop rising from the sea surface off Saba's northwestern shore. Underwater, the rock's steep flanks descend to 30 meters with near-vertical faces colonised by some of the densest soft coral and sponge communities in the Saba Marine Park. The park was established in 1987 and prohibits anchoring and all extractive activity, which is why its sites remain in exceptional condition. The northwest exposure places Diamond Rock in the path of Atlantic swells and tidal flows that funnel nutrient-rich water across the pinnacle year-round, driving the extraordinary invertebrate growth. Visibility averages 20–30 meters. Water temperature runs 26–28°C year-round, occasionally dropping to 24°C during winter upwellings.

## Marine Life

Diamond Rock's current-fed flanks support dense black coral trees at depth and large sea fans across the mid-pinnacle. Hawksbill turtles are year-round residents throughout the Saba Marine Park and frequently rest or feed on the shallow crest. Spotted eagle rays and Caribbean reef sharks patrol the deeper flanks. Caribbean spiny lobster and banded coral shrimp occupy crevices and overhangs throughout. Chromis clouds, creole wrasse, and schools of blue tang populate the upper structure in dense, photogenic numbers. During winter months (January–March), humpback whales pass through Saba's waters and are sometimes heard underwater.

## Dive Profile

Divers descend the mooring line to the pinnacle crest at around 8 meters, then work the flanks at chosen depth. The southern face drops most steeply and tends to carry the heaviest encrustation. A typical dive traces the northwest face, circles the base of the pinnacle at 25–30 meters, then ascends the eastern face for a safety stop on the mooring. NDL at 25 meters allows approximately 35–40 minutes before ascent.

## Entry and Exit

All dives are conducted from authorised dive boats operating out of Saba's Fort Bay harbour. No shore access exists. The boat ties to a permanent mooring buoy maintained by the Saba Conservation Foundation. Entry is typically a giant stride or backward roll.

## Tips and Recommendations

Current at Diamond Rock can be strong and is influenced by tidal cycles — operators brief divers on real-time conditions before descent. The site dives best when north-facing conditions are calm, typically May–October. Always carry a DSMB. The shallow pinnacle crest makes an excellent safety stop location with strong ambient light for photography.

## Safety Considerations

Strong current events occur without warning at this exposed site. Maintain awareness of drift direction throughout the dive and stay close to the pinnacle structure. Never swim away from the rock into open water during a surface swim. A DSMB is mandatory. Monitor depth passively — Saba's steep structures make it easy to drift deep.

## Photography

The west-facing flanks catch excellent morning light. Macro subjects are abundant — nudibranch species, flamingo tongue snails, and arrow crabs are consistently present. Wide-angle lenses suit the sea fan scenes at 15–25 meters. The pinnacle crest in morning light delivers strong silhouette opportunities of schooling fish against the sunlit surface.
