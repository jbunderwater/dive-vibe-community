# Diamond Rock

An exposed volcanic pinnacle near Saba's Torrens Point headland, subject to strong and fast-moving currents, with jacks, barracuda, angelfish, and dense sponge communities across its flanks.

## Overview

Diamond Rock is a pyramidal volcanic outcrop extending from the sea surface off Saba's northeastern shore, near Torrens Point headland. Underwater, the flanks descend to approximately 25 metres (80 feet) with near-vertical and steep faces featuring sponges, cup corals, and sea fans. The exposed position places Diamond Rock in the path of fast-moving currents that bring plankton and attract schooling fish. DAN Alert Diver describes Diamond Rock as an Advanced site and warns of strong currents. Multiple sources note the currents as a defining characteristic — fast-moving current events can occur without warning. Visibility averages 20–30 metres. Water temperature runs 24–28°C year-round.

## Marine Life

The strong currents at Diamond Rock attract schools of horse-eye jacks and great barracuda. DAN confirms queen and French angelfish as consistent species here. Cup corals and sponges are documented on the flanks. The fast-moving water brings in plankton that feeds the dense fish aggregations. *Previous claims about hawksbill turtles, spotted eagle rays, Caribbean reef sharks, chromis, and creole wrasse as confirmed Diamond Rock species have not been independently verified for this specific site by primary sources and have been removed. Species confirmed by DAN: jacks, giant barracuda, queen and French angelfish, sponges, cup corals.*

## Dive Profile

Divers descend the mooring to the pinnacle crest and work the flanks. Maximum depth is 25 metres (80 feet). Due to the strong currents, divers should stay close to the rock structure throughout the dive rather than swimming into open water. Current direction and intensity should be assessed before and during descent. NDL at 25 metres allows approximately 35–40 minutes before ascent.

## Entry and Exit

All dives are conducted from authorised dive boats operating out of Saba's Fort Bay harbour. No shore access exists. The boat ties to a permanent mooring buoy maintained by the Saba Conservation Foundation. Entry is typically a giant stride or backward roll.

## Tips and Recommendations

Strong current is a defining characteristic of Diamond Rock and is the primary reason it is rated Advanced. DAN describes fast-moving current events at this site. Operators brief divers on real-time conditions before descent. Always carry a DSMB. If current becomes too strong, abort the dive and ascend the mooring line — do not attempt to swim against open-water current.

## Safety Considerations

Strong current events occur without warning at this exposed site. DAN rates Diamond Rock as an Advanced site specifically because of these current conditions. Maintain awareness of drift direction throughout the dive and stay close to the pinnacle structure. Never swim away from the rock into open water. A DSMB is mandatory. Do not drift below 25 metres (80 feet).

## Photography

The strong currents bring large schooling fish that are best captured with wide-angle lenses — jacks and barracuda in motion with the pinnacle as backdrop. Cup coral and sponge detail shots work well at mid-depth with supplemental lighting. Schools of fish at the pinnacle crest deliver silhouette opportunities in strong ambient light.
