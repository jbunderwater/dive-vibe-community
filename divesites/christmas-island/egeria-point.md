## Egeria Point

Egeria Point is a wall and reef dive at Christmas Island's southwestern tip, consistently rated among the island's best shark dives.

## Overview

Located in the southwestern corner of Christmas Island adjacent to Pig Rock, Egeria Point combines fringing reef slopes with a pinnacle formation and open-ocean exposure that concentrates large pelagic life. Extra Divers describes it as one of the island's premier shark dive sites, with large whitetip reef sharks commonly resting on the bottom and schools of barracuda patrolling the water column. The SSI blog and ScubaDiver Life both document grey reef sharks, whitetip sharks, barracuda, and wahoo here. Visibility is typically excellent. The site's exposure to open-ocean conditions and the depth of the wall section — reaching around 22 metres — warrants an Advanced classification, with currents possible.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southwestern coast, Christmas Island, Oceania
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
