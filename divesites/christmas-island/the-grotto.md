## The Grotto

The Grotto is a natural limestone sea cave on Christmas Island's northeast coast featuring a pool of mixed fresh and salt water, accessed by a short walk from Golf Course Road.

## Overview

The Grotto is a small limestone cavern approximately 20 metres in length whose pool is fed by both an underground freshwater spring and tidal seawater from the ocean, connected to the sea by an underwater tunnel. The surge as swells push through the tunnel creates a distinctive sound and churn within the cave. At low tide the pool is calm enough for a relaxed swim; at high tide, conditions become significantly rougher.

The site is primarily a swimming and snorkelling destination. Some visitors swim through the tunnel to the open ocean with fins, and the shallow depth (around 5 metres in the pool) makes it accessible without scuba equipment. No established dive operator currently lists The Grotto as a guided scuba dive site. Divers interested in the cave systems of Christmas Island are directed to Thundercliff Cave and West White Beach Cave, which are the island's recognised dive cavern sites.
