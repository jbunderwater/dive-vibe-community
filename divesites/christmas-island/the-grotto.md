## The Grotto

The Grotto is a limestone sea cave on Christmas Island's northeast coast where a freshwater pool connects to the ocean through an underwater passage, creating surge and tidal effects that define the experience.

## Overview

The Grotto sits near the northeast tip of Christmas Island, accessible via a short walk from the road near Golf Course Road. It is a small cave — approximately 20 metres in length — with a pool fed by both freshwater springs and seawater. The pool connects to the open ocean through an underwater tunnel, and tidal surge creates a dramatic swirl effect inside the chamber. The site is used primarily for snorkelling and swimming; some visitors enter through the underwater passage to reach the open reef. Whether this site functions as a formal scuba dive site or primarily as a snorkel and swim-through is not definitively confirmed in available sources — local operators should be consulted for current access conditions. No site-specific marine life data has been found for The Grotto; the surrounding reef fauna typical of Christmas Island's northeast coast applies generally.
