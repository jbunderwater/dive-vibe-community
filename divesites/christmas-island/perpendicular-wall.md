## Perpendicular Wall

Perpendicular Wall is Christmas Island's signature wall dive, located at the northwest tip of the island, where the reef drops nearly vertically from the surface to beyond 36 metres.

## Overview

Located at the northwest tip of Christmas Island, Perpendicular Wall is considered the premier wall dive on the island. The cliff face runs almost exactly perpendicular to the ocean's surface, dropping from a shallow reef at around 5 metres to depths beyond 36 metres, with the wall dropping far beyond recreational limits. Corals grow out horizontally from the face to catch the passing current, and large gorgonian fans extend perpendicular to the wall. Fusiliers school vertically along the wall, while other reef fish — including blue-lined angelfish and feather stars — travel horizontally along it. Diveplanit and multiple operator sources note the presence of hydrocorals and abundant sea fans. Dolphins are frequently encountered before entry. Manta rays and whale sharks are occasionally seen along the wall during their November–April season. The exposed location means strong currents and open-ocean swell are common — this is an Advanced site. One scalloped hammerhead sighting is documented in a professional photographer's dive log (Nigel Marsh); schooling hammerheads are not confirmed for this site.
