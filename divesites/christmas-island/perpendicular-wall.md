## Perpendicular Wall

Considered Christmas Island's premier wall dive and one of Australia's most dramatic wall dives, Perpendicular Wall delivers spectacular underwater scenery reserved for advanced divers with strong current awareness.

## Overview

Perpendicular Wall is located at Christmas Island's north-western tip, earning its name from its nearly vertical drop-off descending just feet from the surface. The wall plunges to depths exceeding 36 meters with excellent visibility frequently reaching 40+ meters. Corals lean outward perpendicular to the wall to maximize sunlight capture, creating dramatic visual compositions. The site features abundant soft corals, gorgonians, and sponges creating a riot of color, with schools of fusiliers sweeping up and down the vertical wall. This is Australia's most dramatic wall dive and requires advanced certification and strong current management.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Christmas Island, Oceania (north-western tip)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 36 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 40+ meters (130+ feet)
- **Current**: Often strong, variable
- **Best Time**: September to February

## Marine Life

Perpendicular Wall hosts abundant reef fish including fusiliers that school up and down the vertical wall creating dynamic visual displays. Blue-lined angelfish, feather stars, and hydrocorals populate the wall. Large gorgonian fans extend perpendicular from the wall to capture passing currents. Soft corals, sponges, and whip corals create spectacular color. Masked moray eels shelter in crevices. Batfish, trevally, barracuda, and reef sharks patrol the wall. Dolphins frequently appear before dives begin. The wall's structure creates distinct vertical ecosystem zones with species changing at different depths.

## Dive Profile

Begin along the reef top at shallow depths before descending along the nearly vertical wall. The wall drops steeply from just a few meters depth. Most interesting features and marine life are found between 10-36 meters. The wall's visual interest and dynamic marine life encourage divers to explore thoroughly, but careful depth management is essential to avoid unintended deep descents. Monitor your depth gauge and bottom time regularly. Strong currents can develop at this site, requiring careful pre-dive current assessment and conservative dive planning. This is an advanced-only dive requiring strong buoyancy control and extensive wall diving experience.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Watch your depth carefully — wall dives can lead to unintentional deep descents
- Look in crevices and overhangs for hidden marine life
- Book with reputable local dive operators who know the site conditions
- Bring an underwater camera — this site offers excellent photography opportunities
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of strong currents, jellyfish (box jellyfish in summer), crocodiles (north) in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. This site is recommended for experienced divers only. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

Wall dives offer stunning wide-angle opportunities with dramatic depth perspectives. The interplay of light and shadow along the wall creates compelling compositions. Look for macro subjects in wall crevices and overhangs.
