## West White Beach

West White Beach Cave is a cavern dive on Christmas Island's north coast featuring two spacious entrances separated by a limestone column, with a vibrant reef wall just outside.

## Overview

The cave sits at around 12 metres depth and is accessed by boat. Two large entrances divided by a rock column lead into a generous chamber — the open design makes it non-claustrophobic and suitable for beginner divers. Inside, the walls and ceiling carry colourful encrustations: whip corals, sea fans, and electric clams. Lionfish rest under sheltered ledges within the cave. DivePlanIt's site report notes that the interior provides good backlit photographic opportunities, with the silhouette of divers framed against the brighter water at the cave mouth.

Outside the entrances, the reef extends from 12 to around 18 metres before dropping away into deeper water. The Nigel Marsh dive log documents anemonefish, Meyer's butterflyfish, and power-blue tangs on the reef immediately outside. Extra Divers lists the site as a beginner dive, consistent with its shallow depth and open-cavern design.

The beach itself, reached by a rope-assisted descent through dense rainforest, is on the north coast of Christmas Island despite the "west" in its name.

## Site Information

- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
