## Thundercliff Cave

Thundercliff Cave is a submerged limestone sea cave on Christmas Island's north coast, considered by many divers to be the island's signature dive when conditions are right.

## Overview

The entrance to Thundercliff Cave is fully submerged, requiring divers to descend to around six metres and swim into the cave along a rippled sandy floor. Inside, the cavern opens dramatically into a large air-filled chamber — large enough, according to one dive log, to fit three double-decker buses — where divers can surface and look up at stalactites dripping from the ceiling above the waterline. A second inner chamber, accessed through the first, contains more intricate geological formations including an unusually thin, near-cylindrical stalagmite column and a feature described as a hanging shroud. The cave exit is always visible as a faint blue glow, keeping orientation straightforward.

Outside the cave entrance, the reef flattens at around 12 metres before the terrain descends further. The outer reef hosts typical Christmas Island reef species including leatherjackets, ornate butterflyfish, velvet surgeons, goldspot seabream, and spotted hawkfish. Inside the cave, batfish and bullseye fish gather in the chamber. The site is accessed by boat and requires intermediate comfort with overhead environments; open-water divers should treat the swim-through as a cavern dive with a clear exit visible at all times.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North coast, Christmas Island, Oceania
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
