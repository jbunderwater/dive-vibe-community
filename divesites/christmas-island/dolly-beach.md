## Dolly Beach

Dolly Beach is a remote, lightly dived reef site on Christmas Island's southeastern coast, best accessed by boat, and notable for its turtle nesting activity and undisturbed coral.

## Overview

Dolly Beach is accessible by 4WD to a carpark and a boardwalk through dense rainforest, but diving here is typically arranged as a boat dive with Extra Divers rather than a shore entry — the rocky shoreline and potential strong currents make shore entry inadvisable in most conditions. The surrounding reef is shallow and relatively undisturbed given the site's inaccessibility.

The beach is documented as a nesting site for both hawksbill and green turtles, and turtle tracks and nests are visible on the sand. The offshore reef carries small hard corals, red algae, and rock pool communities extending into the shallows. Tiny moray eels have been observed hunting in ankle-deep water at the rock pool edges. Beyond the immediate shallows, the fringing reef descends to around 15 metres before the typical Christmas Island wall drop begins.

Because few dive logs specific to Dolly Beach have been published, site-specific species lists for the dive are limited. The island's overall marine biodiversity — 88 coral species and over 650 fish species — applies broadly to undived sites like this one, but specific fauna for Dolly Beach beyond turtles and moray eels in the shallows has not been confirmed in available sources.
