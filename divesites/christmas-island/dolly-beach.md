## Dolly Beach

Dolly Beach is a remote, rarely-dived reef on Christmas Island's southeastern coast, accessible only after a 30-minute drive and a 2 km walk through rainforest.

## Overview

Dolly Beach is on the southeastern coast of Christmas Island, about 30 minutes from Settlement by 4WD, followed by a roughly 2 km boardwalked jungle track and a descent via steep cliff stairs. The beach is a narrow crescent of sand fronted by a vibrant coral reef. The combination of difficult access and no regular commercial dive operations means the reef experiences minimal diving pressure and is reported to be in outstanding condition. Green turtle nesting occurs on the beach, and turtles are seen in the water. The site is suitable for snorkelling and shore diving in calm conditions. No site-specific marine species records have been confirmed by independent dive sources beyond turtles; the broader Christmas Island reef fauna applies generally. Conditions can change rapidly at this exposed location — swell and access to the beach vary seasonally.
