## Flying Fish Cove

Flying Fish Cove is Christmas Island's main settlement and is widely cited as one of the best shore dives in the world, offering easy jetty access to a richly populated reef slope.

## Overview

Entry is from the boat ramp on the western edge of the cove, where two chains laid along the sandy bottom serve as guides back to shore. The reef slopes gradually from the shallows, reaching around 25 metres at the main drop-off approximately 50 metres from entry. Two distinct areas offer different experiences: the Admin Wall is a sheer drop populated with trevally, fusiliers, and reef sharks, while the nearby Sandpatch — a sandy area ideal for new or night divers — hosts garden eels and a wide variety of macro life.

The cove is documented as home to roughly 90 percent of the species found around Christmas Island, including hybrid angelfish (crosses between species not found together elsewhere), the endemic Cocos Angelfish, longnose butterflyfish, raccoon butterflyfish, freckled hawkfish, bluelined surgeonfish, blackspotted pufferfish, ornate hawkfish, blue tangs, and multiple moray species. Hawksbill turtles are regularly encountered. Whale sharks visit Flying Fish Cove seasonally. Facilities at the boat ramp include toilets, showers, and nearby coffee vendors.
