# Tiger Rock

Bocas del Toro's premier advanced dive — an offshore pinnacle in the open Caribbean where currents deliver exceptional visibility and shark encounters in clear Atlantic water.

## Overview

Tiger Rock is the outstanding advanced dive site of Bocas del Toro and one of the best pinnacle dives in Panama. The site consists of a group of seamounts rising from the open Caribbean, approximately 1 hour 15 minutes to 1 hour 30 minutes by boat from Bocas Town. The pinnacles descend to over 40 meters, with the richest diving on the flanks at 15–35 meters. The isolated offshore position in moving Atlantic water brings the conditions that define great pinnacle diving: strong current, excellent visibility (25+ meters), and concentrated pelagic marine life using the rock as a gathering point.

Tiger Rock is only accessible in calm sea conditions, with the best access typically from August through November when the water stabilises. The site is also described as accessible during the dry season (February–March) for maximum visibility. Strong currents are the rule — drift diving experience is an advantage.

## Marine Life

Black-tip reef sharks are the confirmed signature sighting at Tiger Rock — schools circling the pinnacle. Nurse sharks have also been recorded at the site. Manta rays have been reported. Large moray eels and groupers are present on the deeper flanks. The clear water and strong current bring schools of jacks and other pelagics to the pinnacle. Note: specific species occurrence varies seasonally and conditions — the sharks and pelagics are the draw but encounters are never guaranteed.

## Dive Profile

Boat drop at the pinnacle, descent to the crest at approximately 16 meters, then a controlled descent along the flanks as directed by the guide. The deepest sections at 30–42 meters have short no-decompression limits on air — plan carefully and begin ascent before limits are reached. The most productive shark and ray encounters typically occur in the 16–30 meter zone. Open-water safety stop with DSMB.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Bocas Town advanced operators. Tiger Rock requires a long offshore run (1 hour 15 min–1 hour 30 min) and is only conducted in suitable sea state and current conditions. This site is weather and season dependent — conditions must be assessed by the operator before committing. DSMB mandatory. The exposed pinnacle position means self-rescue skills are important.

## Tips and Recommendations

Tiger Rock is strictly a site for Advanced Open Water certified divers with genuine offshore and drift experience. The combination of depth, current, and open-ocean environment is not appropriate as a first advanced dive in Bocas del Toro. Remain calm and neutral when sharks are present — active movement typically causes them to retreat. The guide's judgment on current and conditions is paramount at this site.

## Safety Considerations

Multiple hazards exist at Tiger Rock: strong current, significant depth, open-ocean environment. Dive within your training and certification limits — the deeper pinnacle sections exceed safe recreational no-decompression limits for most divers on air. Never swim far from the pinnacle structure in current. DSMB deployment must be planned before the safety stop.

## Photography

Tiger Rock offers wide-angle shark photography in clear water — the visibility and multiple sharks make it Bocas del Toro's best site for this. The pinnacle flanks with schools of jacks and pelagic action above provide strong wide-angle compositions.
