# Tiger Rock

Bocas del Toro's premier advanced dive — a remote offshore seamount in the open Caribbean where strong current delivers excellent visibility and pelagic shark encounters.

## Overview

Tiger Rock is the standout advanced dive site of the Bocas del Toro region. The site is an isolated submerged seamount with its tip rising less than two meters above the ocean surface. Three interconnected pinnacles form the site, descending to over 42 meters. The isolated position in open Atlantic water creates conditions that characterise great pinnacle diving: strong current, concentrated marine life, and the exposure of the open ocean. The richest diving occurs in the 15–30 meter zone on the pinnacle flanks. Visibility averages 20 meters or greater. The site is accessible only when sea conditions are calm — sources indicate September–October as the most reliable window, with February–March as a secondary season.

## Marine Life

Tiger Rock is known for pelagic shark encounters. Multiple sources confirm blacktip reef sharks and nurse sharks are the most commonly seen shark species at this site. Schools of blacktip reef sharks have been reported circling the pinnacle. Hammerhead and bull sharks are mentioned by multiple sources as occasional visitors, though not guaranteed sightings. Groupers are also documented at the site. Manta rays have been reported; the overall Bocas del Toro region sees manta rays primarily January through May according to the PADI diving blog. Schools of jacks and barracuda are typical of this type of offshore pinnacle environment.

No site-specific sources confirmed whale sharks as a regular Tiger Rock species. Whale shark sightings in the wider Bocas del Toro region are reported to peak December–March.

## Dive Profile

Descent to the pinnacle crest, then a controlled spiral descent down the flanks following the guide. The primary shark and jack activity is in the 15–25 meter zone. The deepest sections of the pinnacle at 30–40+ meters are for divers with appropriate training — NDL limits at these depths are short. Ascent along the pinnacle structure before an open-water safety stop with DSMB.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Bocas Town advanced operators. Tiger Rock requires approximately 1–1.5 hours of offshore travel and is only attempted in calm sea conditions. Entry by backward roll or giant stride. DSMB mandatory. The exposed location means the ability to deploy your own DSMB is required.

## Tips and Recommendations

Tiger Rock is strictly a site for Advanced Open Water certified divers with genuine offshore experience. The best access window is September–October when seas are typically calmest; February–March is also documented as feasible. Current direction and strength change unpredictably — guide leadership is critical.

## Safety Considerations

Multiple hazards exist at Tiger Rock: strong current, significant depth (beyond recreational limits on the deeper sections), open-ocean environment, and shark encounters. Dive within recreational limits. Never swim far from the pinnacle structure in current. DSMB deployment must be prepared before the safety stop.
