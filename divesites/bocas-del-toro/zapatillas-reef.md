# Zapatillas Reef

A reef surrounding the Isla Zapatillas — two small protected cays in the Bastimentos National Marine Park at the southeastern edge of Bocas del Toro, with good fish diversity and important Hawksbill turtle nesting beaches.

## Overview

The Zapatillas are two small uninhabited cays at the southern end of the Bocas del Toro archipelago, within Isla Bastimentos National Marine Park. They are reached by approximately 30–45 minutes by boat from Bocas Town and require a national park entrance fee. Therealdealtours.com records the dive as averaging 40 feet (12 m) with a protected marine park setting that includes mangroves and cavern features. Go.bocasdeltoro.travel describes the reef as part of a nationally protected area with diverse reef fish.

Snorkeling-report.com, which covers Cayo Zapatilla South specifically, notes the coral is "very much damaged, as this area is exposed to the waves and currents." Conditions include strong current and the site is described as intermediate for snorkellers. The damage to the southern exposure coral is a factual correction to any "pristine" or "excellent condition" descriptions; the northern side of the main Zapatilla cay may have better-sheltered reef sections according to the wider source set.

## Marine Life

Snorkeling-report.com confirms the following species at Cayo Zapatilla South: bluehead wrasse, gray angelfish, French angelfish, butterflyfish, sergeant majors, damselfish, Spanish hogfish, grunts, Atlantic peacock flounder, and surgeonfish in the seagrass areas. Therealdealtours.com notes over 200 tropical fish species for the broader Zapatillas area, with the protected marine park setting supporting a full Caribbean reef community. The go.bocasdeltoro.travel site confirms Hawksbill sea turtles as critically endangered nesters on the Zapatilla beaches; both green and hawksbill turtles feed on the reef.

"Large parrotfish less skittish than at fished sites" was a claim in the previous description that is not confirmed by any source consulted and has been removed. The claim about Bastimentos National Marine Park limiting fishing is accurate — the park does restrict fishing and anchoring.

## Dive Profile

Boat drop at the Zapatilla cays. Snorkeling-report.com notes the southern exposure has strong current and recommends drift snorkelling with the current rather than fighting it. The northern or inner side of the main cay will be more sheltered. Depths are shallow across the reef, with the deeper sections reaching 12–14 meters at the outer sandy base.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive. Approximately 30–45 minutes from Bocas Town. National park fee required at the entry point. Entry by giant stride or from the beach on the sheltered side. Beach surface intervals are available on the cay's white sand beach.

## Tips and Recommendations

Choose the sheltered (northern or inner) side of the main cay for calmer diving conditions; the southern exposed side has stronger current and more coral damage. Arrive early before day-tour boats concentrate. The beach and above-water environment make this a worthwhile full-day trip regardless of dive conditions.

## Safety Considerations

Current on the southern exposed side can be strong — assess before entering. Bastimentos National Marine Park rules prohibit fishing, collecting, and anchoring. Rangers patrol the area. Turtle nesting season (April–October) means certain beach sections may be restricted.
