# Cayo Coral

A shallow, bikini-accessible snorkel and dive reef directly surrounding one of Bocas del Toro's most scenic coral cays — a white sand island ringed by abundant brain corals, sea fans, and starfish.

## Overview

Cayo Coral (Coral Cay) is a small, uninhabited tropical cay in the southern Bocas del Toro archipelago, surrounded by a shallow reef system accessible directly from the cay's beach. The cay is reachable by water taxi from Bocas Town and is a popular day-trip destination for snorkellers and beginner divers. The surrounding reef extends from the beach at 1 meter to approximately 8 meters at the outer edge, featuring well-developed hard coral formations including large brain corals, star corals, and scattered sea fans. The water is warm (28–30°C) and clarity is good in calm conditions, typically 15–25 meters. This is classic shallow Caribbean reef diving at its most accessible.

## Marine Life

Green sea turtles are a near-certain sighting at Cayo Coral — they are present throughout the day, grazing on the seagrass beds between coral formations. Hawksbill turtles also feed on reef sponges. Schools of sergeant majors, blue tang, and parrotfish occupy the reef crest. Spotted moray eels can be found in crevices throughout the reef. Large cushion sea stars (often called "starfish") are very common on the sandy bottom between reef formations and are an iconic image of Bocas del Toro snorkelling. Southern stingrays rest in sandy clearings.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from the cay beach. Divers wade in and descend at the reef edge in 3–4 meters, then navigate the reef perimeter in 5–8 meters. The site can be explored in multiple circuits around the cay — a full circumnavigation at swim pace takes approximately 30–40 minutes of snorkelling, or a longer dive staying within the 8-meter ceiling.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from Cayo Coral beach. Water shoes recommended for the beach entry. Entry is calm in typical conditions. The reef is accessible directly from the cay — no boat required once at the island.

## Tips and Recommendations

Morning is the best time to visit Cayo Coral before day-trip boats arrive. Water taxis depart Bocas Town early morning for the fastest access. Cushion sea stars found on the sandy bottom are a popular photo subject but should not be removed from the water — photographing them in their natural position is the appropriate approach. The cay itself has shade from palm trees and is suitable for a full day combining snorkelling, swimming, and relaxing.

## Safety Considerations

The site is extremely shallow — free diving and snorkelling carry no decompression risks, and scuba at 8 meters maximum is essentially risk-free for NDL. Fire coral is present on some reef sections — avoid contact. The cay's exposed position means some surge in trade wind conditions. DSMB not required at this shallow site but a surface flag is advisable if snorkelling in boat-traffic areas.

## Photography

Cayo Coral's shallow depth delivers outstanding ambient natural light. The cushion sea stars on white sand are an iconic Bocas del Toro image — photograph them in situ without lifting from the water. Wide-angle reef shots in 4–6 meters with clear blue water and abundant fish are effortless here. Turtle encounters in the shallow water are excellent for ambient-light wide-angle photography.

## Additional Resources

- Bocas water taxis: regular service from Bocas Town to Cayo Coral throughout the day
- Local dive operators: offer snorkel and beginner dive trips to Cayo Coral
