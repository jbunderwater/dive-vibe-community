# Cayo Coral

A shallow, shore-accessible reef surrounding a small uninhabited cay in the southern Bocas del Toro archipelago — a popular snorkel and beginner dive stop with colourful sponges and good fish diversity.

## Overview

Cayo Coral is a small cay in the southern Bocas del Toro archipelago, reachable by boat from Bocas Town (approximately 20 minutes). The surrounding reef is a constant 3–4 meters depth over most of the area, making it one of the more uniformly shallow sites in the region. Snorkeling-report.com documents the site extensively and describes small coral heads covered in turquoise, golden, and carmine sponges forming a colourful underwater landscape, with sea fans beyond the central reef area. The site uses mooring buoys to protect it from anchor damage and is a frequent stop on day-tour itineraries.

## Marine Life

Snorkeling-report.com confirms the following species at Cayo Coral: sergeant major, wrasse (including bluehead wrasse), grunt schools, stoplight parrotfish, foureye butterflyfish, graysby, gray angelfish, French angelfish, queen angelfish, schoolmaster snapper, barred hamlet, arrow crabs, brittle stars (described as "very common throughout the area"), and colourful sponge communities. The rare yellowhead jawfish is noted as an inhabitant of sandy zones, though "shy and difficult to spot." Sea fans are present beyond the central reef. Therealdealtours.com adds reef fish, giant jellyfish, and barracuda.

Cushion sea stars, which are commonly associated with other Bocas del Toro sites like Hospital Point, were not specifically confirmed at Cayo Coral by snorkeling-report.com (the dedicated source for this site). Sea turtles and southern stingrays occur throughout the wider archipelago but were not specifically documented at this site by the sources consulted.

## Dive Profile

Shore or boat entry. The reef sits at a nearly constant 3–4 meters across most of its area, with some deeper sections to 8 meters at the outer edge. A full circuit of the cay at snorkel pace takes approximately 30–40 minutes. Scuba divers will have limited working depth but generous bottom time.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the cay beach or boat entry from a tour vessel. The site is accessed by day trip from Bocas Town. Water shoes are recommended for the beach entry over the shallow reef edge. Mooring buoys are in place to protect the reef from anchoring.

## Tips and Recommendations

Morning visits, before peak day-tour traffic, offer calmer conditions and less disturbance. The colourful sponge-coated coral heads photograph well in the clear shallow water with natural light. The brittle stars and arrow crabs in the sandy areas between coral heads reward slow, patient observation.

## Safety Considerations

The site is extremely shallow — no decompression risk for scuba at 8 meters maximum. Jellyfish are reported at this site; avoid contact. Boat traffic from day tours is the main surface hazard — use a surface marker if in open water. Fire coral may be present.
