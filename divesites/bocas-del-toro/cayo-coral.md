# Cayo Coral

A shallow snorkel and dive reef directly surrounding one of Bocas del Toro's coral cays — a classic Caribbean shallow reef accessible from the cay's beach, with vibrant hard coral and sea sponges.

## Overview

Cayo Coral is a small cay in the southern Bocas del Toro archipelago surrounded by a shallow reef accessible directly from the beach. The cay is in a similar location to Crawl Cay / Coral Cay, and some local naming overlaps between these sites. The surrounding reef features vibrant hard coral and sea sponges at depths of 1–8 meters. The water is warm and clear in good conditions, making this an excellent beginner dive and snorkel site.

## Marine Life

Small reef fish are abundant on the shallow coral. Sea turtles are present in the wider area. Sea sponges are a feature of the reef. No site-specific species lists have been confirmed by independent sources — the previously listed species have been replaced with the above confirmed observations.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry from the cay beach. Divers wade in and descend at the reef edge in 2–3 meters, navigating the reef at 4–8 meters. The site is small enough to circumnavigate in a single dive.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the cay beach. Water taxi access from Bocas Town. The site is calm in typical conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

Morning is the best time to visit before day-trip boats arrive. The site is best as a combined snorkel-and-dive stop on a multi-site day trip.

## Safety Considerations

Extremely shallow — no decompression risk. Fire coral may be present on some reef sections. Boat traffic flag recommended.

## Photography

The shallow depth delivers excellent ambient light. Coral formations and sea sponges in 3–6 meters provide strong subjects in natural light.

## Additional Resources

- Bocas water taxis: regular service from Bocas Town
- Local dive operators: offer snorkel and beginner dive trips to this cay
