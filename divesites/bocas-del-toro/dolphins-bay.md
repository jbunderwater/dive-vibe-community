# Dolphins Bay

A sheltered mangrove-fringed bay about 15–20 minutes south of Bocas Town, also known as Laguna Bocatorito — known for its resident bottlenose dolphin population and as a standard stop on day-tour itineraries.

## Overview

Dolphins Bay, known locally as Laguna Bocatorito, is a broad bay approximately 12 km south of Bocas Town, approximately 15–20 minutes by boat. The bay is enclosed by mangroves on three sides with palm-lined shores, forming a sheltered dolphin nursery habitat. Bocastours.com and therealdealtours.com describe the bay as home to a resident bottlenose dolphin population that uses the mangrove-sheltered waters as a nursery due to the abundant shrimp and fish food supply and protection from open-sea predators and swells. The dive site itself features a fringing reef in the bay in 3–10 meters of water.

The bay is primarily visited as a dolphin-watching and snorkelling stop on the standard Bocas del Toro day tour. Scuba diving is sometimes offered here but the primary attraction is the dolphin encounter at or near the surface.

## Marine Life

Bottlenose dolphins are the documented resident species in this bay, confirmed by multiple sources including bocastours.com and therealdealtours.com. Stingrays are also noted in the bay. Tour descriptions mention snorkelling at "two incredible coral reefs" near the bay. The surrounding mangroves support abundant shrimp and crustacean populations.

No site-specific sources confirmed nurse sharks, green sea turtles, cushion sea stars, or southern stingrays as specific species at the reef dive site within Dolphins Bay. These are common throughout the wider Bocas del Toro region but no dedicated dive source described them here specifically.

## Dive Profile

Boat arrives at the bay entrance. Dolphin encounters typically happen near the surface in the bay itself. Snorkelling and diving at the adjacent reef sections at 3–10 meters. Dolphin encounters during scuba dives are possible when animals approach divers from curiosity, but are more commonly experienced at the surface.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Bocas Town. Approximately 15–20 minutes travel time. Entry by giant stride or swimming from the boat. The calm, sheltered bay conditions make entry and exit straightforward.

## Tips and Recommendations

Dolphin encounters are most reliable in the morning before heavy boat traffic. Remain still and passive in the water if dolphins approach — movement causes them to depart. The dolphin encounter is not guaranteed. The bay is typically part of a multi-stop day tour that also includes Zapatilla Cay and other sites.

## Safety Considerations

Do not chase, pursue, or corner the dolphins. Boat traffic in the bay can be busy during peak tour hours — be visible on the surface. Standard beginner dive precautions apply.
