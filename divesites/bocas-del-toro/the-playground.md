# The Playground

A shallow inner-archipelago dive site in Bocas del Toro known as an accessible entry point for beginner and student divers, with coral reef and sand at moderate depths.

## Overview

The Playground is one of the more accessible dive sites in the Bocas del Toro inner archipelago, situated less than 5 minutes from the main Bocas Town dock. The site is listed by multiple local operators as a beginner and student dive location. Therealdealtours.com notes its excellent photography conditions and describes it as reaching an average of 22–45 feet (7–14 m) with a maximum of 72 feet (22 m). The tourismpanama.com regional overview lists The Playground as a shallow reef site at approximately 50 feet (15 m). Sea stars are specifically cited as common at this site. The site is used extensively for discover scuba diving experiences and open water certification courses.

## Marine Life

Sources confirm sea stars (starfish) as common at The Playground. Therealdealtours.com also notes Hawksbill turtles visit this site (April–June), along with colorful fish, mollusks, and nudibranchs. The tourismpanama.com overview lists starfish as the highlighted marine life at this site.

No site-specific sources confirmed nurse sharks, green sea turtles, or southern stingrays as regular species at The Playground specifically (these are widespread throughout the Bocas del Toro area but were not cited for this site in particular by the sources consulted).

## Dive Profile

Boat drop close to the site. Descent to 5–7 meters on the reef, with navigation to the deeper sections at 14–18 meters. The site's proximity to town means it is often the first or last dive of the day and is frequently used for check dives. Total dive: 40–55 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Bocas Town operators. Entry by giant stride into calm water. The site is suitable for open water certification dives given the forgiving conditions and proximity to town. Exit by standard boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

The Playground is well-suited as a first dive for new visitors to Bocas del Toro or as a check dive after a travel layoff. The calm conditions, warm water, and shallow maximum depth allow focus on skill development. It pairs well with Hospital Point for a half-day of inner-archipelago diving.

## Safety Considerations

Light current and shallow depth make this a low-risk site. Fire coral may be present on reef sections. Standard beginner precautions apply. DSMB recommended for boat pickup.
