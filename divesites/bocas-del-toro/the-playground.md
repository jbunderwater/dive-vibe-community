# The Playground

A wide, easily navigated reef in the sheltered inner archipelago of Bocas del Toro — named for its accessible, exploratory character and featuring swim-throughs, small caverns, and excellent turtle encounters.

## Overview

The Playground is one of Bocas del Toro's most popular beginner and intermediate dive sites, located approximately 5 minutes by boat from the dive centers on Isla Colón. The site's name reflects its accessible, freely navigable character. Bocas Dive Center describes the site as featuring "swim-throughs, small caverns, and coral formations" alongside the standard reef structure — making it more interesting topographically than a plain sandy reef.

The site descends from 7 meters to a maximum of 22 meters, with the reef crest, swim-throughs, and cavern sections in the upper 15 meters and a sandy base below. The warm, calm conditions typical of Bocas del Toro's sheltered inner archipelago make this an ideal first or second dive. Visibility is 15–20 meters.

## Marine Life

The site supports a typical Caribbean inner-reef community. Hawksbill turtles have been confirmed at the site (notably April–June per therealdealtours.com). Sea stars, nudibranchs, and colourful molluscs are noted in descriptions. The swim-through and cavern sections attract fish that shelter in reduced-light environments.

## Dive Profile

Descent to the reef crest, exploration of the swim-throughs and small caverns in the 7–15 meter range, then navigation to the sandy base at 22 meters if desired. The site's open structure allows divers to cover a large area without complex routing. Total dive: 50–65 minutes at an easy pace.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Bocas Town operators. Entry by giant stride into calm water. Suitable for open water certification dives and refresher dives.

## Tips and Recommendations

The swim-throughs and small caverns are what distinguish The Playground from other inner reef sites — a torch enhances the experience in the shaded sections. The site dives well as a morning dive paired with Hospital Point.

## Safety Considerations

The swim-throughs require good buoyancy to avoid silt disturbance. Fire coral is present on some reef sections. DSMB recommended.

## Photography

The swim-throughs and cavern openings make stronger compositional subjects than plain reef photography. Hawksbill turtles in the coral garden photograph well in ambient light. Wide-angle reef scenes with the swim-through entrances as focal points are achievable.
