# Veronica L Wreck

A Trinidad cargo vessel now resting upright at approximately 15 metres on Boss Reef — it sank accidentally in St. George's harbour in 1993 and was relocated here before cruise-ship terminal construction, where it has become one of Grenada's most popular beginner wreck dives. Sources give her length as 120–130 feet (approximately 40 metres); ECO Dive and x-ray mag say 40m, DreamWrecks says 130 feet.

## Overview

The Veronica L was a cargo vessel that operated between Trinidad and Grenada. In 1993 she began taking on water while at anchor in St. George's harbour and sank. She was subsequently refloated, loaded onto a barge, and sunk on the outer edge of Boss Reef near Grand Anse, where she settled upright on her keel at approximately 15 metres (50 feet). The vessel's open hold, bow anchor windlass, and freight crane arm extending sideways are distinctive features. Orange cup coral and encrusting sponges in yellow, orange, pink, and purple have colonised the hull surfaces. The shallow depth means excellent natural light throughout the dive. Visibility averages 20–25 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C year-round. **Vessel length conflict**: ECO Dive and x-ray magazine describe the vessel as 40 metres (approximately 120–131 feet); DreamWrecks says 130 feet. Both measurements are broadly consistent. Build year of 1963 is stated in some sources but not independently confirmed.

## Marine Life

The Veronica L's open hold and encrusted superstructure host clouds of small fish and shy squirrelfish. Creole wrasse, frogfish, seahorses, French angelfish, and damselfish are reported at this site. Schools of horse-eye jacks pass above the wreck. The sandy bottom adjacent to the hull attracts eels and bottom-dwelling species. The bright orange cup coral and multicoloured encrusting sponges on the hull surfaces are striking in good visibility.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the main deck at 10–12 metres and explore bow to stern along the deck before descending to the sandy bottom at 15 metres to view the hull exterior. The entire wreck can be circumnavigated in a single dive with time to investigate the interior sections accessible from the deck openings. Safety stop in the water column at 5 metres. Total dive duration 40–55 minutes within comfortable limits.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from True Blue Bay or Grand Anse operators — the wreck is a short run from the main dive centre areas. Entry by giant stride or backward roll to the mooring line. DSMB recommended for the safety stop ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

The Veronica L is an excellent first wreck dive for Open Water divers — the depth is manageable, the orientation is intuitive (upright, clear bow to stern), and the light is good throughout. Carry a small torch to illuminate the interior deck spaces, even though ambient light reaches the deck level. The wreck is well-suited as a second dive on a morning two-tank trip after a deeper site.

## Safety Considerations

The shallow depth and calm conditions make this a low-risk site, but standard wreck diving precautions apply. Do not penetrate into enclosed compartments without proper wreck diving training — even at 15 metres, silt disturbance can quickly reduce visibility to zero inside enclosed spaces. Fire coral is present on some hull surfaces. Deploy DSMB before surfacing.

## Photography

The Veronica L's excellent natural light at 15 metres makes this a strong photography site without strobes in calm conditions. Wide-angle shots of the full hull structure in clear water, moray portraits in hull crevices, and school-of-fish compositions around the superstructure are the standout subjects. The encrusted deck with coral and fish creates a classic shallow Caribbean wreck image in ambient light.
