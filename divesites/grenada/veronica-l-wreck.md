# Veronica L Wreck

A 130-foot Trinidad cargo vessel now resting upright at 15 metres on Boss Reef — it sank accidentally in St. George's harbour in 1993 while at anchor, was refloated and relocated to its current site before cruise-ship terminal construction, and has become one of Grenada's most popular beginner wreck dives.

## Overview

The Veronica L was a Trinidad-registered 130-foot cargo vessel that operated in the Caribbean. In 1993 she began taking on water while at anchor in St. George's harbour, at the site of the planned cruise-ship terminal. The wreck was refloated, loaded onto a barge, and sunk on the outer edge of Boss Reef near Grand Anse. She settled upright on her keel at less than 50 feet (approximately 15 metres), making this one of Grenada's most accessible wreck dives. Build date and earlier vessel name are not confirmed by primary dive sources. The vessel's open hold, bow anchor windlass, and freight crane arm are distinctive features; DreamWrecks notes the propeller is missing. Orange cup coral and encrusting sponges have colonised the hull surfaces. The shallow depth means excellent natural light throughout the dive. Visibility averages 20–25 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C year-round.

## Marine Life

Confirmed marine life from DreamWrecks and freedomtourtravel.com: seahorses, frogfish, eels, cleaner shrimp, arrow crabs, barracudas, French angelfish, and trumpetfish. The ECO Dive site listing confirms squirrelfish are abundant. The entire wreck is covered with orange cup coral and encrusting sponges. The hull keel and rudder carry notable coral growth per DreamWrecks. Clouds of small fish shelter in the open hold. Schools of fish circulate around the superstructure.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the main deck at 10–12 metres and explore bow to stern along the deck before descending to the sandy bottom at 15 metres to view the hull exterior. The entire wreck can be circumnavigated in a single dive with time to investigate the interior sections accessible from the deck openings. Safety stop in the water column at 5 metres. Total dive duration 40–55 minutes within comfortable limits.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from True Blue Bay or Grand Anse operators — the wreck is a short run from the main dive centre areas. Entry by giant stride or backward roll to the mooring line. DSMB recommended for the safety stop ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

The Veronica L is an excellent first wreck dive for Open Water divers — the depth is manageable, the orientation is intuitive (upright, clear bow to stern), and the light is good throughout. Carry a small torch to illuminate the interior deck spaces, even though ambient light reaches the deck level. The wreck is well-suited as a second dive on a morning two-tank trip after a deeper site.

## Safety Considerations

The shallow depth and calm conditions make this a low-risk site, but standard wreck diving precautions apply. Do not penetrate into enclosed compartments without proper wreck diving training — even at 15 metres, silt disturbance can quickly reduce visibility to zero inside enclosed spaces. Fire coral is present on some hull surfaces. Deploy DSMB before surfacing.

## Photography

The Veronica L's excellent natural light at 15 metres makes this a strong photography site without strobes in calm conditions. Wide-angle shots of the full hull structure in clear water, moray portraits in hull crevices, and school-of-fish compositions around the superstructure are the standout subjects. The encrusted deck with coral and fish creates a classic shallow Caribbean wreck image in ambient light.
