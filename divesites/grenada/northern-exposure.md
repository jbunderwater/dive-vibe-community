# Northern Exposure

Part of the extensive Boss Reef system just outside St. George's harbour — an undulating reef carpeted in finger and pillar corals, sea rods, and soft brown coral trees, with an eel garden in the shallows and a small surprise shipwreck on the eastern sand patch.

## Overview

Northern Exposure lies within the Grenada Marine Protected Area, part of the same Boss Reef complex that extends up to six miles from St. George's harbour toward Point Salines. Like other Boss Reef sections, it is accessible from the main southwest coast dive bases, making it a practical site for divers of all levels including day-visit cruise passengers. The reef begins in shallow sand patches, where crustaceans and conch are abundant and an eel garden forms at around 6 metres. As the reef slopes gently to a maximum of around 23 metres, coral density increases — flower, brain, sheet, and star corals create good structural variety, with azure sponges and sea fans on the lower reef face. ECO Dive Grenada describes a small shipwreck that "appears out of nowhere" on the eastern sand patch edge, adding an unexpected wreck element to what is primarily a reef dive. Visibility averages 20–30 metres. Water temperature is 26–28°C year-round.

## Marine Life

The reef supports a full cross-section of southwest Grenada's reef community. Schools of Creole wrasse, grey and blue chromis, French grunts, blue tangs, and damselfish are confirmed throughout. Caribbean lobsters shelter in crevices; stingrays, conch, and octopus occupy the sand patches. Occasional nurse sharks, hawksbill turtles, mackerel, and barracuda pass through. Vase sponges and barrel sponges increase in size on the deeper reef sections at 15–23 metres.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the shallow sand flats and eel garden at 5–6 metres before following the slope toward the deeper reef. The site works well in either direction — the coral-carpeted slope to 23 metres and then returning via the shallows for the safety stop. The small shipwreck on the eastern sand patch is worth locating with a local guide. Total dive duration 45–55 minutes at recreational limits.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from True Blue Bay or Grand Anse area operators — Northern Exposure is part of the Boss Reef system and a short run from the main dive centres, not a remote north coast site. Giant stride entry; DSMB on ascent. Often paired with another Boss Reef section or a nearby reef for a two-tank morning.

## Tips and Recommendations

Ask the dive guide to point out the small shipwreck on the eastern sand patch — it does not appear on most site maps and is a pleasant surprise on what divers might otherwise assume is a straightforward reef dive. The eel garden in the shallows rewards a few minutes at the start or end of the dive. The site is accessible to beginners and cruise passengers, so group sizes can vary; early morning dives are quieter.

## Safety Considerations

Straightforward site. Deploy DSMB before ascending. The larger Boss Reef complex can disorient divers — stay with the guide on the first visit. Fire coral present on some coral heads.

## Photography

The undulating reef topography and mix of hard corals with sea fans creates good wide-angle compositions. Creole wrasse schools in mid-water, lobster portraits in crevices, and the surprise shipwreck make distinctive subjects. Natural light at 5–15 metres is excellent in Grenada's clear southwest waters.
