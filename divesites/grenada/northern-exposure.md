## Northern Exposure

Northern Exposure is a reef dive on Grenada's less-dived north coast, offering divers willing to make the longer boat journey from St. George's an opportunity to explore undisturbed reef in a setting far from the tourist-heavy southwest.

## Overview

Located off Grenada's northern coastline, Northern Exposure features a broad reef that descends to around 23 meters. The north coast sees significantly fewer dive boats than the southwest, and the reef reflects this lower human traffic pressure — coral coverage is dense and marine life is notably less conditioned to human presence. The reef features good hard coral diversity including brain, star, and pillar corals, with substantial barrel sponge growth at depth. Visibility can be exceptional in good conditions, reaching 30 meters or more. Water temperature is 26–28°C year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North coast, Grenada
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Relatively undisturbed fringing reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner (but requires a longer boat ride)
- **Maximum Depth**: 23 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate; north coast exposure can increase this
- **Best Time**: January to May

## Marine Life

The lower dive pressure on the north coast means marine life behaves less predictably around divers — which is actually a positive, as encounters feel more naturalistic. Sea turtles are less habituated to boats and divers, making interactions feel special. Large parrotfish, groupers, and Caribbean reef sharks are more common here than at the south coast sites. Eagle rays and hammerheads are occasional visitors to the deeper reef sections.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the reef crest and follow the slope to 23 meters, then ascend along the reef face. The less-explored terrain rewards divers who look carefully — the north coast reef has corners and structures that aren't well-documented and may yield unexpected finds. Allow adequate air for the full safety stop and the boat return.

## Entry and Exit

Longer boat ride (30–60 minutes) from south coast operators. Giant stride entry; SMB essential on ascent given the more remote location. Typically offered as a half-day excursion combining two dives on the north coast. Check conditions carefully with the operator.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The journey is worth it — this site offers a noticeably different character to the more visited south coast
- Less frequent dive traffic means marine life behaviours are more natural and encounters more spontaneous
- Bring lunch and plan for a full half-day north coast excursion
- The remoteness means self-sufficiency in navigation and safety equipment is important

## Safety Considerations

The north coast is more exposed to Atlantic swell than the south — conditions can change rapidly. Only attempt the journey with an operator who knows the north coast and monitors weather carefully. The longer distance from shore means any equipment problem is more serious than at the nearshore sites.

## Photography

The undisturbed quality of Northern Exposure's reef makes it ideal for wildlife photography — animals behave naturally and don't shy away. The higher probability of large species encounters (sharks, eagle rays) rewards wide-angle setups.
