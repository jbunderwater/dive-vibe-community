# Northern Exposure

A section of the Boss Reef system on Grenada's southwest coast — a gently undulating reef with finger and pillar corals, an eel garden in the shallows, and the schooling Creole wrasse activity that characterises the best of Grenada's protected reef dives.

## Overview

Northern Exposure is part of the extensive Boss Reef system that extends approximately five miles from St. George's harbour southwest to Point Salines. It is located in Grenada's Grand Anse Marine Protected Area and is suitable for all levels. The reef begins along a shallow sandy patch with eels and crustaceans at around 6 metres, then slopes gently to the sandy seabed at 23 metres. ECO Dive describes it as "undulating reef carpeted in patches of finger and pillar corals, branching sea rods, sea whips, and forests of soft brown coral trees." Sportif Dive notes the presence of Azure Vase sponges. Visibility can reach 30 metres in good conditions. Water temperature is 26–28°C year-round.

## Marine Life

ECO Dive and Sportif Dive confirm the following species at Northern Exposure: porcupine fish, Spanish hogfish, yellowtail snapper, schoolmasters, Creole wrasse, and parrotfish are the characteristic fish community. Large conch are abundant in the sandy shallows, and an eel garden is present at around 6 metres. Occasional turtles visit the reef. Note: Caribbean reef sharks, eagle rays, and hammerhead sharks mentioned in previous descriptions were not confirmed at Northern Exposure by any primary source — these species are documented at other Grenada sites (Shark Reef, Bianca C) but not specifically at this location.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the shallow sandy patch at around 6 metres where the eel garden begins. Follow the gently sloping reef progressively deeper through the finger and pillar coral formations, pausing at the Azure Vase sponges visible at mid-depth. The reef meets the seabed at 23 metres. Ascend along the reef face, taking time in the 10–15 metre zone where fish density is highest. Allow adequate air for the full safety stop and boat return.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from Grand Anse or True Blue operators within the Marine Protected Area. Giant stride entry; DSMB on ascent. Northern Exposure is part of the southwest reef system, accessible on standard dive-day trips. Often combined with other Boss Reef sections or wreck dives for a two-tank day.

## Tips and Recommendations

Start the dive in the shallow sandy patch — the eel garden at 6 metres is an easy-to-miss detail that rewards a slow approach. Look carefully in the sand for conch and crustaceans before following the reef slope deeper. The finger and pillar coral formations are among the best-developed in the southwest reef system. Combine with a wreck dive at the Veronica L or Shakem for a varied two-tank morning.

## Safety Considerations

This is a protected Marine Park site with typically calm conditions. Standard precautions apply. Deploy DSMB before ascending — boat traffic in the southwest Grenada dive area is active. Monitor air consumption on the sloping reef profile.

## Photography

The finger and pillar coral formations and schooling Creole wrasse make Northern Exposure a good wide-angle reef photography site. The eel garden in the shallows offers macro opportunities, and the Azure Vase sponges at mid-depth are distinctive subjects. Natural light at 5–23 metres in Grenada's clear water is excellent throughout the dive.
