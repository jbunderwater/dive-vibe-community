## Sylph's Hole

Sylph's Hole is one of three natural seabed depressions in Lord Howe Island's lagoon, located approximately 100 metres offshore from Old Settlement Beach and regularly visited by resident Green Sea Turtles.

## Overview

Sylph's Hole is the lagoon depression closest to the main settlement, making it the easiest of the three lagoon holes (alongside Comets Hole and Erscott's Hole) to access from shore. Depth is approximately 10 metres in the depression. The site lies within a sanctuary zone and is accessible by a short swim from shore. It is a popular snorkelling and beginner dive spot, particularly when the high tide brings in schools of fish. A yellow mooring buoy marks the snorkelling zone near the hole.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Approximately 100 metres offshore from Old Settlement Beach, Lord Howe Island lagoon
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Special Note**: Within a sanctuary zone; yellow mooring buoy marks the snorkelling area

## Marine Life

Green Sea Turtles are the signature species at Sylph's Hole — several individuals are resident in the lagoon shallows and are regularly encountered here. Three-striped Butterflyfish are reported at this specific site. The broader lagoon fauna, including Double-header Wrasse (endemic) and Lord Howe Island Moray Eel (endemic), is present throughout the area. No site-specific published marine life lists beyond turtles and three-striped butterflyfish were found for Sylph's Hole; the regional lagoon fauna of 500 fish species and 90 coral species applies broadly.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the beach at Old Settlement Beach and swim approximately 100 metres to the mooring buoy. Maximum depth 10 metres in the depression. The protected lagoon setting means minimal current. High tide brings the most fish activity. Ideal for beginner divers and photographers.
