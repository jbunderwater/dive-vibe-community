## Sylph's Hole

Sylph's Hole is one of three seabed depressions in Lord Howe Island's lagoon, accessible from Old Settlement Beach, known for encounters with green sea turtles and recently discovered endemic species.

## Overview

Sylph's Hole is the third of Lord Howe Island's famous lagoon "holes" (alongside Erscott's Hole and Comets Hole), accessible by a short swim from Old Settlement Beach. At approximately 10 metres deep, the site is shallow enough for snorkellers and easy for beginner divers. The protected lagoon waters are calm year-round. The site has attracted scientific attention in recent years as several new and unusual species have been recorded here, including the lattice butterflyfish — photographed for the first time in the Lord Howe Island Marine Park at Sylph's Hole. Friendly green sea turtles are a consistent highlight.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Old Settlement Beach, Lord Howe Island lagoon
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Special Note**: One of three lagoon "holes"; accessible to snorkellers; introductory dive site

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at Sylph's Hole include green sea turtles (resident and approachable), bentstick pipefish, goldstripe butterflyfish, great seahorse, and lattice butterflyfish (first recorded in Lord Howe Marine Park here). Broader lagoon endemics such as double-header wrasse and Lord Howe moray eel are present throughout the lagoon system. The site continues to yield new species records, reflecting the high biodiversity of the Lord Howe marine park.

## Dive Profile

Maximum depth is approximately 10 metres. The short swim from Old Settlement Beach makes this accessible without boat support. The calm lagoon conditions and shallow depth allow extended exploration. Explore the depression and surrounding reef structure, watching for turtles resting on the bottom and small endemic fish in the reef crevices.
