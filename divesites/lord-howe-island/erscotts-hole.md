## Erscott's Hole

Erscott's Hole is a shallow protected lagoon reef, a five-minute boat ride from the boatshed, known for consistently excellent visibility and a resident Galapagos whaler shark.

## Overview

Erscott's Hole is one of Lord Howe Island's most popular beginner dives, situated inside the lagoon as a designated marine sanctuary zone. The site is dominated by a luxuriant garden of staghorn coral and is diveable in nearly all weather conditions. Fish are accustomed to daily visits and are notably tame, making it an ideal site for photographers and trainee divers. The site is not a cave — it is an open intertidal reef area in protected waters, free of significant current.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Inside Lord Howe Island lagoon, five-minute boat ride from the boatshed
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters
- **Special Note**: Marine sanctuary zone; fish feeding occurs; diveable year-round

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at Erscott's Hole include Bluefish (Double Header Wrasse), Spangled Emperors, Neon Damsels, Coleman's Pygmy Seahorse, Foster's Hawkfish, Spotted Snake Eel, and Beaked Leatherjacket. A resident Galapagos whaler shark patrols the site. The broader lagoon ecosystem hosts over 500 fish species and 90 coral species. Staghorn coral gardens dominate the bottom.

## Dive Profile

Maximum depth is approximately 8 metres. The shallow depth allows extended bottom time. The lagoon location means conditions are calm and current-free in almost all weather. Begin with the staghorn coral gardens and work outward along the reef edges. The site's tame fish and macro subjects make it especially rewarding for underwater photography.
