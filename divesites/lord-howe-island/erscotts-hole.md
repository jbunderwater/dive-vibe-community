## Erscott's Hole

Erscott's Hole is a deep natural depression inside Lord Howe Island's shallow lagoon, reached by a five-minute boat ride and known for consistently excellent visibility and rare macro species.

## Overview

Erscott's Hole sits within the protected lagoon, surrounded by an otherwise very shallow reef. Because it is deeply sheltered and generally current-free, it is often the first choice when conditions prevent diving at outer sites. Maximum depth is approximately 8 metres. Visibility is typically excellent. The site is particularly valued for macro photography, and trained glass-bottom boat guides regularly visit, keeping fish accustomed to human presence. It is suitable for novice divers and is also used as a training site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Inside Lord Howe Island lagoon, 5-minute boat ride from the boatshed
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters

## Marine Life

Erscott's Hole is home to Bluefish (endemic to Lord Howe Island, capable of shifting body colour from dark green to sky blue in seconds), Double Header Wrasse, Spangled Emperors, and Neon Damsels. Rare species observed here include Coleman's Pygmy Seahorse, Foster's Hawkfish, Spotted Snake Eel, and Beaked Leatherjacket. Staghorn coral dominates the surrounding reef structure.

## Dive Profile

Maximum depth is approximately 8 metres. Dive the outer rim of the lagoon depression and explore the coral-lined walls of the hole. The sheltered position means no significant current. Extended bottom time is easily achievable at this depth. Ideal for photography — the combination of excellent visibility and macro species makes this a popular first dive of the day.
