## Tenth of June

Tenth of June is one of the most-dived sites at the Admiralty Islets, featuring a deep plateau at 36 metres, swim-throughs and gutters at 24 metres, and a system of tunnels riddling the reef — known for massive black cod, gorgonians, and black coral trees on the walls.

## Overview

Located at one of the Admiralty Islets north of Lord Howe Island, Tenth of June encompasses multiple dive variants: the shallower Tenth of June Bombora (7–18 m, with pinnacles that can be circumnavigated, Spanish Dancers, and schools of tropical reef fish) and the deeper Tenth of June Deep (20–36 m, featuring crevices, canyons, tunnels, swim-throughs, soft corals, and gorgonians). The site sits at the northern end of the Admiralty Islands group, where the Coral Sea current induces prolific soft coral and gorgonian growth during summer months. Tunnels at the 24 metre level connect cave-like passages through the reef. The deep plateau drops to 36 metres and requires good depth management and air monitoring.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Admiralty Islets, 15 minutes north of Lord Howe Island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 36 meters
- **Special Note**: Shallower variant (Tenth of June Bombora, 7–18 m) suits intermediate divers; deep variant requires Advanced certification

## Marine Life

The shallow Bombora variant hosts Spanish Dancers, Blue Angelfish, Green Jobfish, Japanese Boarfish, Lord Howe Tricolour Fairy Basslets, and Round-backed Coral Crabs. The deeper variant is known for black cod reaching up to 180 kg sheltering in the tunnels and gutters. The walls carry extensive gorgonians and black coral trees. Additional species confirmed at this site include Harlequin Tuskfish, Long-nosed Butterflyfish, and large soft coral structures on the vertical faces. Large pelagic fish are commonly encountered in the blue water adjacent to the pinnacle.

## Dive Profile

The shallower Tenth of June Bombora (7–18 m) features a pinnacle that rises to within a few metres of the surface and can be circumnavigated in a single dive. The deeper Tenth of June Deep begins at approximately 20 m on the plateau and descends through a system of gutters and swim-throughs at 24 m before reaching the deep base at 36 m. Crevices and tunnels offer multiple routes through the reef. Plan the dive to begin at depth and ascend progressively, using the shallow reef top for safety stop time. Current awareness is important — the northern Admiralty sites receive the Coral Sea current and can have noticeable water movement.
