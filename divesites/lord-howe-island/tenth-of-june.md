## Tenth of June

Tenth of June is one of the Admiralty Islands' most popular dive sites, comprising two distinct pinnacle formations: Tenth of June Bombora (a circumnavigable vertical pinnacle rising from 18m to 7m) and Tenth of June Deep (a plateau at 20m dropping via a 12m wall to 30m, riddled with canyons, tunnels, and swim-throughs).

## Overview

Tenth of June sits within the Admiralty Islands group, approximately 15 minutes north of Lord Howe Island by boat. The site name covers two closely related but distinct formations. The Bombora is a vertical pinnacle that can be fully circumnavigated; it rises from 18 metres to just 7 metres at the top. The Deep section begins on a 20-metre plateau and drops via a wall to 30 metres, with a series of canyons and swim-through tunnels — sources report the overall depth range for the complex as 24–36 metres. Maximum depth has been corrected to 36 metres based on multiple sources (previously listed as 30 metres).

The site is washed by the Coral Sea current in summer months, bringing increased soft coral growth, gorgonian fans, black coral trees, and tropical pelagic fish species. It is one of Lord Howe's benchmark pinnacle dives.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Admiralty Islets, 15 minutes north of Lord Howe Island by boat
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 36 meters
- **Variants**: Tenth of June Bombora; Tenth of June Deep

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at Tenth of June Bombora include Spanish Dancers, Blue Angelfish, Green Jobfish, Japanese Boarfish, Tricolour Fairy Basselets (endemic to Lord Howe Island), and Round-backed Coral Crabs. Tenth of June Deep hosts large Black Cod, Harlequin Tuskfish (with distinctive blue teeth), Long-nosed Butterflyfish, soft corals, Gorgonians, and Black Coral trees. Japanese Boarfish occur in small schools on the deeper reefs. Coral Sea current in summer brings greater variety of soft corals and tropical fish life.

## Dive Profile

Tenth of June Bombora: descend to 18 metres and ascend along the pinnacle wall to the 7-metre summit, then circumnavigate the pinnacle rim. A good site for intermediate divers with pinnacle experience. Tenth of June Deep: begin on the 20-metre plateau, explore the canyons and tunnels, then drop to 30–36 metres along the wall. Careful air and depth management required at the deeper sections. Visibility typically 20–30 metres.
