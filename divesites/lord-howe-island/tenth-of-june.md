## Tenth of June

Tenth of June is one of the most popular dive sites at the Admiralty Islands — a circumnavigable pinnacle rising from 18 metres to 7 metres, with a deep variant featuring a plateau at 20 metres dropping into canyons, tunnels, and swim-throughs to 30 metres.

## Overview

Tenth of June sits among the Admiralty Islets, 15 minutes north of Lord Howe Island, and exists in two distinct forms that are often dived on the same trip. The shallower Tenth of June is a pinnacle rising from 18 metres to 7 metres at its crest, circumnavigable with good depth management. Tenth of June Deep presents a large reef plateau at 20 metres riddled with crevices, canyons, tunnels, and swim-throughs, with the wall dropping a further 12 metres to 30 metres on the seafloor. The deep variant is designated advanced due to depth, complex navigation, and nitrogen management requirements.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Admiralty Islets, approximately 15 minutes north of Lord Howe Island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Special Note**: Two variants — pinnacle circuit (shallower) and deep plateau/wall (advanced)

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at Tenth of June include Spanish dancers, blue angelfish, green jobfish, Japanese boarfish, tricolour basselets, and round-backed coral crabs on the shallower pinnacle. The deep variant hosts black cod, harlequin tuskfish (distinctive blue teeth), long-nosed butterflyfish, soft corals, gorgonians, and black coral trees on the vertical walls. Large black cod up to 180kg have been reported across the Admiralty Islands, including this site.

## Dive Profile

On the pinnacle variant, divers circumnavigate the formation from 18 metres up to the 7-metre crest — a straightforward circuit with good light and moderate depth. On Tenth of June Deep, the dive begins on a 20-metre plateau and explores crevices before following the wall down to 30 metres through canyons and swim-throughs. Bottom time is limited at depth; careful air management is required. This site is best suited to divers comfortable with 25–30 metre dives and complex navigation.
