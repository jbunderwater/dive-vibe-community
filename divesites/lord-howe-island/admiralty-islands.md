## Admiralty Islands

The Admiralty Islands are a group of small volcanic islets 15 minutes north of Lord Howe Island, offering approximately 25 distinct dive sites around their pinnacles, walls, and bommies at depths from 5 to 40 metres.

## Overview

The Admiralty Islands — also called the Admiralty Islets — sit at the northeastern tip of Lord Howe Island and are exposed to the Coral Sea current during summer months, which drives greater soft coral and gorgonian growth than sites closer to the main island. The individual islets (Sugar Loaf, Noddy, Ruperts, Ryans, Roach, and No Name) each offer distinct terrain: steep walls, flat reef plateaus, boulder-covered bommies, and gently sloping ridges. Depths range from 5 metres on the reef tops to 40 metres at the base of the pinnacle structures. Around 25 named dive sites are distributed across the group, making this the most extensive dive area accessible from Lord Howe Island. The sites suit intermediate to advanced divers depending on which specific location is dived.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Admiralty Islets, 15 minutes north of Lord Howe Island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Special Note**: ~25 named dive sites across the group; conditions vary by islet

## Marine Life

The Admiralty Islands are noted for several rare and endemic species. Confirmed sightings include Blue Angelfish, Green Jobfish, Japanese Boarfish, Lord Howe Island Tricolour Fairy Basslet (*Pseudanthias trisignatus*), and Round-backed Coral Crabs on the pinnacle tops. Black cod up to 180 kg are found on the deeper reefs. The group is described as a Spanish Dancer (nudibranch) haven. Additional species documented at specific Admiralty sites include Double-header fish, Tasselled Scorpionfish, moray eels, Sydney Octopus, Conspicuous Angelfish, Stingrays, Peacock-tail Anemone Shrimp, large soft corals, and black coral trees on the walls. At Noddy Reef (a wall site within the group), Grey morays, kingfish, blue drummers, Galapagos sharks, Halfbanded Angelfish, and Conspicuous Angelfish have been documented. At Rupert's Reef, a resident green turtle, sea fans, squat lobsters, Grey Coral Gobies, Saddled Rock Cod, and Painted Crayfish are regularly encountered.

## Dive Profile

Diving across the Admiralty Islands varies significantly by site. Shallow reef tops (5–10 m) offer easy circumnavigation of pinnacles covered in hard and soft corals. Deeper sites descend walls to 30–40 metres, with swim-throughs and boulder fields in the mid-water zone (15–25 m). Current can be present on the more exposed islets — particularly notable during summer when the Coral Sea current flows through. Most guided dives to this area allow divers to choose sites suited to their certification and experience. A surface interval at Ned's Beach (a short distance away) is commonly offered between dives.
