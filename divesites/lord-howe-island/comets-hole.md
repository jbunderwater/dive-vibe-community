## Comets Hole

Comets Hole is a shallow lagoon depression inside Lord Howe Island's protected lagoon, formed by freshwater upwelling and surrounded by a 3–4 metre coral reef wall, renowned as a "one species a minute" dive for its extraordinary fish density.

## Overview

Comets Hole is one of three named lagoon depressions at Lord Howe Island, accessible by a short boat ride from the boatshed. The hole was formed by natural freshwater upwelling, which keeps the water 1–2 degrees cooler than surrounding lagoon sites and prevents the depression from silting over. The surrounding coral reef wall rises 3–4 metres from the sandy bottom. At 7 metres maximum depth, it is one of the most accessible dive sites on the island and is reliably diveable in virtually all weather.

Note: Entry is by boat into the lagoon — the site is not a practical shore entry despite its lagoon position.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Inside Lord Howe Island lagoon, short boat ride from boatshed
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 meters
- **Conditions**: Protected; consistently excellent visibility; no current; suitable for all skill levels

## Marine Life

Comets Hole is one of the most species-dense dive sites on Lord Howe Island. Confirmed species include: endemic McCulloch's Anemonefish, large schools of Lined Catfish, Decorator Crabs, Coral Gobies, White-mouthed Moray, Slipper Crayfish, Marble Shrimp, Trevally, Painted Morwong, Silver Drummer, Three-striped Butterflyfish, and three species of Lionfish. The Lord Howe Island Moray Eel — an endemic species — is also confirmed here. Larger visitors along the reef edge include Bull Rays, Long-tailed Whip Rays, and Galapagos Whaler Sharks. Porites, Acropora, and Needle corals provide the structure.

## Dive Profile

Maximum depth is 7 metres. The dive centres on the freshwater-formed depression and its surrounding 3–4 metre coral wall. Extended bottom time is easily achievable. Explore the depression interior, then follow the reef wall circumference. The freshwater upwelling at the bottom of the hole is a notable feature. Excellent site for macro photography and fish observation.
