## Comets Hole

Comets Hole is Lord Howe Island's house reef — a freshwater-formed lagoon depression described as a "one species a minute" dive, famous among underwater photographers and the best site on the island for endemic species.

## Overview

Located just minutes from the boatshed within the shelter of the lagoon, Comets Hole is diveable when almost every other site is inaccessible due to weather. The hole was formed by natural freshwater upwelling and never fills with sand, keeping it permanently open. Maximum depth is 7 metres, with the site surrounded by a 3–4 metre high coral reef wall. This is consistently rated as a must-do dive for underwater photographers and beginners alike. Species diversity is exceptional for such a shallow site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Within Lord Howe Island lagoon (house reef), minutes from the boatshed
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Freshwater-formed lagoon reef hole
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 7 metres
- **Coral Wall**: 3–4 metre high coral reef wall surrounding the hole

## Marine Life

Comets Hole hosts the endemic McCulloch's Anemonefish (one of Australia's rarest anemonefish, found only at Lord Howe Island). Large schools of Lined Catfish are a signature sight. Other confirmed species include Decorator Crabs, Coral Gobies, White-mouthed Moray, Lord Howe Island Moray Eel, Slipper Crayfish, Marble Shrimp, Trevally, Painted Morwong, Silver Drummer, Three-striped Butterflyfish, three species of Lionfish, and Zebra Morwong. Larger visitors include Black Stingrays, Bull Rays, Long-tailed Whip Rays, Kingfish, and Galapagos Whaler Sharks cruising the reef edge. The corals include Porites, Acroporas, and Needle corals.

## Dive Profile

Maximum depth is 7 metres. Enter from shore and swim to the hole. Explore the freshwater-formed depression and the surrounding 3–4 metre coral walls. The protected lagoon position means minimal current. Extended bottom time allows thorough exploration of the hole's interior. Early morning visits typically offer the best fish activity.
