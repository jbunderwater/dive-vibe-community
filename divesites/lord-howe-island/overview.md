## Lord Howe Island

The world's southernmost coral reef ecosystem at 31°33'S, home to unique endemic species and pristine diving across 60+ dive sites.

## Description

Lord Howe Island is Australia's most iconic subtropical diving destination, located at the crossroads of five major ocean currents including the warm East Australian Current. The island hosts the world's southernmost coral reef ecosystem—a unique environment found nowhere else on Earth with over 90 species of coral and 500 species of fish. Diving depths range from 7 to 36 meters, with water temperatures averaging 20-24°C (68-75°F) and excellent visibility of 15-40 meters (50-130 feet) year-round. Best diving conditions occur September to February.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Lagoon Diving**: Shallow, sheltered sites like Comets Hole, Erscott's Hole, and Sylph's Hole offer beginner-friendly diving in protective lagoon waters with abundant marine life and reliable conditions in nearly all weather.
- **Reef Diving**: Malabar Reef and other sites feature healthy coral structures with pelagic fish activity and excellent photography opportunities.
- **Pinnacle Diving**: The Admiralty Islands (25+ dive sites) and Tenth of June feature dramatic volcanic formations, towering pinnacles, caves, and diverse ecosystems at depths to 40 meters.
- **Wall Diving**: North Bay Dropoff and other sites feature dramatic vertical walls dropping to 25+ meters.
- **Advanced Pelagic**: Ball's Pyramid (26km offshore, Advanced-only) is the world's tallest sea stack featuring rare endemic fish, large pelagic schools, and exceptional wall diving at 25 meters average depth.

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Lord Howe Island is accessible via regional flights (NSW). Check with operators for current flight schedules.
- **Dive Operators**: Professional operators including Dive Lord Howe offer equipment rental, guided dives, certification courses, and boat access to all sites.
- **Accommodation**: Island lodges and guesthouses cater to divers at various price points.
- **Dive Site Access**: Shore dives accessible from beaches; boat dives typically 5-20 minutes from shore.

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Temperatures 20-24°C (68-75°F) with visibility 15-40 meters (50-130 feet) year-round. Currents are generally moderate and variable.
- **Marine Biodiversity**: Crossroads location supports tropical, subtropical, and temperate species coexisting nowhere else. Notable species include endemic Ballina Angelfish (at Ball's Pyramid), green sea turtles, kingfish, trevally, garibaldi fish, sharks, rays, diverse coral gardens, and 500+ fish species.
- **Endemic Species**: Unique endemic species include McCulloch's anemonefish, Lord Howe Island moray eel, and other endemic fauna.
- **Conservation**: Lord Howe Island Marine Park protects the ecosystem; dive site conservation is essential.

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: September to February (spring/summer). Year-round diving is possible.
- **Currency**: Australian Dollar (AUD)
- **Language**: English
- **Safety**: Dive within certification limits. Be aware of variable currents and maintain buoyancy discipline. The island is a UNESCO World Heritage site with strict environmental protections.
