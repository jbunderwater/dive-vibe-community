## Malabar Reef

Malabar Reef is a multi-section reef system on the exposed outer coast of Lord Howe Island, comprising several distinct dive sites (Malabar, Malabar West, Malabar Third Reef, and Landslide) with overhangs, coral walls, and ledge habitats from 10–21 metres.

## Overview

The Malabar reef system runs along the island's outer coast and encompasses several named subsites offering varied terrain: rocky ledges at Malabar West (10–16 m), deeper ledge-and-cave diving at Malabar Third Reef, and mixed overhangs and finger reefs at Malabar proper. The site is exposed to ocean swell on some headings and is best dived by boat. At depths around 18–21 metres the reef features strong hard coral cover, ledge habitats, and diverse cryptic species making it a productive macro photography destination. Plate and table corals are noted features in the shallower sections.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Outer reef, western/southern coast of Lord Howe Island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Species confirmed at the Malabar reef system: cardinalfish, bullseyes, soldierfish, lionfish, octopus, spiny lobsters, nudibranchs, Spanish dancers, anemones, clownfish, damsels, sweetlips, leatherjackets, drummer, pufferfish, boxfish, and morays. At Malabar Third Reef specifically, ledges and caves shelter slipper lobsters, morays, nudibranchs, and an aggregation of up to 25 black-blotched stingrays, which is a notable feature. The shallow sections support plate and table corals with associated reef fish. Lord Howe Island's broader marine environment also brings pelagic visitors, though site-specific pelagic sightings at Malabar are not independently documented.

## Dive Profile

Begin in shallower sections (10–12 m) and follow the reef slope and ledge system deeper. The Malabar West subsite runs 10–16 m with rocky reef and numerous ledges sheltering cryptic species. Deeper sections of Malabar proper and Malabar Third Reef reach 18–21 m and feature more pronounced ledge and cave structures. The multi-site nature means a single dive can focus on one section, and repeat dives reward exploration of the different subsites. Best suited to divers comfortable with 18–21 m and moderate surge on exposed reef.
