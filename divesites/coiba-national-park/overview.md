## Coiba National Park

Panama's UNESCO World Heritage marine park on the Pacific coast, where whale sharks, hammerhead schools, and giant mantas patrol the second-largest coral reef in the Eastern Pacific.

## Description

Coiba National Park is a remote UNESCO World Heritage Site off Panama's Pacific coast, often called the "Central American Galapagos" for its extraordinary pelagic encounters. The park encompasses 10 documented dive sites ranging from 18 to 40 meters depth, all boat-accessible and rated Advanced due to strong currents and remote location. Highlights include the Bahia Damas reef system -- the second-largest coral reef in the Eastern Pacific -- and deep pinnacles like Montana Rusa where hammerheads and whale sharks patrol. With 33 recorded shark species and seasonal humpback whale migrations, Coiba delivers some of the most dramatic big-animal diving in Central America.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Boat Diving**: All 10 sites are boat-access only, departing from Santa Catalina (approximately 75-minute boat ride to the park)
- **Reef Diving**: 5 reef sites including Bahia Damas with its massive hard coral gardens, Granito de Oro with colorful sponges, and Santa Cruz with scenic shallow hard corals
- **Pinnacle Diving**: 3 pinnacle sites including Montana Rusa (35-40m, severe currents, major pelagic action), Shark Point Coiba (rock and coral canyon formations), and Jicarita Island
- **Wall Diving**: Machete Point offers a wall dive to 28 meters with strong currents
- **Pelagic Encounters**: Whale sharks (January-March), hammerhead sharks (April-May schools of juveniles), giant manta rays (March), and humpback whales (July-October)

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: The gateway town is Santa Catalina, reached by a 5.5-hour bus ride from Panama City or by flying to Lago Bay Airstrip followed by a short ground transfer. From Santa Catalina, dive boats take approximately 75 minutes to reach Coiba Island
- **Dive Operators**: Scuba Coiba (PADI 5-star, operating since 2003, the only operator running overnight trips to remote northern sites), Panama Dive Center, and other Santa Catalina-based operators. Day trips and multi-day liveaboard-style overnight excursions are available
- **Accommodation**: Santa Catalina offers hostels, small hotels, and eco-lodges. Overnight trips to Coiba use the park's ranger station facilities on the island
- **Transportation**: No public transport within the park; all movement is by dive boat. Santa Catalina itself is a small village walkable on foot
- **Facilities**: Santa Catalina has basic restaurants, small shops, and limited ATMs. Bring sufficient cash. The park has a ranger station but no tourist facilities

### Marine Life & Environment

- **Water Conditions**: Water temperature 27-29°C (81-84°F) at the surface, but thermoclines can drop to 16°C (60°F) at depth; a 5mm wetsuit is recommended. Visibility is highly variable depending on rainfall and ocean currents. Strong currents are common at most sites
- **Marine Biodiversity**: 33 shark species recorded including whale sharks, hammerheads, whitetip reef sharks, and bull sharks. Giant manta rays, spotted eagle rays, southern stingrays, sea turtles, massive schools of big-eyed trevally, yellowtail snapper, batfish, and humpback whales seasonally
- **Conservation**: UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2005. The island served as a penal colony for decades, which inadvertently preserved 80% of its ecosystems intact. Strict marine protection zones are enforced with ranger patrols and park entry permits required

## Additional Information

- **Best Time to Visit**: December to May (dry season) offers the most reliable conditions. January to March for whale sharks, April to May for hammerhead schools, July to October for humpback whales
- **Currency**: United States Dollar (USD) and Panamanian Balboa (PAB)
- **Language**: Spanish
- **Safety**: All sites are rated Advanced due to strong and unpredictable currents, cold thermoclines, and remote location far from medical facilities. Advanced open water certification and current experience are strongly recommended. Nearest hyperbaric chamber is in Panama City
