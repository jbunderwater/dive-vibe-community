## M/V Transfer

M/V Transfer is a British cargo vessel that ran aground on Lighthouse Reef Atoll in 2005, now a developing artificial reef in Belize's most remote offshore atoll.

## Overview

The M/V Transfer was a 225-foot British cargo vessel that ran aground on Lighthouse Reef Atoll on January 6, 2005, after a faulty autopilot directed the ship into the reef at night. This grounding date is confirmed by 7 News Belize and regional photo records. When Hurricane Emily subsequently passed through the Caribbean, storm surge pushed additional sections of the hull further onto the shallow reef structure. The owner of the vessel was identified as the Ariane Shipping Company in 7 News Belize reporting; this ownership detail appears in a single source and has not been independently confirmed. The wreck sits at around 20 metres in Lighthouse Reef's characteristically clear water and has been slowly colonised with coral and sponge encrustation since 2005, hosting groupers, snappers, and reef fish. The remote location at Lighthouse Reef means fewer divers than mainland Belize sites, offering a quieter exploratory dive in waters with visibility regularly reaching 20–40 metres.

Penetration diving into any wreck carries risk; interior exploration should only be undertaken by properly trained and equipped divers.
