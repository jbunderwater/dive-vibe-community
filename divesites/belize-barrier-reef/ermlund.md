## Ermlund

Ermlund is a wreck dive site at Lighthouse Reef Atoll, Belize Barrier Reef.

## Overview

The Ermlund was a large steel freighter of approximately 4,000 gross tons that lost power during a storm in 1971 and was deposited on the reef by a surge wave, coming to rest approximately 1,000 yards (900 metres) north of Half Moon Caye at Lighthouse Reef Atoll. Sources describe the vessel as approximately 350-400 feet in length, though independent maritime records confirming the vessel's nationality (widely stated as Norwegian in regional dive sources) or detailed build specifications have not been located.

The wreck initially sat intact on top of the reef with its superstructure visible above the waterline. It was subsequently used by the RAF as a bombing target — the wreck is sometimes called "Harrier" after the type of aircraft used — splitting the hull into two main sections. Since then, Caribbean swells have further broken the wreck; it now lies in at least three sections on the shallow reef, visible at both high and low tide. The OSM tag notes the hull is showing above the waterline; actual dive depth over the wreck is shallow, roughly 5-8 metres over the reef-top sections. Divers visiting Half Moon Caye sometimes pair this site with the Half Moon Wall nearby.

**Note on vessel history:** The Ermlund does not appear in the 1971 Wikipedia shipwrecks list. The claim of Norwegian registry is repeated by regional dive guides but could not be confirmed against an independent maritime source. The 4,000 gross ton figure and the 1971 grounding date are consistently reported across multiple regional sources.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Lighthouse Reef Atoll, Belize (1,000 yards north of Half Moon Caye)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 6 meters
