## Long Caye Aquarium

Long Caye Aquarium is a wall dive site on the western side of Lighthouse Reef Atoll, Belize.

## Overview

Long Caye Aquarium — commonly called "The Aquarium" — is located off the northwestern corner of Long Caye at Lighthouse Reef Atoll. The reef top starts at about 6 metres (20 feet) as a table-top formation of brain coral, purple sea fans, and sea plumes, then drops off abruptly at around 11 metres (35 feet) into a sheer wall that descends well beyond recreational limits. Coral ridges run perpendicular to the reef with sandy canyons between them. Moderate to strong currents flow across the reef top almost continuously, though currents along the wall itself can be minimal. Visibility at Lighthouse Reef typically reaches 24 metres (80 feet) or more. The site is classified as a wall dive; it is often the final stop on Blue Hole day trips and is frequently described as the highlight by returning divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Long Caye, Lighthouse Reef Atoll, Belize
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

The table-top reef supports schools of Bermuda chubs, sergeant majors, and creole wrasse. The wall overhangs host tiger groupers, deep-water lace coral, and black coral. Wide-ranging invertebrate life is reported in the crevices — shrimp and spiny lobster are commonly sighted. Spotted eagle rays, barracuda, and tarpon have been documented at the site. The Belize Dive Haven Resort, located directly across from the Aquarium, specifically names black coral at the wall overhangs; hawksbill turtles are frequently reported throughout the Lighthouse Reef system but no source confirms regular sightings at the Aquarium specifically.
