## Long Caye Aquarium

Long Caye Aquarium (commonly called "The Aquarium") is a wall dive off the western side of Lighthouse Reef Atoll — frequently cited by divers as the highlight of the standard Blue Hole day trip.

## Overview

Located off the northwestern corner of Long Caye at Lighthouse Reef, The Aquarium begins as a flat table-top reef at around 6 metres, covered in clusters of brain coral, purple sea fans, and sea plumes. The reef edge drops suddenly at approximately 11 metres into a sheer wall that continues into very deep water — the ledge eventually reaches depths far beyond recreational limits. Coral ridges running perpendicular to the wall face are filled with cavities where marine life concentrates.

The site takes its name from the extraordinary variety of invertebrates found here: decorator crabs, banded coral shrimp, and neck crabs inhabit the same overhangs as black coral, lace coral, and sponge formations. Visibility at Lighthouse Reef is consistently outstanding, typically 24–40 metres. Divers on the Hamanasi-style Blue Hole full-day excursion typically dive here as the final tank, reaching a maximum of 15–18 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Long Caye, Lighthouse Reef Atoll, Belize
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Divers report spotted eagle rays, Caribbean reef sharks, groupers, barracuda, Bermuda chubs, sergeant majors, creole wrasse, parrotfish, and pintano in open water around the wall. Hawksbill turtles are regularly seen grazing sponges along the wall face. The invertebrate diversity — decorator crabs, shrimp, spiny lobster — is what originally gave the site its name, and the wall overhangs support black coral growth.
