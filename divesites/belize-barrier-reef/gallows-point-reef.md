## Gallows Point Reef

Gallows Point Reef is a wall dive site near St. George's Caye, Belize Barrier Reef.

## Overview

Gallows Point Reef is a five-mile section of the barrier reef located off St. George's Caye, approximately 10 miles east of Belize City. Unlike the spur-and-groove reef formations common further north near Ambergris Caye, this section features wall formations dropping from a shallow reef to a sandy slope. The dive begins over sandy bottom at roughly 10 metres, then reaches canyons formed by star, brain, and plate coral that lead to a wall dropping from about 10 metres to a sandy slope at approximately 33 metres. The gorgonian-draped wall is covered in sea fans and soft corals; below the wall, the sand hosts garden eels, jawfish, and tube worms. The site is a boat dive, accessible via operators based in Belize City and St. George's Caye; at least one Belize City operator (Sea Sports Belize) offers regular 2-tank dive trips to Gallows Point Reef.

## Site Information

- **Location**: St. George's Caye, Belize Barrier Reef
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 33 meters

## Marine Life

ECOMAR has conducted underwater surveys at Gallows Point since 2009 and documents the critically endangered hawksbill sea turtle as the most abundant species recorded at this location, with the fore reef confirmed as important developmental habitat for juvenile hawksbills. Spotted eagle rays, snappers, groupers, angelfish, and trumpetfish are commonly reported by dive guides. Garden eels and jawfish inhabit the sandy slope below the wall. Stingrays have been reported during surface intervals by visiting divers.
