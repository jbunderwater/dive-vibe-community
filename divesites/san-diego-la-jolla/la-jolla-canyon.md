## La Jolla Canyon

La Jolla Submarine Canyon is a spectacular advanced shore dive featuring dramatic vertical canyon walls, with the canyon rim beginning at approximately 35-40 feet and recreational diving extending to 130 feet (40 meters).

## Overview

The Scripps/La Jolla Submarine Canyon is a massive geological feature that creates one of Southern California's most dramatic diving experiences. The canyon rim sits approximately 250 yards offshore at 35-40 feet of water, dropping in a series of ledges that range from 10 to 20 feet in height. The recreational diving envelope runs from the rim at ~40 feet down to approximately 130 feet (40 m) — the practical recreational maximum — though the canyon continues to over 600 feet (180 m) total depth. The most popular access point is at the south end of Kellogg Park at Vallecitos Street. The strong vertical topography, clear water, and pelagic encounters make this exceptional diving, but the stamina required for the surface swim, depth management, and gas planning demand genuine advanced diving skills.

## Site Information

- **Location**: La Jolla Shores area, accessed via shore near Scripps Pier (32°51'22"N 117°15'54"W)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Canyon
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters

## Key Features

**Canyon Ledges**: Accessible from 40-80 feet depth, the ledges provide navigation points and marine life observation zones. The "Amphitheatre" site features a prominent shelf at 100 feet.

**Vertical Walls**: Sheer rock faces ascending from the sandy bottom create dramatic photography opportunities and shelter for wall-dwelling species.

**Pelagic Zone**: The blue water beyond the wall offers opportunities to encounter larger fish species, including ocean sunfish, grouper, and occasionally larger sharks.

## Marine Life

**Resident Fish**: Garibaldi, sheephead, kelp bass, rockfish, and lingcod shelter along the wall.

**Bottom Dwellers**: California sea lions occasionally pass through. Bat rays and horn sharks hunt along sandy areas within the canyon.

**Wall Invertebrates**: Anemones, sea urchins, nudibranchs, and other encrusting organisms coat the vertical rock surfaces.

**Pelagic Visitors**: The open blue water beyond the wall attracts mackerel, barracuda, and occasionally larger predatory fish. Sunfish are common in summer. Sea stars and other invertebrates colonize every available surface.

## Dive Profile

Swim out approximately 250 yards from shore to reach the canyon rim at 35-40 feet. Navigate along the ledges, descending to 60-80 feet for most dives; experienced divers may push to 100-130 feet. The gradual ledge system provides clear depth markers. Plan for 30-40 minutes of bottom time with gas reserves for a controlled multi-stop ascent. The surface swim out and back is significant (~250 yards each way) — pre-dive fitness and an SMB are essential. Visibility is generally good, typically 20-40 feet with occasional 60+ feet days.
