## Zuñiga Jetty

Zuñiga Jetty is a reef dive site in San Diego - La Jolla, North America.

## Overview

Zuñiga Jetty extends approximately 7,500 feet from the northern tip of Coronado Island toward Point Loma, forming the southern boundary of the entrance to San Diego Bay. The east side of the jetty — sheltered from the open ocean — is the primary dive zone, where the rocky structure creates reef habitat on a sandy bottom. This is primarily a fish-watching and seal encounter site rather than a wreck or dramatic reef dive; its appeal lies in the abundance of reef fish congregating along the jetty's rock-to-sand interface and the presence of California sea lions, which are common and can be inquisitive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern tip of Coronado Island, entrance to San Diego Bay
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 9 meters
- **Water Temperature**: 15–20°C

## Marine Life

The jetty's rocky structure draws in a classic Southern California reef assemblage. Sand bass (barred sand bass), calico bass (kelp bass), and sheephead are abundant along the rock-to-sand seam. Halibut lie on the sandy bottom on both the ocean and bay sides of the jetty. California spiny lobster congregate in the rocks during season (October–March open season). California sea lions are present and can be territorial — maintain respectful distance. Harbor seals are also occasionally seen. Swell sharks and horn sharks rest in crevices. Opaleye, señoritas, and various wrasse-family fish are common. Garibaldi are present near the rocky sections.

## Dive Profile

The jetty slopes from the surface to approximately 9 metres (30 feet) at the outer end. Divers typically work the shallower sections (3–9 m) where fish density and light are best. The rock-to-sand transition at the base of the jetty structure is the most productive zone. On slack-to-incoming tide, visibility improves significantly — outgoing tide flushes murky bay water outward.

## Entry and Exit

**Important restriction:** It is illegal to dive inside San Diego Bay between Ballast Point and Zuñiga Point — divers have been ticketed in this area. Access the ocean side of the jetty via shore entry from the Coronado area, or approach by kayak or small boat. Target an incoming or slack tide for cleanest water and easiest conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

Dive slack-to-incoming tide for the best water clarity. The best fish action concentrates along the full length of the jetty at the rock-to-sand seam — work parallel to the structure. Bring a light for examining lobster and shark crevices. Check tides carefully — outgoing tide flushes murky bay water outward.

## Safety Considerations

This site sits at the mouth of one of the US Navy's busiest ports. Boat traffic is constant — fly a dive flag and have a dedicated surface tender. Sea lions can be territorial; do not approach or corner them. Tidal currents at the bay entrance can be significant — stick to the slack window. Water temperature is warmer than Puget Sound (15–20°C) but a 5–7mm wetsuit or drysuit is still appropriate for comfort on longer dives.
