A current-swept outer reef drift on the edge of the Great Astrolabe system, where Pacific swells and tidal current concentrate marine life on the exposed wall.

## Overview

The outer wall of the Great Astrolabe Reef drops from a crest at approximately 15–20 meters to depths exceeding 1,000 meters on the ocean-facing side. This outermost section of Kadavu's reef system faces directly into the Pacific Ocean, and when swell and tidal current are running, it becomes a high-energy environment that concentrates marine life. Grey reef and whitetip reef sharks are permanent residents of the outer reef wall. The reef structure on this ocean-facing edge supports gorgonian sea fans and robust hard and soft coral growth maintained by decades of limited human pressure. The outer reef passages within the Great Astrolabe system — including the documented Naiqoro Passage — are known drift dive sites with confirmed strong currents and shark activity.

## Entry and Exit

Kadavu is reached from Nadi by a 45-minute flight to Vunisea, or from Suva by ferry. Small dive resorts operate local dive boats. Outer reef access is weather-dependent — operators redirect to protected lagoon sites when swells make the outer reef unsafe. SMB deployment is mandatory on outer reef dives.

## Safety Considerations

Outer reef diving at Kadavu is Advanced due to exposed conditions, significant current, and remote location relative to medical evacuation. Defer to local operator judgment on daily suitability.
