# Kansas Namena

You're definitely not in Kansas anymore — a surreal Sinularia soft coral landscape that looks like wheat fields swaying in slow motion.

## Overview

Kansas takes its name from the Wizard of Oz line, coined by NAI'A liveaboard crew who found that the site's soft coral coverage reminded them of rolling Kansas wheat fields. Unlike the Dendronephthya "Christmas tree" corals seen at Great White Wall, Kansas is dominated by Sinularia soft corals — the zooxanthellae-bearing type that uses sunlight for photosynthesis and forms dense, branching colonies over the pinnacle top. When current flows through, the Sinularia "bloom," creating the swaying wheat-field effect that gives the site its name. Kansas is part of the North Save-a-Tack Passage area within Namena Marine Reserve, and is cited by the NAI'A as a key macro photography site — macro photographers are drawn specifically by jawfish, ghost pipefish, pygmy seahorses, and nudibranchs.

The pinnacle structure rises from approximately 27 meters to about 5 meters below the surface, with a feature called the "Kansas window" at 10 meters that is particularly productive for nudibranchs and leaf scorpionfish.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Namena Marine Reserve (North Save-a-Tack Passage area), Koro Sea, Fiji
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–35 meters (65–115 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate; varies with tide
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The Sinularia-covered pinnacle top hosts anemones and clownfish (anemonefish) on its upper surface, alongside anthias, fusiliers, and sweetlips as the primary reef fish. The Kansas window at 10 meters is documented by NAI'A as a reliable spot for nudibranchs and leaf scorpionfish. Pygmy seahorses have been recorded at Kansas — NAI'A crew describe it as one of the pygmy seahorses' favorite spots at Namena. Tuna, reef sharks, and Spanish mackerel appear in the open water around the structure. The sandy channels between pinnacles provide resting habitat for whitetip reef sharks.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the base of the pinnacle at 25–27 meters and begin a circuit of the structure, inspecting the deeper faces for sea fans and macro subjects. Work progressively shallower as the dive proceeds. At 10 meters, pause at the Kansas window to search for nudibranchs and leaf scorpionfish. The Sinularia coverage is most pronounced on the upper pinnacle where sunlight reaches — spend the safety stop period on the summit, watching for the soft coral bloom in any current that is running. Total dive time of 60–70 minutes is achievable.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by liveaboard (NAI'A and others on Fiji circuits) or by dive boat from Savusavu-based operators making the approximately 2-hour journey to the reserve. Namena Marine Reserve entry fees apply — these are distributed to the Namena village community that enforces the no-take zone. Entry and exit are generally calm within the reef system. Drift direction varies by tide; ask your guide which face to start on.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Inspect the Kansas window at 10 meters**: This specific depth feature is the best macro zone on the site. Slow down here and look carefully at the coral structure for nudibranchs, leaf scorpionfish, and any pygmy seahorses in gorgonian fans.
- **Kansas coral is Sinularia, not Dendronephthya**: Unlike Great White Wall's spectacular branching white Dendronephthya, Kansas features the fleshier, more compact Sinularia type. It looks very different — the "wheat field" description captures the movement well.
- **Plan multiple Namena dives**: Kansas, Grand Central Station, Chimneys, and North Save-a-Tack Passage are distinct sites within the reserve. A liveaboard day at Namena can cover three or four sites; a day trip from Savusavu is worth planning for a full day.
- **Respect the reserve fees**: The exceptional marine life here — undisturbed by fishing for decades — is directly a product of the village-managed no-take zone. The fees support that enforcement.

## Safety Considerations

Open-water boat travel from Savusavu in trade wind conditions can be rough. Assess sea state on the morning of the journey and follow the operator's advice on whether conditions are suitable. The dive itself is benign; current is manageable and depth is recreational. Deploy an SMB before every ascent.
