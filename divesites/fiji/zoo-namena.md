# Zoo Namena

A reef site in the Namena area of the Koro Sea known for exceptional fish diversity — the "Zoo" name reflects the breadth of species encountered.

## Overview

Zoo Namena is listed as a site in the Koro Sea near Namenalala Island, in the vicinity of Namena Marine Reserve. A site by this name is referenced in Fiji dive literature in connection with the Somosomo Strait system (Tourism Fiji notes the Strait is sometimes called "the Zoo" because of the diversity of sharks, manta rays, and barracuda), but independent confirmation of Zoo as a specifically named standalone site within Namena Reserve — with documented depths, topography, and site-specific marine life — has not been confirmed across three independent sources during this QA pass. The site's coordinates place it in the Koro Sea near Namenalala, consistent with the broader Namena Reserve area.

Divers visiting the Namena Marine Reserve area will encounter exceptional reef fish diversity across the reserve's protected reef system, regardless of specific site naming. The reserve was established in 1997, is managed with strong local community enforcement of no-take rules, and is consistently cited as offering some of Fiji's densest fish populations.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Koro Sea, near Namena Marine Reserve, Fiji
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–35 meters (65–115 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate; varies with tide
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The broader Namena Marine Reserve is documented as a migration route and habitat for grey reef sharks, hammerhead sharks, manta rays, nurse sharks, marlin, dolphins, barracuda schools, trevally, and abundant reef fish including surgeonfish, parrotfish, batfish, sweetlips, and groupers. However, no site-specific marine life documentation has been confirmed for Zoo Namena as a distinct named site distinct from the general reserve. Marine life description here reflects the Namena Reserve ecosystem.

## Dive Profile

Based on the general Namena Reserve character: a complex reef with coral heads, sandy channels, and intermediate walls or ledges. Dive the current direction, ascending progressively through different habitat zones. The upper reef at 5–12 meters provides an excellent safety stop. Allow 60–70 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Namena is reached by dive boat from Savusavu on Vanua Levu (approximately 2 hours) or from liveaboards. Reserve entry fees apply and are distributed to the local village.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Allocate multiple dives**: The Namena area offers multiple named sites. Operators running full-day trips typically combine several sites.
- **Respect the reserve fees**: The fees support the Namena village community's no-take zone enforcement.
- **Verify site name with local operators**: The specific "Zoo Namena" naming may be used by some operators and not others. Confirm with the operator when booking.

## Safety Considerations

Namena Marine Reserve lies in open water; boat travel can be rough in trade wind conditions. Confirm sea state before the journey. The reserve's sites are generally benign in current and depth. Deploy an SMB before ascending.

## Photography

The Namena area's protected fish populations make it one of Fiji's better wide-angle reef photography destinations. Dense fish aggregations, grey reef sharks, and healthy soft coral provide layered compositions at all depths.
