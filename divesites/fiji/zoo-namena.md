# Zoo Namena

The name says everything — an overwhelming density and variety of marine life that turns every dive into a wildlife census.

## Overview

Zoo Namena is one of the signature sites within Namena Marine Reserve, a protected area surrounding the uninhabited island of Namena in the Koro Sea. The reserve is one of Fiji's most successful marine protected areas, and the fish life here is visibly different from unprotected reefs — larger individuals, denser schools, and less-wary behavior from animals that have not been subject to fishing pressure. The site earns its "Zoo" name from the extraordinary diversity of fish species that can be observed in a single dive: reef sharks, schooling pelagics, surgeonfish, parrotfish, batfish, sweetlips, groupers, and nudibranchs all share the same reef system simultaneously. Namena is approximately two hours by boat from Savusavu on Vanua Levu, making it a liveaboard destination or a dedicated day trip.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Namena Marine Reserve, Koro Sea, Fiji
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–35 meters (65–115 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate; varies with tide
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The Namena reserve supports exceptional densities of reef fish, and Zoo Namena represents one of the richest concentrations within it. Grey reef sharks are present throughout the dive, typically 10–20 individuals at any given time. Large schools of bigeye trevally and barracuda spiral above the reef in continuous formation. Bumphead parrotfish appear in groups, their loud chomping audible underwater. Massive groupers sit stationary on coral heads, unconcerned by divers. Surgeonfish aggregate in feeding schools across the reef surface. Soft coral coverage provides habitat for anthias clouds and diverse butterflyfish communities. Eagle rays and marble rays are documented at the site. The reserve's protection is visibly effective — fish here do not show the wariness typical of sites with regular fishing pressure. No site-specific species list has been independently verified for this exact site; the Namena reserve's general fauna extends across all three named sites (Zoo, Kansas, Supermarket).

## Dive Profile

The site topology is a complex reef with multiple coral heads, sandy channels, and intermediate walls or ledges. Most guides take groups across the broadest section of the reef, timing the dive to follow the current direction and minimizing finning effort. Descend to 15–20 meters and move with the current, ascending progressively through zones of different habitat character. The upper reef at 5–12 meters provides an excellent safety stop in a zone of dense coral and fish activity. Allow at least 60–70 minutes — the site repays unhurried observation.

## Entry and Exit

Namena is reached by dive boat from Savusavu on Vanua Levu (approximately 2 hours) or from liveaboards in the Koro Sea. Day trip operators from Savusavu typically depart early to arrive at the reserve during optimal tide windows. Entry fees for the marine reserve are collected and distributed to the local village that manages it. Entry is by giant stride from the boat.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Allocate multiple dives**: Zoo is one of multiple excellent sites within Namena Marine Reserve — Zoo, Kansas, and Supermarket all lie within a short boat ride of each other. Allocate a full day or more to the reserve.
- **Stay still and watch the sharks**: Grey reef sharks in the reserve are habituated to divers but not conditioned. If you stop swimming and remain still, sharks will often approach to within 2–3 meters on their own initiative. Movement spooks them.
- **Descend on the current side**: Ask the guide which direction current is running at briefing time. Descending on the upstream side and drifting across the reef produces the best encounter opportunities.
- **Fish ID enthusiasts will be busy**: Namena is one of the best sites in Fiji for species counting. Bring a dive slate if you're keeping a species list — the diversity easily exceeds 80 fish species per dive.
- **Respect the reserve fees**: The fees go directly to the Namena village community that enforces the no-take zone. The exceptional fish life you encounter is a direct result of that enforcement.

## Safety Considerations

Namena Marine Reserve lies in open water and boat travel can be rough in trade wind conditions. Confirm sea state before committing to the journey — both the 2-hour passage and the return in deteriorating afternoon conditions can be challenging. The dive site itself is benign; current is manageable and depth is limited. Visibility is typically excellent, reducing navigation hazards. Surface an SMB before ascending — small boats may be mooring nearby.

## Photography

Zoo Namena is a wide-angle reef photographer's delight. The combination of shark schools, dense fish aggregations, and healthy coral provides layered subjects at every depth. Shoot wide from mid-water to capture the shark patrol with the reef below and the fish schools above, building a three-dimensional composition. For close-focus wide angle, approach the large groupers — they are remarkably tame and will hold position for a lens placed 30cm from their face. Macro photographers find nudibranchs, small cephalopods, and shrimp species in the rubble zones between coral heads.
