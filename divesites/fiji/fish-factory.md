# Fish Factory

A current-driven site on Taveuni's Rainbow Reef where schooling fish mass in such density that the site earns its industrial name.

## Overview

Fish Factory is a reef dive on the Rainbow Reef system in the Somosomo Strait, named for the volume of fish life produced by tidal current upwelling across this section of the reef. The site consists of coral bommies on sand, reaching depths of 10–20 meters, and is suitable for both beginners and advanced divers. Schools of barracuda, Spanish mackerel, and bigeye trevally move across the site in intersecting formations driven by the current. The site is not a wall dive — it is structured as bommies on a sandy bottom with moderate relief, allowing divers of various experience levels to position themselves at comfortable depths while observing the schooling action. Moray eels occupy crevices throughout the reef structure, and nudibranchs can be found in quieter sections.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Somosomo Strait, Taveuni, Fiji
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters (65–100 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong tidal current; schooling fish action is current-dependent
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Schooling fish are the defining feature: barracuda, Spanish mackerel, and bigeye trevally appear in the formations that characterize current-rich Taveuni diving. Grey reef sharks patrol the outer edges of the bommie field. Moray eels occupy reef crevices at multiple depth levels. The site also rewards slower exploration for nudibranchs and other reef invertebrates on the bommie faces. The shallow profile means the full range of fish activity is accessible to divers at 8–15 meters without descending to the maximum depth.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deepest section of the bommie field (15–20 meters) and position yourself at a current-exposed bommie edge where fish activity concentrates. Drift or fin from bommie to bommie as conditions allow, ascending progressively through shallower zones. The most active schooling typically occurs at mid-depth (8–15 meters) where current accelerates over the reef structure. Safety stop at 5 meters on the shallowest bommie tops. Total dive times of 50–65 minutes are typical.

## Entry and Exit

Accessed by boat from Taveuni operators as part of a Somosomo Strait multi-dive day. Often paired with Great White Wall or Rainbow Reef main areas. Current management on entry — brief confirmation of drift direction before descent — is standard practice. Deploy an SMB before surfacing.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Current intensity drives the experience**: Fish Factory on a slack tide is a pleasant bommie dive. With current running, the schooling action intensifies significantly. Confirm tidal timing with your operator.
- **Suitable for snorkelers**: The shallow bommies at 3–8 meters make this site accessible to snorkelers wanting to observe the fish schools from the surface.
- **Pair with a macro dive**: Fish Factory burns through gas in current. Following it with Blue Ribbon Eel Reef provides a calmer, lower-intensity second dive on the same day.

## Safety Considerations

Current in the Somosomo Strait can push divers away from the bommies and the boat pickup point. Always carry and deploy an SMB before ascending. Maintain buddy contact in reduced visibility that can occur when fish schools are dense.
