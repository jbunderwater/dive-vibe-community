# E6

Named after Ektachrome slide film for a reason — a seamount so saturated with soft coral color it has inspired more underwater photographs than almost any site in Fiji.

## Overview

E6 is a seamount in Bligh Water's Vatu-i-ra channel, rising sheer-sided from over 900 meters depth to within less than a meter of the surface at the tip. The site sits in the narrowest section of the channel between Viti Levu and Vanua Levu, intercepting the nutrient-rich flows that make Fiji the "Soft Coral Capital of the World." The site takes its name from the Ektachrome E6 slide film process — early dive photographers coined the name because the vivid saturation of the soft corals resembled a perfectly exposed E6 transparency. The exposed sides of the seamount face the strongest currents and attract pelagic fish, while the protected lee side carries delicate soft corals and gorgonian fans. E6 is primarily accessed by liveaboard from the Bligh Water circuit, not from Beqa Lagoon.

**Location note:** The coordinates recorded in this database (-17.7747, 177.1575) do not match the known position of E6 in the Vatu-i-ra channel (approximately 17.4°S, 178.5°E). This entry's coordinates should be verified against navigation charts or liveaboard operator GPS logs before relying on them for trip planning.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Vatu-i-ra channel, Bligh Water, Fiji (between Viti Levu and Vanua Levu)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (liveaboard; also accessible from Volivoli Resort, Ra)
- **Site Type**: Seamount / pinnacle
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: ~40 meters (recreational; pinnacle base at 900m+)
- **Typical Depth Range**: 15–40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–45 meters (50–150 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: April–October (best visibility)

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at E6 include schooling barracuda (often several hundred), bigeye trevally, dogtooth tuna, anthias in dense clouds over the coral surface, eagle rays, hammerhead sharks, and leaf scorpionfish. The exposed current-facing sides of the pinnacle are where pelagic fish concentrate; the protected lee side carries delicate soft corals and gorgonians. Fusiliers and anthias are present in large numbers.

Note: Some species commonly attributed to Fiji pinnacle sites (silvertip sharks, moray eels, sea turtles, moorish idols) are part of the broader Bligh Water ecosystem and may be encountered, but only the species listed above have been confirmed at E6 specifically in sources reviewed.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the base of the pinnacle at 25–28 meters and begin a clockwise circuit, observing the coral coverage and crevice life. Ascend the pinnacle sides, noting the change in soft coral species with depth — deeper faces carry more black coral and sea fans, while the upper sections are dominated by soft corals in the shallower, brighter water. Spend time hovering just above the pinnacle peak at 3–5 meters to observe the pelagic action above before completing a safety stop. Current direction determines which face of the pinnacle to start on — your guide will position you appropriately at the surface before descent.

## Entry and Exit

Accessed by liveaboard from the Bligh Water circuit, or by day boat from Volivoli Beach Resort on the Ra coast of Viti Levu. The resort is the closest land-based operator to the E6 seamount. Entry by giant stride; surface current at the mooring is usually manageable. Ascend along the pinnacle structure and surface near the mooring line. In strong current, deploy an SMB and drift to the surface rather than fighting the current back to the line.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Current is your friend here**: E6 is significantly better with current running. If conditions are calm and slack, the soft corals retract and the pelagic action disappears. Check with your operator on tidal timing.
- **Base from Volivoli Resort or a liveaboard**: E6 is in Bligh Water, not Beqa Lagoon. Reach it from Volivoli Resort on the Ra coast (day trip), or include it on a Fiji liveaboard circuit. It is not combined with Shark Reef Marine Reserve (different area of Fiji).
- **Watch the blue**: Some of the best sightings at E6 — silvertips, tuna, large barracuda schools — happen in open water above and around the pinnacle, not on the structure itself. Don't keep your eyes locked on the coral.
- **Ascent management**: Pinnacle dives can produce a strong urge to ascend quickly when the action is above you. Control your ascent rate and complete a full stop at 5 meters regardless of what is happening overhead.
- **Bring a reef hook**: If silvertips are present and you want to hold position in the current without damaging the coral, a reef hook deployed on bare rock is the right tool.

## Safety Considerations

Strong currents can develop rapidly around isolated pinnacles in the Vatu-i-ra channel as tidal conditions change. Agree on an abort depth and gas turn-around before the dive. Do not attempt to touch or corner any shark. Deploy an SMB at the safety stop on every ascent — surface current in the channel can push you away from the mooring quickly.

## Photography

E6 is a showcase wide-angle site. The pinnacle's colorful flanks pair beautifully with silhouetted divers and fish schools in the water column above. Shoot upward from the base of the pinnacle to capture the structure rising toward the surface with soft coral detail in the foreground. For close-focus wide-angle shots, the orange and pink Dendronephthya clusters at 15–20 meters are dense enough to fill the frame. Barracuda and trevally schools above the pinnacle reward patient wide-angle waiting — let them circle into your frame rather than chasing them.
