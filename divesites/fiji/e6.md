# E6

Named after Ektachrome slide film for a reason — a pinnacle so saturated with soft coral color it looks like a perfectly exposed underwater photograph.

## Overview

E6 is a pinnacle site in Beqa Lagoon named by early dive photographers who compared its colors to the vivid saturation of Ektachrome E6 slide film. The pinnacle rises from 28 meters to within a few meters of the surface, and its flanks are encrusted with dense Dendronephthya soft corals in yellows, oranges, and pinks, interspersed with large sea fans and black coral trees. Strong currents regularly sweep the site, bringing nutrient flow that sustains the coral growth and draws pelagic species into the blue water above the pinnacle. E6 is commonly dived as part of a Beqa Lagoon package alongside Shark Reef Marine Reserve — the two sites complement each other, with E6 delivering pure reef spectacle where Shark Reef delivers shark drama.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Beqa Lagoon, Pacific Harbour, Viti Levu, Fiji
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters (50–100 feet)
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The pinnacle's soft coral coverage attracts an attendant cloud of anthias that swarms in permanent residence around the upper structure. Schooling barracuda — often several hundred strong — frequently appear in the blue water column above the pinnacle, spiraling slowly in the current. Bigeye trevally and dogtooth tuna patrol the outer edges. On the pinnacle itself, large moray eels occupy crevices, sea turtles feed on sponges, and lionfish hunt in the shadowed overhangs. Schools of moorish idols and surgeonfish flow across the coral surface. Silvertip sharks are the apex predators here when current is running — they appear from the blue, make a circuit of the pinnacle, and disappear again.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the base of the pinnacle at 25–28 meters and begin a clockwise circuit, observing the coral coverage and crevice life. Ascend the pinnacle sides, noting the change in soft coral species with depth — deeper faces carry more black coral and sea fans, while the upper sections are dominated by soft corals in the shallower, brighter water. Spend time hovering just above the pinnacle peak at 3–5 meters to observe the pelagic action above before completing a safety stop. Current direction determines which face of the pinnacle to start on — your guide will position you appropriately at the surface before descent.

## Entry and Exit

Accessed by dive boat from Pacific Harbour operators. Mooring buoys at the pinnacle allow the boat to hold station in current. Entry by giant stride; surface current at the mooring is usually manageable. Ascend along the pinnacle structure and surface near the mooring line. In strong current, deploy an SMB and drift to the surface rather than fighting the current back to the line.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Current is your friend here**: E6 is significantly better with current running. If conditions are calm and slack, the soft corals retract and the pelagic action disappears. Check with your operator on tidal timing.
- **Combine with Shark Reef**: Most Pacific Harbour operators run E6 and Shark Reef Marine Reserve in the same two-dive package. If independently arranging, confirm the combination is offered.
- **Watch the blue**: Some of the best sightings at E6 — silvertips, tuna, large barracuda schools — happen in open water above and around the pinnacle, not on the structure itself. Don't keep your eyes locked on the coral.
- **Ascent management**: Pinnacle dives can produce a strong urge to ascend quickly when the action is above you. Control your ascent rate and complete a full stop at 5 meters regardless of what is happening overhead.
- **Bring a reef hook**: If silvertips are present and you want to hold position in the current without damaging the coral, a reef hook deployed on bare rock is the right tool.

## Safety Considerations

Strong currents can develop rapidly around isolated pinnacles as tidal conditions change. Agree on an abort depth and gas turn-around before the dive. Silvertip sharks at E6 are generally relaxed but can become more assertive if chum or fish scent is in the water from a previous Shark Reef dive — be aware of your dive sequence. Do not attempt to touch or corner any shark. Deploy an SMB at the safety stop on every ascent — boat traffic in Beqa Lagoon is moderate and surface current can push you away from the mooring.

## Photography

E6 is a showcase wide-angle site. The pinnacle's colorful flanks pair beautifully with silhouetted divers and fish schools in the water column above. Shoot upward from the base of the pinnacle to capture the structure rising toward the surface with soft coral detail in the foreground. For close-focus wide-angle shots, the orange and pink Dendronephthya clusters at 15–20 meters are dense enough to fill the frame. Barracuda and trevally schools above the pinnacle reward patient wide-angle waiting — let them circle into your frame rather than chasing them.
