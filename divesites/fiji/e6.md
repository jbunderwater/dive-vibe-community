# E6

A sheer-sided seamount in Bligh Water's Vatu-i-Ra channel — the pinnacle that made Fiji famous for pelagic diving.

## Overview

E6 is a seamount rising from depths exceeding 900 meters in the Vatu-i-Ra channel, the narrowest passage between Viti Levu and Vanua Levu. The pinnacle's summit breaks the surface at less than one meter depth, while its base descends far beyond sport diving limits. Named by early dive photographers who used prodigious quantities of Ektachrome E6 slide film here when the site was first discovered, E6 is reached by liveaboard — it sits in open water well beyond day-trip range from shore. The channel position means the pinnacle intercepts the full nutrient flow channeled between Fiji's two largest islands. Pelagics concentrate on the current-exposed flanks, while the lee side shelters delicate soft corals and gorgonian fans. E6 is not in Beqa Lagoon; it is in the Bligh Water region and is primarily accessed on NAI'A and other Fiji liveaboard circuits.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Vatu-i-Ra Channel, Bligh Water, between Viti Levu and Vanua Levu, Fiji
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (liveaboard)
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters (recreational limit; pinnacle base far deeper)
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–40 meters (65–130 feet)
- **Current**: Strong channel current; varies with tide
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The current-swept flanks of the pinnacle concentrate pelagic species throughout the dive. Schooling barracuda are a reliable presence, often forming tight spiraling columns above the pinnacle peak. Bigeye trevally and rainbow runners orbit the structure continuously. Occasional hammerhead sharks appear from the blue on the current-exposed sides. Eagle rays cruise past the pinnacle face. The protected lee side supports delicate Chironephthya soft corals and gorgonian fans draped over the mid-depth structure. Anthias, fusiliers, and leaf scorpionfish are documented on the pinnacle itself. Night diving reveals lobsters, arrowhead crabs, and the bioluminescent flashes of flashlight fish in the crevices.

## Dive Profile

Liveaboard vessels moor to the pinnacle's lee side. Descend along the mooring line and begin exploration of the pinnacle structure. The soft coral and fan growth is most prolific on the protected side; the pelagic action is concentrated on the current-exposed flanks. Circuit the pinnacle, using the structure as a current break when needed. Ascend along the pinnacle face and complete a safety stop near the summit, where the dramatic topography — wall-to-wall coral just below the surface — makes the stop rewarding. Total dive time on a single circuit is typically 45–60 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

E6 is exclusively a liveaboard site. NAI'A and other Fiji liveaboard operators include it as a standard stop on Bligh Water itineraries. The NAI'A moors on the lee side, providing a sheltered entry point regardless of surface current. Entry by back roll or giant stride from the dive platform. Ascend the mooring line or pinnacle face; in strong current, deploy an SMB and drift to the surface rather than fighting the flow back to the line.

## Tips and Recommendations

- **Plan a liveaboard specifically**: E6 cannot be reached on a day trip. Fiji liveaboard operators offer 7–14 day circuits including E6 alongside Namena, Gau, and other Bligh Water sites.
- **Current direction determines which face**: The pelagic action is always on the current side. Ask the dive guide which face is exposed to flow before descending, and start there when your gas is freshest.
- **Watch the blue water column**: The big sightings at E6 — hammerheads, eagle rays, large tuna — approach from open water above and around the pinnacle, not from the structure itself. Maintain peripheral vision into the blue throughout the dive.
- **Night dive is exceptional**: The flashlight fish at E6 are a rare experience. If the liveaboard schedules a night dive here, prioritize it.
- **Ascent management**: Excitement about pelagic sightings above can create an unconscious urge to ascend too quickly. Set a hard turn depth and monitor your computer continuously.

## Safety Considerations

Strong channel currents in Vatu-i-Ra can be unpredictable and can accelerate rapidly as tidal conditions change. Agree on a gas turn-around pressure and a depth limit before entry. Liveaboard dive guides know the site's current behavior; follow their briefing on descent direction and abort signals. Deploy an SMB before every ascent — the open-water location means surface current can carry divers away from the boat quickly in either direction. The remote Bligh Water location means the nearest comprehensive medical facility is in Suva — dive conservatively.

## Photography

E6 is a wide-angle site for pinnacle, pelagic, and coral subjects across a single dive. From the base, shoot upward along the pinnacle face toward the surface — the structure framing the blue water above is visually powerful. For pelagic action, position yourself at depth on the current-exposed side and shoot horizontally into the blue as barracuda or trevally schools pass. The Chironephthya soft corals on the lee face are structurally different from the common Dendronephthya and reward close-focus wide-angle shooting. Night photography here requires concentration — the flashlight fish move continuously and require fast shutter speeds.
