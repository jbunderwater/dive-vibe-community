# Santa Iria

A no-fishing protected bay on São Miguel's north coast — volcanic lava reef with prolific fish life.

## Overview

Santa Iria is a bay on the north coast of São Miguel near Rabo de Peixe, designated as a no-fishing area. A relatively sheltered lagoon features a narrow inlet from the sea, with one side comprising a lava flow and the other reinforced with large boulders. The underwater landscape features volcanic lava reef and rocky seafloor typical of the Azores. The no-fishing protection means fish life is prolific compared to unprotected areas.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North coast, São Miguel, Azores
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–15 meters (variable, north coast)
- **Current**: Light inside bay
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Colourful fish in prolific numbers on the rocky lava sides. Sea urchins and starfish. The no-fishing zone supports healthier fish populations than surrounding areas.

## Tips and Recommendations

- No-fishing protected area — healthier fish populations
- Volcanic lava reef landscape typical of the Azores
- North coast visibility can be limited — check conditions
- Also popular as a snorkelling destination
