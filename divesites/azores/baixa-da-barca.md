# Baixa da Barca

One of three reefs in the Pico–Faial Channel — massive schools of blue jack mackerel draw devil rays and ocean sunfish.

## Overview

Baixa da Barca is one of three reefs in the Pico–Faial Channel, located 2 nautical miles north of the Madalena Islets. The shallowest peak sits at 20 metres, with corridors and platforms at 30–50 metres and a maximum depth of 55 metres. The site is known for massive schools of blue jack mackerel that attract devil rays, ocean sunfish, and pelagic predators. Reserved for experienced divers due to depth and currents.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Pico–Faial Channel, Azores
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 55 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–40 meters
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Yellowmouth barracudas, Almaco jacks, Atlantic bonitos, dusky groupers, forkbeards, barred hogfish, island groupers, and red scorpionfish. The highlight is massive schools of blue jack mackerel attracting devil rays and ocean sunfish.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced Open Water minimum due to depth and currents
- The blue jack mackerel schools are the main attraction — they draw pelagics
- Devil rays and ocean sunfish are the highlight encounters
- 20 min by boat from Porto da Madalena
