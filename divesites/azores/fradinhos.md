# Fradinhos

Four uninhabited islets 5 nautical miles southeast of Terceira — the submerged volcanic cone descends to 60 metres and hosts black coral, dusky groupers, and open-water pelagics.

## Overview

Fradinhos are four small uninhabited islets located 5 nautical miles southeast of Terceira Island, comprising the exposed summit of a submerged volcanic cone. The dive profile begins at 25 metres average depth, with the bottom progressively descending to 60 metres. The site is accessible only by boat — approximately 20 minutes from Angra do Heroísmo Harbour or 35 minutes from Praia da Vitória Harbour.

The site is known both for resident demersal species and for open-water pelagic encounters. From 30 metres, black coral (*Antipathella wollastoni*) colonises the flanks alongside imposing dusky groupers and barred hogfish. The exposed offshore position and strong currents make this an advanced dive reserved for experienced divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: 5 NM southeast of Terceira Island, Azores
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (20 min from Angra do Heroísmo)
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/underwater volcanic cone
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 60 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Azores chromis (*Chromis limbata*), large blacktail combers (*Serranus atricauda*), parrotfish (*Sparisoma cretense*), black coral (*Antipathella wollastoni*) from 30 metres down, dusky groupers (*Epinephelus marginatus*), barred hogfish (*Bodianus scrofa*), and red scorpionfish (*Scorpaena scrofa*). Pelagic species include schools of Almaco jacks (*Seriola rivoliana*), bluefish (*Pomatomus saltator*), yellowmouth barracudas (*Sphyraena viridensis*), and devil rays (*Mobula tarapacana*).

## Tips and Recommendations

- 5 miles offshore from Terceira — exposed Atlantic conditions for experienced divers
- Black coral from 30 m is a notable feature
- Pelagic encounters likely in the water column; strong currents require good buoyancy control
- Combine with Terceira's accessible bay sites for a mixed-depth day
