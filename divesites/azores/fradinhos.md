# Fradinhos

Small islets 5 miles south of Terceira — groupers, hogfish, and pelagic schools in open Atlantic water.

## Overview

Fradinhos are small islets located approximately 5 nautical miles south of Terceira island. The site is known for dusky groupers, barred hogfish, and pelagic schools in open Atlantic conditions. The exposed location and depth make this an advanced dive site. Terceira's diving character is defined by its archaeological sites (Angra do Heroísmo bay), cave systems, and proximity to the Dom João de Castro Bank seamount.

## Site Information

- **Location**: 5 NM south of Terceira Island, Azores
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Moderate to strong
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Dusky groupers, barred hogfish, and pelagic schools. The exposed offshore location attracts open-water species.

## Tips and Recommendations

- 5 miles offshore from Terceira — boat dive only
- Exposed Atlantic conditions — for experienced divers
- Good grouper and pelagic encounters
- Combine with Terceira's archaeological sites at Angra do Heroísmo
