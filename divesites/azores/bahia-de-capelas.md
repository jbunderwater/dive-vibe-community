# Bahia de Capelas

A north coast volcanic reef dive at Capelas on São Miguel.

## Overview

Bahia de Capelas is a bay on the north coast of São Miguel near the village of Capelas. The north coast features volcanic rock formations typical of the Azores, with impressive underwater volcanic landscapes rather than coral reefs. The site reaches approximately 15 metres depth and is accessible from shore.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Capelas, north coast, São Miguel, Azores
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–15 meters (variable)
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Regional Azorean marine life including groupers, wrasse, parrotfish, octopus, and moray eels on the volcanic reef.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Volcanic rock formations rather than coral reef
- Shore accessible
- North coast — check sea conditions before diving
