# Caneiro dos Meros

The only voluntary marine reserve in the Azores — the highest concentration of dusky groupers anywhere in the archipelago.

## Overview

Caneiro dos Meros ('Dusky Grouper Passageway') on Corvo Island is the only voluntary marine reserve in the Azores, created by agreement between fishermen and divers. The site has the highest concentration of dusky groupers (Epinephelus marginatus) of any site in the Azores — divers regularly encounter more than a dozen large, curious groupers simultaneously. Lava ravines with sandy patches at 18–25 metres lead to a final wall dropping to a 40–45 metre sandy seafloor. Located just 150 metres from the coast and 3 minutes by boat from Vila do Corvo quay.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Corvo Island, Azores
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 45 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Weak
- **Best Time**: June to September

## Marine Life

Exceptional concentration of dusky groupers — the defining feature. More than a dozen large, curious groupers are regularly encountered simultaneously. Barred hogfish and rare demersal and pelagic species thrive due to the fishing ban.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The highest concentration of dusky groupers in the Azores
- Voluntary marine reserve — created by agreement between fishermen and divers
- Groupers are large, curious, and approach divers
- Only 3 min by boat from Vila do Corvo — but getting to Corvo is the challenge
- Advanced Open Water required due to depth despite weak currents
