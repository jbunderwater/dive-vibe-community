# Gruta dos Enxaréus

Narrow cave corridors where groups of 6+ large stingrays regularly shelter — a dramatic Flores Island cave system.

## Overview

Gruta dos Enxaréus on Flores Island is a dramatic cave system at the base of sea cliffs, featuring two 30-metre-long corridors barely over 1 metre wide in places. Common stingrays regularly use the cave, and divers frequently encounter groups of 6 or more large stingrays inside the narrow passages. Located just 150 metres from the coast and 10 minutes by boat from Santa Cruz quay, the site requires Advanced Open Water certification due to the cave environment and depth.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Flores Island, Azores
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters
- **Current**: Weak
- **Best Time**: June to September

## Marine Life

Common stingrays in groups of 6+ inside the cave corridors — the defining feature. Grey triggerfish, white trevallies, and Almaco jacks in the bay outside.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Groups of 6+ large stingrays regularly shelter inside the narrow corridors
- Two 30m-long corridors barely over 1m wide — a dramatic experience
- Advanced certification required for the cave environment
- 10 min by boat from Santa Cruz, Flores
- Flores is one of the most remote Azores islands — plan logistics carefully
