## The Boardroom

The Boardroom is a muck dive site in the Alor Archipelago, East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia — a sheltered, low-current slope known as Tangaban in local records, where the combination of black volcanic substrate and scattered rubble creates habitat for a range of cryptic reef critters.

## Overview

The Boardroom (Tangaban) is documented in the Starfish.ch Alor dive guide as a muck dive on a slope with low coral cover. It sits in a sheltered area of the archipelago where the absence of significant current allows slow, methodical muck diving. The site features a seabed of volcanic substrate, rubble patches, and limited hard coral growth. The name "The Boardroom" is used by liveaboard operators in the area; the site appears in the Starfish.ch dive guide under the alternative name Tangaban (site 16). Note: a separate source mentions a site also called "Boardroom" on the northern side of Pura Island with a wall and caves — this may be a different site sharing the name or a naming inconsistency between operators.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Alor Archipelago, East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia (also known as Tangaban)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Muck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 metres
- **Current**: Negligible; sheltered conditions
- **Best Time**: April to November

## Marine Life

The Starfish.ch dive guide confirms the following at Tangaban/The Boardroom: pegasus sea moths (*Eurypegasus draconis*), robust ghost pipefish, and a large nudibranch (*Kentrodoris rubescens*). Pegasus sea moths are unusually rare in most of Indonesia and are a notable find. The regional muck fauna of Alor's bay sites typically includes various octopus species, frogfish, and nudibranchs, but additional species at this specific site have not been confirmed by independent sources beyond the above.

## Dive Profile

The site is dived slowly across the slope. Enter over the shallows and work methodically down and across the rubble and sand habitat. Depth rarely exceeds 14–16 metres during the productive portions of the dive. No significant current requires active management; focus is entirely on observation. Long bottom times are possible given the depth and calm conditions.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard or day-boat tender. Entry into calm, shallow water. The tender anchors nearby. Deploy SMB before ascending.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Ask your guide specifically about pegasus sea moths — they are an unusual find at this site and are easily overlooked as they rest flat on the bottom
- Ghost pipefish tend to hover near algae and rubble; scan slowly through these areas
- Move very slowly — the sparse substrate means that more ground covered does not equate to more animals found; patience pays here
- A torch illuminates the underside of rubble where additional critters shelter

## Safety Considerations

The calm conditions at The Boardroom are deceptive — venomous benthic species including stonefish and scorpionfish may be present in the substrate. Maintain buoyancy to avoid fin contact with the bottom. Never touch unfamiliar animals or shells.

## Photography

Pegasus sea moths are the priority macro subject here. They are small, flat animals that blend into the bottom and require a macro lens (60–100 mm equivalent) and patience to photograph well. Ghost pipefish hovering near algae suit close-focus wide-angle or macro lenses depending on the surrounding context.
