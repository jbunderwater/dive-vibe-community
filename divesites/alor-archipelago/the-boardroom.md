## The Boardroom

The Boardroom (Tangaban) is a muck dive site renowned for exceptional critter diversity and rare finds. Located on a low-coral-coverage slope with black sand, this site exemplifies Kalabahi Bay's world-class macro diving character.

## Overview

The Boardroom is a muck dive featuring a gently sloping black sand bottom with sparse coral coverage and remarkable critter density. Maximum depths reach 16 meters, making it accessible and safe for dedicated macro exploration. The site is known for consistent discoveries of unusual species and rare nudibranchs. Currents are typically light and favorable for stationary hunting. This is a specialty macro site rewarding slow, methodical divers who take time to examine small substrate features and crevices.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kalabahi Bay, Alor Archipelago
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Muck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-25 meters
- **Current**: Light to mild; usually favorable
- **Water Temperature**: Warm (26-30°C)
- **Best Time**: Year-round diveable; May-September most consistent
- **Specialty**: Macro, critter, nudibranch diving

## Marine Life

The Boardroom hosts notable finds including rare and unusual nudibranchs (e.g., large Kentrodoris rubescens specimens), pegasus sea moths, robust ghostpipefishes, delicate ghostpipefishes, octopuses, frogfish, seahorses, shrimp species, crabs, and cephalopods. The substrate-focused environment provides continuous opportunities for small-creature discovery.

## Dive Profile

Plan your dive with macro and critter-seeking intention. Descend to the black sand slope and begin a systematic search pattern. Spend time examining small substrate areas, moving slowly and deliberately. Work through the slope between 6-16 meters, scanning for well-camouflaged species and unusual formations. Return to shallow water for final minutes and safety stop. Patience and careful observation yield continuous discoveries.

## Entry and Exit

Access by dive boat only. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Descent to the sand slope. Navigate methodically along the slope, investigating substrate features. Maintain position control to observe small creatures at work. Exit via boat crew pickup with SMB deployment at safety stop.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Use macro lens exclusively at this site
- Bring a second light/torch to illuminate crevices
- Move slowly and pause frequently to scan the sand
- Research nudibranch and critter identification before diving
- Book with Alor operators experienced in muck diving
- This site is ideal for underwater naturalists and macro photographers
- Expect to find multiple new-to-you species on a single dive

## Safety Considerations

The Boardroom's shallow slopes and light currents make it one of Alor's safest muck sites. However, the slow-paced dive can lead to nitrogen buildup—monitor decompression time carefully as you work deeper and spend time at depth. Watch for sea urchins hidden in sand—exercise care with hand placement. Visibility can vary daily—ascend if conditions worsen unexpectedly. Always use SMB and dive with a buddy who shares your macro focus. This is an excellent site for building muck diving skills and specialty knowledge.

## Photography

Exceptional macro photography with continuous critter subjects. Rare nudibranchs provide exotic color and pattern studies. Ghostpipefish portraits, pegasus sea moth detail shots, and crustacean close-ups reward macro technique. Unusual species and color combinations make every dive unique. Dark sand background requires strong strobes and careful technique. Best during morning dives; macro composition techniques emphasize subject detail, behavior, and environmental context.
