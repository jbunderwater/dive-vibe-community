## Shark Close

Shark Close is a wall dive site renowned for hammerhead shark encounters in the Pantar Strait region of Alor. The site's name reflects the proximity to and reliability of shark sightings, particularly schools of scalloped hammerheads during strong-current periods.

## Overview

Shark Close is a vertical wall dive positioned to intercept the strong currents flowing through the Pantar Strait. The site attracts schools of scalloped hammerheads that feed in current-driven nutrient flows. Maximum depths reach 32 meters, with the most dynamic hammerhead action typically occurring at 20-30 meters. Strong, unpredictable currents define the dive experience. This is a current diving site requiring significant experience and excellent buoyancy control, reserved for advanced divers seeking guaranteed pelagic encounters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Pantar Strait, Alor Archipelago
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 32 meters
- **Recommended Experience**: 100+ dives, strong current diving capability required
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Current**: Strong and unpredictable; essential for hammerhead hunting behavior
- **Water Temperature**: Variable; cold upwelling water possible
- **Best Time**: May-September, though hammerheads most active early morning in cooler months
- **Marine Life Focus**: Pelagic sharks, schools of jacks and barracuda

## Marine Life

Schools of scalloped hammerheads are the signature attraction—early morning dives offer best sighting chances. Additional pelagic species include silvertip sharks, grey reef sharks, great barracudas, and trevally schools. Colorful wall corals support smaller reef fish. The current draws large pelagics seeking food, making this a wide-angle photographer's paradise.

## Dive Profile

Coordinate current direction and entry strategy with the dive master. Descend rapidly to the wall and allow current to carry you along the structure. Position yourself at 20-30 meters where hammerhead schools typically hunt. Scan the blue water column for incoming schools. Stay alert for rapid current changes and maintain excellent buddy contact. Plan a safety stop at 5 meters with SMB deployed.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. Drift dive technique—allow current to do the work. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Rapid descent to the wall to position in the current flow. Constant buddy checks throughout. Exit via SMB at planned safety stop depth.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Early morning dives yield most hammerhead sightings
- Request a pre-dive briefing on current direction and shark behavior patterns
- Dive with experienced Alor operators familiar with seasonal hammerhead movements
- Bring a wide-angle lens and be ready for fast-moving schools
- Consider this site a specialty pelagic encounter dive requiring full focus
- Strong currents are the point of this dive—they bring the sharks

## Safety Considerations

Shark Close demands respect for both sharks and currents. Strong currents are guaranteed and can be unpredictable. You must be thoroughly comfortable with drift diving and current diving techniques. Hammerheads are not aggressive toward divers but demand respect—maintain distance and calm demeanor. Nitrogen narcosis at depth (30+ meters) requires conservative bottom time planning. Excellent buoyancy control and buddy awareness essential. Always use SMB and maintain constant buddy contact. Minimum 100 logged dives with proven current diving experience required. Cold water exposure requires adequate thermal protection.

## Photography

Dramatic hammerhead school shots with multiple heads in frame. Silhouette compositions capturing schools against blue water. Pelagic action subjects (large barracudas, trevally, sharks) suit wide-angle technique. Composition techniques should emphasize the dramatic hammerhead head shape and school coordination. Best light during morning dives; shooting position should anticipate school movement along the wall.
