## Mike's Delight

Mike's Delight is a wall dive site in the Alor Archipelago, East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia — a rugged sloping reef on the east coast of Pura Island, dived as a slow drift to approximately 25–30 metres along boulders and coral structures, with potential for dolphin pods and pelagic encounters in the open water.

## Overview

Mike's Delight is located on the east coast of Pura Island, where the reef descends as a rugged slope with large boulders rather than a sheer wall. The site is typically dived as a slow drift, allowing divers to cover significant ground across the varied bottom terrain. Current conditions can range from mild to extreme, classifying this as an advanced dive. Multiple sources confirm the colourful and saturated appearance of the coral and reef fish communities here. One report noted vivid colouration as the site's defining feature. Dolphin pods have been sighted in the open water adjacent to the site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East coast of Pura Island, Alor Archipelago, East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 metres
- **Current**: Mild to extreme; variable
- **Best Time**: April to November

## Marine Life

Multiple sources confirm the following species at Mike's Delight: sea snakes (white and black coloured), lobsters, lionfish, parrotfish, sweetlips, angelfish, damselfish, Napoleon wrasse, jacks, butterflyfish, red-toothed triggerfish, and anthias. Dolphin pods have been reported in the waters adjacent to this site. The rock and boulder terrain hosts moray eels in the crevices.

## Dive Profile

Deploy into the current up-slope and drift slowly along the structure. The productive depth range is 8–25 metres. Cover the boulder zone before ascending through the more coral-rich shallower sections. Monitor drift speed — in stronger current the site moves past quickly; use the sheltered lee sides of boulders to slow progress. Signal the tender with your SMB well before the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access by liveaboard or day-boat tender. Drop in at the up-current end and drift to an agreed pick-up point. SMB deployment is essential before surfacing.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Move slowly across the boulder zone — the varied terrain concentrates critters in unexpected places
- Look for sea snakes hunting across the rubble; they are active and commonly encountered here
- Check the open water column regularly — dolphin pods can appear at any point during the drift
- Strong current days make the drift faster; discuss pace with your guide before entering

## Safety Considerations

Current conditions at Mike's Delight can become extreme without warning; stay close to the reef structure for shelter if conditions deteriorate. All divers must carry SMBs. Moray eels occupy most of the crevices in the boulder zone — maintain distance and do not reach blindly into gaps.

## Photography

The colourful reef fish communities and varied boulder terrain suit wide-angle photography. Sea snakes make striking and unusual subjects. For the smaller reef inhabitants, a medium macro (60 mm equivalent) covers lionfish and crustaceans in the rockwork.
