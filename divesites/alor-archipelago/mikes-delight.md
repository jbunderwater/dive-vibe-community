## Mike's Delight

Mike's Delight is a rugged slope and wall dive site featuring large boulder formations and dramatic topography. Known for excellent opportunities to encounter large pelagic species in extreme current environments, this site delivers world-class wide-angle diving.

## Overview

Mike's Delight features a rugged slope with large boulders and vertical walls subject to extreme currents. The site's appeal lies in the possibility of encountering substantial pelagic animals—the strong currents create feeding opportunities that attract sharks, large jacks, trevally, and other open-water hunters. Maximum depths reach 28 meters. This is a current diving site where conditions must be assessed carefully. Most dives occur during strong-current periods when pelagic activity peaks, making this an advanced-only site for divers comfortable with high-energy water conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Alor Archipelago
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Recommended Experience**: 100+ dives, strong current experience essential
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 meters
- **Current**: Extreme and variable; the site shines when currents are strongest
- **Water Temperature**: Variable; cold upwelling water possible
- **Best Time**: May-September; dive when currents are strong (May-June particularly active)

## Marine Life

The extreme currents attract large pelagic predators: sharks, jacks, trevally, and barracudas dominate sightings. Large groupers and snappers occupy the boulder structures. Colorful soft corals cover the slope and boulders. The abundance of large animals reflects the nutrient-rich environment created by strong current flow.

## Dive Profile

Request a current assessment before entry. Descend rapidly along the slope/wall to position in the current flow. Allow the current to drift you across the boulder formations while scanning the blue water for incoming pelagics. Explore the boulders and slope structures between 15-28 meters. Stay alert for rapid current changes and upwelling. Plan for a shorter, high-energy dive with a safety stop at 5 meters and SMB deployment.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. Drift dive technique is standard. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Rapid descent to the slope/wall. Maintain buddy contact constantly. Exit via SMB at planned safety stop depth.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Only dive Mike's Delight when you're feeling strong and confident in current
- Request current assessment and briefing before entering water
- Expect substantial pelagic encounters—bring wide-angle lens
- Dive with experienced Alor operators who understand seasonal patterns
- Consider this a specialty pelagic encounter site requiring high focus
- Best dives occur during strongest current periods

## Safety Considerations

Mike's Delight is not for casual divers. Extreme currents are the defining feature and prerequisite for good diving. You must be thoroughly comfortable with drift diving, strong current work, and abandoning the reef if necessary. Nitrogen narcosis affects judgment at depth—plan conservative bottom times. Excellent buoyancy control is non-negotiable. Cold water and upwelling require thermal protection. Always use SMB and maintain constant buddy contact. Minimum 100 logged dives with proven strong current experience absolutely essential. This is advanced+ diving.
