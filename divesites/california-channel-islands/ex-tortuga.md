## Ex-Tortuga

Ex-Tortuga is the wreck of a U.S. Navy dock landing ship that grounded on San Miguel Island in 1987.

## Overview

The Ex-Tortuga (ex-USS Tortuga, LSD-26) was a 457-foot Casa Grande-class dock landing ship that ran aground at Cardwell Point on the southeast corner of San Miguel Island on December 15, 1987, after breaking her tow during a storm. From 1983 until the grounding, the vessel had been used as a target ship for Tomahawk missile tests based at Port Hueneme. After the grounding, the ship was scrapped in place over a period of more than eight months; the hull disappeared below the surface on August 21, 1988. Scattered remains and debris from this large naval vessel rest on the reef at the southeast corner of San Miguel Island — the most remote and exposed of the Channel Islands — in waters swept by strong currents and cold Pacific upwelling.

## Wreck History

The USS Tortuga (LSD-26) was a Casa Grande-class dock landing ship of the United States Navy, measuring 457 feet 9 inches (139.5 m) in length and displacing 4,490 long tons. She served in the Navy before being decommissioned and subsequently used as a target vessel for weapons testing based out of Port Hueneme, California. On December 15, 1987, while under tow, she broke free during a storm and ran aground at Cardwell Point at the southeast corner of San Miguel Island. Following the grounding, the Navy undertook a salvage and scrapping operation on site. The process took more than eight months to complete, and on August 21, 1988, the remains of the ex-USS Tortuga slipped below the surface. Scattered wreckage remains at the site.

## Marine Life

San Miguel Island is known for some of the richest marine life in the Channel Islands, driven by cold Pacific upwelling. The surrounding waters support lingcod, various rockfish species, wolf eels, and California spiny lobster. Any hard structure in this area accumulates invertebrate growth rapidly. Site-specific marine life reports for the Ex-Tortuga wreck debris have not been found in sources consulted.

## Dive Profile

The wreck lies at the southeast corner of San Miguel Island (Cardwell Point). The reported depth is approximately 20 meters, though the original ship was scrapped in place and the nature of the remaining debris field is not well documented in publicly available dive sources. San Miguel Island is subject to some of the strongest currents in the Channel Islands.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access only. San Miguel Island is the most remote of the northern Channel Islands, lying approximately 64 miles from Ventura Harbor and 46 miles from Santa Barbara. Overnight liveaboard charters (such as M/V Truth via Channel Islands Expeditions and M/V Peace) are the practical access option. Conditions at San Miguel are frequently poor; this site can only be dived in calm weather windows.

## Tips and Recommendations

San Miguel Island diving is best in September through November when northwest winds typically ease. Drysuit is strongly recommended. Consult your liveaboard captain about current conditions at Cardwell Point before planning a dive on this site.

## Safety Considerations

San Miguel Island is subject to strong winds, heavy swell, and powerful currents. Water temperature is typically 10–13°C (50–55°F) year-round — drysuit diving is recommended. The remote location means full diver self-sufficiency is essential. This is an advanced dive.
