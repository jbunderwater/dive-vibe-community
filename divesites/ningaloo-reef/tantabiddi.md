## Tantabiddi

Tantabiddi is a reef dive site in Ningaloo Reef, Oceania.

## Overview

Tantabiddi is the main boat ramp and reef access point on the outer northern section of Ningaloo Marine Park, and the primary departure point for whale shark tours (season March–July) and most other boat-based dive and snorkel tours in the Exmouth area. The sanctuary zone reef features shallow coral gardens with brain corals, staghorn, plate, and table corals alongside sandy channels and deeper reef slopes. Confirmed marine life includes green sea turtles, white-tip reef sharks, grey reef sharks, leopard sharks, blue-spotted stingrays, reef rays, Queensland grouper, blue tangs, snappers, parrotfish, angelfish, and butterflyfish. Manta rays are mentioned as seasonal visitors to the Ningaloo reef edge but are not confirmed as reliably present at Tantabiddi specifically. The site is accessed via boat from Tantabiddi boat ramp; the reef is not shore-accessible for diving.
