## Blizzard Ridge

Blizzard Ridge is a coral ridge within the Lighthouse Bay sanctuary zone at the northern tip of the North West Cape, approximately 1 kilometre offshore from the Vlamingh Head Lighthouse.

## Overview

The site is a single elongated coral ridge sitting on a sandy bottom at 12–15 metres, and takes its name from two phenomena: on windy days, disturbed sand creates low-visibility blizzard-like conditions, while on calm days the ridge attracts such dense schools of reef fish that divers find themselves engulfed in swirling curtains of baitfish — another kind of blizzard.

Blizzard Ridge is consistently cited as one of Ningaloo's top fish-density dive sites. Confirmed species at the site include white-tip reef sharks, olive-headed sea snakes, giant moray eels, large groupers, rock cod, surgeonfish, pipefish, parrotfish, and anemones. On calm days, feeding frenzies involving mackerel, trevally, and tuna break out as predators work the abundant glassfish schools. Leopard sharks rest on the sand adjacent to the ridge.

The site sits within Lighthouse Bay alongside The Labyrinth and Gullivers, and is typically dived as part of a double-dive boat trip from Exmouth. Conditions within the sanctuary zone are generally mild, though surface chop can affect the ride out. Suitable for all certified diver levels.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Lighthouse Bay, Ningaloo Marine Park, Exmouth, Western Australia (northern tip of North West Cape)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Visibility**: 5–20 metres (weather and sand disturbance dependent)
