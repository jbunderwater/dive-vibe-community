## Turquoise Bay

Turquoise Bay is a reef dive site in Ningaloo Reef, Oceania.

## Overview

Turquoise Bay is a well-known beach and snorkel site within Cape Range National Park where the reef runs close to shore. The bay has two distinct areas: the protected northern section is suitable for beginners, while the southern end supports a drift snorkel along a coral-covered slope in 3-8 metres, carried by currents through the reef gap. Sea turtles and reef fish are commonly reported here. The maximum recorded depth at the site is around 5-8 metres over the inner reef. Claims that scuba divers can reach 15-20 metres by crossing the reef crest into open water are not corroborated by local operator descriptions of this specific site; Turquoise Bay is primarily known as a snorkel destination. Ningaloo Reef's waters support diverse marine life, but no site-specific scuba depth or wall-dive sources were found for this location.
