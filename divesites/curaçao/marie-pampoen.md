# Marie Pampoen

A vibrant reef dive site featuring healthy coral formations, abundant fish life, and excellent visibility, located in the southern waters of Curaçao.

## Overview

Marie Pampoen is a beautiful reef dive site located off the southern coast of Curaçao, known for its healthy coral formations and excellent marine biodiversity. The site features a sloping reef that transitions into a wall, with depths ranging from shallow coral gardens to deeper sections around 25 meters. The reef is well-preserved and supports a thriving ecosystem of Caribbean marine life.

The site is popular among intermediate divers for its combination of accessible depths, excellent visibility, and abundant marine life. The healthy coral formations include both hard and soft corals, creating a colorful underwater landscape that attracts photographers and marine life enthusiasts alike.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern coast of Curaçao
- **Entry Type**: Boat entry
- **Site Type**: Sloping reef transitioning to wall
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate, generally manageable
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal conditions during dry season

## Marine Life

Marie Pampoen supports a rich diversity of Caribbean reef fish and invertebrates. Divers commonly encounter schools of blue tangs, surgeonfish, and various species of grunts and snappers. The coral formations provide habitat for angelfish, butterflyfish, and parrotfish. Look for moray eels in crevices, and the occasional turtle or eagle ray passing by. The reef structure supports various species of groupers, and macro enthusiasts will find plenty of small critters including shrimp, crabs, and nudibranchs.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically begins with a descent along the reef slope, exploring the coral gardens at various depths. Divers can follow the reef line, gradually descending to explore deeper sections. A typical dive lasts 45-55 minutes, with plenty of bottom time available at moderate depths. The site allows for relaxed exploration and is suitable for divers comfortable with moderate depths.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is via boat, typically using a back-roll or giant stride entry. The boat will anchor near the reef, and divers descend along a mooring line or directly to the reef. Exit is via boat ladder. Surface conditions are generally calm on the leeward side of the island, but always check conditions before entering.

While generally a safe dive site, be aware of boat traffic in the area. Maintain proper buoyancy to protect the fragile coral formations. Monitor your depth and air consumption, especially when exploring deeper sections. Always dive with a buddy and follow standard safety protocols. Use a surface marker buoy for boat pickup.
