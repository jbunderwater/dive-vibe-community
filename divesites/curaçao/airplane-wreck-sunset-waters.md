# Airplane Wreck (Sunset Waters)

An artificial reef created from a small aircraft intentionally sunk to create a dive site, now covered in marine life and providing an interesting wreck dive experience.

## Overview

The Airplane Wreck at Sunset Waters is an artificial reef dive site created by intentionally sinking a small aircraft to provide an interesting dive site and habitat for marine life. The wreck sits at a depth of approximately 22 meters and has become completely encrusted with corals, sponges, and other marine organisms. The site offers a unique diving experience, combining wreck diving with reef exploration.

The aircraft structure provides interesting swim-throughs and photo opportunities, while the surrounding area features healthy reef formations. The site is popular among intermediate divers for its combination of wreck diving and the abundant marine life that has made the aircraft their home. The clear waters and good visibility make it an excellent site for underwater photography.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast of Curaçao, near Sunset Waters area
- **Entry Type**: Boat entry
- **Site Type**: Artificial reef (aircraft wreck)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal conditions during dry season

## Marine Life

The airplane wreck has become a thriving artificial reef, supporting a diverse array of marine life. The structure itself is covered with colorful sponges, corals, and various invertebrates. Divers can expect to see schools of fish swimming around the wreck, including grunts, snappers, and various reef fish. The interior and crevices provide habitat for moray eels, lobsters, and various species of groupers. The surrounding reef area supports typical Caribbean marine life, and the combination of wreck and reef creates a rich ecosystem.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically begins with a descent to the wreck, which sits on a sandy bottom. Divers can explore the exterior of the aircraft, swim through accessible areas (if safe and appropriate), and investigate the marine life that has colonized the structure. A typical dive lasts 40-50 minutes, allowing time to explore both the wreck and the surrounding reef area. The moderate depth requires proper dive planning and air management.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is via boat, typically using a back-roll or giant stride entry. The boat will anchor near the wreck site, and divers descend along a mooring line or directly to the wreck. Exit is via boat ladder. Surface conditions can vary, so ensure good communication with the boat captain.

## Safety Considerations

Wreck diving requires additional safety considerations. Only enter enclosed areas if you have proper wreck diving training. Be aware of sharp edges and potential entanglement hazards. Maintain proper buoyancy to avoid damaging the marine life covering the wreck. Monitor your depth and air consumption carefully. Always dive with a buddy and follow standard safety protocols. Use a surface marker buoy for boat pickup.
