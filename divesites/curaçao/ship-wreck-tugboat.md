# Ship Wreck Tugboat

An artificial reef created from a sunken tugboat, now completely encrusted with marine life and providing an excellent wreck dive experience for intermediate divers.

## Overview

The Ship Wreck Tugboat is an artificial reef dive site created by a sunken tugboat that now serves as a thriving habitat for marine life. The wreck sits at a depth of approximately 30 meters and has become completely encrusted with corals, sponges, and other marine organisms. The site offers a unique diving experience, combining wreck diving with reef exploration, and is popular among intermediate divers.

The tugboat structure provides interesting exploration opportunities, with the vessel's features still recognizable despite the marine growth. The surrounding area features healthy reef formations, and the combination of wreck and reef creates a rich ecosystem. The clear waters and good visibility make it an excellent site for underwater photography and marine life observation.

- **Location**: Southern coast of Curaçao, typically accessed by boat
- **Entry Type**: Boat entry
- **Site Type**: Artificial reef (tugboat wreck)
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate to Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25-30 meters

## Marine Life

The tugboat wreck has become a thriving artificial reef, supporting a diverse array of marine life. The structure itself is covered with colorful sponges, corals, and various invertebrates. Divers can expect to see schools of fish swimming around the wreck, including grunts, snappers, and various reef fish. The interior and crevices provide habitat for moray eels, lobsters, and various species of groupers. The surrounding reef area supports typical Caribbean marine life, and the combination of wreck and reef creates a rich ecosystem.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically begins with a descent to the wreck, which sits on a sandy bottom. Divers can explore the exterior of the tugboat, observe the marine life that has colonized the structure, and investigate the surrounding reef area. A typical dive lasts 35-45 minutes, allowing time to explore both the wreck and the surrounding area. The depth requires proper dive planning and air management.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is via boat, typically using a back-roll or giant stride entry. The boat will anchor near the wreck site, and divers descend along a mooring line or directly to the wreck. Exit is via boat ladder. Surface conditions can vary, so ensure good communication with the boat captain and use a surface marker buoy for safety.

## Safety Considerations

Wreck diving requires additional safety considerations. Only enter enclosed areas if you have proper wreck diving training. Be aware of sharp edges and potential entanglement hazards. Maintain proper buoyancy to avoid damaging the marine life covering the wreck. Monitor your depth and air consumption carefully, as the depth can quickly consume air. Always dive with a buddy and follow standard safety protocols. Use a surface marker buoy for boat pickup.
