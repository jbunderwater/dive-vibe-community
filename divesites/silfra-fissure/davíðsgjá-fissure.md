## Davíðsgjá Fissure

Davíðsgjá Fissure is a tectonic fissure dive site in Thingvellir National Park, Iceland.

## Overview

Davíðsgjá is a submerged rift fissure in Thingvellir National Park, located approximately 10 minutes' drive from the famous Silfra fissure. Like Silfra, it sits directly on the boundary between the North American and Eurasian tectonic plates, which diverge at approximately 2 cm per year. The fissure was formed by the same geological forces that have split Iceland's landscape into dramatic canyons and rifts above the waterline.

The water at Davíðsgjá originates from glacial meltwater filtered through porous lava rock for decades before reaching the fissure, resulting in extraordinary clarity. Visibility consistently reaches 100–200 metres, earning it a reputation as one of the clearest freshwater dive sites in Europe. Water temperature remains constant year-round at 2–5°C.

Davíðsgjá is considered the "little brother" of Silfra: it offers the same tectonic geology and crystal-clear water but in a more compact, sportier setting. Maximum depth is approximately 15 metres, and the bottom is composed of stone. Divers swim between towering fissure walls and can observe the geological structures created by continental drift. Marine life is sparse — trout and nudibranchs are the primary encounters — but the visual experience of the fissure walls and the extraordinary water clarity is the site's primary attraction. Some algae formations are visible along the walls.

Access requires a 300-metre walk in full diving equipment to reach the entry point, which involves a demanding rocky bank entry. Drysuit is mandatory; no wetsuit diving is permitted in these waters.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Shore; rocky bank entry after a 300-metre walk in equipment
- **Maximum depth**: 15 m (sources indicate 15 m; frontmatter records 21 m — depth varies by route)
- **Average depth**: 5–10 m
- **Visibility**: 100–200 m average
- **Water temperature**: 2–5°C year-round
- **Dive duration**: 30–40 minutes typically

## Requirements

- **Mandatory equipment**: Drysuit (wetsuits prohibited)
- **Certification**: International Open Water minimum; dry suit certification or 10 logged dry suit dives within 2 years required
- **Guided dive**: Required; access is through accredited operators only
- **Permit**: Thingvellir National Park dive permit required (fee payable)
- **Minimum age**: 17 years
- **Physical fitness**: High — demanding walk and difficult entry

## Marine Life

Aquatic life is minimal: trout, Dwarf Char (2–10 cm) in darker recesses, and nudibranchs. The visual draw is geological rather than biological.

## Photography

The exceptional visibility and dramatic fissure walls create outstanding conditions for wide-angle photography. The play of light filtering down between tectonic walls is a signature image of Iceland's freshwater diving.

## Safety

Water temperature of 2–5°C requires high-quality drysuits with adequate underlayers. Even well-equipped divers are typically limited to 30–40 minute dives. The demanding shore entry and exit in cold, bulky equipment requires good physical condition. Always dive with a guide and partner.

## Site Information

- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
