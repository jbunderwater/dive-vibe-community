## Lighthouse Point

Lighthouse Point is a popular shore-entry reef dive site located on the southern coast of Bonaire, known for its vibrant coral formations and diverse marine life. It offers excellent visibility and moderate depth, making it suitable for divers with some experience.

## Overview

Lighthouse Point is situated near Bonaire’s southern coastline, adjacent to the iconic lighthouse that gives the site its name. The dive site features a gently sloping coral reef with abundant hard and soft corals and a variety of reef fish species. The area is characterized by calm conditions and clear water typical of Bonaire’s protected marine environment. Its layout allows divers to explore a range of depths up to about 30 meters, with areas of coral bommies, sandy patches, and small swim-throughs. The site is well-loved for its accessibility via shore entry and offers a peaceful underwater experience away from more crowded locations.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Southern Bonaire, near Bonaire Lighthouse
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal conditions in the dry season (March to August)

## Marine Life

Divers can expect to see a rich variety of tropical marine species, including colorful reef fish such as parrotfish, angelfish, and damselfish. The coral formations provide shelter for crustaceans, moray eels, and occasional sightings of turtle species. The reef’s health supports a balanced ecosystem, with occasional sightings of rays and small reef sharks. The site is also known for macro life, including nudibranchs and seahorses, making it interesting for underwater photographers.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically begins at the sandy shore entry, descending along a sloping reef that extends down to about 30 meters. The route follows the contours of coral bommies interspersed with sandy patches. There are a few small swim-throughs and overhangs for exploration, but no penetration dives. Divers are advised to maintain good buoyancy control to avoid damaging corals. The profile is suitable for an average dive time of 40 to 60 minutes depending on air consumption and depth.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from shore via a sandy beach area with easy access to the water. The initial swim to the reef edge is short and gentle, with no significant surface current hazards. Exit is at the same point, with a gradual ascent through shallow reef areas. There are no notable entry hazards, but divers should be cautious of slippery rocks near the shore.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Bring a reef hook or marker buoy if planning to drift along mild currents.
- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the fragile coral ecosystem.
- Early morning dives are recommended for best visibility and calmer seas.
- Respect local marine park regulations to help preserve the site.

## Safety Considerations

While currents are generally mild, occasional shifts may require attention to navigation and group cohesion. Depths reaching 30 meters require proper training and attention to no-decompression limits. Avoid contact with corals and marine life to prevent injury and environmental damage. Certification suitable for intermediate-level divers is recommended.

## Photography

Lighthouse Point offers excellent underwater photography opportunities with vibrant coral colors and diverse fish life. The clear water and good ambient light provide ideal conditions for wide-angle shots of the reefscape, while macro photographers can focus on small critters such as nudibranchs and shrimps. Early morning or late afternoon dives provide the best natural lighting.
