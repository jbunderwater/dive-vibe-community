# Weber's Joy

*Also known as Witches Hut — a northern Bonaire reef site with a deep outer slope reaching 40 metres, healthy coral cover, and good fish diversity in a quieter, less-visited setting.*

## Overview

Weber's Joy (also called Witches Hut by some operators and dive guides) is a northern Bonaire shore site located near the junction where the coastal road curves toward the national park area. The site offers a classic Bonaire reef profile with a gradual inner shelf transitioning to a steeper outer slope reaching 40 metres. The northern location and the distance from Kralendijk mean this site sees less traffic than the popular central and southern sites, giving it a quieter character. It is frequently combined with Jeff Davis Memorial and Boka di Tolo as part of a northern shore-dive day.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Bonaire, near the national park approach road
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Morning

## Marine Life

Northern Bonaire reefs support healthy fish populations typical of lightly-visited sites: parrotfish, angelfish, grunts, snappers, and groupers. Turtles are regularly seen. Barracuda patrol the outer slope. The coral cover includes hard coral formations and soft coral on the deeper sections.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry and swim to the reef edge. The shallow shelf gives way to a steeper outer slope. Most of the productive diving is in the 12–25 metre range; the slope continues to 40 metres for those managing depth carefully. Plan gas for the return with a safety stop in the shallows.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry — the exact entry point is marked. Exit at the same location.

## Tips

- The 40m maximum depth means intermediate gas planning is required
- Combine with Jeff Davis Memorial or Boka di Tolo for a productive northern coast day
- Quieter than central coast sites: good choice when escaping crowds

## Safety

The 40-metre slope requires awareness of no-decompression limits and careful gas management for the return to the shallows.

## Photography

The healthy coral cover and good visibility in the north make this productive for both reef-wide compositions and close-up work on coral formations.

## Nearby Sites

Jeff Davis Memorial is immediately to the south along the same coastal road. Boka di Tolo is nearby. Karpata is a short drive further north.
