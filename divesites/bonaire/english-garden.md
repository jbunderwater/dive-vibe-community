## English Garden

English Garden is a shallow, sheltered reef dive site located on the west coast of Bonaire, known for its lush coral formations and calm conditions ideal for beginner divers.

## Overview

English Garden is a classic Bonaire shore dive site characterized by gently sloping coral reefs and an abundance of marine life. The site offers a garden-like underwater landscape composed primarily of hard corals interspersed with sea fans and sponges. Its protected location within Bonaire’s marine park ensures excellent water quality and stable diving conditions year-round. The site is well-suited for novice divers due to its mild currents and moderate depths, making it a favorite for training and leisurely exploration.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bonaire, off the west coast near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner  
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters  
- **Current**: None to mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round; mornings often offer calmest conditions  

## Marine Life

English Garden hosts a wide variety of Caribbean reef fish such as damselfish, sergeant majors, and parrotfish. Divers frequently encounter spotted eagle rays, queen angelfish, and colorful Caribbean wrasses. The coral garden environment supports a variety of invertebrates including sea cucumbers, brittle stars, and occasional seahorses. The site’s healthy coral formations provide shelter for small reef sharks and nurse sharks, which are sometimes spotted resting on the sandy bottom.

## Dive Profile

The dive generally starts from the sandy shore with a gradual descent along the reef slope. Depths range mostly between 5 and 18 meters, making it comfortable for extended bottom time. A typical route follows the coral heads and swim-throughs in a slow, meandering pattern, allowing divers to explore overhangs and crevices inhabited by small reef creatures. There are no wrecks or deep walls at this site. The calm conditions and clear visibility facilitate easy navigation and excellent photo opportunities.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is straightforward from the shore via a gentle sandy beach. Divers enter the water walking in slowly to the reef edge about 10-15 meters from shore. The exit is similarly easy, with a short swim back to the beach. There are no significant surface currents or hazards, making it suitable for divers of all experience levels.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Use reef-friendly sunscreen to protect the vibrant coral ecosystem.  
- Carry a surface marker buoy if planning to drift slightly with mild currents.  
- Morning dives typically feature calmer seas and better visibility.  
- Bring a camera for macro photography of the abundant small reef critters.  

## Safety Considerations

Currents are generally mild to none, but always monitor local conditions as weather can affect surface currents. The maximum depth is moderate, suitable for basic open water certification. Be cautious around coral formations to avoid cuts and to protect fragile corals. No overhead environments or penetration are present, reducing entanglement risks.

## Photography

English Garden offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle shots of colorful corals and reef fish in clear water. Macro photographers can find seahorses, nudibranchs, and small crustaceans among the coral heads. Early morning light enhances colors and reduces backscatter in photos.
