## Carel's Vision

Carel's Vision is a popular shore-accessible reef dive site located on the leeward coast of Bonaire, known for its healthy coral formations, abundant marine life, and moderate depth suitable for intermediate divers.

## Overview

Carel's Vision features a gently sloping coral reef that extends from shallow waters to depths around 25 meters. The site is named after a local dive operator and is renowned for its excellent visibility and calm conditions typical of Bonaire’s protected marine environment. The reef structure includes coral heads, sponges, and occasional swim-throughs, providing a varied underwater landscape. Currents are generally mild, making it accessible for divers with some experience. The site offers consistent water temperatures year-round and is part of Bonaire’s marine park, benefiting from conservation efforts.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Bonaire, leeward west coast near Kralendijk
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal conditions from April to November

## Marine Life

Divers at Carel’s Vision can expect to see a wide array of Caribbean reef fish such as parrotfish, angelfish, damselfish, and grunts. The reef supports healthy populations of nurse sharks, eagle rays, and turtles. Macro life includes colorful nudibranchs, seahorses, and various crustaceans. The coral diversity includes hard and soft corals, sponges, and gorgonians. Occasional sightings of lobsters and moray eels add to the site’s appeal.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically starts at shallow depths of 5-10 meters, progressing gradually to the reef slope reaching about 25 meters. Divers follow the contour of the reef, exploring coral heads and swim-throughs that provide shelter for marine life. The site does not involve penetration of wrecks or caves, but the swim-throughs offer interesting navigation challenges. The dive can be conducted as a drift with the mild current or as a leisurely reef exploration. Standard recreational dive limits apply.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from shore via a sandy beach area with easy water access. Divers walk into the water carrying gear and swim a short distance to the reef edge. Exiting is straightforward by swimming back to the shore entry point. Surface currents are usually mild but should be monitored. There are no significant hazards such as strong surf or sharp rocks at entry and exit.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a reef hook or safety sausage for drift sections in mild currents.
- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the marine environment.
- Early morning dives often provide calmer seas and better visibility.
- Respect the marine park regulations to help preserve the reef health.

## Safety Considerations

- Moderate depth requires good buoyancy control to avoid coral contact.
- Mild current may require some drift diving experience.
- Be cautious of sharp coral and marine life like sea urchins.
- Recommended certification level: Advanced Open Water or equivalent with reef experience.

## Photography

Carel’s Vision offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography and macro shots of the abundant small creatures. Morning light enhances coral colors. Swim-throughs provide dramatic compositions. Use a strobe or video light for best results in shaded areas.
