## Salt Pier

Salt Pier is a popular shore-entry dive site in Bonaire known for its easy access and abundant marine life around the pilings of an old salt dock.

## Overview

Salt Pier is located on the west coast of Bonaire and features a unique underwater structure formed by the pilings of a former salt loading pier. The vertical concrete pilings create an artificial reef environment attracting diverse marine life. The site is sheltered, with calm waters and good visibility, making it ideal for beginner divers and snorkelers. The pier extends into relatively shallow waters, with depths usually not exceeding 15 meters. Currents are typically mild, allowing relaxed exploration around and under the pier.

## Site Information
- **Location**: West coast of Bonaire near the salt flats, close to Kralendijk
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Pier / wreck structure with reef characteristics
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round; mornings often have calmer conditions

## Marine Life

Divers can expect to see a wide variety of tropical fish species that congregate around the pier pilings, including grunts, angelfish, and damselfish. The structure also supports invertebrates such as sponges, soft corals, and anemones, which in turn attract cleaner shrimp and small reef fish. Occasionally, spotted eagle rays and turtles are sighted near the pier. The site is well-regarded for macro photography opportunities due to the rich biodiversity around the pilings.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically involves descending along the pier pilings, which extend vertically from near the surface down to about 15 meters. Divers often swim along the length of the pier, exploring the crevices and overhangs formed by the clustered pilings. There are no penetration options since the structure is open, but the vertical nature offers interesting sightlines both upwards toward the surface and downward. The dive profile is easy and suitable for all levels, with ample opportunities to hover and observe marine life.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from shore, usually at the beach adjacent to the pier. Divers walk into the water and descend alongside the pilings. The entry is straightforward with no significant surface currents or surge. Exiting is done by swimming back along the pier to shore. The sandy bottom near the shore makes for an easy exit.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a camera for macro and wide-angle shots around the pilings.
- Early morning dives often have the best visibility and calmest water.
- Use a dive light to illuminate the pilings and spot hidden critters.
- Stay close to the pier structure to avoid mild current and maximize marine life encounters.

## Safety Considerations

- The pilings can have sharp edges and barnacles; maintain good buoyancy control to avoid cuts or scrapes.
- Mild currents may be present; divers should be comfortable with basic open water skills.
- The site is shallow but be mindful of depth limits to avoid nitrogen buildup on repetitive dives.

## Photography

Salt Pier provides excellent opportunities for underwater photography with photogenic vertical pilings covered in colorful sponges and corals. Macro photographers will find numerous small reef creatures and shrimp. The vertical lines of the pier pilings also create dramatic compositions with natural light filtering from above, especially during mid-morning.
