## Bisé Morto

Bisé Morto is a vibrant reef dive site located off the coast of Bonaire, known for its diverse marine life and healthy coral formations. It offers an appealing underwater landscape suitable for intermediate divers.

## Overview

Bisé Morto lies on Bonaire’s leeward side and features a gently sloping coral reef that extends from shallow waters to depths around 30 meters. The site is characterized by a mix of hard and soft corals, providing shelter and feeding grounds for a variety of fish species. Conditions are generally calm, with mild currents, making it suitable for divers who have some experience but want to explore a rich reef environment. Visibility is typically good, often between 15 to 30 meters. The site is accessed by shore entry via a sandy beach area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bonaire, Caribbean Netherlands, near the southern coast  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate  
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters  
- **Current**: Mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with peak diving conditions from April to August

## Marine Life

Divers at Bisé Morto can expect to see a variety of tropical fish such as parrotfish, angelfish, damselfish, and trumpetfish. The reef hosts colorful corals including brain coral and elkhorn coral, as well as sponges and sea fans. Occasional sightings of turtles, rays, and reef sharks add to the excitement. The biodiversity of the site makes it a popular spot for underwater photography.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive at Bisé Morto begins with a shallow reef swim along the sandy bottom, gradually descending to deeper coral formations around 25 to 30 meters. The reef slope offers gentle to moderate current, allowing for a leisurely drift dive if desired. Divers can explore swim-throughs within coral structures and observe schools of small fish. There are no wrecks or caves, but the reef’s complexity offers interesting terrain with overhangs and coral pinnacles. Caution is advised to avoid contact with corals.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from a sandy beach with easy access to the water, making it convenient for shore dives. Divers should be mindful of sun exposure on the walk in and use reef-safe sunscreen. Exit is at the same point as entry, usually straightforward with minimal waves or surge. A surface swim back to shore is required, but currents are mild.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the coral ecosystem.  
- Carry a surface marker buoy for safety during shore entry dives.  
- Check local dive shops for current conditions and any access advisories.  
- Bring a dive light to explore shaded swim-throughs and crevices.  

## Safety Considerations

Mild currents are present but generally manageable; novice divers should dive with a buddy and stay within training limits. Depths up to 30 meters require proper certification and adherence to no-decompression limits. There are no known entanglement hazards, but careful buoyancy control is essential to avoid damaging corals.

## Photography

Bisé Morto offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography due to its clear waters and colorful coral formations. Early morning or late afternoon dives provide softer light and enhanced colors. Macro photography is rewarding around coral heads where small critters hide.
