## Andrea II

Andrea II is a popular wreck dive site located off the coast of Bonaire, known for its well-preserved cargo ship wreck resting at moderate depths, offering diverse marine life and excellent underwater exploration opportunities.

## Overview

The Andrea II is a steel cargo ship that sank near Bonaire and now serves as an artificial reef. It lies upright on a sandy bottom and attracts a variety of reef fish and other marine organisms. The wreck's structure is largely intact, providing interesting swim-throughs and shelter for marine life. Conditions are generally calm with mild currents, making it accessible primarily by boat. The site offers a combination of natural reef features and wreck diving appeal, popular among intermediate divers.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Bonaire, southern Caribbean Sea, near Kralendijk
- **Entry Type**: Boat
- **Site Type**: Wreck
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with best conditions typically from April to November

## Marine Life

Divers can expect to see a variety of tropical fish species such as angelfish, parrotfish, and grunts. The wreck also attracts larger species like barracudas and occasionally nurse sharks. The structure provides habitat for sponges, soft corals, and schooling fish, creating a vibrant underwater ecosystem. Macro life includes cleaner shrimp and small reef critters in nooks of the wreck.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive on the Andrea II involves descending along the wreck's exterior, exploring the deck area, and swimming through accessible openings in the hull. Depth ranges from 18 to 30 meters, with most activity concentrated around 20-25 meters. Divers should follow a clockwise or counterclockwise route around the wreck to maximize observation of marine life and structural features. Penetration inside the wreck is limited and should only be attempted by experienced divers with proper training due to overhead environment risks.

## Entry and Exit

Dives are conducted from boats that anchor near the site. Entry is by giant stride or backward roll depending on the boat type. Exit is back onto the boat using ladders. Surface conditions are usually calm, but divers should be mindful of boat traffic and ensure clear communication with the dive crew.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Use a dive light for exploring darker areas of the wreck.
- Maintain good buoyancy to avoid stirring up sediment around the wreck.
- Bring a reef hook if mild currents are present to stabilize during exploration.
- Respect marine life and avoid touching the wreck to preserve its condition.
- Confirm boat logistics in advance as shore access is not available.

## Safety Considerations

- Moderate depth requires certification to at least Advanced Open Water level.
- Wreck penetration should only be attempted by divers with wreck diving experience and appropriate equipment.
- Watch for mild currents and ensure safe ascent procedures.
- Avoid sharp edges on the wreck to prevent cuts or entanglement.
- Monitor air consumption carefully due to depth and potential current.

## Photography

The Andrea II offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle photography of the wreck and its marine inhabitants. Early morning dives provide optimal lighting. Macro photographers can find small critters in crevices. Use strobes to bring out colors in the deeper parts of the wreck.
