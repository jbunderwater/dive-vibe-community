## Larry's Lair

Larry's Lair is a popular shore-accessible reef dive site located on Bonaire's west coast, known for vibrant coral formations and abundant marine life in clear Caribbean waters.

## Overview

Larry's Lair offers divers a moderately challenging reef experience characterized by a gently sloping coral reef with varied topography, including swim-throughs and overhangs. The site is named after a local dive operator and provides shelter from open ocean currents, making it a favored spot among intermediate divers. Conditions are typically calm with good visibility, and the reef supports a healthy ecosystem of tropical fish and invertebrates.

## Site Information
- **Location**: West coast of Bonaire, near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate  
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters  
- **Current**: Mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with peak diving conditions from April to August  

## Marine Life

Divers at Larry's Lair can expect to see a diverse array of Caribbean reef fish including parrotfish, angelfish, grunts, and trumpetfish. The reef hosts various species of colorful corals, sponges, and occasionally turtles and eagle rays. Macro life such as nudibranchs and seahorses may also be spotted by attentive divers.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive starts from the shore, following the reef line along a gentle slope descending to about 25 meters. The route often includes swim-throughs and coral overhangs, making it an engaging dive for exploration. Divers should maintain buoyancy control to avoid contact with delicate coral. No penetration of wrecks is involved. The dive is generally a drift along the reef with mild currents aiding the return to shore.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is directly from the shore via a sandy beach area with easy access. Divers should be cautious of shallow reef patches near the shore. Exit is by swimming back along the reef or directly to the beach, with no significant surface hazards or strong currents reported.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Carry reef-safe sunscreen to protect the marine environment.  
- Use a dive flag as the site is near boat traffic areas.  
- Best to dive with a buddy familiar with shore entries and exits in Bonaire.  
- Bring a dive light for better visibility inside swim-throughs and overhangs.  

## Safety Considerations

Mild currents require basic navigation skills; intermediate certification is recommended. The reef is fragile—avoid touching corals. Watch for boat traffic near the shore. Proper buoyancy and awareness of depth limits are essential to protect both diver and environment.

## Photography

Larry's Lair offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography due to clear water and vibrant coral formations. Early morning dives provide the best natural light. Macro photographers can find interesting subjects such as nudibranchs and small reef critters in crevices and overhangs.
