## Tori's Reef

Tori's Reef is a popular shallow reef dive site located off the coast of Bonaire, known for its calm conditions and vibrant marine life, making it ideal for beginner divers.

## Overview

Tori's Reef is situated near Bonaire's western shore and features gently sloping coral formations typical of the island's reefs. The site is accessible directly from shore, making it convenient for shore diving enthusiasts. The reef is characterized by healthy coral growth and a variety of reef fish species. Conditions are generally calm with mild currents, providing a safe environment for less experienced divers. Visibility is typically good year-round, supported by Bonaire's reputation for excellent underwater clarity.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Bonaire, near western shore landmarks
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal conditions from April to August

## Marine Life

Divers at Tori's Reef frequently encounter a diverse assemblage of tropical reef fish including angelfish, parrotfish, damselfish, and wrasses. The reef also hosts invertebrates such as sea fans, sponges, and occasional sightings of sea turtles. While not known for rare species, the site offers reliable sightings of colorful reef inhabitants and healthy coral colonies.

## Dive Profile

The dive at Tori's Reef typically involves a shallow descent to around 12-18 meters, exploring the coral formations and reef slopes. The site is suited for leisurely swims along the reef with no penetration or overhead environments. Mild currents allow for easy navigation, and the reef's structure provides interesting swim-throughs between coral outcrops. Divers should follow a circular route to cover the main features and avoid disturbing marine life.

## Entry and Exit

Entry to Tori's Reef is from shore, usually via a sandy beach area with gradual entry into the water. The approach involves a short walk with equipment, followed by a gentle slope into the reef. Exit is straightforward by swimming back to shore along the entry path. There are no significant surface currents or hazards, making it safe and accessible for divers of all levels.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the fragile coral ecosystem.
- Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB) as a safety precaution, even in mild currents.
- Best to dive in the morning when visibility is often at its peak.
- Respect local marine life and avoid touching corals or animals.

## Safety Considerations

Tori's Reef presents minimal safety risks due to its shallow depth and mild currents. However, divers should always monitor their air consumption and depth, maintain good buoyancy control to avoid coral damage, and be aware of boat traffic near shore. Certification for open water or beginner level is sufficient for this site.

## Photography

The site offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography with vibrant coral formations and schooling fish. Morning light enhances colors and visibility. Macro photography can capture small reef critters such as nudibranchs and shrimp. Avoid stirring up sediment to maintain clear shots.
