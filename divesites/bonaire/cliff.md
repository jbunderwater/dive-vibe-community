## Cliff

Cliff is a popular shore-entry reef dive site located on the west coast of Bonaire, known for its easy access and vibrant marine life along a gently sloping coral reef. It is favored by beginner and intermediate divers for its calm conditions and colorful underwater scenery.

## Overview

Cliff is situated near Kralendijk, the main town of Bonaire, making it easily accessible to divers staying on the island. The site features a gradual reef slope starting around 6 meters and extending down to about 18 meters, with coral formations interspersed with sand patches. The area experiences mild currents and generally excellent visibility, typical of Bonaire’s protected marine environment. The reef hosts a variety of hard and soft corals, sponges, and ample marine biodiversity, offering a classic Caribbean reef diving experience.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast of Bonaire, near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner  
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters  
- **Current**: Mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, especially mornings  

## Marine Life

Divers at Cliff can expect to see a wide range of Caribbean marine species including various reef fish such as sergeant majors, parrotfish, angelfish, and butterflyfish. The site is also home to lobsters, moray eels, and occasional sightings of turtles and rays. The coral reef supports both hard corals like brain and elkhorn corals as well as soft corals, providing habitat for many smaller creatures. Rare species are not commonly reported here, but the consistent biodiversity makes it rewarding.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive at Cliff involves a gentle descent along the reef slope starting from shallow waters around 6 meters down to a maximum of about 18 meters. The dive route follows the contours of the reef, with opportunities to explore coral outcrops and small swim-throughs formed by coral overhangs. The dive is well suited for relaxed exploration with no penetration or wreck features. Divers should maintain buoyancy control to avoid contact with delicate corals.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from shore, generally via a sandy beach area with a short walk from parking. The entry is straightforward with a gentle slope into the water, making it ideal for all skill levels. Exit is via the same beach area. Divers should watch for occasional surface currents near the shore and enter at low tide if possible for easier access.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect marine life.  
- Mornings typically offer the best visibility and calmest conditions.  
- Check local tide charts to optimize entry and exit ease.  
- Carry a surface marker buoy for safety on exit if diving solo or in small groups.  

## Safety Considerations

Currents at Cliff are generally mild but can pick up slightly in the afternoon. Depths are moderate, suitable for beginner divers but still require standard open water certification. There are no overhead environments or entanglement hazards, but divers should avoid touching or standing on corals to prevent injury and reef damage.

## Photography

Cliff offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle and macro photography. The clear water and abundant colorful corals provide vibrant backgrounds, while small reef fish and macro critters such as shrimp and nudibranchs can be found among the coral heads. Early morning light enhances colors and reduces backscatter.
