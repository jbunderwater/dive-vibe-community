## Hilma Hooker

The Hilma Hooker is a famous wreck dive located off the coast of Bonaire, renowned for its accessibility, rich marine life, and clear waters, making it a top attraction for divers of intermediate skill level.

## Overview

The Hilma Hooker is a cargo ship that sank in 1984 and now rests at approximately 30 meters depth. The wreck lies close to the shore off Bonaire’s southern coast and has become an artificial reef teeming with marine life. It is one of Bonaire’s most popular dive sites due to its well-preserved structure, including the hull, deck, and superstructure that divers can explore. The site offers strong visual interest with swim-throughs and coral growth on the wreck, and the water conditions are generally calm with good visibility.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Southern Bonaire, near Kralendijk
- **Entry Type**: Boat
- **Site Type**: Wreck
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with best visibility typically from March to August

## Marine Life

The wreck attracts a variety of Caribbean marine species including large schools of tropical fish, barracudas, tarpons, and sometimes eagle rays. The structure is covered with sponges, corals, and sea fans, providing shelter for smaller creatures like shrimps and crabs. Night dives may reveal nocturnal species inhabiting the wreck.

## Dive Profile

Divers typically descend along the starboard side, exploring the deck, superstructure, and internal swim-throughs. The maximum depth reaches about 30 meters, with most exploration between 18 and 30 meters. The wreck is penetrable in some areas but caution and proper training are advised. The dive is generally a drift dive with a mild current, so divers should monitor their position and air supply carefully.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is by boat, usually from one of Bonaire’s dive operators. Exiting is typically done by boat pickup near the wreck. The site is located close to shore, but currents may require controlled entry and exit procedures to ensure diver safety.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a dive light for exploring the darker interior areas and swim-throughs.
- Use a surface marker buoy (SMB) during ascent due to mild currents and boat traffic.
- Check with local dive operators for current conditions and any site-specific guidelines.
- Avoid penetrating the wreck without proper training and equipment.

## Safety Considerations

- Moderate currents can occur; maintain good buoyancy control and group cohesion.
- The wreck has sharp edges and entanglement hazards; wear appropriate exposure protection.
- Maximum depth reaches 30 meters; divers should have advanced open water certification or equivalent experience.
- Penetration should only be attempted by qualified wreck divers with proper gear.

## Photography

The Hilma Hooker offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle photography, capturing the full wreck and its marine inhabitants. The clear Caribbean waters provide good natural light, especially during midday. Macro photographers will find interesting small critters among the sponges and corals. Using strobes enhances color vibrancy on deeper parts of the wreck.
