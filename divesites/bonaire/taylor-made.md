## Taylor Made

Taylor Made is a popular shore-accessible reef dive site in Bonaire known for its gentle slope and abundant marine life, making it ideal for beginner divers and snorkelers.

## Overview

Located on Bonaire’s west coast, Taylor Made is part of the island’s well-protected marine park. The site features a gradual reef slope starting just a few meters from shore, extending down to about 20 meters. It is characterized by healthy coral formations and sandy patches, providing a safe and scenic environment with typically calm conditions. Visibility is generally excellent, and currents are mild to none, making it a comfortable site for divers new to the area. Its easy accessibility and rich biodiversity have made it a favorite among dive operators and independent divers alike.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Bonaire, Caribbean Netherlands, west coast near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner  
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters  
- **Current**: Mild to none  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with the calmest conditions from April to August  

## Marine Life

Divers at Taylor Made can expect to see a variety of vibrant reef fish such as parrotfish, angelfish, damselfish, and butterflyfish. The reef hosts healthy hard and soft corals, sponges, and sea fans, providing habitats for crustaceans and small reef sharks. Occasional sightings include turtles and rays. The site’s diversity and accessibility make it a prime location for observing the typical Caribbean reef ecosystem.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically begins at the shore entry with a gradual descent down the reef slope. Depths rarely exceed 20 meters, making it suitable for extended bottom times at shallow to moderate depths. The reef structure includes coral outcrops, sandy bottoms, and small swim-throughs formed by coral formations, offering interesting navigation and exploration. Divers should maintain buoyancy control to protect the corals and navigate around the reef gently. No penetration or wreck diving is involved.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is straightforward by walking into the water from the sandy beach area. The gentle slope allows for an easy descent without surge or strong currents. Exit is usually at the same location, with a short swim back to shore. The site is well-suited for shore diving with minimal surface swim.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect the coral ecosystem.
- Use a dive flag if diving independently from shore.
- Early morning dives often provide the best visibility and calmer seas.
- Carry a dive light for exploring shaded areas and swim-throughs.
- Respect the marine park regulations to preserve the site’s health.

## Safety Considerations

Currents are generally mild but can pick up occasionally; always check local conditions before diving. The maximum depth is moderate, suitable for beginner to intermediate divers. The reef’s coral formations require careful buoyancy control to avoid injury or damage. No significant entanglement hazards are reported.

## Photography

Taylor Made offers excellent underwater photography with abundant colorful reef fish and coral landscapes. The shallow depths provide good natural lighting, especially during morning hours. Macro photography enthusiasts can find plenty of small critters and vibrant sponges. A wide-angle lens is useful to capture the reef formations and schooling fish.
