## Invisibles

Invisibles is a well-regarded reef dive site located off the coast of Bonaire, known for its spectacular coral formations and abundant marine life in clear Caribbean waters.

## Overview

Invisibles is a popular boat-access reef site on Bonaire, part of the island's renowned marine park. The site features a gently sloping reef with healthy hard and soft corals, swim-throughs, and sandy patches. It is favored for its calm conditions, good visibility, and diverse underwater topography that appeals to intermediate divers. The site is protected within Bonaire National Marine Park, ensuring rich biodiversity and well-preserved coral structures.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bonaire, Caribbean Netherlands, near the west coast of the island
- **Entry Type**: Boat
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20 to 30 meters
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal conditions from April to August

## Marine Life

Divers can expect to see a variety of tropical reef fish such as parrotfish, angelfish, grunts, and damselfish. The reef is also home to spotted drum, barracudas, and occasional eagle rays. Macro enthusiasts may find seahorses and frogfish among the coral heads. The healthy coral cover supports numerous invertebrates and colorful sponges, making the site vibrant and photogenic.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive at Invisibles starts at shallower depths around 10 meters, progressing along the reef slope to a maximum of 25 meters. The reef features swim-throughs and overhangs that add interest and shelter for marine life. Divers often follow the contour of the reef in a drift dive with the mild current. There are no wrecks or caves requiring penetration, but careful buoyancy control is advised to protect the coral.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is by boat drop-off directly over the reef, with divers descending along a mooring line. The exit is from the boat, which waits nearby for pickup. There are no shore-entry options at this site, so divers should be comfortable with boat transfers and surface swims to the boat after the dive.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the marine environment.
- Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB) for safety during ascent and boat pickup.
- Bring a dive light to explore swim-throughs and overhangs better.
- Plan dives during slack tide to minimize current effects.
- Respect the marine park regulations to preserve the site's biodiversity.

## Safety Considerations

Divers should be aware of mild to moderate currents and maintain good buoyancy to avoid damaging corals. The maximum depth of 25 meters requires appropriate certification and gas management. Boat divers should be prepared for surface swims and use SMBs for safety. No known entanglement hazards exist, but always dive with a buddy and check local conditions.

## Photography

Invisibles offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography due to its clear water and vibrant coral formations. Macro photographers will find interesting subjects in the reef's crevices and sandy areas. Early morning dives provide the best natural light with minimal plankton.
