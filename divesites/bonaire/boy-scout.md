## Boy Scout

Boy Scout is a popular, easily accessible shore dive site located in Bonaire known for its vibrant reef and calm conditions, making it ideal for beginner divers.

## Overview

Boy Scout is a shallow reef dive situated on the western coast of Bonaire, a Caribbean island renowned for its pristine underwater environments. The site features a gently sloping reef starting at about 3 meters and descending to around 18 meters at its deepest point. It is characterized by abundant hard and soft corals, sponges, and a wide variety of reef fish. Conditions are typically calm with minimal current, providing excellent visibility that can reach up to 30 meters on good days. Its accessibility from shore and sheltered location make it a favored site for both novice and snorkel divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bonaire, Caribbean Netherlands, near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner  
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters  
- **Current**: None to mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with peak conditions from March to August  

## Marine Life

Divers at Boy Scout can expect to see a diverse array of marine life including colorful reef fish such as parrotfish, angelfish, sergeant majors, and damselfish. The reef hosts healthy coral formations, barrel sponges, sea fans, and occasional sightings of turtles and rays. Small critters like cleaner shrimp and various nudibranchs are common on the reef. While larger pelagics are rare, the vibrant reef ecosystem offers abundant photo opportunities and marine biodiversity typical for Bonaire.

## Dive Profile

Typical dives start from the sandy beach, heading out over the reef slope. Depth ranges from 3 to 18 meters, suitable for long bottom times. The reef gently slopes downward with coral heads and swim-throughs scattered along the route. Divers often follow the reef contour parallel to shore, allowing easy navigation back. No wreck or wall penetration is involved. The site is ideal for practicing buoyancy control and reef fish identification.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is straightforward from the shore, with a sandy beach entrance and a short swim to the reef edge. There are no significant currents or surge, minimizing entry hazards. Exit is via the same sandy beach area. Divers should be mindful of slippery rocks near the entry and exit points and enter in calm sea states.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect the vibrant coral ecosystem.  
- Use a surface marker buoy if diving solo or as a precaution.  
- Early morning dives offer the best visibility and tranquil conditions.  
- Respect marine life and avoid touching corals or disturbing animals.  

## Safety Considerations

- Minimal currents but always monitor conditions before diving.  
- Max depth around 18 meters, suitable for beginner certification levels.  
- Watch footing on rocky entry points to avoid slips.  
- No overhead environments; standard open water precautions apply.  

## Photography

Boy Scout offers excellent macro and wide-angle photography opportunities with its colorful reef, sponges, and diverse fish life. Early morning light enhances colors and visibility. Use a red filter or strobe to bring out coral colors in photos. The site’s shallow depths provide natural light even at maximum depth.
