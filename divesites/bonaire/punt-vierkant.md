## Punt Vierkant

Punt Vierkant is a popular shore-accessible reef dive site located on the western coast of Bonaire, known for its easy entry and vibrant coral formations suitable for beginner divers.

## Overview

Punt Vierkant is part of Bonaire’s renowned marine park, a protected area that boasts some of the healthiest coral reefs in the Caribbean. The site features gently sloping reefs with abundant hard and soft corals and a variety of marine life. The dive site is characterized by calm conditions and excellent visibility, making it ideal for novice and intermediate divers. Its location near the shore allows for easy access without the need for a boat.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Western coast of Bonaire, Caribbean Netherlands  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner  
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters  
- **Current**: None to mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with calm seas typically from December to April  

## Marine Life

Divers at Punt Vierkant can expect to see a wide range of Caribbean reef fish, including parrotfish, angelfish, grunts, and sergeant majors. The reef is home to colorful sponges, brain corals, and elkhorn corals, as well as occasional sightings of hawksbill turtles and nurse sharks. Macro enthusiasts may find various nudibranchs and small crustaceans hiding among the coral.

## Dive Profile

The dive begins with a gentle shore entry onto a sandy bottom that slopes gradually into the reef. Depths range from 5 to 18 meters, making it suitable for extended bottom time for beginners. The reef formation consists of coral heads and swim-throughs that provide shelter for numerous fish species. Divers are advised to follow the reef contour clockwise to maximize marine life viewing and avoid disturbing the coral.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is straightforward from the shore, with a short walk from the parking area to the water's edge. The seabed near the shore is sandy, easing entry and exit. Exiting the site involves retracing the entry path, with minimal surface current expected. Divers should watch for occasional waves or surge during windy conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect the delicate coral ecosystem.  
- Use a surface marker buoy when diving alone or in small groups for safety.  
- Cameras with macro capabilities are recommended for capturing small reef creatures.  
- Avoid touching or standing on corals to preserve the reef health.  

## Safety Considerations

Currents are generally mild to none, making it a safe site for beginners. However, divers should always dive within their certification level and monitor air consumption due to the length of the dive. The sandy bottom near the shore may have sea urchins, so sturdy footwear during entry and exit is advisable.

## Photography

Punt Vierkant offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography due to clear water and abundant coral formations. Early morning dives provide the best lighting conditions. Macro photography is rewarding around coral heads where small critters dwell.
