## Jeannie's Glory

Jeannie's Glory is a well-known reef dive site located off the coast of Bonaire, offering divers a vibrant underwater landscape with healthy coral formations and abundant marine life.

## Overview

Jeannie's Glory is part of Bonaire's celebrated marine park, recognized for its excellent coral reefs and clear waters. The site features a gently sloping reef with a variety of coral species and sponges, providing a rich habitat for diverse fish and invertebrates. The dive site is popular for its easy access from shore and typically calm conditions, making it a favorite among intermediate divers. Visibility is generally good, and the site benefits from minimal current, allowing relaxed exploration of the reef’s nooks and crannies.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Bonaire, southern Caribbean Sea, near the southern coast
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild to none
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal conditions from April to August

## Marine Life

Divers at Jeannie's Glory can expect to see a diverse array of Caribbean reef fish such as angelfish, parrotfish, and damselfish. The site is also home to hawksbill turtles, spotted eagle rays, and occasional sightings of seahorses and octopuses among the coral heads. The reef’s corals provide shelter for numerous invertebrates including colorful sponges, sea fans, and various crustaceans.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically starts from the shore, descending along the sloping reef to a maximum depth of about 25 meters. Divers can follow the contours of the reef, exploring coral formations and small swim-throughs formed by overhanging ledges. The topography is gentle, suited for a relaxed dive profile with plenty of opportunities for photo stops and marine life observation. No penetration or wreck diving is involved at this site. Divers should monitor air consumption closely due to the site's depth range.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from the shore via a sandy beach area with easy access to the water. Divers usually walk in with gear and perform a giant stride or controlled entry into calm waters. Exit is similarly straightforward, with a short swim back to shore. The area is generally free of strong surface currents or hazards, making it accessible for well-prepared divers.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB) for added safety, especially during busy periods.
- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the fragile coral ecosystem.
- Bring an underwater camera to capture the abundant marine life and coral formations.
- Early morning dives may offer the best visibility and less crowded conditions.

## Safety Considerations

While currents are typically mild, divers should always check local conditions before entry. The depth reaches up to 25 meters, so appropriate certification for intermediate depth is recommended. Avoid touching or disturbing the coral and marine life to preserve the ecosystem. Standard shore entry precautions apply, including watch for slippery rocks and entry conditions.

## Photography

Jeannie's Glory offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography as well as macro shots of small critters such as seahorses and crustaceans. The clear water and bright coral colors are best captured in the morning light when sunlight penetrates the water most directly. Overhangs and swim-throughs provide interesting compositions for photographers.
