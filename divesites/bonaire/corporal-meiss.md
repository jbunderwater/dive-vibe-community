## Corporal Meiss

Corporal Meiss is a popular shore-accessible reef dive site located on Bonaire’s western coast, known for vibrant coral formations and abundant marine life in clear, calm waters.

## Overview

Corporal Meiss is situated along Bonaire’s protected marine park coast, offering a well-structured reef environment with a gently sloping profile descending to about 25 meters. The site features a healthy mix of hard and soft corals, sponges, and numerous small crevices and swim-throughs. It is named after a historical figure related to Bonaire’s past, adding cultural interest. Conditions are typically calm with mild currents, making it accessible for intermediate divers. The reef’s layout includes sandy patches interspersed with coral bommies and ledges, providing habitat diversity.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Western coast of Bonaire, near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate  
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters  
- **Current**: Mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with best conditions from April to August  

## Marine Life

Divers at Corporal Meiss encounter a rich variety of tropical reef species including parrotfish, angelfish, grunts, and damselfish. Notably, the site hosts hawksbill turtles and occasional eagle rays. Macro life such as shrimp, nudibranchs, and various crustaceans is commonly spotted in reef crevices. The vibrant coral cover supports a healthy ecosystem, with colorful sponges and sea fans adding to the visual appeal.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive starts at the shore entry and progresses along the reef slope, following the contours of coral bommies and sandy bottoms. Depth gradually increases to around 25 meters, with swim-throughs and overhangs providing interesting exploration spots. The site has no wreck penetration but offers sheltered areas ideal for practicing buoyancy and reef navigation. Divers should monitor air supply carefully due to the depth and remain aware of mild currents pushing along the reef.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is via a gradual sandy beach, making it straightforward for divers to gear up and enter the water with ease. The shore entry requires a short swim to reach the main reef line. Exit is the same way, with attention to wave action on the shore depending on weather. There are no significant entry hazards, and surface currents are usually minimal.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Carry a reef hook for mild current sections if needed.  
- Bring a dive light to explore crevices and swim-throughs better.  
- Shore entry means you can make multiple dives without boat logistics.  
- Respect Bonaire’s marine park rules to help protect the reef.  

## Safety Considerations

While currents are generally mild, divers should remain vigilant of changing conditions and avoid overextending on depth and time. The reef’s structure includes some sharp coral edges, so careful finning and good buoyancy control are important to avoid injury or environmental damage. This site is suitable for divers with at least intermediate certification and open water experience.

## Photography

Corporal Meiss offers excellent underwater photography opportunities with vibrant coral gardens and a variety of reef fish. Early morning dives provide the best natural light and calmer water for clear shots. Macro photographers will find ample subjects in the reef’s nooks, including small crustaceans and nudibranchs.
