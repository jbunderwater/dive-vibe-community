## Vista Blue

Vista Blue is a popular shore-accessible reef dive site located on the west coast of Bonaire, known for its vibrant coral formations and abundant marine life within moderate depth ranges.

## Overview

Vista Blue is a classic Bonaire reef dive, favored for its accessibility and diverse underwater topography. The site features a gently sloping reef that descends to about 25 meters, with a variety of hard and soft corals, sponges, and numerous swim-throughs. The site benefits from Bonaire’s protected marine environment and generally calm conditions, making it suitable for divers with some experience. Visibility is typically excellent, ranging from 15 to 30 meters, offering clear views of the colorful reef and resident marine species.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West coast of Bonaire, near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate  
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters  
- **Current**: Mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with peak visibility from April to August  

## Marine Life

Divers at Vista Blue can expect to see an array of tropical fish such as parrotfish, angelfish, and damselfish. The reef is home to turtles, nurse sharks, eagle rays, and occasionally spotted eagle rays. Macro life includes frogfish, seahorses, and various nudibranchs. The healthy coral formations support diverse invertebrate populations, and the site is known for occasional sightings of seahorses in the seagrass areas near the reef edges.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically starts with a gentle slope entry from shore, descending along the reef wall. Depths vary from shallow coral gardens at 5 meters down to a maximum of 25 meters. The route includes swim-throughs formed by coral overhangs and small caves, offering interesting swim experiences. Divers should maintain buoyancy control to avoid contact with delicate corals. The profile allows for a leisurely pace while observing marine life both near the bottom and in midwater. No penetration beyond swim-throughs is recommended.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is directly from the shore via a sandy beach area with easy access. Divers should watch for rocky patches on entry but once in the water, the reef is close by and clearly marked with mooring buoys. Exit is typically at the same beach location. Surface currents are generally mild, but divers should remain aware of wind-driven waves and currents on the shore.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the coral ecosystem.  
- Bring a dive light for better viewing inside swim-throughs and overhangs.  
- Shore entry can be slippery; water shoes or reef boots are recommended.  
- Early morning dives offer the best visibility and less boat traffic.  

## Safety Considerations

- Moderate depth and mild currents require divers to have at least intermediate certification.  
- Maintain good buoyancy to avoid damaging coral and prevent injury from sharp reef structures.  
- Watch for sudden changes in surface conditions near shore.  
- No strong currents reported, but always check local conditions before diving.

## Photography

Vista Blue offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography and macro shots of the diverse marine life. The clear water and vibrant coral provide vivid colors, especially in the morning light. Swim-throughs and overhangs create interesting compositions but require good buoyancy and lighting. Using a strobe or video light enhances shots inside shaded areas.
