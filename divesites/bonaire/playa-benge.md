## Playa Benge

Playa Benge is a popular shore-accessible reef dive site located on the western coast of Bonaire, known for its calm conditions and vibrant marine life, making it ideal for beginner divers.

## Overview

Playa Benge is a well-loved shore dive spot situated near Kralendijk on Bonaire’s west coast. The site features a gently sloping reef with a sandy bottom and abundant coral formations. It is characterized by calm, clear waters with typically mild currents, making it suitable for beginner-level divers and snorkelers. The reef extends from shallow waters down to approximately 18 meters, offering a variety of coral species and marine life. Playa Benge’s accessibility and protected location within Bonaire’s marine park contribute to its popularity and excellent underwater visibility.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Bonaire, near Kralendijk on the west coast of the island
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with calmer seas generally from April to August

## Marine Life

Divers at Playa Benge can expect to encounter a diverse array of reef fish such as angelfish, parrotfish, damselfish, and grunts. The coral formations support sponges, sea fans, and occasional sightings of green sea turtles. Macro life includes spotted jawfish and various crustaceans. The site is also known for its peaceful ambiance, allowing for good chances to observe marine life undisturbed.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically starts at the sandy beach entry, descending gradually along the reef slope. Depth ranges from 3 to 18 meters, suitable for extended bottom times with minimal decompression concerns. The reef features scattered coral heads with swim-throughs and small overhangs but no wrecks or walls. Divers are advised to follow the reef contour and avoid touching corals to preserve the ecosystem. The gentle current allows for a relaxed drift back toward shore.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is via an easy sandy beach with a gradual water slope. Divers walk into the water until they reach the reef edge and then descend. Exit is the same way, typically a straightforward swim back to shore. There are no significant surface currents or hazards, making it accessible and safe for novice divers.

## Tips and Recommendations
- Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect the delicate coral environment.
- Early morning dives often provide the best visibility and calmest conditions.
- Carry an underwater slate or guide to help identify common fish species.
- Stay close to shore and within your certification limits, as currents can occasionally increase.
- Respect the marine park rules, including no touching or collecting marine life.

## Safety Considerations

Currents are generally mild, but divers should be aware of occasional shifts in direction. The site’s maximum depth is suitable for beginner divers, but always monitor air consumption and depth limits. There is minimal risk of entanglement or overhead environments. Certified divers should always dive with a buddy and maintain good buoyancy control to protect the reef.

## Photography

Playa Benge offers excellent opportunities for underwater photography due to its clear waters and colorful reef life. Macro photographers will find interesting small critters among the coral heads, while wide-angle shots capture the expansive reef and schooling fish. Morning light improves color vibrancy and reduces backscatter.
