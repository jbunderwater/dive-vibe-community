## Pink Beach

Pink Beach is a popular shore dive site located in Bonaire, known for its vibrant coral reefs and calm, clear waters ideal for beginner divers.

## Overview

Pink Beach is situated on the southern coast of Bonaire and is named for the pinkish hue of the sand found on the shore. The site features a gently sloping reef that starts shallow and gradually deepens to around 18 meters. It is well-known for its healthy coral formations and easy conditions, making it an excellent spot for new divers and snorkelers. The reef is protected by Bonaire’s marine park regulations, ensuring good visibility and abundant marine life. Conditions here are typically calm, with mild currents and consistently warm water temperatures.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Southern Bonaire, near Kralendijk
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: April to November (dry season)

## Marine Life

Divers at Pink Beach can expect to see a variety of Caribbean marine species including parrotfish, angelfish, trumpetfish, squirrelfish, and barracudas. The reef hosts healthy coral species such as elkhorn and staghorn corals, along with sponges and sea fans. Occasional sightings of sea turtles and rays are possible. The site is also known for its colorful reef fish and small critters, making it excellent for underwater photography.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive at Pink Beach starts from the sandy shore, moving outwards along the reef. Depths range from 3 to 18 meters, making it suitable for extended bottom times for beginners. The reef gently slopes with some coral heads and swim-throughs, but no significant walls or wrecks. Divers should follow the reef contour, observing marine life and coral formations. There are no penetration dives here, and the site offers a relaxed, non-technical diving experience.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from the sandy beach with a gradual slope into the water. There is no boat access typically used here. Exiting is straightforward, retracing the route back to shore. The sandy bottom provides easy footing, and currents are generally mild, so surface swimming is comfortable. Divers should watch for occasional surge during windy conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect the delicate coral ecosystem.
- Use a surface marker buoy (SMB) as a safety precaution in case of boat traffic.
- Early morning dives offer the best visibility and calmer seas.
- Respect marine park rules and avoid touching the coral or disturbing wildlife.

## Safety Considerations

Currents are usually mild but can pick up during windy days, so monitor conditions before diving. Maximum depth is shallow but still requires proper buoyancy control to avoid damaging coral. No overhead environments or strong currents, so suitable for beginner certification levels. Always dive with a buddy and keep an eye on air supply near the shore exit.

## Photography

Pink Beach offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography with vibrant corals and schooling fish. Macro enthusiasts can find small critters like gobies and shrimp among the coral branches. Early morning light enhances colors and visibility, providing optimal conditions for underwater shots.
