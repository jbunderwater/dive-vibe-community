# Atlantis

*A southern Bonaire shore reef on the southwest coast, in the quiet stretch between Margate Bay and Red Beryl — intermediate conditions with typical southern reef diving.*

## Overview

Atlantis is a southern Bonaire shore site on the southwest coast, south of Margate Bay and north of Red Beryl. The site is in a less-visited stretch of the southern coast and offers quiet fringing reef diving with the healthy coral typical of the Bonaire National Marine Park's protected reef belt. The intermediate difficulty reflects the moderate exposure of the southwest coast.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southwest Bonaire
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Morning

## Marine Life

Southern Bonaire reef species: parrotfish, angelfish, grunts, snapper, groupers, and turtles.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry and gradual reef slope to 30 metres. Most productive at 8–22 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the road. Exit at the same point.

## Tips

- Quiet, less-visited site — good choice for escaping the busy central corridor

## Safety

Intermediate depth and some current exposure. Standard precautions.

## Photography

Clear southern water with good reef photography opportunities.

## Nearby Sites

Margate Bay is to the north. Red Beryl is to the south. Vista Blue is further south.
