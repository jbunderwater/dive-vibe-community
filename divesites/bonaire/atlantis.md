## Atlantis

Atlantis is a popular, shallow reef dive site located off the coast of Bonaire, known for its calm conditions and abundant marine life, ideal for beginner divers and snorkelers alike.

## Overview

Atlantis is situated near Bonaire’s western shore and is characterized by a gently sloping reef with coral formations and sandy patches. The site offers easy access from shore, making it a favorite among divers seeking a relaxed dive with good visibility and minimal current. The site features a variety of coral species and typical Caribbean reef fish, providing a classic Bonaire diving experience. Visibility is generally excellent year-round, with mild currents that rarely pose a challenge.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bonaire, near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner  
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters  
- **Current**: Mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal conditions from April to December  

## Marine Life

Divers at Atlantis can expect to see a variety of tropical reef fish such as parrotfish, angelfish, damselfish, grunts, and sergeant majors. The reef supports healthy hard and soft corals, sponges, and occasional sightings of sea turtles and rays. While no rare species are specifically noted at this site, the biodiversity is typical of Bonaire’s protected reefs.

## Dive Profile

The dive at Atlantis is typically a relaxed drift or reef dive along a shallow to moderate slope, descending to a maximum of about 20 meters. The reef structure includes coral heads and sandy patches, with opportunities to explore swim-throughs and overhangs formed by coral growth. The site is well-suited for a 45-60 minute dive with a gradual ascent and safety stop in shallow water.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from the shore via a gentle sandy beach area with minimal entry hazards. Divers walk a short distance into the water and can descend immediately to the reef. Exit is similarly straightforward, with no significant surface currents or hazards reported. The site is accessible with basic shore diving equipment and does not require boat support.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring a reef hook or buddy line if diving during mild currents.  
- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect Bonaire’s marine environment.  
- Carry an underwater slate or marker to communicate easily in low current conditions.  
- Early morning dives often offer the calmest waters and best visibility.  

## Safety Considerations

Currents are generally mild but can increase slightly with weather changes; remain aware of your surroundings. Depth is moderate but always monitor air consumption and no-decompression limits. The reef has some overhangs and swim-throughs—avoid any tight penetration without proper training. Basic open water certification is sufficient for this site.

## Photography

Atlantis offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography due to its clear waters and vibrant coral formations. Early morning light enhances colors, and the abundance of reef fish provides dynamic subjects. Macro photographers can find small critters among the corals, though this is not a specialized macro site.
