## Boka Bartol

Boka Bartol is a popular shore-entry reef dive site located on the west coast of Bonaire, known for its easy accessibility and vibrant coral formations. It offers a moderate depth and is suitable for intermediate divers seeking diverse marine life in calm waters.

## Overview

Boka Bartol is situated on Bonaire’s leeward west coast, a region renowned for excellent shore diving due to protected conditions. The site features a gently sloping reef that extends from shallow coral gardens to a maximum depth around 25 meters. Its layout includes extensive coral formations with swim-throughs and ledges, making it an engaging dive for those comfortable with moderate depths. The site benefits from Bonaire’s typically calm waters and good visibility, offering a safe and scenic environment for divers. Conditions are generally stable, with mild currents that add interest without posing significant challenges.

## Site Information
- **Location**: West coast of Bonaire, near the town of Kralendijk
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with optimal visibility in the dry season (February to June)

## Marine Life

Divers can expect to see a wide variety of Caribbean reef fish such as angelfish, parrotfish, grunts, and damselfish. The reef hosts colorful corals, sponges, and occasional sightings of sea turtles and rays. Small reef sharks may be spotted occasionally but are generally non-aggressive. The site’s swim-throughs and overhangs provide shelter for lobsters and crustaceans. The biodiversity is typical of Bonaire’s healthy reef systems, offering good opportunities for observing reef ecology.

## Dive Profile

The recommended dive profile begins with a gradual descent along the reef slope to explore coral heads and swim-throughs between 5 and 25 meters. Divers typically follow the contour of the reef, circling back toward shore after reaching the maximum depth. There are no wreck penetrations, but swim-throughs and ledges add interest. The dive is suitable for those with good buoyancy control due to the reef’s vertical structures. Ascents should be slow and controlled, with safety stops generally performed at 5 meters for 3 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is made directly from the shore via a gently sloping rocky beach. Divers should watch for slippery rocks and wear appropriate footwear to protect against sharp coral rubble. The entry is straightforward with no strong surface currents, making it accessible for most divers. Exit is usually from the same point as entry, requiring a short swim back along the reef slope.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Carry reef-safe sunscreen to protect the coral ecosystem.
- Bring a dive flag to signal boats in the area.
- Check local tide and weather conditions before diving.
- Use a reef hook if planning to stay stationary in mild currents.
- Respect marine life and avoid touching corals or disturbing animals.

## Safety Considerations

Divers should be aware of occasional mild currents and ensure they have proper buoyancy control to avoid contact with the reef. Depths can reach 25 meters, so appropriate certification beyond beginner level is recommended. There are minimal entanglement hazards, but caution near swim-throughs is advised. Always dive with a buddy and follow local dive operator guidelines.

## Photography

Boka Bartol offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle shots of coral formations and reef landscapes. The clear water and abundant fish provide good conditions for macro and close-up photography of reef creatures. Early morning dives often yield the best light and calmest water for photography.
