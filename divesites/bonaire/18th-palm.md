# 18th Palm

*A central Bonaire fringing reef named for a row of palm trees marking the shore entry — an easy beginner dive in the busy central dive belt.*

## Overview

18th Palm (BNMP #34) is a central west coast shore dive named for the palm trees visible from the road that mark the entry point. It lies in the busy stretch of diving between Calabas Reef and Windsock, offering typical central Bonaire fringing reef conditions: easy entry, calm water, and a gradual reef slope. The site is well-suited to beginner divers and those wanting a relaxed, uncomplicated dive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Central Bonaire, BNMP Site #34
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 metres
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Usually light
- **Best Time**: Morning to afternoon

## Marine Life

Central Bonaire reef species: parrotfish, grunts, angelfish, snapper, and groupers. Turtles seen along the coast. Moray eels in reef crevices.

## Dive Profile

Entry over sand/shore and gradual swim to the reef. Reef begins within a short swim and slopes to 25 metres. A relaxed out-and-back profile.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the pull-off near the palm trees. Exit at the same point.

## Tips

- The palm trees make this entry easy to identify from the coastal road
- Good between-sites dive when touring the central corridor

## Safety

Easy conditions. Standard shore-dive precautions.

## Photography

Standard reef photography in good visibility.

## Nearby Sites

Calabas Reef (Divi Flamingo house reef) is to the south. Windsock is nearby to the north. Bachelor's Beach is also close.
