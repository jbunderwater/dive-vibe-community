## Alice in Wonderland

Alice in Wonderland is a well-known shore-accessible reef dive site located on the western coast of Bonaire, featuring vibrant coral formations and diverse marine life in clear, calm waters.

## Overview

This site is named for its maze-like coral formations that resemble a fantastical underwater landscape, reminiscent of the story "Alice in Wonderland." The reef is characterized by a mix of shallow coral gardens and deeper drop-offs, making it a popular choice for intermediate divers. The area is protected within Bonaire’s marine park, ensuring healthy coral growth and abundant fish populations. Conditions are typically calm with good visibility, making it ideal for leisurely exploration of the reef’s topography and marine inhabitants.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Bonaire, west coast near Kralendijk  
- **Entry Type**: Shore  
- **Site Type**: Reef  
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate  
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters  
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters  
- **Current**: Mild  
- **Best Time**: Year-round, best during dry season (February to June)  

## Marine Life

Divers can expect to see a variety of tropical reef fish including parrotfish, angelfish, trumpetfish, and grunts. The reef also hosts sea turtles, eagle rays, and occasional spotted eagle rays. Macro life such as seahorses and frogfish can sometimes be spotted among the coral branches. The coral itself is vibrant with healthy hard and soft corals, sponges, and occasional barrel sponges.

## Dive Profile

A typical dive at Alice in Wonderland starts in shallow waters around 5 meters, progressing along the reef’s coral formations to depths approaching 25 meters. The route weaves through coral pinnacles and swim-throughs that offer interesting swim paths and shelter for marine life. Divers should take care to avoid damaging the delicate corals. No penetration into wrecks or caves is available here, but the swim-throughs and overhangs provide natural points of interest.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from shore via a gentle sandy slope leading into the reef area. Divers typically walk in with equipment or use a short fin swim to reach the reef. Exit is at the same location; currents are mild, minimizing drift concerns. The entry is straightforward without significant hazards, suitable for intermediate divers comfortable with shore entries.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Use reef-safe sunscreen to protect the marine environment.  
- Bring a dive light for better viewing inside swim-throughs and crevices.  
- Maintain good buoyancy control to avoid contact with the coral.  
- Early morning dives tend to have calmer conditions and better visibility.

## Safety Considerations

Mild currents can occur but are generally manageable for intermediate divers. The reef’s coral structures require careful navigation to avoid cuts and damage to the reef. Recommended certification level is Advanced Open Water or equivalent due to depth and entry type. Watch air consumption closely during deeper sections.

## Photography

Excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef shots with vibrant coral and schooling fish. Macro photographers will appreciate the opportunities to capture small critters like shrimp and seahorses in crevices. Early morning or late afternoon light enhances colors and reduces backscatter.
