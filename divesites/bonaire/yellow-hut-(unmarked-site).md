## Yellow Hut (unmarked site)

Yellow Hut is a popular unmarked shore dive site located on the western coast of Bonaire, known for its easy access and rich coral reef ecosystem.

## Overview

Yellow Hut is an unmarked shore dive site situated just north of the Kralendijk town area in Bonaire. The site features a gently sloping coral reef starting at shallow depths and extending to about 18 meters. It is favored by beginner divers due to its calm waters, easy entry from the shore, and excellent visibility. The reef is healthy and vibrant, offering a diverse underwater landscape with abundant hard and soft corals. Conditions are typically calm, making it a comfortable dive site year-round. The location is less crowded compared to some marked sites, providing a more relaxed diving experience.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Western coast of Bonaire, near Kralendijk
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: None to mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with calm mornings preferred

## Marine Life

The Yellow Hut reef supports a healthy population of reef fish such as sergeant majors, parrotfish, angelfish, and damselfish. Divers may also encounter spotted eagle rays, hawksbill turtles, and various species of nudibranchs. The coral formations are home to numerous invertebrates including colorful sponges and sea fans. Seahorses have been occasionally reported in nearby seagrass patches, making it a rewarding site for macro photographers.

## Dive Profile

Typical dives at Yellow Hut begin in shallow water around 3-5 meters, gradually descending along the reef slope to a maximum depth of approximately 18 meters. The reef is expansive and flat with some coral bommies and small swim-throughs. Divers are advised to maintain buoyancy control to avoid contact with the corals. The site is ideal for relaxed exploration and underwater photography. There are no wrecks or caves to penetrate.

## Entry and Exit

Divers enter Yellow Hut directly from the rocky shoreline, typically walking into waist-deep water before beginning the dive. Exit is from the same shore point, requiring a short swim back. The entry area is generally free of strong surge or waves, but care should be taken during windy conditions. There are no boat accesses here.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Arrive early in the morning for the best visibility and calm sea conditions.
- Bring reef-safe sunscreen and avoid touching the corals to preserve the ecosystem.
- Carry a dive flag if diving solo or with a small group to alert boaters.
- Use fins suitable for rocky entry to prevent slipping or injury.

## Safety Considerations

- Currents are typically mild or absent but monitor conditions before diving.
- Shallow coral formations require careful buoyancy to prevent contact injuries.
- Recommended for divers with basic open water certification or higher.
- No lifeguard or onsite dive professional; always dive with a buddy.

## Photography

Yellow Hut offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle shots of vibrant coral gardens and schooling reef fish. The clear waters and good light penetration make it suitable for both macro and wide photography. Early morning dives provide the best natural lighting with minimal backscatter.
