# Margate Bay

*A southern Bonaire shore reef on the southwest coast — intermediate conditions with a gradual slope and typical southern reef marine life.*

## Overview

Margate Bay is a southern Bonaire shore site on the southwest coast, south of Pink Beach. The site offers intermediate-depth fringing reef diving in the quieter southern stretch between Alice in Wonderland and the southern tip. The Papiamentu-influenced name reflects the mixed colonial heritage of Bonaire's place names.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southwest Bonaire, south of Pink Beach
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 metres
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: Morning

## Marine Life

Parrotfish, angelfish, grunts, snapper, and groupers. Turtles are seen along the southern coast. Occasional stingrays on the sandy bottom.

## Dive Profile

Shore entry and gradual reef slope to 30 metres. Most productive at 8–22 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the road. Exit at the same point.

## Tips

- Less visited than northern sites — good for a quiet southern dive
- Combine with Pink Beach or English Garden for a southern coast day

## Safety

Light-to-moderate current possible. Standard intermediate dive precautions.

## Photography

Standard southern reef photography.

## Nearby Sites

Pink Beach is to the north. English Garden is nearby. Atlantis is to the south.
