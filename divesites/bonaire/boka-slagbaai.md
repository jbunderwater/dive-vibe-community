## Boka Slagbaai

Boka Slagbaai is a popular shore dive site located within Washington Slagbaai National Park on Bonaire, known for its beautiful coral reefs and diverse marine life in clear, calm waters.

## Overview

Boka Slagbaai is situated in a protected marine area inside Bonaire’s Washington Slagbaai National Park, a well-preserved natural reserve. The site features a gently sloping reef with coral formations and sandy patches, making it accessible and attractive for divers of varying skill levels. Conditions are typically calm with mild currents, and visibility is often excellent. The area is renowned for its healthy coral ecosystems and abundant marine fauna, offering a classic Bonaire diving experience characterized by vibrant reefs and diverse tropical fish.

## Site Information
- **Location**: Washington Slagbaai National Park, Bonaire
- **Entry Type**: Shore
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-30 meters
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round, with best conditions in the dry season (January to May)

## Marine Life

Divers at Boka Slagbaai can expect to see a wide variety of Caribbean reef fish, including angelfish, parrotfish, and grunts. The reef supports healthy coral species and sponges, with occasional sightings of sea turtles and rays. Macro life such as nudibranchs and seahorses may also be spotted by attentive divers. The site’s protected status contributes to the abundance and diversity of marine life.

## Dive Profile

The dive typically starts from a sandy shore entry, descending onto a gently sloping reef that extends to about 25 meters deep. Divers can explore coral heads, swim-throughs, and sandy patches while enjoying good visibility. The reef structure offers natural swim-throughs and overhangs to explore, with a relatively easy swim back to shore. Depths are manageable for intermediate divers, and the mild current allows for a relaxed dive pace.

## Entry and Exit

Entry is from the shore via a sandy beach area with minimal surf, making it straightforward for divers. Exiting is usually done by swimming back along the reef to the original entry point. There are no significant hazards, but divers should be mindful of the sun exposure and bring adequate water and protection.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Bring reef-safe sunscreen to protect the coral ecosystem.
- Check park opening hours and regulations as the site is inside a national park.
- Shore entry can be sandy; water shoes or booties are recommended.
- Carry a dive flag or marker buoy for safety.
- Best visited in the morning for calm water and optimal visibility.

## Safety Considerations

- Mild currents may be present; monitor conditions before diving.
- Depths can reach 25 meters; appropriate training and experience are advised.
- As the site is within a protected park, follow all local regulations to preserve marine life.
- No penetration into caves or wrecks; dive within reef boundaries.

## Photography

Boka Slagbaai offers excellent opportunities for wide-angle reef photography thanks to its clear water and vibrant coral formations. Macro photographers will find interesting subjects such as nudibranchs and small reef fish. Early morning dives provide the best natural lighting conditions.
