## MV Mars

MV Mars is a small wooden cargo vessel that sank in a storm in April 2006 and rests upright on the sandy bottom northwest of Havelock Island at approximately 15 metres depth.

## Overview

The MV Mars is a small wooden cargo ship that sank during a storm in April 2006, coming to rest upright on the sandy bottom just off the fringing reef near Sir William Peel Island, northwest of Havelock Island. The wreck is intact and slightly tilted to port, with the wheelhouse roof rising to around 7 metres. Over the years the hull has become encrusted with soft corals and sponges, turning it into a small artificial reef. The shallow depth and protected position make this one of the most accessible wreck dives in the Andamans, suitable for beginners.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest of Havelock Island (Swaraj Dweep), approximately 4 km from shore, near Sir William Peel Island, Andaman Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Limited (typical for wreck sites in this area)
- **Current**: Mild
- **Best Time**: November to April

## Marine Life

Confirmed resident species at the wreck include cardinalfish, parrotfish, triggerfish, batfish, sweetlips, crocodilefish, seahorses, snappers, and fusiliers. The wooden structure and encrusting growth provide shelter for smaller reef fish. A nearby reef approximately 10 metres away offers additional exploration.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wreck at approximately 15 metres. The wheelhouse roof is at around 7 metres, providing a multi-level dive. Explore the exterior of the upright hull and the surrounding reef patch nearby. Begin ascent with sufficient reserve for a 5-metre safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by boat from Havelock Island dive operators. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during ascent for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Suitable for Open Water certified divers
- Bring a dive torch to illuminate the wreck interior and encrusting life
- Do not attempt penetration of the wooden hull without proper training and equipment
- The nearby reef is worth including in the dive plan

## Safety Considerations

Dive within your certification limits. Visibility can be limited at this wreck. Always dive with a buddy and carry an SMB.

## Photography

The small, intact wreck makes a good wide-angle subject, and the resident crocodilefish and seahorses offer macro opportunities on the hull.
