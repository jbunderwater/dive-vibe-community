## Payong

Payong is a reef dive site in Komodo National Park, East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia.

## Overview

Payong is a reef site in the central Komodo National Park area, positioned in the channel waters between Komodo and Rinca islands where strong bidirectional tidal currents are a defining feature. "Payong" means umbrella in Indonesian, a name likely referring to the broad, sheltering shape of the reef's main coral formations when viewed from the water. The site consists of a sloping reef extending from a shallow coral garden into a more current-swept lower section, giving divers options for both relaxed reef exploration and drift diving depending on tidal conditions.

The upper reef at 5–10 metres is densely colonised with hard corals, particularly table Acroporas and massive Porites colonies that have withstood decades of heavy tidal flow. Cleaner wrasse stations are active along the reef crest, attracting a steady parade of reef fish — surgeonfish, triggerfish, and wrasse dominate the scene. Deeper on the slope, sea fans emerge and the substrate transitions to a mix of broken coral rubble and coarser sand, good habitat for flatworms, mantis shrimp, and occasional reef octopus. The consistently productive reef structure and manageable depth range make Payong a reliable site for intermediate divers.

Tidal timing matters significantly here: the current in the central Komodo channel is among the strongest in the park. On peak tidal flow, only experienced current divers should attempt Payong; on slack or neap tides, the site relaxes considerably and is accessible to divers with moderate current experience.

## Key Species

- Mantis shrimp and reef octopus on sandy patches
- Cleaner wrasse stations with queuing reef fish
- Triggerfish (titan and clown) nesting on the shallower reef
- Moray eels sheltering in coral heads
- Schools of snappers and fusiliers mid-water

## Dive Profile

Entry is typically made upcurrent, allowing a drift along the reef crest before descending to explore the slope. Most divers stay between 8 and 20 metres, using the upper reef for the safety stop and to extend bottom time in the shallows. The slope provides a natural current refuge if conditions intensify.

## Tips for Divers

Dive Payong in the morning when visibility is clearest and the outgoing tide has not yet built to full strength. The site combines well with other central Komodo sites like Batu Bolong or Manta Point in a full-day boat itinerary. Watch for titan triggerfish around the 5–8 m reef crest, particularly during their nesting season (November–January), and give them a wide berth.
