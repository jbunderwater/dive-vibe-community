## Tatawa Kecil dive site

Tatawa Kecil (Small Tatawa) is one of Komodo's most intense drift dives — a narrow island in the central park whose flanks are swept by powerful currents, producing wall-to-wall soft coral and an almost overwhelming density of reef fish.

## Overview

Tatawa Kecil is a small island in the central section of Komodo National Park, its sloping reef sides channelling tidal flow into fierce currents that keep the coral vibrant and the fish life extraordinary. The site is typically dived as a drift along the southern or northern flank depending on tidal direction, descending to 20–30 meters on a steep slope before riding the current up to 5–10 meters and drifting to the boat. The coral cover here — both hard and soft — is considered among the best in Komodo. Advanced drift diving skills are required: the current can run at 2–3 knots and occasionally reverses without warning.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Komodo National Park, East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters
- **Current**: Strong to very strong; direction tide-dependent
- **Best Time**: April to November (dry season)

## Marine Life

The steep soft coral-draped slope hosts a spectacular mix of reef species. Schools of sergeant majors, anthias, and fusiliers create curtains of colour against the coral backdrop. Hawksbill turtles graze on sponges at mid-depth. White-tip and grey reef sharks patrol below 20 meters. Napoleon wrasse cruise through the current edge, and bumphead parrotfish occasionally pass in tight schools. Blue-ribbon eels peer from burrows in sandy patches between coral heads. At depth, large sea fans and black coral bushes harbour small critters including gobies and porcelain crabs.

## Dive Profile

Drop into the current on the upstream side of the island and let it carry you along the slope. The guide typically leads the group deeper early to take advantage of the richest coral terrain, then angles shallower as the dive progresses. A blue-water safety stop is common when the current is strong — inflate your SMB at 5 meters and let the boat track your position. In very strong flow, consider a reef hook on the first section to allow observation without exhausting effort.

## Entry and Exit

Liveaboard or day boat access. The skipper positions upstream and divers drop in together, descending immediately. Exit is via pickup from the boat after a blue-water safety stop — SMB deployment is essential.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A reef hook is a worthwhile addition to your kit for this site
- Dive the site early before the tide strengthens for the most manageable conditions
- Wide-angle photography is ideal — coral-draped slope with fish-packed water column
- The shallows at the island's tip are sometimes calm enough for a relaxed end to the dive
- Bring a 3mm wetsuit; thermoclines can occur in this area

## Safety Considerations

Strong and occasionally shifting currents are the primary hazard. Down-currents can develop on the downstream end of the island — experienced guides will brief on which side to avoid. Stay close to your buddy and the guide. Always carry an SMB and never ascend without deploying it in open water. This site is not appropriate for divers with less than 50 logged dives.

## Photography

Tatawa Kecil is a wide-angle dream: slopes blanketed in orange and purple soft corals, schools of fish backlit against blue water, and the occasional shark or turtle gliding through. The high fish density makes it easy to fill the frame — choose your exposure for the coral and let the fish come to you.
