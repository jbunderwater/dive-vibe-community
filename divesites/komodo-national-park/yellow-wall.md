## Yellow Wall

A dramatic wall along the east coast of Nusa Kode named for the profusion of bright yellow soft corals (Tubastrea) and robust sea cucumbers. Crevices harbour nudibranchs, cleaner shrimp, and decorator crabs, with clown triggerfish, fork-tailed fairy basslets, whitetip reef sharks, and green turtles observed along the face. Best dived in the afternoon for optimal light. Medium to strong currents.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East coast of Nusa Kode, southern Komodo National Park
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Depth Range**: 5–40 meters (richest life at 15–25 meters)
- **Current**: Medium to strong
- **Best Time**: Afternoon for optimal sunlight on the wall face
