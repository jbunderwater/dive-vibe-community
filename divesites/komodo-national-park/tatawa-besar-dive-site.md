## Tatawa Besar dive site

Pristine shallow reef with extensive coral plateau and vibrant fish life.

## Overview

Tatawa Besar is part of the Tatawa Island group and features one of Komodo's most pristine reefs. The site is a long, gently sloping plateau that extends from 3 meters near the shore down to about 14–15 meters before dropping into deeper waters. The reef is covered with healthy hard and soft corals, and the concentration of marine life is highest between 5–18 meters. Tatawa Besar is suitable for a relaxed dive at all experience levels and offers good visibility and reliable marine encounters.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Central Komodo, Tatawa Island group
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 meters (50–100 feet) dry season; 10–20m wet season
- **Current**: Mild to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October (dry season); year-round possible

## Marine Life

Large schooling fish (fusiliers, jacks, trevally), reef sharks, barracuda, groupers, snappers, napoleon wrasse, sweetlips, and parrotfish. The healthier coral areas support higher concentrations of reef fish and invertebrates, including nudibranchs and small crustaceans.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the plateau at 10–15 meters and cruise gently along the reef, staying in the zone where most marine life is concentrated (5–18 meters). Work the coral heads and crevices for macro subjects. The plateau extends far, so plan your route with the current to maximize air efficiency. Ascend gradually, finishing your safety stop in the shallower sections.

## Entry and Exit

Boat drop-off near the reef. Giant stride entry. Descend to the plateau and follow your guide's briefing. Exit via SMB deployment.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Approach fish schools slowly to avoid scattering them
- Spend time among the coral bommies for the highest marine life concentration
- Bring macro and wide-angle lenses for variety
- Scout the best coral heads early in the dive
- Plan air consumption for a comfortable descent and ascent
- A relaxed pace allows extended bottom time

## Safety Considerations

Currents are generally mild but can increase. Plan your dive profile to navigate with the current rather than against it. Maintain neutral buoyancy to protect the delicate coral. Avoid spooking fish unnecessarily. Standard decompression and safety stop procedures apply.

## Photography

Excellent reef composition with healthy coral and abundant fish life. Wide-angle work captures schooling behavior and reef topography. Macro subjects are plentiful in the coral heads. Good light in the 5–18-meter zone for color saturation.
