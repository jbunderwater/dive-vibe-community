## The Cauldron Dive Site

Technical drift dive through narrow channels and caves with accelerating current—expert only.

## Overview

The Cauldron (also called "The Shotgun") is one of Komodo's most challenging dives. Located on a small rocky island, the site is a series of coral-covered rocks, caves, and narrow channels forming a whirlpool-like eddy when current flows at the right phase. Divers enter in a coral garden during slack or beginning tidal flow, then ride the accelerating current through the narrow passages until reaching maximum speed at "the shotgun"—a final accelerating point where the current peaks. The dive is tidal-dependent and only diveable on falling tides. This is purely an advanced/expert site requiring perfect buoyancy control, comfort with current, and cave diving awareness. Not suitable for most divers.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Central Komodo, small rocky island
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–25 meters (50–80 feet)
- **Current**: Slack to extremely strong; tidal-driven and site-critical
- **Best Time**: May to October; diveable only on favorable falling tide days

## Marine Life

Coral gardens at the site entrance host reef fish and small invertebrates. As current picks up, larger pelagic fish (jacks, trevally, barracuda) may be present. The cave passages shelter colorful soft corals, sea fans, and small reef fish.

## Dive Profile

Divers descend into a coral garden (initial depth 10–15 meters) and follow the underwater topography through narrow passages and around rock formations. As the tidal current begins flowing, the divers start drifting faster through progressively narrower passages. The dive culminates at "the shotgun"—a final acceleration point—where current may reach 3–4 knots or more. Buoyancy control is absolutely critical; any mistake results in collision with rocks or loss of control. Most divers ride the current passively with perfect weighting and streamlined position.

## Entry and Exit

Giant stride entry into the coral garden. The drift naturally completes the dive—you exit in fast current via SMB deployment far from the site. Timing and coordination with the dive boat are critical.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Only attempt with experienced Komodo guides
- Perfect buoyancy control is non-negotiable
- Use a dive computer to manage depth in the current
- Maintain streamlined equipment and positioning
- Do not fight the current; ride it
- Consider this a bucket-list goal dive, not a casual experience
- Expect adrenaline rush and genuine risk
- Practice neutral buoyancy thoroughly first

## Safety Considerations

This is a technical current dive with hazards (rocks, caves, high-speed flow). Strong current can cause loss of orientation and buoyancy problems. The narrow passages leave no room for error. Hypothermia is unlikely, but panic is a real risk in unprepared divers. Absolutely require experienced guide and proper pre-dive briefing. Have clear abort signals with your guide. Many Komodo operators do NOT offer this dive due to liability.

## Photography

Difficult to impossible during the dive due to speed and need for buoyancy control. Some operators document the dive with go-pro style video. Not a dive for photography.
