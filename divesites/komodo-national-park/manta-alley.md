## Manta Alley

Manta Alley is a rocky channel at the far southern tip of Komodo Island where reef manta rays aggregate at cleaning stations, with multiple mantas common on a single dive during peak season. Strong currents and cold thermoclines make this a challenging but rewarding dive.

## Overview

The site consists of shallow rocky sections and a small channel cut through rock where mantas feed and use cleaning stations. The shallower areas host cleaning stations at 5–15 metres; sandy channels descend to around 20–25 metres, with a maximum depth of 40 metres. Water temperature is notably cooler here than at northern Komodo sites, typically 22–23°C, which can make for cold dives.

**Peak season note: Sources conflict.** [DiveHappy](https://divehappy.com/manta-alley-komodo/) and [Dragon Dive Komodo](https://dragondivekomodo.com/en/top-10-best-dive-sites-of-komodo/) both report the primary aggregation season as September to January, driven by nutrient-rich southern currents. The site's original description cited November to March as peak season. Both windows overlap (September–March covers both), so planning for October through January is a safe range. Mantas can be seen year-round, though sightings are less reliable from February to August.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southern tip of Komodo Island, Komodo National Park, East Nusa Tenggara, Indonesia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Drift dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Depth**: 5–25 meters (mantas concentrate at shallower cleaning stations)
- **Water Temperature**: 22–23°C (cooler than northern Komodo sites)
- **Current**: Strong; variable direction

## Marine Life

Reef manta rays at cleaning stations. Giant trevally, sweetlips, and mobula rays are also reported. The cooler, nutrient-rich water supports invertebrate communities typical of southern Komodo.
