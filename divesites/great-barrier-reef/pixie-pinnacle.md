## Pixie Pinnacle

Pixie Pinnacle is a celebrated macro dive site in the Ribbon Reefs section of the northern Great Barrier Reef, Queensland, Australia.

## Overview

Pixie Pinnacle is a slender coral bommie rising from a sand and rubble bottom at 18 metres up to within a few metres of the surface. What sets it apart is density rather than scale — every square centimetre of the pinnacle is colonised by soft corals, sea fans, encrusting sponges, and tunicates in purples, oranges, and yellows. Divers routinely describe it as one of the best macro dive sites in the entire Indo-Pacific, and its reputation is well-founded.

The site is calm, shallow, and easily navigated, making it genuinely accessible to recently certified divers while offering the kind of subject matter that keeps experienced photographers occupied for multiple dives.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ribbon Reefs, northern Great Barrier Reef, Queensland, Australia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 metres
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: July to November

## Marine Life

Pixie Pinnacle is the GBR's premier site for pygmy seahorses, with multiple individuals reliably found on the sea fans near the base of the pinnacle. Nudibranchs are abundant and varied — ghost pipefish, seamoths, and juvenile frogfish have all been documented here. Clownfish inhabit the anemones tucked into the coral, while pipefish weave through soft coral branches. Glassfish schools drift around the upper pinnacle in shimmering clouds, attracting small trevally and fusiliers. On the sand at the base, mantis shrimp and flatworms add further variety for methodical macro hunters.

## Dive Profile

The pinnacle rises from 18 metres to roughly 4 metres depth. Most of the macro life is concentrated in the 8–15 metre band where soft coral coverage is most intense. A single circumnavigation of the bommie typically takes 30–40 minutes; multiple circuits at different depths are rewarding given the site's richness. The sand patch around the base is worth exploring for critters before ascending.

## Entry and Exit

Liveaboard access only from Cairns or Port Douglas; the site is approximately 220km north of Cairns. Shot line descent to the pinnacle top is typical. Exit via SMB with boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Carry a focus light or torch — the soft coral canopy creates shadows that hide excellent subjects
- Slow down: the best macro finds at Pixie Pinnacle require patience and a systematic approach
- Ask your dive guide to point out pygmy seahorses — they are tiny and camouflaged against the sea fan branches
- Night dives here are exceptional; octopuses, crabs, and sleeping fish round out the diversity
- A macro lens (60mm or 100mm equivalent) is ideal, though a wet diopter opens up the smallest subjects

## Safety Considerations

Conditions are generally benign. The main hazard is losing your sense of time — the site is engaging enough that air consumption goes unnoticed. Monitor your SPG regularly. Surface traffic around liveaboard moorings requires a visible SMB on ascent.

## Photography

Pixie Pinnacle may be the single best macro photography site on the GBR. The diversity of subjects — pygmy seahorses, nudibranchs, ghost pipefish, clownfish, glassfish schools — means every dive produces publishable images. Shoot the wide soft coral structures first for establishing shots, then work systematically through the smaller subjects. The ambient light in the 8–12 metre zone is excellent during mid-morning dives.
