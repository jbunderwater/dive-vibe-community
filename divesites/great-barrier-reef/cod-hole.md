## Cod Hole

Cod Hole is one of the most famous dive sites in the world, located at Ribbon Reef No. 10 in the northern Great Barrier Reef, Queensland, Australia.

## Overview

Cod Hole earned its legendary status through decades of fish-feeding encounters with giant potato cod — massive groupers that can reach 100kg and grow to nearly two metres in length. These fish have been interacting with divers since the 1970s and are completely unafraid, approaching within touching distance and hovering motionlessly in front of your mask. On a typical dive, 10 to 20 individual potato cod may be present simultaneously, an experience unlike anything else in Australian waters.

The site itself is a coral pinnacle rising from the sandy seafloor to around 8 metres depth, surrounded by sloping reef and rubble at 25 metres. Though the feeding program has been discontinued in recent years, the cod remain resident and habituated to divers, continuing to seek out human visitors with uncanny boldness.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Ribbon Reef No. 10, northern Great Barrier Reef, Queensland, Australia
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–40 metres
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; can be stronger on the outgoing tide
- **Best Time**: July to November (optimal conditions and visibility)

## Marine Life

Potato cod are the headline act, but Cod Hole also holds a resident Napoleon (humphead) maori wrasse that matches the cod for size and audacity. Moray eels — including giant morays — emerge from crevices along the pinnacle. Coral trout patrol the reef perimeter, while schools of batfish, sweetlips, and snapper fill the mid-water. At depth on the sandy bottom, whitetip reef sharks rest in loose groups. Occasional hammerheads have been reported on the deeper approaches. The pinnacle itself is decorated with sea fans, soft corals, and encrusting sponges that attract nudibranchs and smaller invertebrates.

## Dive Profile

Descend directly onto the pinnacle, which tops out at around 8 metres. Most divers spend the bulk of the dive between 10 and 18 metres, where the cod congregate along the pinnacle walls and overhangs. The sandy bottom at 25 metres offers a brief exploration before ascending back up the pinnacle for the safety stop in the shallows. Currents are typically manageable but become stronger on the outgoing tide — arrive on the slack for best conditions.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by liveaboard from Cairns or Port Douglas. The site sits approximately 240km north of Cairns and is not reachable by day-trip vessels. Descent is by shot line or free descent to the pinnacle top. Exit via surface marker buoy (SMB) and boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Approach the potato cod slowly and let them come to you — they will
- Do not attempt to touch or feed the cod; respectful observation produces better encounters
- Wide-angle photography is the standard here, but get close — the fish will cooperate
- A liveaboard itinerary combining Cod Hole with Pixie Pinnacle and the Osprey Reef sites is the classic northern GBR trip
- Bring a torch for the pinnacle crevices

## Safety Considerations

Currents can strengthen rapidly with tidal changes. Monitor depth carefully when following the cod down the pinnacle walls. Box jellyfish are present in inshore and mid-reef waters during the November–April wet season. Always deploy an SMB on ascent — liveaboard traffic in the Ribbon Reefs can be significant.

## Photography

Cod Hole is a wide-angle dream. The sheer size of the potato cod demands a fisheye or rectilinear wide-angle lens to capture them in full. Shoot upward with ambient light behind the pinnacle for dramatic silhouettes, or use strobes close in to reveal the cod's spotted patterns. The Napoleon wrasse, coral trout, and schooling fish provide constant compositional variety throughout the dive.
