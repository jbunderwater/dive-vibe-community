## SS Lord Strathcona

SS Lord Strathcona is a WWII iron ore carrier sunk by German U-boat U-513 off Bell Island — a magnificent deep wreck with intact superstructure, deck gun, and vibrant cold-water marine life.

## Overview

On September 5, 1942, U-513 attacked Bell Island a second time, striking the SS Lord Strathcona with two torpedoes as she lay at anchor in Wabana Roads. Unlike the Saganaga which sank in under a minute, the Lord Strathcona's crew had witnessed the earlier attack and were ready — all 44 crew survived the sinking, abandoning ship before she went under. The wreck now lies stern at 27–37 meters (90–120 feet), making her somewhat deeper than Saganaga and PLM-27, and appropriate for Advanced Open Water certified divers with drysuit experience. The superstructure is described as "magnificent" and well preserved, with swim-throughs, underwater hallways, and the deck gun as standout features.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bell Island, Conception Bay, Newfoundland
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Stern Depth**: 27–37 meters (90–120 feet)
- **Maximum Depth**: 37 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 12–30 meters (40–100 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 2–6°C (36–43°F) — drysuit mandatory
- **Current**: Minimal; Conception Bay is well protected
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

White, orange, and gold plumose anemones coat the hull and superstructure, creating a spectacular cold-water garden. Purple sea stars and pink lion's mane jellyfish drift through the water column near the wreck. Red-phase lumpfish are regularly seen spawning on the hull during spring months — a remarkable seasonal behaviour unique to cold North Atlantic wrecks. Wolffish, cod, and nudibranchs shelter in the structural overhangs.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the deck and explore the well-preserved superstructure with intact swim-throughs and corridor-like internal passages. The deck gun is a highlight and popular photography subject. The stern sits at the deepest point, requiring careful depth monitoring and planning. No-decompression limits at 37 meters are short — plan your dive profile carefully and begin your ascent with a generous reserve. Plan a 5-meter safety stop before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boats depart from Bell Island (accessible by ferry from Portugal Cove, Newfoundland). The wreck is moored. Entry is giant stride or back roll from the dive boat. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Drysuit certification is mandatory — water stays near 2–6°C year-round
- Cold-water regulators (rated to -10°C) are essential
- Deeper than PLM-27 and Saganaga — plan bottom time carefully for NDL at 37m
- Visit in spring (May–June) for a chance to see spawning lumpfish on the hull
- Torch essential for swim-throughs and interior exploration
- The deck gun and "magnificent" superstructure are the standout features of this wreck

## Safety Considerations

The deeper depth of Lord Strathcona compared to PLM-27 and Saganaga significantly reduces no-decompression limits. Water temperature near freezing accelerates air consumption and physiological cold stress. Drysuit and quality undersuit are mandatory. Plan conservatively, carry a redundant gas source for the deeper sections, and always dive with a buddy. Deploy an SMB on every ascent.

## Photography

The deck gun makes for an iconic wide-angle wreck photograph. Plumose anemone colonies provide stunning color contrast against the dark steel hull. Spawning lumpfish clinging to the hull (spring months) offer unique wildlife photography not available at most dive destinations. A strobe or powerful video light is essential to reveal the colors at this depth.
