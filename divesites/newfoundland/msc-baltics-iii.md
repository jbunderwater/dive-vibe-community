## MSC Baltics III

MSC Baltics III is a 207-meter container ship that grounded in February 2025 near Wild Cove in Newfoundland's Bay of Islands — a very recent casualty that is NOT currently accessible to divers due to an active government exclusion zone.

## Overview

On February 15, 2025, the MSC Baltic III lost power during severe winter weather off the coast of western Newfoundland and ran aground near Wild Cove, Bay of Islands. The 207-meter container vessel carried cargo and remained stuck despite multiple salvage attempts through spring 2025. Subsequent winter storms severely damaged the hull — dive surveys confirmed the engine room and fuel tanks were breached, with oil contamination concerns in the surrounding water. As of March 2026, a two-nautical-mile exclusion zone remains active around the vessel, established by Transport Canada and the Canadian Coast Guard. The wreck is NOT accessible to divers.

## Current Status

Canadian authorities established a two-nautical-mile restricted zone around the vessel following the grounding. The hull has sustained severe damage from winter storms, with 75% of the hull resting on the seabed in some sections. The engine room and fuel tanks are breached. Salvage operations were ongoing through late 2025, with the possibility that the vessel would be demolished in place rather than refloated.

Divers should not approach the site. This record is maintained as a placeholder for a future dive site when and if the exclusion zone is lifted and the wreck is cleared for recreational access.

## Safety Considerations

The site is currently prohibited to divers. Approaching within two nautical miles is a violation of Transport Canada regulations. The hull is severely compromised and contaminated with fuel oil. Do not dive this site until authorities clear it for civilian access.
