## MSC Baltics III

MSC Baltics III is a historic wreck dive in Newfoundland, North America.

## Overview

The MSC Baltics III is a large cargo vessel that grounded on the northern coast of Newfoundland in February 2025 and remains subject to an active 2-nautical-mile exclusion zone as of early 2026 while salvage and environmental response operations continue. The wreck is not currently accessible for recreational diving, and approaching the site is prohibited by Transport Canada marine notices. Divers interested in this site should monitor official Canadian Coast Guard and Transport Canada advisories for updates on the exclusion zone status before planning any visit.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Newfoundland, North America

- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters

## Current Status

The MSC Baltic III grounded on February 15, 2025, after losing power in a Force 12 storm near Wild Cove (Cedar Cove), west of Lark Harbour, Bay of Islands, Newfoundland. All 20 crew members were safely evacuated by Royal Canadian Air Force Cormorant helicopter. As of mid-2026, the vessel has been determined too structurally damaged to tow and is being broken apart in place at Cedar Cove and removed by truck for recycling. Resolve Marine was contracted for the removal operation; bulk fuel has been removed from internal tanks. Some residual fuel, contaminated water, and approximately 63 cargo containers remain on board during the dismantling process. An exclusion zone remains in effect. **The site is not accessible for recreational diving and is not expected to become a dive site, as the vessel is being actively dismantled.**

## Safety Considerations

This site is off-limits to recreational divers as of 2026. An active exclusion zone enforced by the Canadian Coast Guard prohibits approach. Monitor Transport Canada and Canadian Coast Guard advisories for any updates on exclusion zone status before considering any visit.
