## Timur 2

Timur 2 is the northern section of Bunaken Island's eastern wall system, located between Pangalisan and Sachiko's Point. Like Timur 1, it benefits from the calmer conditions typical of the eastern side of the island, making it accessible for intermediate divers while still offering 40-metre wall diving and rich marine life.

## Overview

The wall at Timur 2 is a steep drop-off covered with hard and soft corals, part of the continuous eastern wall that wraps around Bunaken Island. The site offers the same character as Timur 1 — mild current, excellent visibility, a pristine reef top, and a productive wall face with many small caves and crevices — but with its own distinct section of wall to explore. The proximity to Sachiko's Point to the north means this site can be combined with that advanced site on the same dive day.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northeast Bunaken Island, Bunaken National Marine Park
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 metres
- **Current**: Usually mild, occasionally moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Green and hawksbill turtles are regular sightings on both the wall and reef top. Occasional eagle rays pass through the blue water. Whitetip reef sharks rest in the wall's crevices and caves. Bumphead parrotfish and Napoleon wrasse are regularly seen. The wall's many caves and cracks support a variety of macro subjects including moray eels, crabs, and nudibranchs.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wall face and follow it at a comfortable working depth. The section can be dived as a drift or at a relaxed exploratory pace depending on current conditions. Check wall crevices for marine life throughout. Ascend to the reef top for a safety stop in the pristine shallow coral garden.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Bunaken Island. The eastern side's calmer conditions make this a reliable and accessible dive regardless of experience level, subject to current conditions on the day.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Good intermediate option on days when Sachiko's or other advanced sites are running strong current
- Look carefully at the wall's crevices — this site is known for good macro diversity
- The reef top is excellent for extended safety stops
- Can be combined with Sachiko's Point or Pangalisan as part of an eastern wall dive day
- Check with your guide on current direction before entering — a gentle drift is ideal

## Safety Considerations

Current is typically mild but monitor conditions throughout the dive. Carry an SMB. Do not exceed dive plan depth — the wall continues below 40 metres.

## Photography

Similar to Timur 1, the calmer current makes this a good site for macro photography in wall crevices. Turtle portraits and wider reef scenes on the pristine reef top are strong wide-angle opportunities.
