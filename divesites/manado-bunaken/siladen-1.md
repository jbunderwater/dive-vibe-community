## Siladen 1

Siladen 1 is one of the dive sites around Siladen Island, a small island approximately three kilometres from Bunaken that is surrounded by beautiful coral reefs. Siladen's walls are noted for their colorful coral growth and resident blacktip and whitetip reef sharks, which are seen in reliably shallower water here than at many other Bunaken Marine Park sites.

## Overview

The wall on the southwest side of Siladen Island is colorful and vibrant, with a steep drop to about 35 metres and very good coral growth throughout. The site features mild to medium currents, making it accessible for intermediate divers. Siladen's reef sharks tend to come shallower in the early morning and afternoon, spending the middle of the day at greater depths, which makes timing of dives here relevant.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Siladen Island, Bunaken National Marine Park
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 metres
- **Current**: Mild to medium
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Blacktip and whitetip reef sharks are resident and frequently seen. White-spotted eagle rays make regular appearances. Sea turtles, Napoleon wrasse, barracuda, and large schools of trevally are standard sightings. The reefs around Siladen are also excellent for macro species: mandarin fish, pontohi seahorses, ghost pipefish, sponge crabs, and diverse nudibranchs reward careful searching.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the wall and work along it at a chosen depth. Early morning dives are recommended for the best reef shark encounters at shallower depths. The wall's coral coverage is excellent throughout, with the richest concentration between 10-25 metres. Ascend to the shallow reef for a safety stop with additional reef life activity.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from operators based at Siladen Island or from Bunaken or Manado. Mild to medium current makes entry and exit manageable for intermediate divers. Timing dives for early morning or afternoon improves reef shark encounter probability.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Dive early morning or late afternoon for shallower reef shark encounters
- Siladen's macro life is exceptional — bring a macro lens and look carefully at the reef
- Ask your guide specifically about mandarin fish locations at this site
- Combine with Siladen 2 for a full exploration of the island's dive sites
- Siladen Island makes a good alternative base to Bunaken for divers staying at the resort there

## Safety Considerations

Currents can build to medium strength. Monitor conditions and avoid overextending the dive. Carry an SMB. Reef shark encounters are usually unthreatening — maintain calm behaviour and do not chase or corner them.

## Photography

The combination of reef sharks, turtles, and eagle rays makes this a strong wide-angle site. The macro life — particularly mandarin fish, pontohi seahorses, and nudibranchs — is among the best in the Bunaken Marine Park system. Carry both macro and wide-angle setups if possible.
