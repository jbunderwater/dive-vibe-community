## Tengah

Tengah — meaning "in the middle" in Indonesian — is located in the passage between Bunaken and Manado Tua islands, making it a site in a channel where currents from both sides meet and often intensify. It is a fast drift dive through a dense forest of wire corals, with large sea fans and caves in the deeper sections, and is rated as an advanced site due to the frequently strong currents.

## Overview

The wall at Tengah starts from the shallow reef and descends steeply to 45 metres and beyond. Between 20 and 45 metres, the wall is covered in a dense forest of whip corals — wire corals that carpet the face in a way that requires careful buoyancy to navigate without entanglement. Small caves along the wall house beautiful gorgonians in the deeper sections. At the end of the dive, the wall transitions to a carpet of sponges and hard and soft corals. The site's channel position means current direction and strength can vary significantly.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Channel between Bunaken and Manado Tua, Bunaken National Marine Park
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 metres
- **Current**: Often strong — channel location creates variable and intense flow
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The whip coral forest hosts wire coral shrimp and small cryptic species. Caves in the deeper wall sections contain gorgonians and shelter reef fish. The current passing through the channel attracts pelagic visitors including reef sharks, Napoleon wrasse, and trevally. The sponge and coral gardens at the wall's lower transition zone host diverse reef fish communities.

## Dive Profile

Entry point and direction determined by current conditions. The dive is typically a fast drift through the wire coral forest — buoyancy precision is essential to avoid entangling wires. Explore the caves at depth briefly before ascending through the sponge zone. The site provides a different experience on every dive due to varying current conditions.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Bunaken Island or Manado Tua operators. Entry must account for current direction carefully in the channel. Advanced drift technique and buoyancy control are required. Carry an SMB and deploy before surfacing.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Maintain very precise buoyancy throughout the wire coral zone — contact with the wire corals damages them and can injure divers
- Carry a torch to inspect the small caves at depth for gorgonians and resident species
- The current through the channel can change direction — be prepared to adjust
- Not recommended for divers without significant drift diving experience in strong current
- The site produces very different dives depending on current intensity — some dives are leisurely, others very fast

## Safety Considerations

Strong and unpredictable channel currents are the primary hazard. The wire coral forest requires excellent buoyancy — poor control risks entanglement. Do not dive this site without prior drift experience and instruction from a local guide. Carry an SMB. Monitor depth carefully — the wall continues well below recreational limits.

## Photography

The wire coral forest is visually extraordinary — wide-angle shots through the whip coral landscape are unique to this site. The caves at depth offer dramatic compositions with gorgonians. Current and the need for precise buoyancy make photography challenging but rewarding. Macro opportunities include wire coral shrimp if you can hold position.
