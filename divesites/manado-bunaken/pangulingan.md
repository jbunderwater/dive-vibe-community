## Pangulingan

Pangulingan is an exposed wall dive at the northern tip of Bunaken Island, where two distinct currents meet at a dramatic point, generating intense fish activity and some of the park's most exciting open-water encounters.

## Overview

Pangulingan sits at Bunaken Island's north-west point — a geographic feature that funnels currents from both the island's western and northern flanks. This convergence creates a biologically rich upwelling zone that draws pelagic species to the area and keeps the wall covered in enormous seafan and soft coral colonies that only flourish with consistent water movement. It is one of the more challenging sites in the park due to the unpredictable current behaviour, but the reward for managing those conditions is some of the best big-fish action Bunaken offers.

## What to Expect

The wall at Pangulingan runs east-west along the north face and then wraps around the point. The north-facing section drops sharply to 35 metres with a wall face covered in large gorgonian sea fans, sea whips, and multicoloured soft corals. The point itself is where the action concentrates: schools of bigeye trevally, dogtooth tuna, and rainbow runner regularly pass through, and barracuda in groups of 20 or more are not uncommon. Grey reef sharks patrol the deeper open water, and Napoleon wrasse are consistently present.

The shallower sections of the wall, from 5 to 15 metres, have outstanding hard coral coverage and are excellent territory for turtles. Several green turtles are reliably found resting in crevices or grazing on the wall face. When current is moderate and in the right direction, the dive becomes a thrilling drift along the wall face with fish encountered continuously.

## Conditions

- **Depth range:** 5–35+ m
- **Visibility:** 15–30 m; often exceptional
- **Currents:** Strong and variable; can include strong downwellings at the point
- **Water temperature:** 26–30°C; thermoclines possible from upwellings
- **Best season:** May–October; avoid during peak north-west monsoon

## Tips

The boat crew at Pangulingan needs to be experienced with this site — current reading before entry is essential. Negative entries are standard when surface current is running. Stay at mid-wall depth (10–20 m) rather than venturing into open water at the point unless conditions are clearly manageable. The seafan garden between 15 and 25 metres is the site's most photogenic section and is best approached from below to avoid silting the seafan branches.
