## Fukui Point

Fukui Point is Bunaken National Park's only sloping reef dive site — every other site in the park is a wall. This distinction makes it a standout beginner dive and a reliable spot for a completely different experience from the area's famous vertical drops.

## Overview

Unlike the sheer walls that define Bunaken diving, Fukui Point is a gently sloping reef that descends from the shallows down to a sandy plateau at around 30 metres. Giant clams form impressive arrays at about 20 metres, and rose corals carpet the slope from 18 to 30 metres. A large sandy plateau regularly hosts resting whitetip reef sharks. The site also functions as a cleaning station for large pelagic fish.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Bunaken Island, Bunaken National Marine Park
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-25 metres
- **Current**: Usually mild
- **Best Time**: May to October (dry season)

## Marine Life

The sandy slope and cleaning station character attract large pelagic visitors: giant trevally, Napoleon wrasse, and very large snappers are regularly seen. Giant clams at 20 metres are a signature feature. Resting whitetip reef sharks occupy the sandy plateau at depth. The hard coral and rose coral terrain also supports smaller reef fish communities.

## Dive Profile

Enter the water and descend along the gently sloping hard coral reef. Giant clams become prominent around 18-20 metres, and the rose coral fields extend to 30 metres. The sandy plateau at depth is a good place to pause and look for resting reef sharks. Ascend slowly back along the slope, finishing the dive on the shallow reef top. Suitable for Open Water certified divers.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by boat from operators on Bunaken Island or from Manado. Descent is along the reef slope. The mild current at most times makes this accessible for newer divers and a calm option when other Bunaken sites are running strong current.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Best dived when other wall sites are experiencing strong current
- Look carefully at cleaning station activity mid-water for large pelagic species
- Giant clams at 20m are a highlight — approach slowly and avoid casting shadows
- The sandy plateau at 30m is an excellent spot to observe resting whitetip reef sharks
- An excellent alternative when conditions at Lekuan or Raymond's are rough

## Safety Considerations

Current is usually mild, making this one of the more straightforward dives in Bunaken. Be aware of occasional downwellings, particularly on the outer slope. Maintain buoyancy above the coral and sandy bottom. Carry an SMB for boat pickup.

## Photography

Fukui Point is a macro and wide-angle opportunity in one. Giant clams and rose coral gardens reward close-focus work, while the cleaning station invites wide-angle shots of large pelagics being attended by smaller reef fish. Morning light is ideal.
