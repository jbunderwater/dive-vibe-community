## Gorango

Gorango is a wall dive site in the Manado Tua area of Bunaken National Marine Park. Its name means "shark" in the local Manado dialect — though divers should not expect to see more sharks here than at other sites in the park. The wall is very long — longer than a single dive can cover — and is uniformly covered with soft and hard corals, making it a satisfying and scenic wall dive.

## Overview

Gorango's defining characteristic is its exceptional length — the wall extends so far that multiple dives are required to explore it fully. The wall face is continuously covered with soft and hard corals throughout its extent, with variable visibility ranging from 15 to 35 metres depending on conditions. The site is in the Manado Tua portion of the park, which offers a different profile from the Bunaken Island sites, with the volcanic island backdrop adding to the diving atmosphere.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Manado Tua area, Bunaken National Marine Park
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-35 metres (variable)
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Despite its shark-referencing name, Gorango offers the typical Bunaken Marine Park biodiversity: sea turtles, reef sharks, Napoleon wrasse, trevally, and diverse reef fish communities. The soft and hard coral coverage on the long wall face supports a full complement of invertebrates, sponges, and resident reef species. Visibility variability at this site can influence the encounter opportunities with larger, more open-water species.

## Dive Profile

Select a section of the wall to explore for each dive — the length means a systematic approach across multiple dives is needed to cover the site. Work along the wall face at your chosen depth, monitoring air consumption and nitrogen loading. The coral coverage is consistent enough that any section of the wall will provide rewarding diving.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from operators covering the Manado Tua area. Entry and current assessment as per standard Bunaken protocols. The boat follows divers along the wall and picks up from the SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Plan to dive this site more than once — the wall's length means there is always more to see
- Variable visibility here means conditions vary significantly between dives — ask operators about recent conditions
- Coral coverage is consistently excellent throughout the wall, so any section is worthwhile
- The site's name may attract expectations of shark encounters — be aware that "gorango" simply reflects the local language and is not predictive of encounters

## Safety Considerations

Variable current. Monitor conditions throughout the dive. Carry an SMB. Variable visibility can make boat tracking more challenging — communicate with the boat crew before entry.

## Photography

The extensive coral-covered wall offers wide-angle opportunities for reef scenes throughout. The length of the site means distinctive features may appear on any dive. Consistent coral coverage rewards macro searching for invertebrates and small fish throughout the wall face.
