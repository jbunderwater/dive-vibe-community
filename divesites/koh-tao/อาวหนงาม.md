## อ่าวหินงาม (Hin Ngam)

Hin Ngam (อ่าวหินงาม — "Beautiful Stone Bay") is a sheltered reef dive site on Koh Tao's southeast coast, situated between Aow Leuk Bay and Shark Island. The site consists of three rocky pinnacles just offshore, with a fringing reef encircling the bay and coral bommies scattered across a sandy floor.

## Overview

Hin Ngam offers a diverse macro environment within a compact, protected bay. The site is accessible to all certification levels due to its shallow depth (3–12 metres) and calm conditions. Three nearshore pinnacles form the structural core of the dive, providing cracks and crevices that shelter a variety of small invertebrates and reef fish. A government-sponsored concrete block structure (DMCR artificial reef) has been installed here, adding further habitat.

## Marine Life

Frogfish and scorpionfish occupy the rubble and rocky substrate. Nudibranchs appear throughout the site, and Jann's pipefish have been recorded in sandy patches. Porcupine pufferfish, white-eyed moray eels, and various cleaning shrimp inhabit the pinnacle crevices. Jenkins whiprays rest on the sandy bottom. The artificial reef structure hosts additional encrusting invertebrates and reef fish seeking shelter.

## Dive Profile

The site is best accessed by boat, with a maximum depth of approximately 12 metres. Visibility averages 5–30 metres depending on conditions. Current is typically minimal. The concave shape of the bay provides protection from prevailing swells, making it diveable when more exposed east coast sites are rougher.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Look carefully in crevices on the pinnacle faces for frogfish and scorpionfish — camouflage is excellent
- The sandy areas between bommies are good for nudibranchs and pipefish
- The artificial reef block structure is worth exploring for encrusting life
