## อ่าวหินงาม (Aow Hin Wong)

A scenic east coast bay on Koh Tao known for its beautiful rock formations, clear water, and diverse reef life in a peaceful setting.

## Overview

Aow Hin Wong (Beautiful Rock Bay) is a striking east coast bay on Koh Tao featuring dramatic granite boulder formations along its shoreline that continue underwater, creating varied topography from the shallows to 18 m. The site is less visited than the main west-side dive locations, contributing to healthier corals and undisturbed marine life. The bay's orientation provides shelter during the main diving season and its combination of rock formations, sandy patches, and coral growth makes for interesting exploration at beginner to intermediate depth ranges.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East coast, Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Bay / Boulder Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 m
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–18 m
- **Current**: Generally minimal inside bay
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

The rock formations provide excellent habitat for moray eels, groupers, and lionfish in overhangs. Turtles are sighted regularly along the reef crest. Sea snakes cruise the boulder channels. Triggerfish, parrotfish, and anthias are abundant. Sandy patches between boulders host garden eels and stingrays. Nudibranchs inhabit the rocky substrate throughout.

## Dive Profile

Descend at the bay entrance and explore the granite boulders from 5–18 m. The underwater rock formations mirror the dramatic above-water landscape. Check crevices and overhangs between boulders for sheltering fish. Ascend along the shallower inner bay where coral gardens provide a relaxed safety stop environment.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Koh Tao. East coast access is best May–October when sheltered from southwest monsoon. Entry via back roll. Minimal current for straightforward in and out diving.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Less boat traffic than west-side sites means more relaxed encounters with resident marine life
- The rock formations reward slow, methodical exploration — rushing past misses the critters in crevices
- Combine with Aow Leuk nearby for a full east coast diving afternoon
- Good site for practising navigation skills in a low-stress environment
- Visit at sunrise for the most dramatic lighting on the coastal boulders from the boat

## Safety Considerations

Boulder swim-throughs are generally safe at this site but check for current before entering. Sea snakes are non-aggressive. Lionfish in overhangs are venomous — observe without touching. Standard buoyancy care near coral and rock formations.

## Photography

The dramatic coastal boulders continuing underwater create compelling wide-angle subjects. Sea snakes in the boulder channels are approachable portrait subjects. The contrast between the boulder landscape and clear water suits natural light wide-angle photography.
