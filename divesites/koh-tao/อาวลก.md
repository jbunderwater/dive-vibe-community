## อ่าวลึก (Aow Leuk)

A shallow, calm east coast bay on Koh Tao — one of the island's most accessible snorkelling and shallow diving spots with impressive coral gardens.

## Overview

Aow Leuk (Deep Bay, though paradoxically very shallow) is one of Koh Tao's most popular east coast bays for snorkelling and very shallow diving. Despite the name suggesting depth, the bay maxes out at 10 m and is typically much shallower in the main areas where corals are densest. The protected bay is famous for its good coral coverage and fish diversity, particularly for an east coast location. Sea turtles are regularly sighted here, making it a popular destination for snorkel tours as well as certified divers. The calm, turquoise water makes it especially photogenic.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East coast, Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Bay
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 10 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 m
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Green and hawksbill turtles are a reliable sighting here. Clownfish and anemones throughout the shallow reef. Parrotfish, angelfish, and triggerfish are abundant. Moray eels in rocky crevices. The sandy patches host stingrays and garden eels. Sergeant major fish swarm in the open water above the reef.

## Dive Profile

This is primarily a shallow exploration dive. Descend to the sandy bottom at the deepest point and explore the coral gardens. The densest coral is typically at 3–7 m. Circle the bay perimeter and return through the central coral area. The very shallow depth means extended bottom time is easy to achieve.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Koh Tao or short-tail boat from the beach if staying on the east coast. Calm entry conditions year-round on most days. The shallow beach entry is possible when conditions are calm.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Ideal for the first open water experience after pool training
- Snorkelling directly from the surface gives an excellent preview before submerging
- Move slowly along the reef edge to maximise turtle encounter probability
- Visit early morning before snorkel tour boats arrive for the quietest experience
- The very shallow depth makes this an excellent site for extended air dives

## Safety Considerations

Very shallow conditions are ideal for beginners but maintain buoyancy to avoid coral contact. Boat and longtail boat traffic in the bay — surface clearly visible from above and deploy a safety sausage if ascending away from the boat. Sea urchins in the rocky areas near the bay edges require footwear.

## Photography

Bright, shallow water produces vivid natural light shots without strobes. Turtle portraits in clear shallow water are the signature subject. Wide-angle shots of the coral garden from just above the reef create stunning images with minimal equipment.
