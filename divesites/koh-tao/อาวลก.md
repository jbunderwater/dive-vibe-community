## อ่าวลึก (Aow Leuk)

Aow Leuk (อ่าวลึก — "Deep Bay") is a sheltered bay dive site on the southeast coast of Koh Tao, one of the island's most popular sites for beginner training and relaxed reef exploration. Despite the name, the bay is shallow, and the name refers to its concave, enclosed shape rather than water depth.

## Overview

Aow Leuk sits in a protected bay on Koh Tao's southeast coast, sheltered from the west monsoon and diveable year-round under typical conditions. Marine surveys have recorded 65 coral species here, including uncommon blue corals that glow under UV light at night, making it one of the more biodiverse reef sites on the island.

## Marine Life

Sergeant majors, rabbitfish, squirrelfish, damselfish, and parrotfish are abundant throughout the reef. Blacktip reef sharks visit the shallow flats, and seahorses can be found in sandy patches. Blue-spotted ribbontail rays rest on the sandy bottom, and moray eels inhabit crevices in the boulder formations. The reef hosts hard corals including brain, table, and staghorn varieties, alongside soft mushroom corals and toadstool leather corals. A Giant Clam nursery is located within the site. Marine conservancy structures installed in 2010 form an artificial reef section (Suan Olan / Olan's Garden) at 14–16 metres on the outer side of the bay.

## Dive Profile

The site splits into two main routes: north toward Aow Leuk Point along boulders and hard coral formations, and south toward Hin Ngam (a separate pinnacle site). Maximum depth is approximately 15 metres; average dive depth around 8 metres. Current is minimal, making it suitable for Open Water divers and training.

## Entry and Exit

Boat entry. The bay is accessed by dive boat from nearby dive centres. Visibility typically ranges from 5–10 metres, occasionally higher in calm conditions.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Slow down and look for seahorses in sandy patches between coral heads
- Night dives reveal the rare blue corals under UV light
- The outer artificial reef (Suan Olan) adds variety for those wanting a second dive in the same bay
- The site is best dived early morning before dive boat traffic increases
