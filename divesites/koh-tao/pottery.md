## Pottery

A shallow granite pinnacle site on Koh Tao's south side — often dived with neighbouring Three Rocks, with hard corals, sea cucumbers, and cuttlefish on night dives.

## Overview

Pottery Pinnacle sits approximately 800 m from Mae Haad beach on the southern side of Koh Tao, in Mae Haad Bay. The site consists of small granite rock pinnacles just below the surface covered mainly with hard corals, sea urchins, and marbled sea cucumbers. Depth ranges from 3 m at the shallowest to 12 m maximum. Rock overhangs and crevices, plus a small swim-through passage, provide habitat for a range of critters. The adjacent Three Rocks site (maximum 8–9 m) is typically dived on the same trip, extending the dive time and offering a table coral formation. The proximity to Mae Haad pier makes this a convenient option for review dives and night dives. Visibility averages 2–10 m and can be poor.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Mae Haad Bay, south Koh Tao, approximately 800 m from Mae Haad beach
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 2–10 m (variable; can be poor)
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: Year-round (sheltered bay)

## Marine Life

Confirmed species include Jenkins whiprays, blue-spotted ribbon-tail rays, starry-eyed pufferfish, blue-lined and hexagon groupers, bannerfish, masked and gold-spotted rabbitfish, Indian walkman (a scorpionfish species), crabs, shrimps, hermit crabs, octopus, nudibranchs, flatworms, giant pufferfish, and broadclub cuttlefish (particularly on night dives). Note: earlier descriptions listing soft whip corals, sponges, and a deeper depth range of 18 m were inaccurate for this site; Pottery is predominantly hard coral with a maximum depth of 12 m.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the base of the site (8–12 m) and explore the pinnacles from below. Follow the hard coral formations and the small swim-through. Spend the second half of the dive in the shallows at 3–6 m where reef fish density is highest. Swim to Three Rocks for the safety stop if air allows.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Koh Tao (approximately 10 minutes to the south coast / Mae Haad area). Entry via back roll. Mild conditions suitable for newly certified divers. Proximity to Mae Haad pier makes this convenient for evening night dives.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Pair with Three Rocks nearby for a combined south coast dive day
- Night dives are worthwhile — broadclub cuttlefish are documented here after dark
- A good site for practising buoyancy skills in calm conditions
- Indian walkman (stonefish relative) in the rubble areas — do not touch any substrate

## Safety Considerations

Conditions are mild. Indian walkman and other scorpionfish relatives in the rubble require careful buoyancy to avoid contact. Visibility can be poor; a torch is useful. Avoid stirring up sand near the pinnacles.

## Photography

Nudibranchs and flatworms on the rock surfaces are classic macro subjects. Broadclub cuttlefish on night dives reward patient close-focus wide-angle or macro photographers.
