## Shark Island

Koh Tao's southeastern granite islet — named for its dorsal-fin silhouette, with some of the island's richest soft coral growth, boulder labyrinths, and occasional blacktip reef sharks.

## Overview

Shark Island is a small granite rock islet off the southeast coast of Koh Tao whose above-water profile resembles a shark's dorsal fin. Underwater, large boulders tumble from the island's base down to sandy bottom at 20–28 m, creating gullies, ridgelines, swim-throughs, and overhangs covered in hard corals, purple soft tree corals, large gorgonian fans, whip corals, barrel sponges, and brain corals. Multiple surveys have recorded 58 coral species at the site, with particularly healthy soft coral coverage cited by New Heaven Dive School. The exposed location means currents can be moderate to strong; AOW certification is recommended by Black Turtle Dive for the deeper sections, though calm-condition dives are accessible to Open Water divers on the shallower side.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Southeast Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef with boulder terrain
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–30 m (in calm conditions)
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; can be strong on exposed faces
- **Best Time**: May to August

## Marine Life

Confirmed species include blue-spotted ribbon-tail rays, giant moray eels, white-eyed moray eels, titan triggerfish, scribbled filefish, harlequin sweetlips, yellow-tail barracuda in schools, trevally, blackcap butterflyfish, cuttlefish, groupers, batfish, nudibranchs, and sea turtles. Blacktip reef sharks are occasionally seen cruising the deeper sections. Whale sharks are reported as seasonal visitors in peak migration periods.

## Dive Profile

Descend on the sheltered side and circle the island, following the coral slope from the shallows to 20–28 m on the deeper faces. The deeper boulder sections (20–28 m) are where sharks are most frequently encountered. Ascend via the shallower side and complete a 3-minute safety stop before returning to the boat.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Koh Tao (approximately 15–20 minutes to the southeast). Entry via back roll. Currents on the exposed face can be significant — divers should have experience managing moderate current. SMB deployment for ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Circle the island to experience different habitats on each face — shallow coral garden one side, deeper boulders the other
- Stay calm and move slowly when blacktip reef sharks are nearby — they are not aggressive
- The shallower boulder zone at 3–8 m offers excellent reef fish variety at safety stop depth
- Pair with nearby south Koh Tao sites for a southeast coast day

## Safety Considerations

Current can be stronger on the exposed southern and eastern faces. Maintain awareness of your depth on the steeper boulder slopes. Blacktip reef sharks are not aggressive but require respectful distance. Always carry an SMB for drift ascents.

## Photography

The soft coral diversity makes Shark Island one of Koh Tao's best wide-angle reef landscape sites. Gorgonian fan corals with a diver in the background suit medium-wide compositions. Blacktip reef shark portraits with the coral slope are the signature subject. Schools of barracuda in open water above the island make compelling wide-angle shots.
