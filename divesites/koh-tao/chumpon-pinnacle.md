## Chumpon Pinnacle

Koh Tao's most celebrated offshore pinnacle — a granite monolith draped in anemones where whale sharks and dense barracuda schools gather at the surface of the Gulf of Thailand.

## Overview

Chumpon Pinnacle (also spelled Chumphon) lies approximately 12 km northwest of Koh Tao, a 30–45 minute boat ride from Mae Haad Pier. The pinnacle top sits at 14 m depth and the rocky flanks descend to a flat sandy bottom at around 40 m. A smaller rocky formation called Barracuda Rock lies to the southwest at approximately 25 m depth. The pinnacle surface is carpeted with sea anemones hosting pink anemonefish, and the deeper flanks support soft corals and sea fans. Currents flowing past the structure create excellent visibility — often exceeding 20 m — and draw the large marine life that makes this Koh Tao's premier big-animal dive. Chumpon Pinnacle records more whale shark sightings than any other Koh Tao site; encounters are concentrated in the March–May period.

Advanced Open Water certification is recommended for the deeper flanks (below 25 m), though the pinnacle top at 14–18 m is accessible to Open Water divers in calm conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: 12 km northwest of Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand; ~30–45 min by boat
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced (pinnacle top accessible to intermediate divers in calm conditions)
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15–40 m
- **Current**: Mild to moderate; can be stronger depending on conditions
- **Best Time**: March to May for whale shark season; May to August generally

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at the site include whale sharks (regular visitors, peak March–May), chevron and yellow-tail barracuda in dense schools, giant grouper, batfish, trevally, white-eyed moray eels, blue-spotted stingrays, lionfish, pink anemonefish in the anemone carpet, butterflyfish, and angelfish. Chumpon Pinnacle is described by Black Turtle Dive as the site with the highest whale shark encounter rate on Koh Tao. Barracuda Rock to the southwest hosts additional fish schools.

Note: some online sources mention grey reef sharks at this site, but this is not consistently confirmed across multiple operators; expect barracuda schools and groupers as the reliable large-animal encounter.

## Dive Profile

Descend the mooring line or descent line to the pinnacle top at 14 m and orient with the formation before exploring the flanks. The upper section at 14–20 m suits Open Water divers and is where whale sharks tend to hover near the cleaning activity on the anemone field. Descend the flanks to 25–30 m for grouper encounters and Barracuda Rock. Advanced divers may extend to the sandy bottom at 40 m. Ascend slowly and conduct a 5-minute safety stop on the pinnacle top.

## Entry and Exit

Boat only — approximately 30–45 minutes from Mae Haad Pier, Koh Tao. Entry via back roll or giant stride. Operators time departures based on current and whale shark activity reports. Deploy SMB during safety stop for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Dawn and early morning dives increase whale shark encounter probability
- Stay near the pinnacle top at 14–18 m when whale sharks are present
- Nitrox recommended to extend bottom time on the deeper flanks
- Don't chase whale sharks — hover neutrally and allow them to approach
- Barracuda Rock to the southwest is worth a swim if conditions allow

## Safety Considerations

Currents can develop quickly at this exposed offshore site. If swept off the structure, do not fight the current — surface with your SMB and wait for the boat. Stay within your planned no-decompression limits on the deeper flanks. Whale shark protocol: maintain 3 m distance, no flash photography, no touching.

## Photography

Whale shark silhouettes in the open blue water require fast shutter speeds and wide angle. Dense barracuda schools spiralling above the pinnacle are the iconic wide-angle shot. Anemone fields with pink anemonefish suit macro or close-focus wide-angle.
