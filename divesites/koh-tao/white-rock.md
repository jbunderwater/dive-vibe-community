## White Rock

One of Koh Tao's most popular and versatile dive sites — raised granite shelves with honeycomb groupers, Christmas tree worms, and the island's most recommended night dive.

## Overview

White Rock is located on the west side of Koh Tao and is one of the island's most frequently visited sites due to its accessibility, variety of depth zones, and reliable marine life. The site consists of a series of raised granite shelves from around 5 m all the way to 22 m, with hard coral growth, anemone fields, and rocky overhangs providing habitat diversity. Honeycomb groupers (hexagon groupers) are the signature species — large, distinctive, and reliably present on the shelves. Christmas tree worms in multiple colour forms are found across the rock surfaces. The site's shallow sections make it accessible to Open Water divers while the deeper shelves provide interest for intermediate divers. White Rock is consistently cited by operators as the best night dive on Koh Tao.

## Site Information

- **Location**: West side of Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 m
- **Current**: Generally mild
- **Best Time**: May to August

## Marine Life

Confirmed species include honeycomb groupers (the site's signature animal), Christmas tree worms in multi-colour forms, titan triggerfish, pufferfish, sea turtles (green and hawksbill, occasional), blue-spotted ribbon-tail rays, butterflyfish, triggerfish, and angelfish. Barracuda and trevally patrol the outer reef sections. Night dives reveal octopus, hunting crabs, and various crustaceans not active during the day. Lionfish inhabit the overhangs throughout the site.

## Dive Profile

Descend to 20–22 m to explore the deeper rock formations and then ascend progressively up through the shelf zones. The middle depth at 8–15 m is the most productive for fish life. The 2–5 m shallows host the densest Christmas tree worm concentrations. Allow ample time in the shallows before completing the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Koh Tao west coast (5 minutes). Entry via back roll or giant stride. Currents are mild. SMB deployment for ascent is recommended.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Night dives at White Rock are among the best on Koh Tao — book in advance
- Explore the rock shelves methodically; crevices hide groupers and moray eels
- Christmas tree worms retract instantly when approached — move slowly for best views
- Combine with a morning dive at HTMS Sattakut for a varied two-site day
- The shallow sections are well suited to open water training dives

## Safety Considerations

Currents are generally mild but can increase on the outer reef. Long-spined black sea urchins on the rock shelves cause injuries if contacted — maintain spatial awareness. Night dives require dive lights and a close buddy system. Lionfish inhabit overhangs throughout the site.

## Photography

Christmas tree worms in multi-colour clusters make outstanding close-focus wide-angle or macro subjects. Honeycomb grouper portraits are achievable with a patient approach. Night dive photography reveals octopus colour changes and hunting crustaceans.
