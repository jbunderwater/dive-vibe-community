## อ่าวมวง (Mango Bay)

Mango Bay (อ่าวมวง) is a sheltered bay on Koh Tao's north coast, widely regarded as the island's premier training and beginner dive site. Approximately 80% of Koh Tao's dive schools use Mango Bay for introductory and Open Water certification dives due to its calm conditions, sandy bottom, and excellent visibility. The bay is also genuinely rewarding for experienced divers interested in reef exploration.

## Overview

Mango Bay offers calm, turquoise waters with minimal current and an average depth of around 8 metres, making it safe and welcoming for new divers while still offering diverse marine life. The bay splits into two areas: the western side features coral gardens and soft corals, while the eastern side showcases large mushroom and table corals, staghorn formations, and barrel sponges teeming with reef life.

## Marine Life

Schools of scad patrol the open water, while Jenkins whiprays and peacock sole rest on the sandy bottom. Crocodile snake eels have been reported in sandy patches alongside seahorses. Damselfish, wrasses (including the Red-Breasted Splendour Wrasse), pairs of Weibel's butterflyfish, and yellow-spot goatfish inhabit the coral gardens. Harlequin sweetlips, groupers, large schools of yellowtail barracuda, giant clams, moray eels, sea turtles, and eagle rays complete the resident fish list.

## Dive Profile

Depth ranges from 1–20 metres, with most training and recreational diving conducted in the 5–12 metre range. Visibility typically reaches 10–20 metres. The sandy floor and lack of significant current make navigation straightforward. Both shore and boat entries are possible.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The eastern coral garden is the most rewarding area for reef fish variety
- Sandy patches between coral heads are good for seahorses and snake eels
- Night dives reveal cuttlefish and other nocturnal species on the sandy flats
