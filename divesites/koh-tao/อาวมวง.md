## อ่าวมวง (Aow Muang)

A sheltered east coast bay on Koh Tao offering gentle shallow diving with reef fish and coral for beginner divers.

## Overview

Aow Muang (Purple Bay) is a sheltered bay on the northeast coast of Koh Tao, accessible by boat and offering calm conditions that make it suitable for beginner divers and Open Water training. The bay's sheltered position protects it from the prevailing southwest swell during the main diving season, and its shallow depth profile of up to 14 m makes it a relaxed, low-pressure environment. Coral formations and reef fish are present throughout the bay, and the site offers a peaceful alternative to the more heavily trafficked west-side sites.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northeast coast, Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Bay / Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 14 m
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 m
- **Current**: Minimal inside the bay
- **Best Time**: May to October (sheltered from south swell)

## Marine Life

Reef fish typical of Koh Tao's east coast — parrotfish, butterflyfish, surgeonfish, and triggerfish. Clownfish in anemones are present on the coral heads. Turtles occasionally graze the reef. Moray eels inhabit crevices between rocks. Sandy patches host garden eels and stingrays.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sandy bottom at 8–14 m and explore the coral heads and rock formations around the bay perimeter. The shallows inside the bay are accessible and calm. Ascend gradually for a standard safety stop at 5 m. Total dive time 40–50 minutes at the shallow maximum depth.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Koh Tao. East coast bays are generally sheltered May–October. Entry via back roll or giant stride from the boat. Calm conditions for comfortable entry and exit.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A low-stress site ideal for anxious beginners or divers returning after a long break
- Snorkelling is possible directly from the bay surface for non-divers
- Combine with nearby east coast sites such as Laem Thian for a half-day east coast trip
- Visit May–October when the bay is sheltered from the main monsoon swell direction
- The quiet, less-visited nature of this site makes for undisturbed wildlife encounters

## Safety Considerations

Conditions are calm and beginner-appropriate. Standard reef precautions apply — do not touch coral, maintain buoyancy. Stingrays on sandy patches can sting if accidentally disturbed. Boat traffic is minimal but surface well clear of the bay entrance.

## Photography

Good natural light in the shallows suits beginner photographers learning composition. Clownfish in anemones are accessible subjects. The quiet reef provides undisturbed fish portrait opportunities.
