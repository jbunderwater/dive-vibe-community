## Green Rock

An advanced diver's playground near Koh Nang Yuan — a labyrinth of granite boulders, swim-throughs, and caverns with unpredictable currents and diverse critter life.

## Overview

Green Rock sits off the northwest coastline of Koh Nang Yuan and is one of Koh Tao's most dynamic sites for experienced divers. Large granite boulders have tumbled together to create a complex network of caves, caverns, and swim-throughs that can be explored over multiple dives without exhausting the possibilities. The site runs from 4 m at the shallowest boulder tops to around 28 m in the deeper channels. The exposed ocean-side location means visibility and currents are variable — conditions can change around the full moon — and this unpredictability is part of what makes the site better suited to Advanced Open Water divers and above. Black Turtle Dive confirms AOW certification as the minimum recommendation.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest of Koh Nang Yuan, near Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 2–20 m (highly variable)
- **Current**: Variable; can be significant, especially around the full moon
- **Best Time**: May to August

## Marine Life

Confirmed species include titan triggerfish and yellow-margin triggerfish (particularly prominent during nesting season June–August), hawksbill and green sea turtles, banded sea kraits (frequently seen in the boulder channels), yellow-tail barracuda in large schools, chevron barracuda, dancing Durban shrimp, nudibranchs, and various angelfish and butterflyfish. Occasional whale shark sightings are reported seasonally. Blue-ring angelfish are associated with the swim-through entrances in multiple operator descriptions.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the site and orient yourself with the boulder system before exploring the swim-throughs. Work from the deeper channels (20–28 m) up through the shallower passages (5–15 m). Current can funnel through individual channels — assess each passage before entering. Take time in each passage as no two are alike. Ascend via the shallower boulder zones for the safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Koh Tao (approximately 10 minutes). Entry via back roll. The complex topography makes a proper dive briefing essential — follow the guide on first visits. SMB deployment from open water at the end of the dive.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced Open Water certification minimum — complex topography and variable current
- Do not enter swim-throughs alone; always have a buddy and a confirmed exit
- During June–August nesting season, maintain a wide arc around titan triggerfish nests (circular depressions in sand)
- Multiple dives are needed to explore the full extent of the boulder system
- Combine with Japanese Garden for a varied Koh Nang Yuan diving day

## Safety Considerations

Titan triggerfish attack without warning during nesting season — their territory extends in a cone from the nest upward. If charged, swim horizontally out of the cone rather than ascending. Do not enter narrow swim-throughs without confirming an exit. Variable currents can make it difficult to hold position in channels. Always carry an SMB. Visibility can drop to 2 m around the full moon; plan accordingly.

## Photography

Swim-through silhouette shots are the defining image of Green Rock — position a diver in the exit opening and shoot from inside the passage. Banded sea kraits in the boulder channels make atmospheric subjects. The complex boulder topography creates compelling wide-angle compositions with dramatic light angles.
