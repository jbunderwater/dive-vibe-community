## อ่าวโตนด (Aow Tanot)

A secluded east coast bay with pristine corals, known among Koh Tao divers for its untouched reef quality due to limited visitor access.

## Overview

Aow Tanot (Tanote Bay) is a secluded bay on the east coast of Koh Tao that benefits from lower diver traffic than the more famous west-side sites. The bay features a combination of rocky reef, coral bommies, and sandy channels down to 16 m, with fish life that is notably undisturbed compared to high-traffic sites. Green turtles are frequently encountered here, and the healthy coral coverage reflects the site's lower visitation rate. The bay is accessible by longtail boat from the beach or by dive boat from Koh Tao's main pier.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East coast, Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Bay / Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 16 m
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–18 m
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: May to October

## Marine Life

Green turtles are a consistent sighting, often grazing on the reef crest in good numbers. Parrotfish, angelfish, and surgeonfish are abundant. Moray eels and grouper inhabit rock crevices. Cuttlefish are seen hovering near the reef edge. Sandy patches hold blue-spotted stingrays. The lower diver pressure here results in less skittish marine life overall.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the bay floor at 10–16 m and follow the reef perimeter. Turtle encounters are most likely on the reef crest at 5–10 m. The sandy channels between bommies are worth searching for cuttlefish and stingrays. Ascend via the reef and complete a 3-minute safety stop in the shallows.

## Entry and Exit

Dive boat from Koh Tao (approximately 15–20 minutes to the east coast). Alternatively, longtail boats run from the beach at Tanote Bay. Entry via back roll or shore walk-in from the beach. Conditions are typically calm inside the bay.

## Tips and Recommendations

- One of the best bays on Koh Tao for undisturbed turtle encounters due to lower traffic
- The beach at Tanote Bay is one of the most scenic on the island — combine diving with a surface interval on the beach
- Visit outside of peak season to maximise the "remote east coast" experience
- The calm conditions make this ideal for photographers wanting cooperative marine life
- Night dives are possible here — octopus and crabs become active after sunset

## Safety Considerations

Standard beginner bay precautions apply. Maintain buoyancy above coral heads. Stingrays on sandy patches can sting if disturbed. Sea urchins in rocky shoreline areas — use booties for beach entry. Longtail boats moving in and out of the bay require surface awareness.

## Photography

Undisturbed turtles at close range provide excellent portrait opportunities. Cuttlefish colour changes make compelling close-up subjects. The scenic bay character with clear water and coral suits wide-angle reef photography.
