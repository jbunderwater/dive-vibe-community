## อ่าวโตนด (Tanote Bay / Aow Tanote)

Tanote Bay (อ่าวโตนด) is one of Koh Tao's most popular east coast dive sites, offering a wide sandy beach with easy shore or boat access and a diverse mix of coral reef, rocky structures, and artificial features. The bay is suitable for all certification levels and is notable for its resident blacktip reef sharks and a small sunken catamaran wreck.

## Overview

Tanote Bay sits on Koh Tao's east coast and provides a sheltered, multi-feature dive within a single bay. Natural reef runs along the bay edges, large barrel sponges and brain and boulder corals dominate the middle sections, and artificial conservation structures — including concrete cubes and Reef Ball modules — have been installed at approximately 10 metres depth on the south side to support coral regeneration. The catamaran wreck at around 18 metres is the site's main centrepiece for macro photography and critter hunting.

## Marine Life

Large schools of yellowtail barracuda are a consistent presence throughout the bay. Sea turtles, titan triggerfish, and banded sea kraits are regularly sighted. Blacktip reef sharks are frequently seen in the shallow flats during early morning hours. The catamaran wreck provides shelter for Jenkins whiprays resting in its shadow, small-banded pipefish in the hull openings, spider crabs on whip coral around the structure, and various cleaning shrimp. The artificial concrete cubes in the south bay host encrusting corals and a range of small reef life in their cracks and crevices.

## Dive Profile

Maximum depth is approximately 18 metres at the catamaran wreck; shallower coral areas begin from 5 metres. Visibility averages 2–10 metres. Entry from shore (sandy beach) is straightforward. The site can be done in two separate dives — one exploring the reef and artificial structures, one focused on the wreck.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Visit early morning for the best chance of seeing blacktip reef sharks in the shallows
- Spend time on the underside of the catamaran hull looking for whiprays
- The artificial reef cubes on the south side reward slow searching for nudibranchs and small crustaceans
