## Buoyancy

Koh Tao's dedicated buoyancy training site — purpose-built artificial structures and coral nurseries adjacent to the Twins pinnacles.

## Overview

Buoyancy World (locally called Buoyancy) was funded, developed, and deployed by the local Koh Tao dive community, local government, and local businesses to provide a dedicated training site that relieves pressure on the natural reefs. Located a short swim north of the Twins site near Koh Nang Yuan, the site lies in water 10–16 m deep with a sandy bottom. Structures include tunnels, hoops, and an obstacle course for buoyancy practice, plus concrete marine-animal sculptures (octopus, shark, turtle, and fish forms) that have become home to reef fish over time. Coral nurseries with staghorn coral fragments have developed successfully around the structures. The site diverts training activity away from natural reefs while building genuine habitat. Batfish, red-breasted splendour wrasse, yellow-spotted goatfish, moon wrasse, parrotfish, and juvenile sweetlips are confirmed resident species.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North of Twins, near Koh Nang Yuan, Koh Tao
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Artificial_reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–18 m
- **Current**: Minimal
- **Best Time**: Year-round

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at the site include batfish, red-breasted splendour wrasse, yellow-spotted goatfish, moon wrasse, parrotfish, and juvenile sweetlips. The growing staghorn coral nursery structures attract additional small reef fish. Sand-dwelling species including cuttlefish and stingrays inhabit the sandy bottom between structures.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the sandy bottom at 10–15 m and navigate between the purpose-built structures. Practice buoyancy exercises through the hoops before exploring the coral nursery and sculpture areas. The dive is typically 40–50 minutes at this shallow depth. Ascend slowly from the sandy bottom for a standard safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat from Koh Tao (approximately 10 minutes). Entry via back roll or giant stride. Conditions are consistently calm and minimal current. Typically combined with a Twins dive on the same boat trip.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Ideal for pre-certification buoyancy exercises before diving more complex natural sites
- The coral nursery area is a tangible example of reef conservation — observe the staghorn growth
- Combine with Twins in the same dive by swimming between the two adjacent sites
- Works well as the second dive when air supply is lower after a deeper first dive

## Safety Considerations

Conditions are consistently calm and suitable for the most inexperienced divers. Standard buoyancy care applies near coral nursery frames. Avoid stirring sand near the structures, which reduces visibility and disturbs critters.

## Photography

The concrete marine sculptures make quirky wide-angle subjects with a diver in the frame. Coral nursery frames provide context shots showing reef restoration in progress. The clean sandy bottom and bright natural light produce well-exposed images for beginner underwater photographers.
