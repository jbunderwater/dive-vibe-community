## Fearless and Willy T

Two sunken vessels resting near Peter Island — the Fearless, believed to be a sister ship of Cousteau's Calypso, and the Willy T, the BVI's legendary floating bar, sunk in 2019 as a pirate-themed underwater attraction.

## Overview

Fearless and Willy T is a twin-wreck dive site located approximately half a mile north of Great Harbour near Peter Island. The site combines two vessels with very different histories. The Fearless is widely believed to be a sister ship of Jacques Cousteau's research vessel Calypso; it is a wooden wreck and has degraded significantly, requiring caution when exploring. The Willy T was a floating bar and restaurant famous throughout the BVI, washed ashore during Hurricane Irma in September 2017. The vessel was later repaired by Beyond the Reef, a local nonprofit, fitted with pirate-themed decorations, and deliberately scuttled on August 7, 2019. It now rests upright off Key Bay at Peter Island with the crow's nest accessible at around 10 meters depth and the deck at approximately 20 meters. Both wooden wrecks are fragile — caution is required throughout the dive.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Near Peter Island, approximately half a mile north of Great Harbour
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Good in calm conditions
- **Current**: Low
- **Best Time**: Year-round; check conditions locally

## Marine Life

Fairy basslets are present around both wrecks. Eagle rays and sea turtles are regularly sighted in the vicinity. Southern stingrays are reported near the sandy bottom around the wrecks. The Willy T's crow's nest has already become a fish aggregation point with reef fish inhabiting the structure.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the Willy T, where the crow's nest sits at around 10 meters — a good starting reference point. Work your way down to the deck and along the exterior, observing the pirate decorations placed before sinking. The Fearless lies nearby; approach it carefully as the wooden structure is severely degraded. Penetration of either wreck requires proper overhead environment training and is not recommended without it. Ascend with adequate air for a 5-meter safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators based near Peter Island or Norman Island. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Deploy an SMB during your safety stop for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Willy T is the more intact and visually interesting of the two wrecks
- $5 donations to Beyond the Reef are encouraged to support BVI children's swim programs
- Do not attempt penetration of the Fearless — the wooden structure is severely degraded and dangerous
- Bring a dive torch to illuminate interior details and bring out colors on encrusted surfaces
- Maintain careful buoyancy to avoid disturbing silt and fragile wreck structure

## Safety Considerations

Both wrecks are wooden and in advanced states of decay. Penetration is dangerous and requires proper training. The Fearless in particular should be treated as a no-penetration site. Dive within your certification limits. Carry an SMB. Dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The Willy T offers unique photo opportunities: pirate props, an intact superstructure with natural light filtering through, and resident fish. Wide-angle lenses capture the exterior profile against the sandy bottom. A torch enhances interior color shots. The unusual pirate theme makes this a distinctive subject compared to typical Caribbean wreck dives.
