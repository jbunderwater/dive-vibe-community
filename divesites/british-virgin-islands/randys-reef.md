## Randy's Reef

A deep-water reef and harbour east of Little Harbour, known for large rays resting in front of the beach club and proximity to the Rhone's Anchor site — avoid the area in the afternoons when local fishermen set nets.

## Overview

Randy's Reef sits east of Little Harbour in the Sir Francis Drake Channel, in a deep-water harbour used by both recreational divers and local commercial fishermen. The reef provides mooring on the western side, with deeper anchorage options in the southeastern corner. The site is closely associated with neighboring Rhone's Anchor — many boats visit both on the same dive trip, as they lie within swimming distance of each other.

The harbour character differs from the more dramatic wall and canyon sites nearby: this is a reef and sandy-bottom environment, with the distinctive draw of large southern stingrays and eagle rays commonly seen resting or cruising in front of the beach club structure. The site reaches 24 meters at its deepest.

## Site Information

- **Location**: East of Little Harbour, Sir Francis Drake Channel
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–25 meters
- **Current**: Low
- **Swell**: Low
- **Best Time**: Morning dives preferred; avoid afternoons (fishing net activity)

## Marine Life

Large southern stingrays and eagle rays are regularly sighted in front of the beach club area. The reef supports a typical BVI reef community: parrotfish, wrasse, snapper, grunt, damselfish, and chromis. Look along the reef structure for moray eels in crevices and lobsters under ledges. The harbour environment also attracts smaller juvenile fish species that shelter in the reef structure.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the mooring on the western side and work along the reef structure. The depth range from the shallows to the 24-meter maximum covers several distinct habitat zones. Rays are most commonly encountered in the shallower section near the beach club. Mid-range depths on the reef wall offer typical Caribbean reef life. The southeastern area offers the deepest portion of the dive — plan bottom time accordingly and begin your ascent with adequate air for a 5-meter safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from the harbour. Mooring on the western side; limited space so arrive early. Entry by giant stride or back roll. During afternoon hours, local fishermen set nets in the area — be aware of net lines during both the dive and surface interval, and exercise caution navigating the harbour approach.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Plan for a morning dive to avoid the afternoon net-laying by local fishermen
- Combine with a visit to Rhone's Anchor, which lies between Fearless and Randy's Reef
- Spend time in the shallower section in front of the beach club where rays congregate
- The site is close enough to the Rhone park area to make a good complementary dive
- Watch for mooring availability — space is limited on the western side

## Safety Considerations

The primary hazard specific to this site is fishing net activity in the afternoons — do not dive if nets are visible in the water, and surface with care if there is any chance of net activity above. Standard precautions apply: maintain buoyancy on the reef, carry an SMB, and dive within your certification limits.

## Photography

Wide-angle for ray encounters in the sandy shallows. The reef sections offer standard Caribbean reef photography. Rays resting or hovering near the beach club structure provide open-water portrait opportunities. Afternoon light filtering down can create good conditions if nets are not a factor.
