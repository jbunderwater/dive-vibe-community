## Rhone Reef

One of the most vividly coloured reefs in the BVI, adjacent to the RMS Rhone wreck site, with rocky canyons, nurse sharks, turtles, and visibility that is best in the northern section.

## Overview

Rhone Reef sits within the Rhone National Marine Park, a protected marine area of nearly 800 acres between Dead Chest Island and Salt Island. While the RMS Rhone is the park's most famous attraction, the adjacent reef is considered one of the BVI's most colourful dive sites in its own right. The reef features extensive soft and hard coral formations alongside rocky canyons and crevices that provide habitat for a diverse marine community. The site functions largely independently of weather conditions — a useful characteristic in an area where swell can sometimes limit other dives — though occasional currents can run through the site. Visibility is notably better in the northern section of the reef, where cleaner water conditions prevail.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Adjacent to the RMS Rhone wreck, between Dead Chest Island and Salt Island, BVI
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 21 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Better in the northern section; variable with currents
- **Current**: Low to occasionally demanding
- **Swell**: High
- **Best Time**: Year-round; relatively weather-independent

## Marine Life

Nurse sharks are commonly encountered resting in the rocky canyons and under ledge overhangs. Sea turtles are frequent visitors to the reef. Occasional reef sharks pass through the site. The hard and soft coral structures support a broad community of colorful Caribbean reef fish throughout the formation. The crevices and nooks throughout the reef shelter smaller invertebrates and juvenile fish that reward careful examination.

## Dive Profile

Enter the water and orient toward the reef structure, which features a series of rocky canyons running through the coral formations. Explore the canyon systems at mid-depth (12–20 meters), looking into the rocky overhangs for nurse sharks and examining crevices for smaller species. The northern portion of the reef tends to have cleaner water and better visibility, making it the preferred area for photography and fish watching. The reef's topography varies between more open coral flats and tighter canyon passages — both are worth exploring. Surface with a safety stop and deploy SMB for boat pickup. This site can be combined with a dive on the nearby RMS Rhone, though the wreck dive typically takes precedence given its iconic status.

## Entry and Exit

Access by boat only from operators including Dive BVI, Blue Water Divers, and Sail Caribbean Divers. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Occasional currents can be present — assess before descent. Deploy SMB during safety stop for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Head to the northern section for best visibility conditions
- The rocky canyons are where nurse sharks tend to rest during the day — approach slowly and from a distance to avoid disturbing them
- This site can be conveniently combined with an RMS Rhone dive on the same day
- The site is relatively weather-independent, making it a good backup option when swell affects other sites

## Safety Considerations

Currents can occasionally be stronger than expected in the channel area. The site is rated Advanced, in part due to the exposed location and variable currents. Always dive with a buddy. Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB). The Rhone Marine Park has mooring buoys — use them; anchoring is not permitted in the park.

## Photography

The high colour saturation of the reef — dense soft corals and hard coral structures — makes Rhone Reef an excellent reef photography site. Wide-angle shots benefit from the dramatic rock canyon walls as compositional elements. Nurse sharks in the canyons make compelling subjects with the textured rock as background. The turtle encounters in open water reward divers patient enough to position for a clean shot against the water column. Strobes or video lights are recommended in the deeper canyon sections.
