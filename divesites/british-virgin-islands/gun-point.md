## Gun Point

A shallow, uncrowded shore-entry reef accessible via a dirt road, offering colorful tropical fish over an offshore coral reef with excellent visibility in clear, shallow water.

## Overview

Gun Point is a hidden gem in the western BVI, reached via a dirt road to a white sand beach from which divers enter directly into the water. The offshore reef sits in very shallow water — maximum depth around 6 meters (20 ft) — making it a low-pressure environment with naturally excellent visibility due to the minimal depth. The site is notably uncrowded compared to better-known BVI spots, and the clarity of the water at this depth means colors remain vivid without relying on a torch. The shore entry approach gives the site a self-guided, exploratory character.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Western BVI
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 6 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Excellent; shallow depth keeps colors vivid
- **Current**: Generally low
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmer conditions in summer

## Marine Life

The reef supports a range of colorful tropical fish typical of healthy Caribbean shallow-water habitats. Parrotfish, angelfish, and various reef species are present throughout. The shallow coral formations attract invertebrates and small reef fish that benefit from the excellent light penetration at this depth.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the white sand beach and swim out to the offshore reef. The shallow maximum depth of 6 meters means bottom time is essentially unlimited on a single tank for most certification levels. Follow the coral formations along the reef edge, watching for fish in the coral heads and between the formations. The site is small enough to cover completely in a single dive. Exit back along your entry path to the beach.

## Entry and Exit

Shore entry from the white sand beach reached by a dirt road. Assess surf and surge conditions before entering — while generally calm, exposed locations can be affected by swell. Use fins in shallow water to avoid contact with the reef. Exit along the same beach entry point.

## Tips and Recommendations

- A good site for snorkeling as well as diving given the shallow depth and excellent visibility
- Confirm road conditions and access before driving — the dirt road approach may be affected after heavy rain
- The uncrowded nature of the site makes it a good option for those seeking a quiet, unhurried dive
- Pack out all equipment carefully — the remote access means there are no nearby facilities

## Safety Considerations

Check weather and wave conditions before entering from shore. The shallow depth reduces decompression risk but exposed sites can experience unexpected surge. Always dive with a buddy. Be aware of any boat traffic in the area when surfacing.

## Photography

Shallow depth and excellent natural light make this an ideal site for natural-light photography without strobes. The clear, bright water keeps colors vivid to the eye and in photographs. Wide-angle shots of reef formations with natural sunlight work particularly well.
