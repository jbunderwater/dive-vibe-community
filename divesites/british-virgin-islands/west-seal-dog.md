## West Seal Dog

An advanced cave and cavern dive on a secluded volcanic islet in the Dog Islands group, where colorful sponges and corals pack rocky formations and lobster, mackerel, and nurse sharks reward patient exploration.

## Overview

West Seal Dog is the western islet of the Dog Islands group, lying between Tortola and Virgin Gorda — approximately 15 minutes by boat from Spanish Town, Virgin Gorda. The entire Dog Islands area has been a national park since 1974, and West Seal Dog is one of its more secluded dive sites, rarely crowded even during peak season. The underwater topography is volcanic in origin: caves, ledges, and steeply dropping rocky formations blanketed in colorful sponges and encrusting corals create a sheltered habitat for a wide variety of fish and invertebrates. Divers enter the shallows and follow the wall outward as it drops to significant depth, with abundant life throughout. Swell can be high at this exposed site, so conditions must be checked before diving.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Dog Islands group, between Tortola and Virgin Gorda; ~15 minutes by boat from Spanish Town
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Near-excellent in settled conditions
- **Current**: Low
- **Swell**: High (exposed site — check conditions)
- **Best Time**: Calmer summer months; avoid during strong northern swells

## Marine Life

The cave and ledge formations shelter lobster throughout the site. Spanish mackerel and four-eyed butterflyfish are regularly sighted, and nurse sharks rest in the deeper sections. The colorful sponges and encrusting corals attract a variety of reef fish. The secluded nature of the site means marine life is relatively undisturbed.

## Dive Profile

Enter in the shallows and follow the wall downward as it drops through caves and ledges. The upper section features the most abundant sponge and coral encrustation — worth exploring slowly before committing to depth. The deeper formations hold lobster in crevices and nurse sharks resting on the sandy patches. Ascend back along the wall with good air reserves, completing your safety stop in the shallows before the boat pickup.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Virgin Gorda or Tortola operators. Entry is via giant stride or back roll. Swell levels at this exposed site can affect entry and exit — follow the dive crew's guidance. Deploy an SMB during your safety stop for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Dive on calm days only — West Seal Dog is exposed and swell can make the site challenging or inaccessible
- This is a secluded site; expect fewer divers than at more popular Dog Islands spots
- Explore the caves and ledges thoroughly — lobster and shy reef fish tuck into every crevice
- The colorful sponge encrustation rewards close-focus photography

## Safety Considerations

This site is rated Advanced due to the exposed location, depth, and cave/cavern features. Strong swell is possible and can complicate entry and exit. Dive within your certification limits. Cave sections require proper overhead environment training if you intend to penetrate beyond daylight zones. Always carry an SMB. Dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The sponge and coral-encrusted rocky formations make excellent wide-angle subjects, and the cave entrances offer dramatic framing opportunities. Low ambient light inside the caves means a dive torch is valuable. The secluded setting means you can work subjects without distraction from other diver groups.
