## Cow's Mouth

A distinctive rocky reef on Virgin Gorda's northwest shore named for a boulder shaped like an open cow's mouth, offering exceptional visibility and the chance to explore the interior of the rock formation itself.

## Overview

Cow's Mouth sits on the northwest side of Virgin Gorda, in close proximity to Mountain Point. The site takes its name from a large rock formation whose opening resembles the mouth of a cow — an unusual underwater landmark that divers use as a focal point for the dive. Visibility at this site is notably good, frequently reaching 30 meters (100 feet), making it particularly suited to wide-angle photography and fish watching. The reef features cracks and crevices where smaller colorful species shelter, and eagle rays and barracuda are commonly encountered in the water column. Experienced divers can swim through or into the rock formation itself, where exotic fish are often found in the sheltered interior.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northwest Virgin Gorda, near Mountain Point, BVI
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: Up to 30 meters (100 feet)
- **Current**: Low
- **Swell**: Can be high
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmer in winter dry season

## Marine Life

Eagle rays are a highlight at Cow's Mouth, spotted cruising along the reef. Barracuda and jacks move through the water column above the structure. Inside the rock formation, the sheltered environment attracts a variety of smaller, colorful fish including species not commonly seen on the open reef. The surrounding reef hosts a wide assortment of smaller Caribbean species in the nooks and crevices of the structure.

## Dive Profile

Dive begins from a mooring near the rock formation. Descend and orient to the distinctive cow's-mouth-shaped opening in the rock, which serves as the primary navigational landmark. Explore the exterior reef at depths down to about 15 meters, watching the open water above for eagle rays and jacks. Intermediate and advanced divers can enter the rock formation and investigate the sheltered interior. The maximum depth is manageable for most certified divers, and the excellent visibility allows for easy orientation throughout the dive. Return to the mooring for a safety stop before surfacing.

## Entry and Exit

Access by boat only. Entry via giant stride or back roll. The site can experience swell from the northwest, so entry conditions should be assessed prior to descent. Deploy SMB during safety stop for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The interior of the rock formation is best explored by divers with good buoyancy control
- Visibility here is among the best in the Virgin Gorda area — good conditions for photography
- Watch the water column above the reef for eagle ray passes
- The site is close to Mountain Point, making it convenient to combine with nearby dives

## Safety Considerations

Swell can be pronounced at this exposed northwest-facing site; conditions should be checked before diving. The interior of the rock should only be entered by divers comfortable with overhead environments and proper buoyancy control. Always dive with a buddy. Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB).

## Photography

The exceptional visibility makes Cow's Mouth ideal for wide-angle work — the dramatic rock formation, eagle ray encounters, and schooling jacks all lend themselves to broad compositions. The rock's interior offers dramatic framing opportunities with natural light filtering through the opening. Macro subjects can be found among the reef crevices.
