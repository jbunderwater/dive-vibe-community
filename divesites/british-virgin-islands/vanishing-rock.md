## Vanishing Rock

A shallow exposed pinnacle off Salt Island's eastern shore with some of the BVI's largest pillar coral formations, home to "Sergeant Major City" and "Sergeant Major Suburbs."

## Overview

Vanishing Rock is a rocky pinnacle on the eastern side of Salt Island, with Cooper Island to the east. The site takes its name from the way the top of the pinnacle breaks the surface and then disappears below the waves — there, then not. It lies in the passage between Cooper and Salt Islands, where currents sweep through and create an exceptionally healthy reef system. The water clarity and nutrient flow support extensive pillar coral structures, including two named formations: Sergeant Major City in the deeper section and Sergeant Major Suburbs beyond it — both architectural pillars of coral teeming with reef life.

The site sits about 20 minutes by boat from Road Town and is considerably less crowded than nearby RMS Rhone, making it a rewarding choice for divers seeking a quieter experience in the same area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Eastern side of Salt Island, passage between Salt and Cooper Islands
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 13 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–30 meters
- **Current**: Light to moderate (can be strong; site may be undivable in high current)
- **Swell**: Low
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmer in summer

## Marine Life

The reef supports a broad range of small reef life. Expect anemones with cleaner shrimps, squirrelfish, grunts, sergeant majors in large numbers (especially around the pillar coral formations), bigeye snappers, jawfish, blennies, Spanish hogfish, green moray eels, lobsters, and nurse sharks resting under ledges. Gorgonians and colorful sponges coat the rocky sections and sandy bottom between formations.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the mooring and descend to the top of the pinnacle, which breaks the surface. Work your way down the rock structure into the gorgonian and sponge-covered lower sections. Deeper in the passage, look for Sergeant Major City — the large pillar coral stands are unmistakable — and continue to Sergeant Major Suburbs for more of the same dramatic formations. Maximum depth is around 13 meters. The site rewards slow, attentive diving; look into crevices for jawfish and blennies.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access, approximately 20 minutes from Road Town. Entry by giant stride or back roll from the mooring line. Watch for current on descent — if it is running hard, reconsider or shorten the dive. Exit via the mooring or as briefed by your operator with SMB deployment.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Check current conditions before committing to the site — strong tidal flow through the passage can make it undivable
- Time your dive for slack water if possible for easier navigation and better photography conditions
- Spend time around the pillar coral formations; look up at the sergeant major schools for good wide-angle photos
- The site is often quieter than nearby Rhone dives — a good choice for a second dive
- Look carefully at sandy patches near the base of the pillars for jawfish

## Safety Considerations

Currents in the Salt Island Passage can be strong and may build quickly with tidal changes. Monitor the flow throughout the dive and stay close to the reef to shelter from the current if needed. Always carry an SMB for ascent.

## Photography

The pillar coral formations are the standout subjects — wide-angle works well to capture the full height of Sergeant Major City with fish life around them. Macro opportunities exist on the rocky sections: look for blennies, cleaner shrimps, and the faces of green moray eels tucked into crevices.
