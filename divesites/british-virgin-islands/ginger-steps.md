# Ginger Steps

A dramatic terraced wall on the south face of Ginger Island — a step-form topography of coral terraces descending to 29 metres in the open waters of the Sir Francis Drake Channel, with current-driven pelagics and exceptional large sponge growth.

## Overview

Ginger Steps takes its name from the distinctive terrace topography on the south face of Ginger Island — a series of coral-covered step ledges descending from the shallows to 29 metres, each step creating a horizontal platform before the next vertical drop. The site faces the open Drake Channel and is exposed to consistent current that delivers nutrients to the wall, supporting impressive barrel sponge and tube sponge growth on the step faces. Black coral bushes develop on the deeper sections. The isolated position of Ginger Island and the open-ocean exposure means this site receives less dive traffic than the more sheltered BVI sites, and the fish life is accordingly less habituated and more interesting. Visibility reaches 25–35 metres in favourable conditions. Water temperature is 26–28°C.

## Marine Life

The current-exposed position of Ginger Steps concentrates pelagic species. Spotted eagle rays cruise the open water beyond the terrace edge, and schools of bar jacks and horse-eye jacks sweep through the current lanes. Caribbean reef sharks are regularly seen along the deeper terrace sections. Hawksbill turtles patrol the upper steps where coral and sponge growth is densest. The step ledges shelter large spiny lobsters and Caribbean reef octopus. Green morays and spotted morays occupy crevices in the terrace faces.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the first terrace at 10–12 metres and work down the steps progressively, spending time on each ledge before dropping to the next level. The most impressive terrain is in the 15–25 metre zone where barrel sponges reach their greatest size and current-borne pelagics are most active. Ascend back through the steps, spending time in the 8–12 metre zone for the safety stop in well-lit, fish-dense shallows.

## Entry and Exit

Boat dive from operators running to the southern islands — typically a 30–45 minute run from Road Town. Live-boat pickup may be standard depending on current conditions. Giant stride entry; DSMB required. Ginger Steps is often paired with Alice in Wonderland on the other side of Ginger Island for a varied two-tank day.

## Tips and Recommendations

The terrace structure means there is always a shallower option if current or conditions require — retreat to the higher step rather than fighting deeper. The exposed southern face dives best with a predictable directional current rather than at full slack. Combine with Alice in Wonderland on the north face of Ginger Island for a complete view of the island's diving character.

## Safety Considerations

The open-ocean facing position means conditions can change significantly with wind direction and tidal phase. Only dive with an operator who specifically monitors conditions for this site. DSMB required — the open Drake Channel position means proper ascent signalling is essential. Do not descend to 29 metres without checking available NDL time given prior dives.

## Photography

The step terrace topography creates compelling layered compositions — shooting across a terrace face with diver on a lower step and the blue open water beyond is the characteristic Ginger Steps image. The large barrel sponges and black coral bushes on the deeper terraces are excellent strobe-lit close-focus subjects. Eagle ray passes in the open water provide blue-water wide-angle opportunities.
