## Ring Dove Rock

A quietly spectacular reef known for low diver traffic and outstanding invertebrate life — giant barrel sponges, golden zoanthids, crinoids, and gorgonians form the backdrop for a diverse cast of fish including yellowhead jawfish, harlequin basslets, and moray eels.

## Overview

Ring Dove Rock appeals particularly to divers who prefer less-crowded sites. While it lacks the celebrity status of the RMS Rhone or The Indians, the reef's patient divers are rewarded with one of the BVI's most diverse invertebrate communities and a fish population that includes several species rarely seen at busier sites. The reef structure features excellent hard and soft coral development with a rich assemblage of filter-feeding invertebrates — giant barrel sponges, golden zoanthids, crinoids, red rope sponges, gorgonians, and flowing water plants create layered habitat across the reef surface. The calm conditions and low current make it accessible to intermediate divers and ideal for long, exploratory dives.

## Marine Life

The reef supports an exceptionally broad species list. Butterflyfish and rock beauties are abundant on the coral surfaces. Bar jacks move in loose schools above the reef. Filefishes hover near coral heads. Yellowhead jawfish are found in the sandy areas adjacent to the reef — watch for them hovering above their burrow entrances. Sergeant majors patrol the reef crest. Juvenile angelfish add color to the ledge faces. Lizardfish rest on flat surfaces, nearly invisible. Moray eels occupy crevices throughout the reef. Lobsters shelter under the larger overhangs. Harlequin basslets inhabit deeper reef sections. Red and white heart urchins appear in the sandy patches. Tube worms and tunicates colonize available rock surfaces throughout. Sea turtles are occasionally sighted.

## Dive Profile

Enter the water and settle into an exploratory loop pattern around the reef — the site lends itself to relaxed, unhurried diving rather than linear navigation. Begin by scanning the large barrel sponge formations and gorgonian fans in the middle reef zone (8–15 meters), then work down toward the base at 18 meters where morays and lobsters concentrate in the larger overhangs. Sandy patches around the reef edges are worth examining for yellowhead jawfish activity. The reef crest at 5–8 meters supports the brightest invertebrate colours with good natural light. Divers can make multiple loops without repeating themselves given the diversity of small species to find. The calm conditions and low current support extended bottom times.

## Entry and Exit

Access by boat only. Entry via giant stride or back roll. Conditions are typically calm with low current, making this an easy dive for intermediate-level divers. Deploy SMB during safety stop for boat pickup.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Move slowly and scan horizontally along the reef face — the most interesting species are often small and easily missed
- Yellowhead jawfish are found near the sandy-reef interface; look for their heads protruding from burrow entrances
- Harlequin basslets occupy specific ledge areas in the deeper sections — note their locations to return during a second dive
- The site's low diver traffic means animals are often less skittish than at more popular BVI sites

## Safety Considerations

Ring Dove Rock is one of the BVI's more accessible dives with low current and moderate maximum depth. Be aware of occasional swell. Dive within your certification limits. Always dive with a buddy. Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB).

## Photography

Ring Dove Rock is outstanding for invertebrate and fish macro photography. The richness and variety of species provides subjects that more famous sites often lack. Yellowhead jawfish require patience — a slow approach from a distance with strobes preset rewards the patient photographer. Giant barrel sponges and gorgonians make compelling wide-angle foreground elements with the reef in the background. Tube worms and tunicates reward extreme close-up work. The calm, low-current conditions allow strobes to be positioned precisely without drift interference.
