## ハナダイの根 (Hanadai no Ne (Anthias Pinnacle))

Named for the spectacular schools of akane-hanagoi (scalefin anthias) and kingyo-hanagoi (sea goldie) that swarm over this rocky reef head off Irabu Island, creating dense curtain-like formations of orange and red that divers describe as one of Miyako's most visually overwhelming sites.

## Overview

Hanadai no Ne is a rocky pinnacle system off Irabu Island. The pinnacle rises from approximately 18 metres to within a few metres of the surface, its upper sections blanketed in hard coral and surrounded by enormous schools of scalefin anthias and sea goldies. The site is often combined with a visit to the adjacent Snake Hole -- a blue cave system with an air dome accessible from 8 metres depth, where pressure changes create transient fog effects and divers can surface inside an enclosed rock chamber. Three distinct dive environments are accessible in the same area: the pinnacle top, the Snake Hole cave entrance, and a deeper wall section with scorpionfish and pipefish.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Irabu Island, Miyako-jima, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters (typical range 10-18m)
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 metres
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to September

## Marine Life

Flame anthias and peach fairy basslets in dense schools are the signature sight. Crevices in the pinnacle harbour frogfish, leaf scorpionfish, nudibranchs, and seahorse relatives. Yellowback fusiliers school in the mid-water. The reef base has garden eels and goby-shrimp pairs in the sand.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the base of the pinnacle at 10-15 metres and work upward, circling the bommie to observe the anthias clouds from different angles. Explore crevices for macro subjects. If conditions allow, visit the nearby Snake Hole air dome. The shallow profile permits long dives of 50-60+ minutes.

## Safety Considerations

Very accessible site suitable for all certification levels. Minimal current. The only hazard is the temptation to surface inside Snake Hole without proper briefing -- confirm protocol with your guide.

## Photography

Both macro and wide-angle excel here. The anthias clouds against coral backgrounds are the wide-angle shot. Macro subjects include frogfish, leaf scorpionfish, and nudibranchs in crevices. One of Miyako's most colourful photo sites.
