## ハナダイの根 (Hanadai no Ne (Anthias Pinnacle))

Named for the spectacular schools of akane-hanagoi (scalefin anthias) and kingyo-hanagoi (sea goldie) that swarm over this rocky reef head off Irabu Island, creating dense curtain-like formations of orange and red that divers describe as one of Miyako's most visually overwhelming sites.

## Overview

Hanadai no Ne is a rocky pinnacle system off Irabu Island. The pinnacle rises from approximately 18 metres to within a few metres of the surface, its upper sections blanketed in hard coral and surrounded by enormous schools of scalefin anthias and sea goldies. The site is often combined with a visit to the adjacent Snake Hole -- a blue cave system with an air dome accessible from 8 metres depth, where pressure changes create transient fog effects and divers can surface inside an enclosed rock chamber. Three distinct dive environments are accessible in the same area: the pinnacle top, the Snake Hole cave entrance, and a deeper wall section with scorpionfish and pipefish.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Irabu Island, Miyako-jima, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 metres
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: May to September

## Marine Life

Scalefin anthias (akane-hanagoi) and sea goldies (kingyo-hanagoi) in dense curtain-like schools are the defining sight -- thousands of fish in shimmering orange and pink. Crevices harbour scorpionfish, dragonets, jawfish, filefish, and sea goldie dottybacks. The Snake Hole cave section hosts soldierfish, cleaner shrimp, and the fog-producing air dome. Giant trevally and bluefin trevally occasionally visit. Nudibranchs populate the pinnacle walls.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the pinnacle base at 15-18 metres and work upward, circling to observe the anthias clouds from different angles. Explore crevices in the upper sections for macro subjects. Transition to the Snake Hole entrance at 8 metres: enter the cave, ascend to the air dome to experience the pressure-generated fog, then exit and return to the reef. The multi-environment shallow profile permits dives of 60+ minutes.

## Safety Considerations

The Snake Hole section is an enclosed overhead environment (the Blue Cave is not accessible by snorkelling or sea kayak). Briefing before entering is essential -- confirm air dome etiquette and bailout protocol. The main pinnacle section is accessible to Open Water divers. Intermediate classification reflects the combined dive including the cave section.

## Photography

Both macro and wide-angle excel here. The anthias clouds against coral backgrounds are the wide-angle shot. Macro subjects include frogfish, leaf scorpionfish, and nudibranchs in crevices. One of Miyako's most colourful photo sites.
