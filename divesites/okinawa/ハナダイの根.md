## ハナダイの根 (Hanadai no Ne (Anthias Pinnacle))

Named 'Anthias Pinnacle' for the spectacular schools of flame anthias and peach fairy basslets that swarm over this coral pinnacle, creating a scene described by Japanese divers as 'like being invited to an underwater dragon palace.'

## Overview

Hanadai no Ne is a coral bommie and pinnacle system in the shallow waters off Shimoji Island. The pinnacle rises from 15 metres to within a few metres of the surface, blanketed in hard and soft corals. Dense clouds of flame anthias (Kingyohanadai) and peach fairy basslets (Akanehanagoi) envelop the pinnacle in shimmering orange and pink. The site is often combined with a visit to the nearby Snake Hole, an air-filled dome where divers can surface inside a rock chamber.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Shimoji Island, Miyako-jima, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 metres
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: Year-round; sheltered location

## Marine Life

Flame anthias and peach fairy basslets in dense schools are the signature sight. Crevices in the pinnacle harbour frogfish, leaf scorpionfish, nudibranchs, and seahorse relatives. Yellowback fusiliers school in the mid-water. The reef base has garden eels and goby-shrimp pairs in the sand.

## Dive Profile

Descend to the base of the pinnacle at 10-15 metres and work upward, circling the bommie to observe the anthias clouds from different angles. Explore crevices for macro subjects. If conditions allow, visit the nearby Snake Hole air dome. The shallow profile permits long dives of 50-60+ minutes.

## Safety Considerations

Very accessible site suitable for all certification levels. Minimal current. The only hazard is the temptation to surface inside Snake Hole without proper briefing -- confirm protocol with your guide.

## Photography

Both macro and wide-angle excel here. The anthias clouds against coral backgrounds are the wide-angle shot. Macro subjects include frogfish, leaf scorpionfish, and nudibranchs in crevices. One of Miyako's most colourful photo sites.
