## 阿真牛瀬・あまうしじ (Ama Ushiji)

Extensive fields of large table corals -- some exceeding 1 metre in diameter -- create one of the most visually impressive coral landscapes in the Kerama Islands. The site sits near Ama Beach on Zamami Island's eastern side.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. Extensive fields of large table corals create a distinctive coral garden at 5-15 metres. Marine Diving Japan describes a site in this area (Amuroushiji) as featuring table corals, anthias, damselfish, ribbon eels, clownfish, and blotcheye soldierfish. Occasional drift diving may be required when currents are running.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Zamami Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Table corals are the dominant feature. Anthias, damselfish, ribbon eels, clownfish, and blotcheye soldierfish are confirmed by Marine Diving Japan for this area. Hawkfish perch on the coral tables. Napoleon wrasse (Humphead wrasse) sightings in this area are reported by a single operator (Aqua-Life) only and should be considered unconfirmed until verified by a second source.
