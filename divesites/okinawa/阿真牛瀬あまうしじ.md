## 阿真牛瀬・あまうしじ (Ama Ushiji)

Extensive fields of large table corals -- some exceeding 1 metre in diameter -- create one of the most visually impressive coral landscapes in the Kerama Islands. Napoleon wrasse have been sighted here, which is unusual for the Kerama region.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. Extensive fields of large table corals -- some exceeding 1 metre in diameter -- create one of the most visually impressive coral landscapes in the Kerama Islands. Napoleon wrasse have been sighted here, which is unusual for the Kerama region.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Zamami Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Table corals (dominant feature), Napoleon wrasse (occasional), anthias, hawkfish, clownfish in anemones. The large coral tables shelter damselfish and small reef species.
