## Middle Book

Middle Book (ミドルブック) is a sand and coral bommie dive site in the Yaeyama Islands near Taketomi Island, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan.

## Overview

Middle Book lies south of Taketomi Island in the Sekisei Lagoon area. The site features a sand floor with scattered coral heads (コモンシコロサンゴの根), typical of the Yaeyama shallow reef landscape. It is primarily a macro and schooling-fish site, not a manta ray cleaning station — the manta cleaning stations of Ishigaki-jima (Manta Scramble, Manta City Point) are separate named sites.

The site name is reportedly named after a local dive instructor ("Naka" = middle, "Moto" = book). Maximum depth is 18 m. The sandy bottom and scattered coral structure support garden eels, jawfish, small crustaceans, and anthias; the quiet sand floor is suited to macro photography.

> **QA Note (2026-06-21):** A previous description incorrectly described Middle Book as a manta ray cleaning station. This is FALSE. Confirmed sources (marinediving.com's 50-site Ishigaki guide, diving blog records) describe Middle Book as a sand-bottom macro site with scattered coral bommies. Manta ray cleaning stations in the Ishigaki area are specific named sites (Manta Scramble, Manta City Point, Panari Big Corner) and do not include Middle Book.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Taketomi South, Yaeyama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters

## Marine Life

Garden eels, jawfish, and small crustaceans in the sandy areas between coral bommies. Anthias and chromis are present on the coral heads. This is a macro photography site; specific species at this exact site should be confirmed with local operators.
