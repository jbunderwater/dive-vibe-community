## 宇論の崎・うるるのさち (Ururu no Sachi)

A very shallow, relaxed dive site near Ama Beach on Zamami Island. Average depth only 5 metres with a maximum of 11 metres, making it ideal for extended dives and complete beginners. Multiple species of clownfish -- at least 4 species -- can be found here, described by visiting divers as a 'wonderful point.'

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. A very shallow, relaxed dive site near Ama Beach on Zamami Island. Average depth only 5 metres with a maximum of 11 metres, making it ideal for extended dives and complete beginners. Multiple species of clownfish -- at least 4 species -- can be found here, described by visiting divers as a 'wonderful point.'

## Site Information

- **Location**: Zamami Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 11 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Four or more clownfish species (Clark's, tomato, pink skunk, orange-fin), blue-green chromis, butterflyfish, anthias, jawfish, filefish, trumpetfish. One of the best sites in Kerama for clownfish diversity.
