## 砂辺

砂辺 (Sunabe) is one of Okinawa's most accessible and beloved shore dive sites, located on the west coast of the main island near Chatan. A long concrete seawall runs parallel to the beach, and divers enter directly from the shore to find a shallow reef system packed with life year-round.

## Overview

Sunabe Seawall is the go-to shore dive for divers based in central Okinawa, stretching several hundred metres along the coast near Mihama. The reef begins immediately below the entry points and slopes gradually to around 18 metres over sandy rubble. Visibility is generally good at 10–20 metres, and the calm conditions make it ideal for skills practice, night diving, and repeated exploratory dives. Water temperature ranges from 23°C in winter to 30°C in summer.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Chatan, Okinawa Main Island
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10–20 meters
- **Current**: Mild, occasionally moderate
- **Best Time**: Year-round; summer for warm water and turtle encounters

## Marine Life

Sunabe is famous for its resident sea turtles — both green and hawksbill turtles are seen on almost every dive. Lionfish, frogfish, and a variety of nudibranchs reward macro photographers, while schools of fusiliers and glassfish hover over coral heads. Octopus, moray eels, and flatfish are common in the rubble zones. Seasonal visitors include juvenile fish in summer and occasional pelagics moving along the wall.

## Dive Profile

Enter from one of the marked seawall steps and descend to the reef crest at 3–5 metres. The reef slopes to around 12–15 metres before giving way to sand and rubble toward 18 metres. Most diving is done along the reef face at 6–12 metres where coral density and fish life are highest. A long, leisurely swim north or south along the wall makes for an easy and rewarding dive. Ascend slowly and perform a safety stop before returning to the steps.

## Entry and Exit

Entry points are clearly marked along the Chatan seawall. Steps and metal ladders make entry and exit straightforward even in moderate surge. Wear booties to protect your feet on the concrete and rocks. Park at one of the nearby dive shops or beach car parks. Night diving is popular here with a torch — the reef transforms after dark with hunting octopus and sleeping turtles.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Arrive early on weekends to secure parking and an uncrowded entry point
- Night dives at Sunabe are outstanding — book a guided night dive with a local shop
- The central section near the main car park tends to have the highest turtle density
- Bring a macro lens — the rubble zones hold exceptional small critters
- Local dive shops along the seawall rent gear and offer guided dives

## Safety Considerations

Boat traffic exists offshore — always dive within the marked dive zone and surface with an SMB if you have drifted. Surge can make entry and exit challenging after strong winds. Watch for sharp coral and sea urchins near the seawall base. Always dive within your certification limits.

## Photography

Sunabe is an outstanding macro site with nudibranchs, frogfish, and ghost pipefish found in the rubble. Wide-angle opportunities come from turtle encounters and schooling fish on the reef. The shallow depth allows extended bottom time in good light — morning dives offer the clearest water and best natural light on the reef face.
