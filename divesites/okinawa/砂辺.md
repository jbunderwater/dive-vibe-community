## 砂辺 (Sunabe Seawall)

One of Okinawa's most iconic shore dives, stretching along approximately 2 kilometres of the Chatan coast near American Village. Famous for a massive soft coral field described as the size of five football fields -- a dense carpet of colourful non-reef-building soft corals unlike most Okinawa dive sites which feature hard coral.

## Overview

Sunabe Seawall is the most accessible and most-dived site on Okinawa's main island. Entry is via concrete stairs along the seawall, with a steel chain guide leading offshore to a shallow valley at approximately 6 metres (home to a famous underwater mailbox, installed April 2016 by the Chatan Diving Cooperative). The reef then drops to a flat sandy bottom along a low wall at 15-20 metres. The soft coral gardens are the main attraction -- dense carpets of colour that look like an underwater flower park. The site is equally popular for night diving, when the reef comes alive with cuttlefish, octopus, and hunting moray eels. Many dive shops use Sunabe for OW certification courses due to the easy shore access and consistently calm conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Chatan, Main Okinawa Island, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-20 metres (average 15m; can drop to 3m near shore after rain)
- **Current**: None to negligible -- one of the calmest sites in Okinawa
- **Water Temperature**: 28-30C summer, 20-24C winter
- **Best Time**: Year-round; best visibility May to October

## Marine Life

The soft coral fields host multiple anemonefish species around resident anemones. Sandy areas between coral heads harbour yellowhead jawfish and various goby species. Sea snakes are commonly encountered throughout the dive. Octopus, nudibranchs, and cuttlefish are regular finds, especially on night dives. Green turtles visit the reef. Pygmy seahorses and juvenile frogfish have been reported in crevices. Clownfish colonies -- reportedly around 25 individuals -- inhabit the reef edge anemones.

## Dive Profile

Enter via the seawall stairs and follow the chain guide offshore to the shallow reef at 5-7 metres. Explore the soft coral gardens at 7-15 metres, then drop to the sandy bottom at 18-20 metres if desired. Most of the best coral coverage is in the 8-15 metre range. Return along the wall to the chain guide for a straightforward exit. The site supports dives of 60-90 minutes easily.

## Safety Considerations

Shore entry can be tricky when swells break against the seawall -- check conditions before entering and use the chain for stability. The site is directly adjacent to a US military base and sees heavy foot traffic along the seawall; be aware of overhead hazards during entry and exit. Visibility near shore drops significantly after heavy rain.

## Photography

Macro is the primary approach -- soft corals with clownfish, jawfish in burrows, and nudibranchs provide excellent subjects. Wide-angle works for the soft coral fields. Night dives offer exceptional macro opportunities with cuttlefish, sleeping fish, and hunting crabs.
