## 砂辺

Sunabe (砂辺) is a renowned shore entry reef dive site in Okinawa, famous for its spectacular display of soft corals and beginner-friendly conditions.

## Overview

Sunabe Seawall is one of Okinawa's most popular shore diving destinations, offering exceptional access and favorable conditions for divers of all levels. The site features a diverse flower garden of soft corals in vibrant colors—pink, orange, yellow, and purple—creating an underwater landscape unlike other Kerama Island sites. With maximum depths around 18 meters and typically calm conditions, Sunabe is ideal for beginners, certification courses, and divers returning after time away from diving. Located on Okinawa's main island, the site offers 15-30 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-30°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Okinawa, Asia
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-40 meters (30-130 feet)
- **Current**: Variable, can be very strong
- **Best Time**: October to April (varies by location)

## Marine Life

The soft coral gardens attract abundant reef fish including sweetlips, fusiliers, and trevally. Common sightings include clownfish, butterfly fish, groupers, snappers, and sea turtles. Macro enthusiasts will find nudibranchs, frogfish, and various crustaceans. Seasonal visitors include rays and occasional reef sharks.

## Dive Profile

The site offers diving at depths ranging from shallow reef areas down to approximately 30 meters. Begin your dive in the shallower sections and gradually work deeper as conditions allow. The most abundant marine life is typically found between 5-20 meters. Plan your dive within your certification limits and allow adequate air for a safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Access is via stairs directly from the seawall, making it one of Japan's most accessible shore dives. Flat seas are the norm, though winter can bring waves up to 6 feet. Good conditions are necessary for safe entry and exit down the stairs. Descent follows the reef slope, with the dive ending at the exit point.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book with reputable local dive operators who know the site conditions
- Bring an underwater camera — this site offers excellent photography opportunities
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef
- Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB) and be prepared for current changes

## Safety Considerations

Sunabe Seawall is generally free of strong currents that characterize other Okinawa sites. Watch for sea urchins and small jellyfish. Winter months may bring wave action affecting entry/exit safety. Always check local weather and sea conditions before visiting. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Always dive with a buddy.

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
