## 新田洞窟・にたどうくつ (Nita Doukutsu (Nita Cave))

A spacious cavern system on the north side of Zamami Island with dramatic light effects. On sunny days, light pours through natural skylights (ceiling openings) in what Visit Okinawa describes as a "large cavern with natural skylights." The summer season produces the best light effect. The cavern is wide with natural light throughout, making it non-technical despite the cave classification.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park. The primary cave/cavern dive site on Zamami Island, located on the north side of the island. The cavern features natural skylights in the ceiling, producing dramatic light beam effects that Kerama cave diving is known for -- strong sun rays in summer penetrate the water through the ceiling openings, creating visible columns of light. The maximum depth is 10-15 metres, making this considerably more accessible than Miyako's deeper cave sites. Summer months produce the best light effects due to sun angle and water clarity.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Zamami Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Silver sweepers (schooling in dense masses inside the cavern) and big-eyed red soldierfish are confirmed by Visit Okinawa Japan for this site. Colourful corals around the cave entrance. The sheltered low-light interior attracts cave-dwelling species. Note: an earlier version of this description mentioned "pygmy seahorse colonies" -- this claim could not be confirmed in any available source for Nita Cave specifically and has been removed.
