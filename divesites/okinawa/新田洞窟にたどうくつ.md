## 新田洞窟・にたどうくつ (Nita Doukutsu (Nita Cave))

A spacious cave system on the north side of Zamami Island with dramatic light effects. On sunny days, a mystical 'light curtain' filters through cracks in the ceiling and is visible only from underwater. The cave is wide with multiple entry and exit points, making it non-technical despite the cave classification.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. A spacious cave system on the north side of Zamami Island with dramatic light effects. ## Site Information

- **Location**: North Zamami Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Colourful corals around the cave entrance, cave-dwelling crustaceans and small fish. Pygmy seahorse colonies have been reported by local dive shops. The sheltered interior attracts species that prefer low-light environments.
