## 新田洞窟・にたどうくつ (Nita Doukutsu (Nita Cave))

A large cavern on the north side of Zamami Island with natural skylights that create a 'light curtain' effect on clear days. Inside, dense schools of silver sweepers (girama-mosugiro) and big-eyed red soldierfish (menpachi) fill the cavern interior -- one of Kerama's best confirmed marine life encounters inside an overhead environment.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility. Nita Cave (新田洞窟) is a non-technical cavern -- the entrance is underwater at about 15 metres, but the exit is at the surface, so the dive involves entering below and ascending into the cave interior to observe the dramatic light effects and resident fish schools. The cave is spacious and well-lit through skylights, making it accessible for intermediate divers despite the overhead environment classification. Best visited in summer when the north side of Zamami is protected from prevailing winds. Surrounding coral gardens are worth exploring on the approach and exit.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North Zamami Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Silver sweepers (girama-mosugiro) in dense schools inside the cavern -- confirmed by Visit Okinawa and Underwater360. Big-eyed red soldierfish (menpachi) populate the ceiling and walls. Longnose hawkfish have been reported inside. The surrounding coral gardens host typical Kerama reef species. Pygmy seahorse sightings have been reported in the sea fans near the entrance but require independent verification.
