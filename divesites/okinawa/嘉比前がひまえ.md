## 嘉比前・がひまえ (Gahi Mae (Front of Gahi Island))

Located on the north side of uninhabited Gahi Island. White sand bottom with scattered rocks and coral bommies starting at 3 metres. Known as a macro photographer's paradise with garden eels, Yasha gobies, and various small crustaceans. Beautiful light ripple patterns on the white sand are a signature feature.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. Located on the north side of uninhabited Gahi Island. White sand bottom with scattered rocks and coral bommies starting at 3 metres. Known as a macro photographer's paradise with garden eels, Yasha gobies, and various small crustaceans. Beautiful light ripple patterns on the white sand are a signature feature.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gahi Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Garden eels dotting the sandy areas, Yasha gobies with partner shrimp, dwarf lionfish, eagle rays (occasional), cardinalfish, grouper. The white sand hosts a rich macro community.
