# 阿嘉桟橋前 (Aka Pier Front)

The outer reef in front of Aka Island's main pier — a convenient, well-dived site offering excellent coral health and reliable turtle encounters in "Kerama Blue" waters.

## Overview

Aka Pier Front (阿嘉桟橋前) describes the dive site directly in front of the main pier on Aka-jima (Aka Island), one of the three inhabited islands of the Kerama Islands group. Aka-jima is connected to adjacent Geruma-jima by a bridge and is a central hub for Kerama diving, with multiple dive operators based on the island. The proximity to the pier makes this a convenient site with short boat rides, and the outer reef system here is representative of the excellent coral health that has made the Kerama Islands famous. The site is suitable for both morning and afternoon dives and is commonly used for multi-dive days based at Aka Island accommodation.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Aka-jima, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (80–130 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate; generally sheltered near the pier
- **Best Time**: Year-round; peak conditions October to April

## Marine Life

The Kerama Islands' reef health supports exceptional wildlife density at all their sites. Aka Pier Front is particularly reliable for sea turtle encounters — both green and hawksbill turtles rest and forage in the reef area near the pier. Spotted sea slugs (nudibranchs) are abundant on the reef surfaces throughout the Keramas, and this site is no exception. Cuttlefish are commonly seen. Schools of yellowfin goatfish probe the sandy areas near the reef base. Reef fish diversity is high — the protected national park status of the Kerama Islands has allowed populations to recover from historical pressure. Octopus in the rocky sections near the pier base are regular finds.

## Dive Profile

Boat access allows divers to position precisely on the outer reef below the pier approach. Descend to the deeper slope sections (20–30 meters) early in the dive when air supply is fullest, then work upward through the mid-reef zone to the shallower reef flat. The reef flat at 5–12 meters is typically the most fish-dense zone and ideal for the final portion of the dive and safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

The convenience of Aka Island's pier infrastructure makes this one of the most logistically straightforward Kerama sites. Multiple operators offer morning and afternoon boat trips. Shore diving in the very shallows is theoretically possible but the pier area is subject to ferry and boat traffic — boat diving is standard.

## Tips

- The convenience of this site for Aka Island-based divers makes it a natural multi-visit choice during a multi-day Kerama stay
- Morning dives before the Naha day-trip boats arrive offer a more relaxed experience
- Ask local operators about any specific nudibranchs or unusual sightings they have observed recently — Kerama guides maintain active logs
- Combine with one of the more exposed Kerama cape sites on the same day for the full range of conditions
- Aka Island has good accommodation options for extended diving stays that allow multiple Kerama dives per day

## Safety

Sheltered near-pier location makes this one of the safer Kerama dive sites. Primary hazard is boat traffic in the pier approach area — maintain awareness of surface boat movements when ascending and deploy an SMB. Current is generally mild. Decompression chamber access via fast ferry to Naha.

## Photography

The excellent Kerama visibility in a convenient, regularly-dived site offers reliable subjects. Turtle encounters near the reef flat reward wide-angle portrait shots. Nudibranch diversity in the Keramas makes macro photography productive on every dive. The overall reef scenery — coral, fish, and clear blue water — is consistently beautiful in standard ambient-light wide-angle work.
