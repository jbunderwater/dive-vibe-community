## 阿嘉桟橋前 (Aka Sanbashi Mae (Front of Aka Pier))

A sheltered, very shallow site directly in front of Aka Port pier. Sandy bottom with small scattered coral heads and bommies. Commonly used for check dives, training dives, and OW certification courses due to the calm conditions and easy access from the harbour.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. A sheltered, very shallow site directly in front of Aka Port pier. Sandy bottom with small scattered coral heads and bommies. Commonly used for check dives, training dives, and OW certification courses due to the calm conditions and easy access from the harbour.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Aka Port, Aka Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Damselfish and anthias are the primary species confirmed in available sources (WTP Japan, Aka Island page). The pier pilings likely attract schooling fish, which is typical of such structures in Okinawa. Specific species beyond these cannot be confirmed without additional source verification.
