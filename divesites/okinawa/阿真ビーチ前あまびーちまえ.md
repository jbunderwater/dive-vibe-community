# 阿真ビーチ前・あまびーちまえ (Ama Beach Front)

The outer reef drop-off in front of Zamami Island's famous Ama Beach — a well-organised intermediate dive in the heart of the Kerama Islands' best diving.

## Overview

Ama Beach Front (阿真ビーチ前) describes the offshore reef in front of Ama Beach on Zamami-jima, one of the Kerama Islands' most popular diving destinations. The beach itself is one of Okinawa's most celebrated — a pristine arc of white sand with turquoise water — but the primary diving interest is in the outer reef drop-off beyond the shallow sandy zone. The reef here is a classic Indo-Pacific spur-and-groove structure transitioning to a slope that descends to 30 meters. The Kerama Islands are a national park with excellent reef conservation; the coral health at Ama Beach Front is notably good. Zamami-jima hosts multiple professional dive operators with resident guides who know every feature of this site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Zamami-jima, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (80–130 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate; generally calmer than exposed Kerama sites
- **Best Time**: Year-round; Kerama Islands are diveable all year with peak conditions October to April

## Marine Life

Zamami-jima's Ama Beach area is particularly famous for its sea turtle population. Green turtles and hawksbill turtles are seen on virtually every dive in this area, often in multiples — the turtle density in the Kerama Islands is among the highest in Japanese waters. The reef supports a full complement of subtropical Okinawan species: unicornfish, parrotfish, various wrasse species, and dense aggregations of anthias on the coral slopes. Cuttlefish are commonly encountered throughout Kerama dives. Spotted eagle rays occasionally traverse the outer slope. Reef sharks visit from time to time.

## Dive Profile

Begin from the boat mooring on the outer reef. Descend to the slope at 15–25 meters and drift or cruise along the reef contour toward the deeper sections. Ascend to the reef flat at 5–12 meters for the second half of the dive where turtle encounters are most common. The shallow sandy areas near the beach are sometimes explored as a finale with excellent natural light.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Zamami-jima's pier. Shore access is possible for the shallow sections from Ama Beach directly. Local operators based on Zamami-jima are the primary means of accessing the outer reef.

## Tips

- Early morning dives on this site frequently yield multiple turtle encounters before the day-trip boats arrive from Naha
- Sea turtles here are accustomed to divers — approach slowly and horizontally for the best, least-disturbing encounters
- The shallow sandy zone near the beach is excellent for snorkelling and a good first-dive zone for brand-new divers
- Zamami-jima offers exceptional land-based facilities and accommodation for multi-day diving trips
- Combine with Ama Ushiji (阿真牛瀬) for two contrasting experiences on the same half-day

## Safety

One of the more sheltered and accessible Kerama dive sites. Current is generally manageable. Sea turtle encounters should never involve touching, chasing, or blocking the turtles' path to the surface. Emergency recompression chamber access is via fast ferry to Naha (approximately 50–60 minutes).

## Photography

Zamami's famous turtle encounters make this one of Japan's top underwater portrait destinations. Turtles in clear "Kerama Blue" water with ambient natural light create exceptional images without complex lighting setup. Wide-angle with a dome port captures full-body turtle portraits with the reef context. The shallow sandy zone near the beach produces beautiful natural-light wide-angle images.
