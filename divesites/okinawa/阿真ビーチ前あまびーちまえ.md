## 阿真ビーチ前・あまびーちまえ (Ama Beach Mae (Front of Ama Beach))

A shallow, sheltered site directly off Ama Beach on Zamami Island -- one of the most famous turtle-watching beaches in the Kerama Islands. White sand bottom with coral patches where sea turtles come to feed during high tide. Ideal for relaxed, extended dives.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. A shallow, sheltered site directly off Ama Beach on Zamami Island -- one of the most famous turtle-watching beaches in the Kerama Islands. White sand bottom with coral patches where sea turtles come to feed during high tide. Ideal for relaxed, extended dives.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Zamami Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Green sea turtles are the primary attraction -- confirmed across multiple sources, they visit daily at high tide to feed on seagrass in the shallows immediately off Ama Beach. The adjacent shoreline snorkeling area (very shallow, 2.5m max) is primarily seagrass and sand; the deeper dive site offshore features coral patches with tomato clownfish, lagoon triggerfish, butterflyfish, and wrasse species. No site-specific deep-water marine life data was found specifically for this named dive point beyond the turtle-watching area.
