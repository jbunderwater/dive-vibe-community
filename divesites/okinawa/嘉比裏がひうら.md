## 嘉比裏・がひうら (Gahi Ura (Back of Gahi Island))

The south/back side of uninhabited Gahi Island, complementing Gahi Mae on the north side. The water channel between Zamami and Gahi islands creates good water flow, meaning this side may see slightly more current than the front. Sea fans have been reported at 15-20 metres in the Gahi channel area.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. The south/back side of uninhabited Gahi Island, complementing Gahi Mae on the north side. The water channel between Zamami and Gahi islands creates good water flow, meaning this side may see slightly more current than the front. Sea fans have been reported at 15-20 metres in the Gahi channel area.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gahi Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Sea fans (Isobana) in deeper sections, sea turtles, Yasha gobies, reef fish. The stronger water flow compared to Gahi Mae may attract more pelagic visitors.
