## 嘉比裏・がひうら (Gahi Ura (Back of Gahi Island))

The south/back side of uninhabited Gahi Island, complementing Gahi Mae on the north side. "Ura" (裏) means the reverse or sheltered side. The site is part of the Kerama inner island dive circuit.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park. Located on the south side of Gahi Island in the inner Kerama waters. No site-specific sources were found describing Gahi Ura as a named dive point. The channel between Gahi and Zamami islands may produce current relative to the more sheltered north side. Typical Kerama reef conditions apply but no factual claims specific to this site can be verified from available sources.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gahi Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November
