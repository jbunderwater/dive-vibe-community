# 嘉比裏・がひうら (Gahi Ura / Gahi Backside)

The sheltered "back side" reef of Gahi Island in the Kerama group — a protected, calmer alternative to exposed Kerama sites with excellent coral condition and consistent turtle sightings.

## Overview

Gahi Ura (嘉比裏, "Gahi behind/inside") refers to the sheltered, lee-side reef of Gahi-jima (Gahi Island), one of the uninhabited islands in the Kerama group southwest of Zamami-jima. The sheltered position protects this site from the dominant current and swell patterns that characterise the exposed Kerama dive sites, making Gahi Ura more accessible in a wider range of weather conditions. The reef here is typical healthy Kerama reef in the national park environment: diverse hard coral coverage, good sponge populations, and the excellent "Kerama Blue" visibility that characterises the entire island group. The calmer conditions at Gahi Ura make it a good choice for divers wanting to maximise observation time without current management demands.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Gahi-jima, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Sheltered fringing reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters (100 feet)
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (80–130 feet)
- **Current**: Light; sheltered lee-side position
- **Best Time**: Year-round; one of the more weather-resilient Kerama sites due to sheltered position

## Marine Life

The sheltered lee side of Gahi Island creates a calmer environment that suits certain species particularly well. Sea turtles rest in the quiet sections of the reef, and Gahi Ura is one of the Kerama Islands' more reliable turtle sites. Cuttlefish occupy the reef regularly — the calmer water makes their colour-change displays easier to observe and photograph without current disruption. Nudibranchs are consistently found on the reef surfaces throughout the Keramas. Octopus and moray eels inhabit the rocky sections of the deeper reef. Reef fish diversity is high throughout the standard depth range.

## Dive Profile

A relaxed dive along the sheltered reef slope from 5 to 30 meters. The calmer conditions allow divers to explore at their own pace without current management demands. Spend time at each depth zone investigating the reef character. The shallow section at 5–12 meters is particularly recommended for turtle encounters and fish density. Comfortable 50–65 minute bottom times at these depths.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Zamami-jima or other Kerama bases. Gahi-jima is uninhabited — all diving access is by boat from the inhabited Kerama islands. The boat ride from Zamami is approximately 15–20 minutes.

## Tips

- The sheltered position makes this site valuable as a backup when exposed Kerama sites have too much current or swell
- Calmer water improves macro photography conditions significantly — use this site for cuttlefish and nudibranch work
- The lee-side position means the morning sun angles may differ from east-facing sites — check light direction before selecting lens and strobe setup
- Turtle encounters here are typically more relaxed than at busier sites due to lighter boat traffic on the Gahi side
- This is an excellent multi-dive site — conditions remain good throughout the day

## Safety

Among the more benign conditions available in the Kerama Islands. The sheltered position reduces current risk. Standard marine park precautions apply: no contact with marine life or reef structures, no collecting. Deploy SMB before ascending as standard. Decompression facilities in Naha.

## Photography

Calm water conditions make Gahi Ura particularly productive for macro and behaviour photography. Cuttlefish colour-change sequences require stillness and patience — the calm water helps both photographer and subject remain stable. Turtle resting portraits in ambient light are achievable here. Wide-angle reef shots in exceptional Kerama visibility produce particularly clean, vivid results without current-induced camera movement.
