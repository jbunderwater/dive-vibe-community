## 西浜・にしはま (Nishi Hama)

One of Aka Island's most popular dive sites, located off the famous Nishibama Beach. White sandy bottom with scattered coral bommies hosting dense schools of cardinalfish and blue-green chromis. Sea turtles are frequently encountered resting on the coral heads.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. One of Aka Island's most popular dive sites, located off the famous Nishibama Beach. White sandy bottom with scattered coral bommies hosting dense schools of cardinalfish and blue-green chromis. Sea turtles are frequently encountered resting on the coral heads.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Aka Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Cardinalfish in dense schools, golden-striped sweeper schools, blue-green chromis, red-spotted grouper, clownfish in resident anemones, sea turtles (green and hawksbill) -- all confirmed by Marine Diving Web which lists this site with these specific species. Garden eels in sandy patches between coral heads.
