# 西浜・にしはま (Nishihama / West Beach)

A western reef adjacent to one of the Kerama Islands' most celebrated beaches — excellent coral in a sheltered bay with outstanding turtle access.

## Overview

Nishihama (西浜, "west beach") is one of the most beautiful beaches in Okinawa Prefecture, located on the western side of one of the Kerama Islands and regularly cited as among Japan's most spectacular white sand beaches. The dive site extends beyond the beach fringe into the reef system that develops offshore. The western exposure provides shelter from easterly swells and morning wind, creating calm conditions that make Nishihama one of the most pleasant Kerama dive sites in terms of surface comfort. The reef below the famous beach is in excellent condition, maintaining the health that characterises the entire Keramashoto National Park.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (80–130 feet)
- **Current**: Light; sheltered western position
- **Best Time**: Year-round; excellent in summer when eastern sites may have swell

## Marine Life

Nishihama is particularly famous in Okinawan diving circles for its sea turtle density — the beach's location creates a protected bay environment that turtles favour for feeding and resting. Both hawksbill and green turtles are extremely common here, and multiple simultaneous sightings are routine. The reef supports all standard Kerama species. Cuttlefish are abundant throughout the western reefs of the Keramas. Octopus occupy the rocky sections near the reef base. Coral coverage is varied and healthy — table corals, brain corals, and sea fans coexist in the protected bay conditions.

## Dive Profile

The sheltered position allows relaxed, unhurried diving. Begin at the outer reef edge at 15–25 meters and work inward toward the beach fringe in the shallows. The 5–15 meter zone near the beach is particularly productive for turtle encounters. Extended bottom times of 55–70 minutes are achievable. The sand bottom near the beach fringe is worth scanning for rays and flatfish.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Kerama Islands operators, landing at or near the beach before diving. Shore entry from the beach for the shallow section is possible in calm conditions. The beach itself is sometimes included in day-trip programs from Naha as a beach stop combined with snorkelling or a short dive.

## Tips

- Visit in the afternoon when day-trippers from the beach have departed — turtle activity continues throughout the day
- The shallow beach fringe at 3–8 meters is outstanding for snorkellers and is as productive as the deeper reef for turtle encounters
- The white sand bottom reflects light beautifully — reduce exposure compared to reef settings to avoid overexposed sandy areas
- This site is ideal for the second or third dive of a Kerama day, allowing a relaxed end to a full day of diving
- Multiple turtles simultaneously is routine — move slowly to avoid disturbing them before you've had a chance to observe

## Safety

Among the most benign Kerama dive conditions. The sheltered western bay position and moderate depth make this accessible in a wide range of conditions. Standard precautions: no touching or pursuing turtles (national park regulations and ethical responsibility), SMB deployment for open-water ascents, awareness of any boat traffic above the beach area.

## Photography

Nishihama is arguably the best turtle photography site in the Kerama Islands given the density of encounters and the calm, clear conditions. Wide-angle turtle portraits in ambient "Kerama Blue" light with the white sand bottom and blue water create exceptional images without complex lighting. Multiple turtles in a single frame are achievable here. Split-level photography from the surface showing the famous white beach above and turtles below is a memorable composition.
