## 西浜・にしはま (Nishi Hama)

One of Aka Island's most popular dive sites, located off the famous Nishibama (Nishi Hama) Beach on Aka Island's northeast coast. Note: despite the kanji 西 (west), "Nishihama" in Okinawan dialect means "north beach." White sandy bottom with scattered coral heads hosting dense schools of swallowtail cardinalfish and golden trevally, with sea turtles commonly encountered.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. The site features rocks scattered across sand at depths of 5-25 metres. Marine Diving Japan describes it as "one of the best spots in the Kerama Islands" with vibrant coral reefs. Access is regulated under Aka Island dive association rules: only 4 operator visits per month, with mandatory mooring buoys only.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Aka Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Swallowtail cardinalfish in dense schools (hundreds to thousands), scalefin anthias (Hanagoi), and damselfish clouds on the coral heads. Green sea turtles are commonly encountered. Golden trevally schools are reported on the sandy areas. Clownfish in resident anemones. Garden eels in the sandy patches between coral heads. Blue-green damselfish and butterflyfish are abundant on the reef.
