## 佐久原漁礁 (Sakubaru Gyosho (Sakubaru Fishing Reef))

A deeper site around Aka Island combining a natural pinnacle with stacked artificial fish aggregation blocks (1 m² concrete fishing pulleys). The pinnacle top at around 8 metres hosts abundant scalefin anthias and fusiliers, while the deeper sections at 30-35 metres attract pelagic visitors.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park. The site features a natural pinnacle structure augmented by artificial reef blocks, starting at 8 metres and descending to approximately 30-35 metres. The artificial blocks concentrate fish density. Aqua-Life classifies this as a more advanced site due to the depth range and open ocean setting. Visibility is variable, reported from 15-50 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Aka Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 34 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Bigeye trevally schools and goby fish are confirmed by multiple sources. Scalefin anthias (Hagoyo) swarms are a signature feature per Aqua-Life. Fusiliers (Gurukun -- Okinawa's prefectural fish) are abundant. Pelagic fish including trevally are confirmed during stronger currents. Nurse shark sightings mentioned in an earlier version of this description could not be confirmed in available sources and have been removed. Eagle rays are reported occasionally in the general Aka Island area but not confirmed at this specific site.
