## 佐久原漁礁 (Sakubaru Gyosho (Sakubaru Fishing Reef))

A dual-attraction site combining a natural submerged reef with artificial fish aggregation structures. The reef top at 10 metres hosts abundant fusiliers -- Okinawa's prefectural fish -- while the deeper sections at 34 metres attract pelagic visitors including dogtooth tuna and trevally schools. One of the deeper and more dynamic sites around Aka Island.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. A dual-attraction site combining a natural submerged reef with artificial fish aggregation structures. The reef top at 10 metres hosts abundant fusiliers -- Okinawa's prefectural fish -- while the deeper sections at 34 metres attract pelagic visitors including dogtooth tuna and trevally schools. One of the deeper and more dynamic sites around Aka Island.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Aka Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 34 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Dogtooth tuna, giant trevally, batfish, eagle rays (occasional), anthias, six-banded angelfish, fusiliers in large schools. Nurse sharks have been reported. The artificial structures concentrate fish density.
