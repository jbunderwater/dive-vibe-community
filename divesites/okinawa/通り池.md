## 通り池 (Tori-ike / Twin Ponds)

One of Miyako-jima's "Big Three" dive sites and among the most famous cave dives in Japan. Two inland limestone ponds -- 75 metres and 55 metres in diameter -- are connected to the open ocean via a massive underwater tunnel, creating one of the most otherworldly dive experiences in the Pacific.

## Overview

Divers enter from the ocean side and swim through a large arch (ceiling at roughly 20 metres, sandy bottom at 40-45 metres) that connects the sea to the inland ponds. The signature experience is passing through the chemocline and thermocline where freshwater and seawater meet, creating a shimmering, distorted visual layer that shifts colours from deep blue to emerald green. Surfacing inside the pond, divers look up at limestone cliffs and sky -- while tourists on the walking path above peer down in surprise. The site is a designated national monument and natural treasure of Shimoji Island.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Shimoji Island, Miyako-jima, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 40 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 metres
- **Current**: Occasionally strong at the ocean entrance
- **Best Time**: September to May; conditions depend on wind direction (south-facing site)

## Marine Life

Giant trevally, dogtooth tuna, and sea turtles are confirmed sightings by Aquatic Adventure and other Miyako operators around the Tori-ike entrance and tunnel. Lobster and grouper are reported on the rock walls. Inside the pond, the halocline mixing layer creates unusual conditions where marine species from both salt and fresh water environments are present, though specific species in the freshwater-influenced zone are not well documented in available sources.

## Dive Profile

Enter from the ocean at the tunnel mouth and descend along the gently sloping sandy bottom to the arch entrance at 20-25 metres. Swim through the tunnel -- the arch is wide enough for several divers abreast -- watching the colour shift from blue to green as you approach the pond. Surface inside the pond briefly to experience the enclosed limestone amphitheatre, then descend and return the same way. Total penetration distance is modest but depth is significant. BigHoliday, one of the primary Miyako operators, notes this site has the fewest completed dives of any major Miyako site due to strict safety and weather requirements -- surfacing inside the pond is not guaranteed. Most guided dives limit bottom time at depth due to the 35-40 metre range.

## Safety Considerations

This is a deep site with real narcosis risk at the 40-45 metre bottom. AOW certification is mandatory and operators enforce this strictly. Current at the ocean entrance can be strong and unpredictable -- abort the dive if conditions are too rough to enter safely. The tunnel itself has no air pockets between ocean and pond, so gas management is critical. Buoyancy control must be excellent to avoid contact with the fragile limestone formations. Do not attempt without a guide who knows the site.

## Photography

Wide-angle is essential. The classic shot is looking up through the tunnel entrance with the green pond light above, or capturing a diver silhouetted against the colour transition at the chemocline. The freshwater-saltwater mixing layer creates abstract, ethereal images. Morning light produces the best colour in the pond.
