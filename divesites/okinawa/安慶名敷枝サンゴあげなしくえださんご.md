## 安慶名敷枝サンゴ・あげなしくえださんご (Agenashiku Eda Sango (Agenashiku Branching Coral))

Named for the dense fields of branching coral (Eda Sango -- Acropora-type staghorn coral) around uninhabited Agenashiku Island, situated between Aka and Zamami. This small island is known for its pristine coral coverage. Dense staghorn and branching coral colonies with schools of chromis and damselfish weaving through the branches.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. Named for the dense fields of branching coral (Eda Sango -- Acropora-type staghorn coral) around uninhabited Agenashiku Island, situated between Aka and Zamami. This small island is known for its pristine coral coverage. Dense staghorn and branching coral colonies with schools of chromis and damselfish weaving through the branches.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agenashiku Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 18 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Branching Acropora corals (dominant feature), damselfish, chromis, anthias, butterflyfish. The branching coral structure provides habitat for juvenile fish and small invertebrates.
