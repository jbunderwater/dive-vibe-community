# 中の島チャンネル (Nakanoshima Channel)

A coral-lined channel between Miyako Island formations featuring cavern sections, dense reef walls, and excellent natural light — a productive beginner cave experience.

## Overview

Nakanoshima Channel (中の島チャンネル, literally "Middle Island Channel") is a natural passage between reef formations on the Miyako-jima underwater landscape. The channel walls are heavily coated in hard and soft corals, creating a tunnel-like environment that functions as a cavern dive in parts. Unlike fully enclosed cave systems, the channel has open sky visible for most of its length, making it genuinely accessible to beginner divers. The 22-meter maximum depth provides adequate water for a substantial dive while keeping gas management simple. The channel's shape concentrates current, making it ideal for drift-style navigation with minimal finning.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Miyako-jima, Miyako Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20–35 meters (65–115 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate through-channel drift
- **Best Time**: October to April; year-round access possible in settled conditions

## Marine Life

Channel environments concentrate marine life from multiple reef ecosystems — species from both walls meet in the central water column. Schooling fish are particularly impressive here; yellowfin goatfish, various snapper species, and fusiliers congregate in the channel. Hawksbill turtles are regular visitors, often observed in the channel walls grazing on sponges. The cavern sections shelter soldierfish and squirrelfish in their characteristic low-light clusters. Reef sharks occasionally traverse the channel. The wall surfaces host extensive communities of cup corals, tunicates, and crinoids.

## Dive Profile

Enter the channel at one end and drift through on the current, or explore both walls methodically from a central mooring position. The cavern sections are short and well-lit — explore them comfortably at 15–22 meters before ascending to explore the shallower wall tops at 5–10 meters. The open sky above most of the channel means orientation is always possible. Extended bottom times of 45–65 minutes are typical at these depths.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Miyako-jima operators. Entry and exit can be at either end of the channel or from a mooring in the centre. Operators coordinate the drift direction based on the day's current.

## Tips

- Allocate time equally between the deep channel floor and the shallower wall tops — both zones are rewarding
- The cavern sections are short; pause in each to allow eyes to adjust to the lower light before exploring
- Strong torch makes the cup corals and crinoids on the channel walls significantly more colourful
- Watch for hawksbill turtles grazing in the crevices along the channel walls
- The channel serves as an excellent orientation dive for divers new to Miyako before attempting more complex sites like Tori-ike

## Safety

Beginner-rated due to the open sky visible above most of the channel. The true cavern sections are brief and well-lit. Standard cave/cavern awareness: maintain orientation to exits, bring a torch, and do not disturb the floor silt. Current can concentrate in the narrower sections of the channel — if it becomes too strong, signal to your buddy and ascend rather than fighting it.

## Photography

The channel walls viewed from the centre create natural corridor compositions with excellent depth perspective. Crinoids on the walls are vivid subjects with artificial light. Wide-angle captures the scale of the wall-to-wall distance, especially in the narrower sections. Natural light from the open sky provides a blue-sky ceiling element that enhances overhead shots looking upward from depth.
