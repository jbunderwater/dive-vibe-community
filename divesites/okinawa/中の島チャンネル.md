## 中の島チャンネル (Nakanoshima Channel)

A sheltered channel site in Nakanoshima Bay on Shimoji Island, featuring a network of crevasses, small arches, and a channel running through the reef. Calm conditions and rich macro life make this an ideal beginner and photography site.

## Overview

Unlike Miyako's dramatic deep cave sites, Nakanoshima Channel offers relaxed, shallow diving through a series of crevasses and small swim-throughs at 5-22 metres. The protected bay location means calm conditions even when other sites are blown out. The reef structure creates a maze-like network of channels that harbour an exceptional density of small marine life. This is Miyako's best macro site and a favourite for underwater photography courses.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Shimoji Island, Miyako-jima, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 metres
- **Current**: Variable
- **Best Time**: Year-round; sheltered bay means this site is diveable in most conditions

## Marine Life

Angelfish, surgeonfish, anemonefish, and butterflyfish are abundant in the channels. Nudibranchs and sea slugs populate every surface. Pygmy seahorses have been found in the sea fans. The sandy areas between reef sections host garden eels and goby-shrimp pairs.

## Dive Profile

Enter over the reef top and descend into the channel system at 5-10 metres. Weave through the crevasses and small arches, exploring the walls for macro life. The channel runs to a maximum of 22 metres at its deepest point. The sheltered conditions allow extended 60+ minute dives.

## Safety Considerations

Very accessible site suitable for Open Water divers. Minimal current. No overhead environment despite the channel and crevasse topography -- all exits are visible.

## Photography

Macro is king here. Nudibranchs, pygmy seahorses, and crustaceans in every crevice. The channel walls provide clean backgrounds. Wide-angle works for the channel topography with schooling fish.
