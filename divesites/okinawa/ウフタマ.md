## ウフタマ (Ufutama)

White sandy bottom with coral formations and the locally famous 'Masuda Rock' (named after its discoverer) at 8 metres. Features extensive soft coral gardens offshore with some specimens as tall as a diver. Garden eels populate the sandy areas between coral heads.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. White sandy bottom with coral formations and the locally famous 'Masuda Rock' (named after its discoverer) at 8 metres. Features extensive soft coral gardens offshore with some specimens as tall as a diver. Garden eels populate the sandy areas between coral heads.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Zamami Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Garden eels, scorpionfish, clownfish, pufferfish, damselfish, groupers. The tall soft coral colonies are a distinctive feature. Masuda Rock is a focal point for schooling fish.
