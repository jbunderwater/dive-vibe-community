# アダン前 (Adan Mae / Screw Pine Front)

A Kerama Islands reef site named for the pandanus (adan) trees on the nearby shoreline — a diverse coral slope with characteristic subtropical Okinawan marine life.

## Overview

Adan Mae (アダン前) is named for the pandanus trees (アダン, adan in Okinawan dialect, *Pandanus odoratissimus*) that line the island shore visible from the dive site. The site itself is a coral reef slope in the Kerama Islands extending from the shallow fringing reef to 30 meters, offering a gradient of habitats from high-light shallow coral gardens to deeper rock and coral formations. The Kerama Islands context provides the extraordinary water clarity — "Kerama Blue" — and national park protection that has allowed the reef here to maintain healthy coral coverage. The pandanus-lined shore creates an unusually attractive approach to the dive, framing the clear blue water between tropical vegetation.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 30 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 25–40 meters (80–130 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: October to April for peak visibility; year-round diving possible

## Marine Life

The coral slope supports typical healthy Okinawan reef assemblages. Hawksbill turtles are abundant throughout the Kerama Islands and are reliable sightings here. Moorish idols and butterflyfish species patrol the coral tops. Parrotfish create a constant background sound of coral grazing. Anthias hover above the deeper coral formations. Cuttlefish — Kerama's quintessential macro subject — occupy territory throughout the reef. Octopus are found in rocky sections. Longnose hawkfish perch on sea fan tips throughout the site.

## Dive Profile

A straightforward slope dive from shallow to moderate depth. Descend to 25–30 meters along the deeper slope section early in the dive, then work upward through the intermediate zone (15–25 meters) to the shallowest coral gardens (5–15 meters). The extended shallow section allows extended bottom time and is typically the most species-diverse portion of the dive. Safety stop in clear, shallow water with excellent natural light.

## Entry and Exit

Boat access from Kerama Islands operators. Short boat ride from the nearest island base in this cluster.

## Tips

- The site is excellent for Kerama first-timers — the slope profile is easy to navigate and the wildlife is representative of the best the islands offer
- Look up from the deeper sections to see the coral slope ascending toward the bright surface — a beautiful perspective
- Cuttlefish encounters in the Keramas reward a slow, non-threatening approach — crouch down and make yourself small
- The pandanus-tree shoreline visible from the surface is a memorable element of the Kerama experience — a brief surface interval to appreciate the environment is worthwhile
- This site works well as part of a multi-tank Kerama day combining slope and cape environments

## Safety

Friendly conditions for intermediate divers. Maintain awareness of current direction and deploy SMB for the ascent. Sea urchins on the rocky sections require care in low-visibility entry/exit zones. Standard Kerama emergency procedures apply: fast ferry to Naha for decompression treatment if required.

## Photography

The slope profile allows varied photography from macro (cuttlefish, nudibranchs on the deeper sections) to wide-angle (turtle portraits, reef panoramas in exceptional Kerama visibility). The shallow section in morning light provides particularly vivid ambient-light images. The pandanus trees on the shoreline can be included in split-level shots during surface intervals for a sense of the island's tropical character.
