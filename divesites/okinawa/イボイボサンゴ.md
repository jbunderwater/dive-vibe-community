## イボイボサンゴ (Iboibo Sango (Bumpy Coral))

Named after its distinctive bumpy/nodular coral formations (also known as Butsubutsu Sango / ブツブツサンゴ). Located in the waters around Gahi Island between Aka and Zamami. The dominant corals are mound-forming Porites and brain coral species with a textured, warty surface. A sheltered site with minimal current.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility. The site is centred on large mound coral formations at 5-20 metres that shelter an abundance of fish life. The protective designation limits operator access, helping preserve the coral condition. Aqua-Life dive shop lists this as one of the standout reef character sites in the Kerama inner islands, particularly photogenic when sea fans take on a snow-covered appearance at optimal tidal conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Aka Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Scalefin anthias (Hanagoi) in schools, damselfish, butterflyfish, soft coral (Isobana sea fans), cuttlefish (winter months), clownfish, filefish, spotted sweetlips, and surgeonfish are all confirmed by Aqua-Life and Redfin dive shops. Sea turtles visit the coral heads. The mound coral surfaces harbour small crustaceans and invertebrates.
