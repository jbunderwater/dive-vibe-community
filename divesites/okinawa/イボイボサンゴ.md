## イボイボサンゴ (Iboibo Sango (Bumpy Coral))

Named after its distinctive bumpy coral formations -- likely Porites or Montipora species with a textured, warty surface. A sheltered site near Aka Island with minimal current, typical of the inner Kerama reef sites. The unusual coral textures give this site its character.

## Overview

Part of Kerama Shoto National Park, known for 'Kerama Blue' water with 20-50 metre visibility and abundant sea turtle encounters. Named after its distinctive bumpy coral formations -- likely Porites or Montipora species with a textured, warty surface. A sheltered site near Aka Island with minimal current, typical of the inner Kerama reef sites. The unusual coral textures give this site its character.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Aka Island, Kerama Islands, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-50 metres ('Kerama Blue')
- **Water Temperature**: 22-29C
- **Best Time**: Year-round; calmest seas March to November

## Marine Life

Reef fish including damselfish, anthias, and chromis. Sea turtles visit the coral heads. The textured coral surfaces harbour small crustaceans and invertebrates.
