## アントニオガウデイ (Antonio Gaudi)

One of Miyako-jima's "Big Three" dive sites, named for its resemblance to Antoni Gaudi's organic architectural style. An intricate network of interconnected arches, holes, and cave formations creates a cathedral-like underwater landscape that is unlike anything else in Japanese waters.

## Overview

The site sits offshore from a shallow reef, dropping to a complex of arches and tunnels between 20 and 37 metres. The defining experience is looking up from the seabed through multiple irregular openings as sunlight filters down through layers of rock, creating patterns described as resembling jack-o-lanterns or stained glass windows. The rock formations are organic and asymmetric -- holes within holes, arches crossing at odd angles -- evoking Gaudi's Sagrada Familia. Bottom time at full depth is limited to roughly 3 minutes before decompression obligation, so the dive is a brief but intense visual experience.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Shimoji Island, Miyako-jima, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 37 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 metres
- **Current**: Occasionally strong
- **Best Time**: September to May; needs calm seas and good sun angle for the light effects

## Marine Life

Red-toothed triggerfish (confirmed by multiple operators as the characteristic species at this specific site) populate the arches. Giant trevally circle the deeper sections. Violet dottyback, barred grouper, yellowfin grouper, red-spined lionfish, and longnose hawkfish inhabit the rock walls. Smithi's Anthias have been observed on the outer drop-off. Scorpionfish occupy cavern crevices. Marine life is secondary to the topography -- divers come for the rock architecture.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the reef top, then follow the slope down to the arch complex at 25-37 metres. Navigate through the interconnected openings, pausing to look up through the lattice of rock and light. The deepest section at 37 metres allows only a few minutes before mandatory ascent. Work upward through shallower arches and exit onto the reef for a gradual ascent and safety stop. Nitrox extends bottom time significantly.

## Safety Considerations

Deep site with very limited bottom time on air. Some operators require Advanced OW certification and 50+ logged dives. Excellent buoyancy control is essential -- the complex rock formations are close on all sides and contact damages both diver and site. Current can be strong and conditions change quickly. Best November to April when conditions are most consistent. Nitrox significantly extends bottom time at this depth.

## Photography

Wide-angle with natural light is the approach here. The signature shot is looking up through the complex rock openings with sunbeams streaming down. Best on sunny days with high sun angle (late morning to early afternoon). Strobes are useful for filling the foreground but the real drama is the natural light geometry.
