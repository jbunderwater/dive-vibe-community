## アントニオガウデイ (Antonio Gaudi)

One of Miyako-jima's "Big Three" dive sites, named for its resemblance to Antoni Gaudi's organic architectural style. An intricate network of interconnected arches, holes, and cave formations creates a cathedral-like underwater landscape. Sources report maximum depths of 33-35 metres at this site.

## Overview

The site sits offshore from a shallow reef, dropping to a complex of arches and tunnels between 20 and 35 metres. The defining experience is looking up from the seabed through multiple irregular openings as sunlight filters down through layers of rock, creating patterns described -- by Aquatic Adventure Miyako -- as resembling "Jack O'Lanterns." The rock formations are organic and asymmetric -- holes within holes, arches crossing at odd angles. BigHoliday ranks Antonio Gaudi as its most-dived site (66 dives in 2025), though conditions require advance planning and a 2-3 day window. The intricate topography can only be fully appreciated across multiple dives.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Shimoji Island, Miyako-jima, Okinawa Prefecture, Japan
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 20-40 metres
- **Current**: Occasionally strong
- **Best Time**: September to May; needs calm seas and good sun angle for the light effects

## Marine Life

Giant trevally patrol the arches. Noble file clams (Flame file shells), leaf scorpionfish, and various anthias are confirmed by Marine Diving Web for this site. The deeper sandy areas may host garden eels. Marine life is secondary to the topography at this site -- divers come for the rock architecture. The reference in older descriptions to "yellowfin grouper" could not be verified in available sources and has been removed.

## Dive Profile

Descend from the boat to the reef top, then follow the slope down to the arch complex at 20-35 metres. Navigate through the interconnected openings, pausing to look up through the lattice of rock and light. Work upward through shallower arches and exit onto the reef for a gradual ascent and safety stop. Nitrox extends bottom time at depth significantly.

## Safety Considerations

Deep site with limited bottom time on air. Excellent buoyancy control is essential -- the complex rock formations are close on all sides and contact damages both diver and site. BigHoliday emphasises stable neutral buoyancy, confident ear equalization, and air consumption awareness as prerequisites. Current can be strong and conditions change quickly. Only dive with operators who know the site well; the guide determines final site selection based on daily conditions.

## Photography

Wide-angle with natural light is the approach here. The signature shot is looking up through the complex rock openings with sunbeams streaming down. Best on sunny days with high sun angle (late morning to early afternoon). Strobes are useful for filling the foreground but the real drama is the natural light geometry.
