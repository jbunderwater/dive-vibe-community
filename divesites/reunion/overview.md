## Réunion

Volcanic Indian Ocean island with sheltered west-coast reefs, lagoon snorkeling, and seasonal humpback whale encounters.

## Description

La Réunion is a French overseas department in the western Indian Ocean, approximately 700 km east of Madagascar and 175 km southwest of Mauritius. The island is dramatically volcanic — its active Piton de la Fournaise is one of the most frequently erupting volcanoes in the world — and its diving is concentrated on the sheltered west coast around Saint-Gilles-les-Bains, Saint-Leu, and L'Étang-Salé, where fringing reefs and lagoons offer protected conditions. The east coast is exposed to constant trade-wind swell and is generally not divable.

Diving here is best known for the cluster of historic wrecks off Saint-Gilles (Hai Siang, Navarra, Antonio Lorenzo, Sea Venture, La Barge de Saint-Paul), the dramatic Tombant de la Pointe au Sel wall, accessible coral reefs from beginner to intermediate level, and the seasonal presence of migrating humpback whales between June and October.

### Diving Opportunities

- **Reef Diving**: Fringing coral reefs from shore-accessible lagoons (Passe de l'Ermitage) to outer reef edges
- **Wreck Diving**: Cluster of ship and barge wrecks off Saint-Gilles port, mostly at advanced depths (40-54 m)
- **Wall Diving**: Tombant de la Pointe au Sel — drop-off from 27 m past 80 m
- **Whale Watching / Snorkel** (Jun–Oct): Humpback whale season; in-water encounters regulated by the marine reserve

### Accessibility

- **Getting There**: Roland Garros Airport (RUN) at Saint-Denis. Direct flights from Paris (Air France, French Bee, Corsair, Air Austral), Mauritius, Madagascar, Bangkok, and a few other hubs.
- **Dive Operators**: Multiple operators based in Saint-Gilles harbour and Saint-Leu, including Bleu Marine Réunion, Abyss Plongée, Corail Plongée, Bourbon Diving, and others.
- **Accommodation**: Range of hotels in Saint-Gilles, Boucan Canot, Saint-Leu; dedicated dive lodging is limited.
- **Transportation**: Rental car recommended for accessing the west coast dive ports.

### Conservation

The **Réserve Naturelle Marine de La Réunion** (created 2007) protects approximately 35 km of the west coast in three zones — integral protection, enhanced protection, and general protection — with corresponding regulations on diving, anchoring, fishing, and species collection. Mooring buoys are widely used; do not anchor on coral.

## Additional Information

- **Currency**: Euro (EUR)
- **Language**: French (official); Réunion Creole widely spoken
- **Best Season**: Year-round diving possible. Austral summer (Oct–Mar) is the warmer, calmer season; austral winter (Jun–Oct) overlaps with humpback whale migration but brings cooler water and stronger trade winds.
- **Safety**:
  - **Shark crisis (2011–present)**: Increased bull and tiger shark activity led to multiple fatal incidents on surfers and bathers. Most attacks are along the west coast. The risk is primarily for surface activities (surfing, bodyboarding) — scuba diving has not seen attacks at the same rate, but operators monitor conditions actively and may close sites. Always dive with a local operator and follow their guidance.
  - Verify the location of the nearest hyperbaric chamber before diving (chamber operated at the CHU at Saint-Pierre).
  - The east coast is generally unsafe for water entry due to strong swell.
