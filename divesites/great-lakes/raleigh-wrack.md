## Raleigh (Wrack)

The Raleigh is a 19th-century Lake Erie wreck lying in approximately 20 metres of water off the Ontario north shore between Port Colborne and Port Dalhousie. This wooden schooner is part of the wreck-rich heritage trail that lines the northern Lake Erie lakebed.

## Overview

The eastern end of Lake Erie's north shore between Port Colborne and the Welland Canal area has been one of the most important shipping corridors in Great Lakes history. The canal entrance and the turbulent conditions at the eastern end of the lake combined to produce significant losses, and the Raleigh is one of many vessels that now rest preserved in Lake Erie's cold freshwater. At 20 metres, the site is at an ideal recreational diving depth, and the flat sandy bottom of the eastern Lake Erie basin makes navigation and buoyancy management straightforward. Visibility in the eastern Lake Erie basin is typically better than the western basin — often reaching 10–15 metres in good conditions.

## Marine Life

Eastern Lake Erie wreck sites share the productive fish community of the lake as a whole. Yellow perch are abundant. Walleye are regular visitors, particularly in the lower light conditions of dawn, dusk, or overcast days. Smallmouth bass are resident in the hull. Lake trout are occasionally seen in the eastern basin's colder, deeper water. Round gobies populate the bottom sediment. Zebra mussels coat all hard surfaces.

## Dive Profile

The Raleigh lies on a flat sandy bottom. Standard bow-to-stern exploration works well. The eastern Lake Erie basin's better clarity compared to the western basin makes this a rewarding dive in good conditions. Ascend on the anchor line. Safety stop at 5 metres for 3 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Charter dive boats from Port Colborne or the Welland Canal area provide access. Entry by giant stride. Deploy SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Welland Canal area has interesting diving history in its own right — the canal connects Lake Erie to Lake Ontario
- Drysuit or thick wetsuit recommended
- Torch for hull inspection
- Combine with other nearby Lake Erie wrecks on the same charter

## Safety Considerations

Lake Erie weather is the primary safety consideration — watch forecasts carefully. Cold bottom water requires thermal protection. Dive with a buddy and carry an SMB.

## Photography

Eastern Lake Erie wreck photography typically rewards better visibility than the western basin. Yellow perch schools and the preserved wooden hull provide engaging subjects. A torch significantly improves hull detail photography.
