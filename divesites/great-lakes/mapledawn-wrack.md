## Mapledawn (Wrack)

The Mapledawn is an extremely shallow Georgian Bay wreck lying in just 8 metres of water near Collingwood, Ontario. This 20th-century vessel provides an exceptionally accessible wreck dive — sunlit, colourful, and inviting even for snorkellers and new divers building confidence in an authentic wreck environment.

## Overview

The Mapledawn grounded on a Georgian Bay shoal and was lost in relatively shallow water, where she has rested for decades in the cold, clear freshwater. At a maximum depth of 8 metres, the wreck sits in the sunlit zone — natural light penetrates fully to the bottom, revealing the hull, deck fixtures, and structure in vivid colour without the need for dive lights. The site is one of the most beginner-friendly wreck dives in the Great Lakes system, combining easy depth with a genuine historic vessel. Collingwood, Ontario is a popular weekend destination for Ontario divers, and the Mapledawn is regularly listed as a must-do for divers of all experience levels visiting the area. Visibility ranges from 5–12 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Georgian Bay, Lake Huron, Ontario, Canada
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 8 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–12 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 8–20°C at surface in summer; cooler at depth
- **Current**: Minimal in protected Collingwood area waters
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

The sunlit shallow wreck is rich with freshwater life. Smallmouth bass are conspicuous and bold, approaching divers at close range. Yellow perch school around the hull and through the deck openings. Sunfish (pumpkinseed) hover near the shallowest hull sections. Crayfish populate every crevice. In summer, the hull's shallowest sections are covered in aquatic plants that provide habitat for juvenile fish and insects. Northern pike occasionally cruise the area.

## Dive Profile

This site is ideal for beginner wreck dives. At 8 metres maximum depth, there are no significant NDL concerns, and the full wreck can be explored in a single, extended dive. Swim along the hull from end to end, noting deck features, hull fittings, and any visible interior spaces through hatches. The site also works well as a snorkelling destination from a boat. No safety stop required at this depth, but a brief stop at 5 metres before surfacing is still good practice.

## Entry and Exit

Charter dive boats from Collingwood provide access. The site can also be reached with a small boat and is well-marked. Entry by ladder or roll from the dive boat. Exit by ladder.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Outstanding first wreck dive or refresher dive — manageable depth with an authentic historic vessel
- Snorkelling is a viable option for non-divers wanting to experience the wreck
- A wetsuit of at least 3mm is recommended — even in summer, Georgian Bay water is refreshingly cool
- Natural light is excellent here — an underwater camera without additional lighting still produces good images
- Combine with a deeper Georgian Bay wreck dive for a varied day

## Safety Considerations

This is one of the safest wreck dive sites in the Great Lakes system. The shallow depth, minimal current, and protected location minimize most diving hazards. Cold water (relative to tropical diving) still requires appropriate thermal protection. Always dive with a buddy and maintain boat awareness on ascent.

## Photography

The Mapledawn is a photographer's delight — sunlit wreck photography at 8 metres produces images with excellent natural colour and contrast. The hull framed against the lighter water above, with smallmouth bass in the foreground, is a classic composition. Close-up images of hull hardware and marine growth are equally rewarding without supplemental lighting.
