## Olive Branch (Wrack)

The Olive Branch is a 19th-century wooden schooner wreck in Lake Ontario near Kingston, Ontario. One of many well-preserved historic vessels in the Kingston area's extraordinary underwater heritage collection, the Olive Branch lies in approximately 20 metres of cold Lake Ontario water.

## Overview

Kingston, Ontario is often called the freshwater wreck diving capital of North America, and the Olive Branch is one of the vessels that earns that title. This 19th-century wooden schooner sank in Lake Ontario near Kingston and now rests in approximately 20 metres of cold, oxygen-depleted freshwater that has preserved the hull, rigging, and cargo hold in remarkable detail. The Kingston area's exceptional density of historic wrecks means the Olive Branch is typically dived as one of two or three sites on a single charter day. Visibility ranges from 5–10 metres, with cleaner conditions typically in the morning.

## Marine Life

The Kingston-area wreck community is consistent across the dozen-plus wrecks in the area. Smallmouth bass are dominant — bold, curious fish that approach divers closely and add life to any wreck exploration. Yellow perch school around the masts and rigging. Round gobies carpet the silty bottom. Occasional walleye sightings add excitement. Heavy zebra mussel colonization below 8 metres armours the hull surfaces while providing invertebrate habitat.

## Dive Profile

The Olive Branch lies upright (or close to it) on the lakebed. Standard bow-to-stern exploration. The hull features — windlass, capstan, hatches, and tiller or wheel — are the primary points of interest. Ascend on the mooring line. Safety stop at 5 metres for 3 minutes.

## Entry and Exit

Kingston dive charter operators run regular trips to the Olive Branch as part of multi-wreck day tours. Multiple operators work out of Kingston Harbour. Entry by giant stride. Deploy SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Pair the Olive Branch with the Annie Falconer for a focused Kingston south-shore wreck day
- The Kingston dive community hosts events and dive days — connecting with local divers adds insight
- Cold water gear essential — drysuit preferred
- Torch recommended for hull shadow areas
- Do not disturb or remove artifacts

## Safety Considerations

Standard Lake Ontario/Kingston area safety precautions. Cold water, limited visibility, and boat traffic. Always dive with a buddy and deploy an SMB before ascending.

## Photography

The Olive Branch offers typical Kingston wreck photography: wooden hull detail, hardware, and resident smallmouth bass in the characteristic blue-green of Lake Ontario. A torch or video light significantly improves image quality.
