## Michigan (Wrack)

The Michigan is an extremely shallow wreck lying in just 4 metres of water near Collingwood, Ontario in Georgian Bay. This site is effectively a snorkelling destination as much as a scuba dive, with the wreck visible from the surface in clear conditions and accessible to the most novice divers.

## Overview

The Michigan (not to be confused with the lake of the same name) lies in 4 metres of water in the protected Collingwood area of Georgian Bay — one of several shallow wrecks that make this region a popular destination for beginner divers and snorkellers. At 4 metres, the wreck is fully sunlit, the hull structure is clearly visible from the surface, and divers can spend as long as desired exploring without decompression concerns. The combination of very shallow depth, calm protected waters, and genuine historical wreck character makes this an outstanding introduction to wreck diving. Water temperatures in this sheltered, shallow bay reach 18–20°C at the surface in summer, making it one of the more comfortable freshwater dive experiences in the Great Lakes.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Georgian Bay, Lake Huron, Ontario, Canada (Collingwood area)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 4 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 3–8 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 10–20°C in summer at this shallow, sheltered location
- **Current**: Minimal in protected Collingwood waters
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

The very shallow, sunlit Michigan hosts a warm-water freshwater community in summer. Sunfish (pumpkinseed and bluegill) are abundant and approachable. Smallmouth bass patrol the hull's shadowed areas. Crayfish are everywhere on the hull and bottom. Juvenile perch move through in large groups. Aquatic vegetation on the hull and surrounding bottom provides habitat for insects, small fish, and invertebrates. This is an excellent site for observing the interactions of a warm, shallow freshwater ecosystem.

## Dive Profile

At 4 metres maximum depth, this is as simple as scuba diving gets. Descend from the boat and explore the hull at leisure — no NDL concerns, no decompression requirements, and easy self-rescue if needed. The site is ideal for students on discovery or checkout dives. A brief stop at 3 metres before surfacing is good practice.

## Entry and Exit

Charter dive boats from Collingwood provide access. The site may also be accessible by private boat. Entry by back roll or stride. Exit by ladder. Snorkelling access may also be available.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Outstanding site for children and first-time divers
- Snorkelling is viable and rewarding — the wreck is visible from the surface
- Summer surface temperatures are comfortable even for a thin wetsuit
- Can be combined in a day with deeper Collingwood-area wrecks like the Marquette and Mapledawn
- Great for underwater photography — no expensive lighting needed at 4 metres in summer

## Safety Considerations

This is one of the safest dive sites in the Great Lakes. The shallow depth, protected location, and warm summer temperatures minimize most diving hazards. Standard buddy system and boat awareness still apply. A wetsuit is recommended for comfort even in summer.

## Photography

4-metre depth in summer sunlight produces exceptional natural-light photography. Sunfish and juvenile perch are highly photogenic subjects. The hull structure, silhouetted against the bright surface above, creates evocative images with a standard underwater camera and no additional lighting.
