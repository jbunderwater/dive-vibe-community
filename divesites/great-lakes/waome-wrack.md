## Waome (Wrack)

The Waome is a 20th-century Georgian Bay passenger vessel wreck lying in approximately 20 metres off the Parry Sound area of Georgian Bay, Ontario. This steel-hulled vessel sank in the 1930s or 1940s, adding a 20th-century chapter to Georgian Bay's long maritime history.

## Overview

Georgian Bay's eastern shore around Parry Sound has been an important navigation corridor since the late 19th century, serving summer cottagers, fishing communities, and commercial interests on the bay's famous 30,000 Islands. The Waome was a passenger vessel of the type that served Georgian Bay communities before road access made water transport less necessary. She now lies in approximately 20 metres of cold Georgian Bay freshwater near Parry Sound, where the visibility (8–12 metres, somewhat less than the Tobermory area) and cold bottom temperatures have preserved the hull in good condition. The Parry Sound area is one of the less-visited diving regions in Georgian Bay, rewarding divers with less crowded sites and a beautiful natural setting.

## Marine Life

Eastern Georgian Bay near Parry Sound supports a diverse freshwater community. Smallmouth bass are abundant around the hull. Northern pike cruise the area. Lake trout are occasionally seen. Yellow perch school around the superstructure. The scenic 30,000 Islands setting means diving here is accompanied by sightings of loons, great blue herons, and other Georgian Bay wildlife above the waterline.

## Dive Profile

The Waome lies at 20 metres on the Georgian Bay bottom. Explore the steel hull, superstructure, and any accessible interior sections. The 20th-century construction means identifiable machinery, portholes, and structural features that connect to living memory of Great Lakes passenger travel. Standard ascent with 3-minute safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive charters from Parry Sound or small marina operators in the area provide access. The Parry Sound area has less dive infrastructure than Tobermory but local knowledge makes access feasible. Entry by giant stride. Deploy SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Parry Sound's 30,000 Islands setting is spectacular — combine diving with exploring this scenic area
- Less crowded than Tobermory or Kingston — rewards independent-minded divers
- Drysuit recommended for cold bottom temperatures
- Local knowledge of the site is important — connect with Parry Sound area dive operators
- The Georgian Bay Islands National Park area provides outstanding scenery in conjunction with diving

## Safety Considerations

The Parry Sound area's rocky Georgian Bay seabed requires navigation awareness. Cold water demands thermal protection. The relative remoteness compared to larger dive destinations means conservative dive planning is important. Always dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The Parry Sound area's beauty extends underwater. The 20th-century steel hull of the Waome, surrounded by Georgian Bay's cold, clear water and flanked by rocky outcrops and aquatic vegetation, creates photography subjects distinctive from the mussel-encrusted lake wrecks further south.
