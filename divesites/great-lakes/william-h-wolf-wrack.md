## William H. Wolf (Wrack)

The William H. Wolf is a 19th or early 20th-century vessel wreck lying in Lake Huron near Sarnia, Ontario, in the southern reaches of the lake close to the St. Clair River. This wreck is part of the maritime heritage of one of North America's most historically significant shipping corridors.

## Overview

The southern end of Lake Huron near Sarnia, Ontario and Port Huron, Michigan is one of the world's busiest inland waterways — all Great Lakes shipping must pass through the St. Clair River connecting Lake Huron to Lake Erie. The William H. Wolf lies in approximately 20 metres of southern Lake Huron water near the Ontario shore, in an area that saw intensive commercial shipping throughout the 19th and 20th centuries. Cold, oxygen-depleted freshwater at depth has preserved the hull in typical Great Lakes fashion. Visibility in southern Lake Huron is typically 5–12 metres, improving in calm summer conditions.

## Marine Life

Southern Lake Huron supports a typical Great Lakes freshwater community. Smallmouth bass are the most visible wreck residents. Yellow perch school around the hull structure. Walleye patrol the wreck edges. Zebra mussels cover all hard surfaces below the thermocline. Round gobies are established in this part of Lake Huron.

## Dive Profile

The wreck lies at 20 metres on a relatively flat bottom. Explore from bow to stern. Note hull construction and preserved fittings. Current awareness is important given the proximity to the St. Clair River system. Standard ascent with safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Charter dive boats from Sarnia or Port Huron area provide access. Local operators know the current patterns and best diving conditions for southern Lake Huron wrecks. Entry by giant stride. Deploy SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Southern Lake Huron's maritime history is fascinating — research the St. Clair River corridor context
- Drysuit recommended for cold bottom temperatures
- Current assessment is more important here than at sites further from the St. Clair River
- Heritage protection applies in both Canadian and US waters

## Safety Considerations

Current conditions near the St. Clair River end of Lake Huron require assessment before each dive. Weather on Lake Huron can change rapidly. Cold water demands thermal protection. Always dive with a buddy and carry an SMB.

## Photography

Standard Great Lakes wreck photography approach — supplemental lighting improves hull detail. Smallmouth bass and perch schools are engaging subjects around the hull.
