## Munson (Wrack)

The Munson is a 19th-century wooden schooner wreck in Lake Ontario near Kingston, Ontario. Part of the exceptional Kingston-area wreck collection — one of the most concentrated dive destinations in North America — the Munson lies in cold, clear Lake Ontario water at a comfortable 20-metre depth.

## Overview

The Kingston area's remarkable concentration of historic shipwrecks results from two centuries of intensive commercial traffic through the northeast corner of Lake Ontario and into the St. Lawrence River. The Munson was a wooden cargo schooner typical of the 19th-century Great Lakes trading fleet. She now rests at approximately 20 metres in Lake Ontario near Kingston, where cold, dark, low-oxygen freshwater has preserved her wooden hull with remarkable fidelity. The hull planking, iron hardware, rigging attachments, and structural members are all identifiable, offering a vivid picture of 19th-century Great Lakes shipbuilding. Visibility typically ranges from 5–10 metres.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Lake Ontario, Great Lakes, Canada
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 5–10 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 4–18°C (seasonal); bottom 4–6°C
- **Current**: Mild offshore
- **Best Time**: June to October

## Marine Life

Kingston-area wrecks are consistently productive dive sites for freshwater fish observation. Smallmouth bass are the signature species, often encountered nose-to-lens at close range. Yellow perch school around the masts and rigging. Round gobies populate the bottom. Occasional walleye sightings add interest. The hull below 8–10 metres carries heavy zebra mussel colonization.

## Dive Profile

The Munson lies on a silty bottom at 20 metres. Explore from bow to stern, noting preserved hull features. Careful buoyancy control protects both the fragile hull and the visibility. Ascend on the mooring line for a standard 3-minute safety stop.

## Entry and Exit

Multiple Kingston area dive charter operators service this wreck as part of multi-wreck day trips. Kingston has excellent dive tourism infrastructure. Entry by giant stride. Deploy SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Kingston area's wreck density makes a 2–3 day visit worthwhile
- Combine with the George A. Marsh, Comet, and Frontenac for a comprehensive Kingston wreck tour
- Drysuit required for cold bottom temperatures
- Silty bottom requires practiced buoyancy control
- Ontario heritage legislation protects all artifacts

## Safety Considerations

Cold water, limited visibility, and boat traffic are the standard Kingston-area safety considerations. Always deploy an SMB before ascending. Dive with a buddy.

## Photography

Toronto-region divers regularly visit Kingston specifically for the wreck photography opportunities. The Munson's 19th-century wooden construction provides compelling historical documentary subjects. Smallmouth bass are engaging portrait subjects in the characteristic blue-green Lake Ontario ambient light.
