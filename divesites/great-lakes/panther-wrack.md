## Panther (Wrack)

The Panther is a historic wreck in the Straits of Mackinac or northern Lake Huron area near Sault Ste. Marie, Ontario/Michigan. This vessel rests in the cold waters connecting Lake Huron and Lake Superior — a region steeped in Great Lakes maritime history and home to outstanding, less-crowded wreck diving.

## Overview

The northern approaches to the Straits of Mackinac and the waters around the St. Marys River connecting Lakes Huron and Superior were some of the busiest — and most dangerous — passages for Great Lakes shipping. The Panther rests in approximately 20 metres of cold, exceptionally clear northern Lake Huron or St. Marys River water near the Sault Ste. Marie area. This part of the Great Lakes is far less visited for dive tourism than the Tobermory or Kingston areas, but rewards visitors with excellent visibility, cold clear water, and a sense of isolation that the more popular sites lack. Visibility can reach 15+ metres in calm conditions.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northern Lake Huron / Sault Ste. Marie area, Ontario, Canada
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 20 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 8–15 metres
- **Water Temperature**: 4–14°C (seasonal); bottom consistently cold
- **Current**: Varies; can be influenced by Great Lakes water level differentials
- **Best Time**: June to September

## Marine Life

Northern Lake Huron near the Sault supports healthy populations of lake trout, walleye, and northern pike — classic Great Lakes cold-water species. Smallmouth bass are present around the wreck hull. The relative absence of heavy zebra mussel colonization (compared to the lower Great Lakes) means the hull retains a more natural appearance. The clean, cold water supports good freshwater sponge populations and diverse invertebrates on the hull surfaces.

## Dive Profile

The Panther lies at an accessible 20-metre depth. Explore the hull from bow to stern in the clear northern Lake Huron water. The excellent visibility compared to southern Great Lakes sites makes this a particularly enjoyable dive. Standard ascent with safety stop at 5 metres.

## Entry and Exit

Dive charters from Sault Ste. Marie area (Ontario or Michigan sides) provide access. Infrastructure is less developed than at Tobermory or Kingston — plan accordingly. Entry by giant stride. Deploy SMB on ascent.

## Tips and Recommendations

- The Sault Ste. Marie area rewards adventurous divers willing to explore off the beaten dive track
- Drysuit essential — northern Great Lakes water is cold year-round
- The excellent visibility here compared to southern Great Lakes sites is a noticeable advantage
- Combine with the nearby Cedarville for a multi-site wreck diving trip
- Lake trout sightings are more common here than at southern Great Lakes sites

## Safety Considerations

Remote location means longer emergency response times. Conservative dive planning and excellent equipment preparation are important. Current near the St. Marys River is possible. Cold water demands proper thermal protection. Always dive with a buddy.

## Photography

The combination of excellent visibility and cold, clear northern water creates outstanding photography conditions. Lake trout encounters at this type of site are photographic highlights. The hull's natural appearance — less mussel colonized than southern Great Lakes wrecks — photographs differently and beautifully.
