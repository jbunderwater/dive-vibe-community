## Mahi Wreck

The Mahi is the most celebrated wreck dive on Oahu's West Shore—a former U.S. Navy minesweeper resting upright off Waianae, renowned for its resident squadrons of white-spotted eagle rays.

## Overview

Located approximately one mile off the Waianae coast (about 45 minutes from Waikiki), the Mahi sits upright in 90 feet of water with the main deck at approximately 65 feet. The wreck is heavily encrusted with coral and provides a thriving artificial reef habitat. Two-thirds of the main deck has collapsed over the decades, and the stern section has broken away; interior penetration is not recommended due to structural instability. The site is best reached by charter boat from the Waianae area.

## Historical Background

The vessel was originally commissioned as USS Scrimmage (AM-297), an Admirable-class minesweeper built by Winslow Marine Railway and Shipbuilding Company in Seattle. Specifications: 184 feet 6 inches (56.24 m) long, 33 ft beam, 650 tons displacement. She was laid down on 22 February 1943, launched on 16 May 1943, and commissioned on 4 April 1944. Scrimmage served in the Pacific during WWII, earning six battle stars for combat action, then was decommissioned on 22 June 1946.

After military service the vessel entered merchant life as the British-registered MV Giant II (cable ship), then was acquired by the Dillingham Corporation in 1968 and leased to the University of Hawaii as the research vessel MS Mahi. In 1982, the vessel was sold to Dacor Scuba Diving and intentionally sunk as an artificial reef approximately one mile off Waianae. Note: some dive operator websites cite the sinking year as 1986—Wikipedia and the USS Scrimmage article give 1982 as the year it was sold for scuttling.

Note on length: Wikipedia documents 184 ft 6 in for USS Scrimmage. Several dive operator sites report lengths ranging from 175 to 190 feet—these discrepancies are unresolved in available sources.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Off Waianae, West Shore of Oahu
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Main Deck Depth**: ~20 meters (65 feet)
- **Maximum Depth**: 27 meters
- **Penetration**: Not recommended—structural collapse in most areas

## Marine Life

The Mahi is famous for its white-spotted eagle rays, which are frequently observed swimming alongside and above the wreck in groups of up to 20 at once. Blue-line snappers school through the corridors. The coral-encrusted structure hosts schools of reef fish, octopus, moray eels, and occasional large barracuda. Eagle rays are the site's signature encounter.
