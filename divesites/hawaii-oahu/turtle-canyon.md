## Turtle Canyon

Turtle Canyon is the most popular reef dive off Waikiki—a shallow offshore finger reef where Hawaiian green sea turtles and hawksbill turtles congregate at natural cleaning stations, providing some of the most reliable turtle encounters in Hawaii.

## Overview

Located 1.5–2.5 miles offshore from Waikiki on Oahu's south shore and accessible via a 10–20 minute boat ride from Kewalo Basin, Turtle Canyon is a system of volcanic lava finger reefs with ridges, canyons, coral heads, and sandy patches. Depths range from approximately 20–45 feet (6–14 m), making the site comfortably within beginner range. Visibility can reach 100 feet on good days. The site is diveable year-round.

## Site Information

- **Location**: South shore of Oahu, offshore from Waikiki
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Depth Range**: 6–14 meters (20–45 feet)
- **Water Temperature**: 24–27°C (75–80°F) year-round

## Marine Life

Hawaiian green sea turtles and hawksbill turtles are the primary draw, gathering at cleaning stations where gold ring surgeonfish and small wrasses remove algae and parasites from their shells. The reef also hosts butterflyfish, flame angelfish, moray eels, octopi, and abundant reef fish. Large standalone coral heads with Christmas tree worms are a notable feature. Whitetip reef sharks are occasionally sighted.
