## Three Tables

Three Tables is a shallow North Shore dive and snorkel site named for the three (or four, depending on tide) flat sections of reef visible above the surface at low tide, located just south of Sharks Cove within the Pupukea Beach Park Marine Life Conservation District.

## Overview

Situated along Kamehameha Highway within Pupukea Beach Park, Three Tables offers calm-water diving when North Shore conditions allow—typically May through September. The site features a lava shelf with three distinctive flat rock formations extending offshore. Shallow depths near the tables average about 15 feet; the best diving and most marine life are found at 30–45 feet farther out. Ledges, arches, lava tubes, and small caves are scattered throughout the site in deeper water.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Pupukea Beach Park, North Shore of Oahu (south of Sharks Cove)
- **Entry Type**: Shore entry
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Depth Range**: 5–14 meters (15–45 feet)
- **Season**: Summer only (May–September); dangerous in winter swells

## Marine Life

Convict tang, Moorish idol, trumpetfish, yellow tang, surgeonfish, pencil urchins, small moray eels, and octopus in crevices are commonly reported at this site. Hawaiian green sea turtles occasionally visit to graze on algae. No lifeguards on duty—divers should check conditions before entering.
