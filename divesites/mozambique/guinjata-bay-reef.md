## Guinjata Bay Reef

Guinjata Bay Reef is an inshore reef approximately 1.5 km offshore in the Inhambane area, one of 14 local reefs operated by Guinjata Dive Centre, offering beginner-friendly conditions with a maximum depth of around 21 metres.

## Overview

Guinjata Bay sits south of Inhambane and north of Tofo, in an area of Mozambique's "Manta Coast" known for whale shark and manta ray encounters. The bay is home to Guinjata Dive Centre, which operates 24 dive sites across two categories: 14 local inshore reefs (700–3,000 metres from shore, depths 10–30 m) and 10 deeper offshore reefs (including Manta Canyon, Manta Pinnacles, and other advanced sites at 30–70 m). "Guinjata Bay Reef" represents the inshore reef system.

The inshore reefs at Guinjata Bay are characterised by moderate depths (12–22 metres typical), diverse invertebrate life, and anemone formations. Specific inshore sites listed by Guinjata Dive Centre include Turtle Creek (green turtle encounters, 20–28 m), Batfish (anemone-filled pinnacles, 12–18 m), Eel Alley (multiple eel species, 20–28 m), Devil's Peak (swim-through formations, 18–23 m), and Levi's Ledge (beginner-friendly, 10–14 m, with humpback whales June–October). The "Guinjata Bay Reef" entry likely corresponds to these inshore sites.

The deeper Manta Reef sites (Manta Canyon at 35 m and Manta Pinnacles at 30 m) are separate advanced sites within the Guinjata Bay dive portfolio and not the same as this inshore reef. Whale sharks are more commonly encountered at the offshore sites (November–March peak). Manta rays are documented at the canyon and pinnacle sites specifically.

The previous description's claim that Guinjata Bay Reef itself offers "access to the celebrated Manta Reef" is misleading — these are separate dives, not the same site.
