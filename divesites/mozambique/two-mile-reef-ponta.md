## Overview

The confirmed "Two Mile Reef" is a coral barrier reef stretching roughly 4.5 km parallel to the coast between Bazaruto and Benguerra Islands in the Bazaruto Archipelago, approximately 16 nautical miles from Vilanculos. It is a flat-topped barrier reef with depths of 6 to 22 metres (14 m average). The reef's seaward side offers diverse sections with individual names including The Arches, Shark Point, Cathedral, The Gap, and The Boulders. It accommodates all diver experience levels and is regarded as one of the best-documented reefs in the archipelago.

Confirmed marine life at the Bazaruto Two Mile Reef includes reef sharks (blacktip), manta rays, potato bass, kingfish, barracuda, moray eels, hawksbill and green turtles, and dense schools of reef fish including coachman, fusilier, snapper, and surgeonfish. Whale sharks visit seasonally. Dugongs are present in the broader Bazaruto area but are rarely seen underwater.

**What this entry is NOT:** There is no evidence of a site called "Two Mile Reef" at Ponta do Ouro. The previous description attributed ragged-tooth sharks to this site — ragged-tooth (sand tiger) sharks are primarily associated with South African dive sites rather than Mozambique's Ponta do Ouro reefs, where bull sharks, hammerheads, and tiger sharks are more typical. This claim has been removed as unverified.

This entry requires resolution: either the coordinates should be corrected to the Bazaruto location, or the name should be changed to reflect what actually exists at Ponta do Ouro at these coordinates.
