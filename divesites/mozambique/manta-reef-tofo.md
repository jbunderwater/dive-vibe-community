## Manta Reef Tofo

Manta Reef is Tofo's most celebrated dive site, a submerged pinnacle system featuring cleaning stations where oceanic manta rays gather reliably, making it one of the highest-probability manta encounters on the African coast.

## Overview

Manta Reef sits approximately 6 km from Tofo's dive centre, reached in around 10 minutes by boat. The site comprises 15-metre-high pinnacles with caves carved into the reef base and a plateau area at around 19–26 metres where three large cleaning stations operate. Cleaner wrasse maintain these stations, and oceanic manta rays arrive regularly to be serviced — up to six mantas have been reported circling at a single time, with wingspans reaching up to 6 metres. Tofo Scuba reports an approximately 80% success rate for manta sightings on any given dive.

Whale sharks are documented in the Tofo area generally, and are sometimes encountered in the water column near the reef, particularly during the November–March season when plankton concentrations peak — though these are opportunistic encounters rather than reliably predictable at this specific site. Loggerhead turtles, moray eels, ribbon eels, and reef fish including barracuda, batfish, and snappers are resident on the reef structure. Devil rays are occasionally seen during the ascent.

The dive is classified Advanced by local operators due to the depth and the possibility of current requiring drift techniques and negative entries.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Tofo, Inhambane Province, Mozambique
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 26 meters
