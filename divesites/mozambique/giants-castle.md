## Giants Castle

Giants Castle is a drift wall dive 6 km from Tofo, where a crenellated reef ridge drops from 26 to 35 metres and runs more than 350 metres north to south — best experienced as a natural drift along the castle-like ramparts.

## Overview

The wall at Giants Castle starts at around 26 metres and drops a further 7 metres to the seabed at 35 metres, with the eastern side of the reef presenting a series of ledges, overhangs, and crevices. The site extends more than 350 metres north to south and complements the prevailing north-to-south current, making it an ideal drift dive when conditions allow. Operators at Tofo recommend negative entries and proficiency with drift procedures; bottom time on air runs roughly 21 minutes at these depths.

Confirmed marine life at Giants Castle includes large potato bass and stingrays, multiple ray species (eagle rays, devil rays, blotched fantail rays, Kuhl's stingrays), schooling bannerfish, great barracuda, kingfish, and moray eels. Manta rays have been documented at cleaning stations along the wall. Bowmouth guitar sharks have also been recorded here. Sailfish and blue marlin are occasional open-water visitors.

The existing description attributing Zambezi sharks and hammerheads to this site specifically is not confirmed in operator records for Giants Castle — those species are more reliably associated with the Pinnacles sites at Ponta do Ouro to the south. Divers should expect large reef fish and ray encounters; shark sightings are possible but not a primary feature of this site.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Tofo, Inhambane Province, Mozambique
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive (drift)
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 metres
