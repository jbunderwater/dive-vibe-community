## North Point

North Point is an advanced site offering everything from reef exploration to pelagic encounters, with deeper waters, exciting topography, and potential shark sightings.

## Overview

North Point is a dive site for adventurers, featuring a pinnacle with deeper sections (12-35m) that attract pelagic activity and potential shark encounters. The site offers exciting topography and the possibility of encountering larger marine species. This advanced dive site offers 10-40 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-30°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: North tip of Malapascua Island, Cebu
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Minimum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-40 meters (30-130 feet)
- **Current**: Strong, variable
- **Best Time**: October to April

## Site Characteristics

North Point features a pinnacle structure with deeper dive profiles than most Malapascua sites. The topography offers varied exploration opportunities from reef areas to deeper blue water zones that attract pelagic species including reef sharks.

## Dive Profile

Begin your descent toward the pinnacle structure, working down to 12-35m depending on your comfort level and certification. The pinnacle creates a dramatic profile in the water column. Blue water beyond the pinnacle attracts pelagic species. Allow 40-50 minutes underwater with appropriate depth management and safety stops.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Marine Life

Reef fish, pelagic species (trevally, barracuda), sharks (reef sharks and potentially larger species), rays, and various reef-dwelling creatures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Advanced site for experienced divers seeking pelagic encounters
- Bring a wide-angle camera for blue water and pelagic photography
- Check sea conditions—strong currents are common
- Excellent for practiced divers comfortable with depth management
- Maintain excellent buoyancy and depth awareness

## Safety Considerations

This advanced site has strong currents and significant depth (12-35m). Divers must maintain excellent buoyancy and depth awareness. Stay within Advanced Open Water limits. Be aware of sharks (generally not aggressive) and sea conditions. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB). Be prepared to abort if conditions deteriorate.

## Photography

Excellent for wide-angle pelagic photography of sharks, rays, and schooling fish. The blue water provides dramatic backgrounds. Pinnacle topography creates interesting compositional elements.
