## Lapus-Lapus 2

Lapus-Lapus 2 lies right next to the original Lapus-Lapus pinnacle, featuring a sloping reef that transitions into a small wall at greater depth.

## Overview

Lapus-Lapus 2 is a nearby variant of the original Lapus-Lapus site, starting with a sloping reef at approximately 12 meters that transitions into a small wall. The bottom drops to around 20 meters with further slope descending to maximum depth of 30 meters. This site offers slightly deeper diving and more challenging topography than its neighbor. This site offers 10-40 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-30°C.

- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate

## Site Characteristics

Lapus-Lapus 2 features a sloping reef that transitions abruptly into a small wall section. The topography change provides interesting diving with varied depth options. The site is less crowded than the original Lapus-Lapus site.

## Dive Profile

Begin on the sloping reef at 12 meters and follow it down toward the small wall section. The wall transition provides a distinct topographical feature. The bottom slopes to 20-30 meters. Most interesting diving occurs in the 12-25m range. Allow 40-50 minutes to explore both the slope and wall sections.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Marine Life

Reef fish, nudibranchs, seahorses, crustaceans, and wall-dwelling species at depth. The topography transition attracts different species at different depth zones.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Good for intermediate divers practicing depth management
- Offers more topography interest than original Lapus-Lapus
- Check sea conditions before diving
- Bring camera for both reef and wall photography
- Less crowded alternative to the original Lapus-Lapus site

## Safety Considerations

Mild to moderate currents are possible. Divers should maintain awareness of the wall transition and depth changes. Be aware of jellyfish and sea urchins. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

Good opportunities for reef and wall photography. The topography transition creates interesting compositional elements. Wall sections provide depth and dramatic perspectives.
