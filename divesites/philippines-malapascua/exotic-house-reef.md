## Exotic House Reef

Exotic Island Dive Resort's protected house reef offers shallow, sheltered diving perfect for training and familiarization with local marine life.

## Overview

Exotic House Reef (also known as Exotic Island Dive Resort's protected reef) is a beginner-friendly house reef located right at the resort. The site starts at shallow depth and slopes gently to approximately 13 meters. Abundant marine life and healthy coral make this an excellent site for training dives and skill practice. The sheltered location and shallow profile make this one of Malapascua's most accessible dive sites. The site offers 10-40 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-30°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Exotic Island Dive Resort area, Malapascua, Cebu
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Minimum Depth**: 5 meters
- **Maximum Depth**: 13 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-40 meters (30-130 feet)
- **Current**: Minimal, sheltered
- **Best Time**: Year-round (protected location)

## Site Characteristics

Exotic House Reef features coral patches and sea grass, creating diverse micro-habitats. The shallow water and minimal currents provide an ideal environment for training and skill development. Plentiful reef fish are accustomed to diver presence.

## Dive Profile

Begin your descent on the shallow reef and explore at leisure through the 5-13 meter water column. The site is small and easily covered in a single dive. Allow 40-60 minutes for relaxed exploration and training. The entire dive can be conducted in water shallow enough for safety and comfort, making it ideal for confidence-building.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Marine Life

Abundant reef fish, coral patches, sea grass meadows. Healthy populations of small reef fish and macro life due to the protected status.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Perfect for training dives and skill practice
- Excellent for beginners building confidence
- Bring a camera for reef fish photography
- Year-round diving available
- Great for night dives to observe nocturnal behavior

## Safety Considerations

This beginner-friendly, protected site has minimal current and shallow depths. Dive within your certification limits. Be aware of jellyfish and sea urchins. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

Good for reef and fish photography. Shallow depth provides excellent natural light for photography. Fish behavior is easily observable and photographable.
