## Dakit-Dakit

Dakit-Dakit is an exciting boulder pinnacle dive site near Malapascua, featuring colorful soft coral formations and abundant macro life.

## Overview

Dakit-Dakit translates to "near, near" in the local language and sits as a dramatic collection of massive boulders in shallow water. This pinnacle dive offers strong currents that attract large schools of fish and pelagic activity, making it suitable for divers who enjoy dynamic underwater topography. Located in the Asia region, this site offers 10-40 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-30°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Philippines Malapascua, Cebu
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Minimum Depth**: 3 meters
- **Maximum Depth**: 12 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-40 meters (30-130 feet)
- **Current**: Strong currents common
- **Best Time**: October to April (dry season)

## Site Characteristics

Dakit-Dakit features an exciting collection of large boulders rising from the sandy bottom, creating stunning underwater formations. The site gets its nickname from the short distance to Malapascua island. Soft coral adorns the boulders, along with nudibranchs, banded pipefish, seahorses, and cuttlefish. The strong currents make this an excellent spot for encountering large schools of batfish, trevally, mackerel, and other pelagic species.

## Dive Profile

Begin your descent on the current, which can be strong even in shallow water. Navigate through the boulder formations between 3-12 meters, maintaining good buoyancy to avoid disturbing the coral. The most abundant macro life is found on the boulder surfaces. Plan for a 40-45 minute dive, allowing adequate air for safety stops if needed.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from Malapascua operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll, depending on current and sea conditions. Descent on the current is recommended. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures—expect a pickup from the current drift.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Dakit-Dakit can have strong currents, so it's best suited for divers comfortable with drift diving
- Bring a macro lens for underwater photography—excellent opportunities for nudibranch and seahorse photography
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving, especially currents
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the coral-covered boulders
- Carry a surface marker buoy (SMB) and be prepared for current changes

## Marine Life

Soft coral, nudibranchs, banded pipefish, seahorses, cuttlefish, scorpionfish, lion fish, and zebra crabs are regularly sighted. Large schools of batfish, trevally, mackerel, and squid also frequent this site.

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of strong currents—this site is best dived by experienced open water divers. The shallow depth and strong currents require excellent buoyancy control. Jellyfish, sea urchins, and lionfish inhabit the area. Dive within your certification limits. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle photography of boulder formations and macro photography of nudibranchs, seahorses, and crustaceans. Natural light penetration is excellent in the shallow water.
