## Evo House Reef

Evolution Resort's protected house reef offers shallow, sheltered diving with abundant fish and creative artificial structures ideal for beginners.

## Overview

The Evolution House Reef (Evo Reef) is Evolution Diving Resort's protected dive site, perfect for training and skill-building. The reef starts at just 5 meters and gently slopes to 13 meters. Scattered coral patches and sea grass create diverse habitats. The resort has placed several creative artificial structures including old coral-encrusted jeepneys, large concrete blocks, and wire structures to attract and protect marine life. This fish-rich site is perfect for beginners. Visibility averages 10-40 meters with water temperatures at 26-30°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Philippines Malapascua (Evolution Resort area), Cebu
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Coral reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Minimum Depth**: 5 meters
- **Maximum Depth**: 13 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-40 meters (30-130 feet)
- **Current**: Minimal, very sheltered
- **Best Time**: Year-round (protected location)

## Site Characteristics

Evolution House Reef features scattered coral patches and sea grass meadows populated with abundant reef fish. The resort's artificial structures (jeepneys, concrete blocks, swim-throughs) add biodiversity and create excellent habitats. The protected status means minimal collection and healthy fish populations.

## Dive Profile

Begin at the top of the reef (5m) and explore the coral and artificial structures at leisure, working toward the deeper edge at 13 meters. The entire dive is conducted in shallow water with minimal current, making it ideal for training new skills. Allow 45-60 minutes for a relaxed exploration dive. Most sites are found in the 8-13m zone.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Marine Life

Fish-rich reef with abundant reef fish species. Scattered coral supports nudibranchs, nudis, seahorses, and crustaceans. Sea grass beds attract gobies and pipefish.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Perfect for training new divers and practicing skills
- Bring a camera for fish and reef photography
- Sheltered conditions make this ideal during rougher weather
- Year-round diving available
- Great site for night dives to explore nocturnal species

## Safety Considerations

This beginner-friendly, protected site has minimal current and shallow depth. Dive within your certification limits. Be aware of jellyfish and sea urchins. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

Excellent for reef and fish photography. The artificial structures provide interesting compositional elements. Fish are accustomed to divers, making them easier to photograph.
