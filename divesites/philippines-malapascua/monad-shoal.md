## Monad Shoal

Monad Shoal is an iconic deep pinnacle dive site famous for shark encounters, located off the northeast coast of Malapascua. Advanced divers come here seeking encounters with tiger sharks and other pelagic species.

## Overview

Monad Shoal is an underwater island that rises from a dramatic 200m drop-off into the abyss. The shoal features a flat sandy bottom at approximately 10 meters that descends steeply into deep water. This was historically the world's most reliable destination for thresher shark sightings, though tiger sharks have since become more common. The site remains premier for advanced pelagic and shark diving. Visibility averages 10-40 meters with water temperatures at 26-30°C.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Northeast of Malapascua Island, Cebu
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Pinnacle/seamount
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Minimum Depth**: 5 meters
- **Maximum Depth**: 50 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-40 meters (30-130 feet)
- **Current**: Strong and variable
- **Best Time**: October to April (early morning dives)

## Site Characteristics

Monad Shoal is a flat-topped pinnacle with a sandy plateau at 10m that drops sharply. Thresher sharks were reliably sighted here at 25-35m depths until approximately 2022, when they relocated to nearby Kimud Shoal. Tiger sharks now frequent Monad Shoal regularly. The site also attracts large rays, other pelagic sharks, trevally, and various reef fish. The steep drop-off into blue water creates excellent conditions for pelagic encounters.

## Dive Profile

Advanced divers conducting thresher shark hunts typically arrive well before dawn for 5:00am descents. The dive follows the pinnacle down from the shallow sandy area to 25-35m where sharks are encountered. Most dives are conducted as early-morning expeditions, with ascents by mid-morning. Allow 40-50 minutes underwater with decompression/safety stops. The strong currents require excellent buoyancy control and awareness.

## Certification Requirements

All Malapascuan dive shops require minimum Advanced Open Water (PADI Advanced to 30m / 100ft) or equivalent certification. Open Water certified divers must be accompanied by an instructor and may require a checkout dive to demonstrate buoyancy and self-control.

## Entry and Exit

Access is exclusively by dive boat, departing Malapascua in the early morning. Entry is via giant stride or back roll. Descend on the current line, following the pinnacle structure. Maintain awareness of depth, bottom time, and air supply due to the deep profile. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during ascent. Exit by boat pickup from the surface.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book with experienced operators (Evolution Diving, Malapascua's Thresher Shark Divers) who know current shark behavior
- Early morning departures (before dawn) provide best chances for shark encounters
- Bring a wide-angle camera for pelagic photography
- Check sea conditions—strong currents are common
- Maintain excellent buoyancy to avoid disturbing marine life
- Consider the reduced thresher shark presence when planning dives
- Some operators now combine Monad Shoal with nearby Kimud Shoal for better shark sightings

## Marine Life

Historically: Thresher sharks. Current residents: Tiger sharks, large rays (eagle rays, manta rays), other shark species, trevally, mackerel, barracuda, reef fish.

## Safety Considerations

This is an advanced dive site with significant depth, strong currents, and pelagic predators. Divers must:
- Stay within Advanced Open Water certification limits
- Maintain excellent buoyancy control in strong currents
- Monitor air supply closely due to depth
- Stay alert for aggressive shark behavior (rare but possible)
- Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB)
- Be prepared to abort if conditions deteriorate
- Understand that shark sightings are not guaranteed

## Recent Changes (2022-Present)

Thresher sharks have largely relocated to Kimud Shoal, where sighting rates exceed 90%. Tiger sharks have become the primary shark species at Monad Shoal, with lower encounter rates. Many dive operators now offer combined Monad/Kimud Shoal dive trips for optimal shark encounters.

## Photography

Excellent opportunities for wide-angle shark and pelagic photography. The blue water background provides dramatic contrast. Early morning light conditions are optimal for silhouettes and wide-angle shots.
