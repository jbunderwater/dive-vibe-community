## Lighthouse

Lighthouse is a coral-covered wall site famous for night dives to observe mandarin fish, featuring depth transitions and abundant wall life.

## Overview

Lighthouse features a wall covered with sea grass and corals that descends to 25 meters, followed by a plateau at 30 meters and another deeper wall section. The site is particularly famous for night dives to observe mandarin fish conducting their courtship displays. The wall structure and shallow plateau create diverse diving opportunities. This site offers 10-40 meters of visibility with water temperatures averaging 26-30°C.

## Site Characteristics

Lighthouse features a wall with coral and sea grass covering, creating a picturesque setting. The wall descends to 25m, followed by a plateau at 30m and deeper wall sections. The site is named for the lighthouse visible on the nearby shore.

## Dive Profile

Begin at the wall top (5-10m) and work your depth and buoyancy down along the wall toward 25-30m. The plateau provides a relatively stable depth level for exploring. Most day dives occur in the 5-25m range. Night dives target the shallow plateau (10-15m) for mandarin fish observations, typically conducted at dusk during their courtship displays.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Marine Life

Soft corals, sea fans, nudibranchs, seahorses, crustaceans on the wall. Mandarin fish visible during night dives at dusk on the shallow plateau. Reef fish and wall-dwelling species.

## Safety Considerations

Moderate currents are possible. Divers should maintain awareness of depth management along walls. Be aware of jellyfish and sea urchins. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB). Night dives require proper lighting and training.

## Photography

Day dives offer wide-angle wall photography opportunities. Night dives provide excellent macro photography of mandarin fish, nudibranchs, and nocturnal reef creatures. The wall creates interesting depth and compositional layers.
