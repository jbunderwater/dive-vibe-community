## Φαρι

Φαρι is a reef dive site in Greece, Europe.

## Overview

Φάρι (lighthouse) is a shore-entry reef dive site in the Kavala region of northern Greece on the North Aegean coast, where a rocky reef descends to 15 metres alongside a vertical wall offering diverse habitat for Mediterranean species including wrasse, parrotfish, sponges, small grouper, and clams in the sun-catching sections. The Kavala region's clean North Aegean waters are diveable from local centres that explore over 40 dive points around the nearby islands, and this shore site provides direct access to the reef without the need for boat transport. Northern Greece's Aegean diving has a distinctly different character from the southern islands, with cooler water temperatures and the possibility of Lessepsian migrants arriving through the broader Aegean system.

## Marine Life

Divers at this site can expect to encounter groupers, moray eels, octopus, barracuda, sea bream, amberjack, nudibranchs, seahorses. Additional species commonly sighted include posidonia seagrass, red coral, sea fans, sponges.
