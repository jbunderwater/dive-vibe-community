## Φαρι

Φαρι (Fari, meaning "Lighthouse") is a shallow shore-entry dive/snorkel site on Thassos island in the North Aegean, notable for historical pottery fragments on the seabed.

## Overview

**Location note:** The OSM coordinates (40.635°N, 24.524°E) place this site on the coast of Thassos island in the Northeast Aegean Islands, associated with Thassos Town (Limenas). TripAdvisor and other sources list "Fari Beach" under Thassos Town (Limenas), not Kavala on the mainland. Fari Beach is described as a pebblestone beach with turquoise water and a secluded, unorganised character.

The bay at Fari is documented as the former site of a pottery factory; bits and pieces of old pottery can still be found below the surface, making this an underwater site of minor historical interest. Dive centres operating from Thassos offer access to Fari Bay as one of their dive points, noting it is approximately 20 minutes from their bases by car.

The North Aegean waters at Thassos are clear and calm, and the shallow depth of 15 metres maximum makes this suitable for beginners and snorkellers. Typical marine life for this region includes sea bream, wrasse, sea bass, and octopus on the rocky reef sections, with posidonia seagrass in sandy transition zones.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Shore entry (pebble/stone beach)
- **Depth range**: 0–15 m
- **Bottom**: Pebbles and stones, sandy areas, rocky reef
- **Historical feature**: Pottery fragments from former factory on seabed
- **Conditions**: North Aegean; typically calm; sheltered
- **Visibility**: 10–20 m typical for this coast
- **Best season**: May–October

## Marine Life

Mediterranean species typical of the North Aegean: sea bream, wrasse, sea bass, octopus. Posidonia seagrass in adjacent sandy areas. No site-specific marine life survey has been documented for Φαρι.
