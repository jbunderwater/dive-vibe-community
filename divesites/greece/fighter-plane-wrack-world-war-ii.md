## fighter plane wrack world war II

This is a shallow WWII aircraft wreck in the Cyclades, Greece. The OSM entry uses a generic name; the coordinates (36.83°N, 25.42°E) correspond to the Irakleia (Herakleia) island area of the Small Cyclades.

## Overview

The coordinates of this OSM node (36.83°N, 25.42°E) and the recorded shallow depth (7–11 m) are consistent with the German Arado Ar196 seaplane wreck located in Alimnia Bay on Irakleia island, which lies at 9–11 m depth and is marked by a red surface buoy. The Arado Ar196 (designation D1+EH) was a two-seat German naval reconnaissance seaplane. On 17 September 1943, while escorting a convoy near Naxos, it was attacked by British aircraft, damaged, and ditched at sea. The crew (Fritz Schaar and Herbert Schneider) were rescued. The aircraft sank to 91 m, where it was caught in fishermen's nets in October 1982 and relocated to the current shallow bay on Irakleia by local fishermen. Much of the internal equipment was removed over subsequent decades; today the airframe — fuselage frame, engine, wing structure, and German insignia — remains at 9–11 m depth. An ecosystem of marine species has colonised the structure.

**Important note on identification:** The OSM entry uses a generic name ("fighter plane wrack world war II") and no aircraft type is specified. A separate RAF Bristol Beaufighter (serial JM225, 47 Squadron RAF) was shot down near Naxos on 30 October 1943 and lies at 34 m depth off Cape Kouroupas, west coast of Naxos — this is a different site at a different location and depth. The OSM coordinates and 7 m recorded depth do not match the Beaufighter location.
