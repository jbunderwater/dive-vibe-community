## fighter plane wrack world war II

A WWII aircraft wreck recorded by local survey in 2019 in the Paros/Antiparos area of the Cyclades at 7 metres depth. Aircraft type and precise identity not confirmed from independent sources.

## Overview

This site was recorded in OpenStreetMap by a local survey and GPS mark in 2019 at coordinates 36.83°N, 25.42°E, placing it in the strait between Paros and Antiparos islands in the Cyclades. The recorded depth is 7 metres.

The area around Paros and Naxos has at least one confirmed WWII aircraft wreck: a Bristol Beaufighter shot down in 1943, documented by Paros Diving Center at 35 metres depth. The relationship between the site recorded here at 7 metres and the documented Beaufighter at 35 metres is unclear — they may be different aircraft, or the depth recorded in OSM may be inaccurate. No independent dive source confirming an aircraft wreck at 7 metres depth at these specific coordinates has been located.

The Greek Ministry of Culture and Sport has authorised more than 90 WWII wrecks for recreational diving. Greece's wartime aircraft wrecks are protected archaeological heritage sites; touching or removing artefacts is prohibited.
