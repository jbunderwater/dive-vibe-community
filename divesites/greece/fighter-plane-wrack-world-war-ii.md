## fighter plane wrack world war II

A shallow WWII aircraft wreck lying at just 7 metres depth in the central Aegean, near the Cyclades island of Paros/Naxos area.

## Overview

This WWII fighter aircraft wreck rests at a recorded depth of 7 metres on the seabed in the central Aegean (coordinates: 36.83°N, 25.42°E, placing it in the sea between the Cyclades islands of Paros and Naxos). OSM data recorded in 2019 by a local survey identifies it as a submerged, non-dangerous WWII aircraft wreck.

The Cyclades area was the site of significant WWII aerial activity during the German campaigns against the Dodecanese and Crete (1941–1943). Several WWII aircraft wrecks have been documented in this region: for example, a Bristol Beaufighter (shot down in 1943 after bombing a German airfield on Paros) was discovered near Naxos at approximately 30–34 metres depth, and is accessible as an advanced dive through Naxos dive operators.

The 7-metre depth of this wreck makes it accessible for recreational divers and suitable for natural-light photography. However, no specific aircraft type, historical identity, or further documentation has been located for this particular wreck at these exact coordinates. The type and identity of the aircraft remain unconfirmed in publicly available sources.

Greece has designated numerous historical wrecks as protected cultural heritage sites; removing or touching artefacts is prohibited by Greek law.
