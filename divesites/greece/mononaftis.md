## Mononaftis

Mononaftis, meaning "lonely sailor," is a large rock pinnacle rising from the sea near Agia Pelagia, Crete, offering reef diving from 18 to 35 metres with amphora fragments, a 400-year-old anchor, and large schools of barracuda.

## Overview

Mononaftis is a huge rock that erupts from the sea approximately 50 metres from shore, close to Agia Pelagia on Crete's north coast. It is described by the local dive club as the largest of their dive sites in the area. The reef complex includes small canyons, rocky holes, and cavities beneath the rock structure.

Two depth tiers serve different certification levels: Level 1 divers explore the reef to 18 metres while Level 2 and above can reach 32–35 metres. Along the deeper reef at around 30 metres, divers encounter large schools of barracuda. At 32 metres, a 400-year-old anchor rests on the seabed — one of several older anchors in the area, evidence that this was once a natural harbour anchorage. Pieces of ancient amphoras are scattered across the reef, consistent with Minoan-era trade routes through the Aegean.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat (approximately 50 m offshore)
- **Depth range**: 18–35 m
- **Level 1**: Maximum 18 m; reef, canyons, and holes
- **Level 2+**: Full depth to 35 m; anchor at 32 m; barracuda schools at 30 m
- **Conditions**: Typically calm; good visibility

## Marine Life

Confirmed at this site by the local dive club: groupers, octopus, moray eels, schools of salemas, banded bream, saddled bream, stingrays, sea rabbits, red scorpionfish, black scorpionfish, sponges, sea anemones. Large schools of barracuda are reported along the 30-metre reef route.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agia Pelagia, Heraklion, Crete, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
