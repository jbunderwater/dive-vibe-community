## Mononaftis

Mononaftis is a reef dive site near Agia Pelagia, Crete, Greece.

## Overview

Mononaftis — Greek for "lonely sailor" — is a large rock formation rising from the sea approximately 50 metres from shore near Agia Pelagia, around 20 km west of Heraklion. The Divers Club Crete (the on-site operator) describes it as the largest dive site in the Agia Pelagia area. The site depth ranges from 18 to 35 metres: certified divers at entry level dive to 18 m, while more experienced divers reach 32 m. The site offers reef passages, small canyons, large holes, small caves, and numerous rock cavities.

Resident and regularly sighted species confirmed by the on-site operator include groupers, octopus, moray eels, schools of salemas, banded and saddled bream, stingrays, sea rabbits, red and black scorpionfish, sponges, and sea anemones. Large schools of barracuda are encountered by divers at approximately 30 m depth.

Note: A separate shore dive accessible from Mononaftis beach — the Amphora Cave (Eco Diving Center, 17 m depth) — contains amphoras attributed to the Minoan civilisation. This is a distinct dive from the Mononaftis rock site. Anchor and amphora claims attributed to the rock site in previous descriptions were removed as they could not be confirmed by the on-site operator's documentation of the rock dive itself.

- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
