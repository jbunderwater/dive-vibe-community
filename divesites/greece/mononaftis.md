## Mononaftis

Mononaftis ("lonely sailor") is considered the largest dive site at Agia Pelagia, Crete, a complex rocky formation with reef terraces, small canyons, caves, and a 400-year-old anchor at 32 metres.

## Overview

Mononaftis is a massive rock that rises approximately 50 metres from the shore near Agia Pelagia on Crete's north coast. It is the largest and most varied dive site in the area, accommodating multiple dive objectives across a depth range of 18–35 metres. Level 1 (Open Water) divers typically dive to 18 m; Level 2 and advanced divers reach 30–35 m.

The reef system includes small canyons, large holes, and cave cavities to investigate. A notable landmark is a large anchor lying at 32 metres that local dive operators date to approximately 400 years ago. A nearby cave accessible from the Mononaftis beach area — documented separately as the "Amphora Cave" by Eco Diving Center — contains what operators describe as ancient amphoras at approximately 17 metres depth. Whether these are genuinely Minoan in origin has not been independently verified by archaeological sources; dive operator descriptions attribute them to Minoan civilisation, while no peer-reviewed source was consulted during this research.

On the deeper terrace at 30 metres, large schools of barracuda frequently congregate on the approach back to the mooring. The site is described by Divers' Club Crete as offering "the greatest number and variety of fish and plants around Agia Pelagia."

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat from Agia Pelagia dive centre
- **Depth range**: 18–35 m
- **Level 1 route**: up to 18 m along the reef
- **Level 2+ route**: 30–35 m to the anchor and deeper terraces
- **Conditions**: Open north-coast Crete; mild swell possible
- **Best season**: May–October
- **Minimum certification**: Open Water Diver (shallower route); Advanced for deeper sections

## Marine Life

Documented at this site by Divers' Club Crete: groupers, octopus, moray eels, schools of salemas, banded bream, saddled bream, stingrays, red and black scorpionfish, sponges, and sea anemones. Large barracuda schools reported at depth on the return route.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agia Pelagia, Heraklion, Crete, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
