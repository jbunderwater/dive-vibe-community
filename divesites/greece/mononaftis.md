## Mononaftis

Mononaftis is a reef dive site off the north coast of Crete near Agia Pelagia, Greece.

## Overview

Mononaftis, meaning "lonely sailor" in Greek, is a large isolated rock that erupts from the sea approximately 50 metres offshore near Agia Pelagia on Crete's north coast. It is considered one of the area's most productive dive sites, with a maximum depth of 35 metres and varied topography across its reef system: small canyons, large holes, caves, and rock cavities shelter a diverse range of Mediterranean species.

The site also features an Amphora Cave at approximately 17 metres depth, accessible from the beach at Mononaftis, where amphoras described by local operators as ancient (and attributed by some to Minoan-era trade) are visible on the cave floor. The attribution to the Minoan period has not been independently verified by archaeological sources reviewed for this entry — divers should treat this claim with appropriate caution. No anchor has been confirmed at the Mononaftis rock itself; the well-documented 400-year-old anchor in the area sits at the nearby Big Anchor Reef dive site, which local operators list separately.

The site supports a wide diversity of marine life at all depth levels and is dived by local operators from Agia Pelagia year-round.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat to the rock; Amphora Cave accessible from shore
- **Depth range**: 6–35 m (18 m limit for entry-level divers; 32–35 m for advanced)
- **Bottom**: Rocky reef with canyons, caves, and cavities
- **Conditions**: Generally calm; visibility up to 30 m
- **Best season**: April–October

## Marine Life

Groupers, octopus, moray eels, schools of salemas, banded bream, saddled bream, stingrays, scorpionfish (red and black), sponges, sea anemones, and shells. Large barracuda schools reported at depths approaching 30 metres. No site-specific cetacean sightings confirmed at this location.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agia Pelagia, Heraklion, Crete, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive (Amphora Cave: shore entry)
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 35 meters
