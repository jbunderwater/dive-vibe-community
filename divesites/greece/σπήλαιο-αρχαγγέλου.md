## Archangelos Cave (Kos)

Archangelos Cave on Kos is an underwater cave with striking coral and sponge-covered walls. Located on Pserimos island between Kos and Kalymnos, this site requires cave diving training.

## Overview

Archangelos Cave is accessed from diving centers on Kos island and represents the primary technical cave diving opportunity in the immediate area. The cave entrance measures approximately 7 meters high by 5 meters wide, extending roughly 25 meters into the limestone formation. Walls are heavily colonized by orange coral and sponges.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Pserimos Island (between Kos and Kalymnos), Dodecanese
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Advanced
- **Maximum Depth**: 25 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 15-30 meters (50-100 feet)
- **Current**: Light
- **Best Time**: May to October
- **Certification Required**: Cavern or cave diving certification

## Marine Life

Cave walls host extensive coral and sponge colonies. Light-adapted fish species inhabit the deeper portions. Limited marine life due to cave environment and reduced light.

## Dive Profile

The cave entrance begins at 7 meters with widest opening at this depth. The formation narrows as you penetrate deeper, reaching tapered sections. Maximum safe penetration is approximately 25 meters. This dive requires proper cave training, guideline management, and redundant lighting. No further than 1/3 gas rule applies.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book with reputable local dive operators who know the site conditions
- Bring an underwater camera — this site offers excellent photography opportunities
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of cold water (north), currents, boat traffic in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
