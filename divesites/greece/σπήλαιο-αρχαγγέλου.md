## Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου (Archangelos Cave) is a cave dive site in Rhodes, Greece.

## Overview

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου — Greek for "Archangelos Cave" — is a sea cave dive site on the eastern coast of Rhodes, near the village of Archangelos in the Dodecanese. The cave is part of a dramatic coastal limestone formation and is accessible both above and below the waterline. Above water, the cave features a pebble beach inside its main chamber; a second, smaller cave extends further inward. The external setting is a secluded cove with crystal-clear water and striking rock formations.

Underwater, the cave extends into the rock with typical features of Greek sea cave diving: sculpted limestone walls, filtered light effects at the entrance, and the transition from open-water conditions to the cave interior. The maximum depth is 25 metres, and the Advanced difficulty rating reflects both the depth and the overhead environment requirement of cave diving. Marine life in and around the entrance zone is typical of the Greek Aegean: grouper sheltering in the deeper cave sections, moray eels and octopus in rock crevices, and Mediterranean reef fish including bream and wrasse around the entrance.

The site is primarily boat-accessed from dive operators in the Archangelos area or from Rhodes Town. The cave is also a popular snorkelling and swimming destination for surface visitors, who access the entrance chamber from the shoreline or by sea kayak. The diving section extends beyond the reach of recreational snorkellers.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat; approach to cave entrance
- **Depth range**: 5–25 m
- **Environment**: Overhead (cave); Advanced certification and cave/cavern experience required
- **Conditions**: Sheltered eastern Rhodes; visibility typically 15–20 m in Aegean
- **Best season**: May–October

## Marine Life

Grouper in deeper cave sections; moray eels and octopus in rocky crevices at the entrance zone; sea bream, wrasse, and damselfish in the open sections near the cave mouth. Mediterranean Aegean species typical of the Dodecanese coast.

## Safety

Cave diving requires specialist training beyond Open Water certification. Advanced divers without cave training should restrict exploration to the cavern zone (daylight visible from entry). Full cave diving requires appropriate certification (CMAS Cave Diver, PADI/TDI Cave Diver, or equivalent) and appropriate line and light equipment.
