## Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου is a cave dive site in Greece, Europe.

## Overview

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου (Archangelos Cave) is a coastal sea cave on Rhodes accessible by boat from the village of Archangelos, with a swimmable outer chamber and a second inner cave containing its own pebble beach, the underwater sections reaching 25 metres. The cave interior is known for dramatic light contrasts and the crystal-clear Aegean water that flows through it, supporting groupers, moray eels, sponges, sea fans, and the crustaceans and small fish typical of sheltered cave environments in the Dodecanese. The site is tagged as both a cave dive and a tourism attraction in the area, combining a natural geological wonder with the clear warm-season Aegean visibility that makes Rhodes a popular Mediterranean diving destination.

## Marine Life

Divers at this site can expect to encounter groupers, moray eels, octopus, barracuda, sea bream, amberjack, nudibranchs, seahorses. Additional species commonly sighted include posidonia seagrass, red coral, sea fans, sponges. The cave environment shelters species adapted to low-light conditions, including crustaceans and small fish that seek protection in the overhangs.

## Dive Profile
