## Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου (Archangelos Cave) is a sea cave dive site on the eastern coast of Rhodes, near the village of Archangelos in the Dodecanese.

## Overview

The cave is part of a dramatic coastal limestone formation. Above water, it features a pebble beach inside the main chamber and a second, smaller inner cave accessible from the first. The entrance has a distinctive blue-green water colour from the light filtering through. The cave is accessible from above water via stairs from the nearby Grande Blue Restaurant and is popular with swimmers and snorkellers who can enter the first chamber.

The underwater portion extends beyond the reach of recreational snorkellers. The OSM data records this as a dive site with a maximum depth of 25 metres and classifies it as an Advanced site, reflecting both the depth and the overhead environment. Site-specific scuba diving documentation from Rhodes dive operators for Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου has not been located in available sources.

The cave's above-water features — clear turquoise water, sculpted limestone, and filtered light effects at the entrance — suggest a photogenic underwater environment, but no dive-specific marine life lists, entry details, or dive conditions have been confirmed for the submerged sections from operator or diver sources.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat approach to cave entrance
- **Depth range**: Up to 25 m (per OSM)
- **Environment**: Overhead (cave); cave/cavern training required
- **Best season**: May–October

## Safety

Cave diving requires specialist training beyond Open Water certification. Advanced divers without cave training should restrict exploration to the cavern zone (natural light visible from entry point). Full cave diving requires appropriate certification (PADI/TDI Cave Diver or equivalent), plus line, primary, and backup lights.
