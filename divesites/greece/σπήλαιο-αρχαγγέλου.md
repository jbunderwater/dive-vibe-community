## Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου (Archangelos Cave) is a sea cave on the eastern coast of Rhodes, near the village of Archangelos. The OSM node marks it with scuba_diving:divespot=no, and no site-specific scuba dive documentation was found.

## Overview

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου — Greek for "Archangelos Cave" — is a coastal sea cave on the eastern side of Rhodes, accessible by sea from Stegna Beach near the village of Archangelos. Visitors access the cave by renting boats or pedal boats, or by swimming from the main bay; adventurous swimmers can pass under a natural arch to reach the entrance.

Above water, the cave features stalactites, stalagmites, and a pebble beach inside the main chamber. A second, smaller cave extends further inside. The water at the cave entrance is clear and shallow, and the site is described primarily as a swimming and snorkelling destination.

The OSM record tags this site with `scuba_diving:divespot: no`, which is consistent with the absence of any scuba dive operator documentation found for this location. The cave opening, approach, and interior are accessible for recreational swimming; the cave is not documented as an active scuba diving destination by any Rhodes operator found during research.

The maximum depth of 25 metres listed in OSM likely refers to depths reachable in the surrounding coastal water, not within the cave itself.

## Safety

Cave diving requires specialist training beyond Open Water certification. The OSM tag scuba_diving:divespot=no and the absence of operator documentation for this site suggest this is not a routinely dived cave. Swimmers and snorkellers access the above-water portions; scuba diving the cave interior has not been confirmed by any source.
