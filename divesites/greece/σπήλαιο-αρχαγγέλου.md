## Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου (Archangelos Cave) is a sea cave dive site on the eastern coast of Rhodes, near the village of Archangelos.

## Overview

Σπήλαιο Αρχαγγέλου — Greek for "Archangelos Cave" — is a sea cave on the eastern coast of Rhodes near the village of the same name in the Dodecanese. The cave is accessible from the sea and features at least two chambers: the main cave includes a pebble beach inside, and a second smaller cave extends further inward. Visitors swimming or kayaking from the shoreline can access the main entrance chamber.

According to RhodesVacay and visitor reviews, the underwater approach features a natural arch, and reviewers note "amazing" light contrasts inside the caves and "most beautiful water." The maximum depth is 25 metres. The Advanced difficulty rating reflects the overhead environment requirement: cave diving beyond the cavern zone requires specialist training.

No published documentation from dive operators was located during research specifically for this cave as a scuba dive site. The OSM tag `scuba_diving:divespot = "no"` suggests the site may primarily attract snorkellers and swimmers rather than scuba divers, despite the sport=scuba_diving tag. The site appears in OpenStreetMap as a natural cave entrance with tourism=attraction.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat approach to cave entrance
- **Depth range**: 5–25 m
- **Environment**: Overhead (cave); cavern zone limited to daylight visibility from entry
- **Conditions**: Eastern Rhodes coast; typically calm; visibility 15–20 m
- **Best season**: May–October

## Safety

Cave diving beyond the cavern zone requires specialist certification (PADI Cave Diver, TDI Cave Diver, CMAS Cave Diver, or equivalent) and appropriate line and light equipment. Advanced divers without cave training should restrict exploration to the entrance cavern area where daylight remains visible. The OSM data suggests this may not be formally operated as a scuba dive site.
