## Καλλιθέα

Καλλιθέα (Kalithea) is a cave and cavern dive site in Rhodes, Greece.

## Overview

Kalithea Bay is a sheltered cove located a few kilometres south of Rhodes City on the island of Rhodes, Dodecanese. The site is within the monument grounds of the historic Kalithea Spa (Thermes Kalitheas), a remarkable Italian-built thermal bath complex from the 1920s–30s now recognised as a cultural heritage site. The name Kalithea means "beautiful view" in Greek.

Underwater, the site is no less striking. The bay features stunning cavern formations, swim-throughs, and a "mushroom cave" — shallow arch and overhang structures formed in the rocky shoreline that create dramatic light effects and shelter large numbers of fish. The bottom is sandy with rocky outcrops, and posidonia seagrass is present in adjacent areas. The maximum depth is approximately 12–15 metres, making this one of the most accessible dive sites in Rhodes and a popular location for introductory dives, Open Water students, and snorkellers.

Visibility typically reaches 20 metres in the clear Aegean water. Currents are weak to none inside the sheltered bay. Marine life includes bream, damselfish, pipefish, octopus, and rock fish, with occasional sightings of Mediterranean moray eels, flounder, sea needlefish, and the possibility (noted by dive operators) of encountering monk seals, loggerhead turtles, and barracuda in the broader bay area. The cavern formations provide shelter for large grouper in deeper sections.

The site is regularly dived by Rhodes-based operators and is suitable for all certification levels. Entry is typically by boat to the bay, with guided dives through the cavern features. The surrounding Italian-era architecture visible from the water adds to the distinctive character of the site.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat to bay; some guided shore entry options
- **Depth range**: 5–15 m
- **Bottom**: Sandy, rocky outcrops, posidonia seagrass patches
- **Conditions**: Sheltered; weak to no current
- **Visibility**: 15–20 m typical
- **Best season**: April–October

## Marine Life

- **Fish**: Sea bream, damselfish, Mediterranean pipefish, wrasse, rock fish, flounder, sea needlefish
- **Occasional**: Moray eels, octopus, grouper in cavern sections
- **Rare sightings reported by operators**: Mediterranean monk seal, loggerhead turtle, barracuda in the bay
- **Seagrass**: Posidonia oceanica beds in adjacent areas

## Photography

The cavern swim-throughs and light shafts through the mushroom cave structures create dramatic opportunities for wide-angle photography. The shallow depth allows excellent natural light. The historic spa architecture above water provides topside subjects.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kalithea, Rhodes, Dodecanese, South Aegean, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
