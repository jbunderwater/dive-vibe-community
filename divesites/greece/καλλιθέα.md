## Καλλιθέα

Kalithea Bay features a historic Italian spa complex perched above spectacular underwater caverns. This Rhodes-based dive site is renowned for its swim-throughs and mushroom cave formations at moderate depths.

## Overview

Kalithea Bay offers a unique combination of cultural and natural diving attractions. The restored Italian thermal springs complex above water pairs beautifully with stunning underwater caverns below. Visibility ranges from 10-30 meters with excellent water clarity during summer months. Water temperatures average 17-25°C depending on season.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Rhodes, Dodecanese, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
- **Typical Visibility**: 10-30 meters (30-100 feet)
- **Current**: Light to moderate
- **Best Time**: May to October
- **Facilities**: Accessible from Rhodes Town via the Waterhoppers diving platform

## Marine Life

Common sightings include bream, damselfish, pipefish, and octopus. Encounters with monk seals, sea turtles, and barracuda are possible. The limestone caverns and rock formations provide excellent habitat for groupers, moray eels, and various fish species.

## Dive Profile

The dive begins with a descent over sandy areas with scattered rock formations. Explore the famous mushroom cave and swim-through passages at 10-15 meters. Multiple cavern entrances provide dramatic photo opportunities with natural light filtering through openings. Most marine life congregates in the shallow to moderate depth range of 5-15 meters.

## Entry and Exit

Access is by dive boat from local operators. Entry is typically via giant stride or back roll. Follow the dive briefing for descent and ascent procedures. Deploy a surface marker buoy (SMB) during your safety stop for boat pickup. Coordinate with the boat crew for exit procedures.

## Tips and Recommendations

- Book with reputable local dive operators who know the site conditions
- Bring an underwater camera — this site offers excellent photography opportunities
- Check local weather and sea conditions before diving
- Respect marine life and maintain proper buoyancy to protect the reef

## Safety Considerations

Be aware of cold water (north), currents, boat traffic in this area. Dive within your certification limits and experience level. Always dive with a buddy and carry a safety sausage (SMB).

## Photography

This site offers excellent opportunities for both wide-angle and macro photography. The reef structures and marine life provide diverse subjects. Natural light conditions are typically best during morning hours.
