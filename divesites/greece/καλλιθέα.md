## Καλλιθέα

Καλλιθέα (Kalithea) is a cave and cavern dive site in Rhodes, Greece.

## Overview

Kalithea Bay is a sheltered cove located a few kilometres south of Rhodes City on the island of Rhodes, Dodecanese. The site is within the monument grounds of the historic Kalithea Spa (Thermes Kalitheas), an Italian-built thermal bath complex from the 1920s–30s recognised as a cultural heritage site. The name Kalithea means "beautiful view" in Greek.

The bay features documented cavern formations, swim-throughs, a Mushroom Cavern, and a Blow Hole Cave — shallow arch and overhang structures formed in the rocky shoreline that create dramatic light effects. Divers access the site by boat from Mandraki Harbour, with depth ranging from the surface to approximately 15 metres. Currents are none to very weak inside the sheltered bay, and the minimum certification required is Junior Scuba Diver, making this among the most accessible dive sites on Rhodes.

Visibility is 10–20 metres in the clear Aegean water. Confirmed resident species per Waterhoppers Diving School (operating at this site since 1990) include sea bream, damselfish, pipefish, rock fish, flounder, sea needlefish, and octopus. Waterhoppers notes possible — not regular — encounters with Mediterranean barracuda, monk seals, loggerhead turtles, and nudibranchs in the broader bay area. Grouper are present in the cavern sections.
