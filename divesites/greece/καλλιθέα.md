## Καλλιθέα

Καλλιθέα (Kalithea) is a cave and cavern dive site in Rhodes, Greece, set within the sheltered bay below the historic Italian-era Kalithea Spa.

## Overview

Kalithea Bay sits a few kilometres south of Rhodes City on the Dodecanese island of Rhodes. The site is embedded within the grounds of the Thermes Kalitheas, a 1920s–1930s Italian-built thermal bath complex now recognised as a heritage site. The name Kalithea means "beautiful view" in Greek.

The underwater setting matches the topside grandeur. The bay features cavern formations, swim-throughs, and a rock structure known as the "Mushroom Cavern" — shallow arches and overhangs that produce dramatic light effects and provide shelter for large numbers of fish. A feature called the "Blow Hole Cave" and connecting swim-throughs make this one of the most geologically varied shallow dives in Rhodes. The seabed is sandy with rocky outcrops. Maximum depth is 10–15 metres, making it one of the most accessible dive sites in Rhodes and a regular destination for introductory dives, Open Water students, and snorkellers.

Visibility typically reaches 15–20 metres in the clear Aegean water. Currents inside the sheltered bay are weak to none. Divers are launched from a custom-built dive platform rather than direct beach access.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat to bay via dive platform; Waterhoppers Diving School operates the site
- **Depth range**: 5–15 m
- **Bottom**: Sandy, rocky outcrops
- **Conditions**: Sheltered; weak to no current
- **Visibility**: 15–20 m typical
- **Best season**: April–October
- **Minimum certification**: Junior Scuba Diver / Open Water

## Marine Life

Documented species at Kalithea include sea bream, damselfish, rock fish, pipefish, flounder, sea needlefish, and octopus. Moray eels and grouper shelter in the deeper cavern sections. Monk seals, loggerhead turtles, and barracuda are occasionally sighted in the broader bay area according to Waterhoppers, but these are not reliable encounters and should not be expected on any given dive.

## Photography

The cavern swim-throughs and light shafts through the Mushroom Cavern create good opportunities for wide-angle photography. The shallow depth allows excellent natural light. The historic spa architecture visible from the water adds a distinctive topside backdrop.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kalithea, Rhodes, Dodecanese, South Aegean, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 15 meters
