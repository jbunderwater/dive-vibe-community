## Καλλιθέα

Καλλιθέα (Kalithea) is a cave and cavern dive site in the sheltered bay of Kalithea, a few kilometres south of Rhodes City.

## Overview

Kalithea Bay lies within the grounds of the historic Thermes Kalitheas (Kalithea Spa), an Italian-built thermal bath complex from the 1920s–1930s recognised as a cultural heritage site. The underwater site is equally striking: the bay features cavern formations, swim-throughs, a "mushroom cave," and a "blow hole cave" — shallow arch and overhang structures formed in the rocky shoreline that create dramatic light effects.

Working depth is 10–15 metres, making this one of the most accessible dive sites in Rhodes. Waterhoppers Diving School, which operates qualified dives here, notes a minimum qualification of Junior Scuba Diver (JSD) — the site is suitable for a wide range of certification levels. The bottom is sandy with rocky outcrops. Visibility typically reaches 10–20 metres. Currents are weak to none inside the sheltered bay.

Entry is by boat or platform (no direct beach access at the Thermes grounds). The site operates June through mid-October (reduced schedule in May and late October).

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat or platform to bay
- **Depth range**: 5–15 m (typical working depth 10–15 m)
- **Bottom**: Sandy, rocky outcrops, posidonia seagrass in adjacent areas
- **Features**: Mushroom cave, blow hole cave, swim-throughs
- **Conditions**: Sheltered; weak to no current
- **Visibility**: 10–20 m typical
- **Best season**: June–October (May and October reduced)

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at Kalithea by Waterhoppers Diving School: sea bream, damselfish, Mediterranean pipefish, rock fish, flounder. Occasional sightings of Mediterranean barracuda, nudibranchs. Rare but documented sightings of Mediterranean monk seals and loggerhead turtles in the broader bay area — Waterhoppers has recorded monk seal encounters at this site.

## Photography

The cavern swim-throughs and light shafts through the mushroom cave structures create dramatic opportunities for wide-angle photography. The shallow depth allows excellent natural light throughout the dive. The historic Thermes Kalitheas architecture above water provides topside subjects.

## Site Information

- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Cave/cavern
