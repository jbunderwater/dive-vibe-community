## Psarapoula

Psarapoula is a reef dive site off Rhodes in the Dodecanese Islands of Greece.

## Overview

Psarapoula is recorded in OpenStreetMap as a dive site off the northern coast of Rhodes. The site name appears in one indirect source (a Rhodes diving summary) that describes a "Psarapoula Maze" — a deep dive at the entrance of an ancient port, featuring a maze of large granite blocks visited by schools of pelagic fish. However, this description could not be confirmed from a primary dive operator source during research.

Research checked the websites of Waterhoppers Diving School, H2O Scuba Diving Center, Lepia Dive Centre, and Trident Diving School — none listed Psarapoula by name. It did not appear in ScubaBoard searches for Rhodes diving.

The coordinates (36.45°N, 28.22°E) place it off the northern Rhodes coast, in an area operated by multiple dive centres. The depth of 22–25 metres and Intermediate difficulty are consistent with typical Rhodes boat diving in this area.
