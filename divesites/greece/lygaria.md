## Lygaria

Lygaria is a reef dive site around a small peninsula on Crete's north coast near Agia Pelagia, known for its stepped reef profile, a swim-through tunnel, and three intact WWII bombs resting at 11 metres.

## Overview

Lygaria lies 0.8 nautical miles from the Divers' Club Crete centre at Agia Pelagia. The dive follows the reef wall around the peninsula in a series of depth steps: 6 metres, 12 metres, and 18 metres, reaching a maximum of 24 metres at the north-west corner. The route then moves shallower along the wall on the return leg, finishing at 10–12 metres.

The standout feature is a swim-through tunnel entered at 14 metres that exits at 8 metres. The tunnel is described by Divers' Club Crete as "one of the most exciting parts of the dive" and accommodates one diver at a time. Inside the passage, moray eels, crabs, scorpionfish, and seasonal lobsters shelter in the rocky walls. Just before the safety stop, three intact WWII bombs lie undisturbed at 11 metres — a consistent landmark on every dive of the site.

Throughout the dive, groupers of various sizes, moray eels, anemones, nudibranchs, octopus, crabs, and colonies of small red coral are found in holes and crevices. Amberjacks form large schools seasonally in the shallower sections.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat from Agia Pelagia dive centre
- **Depth range**: 6–24 m
- **Bottom**: Rocky reef with stepped terraces
- **Conditions**: Typically calm; beginner-friendly
- **Best season**: May–October
- **Minimum certification**: Open Water Diver

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agia Pelagia, Heraklion, Crete, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
