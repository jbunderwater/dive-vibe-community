## Lygaria

Lygaria is a reef dive site near Agia Pelagia, Crete, Greece.

## Overview

Lygaria is a quiet bay 0.8 nautical miles from the Divers Club Crete base at Agia Pelagia, around 20 km west of Heraklion on Crete's north coast. The reef wraps around a small peninsula and descends in distinct steps: 6 m at the boat anchor point, then 12 m, 18 m, and 24 m at the deepest northwest corner. Entry-level certified divers can dive to 18 m; the full site to 24 m is within beginner range.

The site's signature feature is a swim-through tunnel entered at 14 m and exiting at 8 m — wide enough for one diver at a time. The Divers Club Crete confirms the tunnel shelters moray eels, crabs, scorpionfish, and seasonally lobsters. Elsewhere on the reef: damselfish and sea bream on the upper sections; groupers, sea anemones, nudibranchs, octopus, amberjacks, and red coral colonies in the crevices and deeper areas. Three World War II bombs lie undisturbed at 11 m depth, visible near the safety stop point.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agia Pelagia, Heraklion, Crete, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
- **Notable Features**: Swim-through tunnel (14 m–8 m), three WWII bombs at 11 m
