## Lygaria

Lygaria is a stepped reef dive around a small peninsula on Crete's north coast, notable for a swim-through tunnel and three undisturbed WWII bombs at 11 metres.

## Overview

Lygaria reef wraps around a small peninsula on Crete's north coast near Agia Pelagia, approximately 0.8 nautical miles from the dive centre there. The reef descends in distinct steps — 6 metres, 12 metres, 18 metres — reaching a maximum of 24 metres at the northwest corner. Moving parallel to the wall at 14 metres, divers encounter a swim-through tunnel with an entrance at 14 metres and an exit at 8 metres. The tunnel is wide enough for a single diver and is noted as manageable even for those with minimum experience.

Just before the boat return and safety stop, three WWII bombs rest undisturbed at 11 metres depth. These are protected heritage objects; touching or removing them is prohibited.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat
- **Depth range**: 6–24 m (maximum at northwest corner)
- **Key feature**: Swim-through tunnel, entrance 14 m, exit 8 m
- **Safety stop area**: 11 m (WWII bombs visible here)
- **Conditions**: Typically calm; weak current
- **Visibility**: Good; Aegean typical

## Marine Life

Confirmed species at this site include damselfish, sea bream, amberjack, groupers of various sizes, moray eels, anemones, nudibranchs, crabs, scorpionfish, octopus, and lobster (seasonal). Small colonies of red coral are present in the deeper sections.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agia Pelagia, Heraklion, Crete, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
