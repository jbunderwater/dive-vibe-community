## Lygaria

Lygaria is a reef dive site on Crete's north coast, near Agia Pelagia, Greece.

## Overview

Lygaria reef wraps around a small peninsula approximately 0.8 nautical miles from the Agia Pelagia Diving Center. The reef descends in a series of steps — 6 metres, then 12, then 18 — to a maximum of 24 metres at the northwest corner. The site is suitable for both entry-level and experienced divers due to its graduated depth profile.

After rounding the northwest corner, divers follow the wall parallel to a swim-through tunnel: entrance at 14 metres, exit at 8 metres. The tunnel is wide enough for one diver at a time and shelters moray eels, crabs, scorpionfish, and seasonally, lobsters. Three unexploded WWII bombs lie undisturbed on the seabed at 11 metres depth near the safety stop point — these are a prominent landmark confirmed by the site's primary operator. Touching them is prohibited; they remain in place as a protected historical artefact.

The upper reef supports schools of damselfish and sea bream; groupers of various sizes, anemones, nudibranchs, octopus, and colonies of small red corals inhabit crevices throughout.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat (0.8 nm from Agia Pelagia)
- **Depth range**: 6–24 m (18 m max for beginner/level 1 divers)
- **Bottom**: Rocky reef steps with crevices and a swim-through tunnel
- **Conditions**: Generally calm; visibility regularly 20+ m
- **Best season**: April–October

## Marine Life

Moray eels, crabs, scorpionfish, lobsters (seasonal), damselfish, sea bream, amberjacks, groupers, anemones, nudibranchs, octopus, small red coral colonies. No site-specific marine mammal or shark sightings confirmed in sources reviewed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Agia Pelagia, Heraklion, Crete, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 24 meters
