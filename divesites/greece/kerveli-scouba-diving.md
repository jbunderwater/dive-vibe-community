## Kerveli scouba diving

Kerveli Bay is a naturally sheltered bay on the eastern coast of Samos, home to the Kerveli Village Diving Center and a craggy rocky reef system reaching 22 metres.

## Overview

Kerveli Dive Center operates within the Kerveli Village Hotel on the eastern coast of Samos, in a naturally protected bay with a private dock. The reef at Kerveli is described as a series of smaller reefs leading to the main reef at up to 22 metres, with spectacular steep walls including "Knife Wound Rock" and "Kerveli Reef." Visibility is reported to reach up to 40 metres with weak or no current, and the site is considered diveable in almost all weather conditions. The reef has both shallow sections suitable for beginners and deeper wall sections for advanced divers.

Marine life documented by the dive centre at Kerveli Bay: octopus, gilthead bream, marble bream, white bream, mullet, sea bass, and schools of golden stripe (Sardinella).

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat or shore from Kerveli Village Hotel dock
- **Depth range**: 5–22 m
- **Features**: Craggy reef, steep walls ("Knife Wound Rock," "Kerveli Reef")
- **Conditions**: Sheltered bay; almost always diveable; weak to no current
- **Visibility**: Up to 40 m in clear conditions
- **Best season**: May–October

## Marine Life

Confirmed by Kerveli Dive Center at this site: octopus, gilthead bream, marble bream, white bream, mullet, sea bass, golden stripe (Sardinella). The eastern Samos coastline is noted for good Aegean clarity.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kerveli Bay, Samos, Northeast Aegean Islands, Greece
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Reef
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
