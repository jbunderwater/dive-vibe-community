## Kerveli scouba diving

Kerveli Bay is a reef dive site on the eastern coast of Samos, Greece.

## Overview

Kerveli Bay is located on the eastern side of Samos, in the northeastern Aegean. The Kerveli Dive Center (samos-scuba.com), operating directly from this bay, describes it as a smooth, easy site with excellent visibility up to 30 metres, no currents, and a maximum depth of 22 metres — suitable for all certification levels. The eastern coast of Samos is widely considered to have the best underwater scenery on the island.

The reef is characterised by rocky reef formations, craggy coastline with drop-offs, walls, small caves, and caverns that provide habitat for marine life. The Kerveli Dive Center confirms the following species at the Kerveli reef: moray eels, octopuses, squids, and a variety of fish groups amid multicoloured Mediterranean sponges.

Note: A previous version of this description included claims about seahorses, posidonia seagrass beds, red coral, and sea fans "in the deeper sections," plus a reference to "Temple Bay" with ancient artefacts at 18 m depth. The seahorse, red coral, and sea fan claims could not be confirmed by the on-site operator's site documentation. The Temple Bay claim is unverified and has been removed. Regional marine life applies for unconfirmed species.
