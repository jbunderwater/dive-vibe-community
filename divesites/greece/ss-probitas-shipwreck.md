## SS Probitas Shipwreck

## Location Warning

The coordinates of this site (39.87°N, 20.01°E) place it in Saranda Bay, Albania — not in Greece. PADI, Mapcarta, the Albanian Diving Federation, and multiple independent diving sources consistently list this as an Albania dive site. The OSM node appears to have been incorrectly included in the Greece dataset. This entry is retained with its original coordinates and OSM ID pending review.

## Overview

The SS Probitas was an Italian cargo vessel sunk on 25 September 1943 in Saranda Bay, Albania, during World War II. The ship was part of a convoy and, following a mechanical breakdown that prevented it from departing with the rest of the convoy, was sunk in the afternoon of 25 September 1943 by German Junkers Ju 87 (Stuka) aircraft. Two sister vessels (the motor ship Salvore and a transport from Dubac) survived the attack by returning to Italy; the Probitas, moored at the dock, did not. Traces of the bomb damage are still visible on the wreck's sides.

The vessel is approximately 100–115 metres in length and rests on its port side in Saranda Bay, approximately 50–300 metres from shore (sources vary). Depth ranges from 3 metres at the shallowest point to 18 metres at the deepest sections. The wreck is largely intact. Marine life confirmed by PADI and dive operators at this site includes lionfish, grouper, bream, moray eels, nudibranchs, octopuses, and sponges. The shallow sections are accessible to snorkellers; scuba divers can explore decks, cargo holds, and remnants of wartime equipment. Open Water certification is the minimum recommended level.

**Discrepancy note:** The OSM record states a maximum depth of 22 m; confirmed sources report 18 m as the maximum depth. The previously recorded 22 m figure has been corrected to 18 m.
