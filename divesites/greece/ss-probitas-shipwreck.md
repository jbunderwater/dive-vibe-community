## SS Probitas Shipwreck

SS Probitas Shipwreck is a WWII wreck dive located in the Bay of Saranda, Albania — not in Greece.

## Overview

**Location note:** The coordinates for this site (39.870°N, 20.011°E) place it in Saranda Bay, Albania — a city in Vlorë County at the southernmost tip of Albania, approximately 14 km east of the northern end of Corfu. PADI lists this site under Albania, not Greece. It has been included here because the OSM data tags it as part of the Greece destination, but divers should be aware it requires entry into Albanian waters and is typically accessed from Saranda, Albania.

The MV Probitas was an Italian cargo vessel with a recorded tonnage of 5,084 gross tons and a length of 115.2 metres. It was sunk on 25 September 1943 by German Ju-87 (Stuka) dive bombers. The previous day the ship had been forced to remain in Saranda harbour due to a mechanical breakdown while its convoy departed, leaving it an isolated target. Traces of bomb damage are reportedly still visible on the hull.

The wreck lies on its port side on the sandy seabed, with the shallowest sections approximately 3–5 metres below the surface and the maximum depth at 20–22 metres. At 115.2 metres in length, it is a large wreck with significant structure available for exploration.

Penetration diving is possible at the accessible depths but requires appropriate wreck training and buoyancy control given the size of the structure.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat from Saranda, Albania (approximately 300 metres from shore)
- **Depth range**: 3–22 m
- **Bottom**: Sandy seabed; wreck on port side
- **Conditions**: Ionian Sea; visibility often 20–30 m; calm conditions typical
- **Certification note**: Rated Intermediate given the wreck complexity
