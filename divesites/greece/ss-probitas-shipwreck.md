## SS Probitas Shipwreck

The SS Probitas is an Italian WWII-era cargo ship lying in Saranda Bay — coordinates place this site within Albanian territorial waters, approximately 14 km from Corfu, Greece.

## Overview

**Location note:** The OSM coordinates (39.87°N, 20.01°E) place this wreck in the waters off Saranda, Albania, near the Albania–Greece border. PADI and the Albanian Diving Federation list this as an Albanian dive site. It is included here due to its OSM tagging under the Greece region, but divers should note it is accessed from Saranda, Albania.

The motor ship Probitas was built in 1919 by the Ansaldo shipyard at Muggiano, Italy. The vessel was 115.2 metres long and 15.7 metres wide, with a gross register tonnage of 5,084 tons. On 25 September 1943, the Probitas was in Saranda harbour (then called Porto Edda) due to a mechanical breakdown, separated from its convoy. German Ju-87 (Stuka) dive-bombers attacked the harbour and sank the ship. Bomb impact holes are still visible on the hull.

The wreck rests on its port side with its shallowest sections 3 metres below the surface and the deepest point at approximately 18–22 metres (sources vary). The well-preserved hull allows entry into the open cargo holds, where items including old helmets and tyres remain. The shallow depth and proximity to shore make this one of the most accessible WWII wrecks in the Adriatic/Ionian region.

Distance from shore: sources conflict — PADI states approximately 50 metres; other sources report approximately 300 metres. Access is by short boat trip from Saranda.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat from Saranda, Albania
- **Depth range**: 3–22 m (shallowest: 3 m; deepest: approximately 18–22 m)
- **Orientation**: Wreck on port side
- **Conditions**: Generally calm; sheltered bay
- **Visibility**: Clear Mediterranean conditions typical

## Marine Life

Marine life confirmed by dive operators at this site includes grouper, sea bream, moray eels, octopus, sponges, nudibranchs, and lionfish.

## Safety

Interior penetration (cargo holds) is accessible through open structures. Divers without wreck diving training should stay in open-water sections of the hull. Wreck Diver certification is recommended for interior exploration.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Saranda Bay, Albania (near Albania–Greece border)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wreck dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Beginner
- **Maximum Depth**: 22 meters
- **Vessel**: Italian motor cargo ship (1919–1943)
- **Length**: 115.2 metres; 5,084 GRT
- **Sunk**: 25 September 1943 by German Ju-87 aircraft
