## Uçak Batığı

Uçak Batığı (Turkish: "aircraft wreck") is a deeply submerged WWII aircraft at approximately 72 metres depth, located in the sea channel between Rhodes/Kos (Greece) and the Bodrum peninsula (Turkey).

## Overview

This aircraft wreck was documented by Waterhoppers Diving School (Rhodes) in July 2018, who identified the wreck as a **Junkers Ju-52** — a German WWII tri-engine transport aircraft — lying at approximately 72 metres depth on the seabed. Waterhoppers described the aircraft as "probably crash-landed on its way to the airfield" and noted that "further investigation to identify the plane is required." No serial number or specific mission history has been confirmed at the time of writing.

The Junkers Ju-52 was a standard Luftwaffe transport and paratrooper aircraft used extensively during the Battle of Crete (1941) and operations in the Dodecanese (1943). Multiple Ju-52 wrecks are present in Greek waters: a well-documented example lies at 65 metres off Kea island (Cyclades), and another was found at approximately 80 metres off Rhodes in 2018 — likely the same aircraft as this OSM entry given the geographic proximity.

The Waterhoppers blog post (2018) classified this as a "TEC Trimix" dive, confirming that it is a technical diving site beyond recreational limits.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat
- **Maximum depth**: 72 metres (technical diving depths)
- **Minimum certification**: Technical diving — advanced nitrox or trimix required; recreational certification does not cover depths below 40 m
- **Support**: Technical dive with support divers and staged decompression gas required
- **Location**: Greek/Turkish maritime border area — Kos–Bodrum strait

## Safety

At 72 metres, this dive requires technical diving certification (minimum: TDI Advanced Nitrox + Decompression Procedures, or equivalent). Significant decompression obligations apply. This site is not suitable for recreational divers without technical training and appropriate equipment.
