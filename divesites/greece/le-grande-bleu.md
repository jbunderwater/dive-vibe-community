## Le Grande Bleu

Le Grande Bleu is a reef dive site off Ios in the Cyclades, Greece, associated with the filming locations of Luc Besson's 1988 film "The Big Blue."

## Overview

The OSM entry for this site records its name in French ("Le Grande Bleu") with an English alias "The Big Blue," and its source tag notes it was added based on an "interview with Manganari local diver, records of Spiti Louka." The coordinates (36.650°N, 25.382°E) place it off the southern coast of Ios, near Manganari Bay.

The childhood scenes of Luc Besson's 1988 freediving film "The Big Blue" (Le Grand Bleu) were filmed at Manganari on the eastern coast of Ios — confirmed by multiple film location sources. Besson spent part of his childhood on the island, and the film's opening sequences were shot there. The adult competition sequences were primarily filmed on Amorgos. The dive site appears to be connected to the film's legacy rather than being a formally named dive location in operator databases.

No dedicated dive operator listings for "Le Grande Bleu" as a named dive site were located during research. The site's character, based on its location off a volcanic Cycladic island at a depth of approximately 20 metres, would be typical Mediterranean reef: posidonia seagrass, rocky formations, and standard Aegean species. However, specific marine life, conditions, or features cannot be confirmed from sources reviewed.
