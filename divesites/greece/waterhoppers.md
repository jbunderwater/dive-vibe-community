## Waterhoppers

Waterhoppers is an OSM point marking the operations area of Waterhoppers Diving School on Rhodes, Dodecanese, Greece.

## Overview

This OSM node (tagged "Info point only" in OSM) marks the general operations area of Waterhoppers Diving School near Rhodes Town, rather than a specific named dive site. Waterhoppers was established in Greece in 1978, began operations on Rhodes in 1990, and is the island's first PADI 5 Star IDC Center. The school runs dives to multiple sites along the Rhodes coastline including Kalithea, Kolymbia, Ladiko, Lindos, Pefkos, Plimiri, and a Marine Park.

The Waterhoppers website describes the general marine life encountered across its dive programme: sea bream (schools), cuttlefish, squid, octopus, grouper, microlife, and nudibranchs, with possible sightings of seahorses, amberjack, tuna, stingrays, turtles, and monk seals. Red Sea immigrant species (soldierfish, rabbitfish, occasional lionfish) have been observed in the area. These species descriptions are for the general Rhodes programme area, not for a single point.

No specific dive site named "Waterhoppers" was identified in independent dive databases. This OSM entry appears to mark a school/operator location rather than a defined dive site.
