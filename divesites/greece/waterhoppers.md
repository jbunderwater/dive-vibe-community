## Waterhoppers

Waterhoppers is an OSM-recorded dive point in the Rhodes Town area associated with the operations area of Waterhoppers Diving School, one of the longest-established dive operators in Greece.

## Overview

This OSM node is tagged as an "info point only" (per the OSM description tag) and represents the operating area or an entry point associated with Waterhoppers Diving School, a PADI 5-Star IDC centre established in Greece in 1978 and operating on Rhodes since 1990. The coordinates (36.447°N, 28.226°E) place it off the northeast coast of Rhodes, roughly in the Kalithea area.

Waterhoppers operates multiple named dive sites around Rhodes, including Kalithea, Kolymbia, Ladiko, Lindos, Marine Park, Pefkos, and Plimiri. The dive sites around Rhodes feature rocky Mediterranean seabed with walls, caverns, and swim-throughs. The general area supports diverse marine life including bream, cuttlefish, octopus, grouper, moray eels, and an increasing presence of Red Sea immigrant species (soldierfish, rabbitfish, lionfish) that have entered the Aegean via the Suez Canal.

The recorded depth of 25 metres and site type of reef are broadly consistent with the types of sites Waterhoppers operates in this area, but no site-specific documentation for a dive site named "Waterhoppers" at these exact coordinates was located.
