## Waterhoppers

Waterhoppers is the name of the Waterhoppers Diving School — a long-established PADI 5-Star IDC centre on Rhodes. This OSM node appears to mark the school's operational area or a dive site in the waters off their base.

## Overview

**Note:** The OSM entry for "Waterhoppers" carries the description "Info point only," indicating this node marks the dive school's location rather than a distinct named dive site. Waterhoppers Diving Schools (founded 1978, operating on Rhodes since 1990) is the largest dive centre in Greece. The school runs dives at multiple sites around Rhodes including Kalithea, Kolymbia, Ladiko, Lindos, the Marine Park, Pefkos, and Plimiri.

The waters off Rhodes in the Dodecanese offer rocky Mediterranean seabed with volcanic formations, caverns, and walls with typical Aegean visibility of 20+ metres in summer. Common marine life reported by Waterhoppers across their Rhodes dive programme includes schools of bream, cuttlefish, squid, octopus, grouper, sea horses, amberjacks, stingrays, and occasional turtles and Mediterranean monk seals.
