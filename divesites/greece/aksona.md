## Aksona

Aksona is a wall dive site in the Kos–Bodrum strait area, near the Greek island of Kos.

## Overview

The coordinates for this site (36.960°N, 27.486°E) place it in the strait between the Greek island of Kos and the Turkish Bodrum peninsula. The name "Aksona" is specifically associated with a well-documented Bodrum dive site named in honour of a famous local sponge hunter, Aksona Mehmet. The SSI MyDiveGuide database lists "Aksona Bay" as a Turkey site. The exact national waters of this coordinate cannot be determined without authoritative maritime boundary data — it may fall on the Greek or Turkish side of the median line.

Aksona Bay is characterised by a shallow plateau starting at approximately 3 metres that slopes steeply down to a wall dropping to 30–35 metres. This makes it a wall dive rather than a simple reef. The site is documented as suitable for intermediate to advanced divers due to the depth and the steep descent.

Archaeological features confirmed at Aksona include amphorae and a Phoenician-era ship's anchor at approximately 13.5 metres depth on a flat rock — these features are documented by multiple Bodrum dive operators.

Marine life includes groupers, barracudas, moray eels, and octopus. The site is heavily draped in corals and sponges.

## Dive Profile

- **Entry**: Boat (approximately 50 minutes from Bodrum; shorter from Kos)
- **Depth range**: 3–35 m (wall drops from ~20 m)
- **Bottom**: Shallow plateau transitioning to steep wall
- **Conditions**: Aegean; visibility typically good
- **Certification**: Intermediate to Advanced recommended

## Marine Life

Groupers, barracudas, moray eels, octopus. Site is described as rich in corals and sponges. No site-specific fish counts or rare species sightings were confirmed in sources reviewed.

## Site Information

- **Location**: Kos–Bodrum strait (Greek/Turkish waters boundary area)
- **Entry Type**: Boat dive
- **Site Type**: Wall dive
- **Difficulty Level**: Intermediate
- **Maximum Depth**: 28 meters
