## Aksona

Aksona is a dive site in the sea channel between Kos (Greece) and the Bodrum peninsula (Turkey), near the Greek–Turkish maritime border.

## Overview

**Location note:** The dive site named "Aksona Bay" is documented by SSI DiverGuide as a Turkish/Bodrum-area dive site. The coordinates of this OSM node (36.96°N, 27.49°E) fall in the sea between Kos island (Greece) and the Turkish coast near Bodrum. It is unclear whether this node represents the Bodrum-side Aksona Bay or a Greek-side dive point accessed from Kos. No Greek dive operator documentation for a site at these exact coordinates has been found.

The Aksona Bay site documented by Bodrum operators begins at approximately 3 metres depth and slopes to 20–35 metres, where the bottom drops over a rock wall. The site is described as exposed to wind and choppy conditions, with corals, sponges, groupers, barracuda, octopus, moray eels, and occasional thornback rays. The bay is named after a local sponge hunter named Aksona Mehmet.

Given the lack of confirmed Greek dive operator records for this site, the description above reflects the Bodrum-side Aksona Bay, which may or may not be the site represented by this OSM node.
