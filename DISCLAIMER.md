# Disclaimer

## Important Safety Warning

**Scuba diving and freediving are inherently dangerous activities that can result in serious injury or death.** The information in this repository is provided for general informational and community reference purposes only. It is **not a substitute** for:

- Professional dive training and certification (PADI, SSI, NAUI, CMAS, or equivalent)
- Local knowledge from qualified dive operators and guides at each destination
- Current conditions assessment (weather, currents, visibility, seasonal hazards)
- Proper dive planning with appropriate equipment, gas management, and buddy procedures
- Official nautical charts, marine notices, and local regulations

**Always dive within your training and certification limits.** Never plan a dive based solely on information from this or any online database.

- **Accuracy of coordinates** — GPS positions may be approximate, offset, or outdated

Portions of the dive site descriptions in this repository were created or edited with the assistance of AI language models. While we follow strict [anti-hallucination policies](CLAUDE.md#anti-hallucination-policy-critical) and validate content against real sources, AI-generated text may contain:

- Factual errors, including fabricated historical details, incorrect species attributions, or inaccurate dimensions
- Outdated information that was accurate at the time of generation but has since changed
- Descriptions that conflate details from nearby or similarly named sites

AI-generated descriptions are **not a primary source** of dive site information. Always verify critical details (depth, access, hazards, required certifications) with local dive operators before diving.

**Before any dive, you should:**

1. Consult with a local dive operator or guide who knows the site
2. Check current weather, tide, and marine conditions
3. Verify that the site is open and legal to dive
